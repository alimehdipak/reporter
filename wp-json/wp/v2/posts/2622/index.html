DC Comics artist and writer George Perez, who illustrated \u201cNew Teen Titans\u201d and helped relaunch \u201cWonder Woman,\u201d has died at the age of 67, the comics publisher announced on Saturday.<\/p>\n

The South Bronx native died of pancreatic cancer peacefully beside his wife, Variety reported.<\/p>\n

\u201cGeorge P\u00e9rez had an art style that was both dynamic and incredibly expressive,\u201d DC Publisher and Chief Creative Officer Jim Lee said in a statement.<\/p>\n

\u201cHis art was the perfect storytelling canvas for some of the most important events in DC history. While he will be sorely missed, his work will live on with a countless number of fans, as well as all the talent he\u2019s influenced over the years.\u201d<\/p>\n

The writer, co-plotter, penciller, and inker worked with author Marv Wolfman on the iconic New Teen Titans series in the 1980s. Perez was also credited as the \u201cartistic force\u201d behind 1985\u2019s crossover series \u201cCrisis on Infinite Earths,\u201d which celebrated the company\u2019s 50th anniversary and streamlined continuity across its titles, according to D.C.<\/p>\n

George Perez was a writer, co-plotter, penciler, and inker \u2014 working with author Marv Wolfman on the iconic New Teen Titans series in the 1980s.Getty Images for Amazing Comic C <\/p>\n

Perez additionally was a contributor in the 1987 relaunch of \u201cWonder Woman,\u201d which helped bring \u201cthe character closer to her roots in Greek mythology.\u201d<\/p>\n

He also designed Superman villain Lex Luthor\u2019s iconic battle suit in Action Comics for the Man of Steel.<\/p>\n

In 2003-2004, he illustrated the D.C. Comics-Marvel crossover \u201cJLA\/Avenger\u201d featuring superheroes from the Justice League of America and the Avengers.<\/p>\n

\u201cJust like all of the comic book fans that he was always so overjoyed at meeting at a signing or convention, I\u2019ll forever cherish the time that I got to spend with George,\u201d said DC Editor-in-Chief Marie Javins. <\/p>\n

\u201cHe was a one-of-a-kind person that brought so much joy to the world, and we are all better for having known George and his impressive body of work.\u201d<\/p>\n
