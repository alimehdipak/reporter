Viewers will be in for quite a sugar rush when they see Jerry Seinfeld\u2019s new film, \u201cUnfrosted: The Pop-Tarts Story.\u201d<\/p>\n

Seinfeld, 68, is starring, writing, directing and producing the origin story of the breakfast pastry \u2014 his first film in 15 years.<\/p>\n

He last lent his acting chops on the silver screen in 2007\u2019s \u201cBee Movie.\u201d<\/p>\n

On Wednesday, the Netflix movie\u2019s delicious cast was announced and includes A-list stars that sound de-lite-ful.<\/p>\n

Melissa McCarthy, Jim Gaffigan, Amy Schumer, Hugh Grant, James Marsden, Jack McBrayer, Tom Lennon, Adrian Martinez, Bobby Moynihan, Max Greenfield, Christian Slater and\u00a0Sarah Cooper are set to appear in the comedy, the Wrap reported.<\/p>\n

The flick will take watchers back to 1963 Michigan, and will chronicle the food manufacturing\u00a0companies, Kellogg\u2019s and Post, who are cereal enemies.<\/p>\n

Seinfeld first got the idea to do a movie about Pop-Tarts during the coronavirus pandemic.NBCU Photo Bank via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201c[It is] a tale of ambition, betrayal, sugar and menacing milkmen,\u201d the logline for the movie said.<\/p>\n

The story follows the two brands as they compete to devise a pastry that will transform how kids eat breakfast for generations to come.<\/p>\n

Seinfeld co-wrote the film with Spike Feresten and Barry Marder and came up with the project idea during the coronavirus pandemic, Deadline reported last year.<\/p>\n

\u201cStuck at home watching endless sad faces on TV, I thought this would be a good time to make something based on pure silliness,\u201d he told the publication. \u201cSo we took my Pop-Tart stand-up bit from my last Netflix special and exploded it into a giant, crazy comedy movie.\u201d<\/p>\n

Melissa McCarthy will be starring in the comedy film alongside Hugh Grant, Jim Gaffigan, Amy Schumer, James Marsden and more. NBCU Photo Bank via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The \u201cComedians in Cars Getting Coffee\u201d star has a passion for the frosted breakfast staple and the film is inspired by a segment from his 2020 Netflix special \u201c23 Hours to Kill.\u201d<\/p>\n

He recalled in his show that, at age 8, \u201cthe back of [his] head blew right off\u201d when he first saw Pop-Tarts in the grocery store.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was in the supermarket with my mother and I was like, \u2018Hold up, hold up \u2014 what the hell is that?\u2019 \u201d he said. \u201cWhen you open the packet, there\u2019s two. Why?
