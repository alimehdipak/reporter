This \u201chaunted\u201d hotel is only for guests who love things that go bump in the night.<\/p>\n

Those brave enough can enjoy a stay at Ye Olde Kings Head in the UK \u2014 although those guests wishing for a spooky evening must sign a waiver upon arrival and agree to have the night filmed to air in a weekly episode on the \u201cMy Haunted Hotel\u201d YouTube channel.<\/p>\n

Built in 1622, the hotel in Chester has a long and colorful past, starting as a townhouse before moving on to a stint as a brothel and then being converted into an inn in 1714.<\/p>\n

With footage showing chairs moving by themselves, guests being screamed at by supposed \u201centities\u201d and \u201chaunted\u201d dolls flying at guests in the nursery, it\u2019s certainly not for the faint of heart.<\/p>\n

The UK hotel was sold to paranormal enthusiast Harry Achilleos in 2012 when it was operating as a fully working hotel. <\/p>\n

Would you spend the night at this reportedly \u201chaunted\u201d hotel?Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel The rooms come with a side of spook at this haunted inn.Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel Guests must agree to be recorded and sign a waiver upon arrival.Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel The stay is complete with a team of paranormal investigators.Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel <\/p>\n

Achilleos, who had his own paranormal group in 2011, knew of the Ye Olde Kings Head and got to know the landlord and started booking the hotel for private events.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe place was so active that I asked the landlord if I could put cameras in the rooms to document any activity,\u201d Achilleos told NeedToKnow.online. \u201cHe informed me that he was selling the building and I purchased it.\u201d<\/p>\n

However, Achilleos was unable to run investigations 24\/7, as it might disturb paying guests, which lead to the hotel fully succumbing to its spooky reputation and opening as a designated ghost-hunting mecca named \u201cMy Haunted Hotel\u201d \u2014 complete with its own team of paranormal investigators on-site.<\/p>\n

The ultimate mood lighting for ghost-hunting.Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel It might not be the hotel stay that inspires your next home renovation. Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel No room, including the nursery, is supposedly safe from paranormal activity. Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel <\/p>\n

However, if you stay, you are expected to fully \u201cparticipate\u201d and agree to be filmed \u2014 no matter how scared you get.<\/p>\n

The eerie hotel got an even spookier makeover by designer and paranormal investigator Brett Jones, with Slender Man-like figures on one wall, satanic crosses, a ghostly nursery, a mirror room and a pentagram-clad floor.<\/p>\n

According to Jones, after redecorating the hotel, the paranormal activity intensified.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe have really disturbed something in this building and every night has been an incredible experience for our guests,\u201d he revealed.<\/p>\n

What does a night in the hotel look like?<\/h2>\n

Brave guests who have booked a stay can check in at 8:30 p.m.; they are then fully briefed ahead of the investigation before going to their rooms.<\/p>\n

There are 16 cameras that monitor the entire location from the six bedrooms, the old brothel section, the top corridor and even the cellars. Throughout the night, guests are contacted via radio by the \u201cMy Haunted Hotel\u201d team, who oversee the entire investigation from the control room.<\/p>\n

A good night\u2019s sleep is not guaranteed in this hotel.Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel The rocking chair in one of the rooms reportedly was spotted moving by itself.Jam Press\/My Haunted Hotel <\/p>\n

Typically, guests investigate and explore their rooms and are encouraged by the team to conduct \u201clone vigils and various experiments throughout the building.\u201d Guests have the option to turn their room cameras off after 2 a.m. when the investigation ends and check-out is the following day at 12:30 p.m.<\/p>\n

Danny Moss, a paranormal investigation team member and also a star of the spooky series \u201cThe Haunted Hunts,\u201d said they\u2019ve captured everything from \u201cdisembodied voices, footsteps and even poltergeist activity.<\/p>\n

\u201cOur most famous capture so far was in room 6 when the rocking chair was moving by itself at 3:28 p.m. on a Wednesday afternoon,\u201d he recalled before opening up about a particularly disturbing piece of the property\u2019s history.<\/p>\n

\u201cOne of the biggest mysteries of the building is when it served as a brothel during the 17th century,\u201d he said. \u201cWe believe that one of the ladies who worked there actually fell pregnant, which, of course, was forbidden. We believe the man who owned the brothel may have murdered her and her child and buried them in a part of the building.\u201d<\/p>\n
