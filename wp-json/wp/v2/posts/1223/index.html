A Brit who plunged into a crevasse while snowboarding has told the incredible story of how his iPhone saved his life.<\/p>\n

Tim Blakely, 41, was boarding 10,000 feet up a Swiss mountain off-piste and alone when he fell 15 feet down a hidden gap in the ice.<\/p>\n

He would have dropped farther and likely died had he not landed on a fragile snow bridge below.<\/p>\n

Writing on\u00a0Instagram\u00a0on March 27, Blakely described how his iPhone\u2019s Emergency SOS feature came to his rescue.<\/p>\n

Activated using a few quick button presses, the tool automatically calls the local emergency number and sends them your location.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis past week has been a wave of emotions and introspection and I\u2019ve not really known what to do with this experience,\u201d Blakely wrote.<\/p>\n

\u201cThank you to @apple, their side button five click to emergency services \u2013 especially great when your screen is constantly being dripped on.\u201d<\/p>\n

Writing on Instagram on March 27, Blakely described how his iPhone\u2019s Emergency SOS feature came to his rescue.Instagram\/@mrtimblakey <\/p>\n

Despite finding himself several feet below the surface of the ice, Blakely said he still had a 3G connection.<\/p>\n

His phone was able to alert Swiss rescue services to his position even though he only had 3 percent of his battery power left.<\/p>\n

It apparently took him 20 minutes to get hold of someone.<\/p>\n

Blakely said he has returned to London \u201crelatively unharmed.\u201dInstagram\/@mrtimblakey <\/p>\n

They told him not to move and, following the call, the New Zealand-born adrenaline junky gingerly took photos of what could have easily been his final resting place.<\/p>\n

Forty-five minutes later, rescuers were able to haul the personal trainer out of the crevasse and he escaped the ordeal with only an injured ankle ligament.<\/p>\n

Blakely said he has returned to London \u201crelatively unharmed.\u201d<\/p>\n

Blakely said his phone saved his life and the thought of not having access to it at the time has been \u201ckeeping me up at night.\u201d<\/p>\n

He advised others not to be so careless.<\/p>\n

\u201cSeventeen years snowboarding, and the majority of those times spending a lot of the time solo and off-piste. Never solo again.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cNo matter how experienced you think you are, it is no joke.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI was lured into a false sense of security which also led me to be very blas\u00e9 about researching the areas I snowboard.\u201d<\/p>\n

He added: \u201cIt\u2019s not fair to say \u2018my luck ran out\u2019 because the circumstances of my survival clearly show I\u2019ve cashed in every last luck token at my disposal.\u201d<\/p>\n

How to make an SOS call on\u00a0iPhone<\/h2>\n

After the call ends, your iPhone will ping the emergency service with your current location, unless you choose to cancel. <\/p>\n

On iPhones without a Home Button:<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Press and hold the side button and\u00a0one of the volume buttons until the Emergency\u00a0SOS slider appears.<\/li>\n
  2. Drag the Emergency\u00a0SOS\u00a0slider to call emergency services. If you continue to hold down the side button and Volume button, instead of dragging the slider, a countdown begins and an alert sounds.<\/li>\n
  3. If you hold down the buttons until the countdown ends, your iPhone automatically calls emergency\u00a0services.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    On iPhone 7 or earlier:<\/p>\n

      \n
    1. Rapidly press the side button five times. The Emergency SOS slider will appear.<\/li>\n
    2. Drag the Emergency\u00a0SOS\u00a0slider to\u00a0call emergency\u00a0services.<\/li>\n
    3. If you started an emergency call by accident, press the Stop button, then tap Stop Calling.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n
