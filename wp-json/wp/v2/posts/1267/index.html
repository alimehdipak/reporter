<\/p>\n

Potter fans can bring the magic to their iPhone with a few special tricks.<\/p>\n

Thanks to Siri and some easy hacks, you can make your device cast all sorts of spells you\u2019ll know from the\u00a0Wizarding World of Harry Potter.<\/p>\n

From Lumos to Silencio, here are 10 you can try out.<\/p>\n

Some will need setting up on the iPhone Shortcuts app, but they\u2019re pretty straightforward.<\/p>\n

Lumos<\/h2>\n

Probably one of the most well-known Potter spells is Lumos, which is used by Harry and friends to light up their wands as they lurk through the corridors of Hogwarts at night.<\/p>\n

Just like a wizard or witch, you can ask your iPhone to light up too \u2013 or your iPhone\u2019s torch at least.<\/p>\n

Just say: \u201cHey Siri, Lumos.\u201d<\/p>\n

Nox<\/h2>\n

Lumos wouldn\u2019t be complete without the ability to switch your torch off too.<\/p>\n

That\u2019s where Nox comes in.<\/p>\n

Just say: \u201cHey Siri, Nox.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tell Siri \u201cLumos\u201d to light your phone as a wizard would to light their wand.WARNER BROS AND J K ROWLING \/ Ro <\/p>\n

Accio<\/h2>\n

Accio is used by magic folk to summon objects to them.<\/p>\n

Unfortunately, you can\u2019t fetch items from across your room like Harry \u2013 that would be something \u2013 but you can use it to open apps.<\/p>\n

Of course, it\u2019s worth noting that you can ask Siri to open apps without saying Accio, so this one is best for the hardcore Potter fans.<\/p>\n

Just say: \u201cHey Siri, Accio\u201d and the app\u2019s name.<\/p>\n

Telling Siri \u201cAccio\u201d is a cool way to open specific apps on your iPhone.Courtesy of Warner Bros Pictures <\/p>\n

Spells with the Shortcuts app<\/h2>\n

The following spells can only be done using the Shortcuts app on iPhone.<\/p>\n

For each, start by tapping the + icon in the top right, followed by Add Action.<\/p>\n

Lumos Maxima<\/h2>\n

Search for \u201cSet Torch\u201d and select it.<\/p>\n

You\u2019ll notice an arrow pointing right next to the words \u201cTurn torch on.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tap it and set the brightness right up.<\/p>\n

Finally, name it \u201cLumos Maxima\u201d at the top and hit the play button at the bottom.<\/p>\n

Now when you say, \u201cHey Siri, Lumos Maxima\u201d the light will switch on to its brightest.<\/p>\n

Similar to Lumos, Lumos Maxima will light up your phone, but this trick will brighten your screen to its maximum strength.Courtesy of Warner Bros Pictures <\/p>\n

Silencio<\/h2>\n

Silencio does what it says on the tin \u2013 silence.<\/p>\n

Search \u201cSet focus\u201d and tap Off so it changes to On.<\/p>\n

Name it \u201cSilencio\u201d at the top and hit the play button at the bottom.<\/p>\n

When you say \u201cHey Siri, Silencio,\u201d it\u2019ll automatically go into do not disturb mode.<\/p>\n

The Silenco spell will turn your phone on its do not disturb setting.Courtesy of Warner Bros Pictures <\/p>\n

Muffliato<\/h2>\n

Muffliato is used in the Wizarding World to create a muffle so others can\u2019t hear private conversations.<\/p>\n

On iPhone, you can do something similar, by setting the volume all the way down.<\/p>\n

Search \u201cSet Volume\u201d and take the percentage right down.<\/p>\n

Name it \u201cMuffliato\u201d at the top and hit the play button at the bottom.<\/p>\n

When you say \u201cHey Siri, Muffliato,\u201d it\u2019ll turn the volume right down.<\/p>\n

By telling Siri \u201cMuffliato,\u201d you can automatically turn your phone\u2019s volume down.\u00a9Warner Bros\/courtesy Everett C <\/p>\n

Sonorus<\/h2>\n

Sonorus is ideal when you want to turn the volume right up.<\/p>\n

Do the same as Muffliato above, but this time set the volume higher and name it \u201cSonorus\u201d instead of course.<\/p>\n

When you say \u201cHey Siri, Sonorus,\u201d it\u2019ll crank up.<\/p>\n

Quietus<\/h2>\n

If you want the volume set to something more moderate, use Quietus.<\/p>\n

Repeat the same process but go for a medium volume instead, like 50 percent.<\/p>\n

When you say \u201cHey Siri, Quietus,\u201d it\u2019ll get working.<\/p>\n

The Muffliato, Sonorus and Quietus spells follow similar iPhone set up guidelines.\u00a9Warner Bros\/courtesy Everett C <\/p>\n

Homenum Revelio<\/h2>\n

Homenum Revelio reveals who is in your presence.<\/p>\n

If you use Find My Friends, you can make it do something similar.<\/p>\n

Simply search \u201cOpen URLs\u201d and add it.<\/p>\n

Set the URL to \u201cfindmyfriends:\/\/\u201d<\/p>\n

Then say: \u201cHey Siri, Homenum Revelio.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cPoint Me\u201d spell is a cool way to open your phone\u2019s compass app.\u00a9Warner Bros\/courtesy Everett C <\/p>\n

Point Me<\/h2>\n

Need help getting about?<\/p>\n

Point Me is the spell to use \u2013 or in the iPhone\u2019s case, the compass app.<\/p>\n

Search compass and rename it \u201cPoint Me.\u201d<\/p>\n

Then say: \u201cHey Siri, Point Me.\u201d<\/p>\n
