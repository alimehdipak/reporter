It would be the biggest comeback in Hollywood history \u2014 and a slap in the face to Will Smith.<\/p>\n

Speculation is mounting that Chris Rock will be named the host of next year\u2019s Oscars \u2014 after being spectacularly smacked down at this year\u2019s ceremony by Best Actor winner Smith.<\/p>\n

ABC\u2019s President of Entertainment Craig Erich told Deadline on Tuesday that he is open to having Rock, 57, helm the next awards show. Erich also said next year\u2019s show, which will be held in March, will be \u201ceven better.\u201d<\/p>\n

The broadcast exec told the publication that he was happy with the ratings for this year, but the decision to have Rock host next time around would likely increase viewership even more. <\/p>\n

\u201cObviously there was a lot of controversy [surrounding this year\u2019s broadcast], which overshadowed a lot of the positive things about the show, but I was really happy with the program,\u201d Erich stated. \u201cIt was the biggest bounce-back of any award show this season.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ratings for this year\u2019s broadcast were up 58%, with an average 16.6 million viewers tuning in. In comparison, a paltry 9.8 million Americans watched the 2021 show. <\/p>\n

Many people tuned in to the ceremony after Smith, 53, stormed the stage and slapped Rock across the face. The violent act was caused by a G.I. Jane joke Rock made about Jada Pinkett Smith, who suffers from\u00a0hair loss condition, alopecia.<\/p>\n

However, Erwich insisted that viewer numbers were already up before the slap took place, stating: \u201cEven before that very unfortunate moment, the show started off up year over year, and we came back in a big way.\u201d<\/p>\n

The ceremony was hosted by Wanda Sykes, Amy Schumer and Regina Hall, with the ABC boss asserting that trio delivered for the network. <\/p>\n

Erwich also said of the show\u2019s producers: \u201cIf you look at what they were trying to do, which is really bring entertainment back to the program, they had three amazing hosts, clips, and incredible musical performances, I thought it was a great way for the show to reclaim its place on the mantle.\u201d<\/p>\n
