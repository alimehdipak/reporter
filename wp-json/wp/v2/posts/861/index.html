<\/p>\n

There is no shortage of sci-fi robot companions \u2013 Luke Skywalker\u2019s robot buddies C3PO and R2D2 and Jimmy Neutron\u2019s robot-dog Goddard come to mind.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Astronauts aboard the\u00a0International Space Station\u00a0have some real-life robotic friends.<\/p>\n

Astrobee and Project CIMON are two initiatives to develop robots to help space crews operate in zero gravity.<\/p>\n

Astrobee, designed by\u00a0NASA, is a group of three cubed-shaped robots that can fly around the ISS, grip pipes to stabilize themselves and have cameras for \u201cseeing\u201d their surroundings.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The set of three robots are aptly named Queen, Honey and Bumble as they\u2019re \u201cbusy bees\u201d aboard the ISS, carrying out simple tasks like taking inventories to reduce the burden on the astronauts.<\/p>\n

Astrobee\u2019s robots can be manually operated by teams on Earth or be programmed to work autonomously.<\/p>\n

Queen, Honey and Bumble replaced an existing group of floating bots and serve as an upgrade on the ISS.<\/p>\n

SPHERES, the first group of flying bots aboard the ISS, were deployed in 2006 and have been put to use for\u00a0over 500 hours\u00a0alongside astronauts.<\/p>\n

One of the aspirations for Astrobee is to design software advanced enough that the robots can\u00a0manage spacecraft upkeep\u00a0on Gateway, the unmanned space outpost set to launch in 2024.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s a lofty goal as the ISS has\u00a0never been vacant\u00a0in its 22-year history and is reliant on humans.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, the German-funded Project CIMON (Crew Interactive Mobile companiON) has developed a similar robot that\u2019s slightly more humanoid.<\/p>\n

Cimon responds to verbal commands from astronauts and has an LCD screen for displaying the bot\u2019s \u201cface\u201d.<\/p>\n

Like the Astrobee bots, Cimon can propel itself about the station and complete basic assignments in service of the astronauts.<\/p>\n

Both the first and second generations of Cimon were brought to the ISS by\u00a0Elon Musk\u2019s space travel company\u00a0SpaceX.<\/p>\n

Part of Cimon\u2019s initiative is to help astronauts deal with the isolation that comes with being in space \u2013 after all, everyone the astronauts know are living miles below them on Earth.<\/p>\n

Astronauts can talk to Cimon the way that Earth-bound humans talk to an Alexa or Siri \u2013 whether that\u2019s conversing or accessing the computer\u2019s database of information.<\/p>\n

Floating robots are carving out a special niche aboard the ISS, working to serve as an astronaut\u2019s right-hand robot.<\/p>\n
