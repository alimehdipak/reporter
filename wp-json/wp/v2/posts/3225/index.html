<\/p>\n

It\u2019s one of those perfect spring days \u2014 70 degrees and nothing but blue skies \u2014 that New Yorkers don\u2019t get enough of before the cold dreariness of winter melts into the sweltering humidity of summer. Julianne Hough\u2019s blond hair and bright smile are glistening in Greenwich Village, just a short walk from the Washington Square Park area where she is living her New York dream. No doubt, she is soaking up the sun in this manifested moment.<\/p>\n

Before she even made her Broadway debut in the new political farce \u201cPOTUS: Or, Behind Every Great Dumbass Are Seven Women Trying to Keep Him Alive,\u201d the 33-year-old dancer, actress, singer and entrepreneur was already choreographing a Big Apple move.<\/p>\n

\u201cBack in October, I remember calling my manager. I was like, \u2018OK, I want to immerse myself in what\u2019s happening in Broadway, because they\u2019ve obviously had the hardest time through the pandemic,\u2019\u201d says Hough over a salade ni\u00e7oise<\/em> and pommes frites<\/em> at Petite Boucherie. \u201cI was like, \u2018Keep an eye out to see if anything\u2019s happening. But I think I just need to put myself in proximity, so maybe I\u2019ll just move there in March.\u2019 Then, in February, I get this script out of nowhere, and it was an offer for the role. So I very much put it out there, manifested it, not really knowing what it was going to be \u2014 just that I wanted to be here.\u201d<\/p>\n

After her big break on \u201cDancing With the Stars\u201d in 2007 led to movies such as \u201cFootloose\u201d and \u201cRock of Ages,\u201d judging stints on both \u201cDWTS\u201d and \u201cAmerica\u2019s Got Talent,\u201d and even a country album, Hough is now exactly where she wants to be \u2014 just a few months after her divorce from former hockey star Brooks Laich was finalized in February. It\u2019s not lost on Hough that she is embracing her new life as both part of the Broadway community and the queer community, just steps from the birthplace of LGBTQ rights at Stonewall. \u201cIt\u2019s a new beginning,\u201d she says.<\/p>\n

In this next chapter, Hough shares the stage at the Shubert Theatre with an all-female cast, including Vanessa Williams, Lea DeLaria and \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d alum Rachel Dratch in a play that, set in the White House, takes satirical aim at the patriarchy.<\/p>\n

\u201cI read the first page, and I thought it was the funniest thing I had read in a very long time,\u201d says Hough. \u201cThe fact that Selina Fillinger, our playwright, is 28 years old \u2026 and our female director [Susan Stroman] is a legend and an icon \u2014 especially for me, because she started as a choreographer \u2014 before I even got to my character, I was like, \u2018Yes!\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

As Dusty, the young and pregnant mistress of the president, Hough has learned well from the seasoned sisterhood in the cast. She made a special connection with Williams, who plays the first lady. \u201cOur first conversation was about the fact that she was a ballroom dancer, and that was how I started my career,\u201d she says. \u201cI remember watching her in \u2018Dance With Me\u2019 and being like, \u2018Wow \u2014 this woman can dance, be an actor and sing.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

A triple threat herself, Hough\u2019s skills have been honed in \u201cPOTUS.\u201d \u201cThe great thing about being a part of an ensemble with this kind of cast is, I don\u2019t have to carry the show and feel the pressure or feel the responsibility,\u201d she says. \u201cI get to be a student and soak in all of the incredible information and the teachings that I feel like they\u2019re sharing, not even realizing that they\u2019re giving me so much.\u201d<\/p>\n

Even among all of that formidable female talent, Hough gets to have perhaps the biggest moment of the show, when her character asserts: \u201cAffordable, safe reproductive health care is a basic human right.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

It\u2019s a hot topic in a time when abortion rights are once again being challenged. \u201cEven before this specific time that has been happening with Roe v. Wade, it was such a powerful line because the rights to privacy and autonomy over our bodies is so important,\u201d she says. \u201cThe fact that that\u2019s being threatened again, it\u2019s just outrageous \u2026 When I said that line for the first time, that was definitely Dusty, but [with] a little bit of Julianne in there for sure. And we all kind of looked at each other, and we weren\u2019t sure if we were gonna start crying onstage because it was so powerful to hear everybody in the audience. We got a standing ovation.\u201d<\/p>\n

But Hough is careful to note that the cathartic powers of performing \u201cPOTUS\u201d \u2014 both the consciousness and the comedy \u2014 have nothing to do with her divorce. In fact, she insists that there\u2019s no bitterness in this breakup.<\/p>\n

\u201cBrooks and I \u2014 we had the most beautiful separation,\u201d she says. \u201cWe went through things amicably. It was filled with love and respect. Like any relationship that means something, you learn and you grow, and you are blessed with those riches that you experienced from that relationship. We both feel beyond blessed for having each other in our lives during that period of life. I want nothing but the best for him. I wish him the best, whatever that looks like. And so that\u2019s truly where we are.\u201d<\/p>\n

In 2019, Hough revealed that she told Laich \u201cI\u2019m not straight\u201d in an interview with Women\u2019s Health magazine. Looking back, she says that it wasn\u2019t meant to be her big coming-out statement \u2014 even if it turned out that way.<\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t really have a moment of like, \u2018I\u2019m going to do this.\u2019 It just was<\/em>,\u201d says Hough, who now identifies as queer. \u201cI just said something and, you know, it just came out that way. And it is my truth. Everybody\u2019s [coming-out] experience is different, and I come from a Mormon background where \u2026 it hasn\u2019t been looked at as something that\u2019s accepted. But I know that my parents, my friends, my family love me, and they\u2019re proud of me. And so I had a really good experience.\u201d<\/p>\n

Hough\u2019s family support system has always included her big brother, Derek Hough, who joined \u201cDancing With the Stars\u201d the season after she did. \u201cSomehow we\u2019re never on the same cycle,\u201d she says. \u201cSo when he\u2019s having a low moment, I\u2019m having a high moment; and when I\u2019m having a low moment, he\u2019s having a high moment. We can help each other and balance each other out.\u201d<\/p>\n

Hough is ready to leap into the new Manhattan life she manifested. <\/p>\n

These days, Hough\u2019s own balancing act includes her fitness business Kinrgy and the Fresh Vine Wine company she co-owns with actress Nina Dobrev. She\u2019s also co-hosting \u201cThe Tony Awards: Act One\u201d with good friend Darren Criss before the main event on June 12. But she\u2019s hoping to make time in her busy schedule for some trips out to the Hamptons and picnics in Central Park this summer.<\/p>\n

And she\u2019s open to hitting the dating scene again, too. \u201cI am leading with love,\u201d she says. \u201cI\u2019m trying to keep my heart open, but I just want to be the best version of myself. I really do believe in energy and \u2026 if I am in the best place for me, I\u2019m going to attract people that are right for me in this moment.\u201d<\/p>\n
