Howard Stern is less than thrilled about his possible summer job. <\/p>\n

The controversial radio personality may have let slip that he could be joining the Marvel Cinematic Universe in a \u201cDoctor Doom\u201d movie.<\/p>\n

During the commercials of his Sirius XM show on Thursday, the 68-year-old left his microphone on while he talked with producer Gary Dell\u2019Abate about possible guests to book for the show.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s difficult to hear with ads playing over the conversation, but Stern tells the producer that he won\u2019t be able to talk much in the next few months \u2014 prompting co-host Robin Quivers to inquire about his summer plans. <\/p>\n

\u201cWell, I\u2019m gonna do \u2018Doctor Doom,’\u201d Stern said in audio recorded by TMZ. \u201cBut believe me, I\u2019m f\u2013lng miserable about it.\u201d <\/p>\n

Stern continued to share that he reached out to MCU vet and \u201cIron Man\u201d himself Robert Downey Jr.\u00a0for acting tips and then asked if Dell\u2019Abate could give him director Jon Favreau\u2019s contact information. <\/p>\n

The apparent blunder comes as the 68-year-old shock jock recently said that he wants to run for president in 2024.GC Images <\/p>\n

Marvel has not announced any upcoming project surrounding \u201cDoctor Doom,\u201d a scientist who can manipulate energy, among other abilities. <\/p>\n

The Post has reached out to reps for Marvel and Stern for comment. <\/p>\n

Maybe Stern is less than enthused about the mystery project because he is more focused on his 2024 presidential campaign. After the Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade, Stern said Monday on his show that he wants to run for president to \u201coverturn all this bulls\u2013t.\u201d<\/p>\n

Stern explained that he wants to have more immediate action to stabilize the Supreme Court.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe other thing is, if I do run for president, and I\u2019m not f \u2013king around, I\u2019m really thinking about it, because the only other thing I\u2019m going to do is appoint five more Supreme Court justices,\u201d he continued.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m not afraid to do it. As soon as I become president, you\u2019re gonna get five new Supreme Court justices that are going to overturn all this bulls\u2013t,\u201d the \u201cHoward Stern Show\u201d host pointed out.<\/p>\n

The comedian also revealed that he would only consider running for president in 2024 if ex-prez Donald Trump was the Republican nominee. <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019ll beat his ass,\u201d he affirmed.<\/p>\n

He even reportedly has a running mate already, too: Bradley Cooper. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis guy is gonna bring in the female vote like you wouldn\u2019t believe,\u201d Stern told Quivers on air on Wednesday.<\/p>\n

Stern dipped into the political pool in 1994 when he ran for New York governor as a libertarian. However, he dropped out due to being required to reveal his personal finances.<\/p>\n
