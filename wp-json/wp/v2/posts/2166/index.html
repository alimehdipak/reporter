Republican lawmakers lauded outspoken tech entrepreneur Elon Musk\u2019s $44 billion Twitter takeover Monday \u2014 celebrating the buyout as a win for free speech and democracy.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter openly censors speech based on political ideologies and makes a mockery of our Constitutional liberties,\u201d Texas Sen. Ted Cruz tweeted Monday morning.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf Elon Musk can turn that around to actually protect free speech and encourage open discourse, Twitter\u2019s full potential will be unlocked.\u201d<\/p>\n

The governor of the Lone Star State, Greg Abbott, also championed the sale \u2014 urging Musk to relocate the social media company to Texas.<\/p>\n

\u201c@elonmusk. Bring Twitter to Texas to join Tesla, SpaceX & the Boring company,\u201d he tweeted.<\/p>\n

\n

\u2014 Greg Abbott (@GregAbbott_TX) April 25, 2022<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

Washington Rep. Cathy McMorris Rodgers, a ranking member on the House Energy and Commerce Committee, heralded the news as a much-needed breakup of Big Tech.<\/p>\n

\u201cBig Tech cannot continue to silence people\u2014they are not and should not be the arbiters of truth. It is time to replace Big Tech censorship with the battle of ideas and I am hopeful for a new free speech direction at @Twitter,\u201d she tweeted.<\/p>\n

Tennesse Sen. Marsha Blackburn also hailed the Tesla CEO\u2019s takeover as a win for the fight against censorship.<\/p>\n

\u201cToday is an encouraging day for freedom of speech. I am hopeful that Elon Musk will help rein in Big Tech\u2019s history of censoring users that have a different viewpoint,\u201d she wrote.<\/p>\n

She added that the purchase should \u201cterrify\u201d progressives.<\/p>\n

\u201cElon Musk buying Twitter terrifies the left because they don\u2019t want their power to censor conservatives threatened,\u201d she tweeted.<\/p>\n

Ohio Rep. Jim Jordan simply put: \u201cFree speech is making a comeback.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk pictured at a \u201cCyber Rodeo\u201d at Tesla Giga Texas on April 7.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

However, the announcement wasn\u2019t enough to bring former President Donald Trump back to the social media site, with the ex-POTUS insisting last week that he won\u2019t return to Twitter \u2013 even if Musk\u00a0lifts the site\u2019s\u00a0permanent ban\u00a0against him.<\/p>\n

Trump told\u00a0Fox News\u00a0that he planned to instead use his own fledgling Truth Social app, which launched in February \u2014 insisting his platform is \u201cmuch better\u201d than Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am not going on Twitter, I am going to stay on Truth,\u201d Trump said. \u201cI hope Elon buys Twitter because he\u2019ll make improvements to it and he is a good man, but I am going to be staying on Truth.\u201d<\/p>\n

Despite Musk urging his \u201cworst critics\u201d to stay on the platform, lawmakers on the left are vowing to re-think their Twitter presence.<\/p>\n

Massachusetts Democrat Sen. Elizabeth Warren didn\u2019t hold back, writing: \u201cThis deal is dangerous for our democracy. Billionaires like Elon Musk play by a different set of rules than everyone else, accumulating power for their own gain. We need a wealth tax and strong rules to hold Big Tech accountable.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, Oregon Sen. Ron Wyden tweeted, \u201cIf the U.S. had a privacy law with teeth, or if Twitter encrypted DMs like I urged years ago, Americans wouldn\u2019t be left wondering what today\u2019s sale means for their private information. The protection of Americans\u2019 privacy must be a condition of any sale.\u201d<\/p>\n

As a result of the sale, Twitter will now be a privately owned company.<\/p>\n

Musk, who has said he wants to buy Twitter in order to restore the platform\u2019s adherence to free speech principles, will pay $54.20 per share for the company, which he has vowed to take private.<\/p>\n

\u201cFree speech is the bedrock of a functioning democracy, and Twitter is the digital town square where matters vital to the future of humanity are debated,\u201d Musk\u00a0said in a statement.<\/p>\n

\u201cI also want to make Twitter better than ever by enhancing the product with new features, making the algorithms open source to increase trust, defeating the spam bots, and authenticating all humans.
