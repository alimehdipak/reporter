Since the last update to the ESPN divisional rankings, Israel Adesanya and Alexander Volkanovski solidified their positions with victories at UFC 276. Both both fighters already were No. 1 in their respective top 10s, though, so there was nowhere for them to rise.<\/p>\n

Then there’s Alex Pereira. He also fought on the same July 2 card in Las Vegas. It was just the seventh fight of his MMA career, and he made the most of it, knocking out top-10 middleweight Sean Strickland in less than three minutes. As a result, Pereira has found himself in the ESPN rankings.<\/p>\n

Pereira is relatively new to MMA, but he’s no combat sports novice. The 34-year-old Brazilian is a former kickboxing world champion who reigned at both middleweight and light heavyweight in the Glory promotion. He has 40 kickboxing bouts on his resume, including two victories over Adesanya prior to “The Last Stylebender” switching to MMA and becoming a UFC champion. One of Pereira’s wins over Adesanya was by knockout.<\/p>\n

Now that Pereira is a top-10 MMA fighter, he appears headed toward another matchup with Adesanya, this time in 4-ounce gloves.<\/p>\n

Pereira comes in at No. 7 among middleweights, and right behind him at No. 8 is another rankings newcomer, Bellator champion Johnny Eblen, who dethroned Gegard Mousasi last month.<\/p>\n
