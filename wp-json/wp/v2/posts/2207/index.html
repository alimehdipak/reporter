Taylor Swift superfans are bugging New York University\u2019s class of 2022 for tickets to their upcoming graduation \u2014 where the \u201c22\u201d singer is set to deliver a commencement speech.<\/p>\n

So-called Swifties have been flooding the social media accounts of NYU students with offers of up to $500 to witness the Grammy-winning artist receive an honorary degree at Yankee Stadium on May 18.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019ve gotten long messages like \u2018I hope this isn\u2019t crazy. I would love to come,\u201d student Sean Nesmith told The Post.<\/p>\n

The senior \u2014 who said he\u2019s gotten bids as high as $500 \u2014 described the fans as both \u201cinvasive and something wacky to laugh about amongst other NYU students.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cSome messages are to the point, like \u2018Are you giving your tickets away?’\u201d he explained. \u201cA girl said she would be my \u2018biggest fan\u2019 with a megaphone and cheering me on during the ceremony if I gave her a ticket.\u201d<\/p>\n

Reddit users have also asked if NYU seniors are in the market to sell their tickets, though several of the posts have since been scrubbed from the site.<\/p>\n

Some students fear disciplinary action based on the school\u2019s ticketing policy, which forbids graduates to auction off their tickets. Students who disobey the policy can face delays in receiving their degrees and diplomas.<\/p>\n

Many students fear the university\u2019s strict policy when it comes to selling graduation tickets. Wang Ying\/Xinhua via ZUMA Press <\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t want to risk losing tickets or facing repercussions from the university if they were to find out,\u201d Nesmith said. \u201cIt\u2019s not worth it.\u201d <\/p>\n

But, \u201cHow iconic would it be if Taylor sang \u201922\u2019?\u201d he joked.<\/p>\n

While the \u201cYou Need to Calm Down\u201d singer is only speaking to the class of \u201922, students who graduated in \u201920 and \u201921 \u2014 but whose ceremonies are also taking place next month \u2014 have been getting offers, too.<\/p>\n

William Ferrante told The Post he was contacted by a Swift fan trying to get a ticket to the graduation.Twitter <\/p>\n

William Ferrante, a 2021 alum of the NYU College of Dentistry, also received an offer to purchase a ticket \u2014 even though his ceremony does not include Swift.<\/p>\n

Ferrante told The Post he was contacted by a fan after he jokingly tweeted \u201cSelling my belated 2021 NYU commencement tickets who wants to see taylor swift!?\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe topic of actually how much they\u2019d be worth wasn\u2019t brought up during the conversation,\u201d he said. \u201cBut if I was able to get tickets to her ceremony I don\u2019t think I\u2019d really charge an exorbitant amount.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cSwifties\u201d have started harassing NYU grads for their graduation tickets so they can see Taylor Swift get her doctorate degree. David McGlynn <\/p>\n

Senior Caitlin Hornick had a fan reach out to her for a ticket in recent days, according to the blog The Click, which first reported on the phenomenon.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s not like a Taylor Swift concert. It\u2019s a graduation ceremony,\u201d she told the blog.<\/p>\n

\u201cSo the fact that people are willing to pay absurd prices just to get into Yankee Stadium to get a glimpse of Taylor Swift to get this honorary doctorate is just absurd to me.\u201d<\/p>\n

Swifties have been flooding the social media accounts of NYU students with offers of up to $500.Kevin Mazur\/Getty Images for The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame <\/p>\n

\u201cI wanna celebrate my peers in my program who went to grad school during a pandemic they deserve a ceremony,\u201d she continued. \u201cNot a ceremony that has bells and whistles because someone has a song called 22.\u201d<\/p>\n

The school\u2019s commencement website updated their policy on the prohibition of ticket sales a week after Swift fans began hassling students for tickers to see the folk artist on stage in the Bronx.<\/p>\n

\u201cNYU strictly prohibits the direct or indirect sale or auction of All-University Commencement tickets,\u201d reads the site. \u201cTickets are free and available only to eligible candidates in the Classes of 2020, 2021, and 2022 and their guests.\u201d<\/p>\n
