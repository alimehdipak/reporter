Julee Cruise, a singer best known for her work on David Lynch\u2019s iconic series \u201cTwin Peaks,\u201d has died at 65. <\/p>\n

Cruise\u2019s husband Edward Grinnan confirmed the icon\u2019s death in a touching Facebook tribute.<\/p>\n

\u201cShe left this realm on her own terms,\u201d the vocalist\u2019s bereaved soul mate wrote. \u201cNo regrets. She is at peace \u2026 I played her [B-52\u2019s song] \u2018Roam\u2019 during her transition. Now she will roam forever. Rest in peace, my love.\u201d<\/p>\n

Cruise\u2019s cause of death was not given, the Guardian reported. However, in 2018, the singer announced on Facebook that she was battling systemic lupus, which left her in chronic pain.<\/p>\n

\u201cI can hardly walk,\u201d she wrote in the heartbreaking post. \u201cAnd now it\u2019s difficult to stand. My spine is crumbling and pinching on nerves.\u201d<\/p>\n

Cruise\u2019s husband confirmed the icon\u2019s death in a touching Facebook tribute.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t take opiates, but the pain is so bad I cry and snap at people,\u201d the singer added. \u201cI\u2019ve had a glorious time\u2026 but I must leave here. Someone wise told me I must go, and so being a recluse, it\u2019s law and boredom for me. Thanks so much for everything\u2026 That\u2019s my final curtsy.\u201d<\/p>\n

Born Dec. 1, 1956, in Creston, Iowa, the songwriter is perhaps best known for her musical collaborations with director David Lynch. The auteur notably used the instrumental version of her 1989 hit \u201cFalling,\u201d written by Angelo Badalamenti, as the theme song to his iconic TV series \u201cTwin Peaks.\u201d<\/p>\n

Cruise announced in 2018 that she had Lupus.Andrew Toth <\/p>\n

Along with her musical contributions, Cruise also played the part of a singer in both the ABC mystery show and its 1992 movie spin-off \u201cTwin Peaks: Fire Walk With Me.\u201d She would reprise the part in the 2017 Showtime revival\u00a0\u201cTwin Peaks: The Return.\u201d<\/p>\n

Cruise also collaborated with Lynch on the 1986 crime drama \u201cBlue Velvet,\u201d which features her Badalamenti collaboration \u201cMysteries of Love.\u201d<\/p>\n

The singer-actress had previously described her working relationship with Lynch in 2018, the Guardian reported. \u201cIt\u2019s like I\u2019m his little sister: You don\u2019t like your older brother telling you what to do,\u201d she said. \u201cDavid\u2019s foppish. He can have these tantrums sometimes.\u201d <\/p>\n

She continued, \u201cAnd have you ever seen his temper? Anybody can look funny when they get mad. But I love him.\u201d<\/p>\n

Cruise performs during the sixth annual Twin Peaks UK Festival in 2015. Redferns <\/p>\n

Aside from her collaborations with the \u201cTwin Peaks\u201d visionary, Cruise is also known in the music sphere for touring as a member of the B-52s throughout the 1990s. She also released several albums, including \u201cThe Voice of Love\u201d in 1993 \u2014 which featured three songs from \u201cTwin Peaks: Fire Walk With Me\u201d \u2014 as well as \u201cThe Art of Being a Girl\u201d in 2002 and \u201cMy Secret Life\u201d in 2011. And, in yet another epic director-songstress collaboration, Cruise covered Elvis Presley\u2019s \u201cSummer Kisses, Winter Tears\u201d for the soundtrack to Wim Wenders\u2019 \u201cUntil the End of the World.\u201d<\/p>\n
