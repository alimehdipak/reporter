Some still like it hot \u2014 but this look is not good for \u201cMarilyn\u2019s legacy\u201d on the 60th anniversary of her death, according to self-proclaimed protectors of her eternal flame.<\/p>\n

While many pondered, \u201cHow do you even go about\u00a0acquiring a dress Marilyn Monroe wore?\u201d \u2014 some Monroe experts were not happy when Kim Kardashian ascended the steps Monday at the Met Gala while wearing\u00a0the Hollywood legend\u2019s most iconic dress.<\/p>\n

The reality star and shape wear maven, 41, borrowed the sparkly beige gown the goddess wore in 1962 to sing \u201cHappy Birthday\u201d to President John F. Kennedy\u00a0from Orlando\u2019s Ripley\u2019s Believe It or Not! Museum, which previously paid $5 million at auction to get ahold of the famous frock.<\/p>\n

Kardashian even forced herself to lose weight to fit into the curvaceous lewk<\/em> and later changed into a replica of it to keep on partying the night away. <\/p>\n

However, while the lawyer-in-training \u2014 alongside boyfriend and \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d star Pete Davidson \u2014 dazzled partygoers at the Metropolitan Museum of Art last night, many people were taken aback by her unearthing of the 60-year-old dress.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am extremely disappointed to see this 60-year-old, iconic dress being worn in public,\u201d Michelle Morgan, author of the 2018 book \u201cThe Girl: Marilyn Monroe, the Seven Year Itch, and the Birth of an Unlikely Feminist,\u201d told The Post. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe gown is extremely fragile and has been previously kept in temperature-controlled conditions, but now here it is, not only being worn, but also in a red carpet environment, where anything could have happened to it,\u201d she continued.<\/p>\n

Monroe\u2019s gown was purchased by Ripley\u2019s Believe It or Not! Museum in Florida at a Julien\u2019s Auctions event in 2016 for $5 million. AFP via Getty Images One critic of the move was concerned that \u201csweat, perfume and makeup\u201d could have possibly gotten onto the dress and caused \u201cuntold damage and staining.\u201dJustin Lane\/EPA \u201cI am extremely disappointed to see this 60-year-old, iconic dress being worn in public,\u201d said author Michelle Morgan of Kardashian\u2019s reveal.Bettmann Archive\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

Morgan noted that \u201csweat, perfume and makeup\u201d could have possibly gotten onto the dress and caused \u201cuntold damage and staining.\u201d <\/p>\n

However, Kardashian reportedly went body-makeup free to the event. In addition, a representative for Ripley\u2019s told The Post that she showed \u201cthe utmost respect\u201d for the garment.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis was no easy decision for Ripley\u2019s, however, Kim Kardashian has continued to show the utmost respect for this opportunity and historic garment,\u201d the spokesperson said in a statement. \u201cFrom extensive research to following guidelines such as no body makeup, only wearing the dress for the short red-carpet appearance, and making absolutely no alterations, she has become a steward \u2014 and added to \u2014 its history.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ripley\u2019s additionally told The Post that \u201cno damage\u201d had, in fact, occurred to the dress.<\/p>\n

Pete Davidson and Kim Kardashian arrive at the Met Gala on Monday night.Andrew Kelly\/REUTERS \t \t <\/p>\n

Nina Boski, producer of the \u201cMarilyn: Behind the Icon\u201d TV docuseries and podcast, echoed Morgan\u2019s stance, telling The Post that the viral Kardashian moment is \u201cnot a positive remembrance of the star.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI can understand why Kim would want to wear the dress for publicity purposes; however, the dress should have only been worn by one person, and that is the iconic Marilyn Monroe,\u201d Boski said.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis doesn\u2019t help Marilyn\u2019s legacy, especially on the 60th anniversary of her death,\u201d she added of the \u201cSome Like It Hot\u201d star, who died in August 1962 at the age of 36.<\/p>\n

Scott Fortner is a Marilyn Monroe historian and collector and also gave his take on the Skims owner\u2019s Met Gala attire.<\/p>\n

\u201cKim did break the internet [with her look] and it was an opportunity for her and Ripley\u2019s,\u201d Fortner told The Post. \u201c[But] the fans are outraged because it is one of those things in the Marilyn community that is considered sacred. The dress is the quintessential Marilyn Monroe item and it was worn one time by her.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cThe Seven Year Itch\u201d actress wore the infamous frock in 1962 for President John F. Kennedy\u2019s birthday.Michael Quan\/ZUMA <\/p>\n

Fortner firmly stated that the wearing of the frock \u2014 a gown that he labeled \u201ca national treasure\u201d \u2014 should have remained a once-and-done thing by Monroe herself.<\/p>\n

\u201cNo one should have reworn this dress,\u201d Fortner said. \u201cIt\u2019s hard because [Kardashian] is one of the most influential people in the world.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf it was up to me, I would not have allowed it simply because of the importance of the piece in American cultural history. But let me be clear, this is nothing to do with Kim Kardashian,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe [Met Gala] moment has raised a level of awareness about Marilyn for a new generation,\u201d Fortner stated. \u201cThe dress has its own importance. [Monroe] is a modern-day Cleopatra and she\u2019ll have a cultural presence forever.\u201d<\/p>\n

In its statement to The Post, the Ripley\u2019s organization also weighed in on precautions that were taken to protect the dress.<\/p>\n

Kardashian wowed onlookers at the 2022 Met Gala with her Marilyn Monroe look.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cRipley\u2019s Believe It or Not! is a privately-owned attractions company with the goal of inspiring and entertaining guests of all ages in unconventional ways. We take great pride in owning the \u2018Happy Birthday\u2019 dress and consulted with multiple textile experts and conservators on this endeavor,\u201d Ripley\u2019s added. \u201cWhile none were adamantly for or against Kim wearing the gown, their input helped us craft a set of precautions and procedures for the Met Gala. This caution proved to be sound, as no damage occurred at last night\u2019s event.\u201d<\/p>\n

The spokesperson noted that the dress will be returned on Wednesday, May 4, to \u201cthe Ripley\u2019s Vault,\u00a0a dark, temperature (68 degrees) and humidity (40 \u2013 50%) controlled room, where it remains in its case, mounted\u00a0on a form covered in acid free cotton muslin. The form helps prevent folds and stress on the dress, while the muslin protects it from light, humidity, and environmental contaminants.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Post also has reached out for comment from representatives for Kardashian. <\/p>\n
