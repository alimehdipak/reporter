Radio personality Tom Joyner, who hosted the nationally syndicated \u201cThe Tom Joyner Morning Show\u201d for 25 years, is selling his Miami mansion for $20 million, The Post can report. <\/p>\n

Located in the gated Golden Beach community, Joyner, 72, initially purchased the three-bedroom, four-bathroom home back in 2015 for $10.5 million. But it\u2019s not exactly the profit you would think if he is able to score his initial asking price. <\/p>\n

Spanning over 7,300 square feet, the space underwent a whopping $7.5 million renovation with an elite team including designer Deborah Wecselman of Wecselman Design, architect Wesley Kean of KoDA, contractor Larqcon Group, and landscape architecture firm Enea.<\/p>\n

Inside, the staircase within the foyer acts as an art gallery with pieces from Picasso, Robert Pruitt, Allora & Calzadilla, and Ernie Barnes. Hints of Joyner\u2019s favorite color red are sprinkled throughout each piece of work while a custom Bocci pendant light hangs from the ceiling.<\/p>\n

Pegged as a \u201cunicorn property,\u201d the primary suite comes with an office, and features a bathroom with a glass encased RiFRA tub in the center of the room, and an expansive open closet. <\/p>\n

The lower level features a professional gym with a boxing ring and bright red floor. <\/p>\n

Described as a \u201csophisticated luxury\u201d residence, oceanfront living lounges are placed throughout the home with ocean views in almost every room. <\/p>\n

A seaside swimming pool can be found out back with spacious verandas, summer kitchen, beachside louvered cabana \u2013 and of course, direct access to your very own private beach.<\/p>\n

Golden Beach is the only private community with homes on the sand in South Florida. The ultra-exclusive gated community has its own police force, private beach with pavilion and beachside service, tennis courts, basketball court, multiple parks and a kids playground.<\/p>\n

Dina Goldentayer with Douglas Elliman holds the listing. <\/p>\n
