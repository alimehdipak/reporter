Supporters of Donald Trump are urging Elon Musk to reinstate the ex-president\u2019s Twitter account after Tesla\u2019s billionaire boss became the social network\u2019s biggest shareholder and got a seat on Twitter\u2019s board.<\/p>\n

Shares of Twitter rose by more than 27% in pre-market trading on Tuesday \u2014 the second day in a row that the company has seen its stock price surge in the wake of Musk\u2019s acquisition of 73.5 million shares.<\/p>\n

Twitter CEO Parag Agrawal also announced on Tuesday that Musk was joining Twitter\u2019s board of directors.<\/p>\n

\u201cLooking forward to working with Parag & Twitter board to make significant improvements to Twitter in coming months!\u201d Musk tweeted in response to Agrawal\u2019s announcement.<\/p>\n

Trump was banned from Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, and all other large social media platforms following the Jan. 6, 2021, rioting at the US Capitol. Trump has since launched a social media platform of his own, Truth Social, but it has been beset by technical glitches and departures of key executives.<\/p>\n

Supporters of Donald Trump want Elon Musk to reinstate the former president\u2019s Twitter account.Anadolu Agency via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Now pro-Trump voices are calling on Musk to burnish his pro-free speech credentials and to bring back the former president.<\/p>\n

\u201cNow that @ElonMusk is Twitter\u2019s largest shareholder, it\u2019s time to lift the political censorship,\u201d Rep. Lauren Boebert (R-Colo.), tweeted on Monday. <\/p>\n

\u201cOh\u2026 and BRING BACK TRUMP!\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter used to be an open field of free thought,\u201d Pete Hegseth said on \u201cFox & Friends\u201d on Tuesday. \u201cNow the blue checkmarks like groupthink leftists police that thought and the corporate types at Twitter have been happy to enforce it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Hegseth, whose comments were cited by Mediaite, added: \u201cThey pushed off Donald Trump. They pushed off a lot of conservatives. If he were to open that up, it opens up the conversation in America.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rep. Lauren Boebert demanded that Musk and Twitter reinstate Trump\u2019s account. <\/p>\n

Journalist Glenn Greenwald, meanwhile, mocked CNN\u2019s Brian Stelter, who said there\u2019s a \u201cfear\u201d among liberals over Musk becoming Twitter\u2019s largest shareholder.<\/p>\n

\u201cDems & liberal activists groups have spent years co-opting the censorship power of tech billionaires and expressing gratitude to Google and FB execs for censoring their enemies off the internet,\u201d Greenwald tweeted. <\/p>\n

\u201cNow they\u2019re petrified a billionaire who won\u2019t censor for them may take over Twitter.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk also could reinstate the account of right-leaning parody site The Babylon Bee, which was suspended from Twitter last month after it referred to a transgender Biden administration official, Rachel Levine, as \u201cman of the year.\u201d<\/p>\n

Seth Dillon, the CEO of Babylon Bee, said Musk spoke to him by phone shortly after the suspension and mused that he may need to buy Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cMusk reached out to us before he polled his followers about Twitter\u2019s commitment to free speech,\u201d Dillon wrote on his Twitter account.\u00a0\u201cHe wanted to confirm that we had, in fact, been suspended. He even mused on that call that he might need to buy Twitter.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trump was barred from Twitter and other social media platforms after the Jan. 6, 2021, riots at the US Capitol.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Tech giants like Twitter, Facebook, Google and Amazon have been accused by conservatives and free speech advocates of banning users with unpopular views.<\/p>\n

Then-Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey said last year that his company erred when it\u00a0censored a New York Post article about Hunter Biden\u2019s laptop\u00a0in the weeks leading up to the presidential election.<\/p>\n

The Tesla boss has criticized social media companies, including Twitter, for de-platforming those with views considered outside the mainstream.<\/p>\n

The former president launched Truth Social to compete with Twitter, but it has struggled to get off the ground.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Just days prior to the SEC filing revealing Musk\u2019s stake, he raised questions about Twitter\u2019s adherence to the principles of free speech.<\/p>\n

\u201cFree speech is essential to a functioning democracy. Do you believe Twitter rigorously adheres to this principle?\u201d he tweeted.<\/p>\n

The next day, Musk took it a step further, writing: \u201cGiven that Twitter serves as the de facto public town square, failing to adhere to free speech principles fundamentally undermines democracy. What should be done?\u201d<\/p>\n

In a\u00a0separate tweet, Musk said he was \u201cgiving serious thought\u201d to creating a new social media platform.<\/p>\n

Before buying a stake in Twitter, Musk was critical of the social media platform\u2019s policies as they related to banning users.Anadolu Agency via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Musk teased Twitter users on Monday evening by asking them if they wanted an edit button for tweets \u2014 this just hours after it was learned that the world\u2019s richest person became the company\u2019s largest shareholder.<\/p>\n

\u201cDo you want an edit button?\u201d Musk tweeted to his more than 80 million followers on the micro-blogging platform. The poll generated more than 2.74 million votes as of Tuesday morning.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s $2.89 billion investment in Twitter makes him the largest shareholder, catapulting him ahead of Vanguard, Morgan Stanley, and BlackRock.<\/p>\n

Due to Twitter\u2019s share structure, Musk\u2019s stake in the company will give him far more influence over the site\u2019s operations than can be exerted by outside investors in tech giants like Meta or Google.\u00a0<\/p>\n

While Mark Zuckerberg holds special dual-class shares that effectively allow him to override Meta investors\u2019 objections and do whatever he wants,\u00a0Twitter only has one class of shares. <\/p>\n

That means Twitter shares held by founder Dorsey give Dorsey the same voting power as shares held by Musk.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In a demonstration of the power that can be wielded by Twitter investors,\u00a0Dorsey\u00a0stepped down as Twitter\u2019s CEO\u00a0in 2021 following a heated activist campaign from shareholder Elliott Management.
