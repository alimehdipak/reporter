Norm Macdonald\u2019s final stand-up special, filmed before the comedian\u2019s death in September, has made its way to Netflix with fellow funny people paying tribute to the late icon.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are honored to bring you Norm Macdonald\u2019s last special, followed by reactions and commentary from a few of his special friends,\u201d Netflix wrote in the opening credits for \u201cNorm Macdonald: Nothing Special,\u201d which was released Monday and includes appearances from David Letterman, Dave Chappelle, Molly Shannon, Conan O\u2019Brien, Adam Sandler and David Spade.<\/p>\n

The special shows Macdonald, who died in 2021 after a private nine-year battle with leukemia, wearing a baseball cap and headphones and talking into a mike, pandemic Zoom style, in a nondescript room where dogs can be heard barking.<\/p>\n

Norm Macdonald\u2019s final stand-up special dropped on Netflix on Memorial Day.Netflix <\/p>\n

\u201cNorm was working hard preparing material for his Netflix special \u2014 until COVID shut things down,\u201d another title card read. \u201cIn the summer of 2020, he was scheduled to undergo a procedure and as he put it, \u2018didn\u2019t want to leave anything on the table in case things went south.\u2019 \u201c<\/p>\n

Norm Macdonald \u201cdidn\u2019t want to leave anything on the table in case things went south,\u201d Netflix said.NBCU Photo Bank\/NBCUniversal via <\/p>\n

The special concludes with Letterman, Chappelle, Shannon, O\u2019Brien, Sandler and Spade gathered together to watch \u201cNothing Special\u201d and honor their longtime pal.<\/p>\n

Netflix said Macdonald \u201cshot this in one take\u201d while \u201cat home, the night before going in.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cHey everybody, it\u2019s Norm Macdonald and this is my comedy special,\u201d he began. \u201cOf course, I\u2019m looking forward to getting back out there, seeing you folks in person. I love doing gigs and I miss it. My God, I miss it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Norm Macdonald secretly recorded a run-through of a planned stand-up special shortly before his death.Getty Images <\/p>\n

However, the \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d alum succumbed to leukemia, which he hid from the public.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe want to make sure his fans see this very funny hour. He left this gift for all of us,\u201d his longtime producing partner Lori Jo Hoekstra said in a statement to the Daily Beast.<\/p>\n

Hoekstra said, \u201cNorm worked so hard on a new hour of material and wanted it to be seen.\u201d<\/p>\n

Norm Macdonald on the \u201cWeekend Update\u201d desk on \u201cSNL.\u201dNBC <\/p>\n

\u201cWhile this version of\u00a0\u2018Nothing Special\u2019\u00a0was not originally meant to be the final product, COVID restrictions prevented him from filming in front of an audience,\u201d Hoekstra noted.<\/p>\n

Topics in the special are far-ranging, but at several points, the \u201cScrewed\u201d star talked openly about his own death and mortality in general. He spoke about how \u201cdepressing\u201d it was to write his \u201cliving will\u201d and joked that his family would pull the plug if he ever hypothetically wound up in a coma.<\/p>\n

He also joked about being a Christian and his fears he \u201cpicked the wrong religion,\u201d Daily Beast reported, and imagined the afterlife. \u201cAhh, it\u2019s you! I thought it was the other fella. I should have been slaying apostates the entire time. Ah well, what are you gonna do?\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cNothing Special\u201d also includes commentary from his friends, with Letterman calling it a \u201cgreat gift.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s not strictly stand-up, it\u2019s something else,\u201d the 75-year-old former \u201cLate Show\u201d host added.<\/p>\n
