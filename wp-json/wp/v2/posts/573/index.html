He was the King of the Monster Movies.<\/p>\n

Akira Takarada, a Japanese actor and voiceover artist best known for appearing in the original 1954 \u201cGodzilla,\u201d died Monday at age 87. His passing was confirmed by Toho studios, which produced the original monster flick.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are saddened to hear of the passing of Akira Takarada,\u201d they wrote in a Thursday tweet. \u201cMay his memory continue to inspire the lives of many Godzilla fans.\u201d<\/p>\n

Toho didn\u2019t disclose a cause of death, but Takarada had been suffering debilitating lower back pain and had been in and out of the hospital in the past few years, the Anime News Network reported. On March 10, Takarada had appeared in a wheelchair at a fan greeting event for his movie \u201cYononaka ni Taete Sakura no Nakariseba\u201d (\u201cEndure the World and Lycee in the Cherry Blossoms\u201d).<\/p>\n

Takarada is most famous for playing sailor Hideto Ogata in the original 1954 film \u201cGodzilla.\u201dCourtesy Everett Collection Takarada\u2019s cause of death is yet unknown.WireImage Takarada (left) in a scene from \u201cGodzilla\u201d (1954).Courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

Born on April 29, 1934, Takarada is most famous for playing sailor Hideto Ogata in the original 1954 film \u201cGodzilla.\u201d His character liaised with the Japanese Coast Guard after the first ship that Godzilla destroyed issued a distress signal, and witnessed the monster\u2019s death from the Oxygen Destroyer at the end of the film. <\/p>\n

The thespian also appeared in the American re-cut \u201cGodzilla, King of the Monsters\u201d (1956) starring Raymond Burr.<\/p>\n

From there, Takarada went on to star in a slew of flicks featuring the King of the Monsters, including \u201cMothra vs Godzilla\u201d (1964) and \u201cGodzilla vs Mothra\u201d (1992).\u00a0The actor\u2019s last appearance in a Godzilla flick was in \u201cGodzilla: Final Wars\u201d (2004), although he filmed scenes for the 2014 US reboot \u201cGodzilla,\u201d which unfortunately didn\u2019t make the final cut. However, he is still featured in the movie\u2019s credits.<\/p>\n

Along with his work on the Godzilla franchise, Takarada appeared in other atomic age monster mashes from \u201cInvasion of the Astro-Monster\u201d (1965) to \u201cEbirah, Horror of the Deep\u201d (1966) and Toho\u2019s \u201cKing Kong Escapes\u201d (1967).<\/p>\n

The actor\u2019s star presence wasn\u2019t limited to in front of the camera. A prolific voiceover artist, Takarada did the Japanese dub for \u201cDoctor Doolittle\u201d (1967), in which he lent his voice to the titular character, as well as Disney animated flicks, \u201cThe Great Mouse Detective\u201d (1986) and \u201cAladdin\u201d (1992), voicing baddies Ratigan and Jafar, respectively. He reprised his latter voiceover role for the \u201cKingdom Hearts\u201d\u00a0video games.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cGodzilla\u201d fans offered their condolences to the monster movie giant.<\/p>\n

\u201cThank you, Akira Takarada, for loving Godzilla so much,\u201d wrote one crestfallen fan on Twitter. \u201cYour enthusiasm, love, and support for the series has meant so much to me and to all of the Godzilla fans around the world. You are an icon. Our hero.\u201d<\/p>\n
