Mark Zuckerberg just can\u2019t quit the concept of creating his own payments system.<\/p>\n

Facebook parent Meta is reportedly working on several financial products to add to its platforms \u2013 including a virtual coin for use in its planned metaverse. Company employees have nicknamed the tokens \u201cZuck Bucks\u201d in a nod to the Facebook founder.<\/p>\n

\u201cZuck Bucks\u201d are expected to be a form of in-app currency controlled by Meta rather than another cryptocurrency linked to blockchain technology, the Financial Times reported, citing several sources familiar with the matter. <\/p>\n

Meta cut ties with its first doomed foray into crypto earlier this year following pushback from regulators.<\/p>\n

The proposed \u201cZuck Bucks\u201d would function similarly to \u201cRobux\u201d currency used to facilitate transactions on the popular children\u2019s video game platform Roblox \u2013 another company dabbling in the metaverse, according to report.<\/p>\n

The in-app currency is one of several financial products in the works for Meta, which is aiming to build fresh revenue streams and maintain its user base despite intense competition from rivals such as TikTok. Meta shares plunged earlier this year after the company reported the first decline in its active user base in its history.<\/p>\n

Meta is reportedly mulling several new financial products.SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Gett <\/p>\n

Meta is also considering the launch of \u201csocial tokens\u201d that could be awarded to users who make positive contributions to Facebook groups, as well as \u201ccreator coins\u201d to support specific influencers active on Instagram, according to the FT.<\/p>\n

Zuckerberg\u2019s company is also dabbling in non-fungible tokens, or NFTs. Earlier this year, the billionaire confirmed that Meta would be \u201cbringing NFTs to Instagram in the near term.\u201d The FT cited a memo indicating Faceook would launch a pilot program allowing NFTs to be shared and \u201cminted\u201d \u2013 or created \u2013 on the platform in mid-May.<\/p>\n

A Meta spokesperson told The Verge that the company has \u201cno updates to share today\u201d on the initiatives.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe continuously consider new product innovations for people, businesses, and creators. As a company, we are focused on building for the metaverse and that includes what payments and financial services might look like,\u201d the spokesperson said.<\/p>\n

Facebook has shifted its focus to the metaverse in recent months.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Facebook initially announced plans to launch its own cryptocurrency, dubbed Libra, in 2019. But the project quickly faced regulatory scrutiny, with federal officials and lawmakers citing concerns about Facebook\u2019s checkered history with user privacy and the potential impacts to the global economy.<\/p>\n

The Libra Association rebranded as \u201cDiem,\u201d but the Meta-backed project was forced to sell off its assets to Silvergate earlier this year after failing to gain traction with regulators.<\/p>\n

Facebook\u2019s attempts to refocus its business on the metaverse have drawn mixed reviews in recent months.
