\u200bCold sores can be a major pain. Not only do they look bad and feel irritating, but they can often carry an annoying social stigma that affects everything, from your relationship prospects to your professional life.<\/p>\n

Luckily, cold sores are reasonably easy to get rid of. Most of the time, they will heal on their own within a couple of weeks. However, for the fastest recovery, there are various cold sore treatments<\/a> through which you can heal quickly.<\/p>\n

We\u2019ve listed some of the most effective and fastest ways to heal a cold sore<\/h2>\n

Cold SoresCold sores are common and mostly harmless. They\u2019re caused by the herpes simplex virus, which spreads quickly from one person to another.<\/p>\n

In some people, the virus might cause a cold sore once or twice and never return again. But for other people, rear its head continuously. They tend to go away on their own within two weeks. However, that doesn\u2019t make them any less annoying.<\/p>\n

Fastest ways to heal a cold sore<\/h2>\n

Below are three things that can help cold sores to disappear quicker.<\/p>\n

1. DIY home remedies when used early, multiple remedies can help ease the pain and reduce inflammation \u2013 and may even shorten the duration of a cold sore, which includes:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • A cold, wet washcloth<\/li>\n
  • Ice compress<\/li>\n
  • Petroleum jelly<\/li>\n
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    2. Apply topical creams and ointments over-the-counter anesthetic creams, like benzocaine or lidocaine, which can help manage the pain. Like benzyl alcohol or docosanol, some product ingredients may even promote faster healing when used early and in the right amounts.<\/p>\n

    3. Prescription medications antivirals drugs, such as acyclovir, penciclovir, and famciclovir, can help you get rid of cold sores quickly. Suppose you\u2019re experiencing some of the severe side effects of having a cold sore. In that case, your doctor may prescribe you antiviral medication.<\/p>\n

    Pro Tip<\/em><\/strong>: to make cold sore heal faster, the best thing you can do is to start cold sore treatment as soon as possible. A burning sensation on your lips or face can be an early sign of cold sore development. The earlier you start reducing swelling and treating the cold sore, the faster it will heal.<\/em><\/p>\n

    Cold Sore PreventionThough the virus that causes cold sores does not have a cure, there are specific measures that you can take to prevent outbreaks. These preventive measures include:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Understanding your cold sore triggers: figuring out your cold sore triggers can help you keep them away in the long run. Some common triggers include sunburn, anxiety, hormonal changes, common cold, or injury to the lip. You may need to avoid extra sun exposure, reduce anxiety levels, or take other preventive actions to prevent future outbreaks.<\/li>\n
    • Replacing contaminated items: once you\u2019ve healed, make sure to replace your toothbrush, lip balm, and any other items that made contact with your cold sore. Wash and disinfect other things that you\u2019ve used. The virus can live on such items for days and can re-infect your lips again.<\/li>\n
    • Talk to your dentist<\/a> or doctor: if you suffer from cold sores often, your dentist or doctor may recommend taking an antiviral medication to help prevent outbreaks. They can also identify your possible triggers and suggest ways to reduce your chances of getting cold sores.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
