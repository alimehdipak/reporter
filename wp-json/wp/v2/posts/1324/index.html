Elon Musk will join Twitter\u2019s board of directors just a day after it was learned that he bought a 9% stake in the company \u2014 becoming the social media platform\u2019s largest shareholder.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m excited to share that we\u2019re appointing @elonmusk to our board!\u201d Twitter CEO Parag Agrawal tweeted on Tuesday.<\/p>\n

\u201cThrough conversations with Elon in recent weeks, it became clear to us that he would bring great value to our Board.\u201d<\/p>\n

Agrawal added: \u201cHe\u2019s both a passionate believer and intense critic of the service which is exactly what we need on @Twitter, and in the boardroom, to make us stronger in the long-term. Welcome Elon!\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk responded to Agrawal: \u201cLooking forward to working with Parag & Twitter board to make significant improvements to Twitter in coming months!\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk is taking a 9.2% stake in Twitter just days after Tesla\u2019s billionaire CEO\u00a0criticized the social network for stifling free speech\u00a0and publicly mused about creating a rival platform.<\/p>\n

An SEC filing revealed on Monday that Musk \u2014 the world\u2019s richest person with a fortune of more than $287 billion, according to Forbes \u2014 bought some 73.5 million shares of the company, which are worth an estimated $2.89 billion.<\/p>\n

News of the acquisition \u2014 which makes Musk\u00a0Twitter\u2019s largest shareholder, ahead of Vanguard, Morgan Stanley and BlackRock.<\/p>\n

Twitter stock was up by more than 25% in pre-market trading on Tuesday.<\/p>\n

Musk has been raising questions about the ability to communicate freely on Twitter, tweeting last month about\u00a0free speech\u00a0and the social media platform.<\/p>\n

\u201cFree speech is essential to a functioning democracy. Do you believe Twitter rigorously adheres to this principle?\u201d he tweeted.<\/p>\n

Musk filed papers with the SEC indicating that he purchased a 9% stake in Twitter.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The next day, Musk took it a step further, writing: \u201cGiven that Twitter serves as the de facto public town square, failing to adhere to free speech principles fundamentally undermines democracy. What should be done?\u201d<\/p>\n

In a\u00a0separate tweet, Musk said that he was \u201cgiving serious thought\u201d to creating a new social media platform.<\/p>\n

Due to Twitter\u2019s share structure, Musk\u2019s stake in the company will give him far more influence over the site\u2019s operations than can be exerted by outside investors in tech giants like Meta or Google.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Before acquiring the largest stake in Twitter, Musk was critical of the companies policies as they relate to free speech.SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Getty Images <\/p>\n

While Mark Zuckerberg holds special dual-class shares that effectively allow him to override Meta investors\u2019 objections and do whatever he wants,\u00a0Twitter only has one class of shares. That means Twitter shares held by founder Jack Dorsey give Dorsey the same voting power as shares held by Musk.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In a demonstration of the power that can be wielded by Twitter investors,\u00a0Dorsey\u00a0stepped down as Twitter\u2019s CEO\u00a0in 2021 following a heated activist campaign from shareholder Elliott Management. Elliott held around 4% of Twitter shares at the time \u2014 less than half of Musk\u2019s current stake.\u00a0<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
