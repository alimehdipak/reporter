Here she comes again.<\/p>\n

Dolly Parton, 76, recently shared that while she has long pondered doing a musical biopic or theater production telling the story of her nearly 66-year career, the project \u2014 like most things \u2014 was delayed due to the coronavirus.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019ve been talking about doing my Broadway musical and we were really, really way out ahead of that, and then COVID hit and that changed my mind about a whole lot of things,\u201d\u00a0Parton admitted during a recent appearance on \u201cMr. Nashville Talks.\u201d <\/p>\n

However, the music legend and celebrated philanthropist has not given up hope on seeing it come to life in some form.<\/p>\n

\u201cI do intend someday to be on Broadway, but I\u2019m thinking now that I might do my life story as a feature,\u201d she said of possibly making a movie. \u201cMaybe possibly even a musical feature, so we\u2019re in talks about that.\u201d <\/p>\n

Parton previously gave hope to other actresses, saying that the project would probably need several women to portray her throughout her storied six decades of success as an entertainer. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019d probably have to have \u2014 as long as my career has been \u2014 like, a little Dolly and a middle Dolly and then the older one,\u201d she said in a 2021 interview with Southern Living magazine. <\/p>\n

But one accomplished singer is practically a shoo-in to replicate her iconic blond bombshell look and twangy classics as one of the Dolly doppelg\u00e4ngers. <\/p>\n

\u201cI love Kristin Chenoweth,\u201d Parton said of the 53-year-old, award-winning Broadway star in a 2020 Marie Claire interview. \u201cShe\u2019s just absolutely fantastic.\u201d<\/p>\n

Both Parton and Chenoweth have expressed their excitement and desire to work on the project together. WireImage <\/p>\n

Luckily for fans of that dream casting, Parton isn\u2019t the only one excited for the anxiously awaited project. Chenoweth has also opened up about her excitement to star as the \u201cBackwoods Barbie\u201d and even gave fans a pseudo-audition. <\/p>\n

During an appearance on \u201cThe Kelly Clarkson Show\u201d in 2021, guest host Jay Leno asked Chenoweth if she plans to star as Parton in a musical, pointing out that she clearly has the voice \u2014 both speaking and singing \u2014 for the part. <\/p>\n

\u201cWell, I want to. I\u2019m lacking a couple things \u2014 and I think we both know what they are,\u201d she quipped, gesturing her hands near her breasts. <\/p>\n<\/p>\n

She noted that Parton has discussed her hopes for the \u201cWicked\u201d star to portray her but shared that there were no solid plans for the project. <\/p>\n

\u201cDolly, hurry up! I\u2019m ready!\u201d\u00a0Chenoweth said to the camera. \u201cI just grew up listening to her, and, you know, I do a pretty good \u2026 \u201d before crooning a perfect imitation of Parton\u2019s classic hit \u201cHere You Come Again.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

She then switched things up, sweetly singing a few lines of \u201cJolene,\u201d seemingly proving her right to be cast as Parton\u2019s replica. <\/p>\n
