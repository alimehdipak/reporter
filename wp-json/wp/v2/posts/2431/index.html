Ric Parnell, the British musician best known for playing drummer Mick Shrimpton in the cult rock mockumentary \u201cThis Is Spinal Tap,\u201d has died. <\/p>\n

Actor Harry Shearer, who starred in the film with Parnell, shared the news on Sunday via Twitter, writing, \u201cNo one ever rocked harder.\u201d<\/p>\n

Parnell\u2019s cause of death was not revealed. <\/p>\n

The London-born drummer was surrounded by a family of musicians. His grandfather Russ Carr was a music hall artist and his dad, Jack Parnell, served as the bandleader for \u201cThe Muppet Show.\u201d<\/p>\n

Parnell played in multiple bands, including Horse, Atomic Rooster, Nova and Stars. He claimed he declined invitations to play in Journey and Whitesnake, but is credited with playing drums on Toni Basil\u2019s song \u201cHey Mickey\u201d in 1981.<\/p>\n

But his big break came in 1984 when Parnell, alongside actor-musicians Michael McKean, Christopher Guest and Shearer portrayed the fictional rock band Spinal Tap on the big screen. The group became a real act, releasing albums and even appearing on \u201cSaturday Night Live.\u201d<\/p>\n

Parnell also played on material by actor Michael Des Barres, who remembered his collaborator on Twitter Monday. <\/p>\n

\u201cSome musicians are playing a role. Others are rock \u2018n\u2019 roll. Ric Parnell was the latter. The real thing,\u201d Barres wrote. <\/p>\n
