Rosmarie Trapp \u2014 the eldest child of famed Captain Georg Johannes von Trapp and Maria Augusta (Kutschera) von Trapp, who inspired \u201cThe Sound of Music\u201d \u2014 has died at age 93. <\/p>\n

Her cause of death has not been revealed but an obituary noted that she passed away May 13 while surrounded by loved ones at a nursing home in Morrisville, Vermont. <\/p>\n

\u201cHer kindness, generosity, and colorful spirit were legendary, and she had a positive impact on countless lives,\u201d her obituary reads. <\/p>\n

Trapp was born on Feb. 8, 1929, in a village on the outskirts of Salzburg, Austria. <\/p>\n

She was raised with seven older half-siblings \u2014 from her father\u2019s widow \u2014 and two younger siblings in the family that was famously memorialized in the Broadway show and classic 1965 film \u201cThe Sound of Music.\u201d The show tells the story of how her parents fell in love and escaped Austria as a family music group after her father was pressured to cooperate with the Nazis. <\/p>\n

The family\u2019s story was altered for the dramatic storyline and no character is meant to represent Trapp. <\/p>\n

Trapp and her family settled in Vermont, opening a lodge and a brewery after escaping to the US. <\/p>\n

Her family settled in the United States in 1938, first living in Pennsylvania and then settling in Vermont. They bought a farm, which became the Trapp Family Lodge, in 1950, and Trapp became a US citizen the next year, simply signing her name: Rosmarie Trapp. <\/p>\n

She spent many years traveling and performing with her family group, the Trapp Family Singers, and worked at the lodge when they began hosting guests. The devoted Christian also worked as a volunteer, missionary and teacher\u00a0around the world. <\/p>\n

When she wasn\u2019t traveling, Trapp settled in Stowe, Vermont where she was remembered as a \u201ccharacter.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cRosmarie was famous for walking everywhere, and locals marveled at her pulling her purchases home in a wagon or on a cart, often for a significant distance,\u201d read a line in her obituary.<\/p>\n

The classic 1965 film \u201cThe Sound of Music\u201d was based on the von Trapp family\u2019s story and escape from the Nazis who occupied their home country of Austria. Corbis via Getty Images The real von Trapp Family Singers, whose lives were featured in \u201cThe Sound of Music.\u201dBettmann Archive <\/p>\n

Over the years, she reportedly wrote so many letters to the weekly Stowe Reporter that the local newspaper gave her her own section, titled \u201cRosmarie\u2019s Corner,\u201d to write \u201cabout whatever happened to pop into her head.\u201d <\/p>\n

Johannes von Trapp, 82, the youngest of the children and last living member of the famous von Trapp family, opened von Trapp Brewing on the family property near her home in 2010. The family reported that Rosmarie \u201cenjoyed the occasional beer right up to the end.\u201d<\/p>\n

She was buried on the family property in a private ceremony but a celebration of life will be held on Sunday, June 5, at the Stowe Community Church. <\/p>\n

Never caring much about her family\u2019s fame, Trapp\u2019s legacy goes beyond \u201cThe Sound of Music,\u201d as she is remembered most for her \u201cgenerosity,\u201d according to the obit. <\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
