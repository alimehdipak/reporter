A \u201cgiant slingshot\u201d contraption will be testing out its catapult capabilities on a NASA craft later this year.<\/p>\n

Spaceflight technology firm SpinLaunch has revealed its futuristic A-33 Suborbital Mass Accelerator that\u2019s said to act just like a slingshot.<\/p>\n

The launcher is aiming to test out its spinning skills on a NASA craft containing a satellite.<\/p>\n

It will spin the craft at up to 5,000 miles per hour.<\/p>\n

Concept images show how SpinLaunch uses a rotating carbon fiber arm within a 300-foot diameter steel vacuum chamber.<\/p>\n

The design means a rocket can be launched with less fuel.<\/p>\n

Jonathan Yaney, founder and CEO of SpinLaunch, said: \u201cSpinLaunch is offering a unique suborbital flight and high-speed testing service, and the recent launch agreement with NASA marks a key inflection point as SpinLaunch shifts focus from technology development to commercial offerings.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhat started as an innovative idea to make space more accessible has materialized into a technically mature and game-changing approach to launch.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe look forward to announcing more partners and customers soon, and greatly appreciate NASA\u2019s continued interest and support in SpinLaunch.\u201d<\/p>\n
