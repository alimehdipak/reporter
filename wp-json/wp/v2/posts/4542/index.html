An incident during the Twelfth Parade in Holyland \u2014 an Ulster Protestant\u00a0celebration in Belfast, Northern Ireland \u2014 has gone viral after a spectator picked up the garbage can from his front yard and threw it at a drummer in what appears to be the Bangor Protestant Boys Band\u00a0as they marched down the street. <\/p>\n

The entire band suddenly rushed the perpetrator, who then ran inside his home. But band members seemingly won the fight when they picked up a large trash can on the man\u2019s property and threw it at \u2014 and smashed \u2014 his front window. <\/p>\n

Two police officers jumped in and attempted to defuse the situation, which was captured in a 40-second clip on Twitter that has been viewed more than 12 million times. <\/p>\n

Members of Orange Order \u2014 an international Protestant fraternal group based in North Ireland \u2014 also waved the band members back to their places, according to a report on Belfast Live. <\/p>\n
