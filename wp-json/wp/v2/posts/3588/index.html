During a routine tooth cleaning, your dentist or dental assistant will have you lay in the automatic chair for the duration of the appointment. They will then conduct the following procedures:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Detailed teeth cleaning to remove sticky plaque or build-up<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Gentle scraping of the plaque along the gum line<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Flossing between each tooth<\/span><\/li>\n
  • A tongue inspection<\/span><\/li>\n
  • A detailed cavity check<\/span><\/li>\n
  • A detailed inspection of the gum area to check for decay or damage<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Pending your age and which check up this is, you may also experience the following procedures during teeth cleaning:<\/span><\/p>\n

      \n
    • X-rays of the teeth to check for alignment problems or problems within the teeth and jaw<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Fluoride treatments help support healthy tooth enamel and protect the mouth against bacteria<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Evaluation of a teeth whitening procedure<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Who performs routine teeth cleaning<\/span><\/h2>\n

      Typically, a dental assistant performs routine teeth cleaning procedures. They are who you will usually see unless the dentist<\/a> has the time to do the procedures. However, you will also see your dentist for a few minutes before you leave so they can do a final check and ensure the dental assistant hasn\u2019t missed anything.<\/span><\/p>\n

      Dentists will also conduct x-rays and fluoride treatments as well as discuss any findings that the dental assistant noticed.<\/span><\/p>\n

      Why are regular teeth cleanings important?<\/span><\/h2>\n

      Having your teeth checked and cleaned by your dentist is critical to maintaining a healthy mouth, teeth, and gums. Your dentist will notice things you do not and can help prevent damage to the mouth overall. They will also be able to talk with you about concerns you may have such as:<\/span><\/p>\n

        \n
      • Tooth\/gum sensitivity<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Pain in the gums<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Bite misalignments<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Abrasions or abscesses inside the mouth<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Potential cavities<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        How often should you get your teeth cleaned by your dentist?<\/span><\/h2>\n

        You should see your dentist every 6-12 months pending your oral conditions. If you have a relatively healthy mouth, you can likely see your dentist once per year and maintain that status! However, if you notice anything new such as unexpected pain or sensitivity, you should make an appointment to see your dentist to avoid further damage to the area.<\/span><\/p>\n

        At what age should you start seeing a dentist?<\/span><\/h2>\n

        Dentists often start seeing patients as young as 6 months old. This is to ensure that there are no obvious signs of oral complications. They can also predict future problems that may occur and what to watch out for in your child<\/a>. From there, you should be seeing a dentist every 6-12 months.<\/span><\/p>\n

        How long does a usual dental cleaning procedure take?<\/span><\/h2>\n

        Pending what you need done during your routine dental cleaning, the appointment can take anywhere from 30 to 60 minutes. Typically, a routine cleaning without any added procedures will only take half an hour and end with you getting a sticker or a new toothbrush!<\/span><\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
