Goth girl icon Christina Ricci has joined the cast of \u201cWednesday,\u201d the Tim Burton-directed \u201cAddams Family\u201d spinoff series coming to Netflix. <\/p>\n

Ricci, 42, was rumored to replace Thora Birch in an unnamed role following the latter\u2019s untimely exit from the show last year, Deadline reported on Monday. The publication said that Ricci is already filming in Romania, where production is expected to wrap at the end of the month.<\/p>\n

Ricci is often remembered for her role as Wednesday Addams in Barry Sonnenfeld\u2019s 1990s franchise \u201cThe Addams Family.\u201d In Burton\u2019s new supernatural series, written by \u201cSmallville\u201d creators Al Gough and Miles Millar, the \u201cYellowjackets\u201d star will be a series regular and play an entirely new character in the Addams family tree, according to Deadline.<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out to Ricci\u2019s representatives for comment.<\/p>\n

Fans themselves pushed for Ricci\u2019s casting with a viral petition last year that called for cinema\u2019s original Wednesday Addams to take part.<\/p>\n

The new live-action \u201cWednesday\u201d follows the titular teenage outcast, played by 19-year-old \u201cScream\u201d actress Jenna Ortega, through her years at Nevermore Academy. Catherine Zeta-Jones, 52, assumed the role of Addams matriarch Morticia, alongside Luis Guzm\u00e1n, 65, as her husband Gomez.<\/p>\n

In 1999, Ricci was cast opposite Johnny Depp, 58, in Burton\u2019s folklore horror film \u201cSleepy Hollow.\u201d<\/p>\n
