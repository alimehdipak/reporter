The Queen of Christmas, Mariah Carey, isn\u2019t having a very merry start to the summer.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In documents acquired by The Post,\u00a0Andy Stone, who co-wrote Vince Vance & the Valiants\u2019 country song \u201cAll I Want For Christmas Is You,\u201d is suing Carey for copyright infringement \u2014 claiming the diva never sought or obtained use of his title for her perennial monster holiday hit. <\/p>\n

Stone is also coming after Carey\u2019s co-writer on the song, Walter Afanasieff, in the civil suit, which was filed in the US District Court of the Eastern District of Louisiana.<\/p>\n

Stone released his 1989 twangy version \u2014 which musically and lyrically sounds nothing like Carey\u2019s pop performance \u2014 before Carey changed the holiday season forever in 1994. In the suit, he claims his song got extensive airplay during the 1993 holiday season, a year before Carey released hers. <\/p>\n

The Scrooge is suing the \u201cObsessed\u201d singer for $20 million in damages.<\/p>\n

\u201cPlaintiff [Stone] personally requested that Defendants [Carey and Afanasieff] cease and desist from further distribution of Plaintiff\u2019s work,\u201d the suit reads in part. \u201cDespite Plaintiff\u2019s request, Defendants continue to exploit Plaintiff\u2019s work \u2018All I Want for Christmas is You,\u2019 reaping tremendous financial awards and other pecuniary benefits to the detriment of Plaintiff.\u201d<\/p>\n

Carey is being sued for $20 million for her perennial holiday hit \u201cAll I Want for Christmas Is You.\u201d Andy Stone, who co-wrote a country song of the same title, is alleging copyright infringement. Right: A still from the music video for Stone-written tune.FilmMagic; Vince Vance and the Valiants <\/p>\n

According to the documents, Carey and Afanasieff \u201cknowingly, willfully and intentionally engaged in a campaign to infringe [Stone\u2019s] copyright in the work \u2026 to the commercial gain, personal profit and unjust enrichment of the defendants and the irreparable injury and financial loss\u201d of Stone.<\/p>\n

Carey\u2019s representative declined to comment when contacted by The Post. <\/p>\n<\/p>\n

Decades later, streams and radio airplay of Carey\u2019s \u201cAll I Want For Christmas Is You\u201d continue to stuff her stocking full of cash. The song has over a billion streams on Spotify alone. <\/p>\n

According to the lawsuit, Stone is a self-employed artist in the business of performing, selling and licensing his copyrighted music and earns his livelihood from fees received from these activities.<\/p>\n

Carey\u2019s \u201cAll I Want For Christmas Is You\u201d is the ultimate gift that keeps on giving, reportedly putting millions into her bank account every year.Mariah Carey <\/p>\n
