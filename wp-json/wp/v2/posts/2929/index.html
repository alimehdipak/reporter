What is a Dental Emergency?<\/b><\/h2>\n

A dental emergency can be defined as any condition that requires immediate attention. The most common conditions include toothache, abscessed tooth, loose tooth, and tooth fracture. Other less common conditions include broken teeth, bleeding gums, gum disease, and cracked teeth. The good news is that it\u2019s easy to identify a dental emergency. If you\u2019re experiencing any of these symptoms, then it\u2019s time to seek help.<\/span><\/p>\n

Tooth Loss (Avulsion)<\/b><\/h3>\n

If you lose a tooth, it is very important that you see a dentist right away. If you wait, you may suffer from serious health problems. A tooth is the most important part of your mouth, and it is very important that you replace it as soon as possible. It is very important to keep all of your teeth healthy so that they can help you to have good oral hygiene.<\/span><\/p>\n

Tooth Displacement<\/b><\/h3>\n

It is important to have a dentist examine your teeth to see if they are displaced. If they are, then your dentist<\/a> will take X-rays of your teeth to determine the best way to treat your tooth. If your tooth is displaced, your dentist may recommend that you get a root canal. A root canal is a dental procedure that removes the nerve inside the tooth and cleans the inside of the tooth. It is done when the nerve inside the tooth becomes infected.<\/span><\/p>\n

Chipped or Broken Teeth<\/b><\/h3>\n

A fractured tooth is one of the most painful injuries you can suffer. Fortunately, if you are able to see a dentist promptly, you can usually save the tooth. You may be able to avoid the need for extraction, or in severe cases, you may need to extract the tooth and then replace it. Either way, you need to get immediate dental attention. To make sure you are getting the best care possible, look for a dentist who has experience in treating fractures and will be able to offer you the best possible outcome.<\/span><\/p>\n

Oral Infections<\/b><\/h3>\n

The most common way to treat this condition is with antibiotics, which are prescribed by a dentist. If the infection does not respond to antibiotic treatment, the dentist will drain the abscess with a needle. The dentist may also need to extract the tooth or teeth. In some cases, the abscess may require surgery to remove the infected tissue.<\/span><\/p>\n
