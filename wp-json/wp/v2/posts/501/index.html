Your iPhone is chockablock with handy tricks and features many of which you probably aren\u2019t aware of.<\/p>\n

A TikTok tech nut recently revealed a raft of lesser-known iPhone tips that make using your device that little bit easier.<\/p>\n

User @katamogz highlighted them in a\u00a0video\u00a0shared with her 2.4million followers earlier this month.<\/p>\n

Mute notifications<\/h2>\n

If an app is sending you too many notifications you can quickly mute it from the Lock Screen.<\/p>\n

The Lock Screen shows the current time and date and your most recent notifications and appears when you turn on or wake your iPhone.<\/p>\n

To stop an app from sending you alerts for a while,\u00a0swipe left\u00a0on a notification and tap\u00a0options.<\/p>\n

You can then select to mute notifications for that service for an hour or the rest of the day.<\/p>\n

Screenshot an entire webpage<\/h2>\n

There\u2019s an easy way to take a screenshot of an entire webpage instead of just the section that\u2019s visible on your display.<\/p>\n

First, you\u2019ll need to take a screenshot by pressing two buttons simultaneously.<\/p>\n

On iPhones without a Home Button, hit the power and volume up buttons at the same time.<\/p>\n

On older devices, press the power button and Home Button to take one.<\/p>\n

From there, tap\u00a0Full page\u00a0at the top of your display and tap\u00a0Done\u00a0to save the entire page as a PDF.<\/p>\n

Siri can send links<\/h2>\n

If you\u2019re feeling particularly lazy, you can ask the iPhone\u2019s voice assistant Siri to send web links to your pals.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s far less fiddly than copying and pasting a link into iMessage or other chat services.<\/p>\n

While looking at a web page on your iPhone, simply say \u201cHey Siri, share this with [name]\u201d (or something similar like \u201csend this to [name]\u201d.<\/p>\n

Siri will share the content on the screen to that person using iMessage.<\/p>\n

Send custom iMessage effects<\/h2>\n

The TikTok user shows that you can customize the emojis in your message effects. TikTok <\/p>\n

You probably already know about the\u00a0colorful animations\u00a0you can add to your iPhone texts using special codewords.<\/p>\n

All you need to do is send someone a special trigger word over iMessage to activate each effect.<\/p>\n

Typing \u201cpew pew\u201d activates a laser effect, for instance, while \u201chappy birthday\u201d causes balloons to float around the screen.<\/p>\n

But did you know you can create a custom screen effect using a combination of whatever emoji you like?<\/p>\n

To try it out for yourself, type out three or fewer emojis and then hold the\u00a0send button\u00a0(that\u2019s the green arrow to the right of the textbox).<\/p>\n

Tap\u00a0Screen\u00a0at the top of your display to send them with a full-screen effect. Tap\u00a0Send.<\/p>\n
