Sean \u201cDiddy\u201d Combs will use his 2022 Billboard Music Awards hosting and executive producer roles to spread \u201clove and forgiveness\u201d around some formerly unlovable dudes. <\/p>\n

The entertainment mogul, 52, backed the show\u2019s decision to include rapper Travis Scott and country singer Morgan Wallen in its star-studded performance lineup.<\/p>\n

In fact, Diddy \u201cdemanded\u201d that Scott perform.<\/p>\n

\u201cI said, \u2018My brother Travis Scott has to perform\u2019 \u2026 And NBC said yes,\u201d Diddy said in an Instagram video.<\/p>\n

\u201cAs a musical family, none of us are saints; none of us are without things that happen to them in life,\u201d he said in a Billboard interview. <\/p>\n

\u201cSo one of the things I\u2019m doing directly is uncanceling the canceled. That\u2019s breaking news because people haven\u2019t been about uncanceling. But canceling is a trend that needs to stop,\u201d he said of the popular Gen Z trend.<\/p>\n

\u201cTravis went through a tragedy; Morgan [used the N-word] while talking to his boy,\u201d the self-proclaimed \u201cPrimetime Puff\u201d reminded readers. \u201cPeople make mistakes. Now we\u2019re moving on with love and respect for everybody that was hurt or affected. It\u2019s time to forgive.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cTo have Morgan and Travis be able to come back and touch the stage again with the mindset of getting a second chance at life. Everybody in the room is getting a second chance at life, you know, because we\u2019re back inside with no masks on. We need love and I\u2019m excited about celebrating that.\u201d<\/p>\n

Both Scott\u2019s and Wallen\u2019s careers took a hit in 2021. <\/p>\n

Video recorded by a neighbor of Wallen, who is white, drunkenly yelling the slur went viral in February. While coverage of the incident seemingly helped his digital album and song sales, according to NBC, radio stations banned his songs and Big Loud Records suspended his deal \u201cindefinitely.\u201d <\/p>\n

The \u201cWasted on You\u201d singer, 29, was dropped from streaming services and deemed ineligible for the Academy of Country Music and Country Music Television Awards.<\/p>\n

He finally apologized for the comment on Instagram and admitted the word came from a \u201c72-hour bender.\u201d He attended rehab, took months off from performing and apologized again on \u201cGood Morning America,\u201d but couldn\u2019t shake the effects of the incident.<\/p>\n

In November, he announced \u201cThe Dangerous Tour,\u201d which kicked off in February. The tour will end in October.<\/p>\n

Morgan Wallen performs during the \u201cWhiskey Glasses Roadshow Tour\u201d in New Orleans in January 2020.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, Scott, now 31, has been laying low ever since 10 people were trampled to death at his annual Astroworld Festival in Houston on Nov. 5. <\/p>\n

The \u201cGoosebumps\u201d rapper, the venue and the festival promoters have been slapped with over 100 lawsuits from the incident.<\/p>\n

Houston Police Chief Troy Finner claimed he personally warned Scott about \u201cpublic safety\u201d in his trailer before the deadly event. <\/p>\n

Travis Scott performs during the 2021 Astroworld Festival on Nov. 5, 2021, in Houston, Texas.WireImage <\/p>\n

Social media was outraged once videos of fans passing out in the stampede surfaced. Scott paused the show several times but received backlash when the show proceeded despite the crowd\u2019s \u201cstop the show\u201d chants, as well as an ambulance aiding struggling fans. <\/p>\n

Scott and \u201cSicko Mode\u201d collaborator Drake celebrated at Dave and Buster\u2019s after the event. <\/p>\n

Scott told Charlamagne tha God that he didn\u2019t know anything about the tragedy until \u201cminutes before the [police] press conference.\u201d<\/p>\n

He performed for the first time since Astroworld on Saturday during the Miami Grand Prix in Florida at E11EVEN nightclub. <\/p>\n

But all of that is old news to Diddy.<\/p>\n
