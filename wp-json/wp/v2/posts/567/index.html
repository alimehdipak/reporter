A mind-blowing 3D reconstruction has revealed what a Stone Age woman looked like 4,000 years ago.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s now on display at V\u00e4sternorrlands Museum in Sweden and is based on skeletal remains that were found in 1923.<\/p>\n

The remains of the Stone Age woman were found next to the body of a seven-year-old boy who may have been her son.<\/p>\n

Scientists have reconstructed her after all this time and designed her expression as if she was watching over her child.<\/p>\n

According to\u00a0LiveScience, Oscar Nilsson, the Sweden-based forensic artist\u00a0who worked on the model, explained: \u201cWith our eyes and perhaps in all times, you tend to think that this is a mother and son.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey could be. Or they could be siblings: sister and brother. They could be relatives, or they could just be tribe friends.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe don\u2019t know, because the\u00a0DNA\u00a0was not that well preserved to establish this relationship.\u201d<\/p>\n

The remains of the Stone Age woman were found next to the body of a seven year old boy who may have been her son.Oscar Nilsson <\/p>\n

Forensic details suggest that the Stone Age woman died in her 20s or early 30s.<\/p>\n

She had been laid to rest in a stone coffin.<\/p>\n

Her remains didn\u2019t show signs of injury or diseases.<\/p>\n

Structure was added to her face as realistically as possible.Oscar Nilsson <\/p>\n

At 4 feet, 11 inches\u00a0she wasn\u2019t very tall and was a similar height to the child she was buried close to.<\/p>\n

A copy of the ancient woman\u2019s skull was made using a 3D printer and several factors such as age, weight and height were used to conjure up a realistic face.<\/p>\n

Her brown hair and eye color was an educated guess.<\/p>\n

Her clothes were coated in moose brains as this is thought to have been a traditional practice.Oscar Nilsson <\/p>\n

It\u2019s likely she was a farmer and a hunter-gatherer.<\/p>\n

Swedish archaeologist Helena Gjaerum designed the woman\u2019s clothes.<\/p>\n

Animal skins were slathered in moose brains to soften them and prevent rotting.<\/p>\n

This skull belonged to a Stone Age woman.Oscar Nilsson <\/p>\n

This is said to be a traditional technique.<\/p>\n

Gjaerum made sure the Stone Age woman\u2019s clothes were realistic and as practical as possible.<\/p>\n
