<\/p>\n

Your gadgets could be in grave danger \u2013 and it\u2019s all linked to a simple Google Chrome mistake.<\/p>\n

Cyber experts have warned that not taking care of the Google web browser could put you at risk of major hack attacks.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s all linked to updates \u2013 and if you don\u2019t install them, it\u2019s bad news.<\/p>\n

Just this week,\u00a0Google\u00a0was forced to\u00a0rush out an emergency update\u00a0to stop a \u201chigh severity\u201d bug from being exploited.<\/p>\n

But if you don\u2019t install updates that squash these bugs, you\u2019ll miss out on key protections.<\/p>\n

\u201cNot only does updating your Chrome browser introduce new features, such as privacy & web tracking protection, but they also guard against critical security threats,\u201d said Mike Varley, cyber-expert and Threat Hunting Lead at Adarma, speaking to The Sun.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe recent update from Google targets what is called a \u201czero-day\u201d.<\/p>\n

\u201cA zero-day is a vulnerability that has just been discovered, is known to attackers and is known to be actively being exploited.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhen this happens, it is a race against time to develop patches and protect end-users whilst they are rolled out.\u201d<\/p>\n

This week\u2019s Google blunder was described as \u201chigh severity\u201d.<\/p>\n

An emergency update was rolled out this week to stop a \u201chigh severity\u201d bug from being exploited.Getty Images <\/p>\n

It also affected Microsoft Edge users, causing the rival firm to push out a similarly urgent update.<\/p>\n

This could indicate that it\u2019s a significant issue that may already be being exploited in the real world.<\/p>\n

That\u2019s why it\u2019s so important to\u00a0update your Google Chrome\u00a0as often as possible.<\/p>\n

\u201cNever updating\u00a0Google Chrome\u00a0can leave dangerous vulnerabilities in your browser, which can be leveraged by malicious actors as an entry point on to your device,\u201d Mike told The Sun.<\/p>\n

\u201cMalicious websites that host exploit kits can identify potential vulnerabilities in connecting clients, and then take advantage of those weaknesses to gain initial access.<\/p>\n

\u201cFrom there, an attacker could be free to roam about your computer or to other devices on your network, collecting personal information, email & bank account details, infecting with malware or deploying ransomware to coerce a payment.<\/p>\n

\u201cAn up-to-date browser, although it seems simple, can greatly contribute towards keeping you safe online.\u201d<\/p>\n

The update introduces new features, such as privacy and web tracking protection, and guarding against critical security threats.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Google\u2019s latest issue was first flagged on March 23, and requires urgent attention.<\/p>\n

Sadly, we don\u2019t know exactly what will happen if you don\u2019t update your browser.<\/p>\n

How to update Google Chrome<\/h2>\n

The update you\u2019re looking for on Google Chrome is version 99.04844.84 or later.<\/p>\n

To do this, go to the three dots in the top-right corner, and then choose Help.<\/p>\n

Now go to About Google Chrome and then begin the update.<\/p>\n

You should do this regularly to keep your PC or Mac protected against the latest hack attacks.<\/p>\n

But it\u2019s not foolproof: stay vigilant online and don\u2019t click unsolicited links, hand over personal information, or download unverified apps.<\/p>\n
