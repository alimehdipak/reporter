<\/p>\n

Sheryl Sandberg, Facebook parent Meta\u2019s second-highest ranked executive, twice pressured the Daily Mail to kill potentially damaging stories about her then-boyfriend, Activision CEO Bobby Kotick, according to a report Thursday.<\/p>\n

The bombshell report alleges Sandberg contacted the British tabloid in 2016 and 2019. <\/p>\n

On both occasions, the Daily Mail was working on a story which would have revealed that one of Kotick\u2019s ex-girlfriends had obtained a temporary restraining order against him in 2014.<\/p>\n

Sandberg and Kotick reportedly worked with a team that included active employees at Facebook and Activision as well as paid outside advisers to develop a plan on how to get the Mail to table the stories.<\/p>\n

Sandberg and her team were said to be concerned that an unflattering report on Kotick would be harmful to her reputation as an advocate for women \u2013 which includes her work with the nonprofit advocacy organization Lean In.<\/p>\n

Sources told the Wall Street Journal that Sandberg was first involved in efforts to scuttle the story in 2016, when she and Kotick had first started dating, as well as in 2019, closer to their breakup.<\/p>\n

Sandberg is Facebook parent Meta\u2019s second-highest executive.Getty Images for Vanity Fair <\/p>\n

Facebook officials are actively probing whether Sandberg\u2019s behavior constituted a violation of company rules.<\/p>\n

The Wall Street Journal reported that the Daily Mail\u2019s digital arm was probing court filing related to the claims against Kotick. The ex-girlfriend obtained a temporary restraining order after the Activision executive allegedly confronted her at her home.<\/p>\n

In both 2016 and 2019, Sandberg purportedly told the Daily Mail that Kotick\u2019s accuser had retracted her claims. The outlet never published a story on the matter.<\/p>\n

A Daily Mail representative did not immediately return a request for comment on the report.<\/p>\n

Court records revealed the temporary restraining order against Kotick was removed shortly after it was implemented. Sources told the Wall Street Journal that Kotick\u2019s accuser had told others that some of her claims were exaggerated or false. Additionally, the Daily Mail is purportedly aware that some of the claims were false.<\/p>\n

Sandberg and her associates reportedly feared potential damage to her reputation as an advocate for women.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Kotick\u2019s former girlfriend slammed the Wall Street Journal\u2019s report in a statement to The Post through her representative.<\/p>\n

\u201cI told the Wall Street Journal that what I said 8 years ago about Bobby was false.\u00a0 It is still false,\u201d the woman said. \u201cIn fact, in 2014, I signed a sworn statement making clear that what I had said was untrue. Nonetheless, the Journal decided to exploit me for an article it wanted to publish about Bobby.\u201d<\/p>\n

Facebook is probing whether Sandberg violated its policies.Getty Images for Step Up <\/p>\n

According to the Journal, Kotick told associates that Sandberg warned the Daily Mail that any article they published regarding the situation could hurt the outlet\u2019s relationship with Facebook \u2013 whose platform is a considerable source of traffic and revenue for countless media outlets.<\/p>\n

Kotick denied that claim in a statement to the Journal, telling the outlet he \u201cnever said anything like that.\u201d<\/p>\n

When reached for comment, a Meta spokesperson said Sandberg \u201cnever threatened the MailOnline\u2019s business relationship with Facebook in order to influence an editorial decision.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThis story attempts to make connections that don\u2019t exist,\u201d the spokesperson added.<\/p>\n

The Daily Mail stories purportedly would have revealed that one of Kotick\u2019s ex-girlfriends obtained a restraining order against him in 2014.Getty Images <\/p>\n

The Journal noted that there were \u201cconflicting accounts\u201d of what Sandberg said during her outreach \u2013 including whether she mentioned Facebook or her role at the company.<\/p>\n

But some Facebook executives are said to be concerned that any involvement from Sandberg \u2013 even a phone call \u2013 could be perceived as a threat because of her position at the company.<\/p>\n

Activision\u2019s board of directors said in a lengthy statement that it has \u201cbeen aware of the circumstances reported involving an incident in 2014\u201d and noted Kotick \u201chas ben fully transparent with the board.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Daily Mail never published the article on the allegations against Kotick.Shutterstock \/ chrisdorney <\/p>\n

\u201cThe Board, through its counsel Skadden Arps, has done a thorough examination of the facts and circumstances of the events, satisfied itself that there was no merit to the allegations, and notes that they concern a personal relationship that has nothing to do with the business of the Company,\u201d the Activision directors said in a statement.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe Board continues to have full confidence in Mr. Kotick\u2019s leadership and his ability to run the Company,\u201d the statement added.<\/p>\n

Microsoft bought Activision-Blizzard earlier this year in a deal valued at nearly $69 billion. The deal followed months of turmoil at Activision \u2014 including intense scrutiny over Kotick\u2019s handling of allegations of widespread workplace misconduct. <\/p>\n

Kotick\u2019s future role at Activision after the deal closes is unclear.<\/p>\n
