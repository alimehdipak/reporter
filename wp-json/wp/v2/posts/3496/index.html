Teeth whitening is the process of lightening the teeth by removing stains and plaque buildup on the front of the teeth. The purpose is to lighten the teeth to their natural shade or even lighter than their natural shade. Pending the severity of the discoloration, the process can take 1 to 3 visits and requires the care of a dentist.<\/span><\/p>\n

Although you can do a teeth whitening treatment at home, it\u2019s recommended to do so under the care of a dentist. This is to ensure no damage<\/a> to the teeth, gums, or mouth occur from improper techniques<\/span><\/p>\n

What are the two types of teeth whitening?<\/span><\/h2>\n

Although the terms are used interchangeably, the two types of teeth whitening include traditional whitening and bleaching. Traditional teeth whitening is the process in which surface stains and stubborn plaque are removed from the teeth to lighten the teeth to their natural shade.<\/span><\/p>\n

However, some people will also engage in bleach whitening which is a more extreme version of teeth whitening. This process uses bleach to remove surface stains and set-in stains to lighten the teeth to a shade that is higher than their natural color.<\/span><\/p>\n

Typically, you can use traditional teeth whitening on select teeth since you\u2019re only whitening them to their natural shade. However, with bleach whitening, you should do the entire mouth to keep them all at one consistent shade.<\/span><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Who conducts a teeth whitening procedure?<\/span><\/h2>\n
    \n
  • Teeth whitening is performed by a dentist in a dentist’s office. The way the procedure works is as follows:<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Your dentist will determine the best treatment method for your needs (bleach or traditional).<\/span><\/li>\n
  • A protective gel is applied to the gums, lips, and tongue to protect these areas from the treatment<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Pending the treatment method chosen, you\u2019ll either experience a laser treatment or a gel treatment. Both treatments are done in 20-minute intervals over the course of one hour.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • You\u2019ll then gently rinse your mouth.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • You may be given at-home trays to continue the whitening process at home if desired by your dentist.<\/span><\/li>\n
  • You may need more than one treatment, but this is determined by your dentist.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Why would you need your teeth whitened?<\/span><\/h2>\n

    There are a variety of reasons you may be considering teeth whitening. However, the most common reasons include:<\/span><\/p>\n

      \n
    • Poor oral care that led to discoloration<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Discoloration due to smoking or alcohol consumption<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Discoloration due to braces<\/span><\/li>\n
    • Discoloration from medical procedures such as chemotherapy<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
