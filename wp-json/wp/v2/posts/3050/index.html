PITTSBURGH — The last time the Pittsburgh Steelers had an open quarterback competition, Jim Miller, Mike Tomczak and Kordell Stewart were vying for the job during the 1996 offseason.<\/p>\n

Now, nearly three decades later, a new trio of challengers are preparing to compete for the job. That evaluation begins this weekend when Kenny Pickett suits up as a Steeler for the first time at the UPMC Rooney Sports Complex for rookie minicamp.<\/p>\n

With Ben Roethlisberger\u2019s retirement, the Steelers spent their offseason retooling and reloading in an effort to find his replacement. After free agency and the NFL draft, the Steelers came away with three additions. Joining Mason Rudolph in the quarterback room will be Mitch Trubisky, Pickett and Chris Oladokun.<\/p>\n

Realistically, seventh-round pick Oladokun isn\u2019t in the mix for the starting job, making this a three-man race. Will it be the rookie, the free-agent acquisition looking to turn things around or the in-house candidate attempting to make a statement?<\/p>\n

The Steelers used their first-round pick, No. 20 overall, to draft Kenny Pickett, but does that mean he’ll get the starting nod in Week 1?\u00a0AP Photo\/Keith Srakocic<\/p>\n

Kenny Pickett<\/h2>\n

NFL experience: Rookie<\/p>\n

Why he has a chance to be the starter: The Steelers don\u2019t view their first-round pick as a developmental project, putting him firmly in the mix to be the Week 1 starter. After drafting the 24-year-old Pickett with the 20th pick, Mike Tomlin said the quarterback will get the opportunity to compete for the starting job, and both Tomlin and general manager Kevin Colbert said Pickett\u2019s decision to stay in college for another year greatly aided in his maturity and development.<\/p>\n

In a Pittsburgh radio interview, Colbert compared Pickett\u2019s last season at Pitt to a rookie season in the NFL.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s almost like he spent his rookie NFL season at the college level, and really mastered it,\u201d Colbert told 105.9 The X. \u201cCoach [Pat] Narduzzi and coach [Mark] Whipple provided him an opportunity to take that step in a pro schematic. And it is easier to project those types of players and those schematics to our level. It\u2019s not that the others that play in a more college-type offense can\u2019t do it — it\u2019s just more guesswork.\u201d<\/p>\n

Pickett also has the benefit of reuniting with offensive coordinator Matt Canada, who recruited him to Pitt. The two never worked together on the college level, as Canada left for LSU, but Pickett is familiar with Canada\u2019s concepts and Canada obviously felt Pickett fit in with what he wanted to do.<\/p>\n

Why he\u2019s vulnerable: Pickett is a rookie. Yes, he\u2019ll be in the friendly confines of Heinz Field and a city that embraced him during his college career, but the transition to the NFL shouldn\u2019t be underestimated. There\u2019s adjusting to NFL defenses and the speed of the game, plus learning a new playbook.<\/p>\n

But Whipple, who coached Pickett for three seasons at Pitt and worked with Roethlisberger as his quarterbacks coach during his first three seasons, believes Pickett\u2019s transition to the NFL won\u2019t be as difficult as it is for other rookies because he\u2019s staying in a familiar environment.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don’t know if it’s the easiest transition,\u201d Whipple said, \u201cbut it’s pretty much easier than maybe Ben had it or other rookies that I had at Cleveland.\u201d<\/p>\n

Mitch Trubisky<\/h2>\n

NFL experience: 5 years<\/p>\n

Why he has a chance to be the starter: The Steelers added Trubisky on the first day of free agency, working quickly to bring in the former No. 2 pick.<\/p>\n

“He\u2019s young and experienced,” Tomlin said in March. “He’s won — to be quite honest with you — he’s probably won more than anybody else that was kind of in the field.”<\/p>\n

He spent the past year in Buffalo as Josh Allen\u2019s backup and saw action in six games, completing 6 of 8 attempts for 43 yards and an interception. But he said that year in Buffalo was valuable after a four-season stint in Chicago, where he flourished in his second year before struggling in his final two.<\/p>\n

Trubisky\u2019s strength in this quarterback competition is his veteran experience and a skill set that meshes well with Canada\u2019s scheme.<\/p>\n

Why he\u2019s vulnerable: Trubisky hasn\u2019t played meaningful minutes since 2020, and in his last season as Chicago\u2019s starter, he threw 16 touchdown passes to eight interceptions. He was also benched in Week 3 for Nick Foles, though Trubisky was eventually reinstated by Week 12. Still, he was inconsistent throughout his career with the Bears, and the work he\u2019s put in since has been mostly out of sight. The Steelers won\u2019t know for certain if the Jekyll-and-Hyde play is behind him until they get him on the field and in game situations.<\/p>\n

Mason Rudolph might have the longest odds of the three quarterbacks vying to start for the Steelers.\u00a0Shelley Lipton\/Icon Sportswire<\/p>\n

Mason Rudolph<\/h2>\n

NFL experience: 4 years<\/p>\n

Why he has a chance to be the starter: With four seasons in the organization, Rudolph is the known quantity of the group. He has the benefit of having worked with Canada for two years, first with Canada as his position coach and then as the Steelers\u2019 offensive coordinator. After Roethlisberger\u2019s retirement, Rudolph talked about getting an opportunity to lead the Steelers the way he wanted, suggesting that he\u2019ll have more freedom to be himself with Roethlisberger gone.<\/p>\n

Why he\u2019s vulnerable: Since a tumultuous start to his Steelers career in 2019, when he got thrown in the starting job after Roethlisberger\u2019s season-ending elbow injury in Week 2, Rudolph has largely been an average-to-below-average quarterback in spot starts. His best outing came against the Browns in Week 17 of the 2020 season when he completed 22 of 39 attempts for 315 yards and two touchdowns with an interception in a loss. Rudolph got another shot in 2021 when Roethlisberger tested positive for COVID-19 less than 24 hours before the Steelers played the Detroit Lions, and Rudolph was thrust into the starting job again. This time, he completed 30 of 50 attempts for 242 yards and one touchdown with one interception, and many of the throws were off target and inconsistent. He did have a 26-yard rush, showing off the mobility the Steelers are searching for in their next signal-caller.<\/p>\n

“Mason is 5-4-1 as a starter,” Colbert said in February. “One of those games this year, he found out he was going to be the starter Saturday night, and he tried to give us what he could. We almost won that game; we didn\u2019t. So, I\u2019m comfortable that Mason has won more games than he\u2019s lost as a starter. Where that can go, we\u2019ll see. To be an NFL starter you can win with, you\u2019ve got to prove it over 16 games, and I\u2019m anxious to see that, if that\u2019s the case.<\/p>\n

The bottom line is Rudolph is a serviceable quarterback, but Pickett and Trubisky have higher upsides and playmaking ability that figure to give them a leg up in the competition.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
