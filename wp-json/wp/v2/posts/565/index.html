One of the top script writers on \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy\u201d has been barred from the writers room in a strange twist that rivals the show\u2019s own melodramatic plot lines.<\/p>\n

Elisabeth Finch has been put on \u201cadministrative leave\u201d from penning the long-running ABC medical drama amid an investigation by Disney\u2019s human resources and legal departments, a production insider has confirmed to The Post.<\/p>\n

Some of Finch\u2019s personal medical traumas \u2014 which were written into the long-running Shonda Rhimes series \u2014 were allegedly fabricated, set sources told the Hollywood Reporter.<\/p>\n

The network and studio launched an internal inquiry into 44-year-old Finch\u2019s life in an attempt to determine if storylines such as her\u00a0cancer diagnosis\u00a0and abortion while doing chemotherapy were, in fact, the real deal.<\/p>\n

Bombshell allegations, first reported by the Ankler, cast doubt on previous reports that Finch was diagnosed with a rare form of bone cancer the year before\u00a0she was hired by \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy,\u201d forcing her to abort her fetus. The outlet also questioned her claims that she lost a kidney and part of her leg and had to undergo knee replacement surgery, only to learn that she had been misdiagnosed by a doctor whom\u00a0she later confronted.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Finch \u2014 who began writing for \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy\u201d in 2015 and also scripted episodes of the CW\u2019s\u00a0\u201cThe Vampire Diaries\u201d\u00a0and\u00a0HBO\u2019s \u201cTrue Blood\u201d \u2014 frequently published personal essays detailing her medical experiences for publications such as THR, Elle magazine and even Rhimes\u2019 own\u00a0Shondaland website. Many of these actually resulted in storylines on the Ellen Pompeo-starring drama.<\/p>\n

Several insiders have noted to THR that others began to doubt details of Finch\u2019s personal life after she claimed she had a family emergency and left the writers room to address the matter. <\/p>\n

Once her co-workers phoned Finch\u2019s wife, Jennifer Beyer, the other writers noticed discrepancies in the story Finch told her colleagues \u2014 which actually matched incidents from Beyer\u2019s own life, the outlet reported.<\/p>\n

Beyer later met with both Disney and Shondaland executives and raised similar concerns. It was then that Finch\u2019s alleged web of \u201clies\u201d started to unravel, the Daily Mail reported, and she was placed on administrative leave. A source noted that Finch and Beyer \u2014 who wed in 2020 \u2014 are going through an \u201cacrimonious divorce.\u201d<\/p>\n

Other industry associates explained to the outlet they have crossed paths with Finch over the years and are shocked by the rumors. While the colleagues are unsure about what the truth is, they cited a lack of a \u201csmoking gun\u201d to confirm speculation.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou believe this poor woman was going through this awful thing and you want to support her,\u201d one of Finch\u2019s longtime colleagues told THR.<\/p>\n

The embattled screenwriter\u2019s attorney, Andrew Brettler, of Lavely & Singer, said in a statement: \u201cMs. Finch will not disclose her private health matters. Likewise, she will not speak about her pending divorce from her estranged wife, Jennifer Beyer, or comment on any statements that Ms. Beyer may have made to third parties about Ms. Finch.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Post was unable to reach Beyer for comment. <\/p>\n
