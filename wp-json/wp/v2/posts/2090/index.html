A tech billionaire running for a US Senate seat from California is focusing on one issue only \u2014 Elon Musk and the \u201cunbelievably bad\u201d autonomous vehicles produced by his company, Tesla.<\/p>\n

Dan O\u2019Dowd, the billionaire founder and CEO of Green Hills Software who is spending his own money to fund a campaign, says he wants to ban Tesla\u2019s full self-driving features if he is elected. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe did not sign up our families to be Tesla crash test dummies,\u201d O\u2019Dowd said in announcing his candidacy on Tuesday for the seat currently held by Sen. Alex Padilla.<\/p>\n

\u201cElon Musk is pushing Tesla\u2019s defective Full Self-Driving technology, and it\u2019s threatening people across the country,\u201d according to O\u2019Dowd. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe government continues to allow Tesla to put unregulated, dangerous, and defective software on the street in the hands of 60,000 untrained drivers.\u201d<\/p>\n

O\u2019Dowd added: \u201cThis is a public safety threat and Congress must take immediate action.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out to Tesla seeking comment.<\/p>\n

O\u2019Dowd said he wants to \u201cmake computers safe for humanity\u201d and that his main issue is banning Full Self-Driving cars made by Tesla.Getty Images <\/p>\n

O\u2019Dowd told POLITICO that his would be a one-issue campaign, and that he declined to say if he truly intended on winning. <\/p>\n

\u201cI am going to win votes,\u201d he told POLITICO. \u201cAnd every vote I win is someone who thinks my issue is more important than [these] other issues.\u201d<\/p>\n

Observers believe his only motivation is to use First Amendment protections afforded to political candidates in order to make claims about Tesla products that would not get any television air time if he were a regular private citizen.<\/p>\n

Padilla, the incumbent Democrat who is heavily favored to hold onto the seat, was picked by California Gov. Gavin Newsom to fill the post after it was vacated by Kamala Harris, who became vice president.<\/p>\n

Democratic Party observers are nervous about O\u2019Dowd\u2019s candidacy because he could potentially siphon off enough votes to force a runoff, which could provide an opening for an unknown candidate that would threaten Padilla\u2019s status as the favorite.<\/p>\n

Tesla CEO Elon Musk has in the recent past acknowledged problems with FSD technology.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Unlike in other states that hold party primaries to determine the voters\u2019 preferred candidates, in the California system the top two vote-getters regardless of party advance to the runoff.<\/p>\n

Democratic Party officials in California are worried that a free-spending candidate like O\u2019Dowd would force them to devote precious resources to helping Padilla instead of shoring up battleground districts in the fight to keep control of the state legislature.<\/p>\n

Tesla\u2019s Full Self-Driving technology has been panned by critics, including Musk himself. The CEO admitted last year that the beta version of FSD was \u201cnot great.\u201d<\/p>\n

Earlier this year, Tesla was ordered by a German court to buy back a $76,000 Model 3 from a disgruntled customer who said the autopilot function was so bad that it mimicked a \u201cdrunk novice driver.\u201d<\/p>\n

In January, Tesla recalled 54,000 cars to disable an FSD feature that allowed vehicles to roll slowly through intersections without stopping.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
