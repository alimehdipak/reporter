Could Katherine Heigl finally be getting her redemption story?<\/p>\n

The former \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy\u201d actress \u2014 who was \u201cblacklisted\u201d from Hollywood amid claims she was \u201cdifficult\u201d and \u201cunprofessional\u201d \u2014 is now being praised by her former co-star, Ellen Pompeo.<\/p>\n

Heigl, 43, starred alongside Pompeo, 52, on six seasons of the ABC medical drama before she quit the series in 2010 after publicly complaining about long hours on set.<\/p>\n

\u201cI remember Heigl said something on a talk show about the insane hours we were working \u2014 and she was 100% right,\u201d Pompeo said Wednesday in a new episode of her podcast, \u201cTell Me With Ellen Pompeo.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cHad she said that today, she\u2019d be a complete hero,\u201d Pompeo continued. \u201cBut she\u2019s ahead of her time. [She] made a statement about our crazy hours and, of course, let\u2019s slam a woman and call her ungrateful.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Heigl and Pompeo are pictured together in 2007. The pair worked together on the first six seasons of \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy.\u201dWireImage <\/p>\n

Pompeo further said Heigl was \u201cf \u2013 \u2013 king ballsy\u201d for speaking out, adding that \u201cshe was telling the truth. She wasn\u2019t lying.\u201d<\/p>\n

Back in 2009, Heigl famously appeared on \u201cThe David Letterman Show\u201d, where she spoke about the long hours she was working on the Shonda Rhimes series.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m gonna keep saying this because I hope it embarrasses them,\u201d Heigl told Letterman, claiming she had just worked a \u201c17-hour day,\u201d which she thought was \u201ccruel and mean.\u201d<\/p>\n

Heigl and Pompeo are pictured on \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy\u201d with co-stars Justin Chambers and Sandra Oh.Disney General Entertainment Con <\/p>\n

Heigl exited the show the following year amid allegations that she was continually clashing with execs. <\/p>\n

However, the actress\u2019 career suffered in the wake of her \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy\u201d exit, with a producer telling the Hollywood Reporter in a 2013 article that she was \u201cdifficult\u201d and \u201cnot worth it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Last year, Heigl hit back in a profile with the Washington Post, discussing how her career had suffered because of her public complaints. <\/p>\n

Heigl has admitted her career suffered after she publicly complained about workplace conditions.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n
