Actress Liz Sheridan, famed for playing Jerry Seinfeld\u2019s sitcom mom and telling all about being James Dean\u2019s 1950s lover, has died at the age of 93.<\/p>\n

The star passed away from natural causes around 2:30 a.m. on Friday, TMZ reports. <\/p>\n

Sheridan starred as overprotective mom Helen Seinfeld in 21 episodes of \u201cSeinfeld\u201d from 1990 to 1998. <\/p>\n

She is the only \u201cSeinfeld\u201d star \u2014 apart from the four main leads \u2014 to be featured in all nine seasons of the hit sitcom. <\/p>\n

Seinfeld paid tribute to his \u201cTV mom\u201d on Twitter Friday afternoon.<\/p>\n

\u201cLiz was always the sweetest, nicest TV mom a son could wish for,\u201d he wrote. \u201cEvery time she came on our show it was the coziest feeling for me. So lucky to have known her.\u201d<\/p>\n

Her death comes less than two weeks after the passing of Estelle Harris \u2014 another famous \u201cSeinfeld\u201d star, who played George Costanza\u2019s mom. Like Sheridan, Harris was also 93. <\/p>\n

The actress also had a recurring role as Raquel Ochmonek in the NBC smash sitcom \u201cALF,\u201d which ran from 1986 \u2013 1990. <\/p>\n

Sheridan, who was born in New York, first began her career working as a dancer in Big Apple nightclubs, where she met Dean in her early twenties \u2014 shortly before his massive \u201cRebel Without a Cause\u201d and \u201cGiant\u201d film fame.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Liz Sheridan, Barney Martin, Julia Louis-Dreyfus and Jerry Seinfeld are pictured in an episode of the hit sitcom.Castle Rock Ent\/Everett Collection <\/p>\n

She claimed the pair enjoyed a short-lived romance in 1952, which Sheridan later chronicled in her 2000 memoir: \u201cDizzy & Jimmy: My Life with James Dean: A Love Story.\u201d<\/p>\n

Back in 1996, Sheridan described her relationship with \u201cEast of Eden\u201d heartthrob Dean in an interview with People magazine as \u201cjust kind of magical.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was the first love for both of us,\u201d she further stated, adding that they lived together in an apartment on the Upper West Side. <\/p>\n

The actress even claimed she was briefly engaged to the doomed Hollywood hunk, before his astronomical rise to fame sparked the end of their relationship. Dean died in a car crash less than three years later. <\/p>\n

Liz Sheridan was 93.WireImage <\/p>\n

Sheridan later transitioned to Broadway, where she appeared opposite Meryl Streep in the 1977 production of \u201cHappy End.\u201d<\/p>\n

Afterward, she relocated to Los Angeles where she set her sights on a screen career. <\/p>\n

Throughout the 1980s, she had cameo roles on TV hits such as \u201cSt. Elsewhere,\u201d \u201cMoonlighting\u201d and \u201cWho\u2019s The Boss.\u201d From 1986 \u2013 1990, she also appeared in 34 episode of \u201cALF.\u201d<\/p>\n

Then, in 1990, she scored the role of overprotective Helen in a new show named \u201cSeinfeld\u201d \u2014 which would go on to become her most famous role. <\/p>\n

Michael Richards as Cosmo Kramer and Liz Sheridan as Helen Seinfeld in \u201cSeinfeld\u201d Season 1 episode \u201cThe Stakeout.\u201dNBCUniversal via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Despite earning international recognition for her part in the iconic series, Sheridan later complained that she was unable to secure work, telling Entertainment Weekly in 1998 that she had become \u201cso associated\u201d with \u201cSeinfeld.\u201d<\/p>\n

In 2000, she published her book about her romance with Dean, which she was determined to turn into a movie. <\/p>\n

Back in 2008, she told Page Six that she wanted to get in touch with Seinfeld to \u201cask him to help me raise the money for the movie.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t even know how to get in touch with him,\u201d she stated. \u201cHe\u2019s got so many \u2018people\u2019 in front of him, you can\u2019t get to him. And I don\u2019t have his phone number.\u201d<\/p>\n

After her romance with Dean, Sheridan went on to find lasting love with jazz musician William Dale Wales. <\/p>\n

The pair wed in 1985, and were married until his death in 2003. Sheridan is survived by their daughter. <\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
