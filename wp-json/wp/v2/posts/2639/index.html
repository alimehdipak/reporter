NBC\u2019s \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d went way back in time to explain why the US Supreme Court is poised to overturn Roe v Wade.<\/p>\n

The show opened with a reenactment of the passage of a 13th Century British treatise cited by Justice Samuel Alito in his leaked draft opinion on the case.<\/p>\n

Host Benedict Cumberbatch and cast members James Austin Johnson and Andrew Dismukes portrayed royals who penned the Middle Ages anti-abortion law.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe should make a law that would stand the test of time so that hundreds and hundreds of years from now they\u2019ll look back and say no reason to change this at all they nailed it,\u201d English actor Cumberbatch said.<\/p>\n

Women that disobeyed the abortion ban would be forced to take a ship off the end of the world, or have sex with a donkey dressed as their husband, the nobles decided.<\/p>\n

Cecily Strong played a peasant woman who asked why penalizing abortion was a top priority.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t understand why everyone\u2019s obsessed with this issue. No one can read or write and everyone\u2019s dying of plague,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

In another nod to current events, Dismukes refused to wear a mask even though he had the infectious disease\u00a0that wiped out 20 million Europeans.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy body, my choice,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Cumberbatch kicked off the Mother\u2019s Day\u2019s episode with a heartfelt tribute to his wife and mom, before joking that being a mother isn\u2019t as hard as opening portals in Marvel\u2019s multiverse.<\/p>\n

In the first post-monologue skit, Aidy Bryant played a mom who received a series of bric-\u00e0-brac wooden signs in bad taste from her extended family.<\/p>\n

Other signs made jokes out of a supposed severe case of maternal alcoholism.<\/p>\n

\u201cOh God, it\u2019s wine o\u2019clock I just love watching the sun rise,\u201d one read.<\/p>\n

\u201cI only drink on days that end in Y and during hours that have numbers in them,\u201d read another one.<\/p>\n

It was revealed the misdeeds spanned several generations when stern grandmother Kate McKinnon was seen throwing her underwear at David Bowie in the 70s.<\/p>\n

The skit ended with a sweet tagline: \u201cYou may not have been a perfect person but you\u2019re a perfect mom.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWeekend Update\u201d dedicated most of the fake news segment to the Roe v Wade decision, which was leaked by \u201cone bad apple.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cOne bad apple is also another legal argument used in Alito\u2019s opinion,\u201d Colin Jost cracked as an over-the-shoulder graphic depicted Adam and Eve\u2019s illicit apple tree.<\/p>\n

Michael Che said an abortion ban would disproportionally affect poor people.<\/p>\n

\u201cMost Americans don\u2019t have access to the same resources that I do,\u201d Che said.<\/p>\n

\u201cI mean the average person can\u2019t just text [\u2018SNL\u2019 Executive Producer] Lorne [Michaels] in the middle of the night and be like \u2018yo it happened again,’\u201d he joked.<\/p>\n

McKinnon sat on the anchor desk as Justice Amy Coney Barrett, and fielded question\u2019s about the court\u2019s decision.<\/p>\n

\u201cI listened to the case with an open mind, and I asked all my question,\u201d she said referencing a singular concern about safe haven laws.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou can leave a baby anywhere in the United States, so what\u2019s the big deal? Just pop it,\u201d McKinnon-as-Barrett said.<\/p>\n
