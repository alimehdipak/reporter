Long before RuPaul had them lip-syncing for their lives on \u201cRuPaul\u2019s Drag Race,\u201d she changed the life of legendary New York club director Steve Adelman.<\/p>\n

Adelman had booked RuPaul to perform at a club in Boston and she had such a good time that she went back to NYC and recommended him to the assistant of the owner of the Roxy, just as the West 18th Street club was opening up.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut I think she highly over-exaggerated my credentials,\u201d said Adelman, whose new memoir \u201cNocturnal Admissions: Behind the Scenes at Tunnel, Limelight, Avalon, and Other Legendary Nightclubs,\u201d was released this week. \u201cSo I appear at the Roxy with a hockey duffel bag, and I\u2019m anointed director.\u201d<\/p>\n

That twist \u2014 or, make that twirl \u2014 of fate in 1989 led to Adelman suddenly being a professional partymeister overseeing the dance-floor revelry and debauchery of New York nightlife\u2019s glory days in the \u201990s. After his nights and, no doubt, early mornings bumping shoulders with celebs such as Madonna, Liza Minnelli and \u201cthe Three Amigos\u201d \u2014 that\u2019s supermodels Naomi Campbell, Linda Evangelista and Kate Moss \u2014 he went on to serve as director for the Limelight, Tunnel, Palladium and Club USA. In fact, during his club-hopping heyday, he was director for all four of the iconic NYC nightlife institutions at once.<\/p>\n

RuPaul made the connection that landed \u201cNocturnal Admissions\u201d author Steve Adelman his job at the Roxy.CREDIT: Alexis DiBiasio, Courtes <\/p>\n

\u201cI had to sort of learn on the fly because I really didn\u2019t know that much,\u201d said Adelman. <\/p>\n

One promoter he learned from was Susanne Bartsch, the nightlife impresario who helped to get the Roxy rocking in its early days and became a fabulous fixture of the \u201990s club scene \u2014 which she says provided a euphoric escape after the worst of the AIDS epidemic.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was time to go celebrate because we are alive and we are living life,\u201d said Bartsch, who still has her On Top! party at Le Bain on Tuesday nights this summer. \u201cIn a way, it was almost like an art gallery, so people would go and show off their looks. And it became a place to be discovered really.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The infamous swing at the Roxy provided a fun and fabulous photo op.Tina Paul, Courtesy “Nocturnal A <\/p>\n

One of those nightlife stars, notorious Club Kids leader Michael Alig, brought Adelman over from the Roxy to meet mega-club owner Peter Gatien, who hired him to start working at the Limelight. <\/p>\n

Adelman says that even his grandmother got caught up with Alig and the Club Kids phenomenon.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy grandma came to visit me when I was there in, like, the first month,\u201d he said. \u201cAnd so all the kids pull her out on the dance floor, and she\u2019s dancing around. And Michael Alig was trying to hire her to come back and book her as \u2018Disco Grandma.\u2019 And I\u2019m like, \u2018No, no, no, no. It\u2019s not gonna happen.\u2019 \u201d <\/p>\n

Photographer Franceso Scavullo, Liza Minnelli and designer Isaac Mizrahi at the Roxy.CREDIT: Tina Paul, Courtesy “Noc <\/p>\n

The over-the-top decadence didn\u2019t last as former Mayor Rudy Giuliani sought to bring down the raging club scene following his election in 1994.<\/p>\n

\u201cBefore Giuliani started interfering with us, it was spontaneous,\u201d said nightlife veteran Gregory Homs, who served as the creative director for flyers, posters, advertising and other communications for various clubs. \u201cIt was like jazz in the sense that someone would come up with an idea for a party and then it would happen in three days.\u201d<\/p>\n

Homs recalled one particular experience that signaled the end of an era was coming. \u201cI had a couple of FBI guys show up at my office, and they confiscated a lot of my invitation samples because they were looking for secret messages \u2026 that were trying to secretly communicate that drugs were available.\u00a0And so they sat there for hours going through invitations.\u201d<\/p>\n

Michael Alig and his fellow Club Kids at the Limelight.CREDIT: Tina Paul, Courtesy “Noc <\/p>\n

Soon everything came crashing down. Alig would serve time for killing fellow Club Kid Angel Melendez over a fight about drugs, dismembering his body and tossing it in the Hudson River in 1996. In 1999, Gatien was imprisoned on a tax-evasion rap.<\/p>\n

Britney Spears at Avalon, where she performed a \u201cvery bizarre\u201d striptease for clubgoers.Courtesy “Nocturnal Admissions” <\/p>\n

Still, Adelman would have his own New York club revival when he bought Avalon, at the site of the old Limelight, which was open from 2002 to 2008. He had a very special guest performer one night in Britney Spears.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cShe was going around different clubs and promoting an album,\u201d he said. \u201cShe did this very bizarre, half-thought-out striptease, an impromptu kind of thing. It was very odd.
