<\/p>\n

NASA is always on the lookout for new planets.<\/p>\n

Scientists at the US space agency and other institutions across the globe hope to find habitable exoplanets that host life or could support us in the future.<\/p>\n

Nasa uses its Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) to make a lot of its discoveries.<\/p>\n

It finds exoplanets, meaning planets outside our Solar System, by measuring if light is dimmed when a suspected planet passes across a star.<\/p>\n

This can be a long and tedious process as several transits of a planet may be needed in order to confirm its existence.<\/p>\n

The US space agency has found quite a few planets this way and\u00a0Space.com\u00a0highlighted ten of the most interesting examples.<\/p>\n

Brown dwarf planet<\/h2>\n

Brown dwarfs are celestial objects that aren\u2019t technically stars or planets.<\/p>\n

This is why it was so unusual when Nasa\u2019s Hubble Space Telescope spotted a planet orbiting a brown dwarf called CFHTWIR-Oph 98.<\/p>\n

An artist\u2019s conception illustrates the brown dwarf, CFHTWIR-Oph 98 b. NASA\/JPL-Caltech <\/p>\n

Planet with two suns<\/h2>\n

Nasa used TESS to detect a planet called TIC 172900988 b.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s unusual because it orbits two stars.<\/p>\n

That\u2019s like Earth orbiting two suns.<\/p>\n

Proxima Centauri C<\/h2>\n

You may have heard of this exoplanet before.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s popular because it orbits a star called Proxima Centauri, which is the closest star to our Sun.<\/p>\n

It was spotted in 2016 and is 4.25-light-years away.<\/p>\n

Proxima Centauri C is said to be a rocky exoplanet but it\u2019s not thought to be habitable.<\/p>\n

Boiling super-Earth<\/h2>\n

A super-Earth is a rocky planet several times the mass of our own world.<\/p>\n

Super-Earth HD 108236 b is over three times the mass of our planet.<\/p>\n

A year on the planet is technically 3.8 days because it orbits its star so quickly.<\/p>\n

We wouldn\u2019t be able to survive on this \u2018super-Earth\u2019 because the surface temperature is estimated at 1,500 degrees Fahrenheit (825 degrees Celsius).<\/p>\n

This 11-Jupiter-mass exoplanet called HD106906 b occupies an unlikely orbit around a double star 336 light-years away.ESA\/Hubble, M. Kornmesser <\/p>\n

An odd orbiter<\/h2>\n

Exoplanet HD 106906 was spotted in 2013 but Nasa didn\u2019t realize it had such a strange orbit until 2020.<\/p>\n

The planet is about 68 billion miles from its star.<\/p>\n

That means one year on the planet is like 15,000 Earth years.<\/p>\n

Recent find<\/h2>\n

Exoplanet OGLE-2019-BLG-0960 b was only spotted last year.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s a rocky planet and is about 1.4 and 3.1 times the mass of Earth.<\/p>\n

Exoplanet TIC 172900988 b is unusual because it orbits two stars.NASA\/JPL-Caltech <\/p>\n

Deep in space<\/h2>\n

The next unusual exoplanet on the list was actually discovered in a different galaxy.<\/p>\n

A lot of exoplanets are found in the Milky Way but planet M51-ULS-1b was found in the Whirlpool Galaxy in 2020.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s 28 million light-years away so we won\u2019t be visiting any time soon.<\/p>\n

Wobbling planet<\/h2>\n

Exoplanet TVLM 513B was only spotted because of its tiny wobble.<\/p>\n

Scientists used a method called astrometry, which uses very precise measurements to spot orbiting planets.<\/p>\n

Hell on Earth<\/h2>\n

Exoplanet GJ 367 b was announced in December 2021.<\/p>\n

It orbits its star in just eight hours and its temperature is said to be 2,700 \u00b0F (1,500 \u00b0C) in its daytime.<\/p>\n

Exoplanet GJ 376 b orbits its star in just eight hours.NASA\/ ESA\/ G. Bacon (STScI)\/ MIT <\/p>\n

Earth-size exoplanet<\/h2>\n

This Earth-size exoplanet was discovered in 2015.<\/p>\n

You wouldn\u2019t want to live on planet GJ 1132.<\/p>\n

Nasa\u2019s Hubble Space Telescope indicates that it has a brutal secondary atmosphere containing cyanide and methane.<\/p>\n
