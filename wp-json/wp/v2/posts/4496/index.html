Ivana Trump \u2014 Donald Trump\u2019s legendary first wife \u2014 was found dead in her New York City home Thursday. <\/p>\n

The 73-year-old Czech American,\u00a0ex-wife of the former president\u00a0and mother of Ivanka, Eric and Don Jr., is believed to have suffered cardiac arrest, authorities told The Post, but her official cause of death has yet to be determined. <\/p>\n

\u201cOur mother was an incredible woman \u2014 a force in business, a world-class athlete, a radiant beauty\u00a0and caring mother\u00a0and friend,\u201d Eric Trump said in a statement announcing her death. \u201cShe will be dearly missed by her mother, her three children and 10 grandchildren.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ivana was Donald\u2019s first wife \u2014 and he was her second husband \u2014 and the two married in 1977. Their 15-year union ended in a messy public divorce in 1992 when it was revealed that Donald had been having an affair with actress and model Marla Maples. <\/p>\n

Ivana married two more times after Donald, but the pair remained friends throughout the years, resulting in her relevance in pop culture and interviews \u2014 in which she often made quite interesting statements. <\/p>\n

Here are some of her more remarkable moments that brought Ivana even more notoriety than being married to Donald. <\/p>\n

Ivana Trump, ex-wife of Donald Trump, dropped many memorable statements over the years.Getty Images <\/p>\n

She won\u2019t say \u2018Marla Maples\u2019<\/h2>\n

When Donald was in office, Ivana joked that she was the real first lady since she married the billionaire before his current wife, Melania. While Ivana said she didn\u2019t have much of a relationship with the current Mrs. Trump, she was at least able to say her name in an interview with the Atlantic in 2019 \u2014 something she couldn\u2019t do for Donald\u2019s mistress-turned-second wife Maples. <\/p>\n

\u201c[Ivanka] likes [Melania] fine,\u201d Ivana said about her daughter\u2019s relationship with her stepmother. \u201cBecause she didn\u2019t cause me to break up the marriage like the other one \u2014 I don\u2019t even want to pronounce her name,\u201d Ivana\u00a0quipped. <\/p>\n

Donald got together with Maples while he was still married to Ivana in 1992. The affair had reportedly been going on since 1989 and resulted in Donald\u2019s first divorce in 1992. <\/p>\n

Trump and then-girlfriend Marla Maples in New York City in 1992.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Her cameo in \u2018The First Wives Club\u2019<\/h2>\n

Ivana turned the real-life messiness she experienced through the divorce into a quick movie role in \u201cThe First Wives Club.\u201d She didn\u2019t say much in the flick, but she said just enough to ruffle Donald\u2019s feathers. <\/p>\n

\u201cLadies, you have to be strong and independent,\u201d she advised actresses Goldie Hawn, Diane Keaton and Bette Midler in a key scene. \u201cAnd remember, don\u2019t get mad \u2014 get everything!\u201d<\/p>\n

Donald was not tickled by his ex-wife\u2019s cameo, later telling shock jock Howard Stern that she was \u201cout of control\u201d for taking part in the 1996 movie. He claimed she did, in fact, take everything when they divorced.<\/p>\n

From left, Diane Keaton, Goldie Hawn and Bette Midler on the set of \u201cThe First Wives Club\u201d in 1996. Paramount <\/p>\n

Ivana backtracked on accusing Donald of rape <\/h2>\n

Ivana and Donald\u2019s divorce was granted on the grounds of \u201ccruel and inhuman treatment\u201d of Ivana. In 2015, the Daily Beast published a report saying that she accused him in court documents of raping her during their marriage. <\/p>\n

Donald\u2019s lawyer, Michael Cohen, responded to Daily Beast reporter Tim Mak, threatening that he would \u201cmess his life up\u201d if he wrote the story about the rape allegations, adding \u201cyou cannot rape your spouse.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ivana seemingly backtracked on her statement, suggesting she made the claim out of anger.<\/p>\n

\u201cI have recently read some comments attributed to me from nearly 30 years ago at a time of very high tension during my divorce from Donald. The story is totally without merit,\u201d Ivana said in a\u00a0statement\u00a0to CNN. \u201cDonald and I are the best of friends and together have raised three children that we love and are very proud of. I have nothing but fondness for Donald and wish him the best of luck on his campaign. Incidentally, I think he would make an incredible president.\u201d<\/p>\n

Donald and Ivana at the Met Gala in 1985.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Becoming New York City\u2019s top real estate developer didn\u2019t leave Donald much time with his three children. Ivana took sole custody of the couple\u2019s kids when she and Donald split 15 years into their marriage. <\/p>\n

\u201cHe did not know how to speak the children\u2019s language. He was not able to do it until the kids were in university,\u201d Ivana told John Catsimatidis on AM970\u2019s \u201cThe Answer\u201d in an interview.<\/p>\n

\u201cI told Donald, \u2018There can only be one chef in the kitchen,\u2019 and it was me,\u201d she recalled.<\/p>\n

Donald and Ivana with their three children \u2014 Eric, Ivanka and Don Jr. \u2014 in 1986.Norman Parkinson \/ Iconic Images <\/p>\n

It was Ivana\u2019s idea for Donald to start using Twitter<\/h2>\n

Donald and Ivana\u2019s marriage was way over by the time he became president in 2017. But that didn\u2019t mean his ex-wife wouldn\u2019t give him advice on what he should do as commander-in-chief. <\/p>\n

In fact, Ivana claimed to be the person who suggested that the Republican president should start using Twitter. <\/p>\n

\u201cI said, \u2018I think you should tweet. It\u2019s a new way, a new technology,\u2019 \u201d she said in a 2017 interview with \u201cCBS Sunday Morning.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201c\u2018And if you want to get your words across rightly, without telling the New York Times, which is going to twist every single word of yours, this is how you get your message out,\u201d\u2019 she added. <\/p>\n

However, Donald was expelled from the social media platform in 2021 \u201cdue to the risk of further incitement of violence\u201d after the Jan. 6 insurrection at the US Capitol. <\/p>\n

Ivana at her home on East 64th Street in Manhattan.Brian Zak\/NY Post <\/p>\n

Ivana claimed she never gained weight \u2014 but couldn\u2019t stop Donald from doing it<\/h2>\n

Like former first lady Michelle Obama, Ivana was passionate about ending obesity in America \u2014 although she claimed to never have worried about that herself. <\/p>\n

\u201cI was always very disciplined \u2014 I didn\u2019t gain weight my whole life,\u201d the then-69-year-old told The Post in 2018. <\/p>\n

However, that didn\u2019t mean she never<\/em> had a cheat day. She was caught paying for street meat at a New York City cart in 2018. <\/p>\n

\u201cI dream of it,\u201d she said of the occasional frankfurter. \u201cI have it once a year, and I\u2019ll have it and won\u2019t eat anything for two days.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThere\u2019s a street vendor on my block and I get a hot dog once a month \u2014 no bread,\u201d she told The Post. \u201cIt\u2019s New York \u2014 live and let live.\u201d<\/p>\n

But when it came to her ex-husband\u2019s growing waistband, Ivana said Donald wouldn\u2019t accept her help. <\/p>\n
