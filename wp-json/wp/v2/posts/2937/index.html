Fred Ward, the veteran character actor best known for his diverse roles in \u201cTremors,\u201d \u201cThe Right Stuff\u201d and Robert Altman\u2019s \u201cShort Cuts,\u201d has died. He was 79.<\/p>\n

A rep for the \u201cExit Speed\u201d star confirmed to The Post that he died last weekend.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am sad to announce the passing of acclaimed actor Fred Ward, who passed away on Sunday, May 8, 2022, at age 79,\u201d the statement read. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe Golden Globe winner, actor and producer is best known for \u2018The Right Stuff,\u2019 \u2018Remo Williams: The Adventure Begins,\u2019 \u2018Tremors,\u2019 \u2018Miami Blues,\u2019 \u2018Henry & June,\u2019 \u2018The Player\u2019 and \u2018Short Cuts.’\u201d<\/p>\n

Ward\u2019s rep Ron Hofmann told The Post his cause of death would not be revealed at this time \u2014 but they did share his last wishes: <\/p>\n

\u201cIt was Fred Ward\u2019s wish that any memorial tributes be made in the form of donations to the Boston University Chronic Traumatic Encephalopathy Center. Please visit https:\/\/bit.ly\/BUSMCTE or contact 617-358-9535 for more information.\u201d<\/p>\n

News of his passing circulated Friday on Twitter with Rolling Stone editor Alan Sepinwall calling out Ward\u2019s beloved cult status.<\/p>\n

\u201cRIP, Fred Ward, a great character actor whose big shot at leading man status, Remo Williams: The Adventure Begins, unfortunately flopped, but who kept giving great, lived-in performances for the rest of his career,\u201d he wrote.<\/p>\n

Film critic Robert Daniels also shared condolences, writing: \u201cFred Ward was absurdly good. A professional in everything he did from TREMORS to THE PLAYER. He could even turn in a great performance is something as patently dumb as JOE DIRT. Totally underrated.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ward began his career in 1979 alongside Clint Eastwood as his jailbreak buddy in \u201cEscape From Alcatraz.\u201d Before his breakout role, the San Diego native performed in cabarets in Europe and Northern Africa, Turner Classic Movies reported, before making his way to Los Angeles, where he supported himself by selling jewelry on the street.<\/p>\n
