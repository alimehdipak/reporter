<\/p>\n

Elon Musk compared himself to the foul-mouthed rapper Eminem in a court filing, alleging that the Securities and Exchange Commission is running roughshod over his First Amendment rights as it clamps down on his Twitter habit.<\/p>\n

The billionaire Tesla CEO\u2019s Tuesday legal salvo cites Eminem and his song \u201cWithout Me,\u201d whose lyrics include \u201cThe FCC won\u2019t let me be or let me be me\u201d \u2014 a lament over a $7,000 penalty that the Federal Communications Commission imposed in 2002 on a Colorado radio station, Citadel Broadcasting, for playing Eminem\u2019s profanity-laden ditty \u201cThe Real Slim Shady\u201d.<\/p>\n

In Musk\u2019s Tuesday filing in the US Southern District of New York, his lawyers quote Eminem\u2019s lyrics \u2014 inserting \u201cSEC\u201d in brackets in place of FCC \u2014 and note that the FCC ultimately rescinded its penalty, ruling that \u201cthe First Amendment is a critical Constitutional limitation that demands we proceed cautiously and with appropriate restraint.\u201d<\/p>\n

At issue is a request by the SEC \u2014 headed by hard-charging Chair Gary Gensler \u2014 for a wide-ranging subpoena covering his tweets from April 30, 2019 to the present to determine whether Musk received pre-approval for each of them by a Tesla lawyer before publication. <\/p>\n

SEC Chair Gary GenslerREUTERS <\/p>\n

The Twitter regulations were imposed as part of a 2018 consent decree to settle a flap over Musk\u2019s\u00a0now-infamous Aug. 7, 2018 tweet,\u00a0in which he claimed he had \u201cfunding secured\u201d to take Tesla private at $420 a share. Musk \u2014 who said at the time that the $420 price was a pot joke to impress his then-girlfriend Grimes \u2014 has recently claimed that he did indeed have backing from Saudi investors to fund a buyout of Tesla.<\/p>\n

Musk is asking US Judge Alison Nathan to quash the subpoena and terminate the first-of-its kind consent decree. In a court filing last week, the SEC claimed that Musk\u2019s lawyer, Alex Spiro of Quinn Emanuel, told the agency on Feb. 25 that Musk would not produce any documents regarding pre-approval or review of his tweets. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe intermittent nature of the SEC\u2019s harassment underscores the [consent decree\u2019s] chilling effect because it is impossible to know ex ante what tweets will raise the Commission\u2019s ire,\u201d Musk\u2019s lawyer wrote in the Tuesday filing. \u201cEven the SEC seems unsure of what tweets require preclearance, leaving only two conclusions: the consent decree is unduly vague or the agency is pursuing its investigation in bad faith.\u201d<\/p>\n

Eminem performs at the halftime show of Super Bowl LVI at SoFi Stadium on Feb. 13.EPA <\/p>\n

The SEC is reportedly investigating whether November stock sales by Musk or his brother, Kimbal, a day before he polled Twitter users about whether he should offload 10 percent of his Tesla stake violated insider-trading rules.<\/p>\n

Last week, the SEC in asking the New York Federal Court to enforce the subpoena said it disagreed with Musk\u2019s First Amendment arguments.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis argument ignores the critical fact that Musk \u2014 represented by experienced, capable counsel \u2014 voluntarily consented to the entry of the court orders that established the preapproval requirement.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cMusk cannot now cast [this] off simply because he has found complying with Tesla\u2019s procedures to be less convenient than he had hoped, or because he wishes the SEC would not investigate whether Tesla\u2019s disclosure controls and procedures are actually being maintained and followed,\u201d the SEC added.<\/p>\n

Judge Nathan is expected to hold a hearing on the matter in the next few weeks, sources said.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
