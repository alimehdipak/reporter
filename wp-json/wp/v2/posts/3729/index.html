The von Trapp children are back together again after almost 60 years apart.<\/p>\n

The child stars who played the characters in the 1965 musical \u201cThe Sound of Music\u201d reunited to honor friend and star Julie Andrews at the 48th AFI Life Achievement Award Gala Tribute on Thursday.<\/p>\n

Andrews, 86, appeared at the event in Los Angeles to receive her lifetime award.<\/p>\n

Nicholas Hammond, who played Friedrich in the musical,\u00a0as well as Duane Chase\u00a0(Kurt),\u00a0Angela Cartwright\u00a0(Brigitta),\u00a0Debbie Turner\u00a0(Marta) and\u00a0Kym Karath\u00a0(Gretl) all strolled the red carpet together at the Dolby Theatre to support Andrews (who portrayed Maria von Trapp).<\/p>\n

The group even belted out \u201cDo-Re-Mi\u201d and took photos with the \u201cPrincess Diaries\u201d alum. <\/p>\n

The movie\u2019s other stars, Charmian\u00a0Carr\u00a0and\u00a0Heather Menzies-Urich, who portrayed Liesl and Louisa von Trapp in the movie, passed away in 2016 and 2017.<\/p>\n

The stars who played members of the singing von Trapp kids 57 years ago in \u201cThe Sound of Music\u201d honored Julie Andrews on Thursday.Chris Pizzello\/Invision\/AP <\/p>\n

Christopher Plummer \u2014 the legendary Captain von Trapp \u2014 died in February 2021 at the age of 91.<\/p>\n

Many of the iconic British actress\u2019 old pals and co-stars such as \u201cPrincess Diaries\u201d star H\u00e9ctor Elizondo also appeared throughout the evening. Princess Mia Thermopolis herself, Anne Hathaway, sent in a lovely recorded message to pay tribute to Andrews.<\/p>\n

Her \u201cMary Poppins\u201d co-star Dick Van Dyke also relayed a message via video. <\/p>\n

Other iconic stars who made appearances included Jane Seymour, Steve Carell,\u00a0Fran Drescher,\u00a0Blake Shelton\u00a0and\u00a0Gwen Stefani.<\/p>\n

Andrews and Christopher Plummer as Maria and Captain von Trapp in the 1965 musical \u201cThe Sound of Music.\u201d Bettmann Archive Debbie Turner, Angela Cartwright, Nicholas Hammond, Duane Chase and Kym Karath attended the ceremony honoring Andrews on Thursday.Chris Pizzello\/Invision\/AP <\/p>\n

Andrews was presented with the prestigious award by comedian Carol Burnett.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis night reminds me with great clarity how many people are involved with making movies,\u201d Andrews said in her acceptance speech. <\/p>\n

\u201cWhat a huge collaborative effort it takes to bring film to the screen. My husband Blake [Edwards] never liked when people referred to filmmaking as the business or an industry. He insisted that film was an art form and should always be called that. And I know that is exactly the way the AFI feels also,\u201d Andrews added.<\/p>\n

The \u201cMary Poppins\u201d star happily accepted the life achievement award at the AFI gala at the Dolby Theatre in LA.Chris Pizzello\/Invision\/AP <\/p>\n

Cynthia Erivo performed a musical tribute to the \u201cVictor\/Victoria\u201d actress \u2014 singing the song \u201cEdelweiss\u201d from \u201cThe Sound of Music.\u201d Broadway alums like Ariana DeBose, Hugh Jackman, Kristin Chenoweth and Lin-Manuel Miranda also sent recorded messages to honor Andrews.<\/p>\n
