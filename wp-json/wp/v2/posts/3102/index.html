We all know the famous movie quote: \u201cNobody puts Baby in a corner with a CGI replica of the late Patrick Swayze.\u201d<\/p>\n

OK, that\u2019s not the exact phrase, but it is nonetheless a distinct \u2014 and mortifying \u2014 possibility for the forthcoming sequel to the 1987 classic \u201cDirty Dancing.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Swayze, who played sexy Catskills dancer Johnny Castle and died of pancreatic cancer in 2009, could return for the sequel in a digitized form \u2014 completely unbeknownst to him, and potentially unbearable for us.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019re going to try to involve as many people from the original as is appropriate,\u201d the director Jonathan Levine told Deadline from the Cannes Film Festival, adding that the production is in conversation with Swayze\u2019s estate. \u201cJohnny is a part of Baby\u2019s journey in the story.\u201d<\/p>\n

One read of that and I can\u2019t disguise \u2014 I\u2019ve got ANGRY EYES!<\/p>\n

While Levine\u2019s exact intentions and artistic vision are unclear \u2014 he did respond to a request for comment \u2014 one can\u2019t help but wonder if 62-year-old Jennifer Grey, who memorably played Baby in the original, will somehow preposterously be made to cha-cha with Swayze from beyond the grave. <\/p>\n

Swayze and Jennifer Grey sizzled as Baby and Johnny in 1987\u2019s \u201cDirty Dancing.\u201d\u00a9Vestron Pictures\/Courtesy Ever <\/p>\n

If the film\u2019s plan was to simply flashback to old footage from the original \u2014 here we are balancing on a log! You\u2019re tickling my arm! \u2014 sources say there would be little point in communicating with the estate about something so common.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThe film company owns the images,\u201d said a producer about the archival footage.<\/p>\n

Another entertainment big shot agrees that the way the news was delivered sounds sneaky.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cYou have to wonder why, if they were going to make this kind of announcement, why they didn\u2019t at least enlist a representative from the estate to make this statement in partnership,\u201d said a film executive who has worked closely with Lionsgate and other studios.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Lionsgate did not respond to request for comment.<\/p>\n

Lionsgate is in talks with the Swayze estate to incorporate the actor into a new sequel. Artisan Entertainment\/ Courtesy: Everett Collection <\/p>\n

The director\u2019s vague niceties sound alarmingly familiar in this sad era of tech gone loco. When actor Peter Cushing was electronically exhumed to play Grand Moff Tarkin in \u201cRogue One: A Star Wars Story\u201d \u2014 looking like a sullen Gumby \u2014 the visual effects supervisor told the Guardian, \u201cthis work was done with a great deal of affection and care.\u201d What a crock.<\/p>\n

A few years later when the \u201cStar Wars Episode IX: The Rise of Skywalker\u201d team awkwardly wedged in archival footage of the late Carrie Fisher in a totally new context, plopped in a forest, J.J. Abrams told The Post, \u201cI think if she had lived, and you were to sort of squint your eyes, it would be the same movie.\u201d No way, J.J.<\/p>\n

We\u2019re lectured over and over again by these dystopian funeral directors that, in turning deceased stars into 2003 screensavers, we\u2019re in fact honoring their legacies. That the doofy doppelg\u00e4ngers are lasting monuments that the actors probably, maybe, kinda would have approved of. No, cynics, Hollywood isn\u2019t making a quick buck off long-gone celebs\u2019 names and likeness at all! <\/p>\n

Grey, now 62, will return as Baby in the sequel to \u201cDirty Dancing.\u201dGetty Images <\/p>\n

One can only marvel at Tinseltown\u2019s proud soullessness. Even James Dean, who died in 1955 at age 24, is said to be headed back to the big screen in the new movie \u201cFinding Jack\u201d thanks to sophisticated new animation techniques. (Dean replaces Elvis Presley, whose estate wisely said no to the stunt.) Just what Dean dreamed of as he was studying at the Actors Studio during a time when talking 3D holograms were practically witchcraft \u2014 being a bucket \u2018o pixels and an algorithm.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s still early enough for Lionsgate (whose god-awful flops \u201cMoonfall\u201d and \u201cChaos Walking\u201d inspire as much confidence as a century-old rope bridge over a canyon) to pick a route that doesn\u2019t make viewers want to bury their heads in their popcorn: a couple of flashback scenes from the 1987 flick paired with a whole new young couple. (The best choice would be to make no sequel at all, but fat chance. It\u2019s one of the studio\u2019s most lucrative properties.) Grey said she\u2019d love to get Harry Styles for the movie. Fine!<\/p>\n
