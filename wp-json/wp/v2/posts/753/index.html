Your iPhone tracks your real-world movements and you can view where you\u2019ve been on a hidden map.<\/p>\n

The little-known feature is buried deep within your Settings and shows your most frequently visited locations over the past few months.<\/p>\n

The feature was launched a few years ago and was the subject of a recent viral TikTok video.<\/p>\n

User @techhackguy brought the feature to the attention of thousands of people, claiming that iPhones are \u201cspying on everyone.\u201d<\/p>\n

What is iPhone Significant Locations?<\/h2>\n

Your iPhone has a feature called Location Services, which uses your location to improve various features on the device.<\/p>\n

This includes Significant Locations, a rolling log of your real-world movements, which are then used to offer other services.<\/p>\n

For instance, if your iPhone knows about your daily commute, it can provide tailored timing information about your journey.<\/p>\n

\u201cAllow your iPhone to learn places significant to you in order to provide useful location-related information in Maps, Calendar, Photos and more,\u201d Apple explains.<\/p>\n

A little-known iPhone feature provides a list of your most frequently visited locations.TikTok <\/p>\n

Is iPhone Significant Locations safe?<\/h2>\n

You might be panicking that Apple is keeping a log of your every move, but that\u2019s not quite the case.<\/p>\n

\u201cSignificant Locations are encrypted and cannot be read by Apple,\u201d according to Apple.<\/p>\n

That means that the location info is stored on your iPhone, and is encrypted and dissociated from you if it needs to leave the device.<\/p>\n

No outsiders can see your Significant Locations, and they\u2019re not accessible through iCloud either.<\/p>\n

And even if a pal is using your iPhone, they can\u2019t get into Significant Locations without getting past a Face ID or Touch ID lock first.<\/p>\n

However, if you\u2019re really worried that a spouse might sneak their way onto your iPhone and catch you at the pub when you were supposed to be at work, it might be worth deactivating the system \u2013 or clearing your history at the very least. We\u2019ll explain how to do that below.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s also worth mentioning that Significant Locations is opt-in, although you probably never knew that.<\/p>\n

It turns on when you activate Location Services during iPhone set-up, but it\u2019s buried beneath several layers of Settings, so most people have no idea it exists.<\/p>\n

The location data tracks back around a year.<\/p>\n

How to find your iPhone Location Map<\/h2>\n

First, launch the Settings app on your iPhone, then tap on the Privacy section.<\/p>\n

Then click on Location Services and scroll to the very bottom of the page, where you\u2019ll need to tap on System Services.<\/p>\n

The Significant Locations feature can be turned off in the settings.TikTok <\/p>\n

Go to the Significant Locations tab, at which point you\u2019ll be asked to log in with Face ID, Touch ID or your passcode.<\/p>\n

You should see a long list of cities, which you can tap into to find various locations you\u2019ve previously been at.<\/p>\n

All of these locations are time-stamped, and are included on a map at the top of the screen.<\/p>\n

It may even highlights your \u2018Home\u2019 and \u2018Work\u2019, which should be your most-visited destinations.<\/p>\n

How to delete iPhone Significant Locations<\/h2>\n

There are a few ways to clean up your Significant Locations on the iPhone.<\/p>\n

The first is to simply toggle Significant Locations off when you\u2019re in the correct menu.<\/p>\n

You can also hit the Clear History button inside Significant Locations, which deletes your locations collected so far.<\/p>\n

Alternatively, you can toggle Location Services off entirely, although this means you\u2019ll lose out on some really useful mapping features.<\/p>\n

We\u2019ve asked Apple how frequently the tracker updates with your location, and how it works out where your home and work addresses are.<\/p>\n
