This summer, get ready for the snowflakes to melt.<\/p>\n

New Netflix reality series \u201cSnowflake Mountain\u201d features a group of delicate, immature 20-somethings who party hard, can\u2019t hold down jobs and still live with their parents. These \u201csnowflakes\u201d \u2014\u00a0which the show defines as young people who are \u201coverly emotional, easily offended and dramatic\u201d \u2014\u00a0are sent into the wilderness, in an effort to push them into growing up. The show premieres Wednesday, June 22, and culminates in an outstanding cast member snagging $50,000.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am very dramatic. People say, \u2018Why do you have to always be on [Level] 10?\u2019 But I don\u2019t feel like I\u2019m spoiled,\u201d contestant Deandra Joseph, 24, told The Post.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cBut to other people, I can see why they\u2019d say that,\u201d said the Brooklyn-based makeup artist, who lives with her parents and has trouble holding down a job. \u201cI feel like I work hard for my stuff. But I understand the term [snowflake].\u201d<\/p>\n

Deandra Joseph, 24, one of the \u2018flakes on \u201cSnowflake Mountain.\u201d Pete Dadds\/Netflix All 10 cast members of \u201cSnowflake Mountain\u201d adrift: Carl (from left), Darriea, Randy, Devon, Olivia, Sunny, Rae, Liam, Solomon and Deandra. Pete Dadds\/Netflix <\/p>\n

On the show, Deandra is joined by a group of equally hapless Zoomers, including Solomon, 26, who is \u201cfunemployed\u201d \u2014 in other words, bankrolled by his wealthy parents \u2014 and spends $500 a week on grooming. There\u2019s also Randy, 23, a wannabe pro wrestler who coasts off his family\u2019s money and complains on the show, \u201cI hate walking. I try to avoid it at all costs. I don\u2019t even know if I\u2019ve walked up a hill before.\u201d<\/p>\n

Deandra and Rae, 25 (right), are two party girl snowflakes who weren\u2019t happy to be sent into the wild on \u201cSnowflake Mountain.\u201d Pete Dadds\/Netflix <\/p>\n

Before the competition kicked off, the snowflakes were told that they were headed to a luxury resort for an all-expenses-paid party with other like-minded 20-somethings. Instead, when they arrived in England\u2019s Lake District, they learned they were actually going to rough it in the wilderness.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey bamboozled me!\u201d said Deandra. <\/p>\n

\u201cWhen I got out of the van and saw that I was in the mountains, I could have cried. And then we had to pick limited items to bring \u2013 I had four suitcases, and I had to pick one. I was so sad [without] my makeup. I could be butt naked, and I would have been A-OK, but . . . I just take so much pride in looking a certain way. I know most people are like, \u2018Girl, that\u2019s not important.\u2019 But as long as my nose is contoured, I\u2019m OK.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Her breaking point on the show? Waking up with a creepy-crawly snuggle buddy.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey had me in a little tent, and I woke up to a spider on my face,\u201d Deandra said. \u201cI still have PTSD, I\u2019m still crying. On my face? That\u2019s just so rude.\u201d<\/p>\n

Deandra, right, with fellow snowflake Solomon, 26 (left). Pete Dadds\/Netflix <\/p>\n

The snowflakes go through various challenges, such as swimming out to the middle of a freezing lake to get food from a floating raft. They\u2019re ordered around by Joel Graves, a former navy officer, and Matt Tate, a former army combat engineer.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI thought that they were supermean,\u201d said Deandra. \u201cI struggle with listening to authority \u2014 that\u2019s been my whole life. I\u2019m not a fan of rules. If somebody tells me not to do something, I\u2019m going to purposefully do it, and then take a picture.\u201d<\/p>\n

Devon, 20 (left), with fellow cast member Solomon on \u201cSnowflake Mountain.\u201d Pete Dadds\/Netflix Devon Smith is one of the struggling 20-somethings on \u201cSnowflake Mountain.\u201d Pete Dadds\/Netflix <\/p>\n

Devon Smith, a New York-based vegan party girl who loves looking at herself in the mirror and is failing out of college, was also shocked by the show\u2019s true premise.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was like, \u2018Oh my God, it\u2019s going to be the new \u201cJersey Shore,\u201d \u2018 \u201d the 20-year-old told The Post of why she signed up. \u201cI was packing bathing suits and corsets and heels and minidresses. I was not prepared.\u201d<\/p>\n

Her breaking point came when she couldn\u2019t complete a challenge that involved chopping a piece of plywood.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was just bawling my eyes out for hours,\u201d she said. \u201cI cried all day and all night. I cried myself to sleep that night. I was the only person who couldn\u2019t do it, so I felt useless and stupid. A 1-inch piece of wood was my enemy on the show.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rae, Devon and Carl, 22, on \u201cSnowflake Mountain.\u201d Pete Dadds\/Netflix <\/p>\n

Despite how tough the experience was, Devon and Deandra both say that they feel like it did help them grow.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIt humbled me, for a fact,\u201d said Deandra. \u201cIt made me feel more prepared for any situation. You could stick me in the wilderness right now, and I\u2019ve got you. Bring me some hay and twigs, and we\u2019ve got a fire!\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIt did push me on my feet to be more of an adult,\u201d said Devon. \u201cI learned it\u2019s OK to fail. Sometimes, you just need to bask in your failure.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
