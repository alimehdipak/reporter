<\/p>\n

Elon Musk slammed Apple\u2019s widely scrutinized practice of taking a 30% slice of revenue from transactions within its App Store on Monday \u2013 asserting that the fee was \u201cdefinitely not ok.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Tesla CEO raised his concerns in response to detailing a complaint from European regulators who say Apple \u201cabused\u201d its leading market position to stifle competition for its mobile payments system, Apple Pay.<\/p>\n

\u201cApple\u2019s store is like having a 30% tax on the Internet,\u201d Musk said.<\/p>\n

Musk added that the App Store fee was \u201cliterally 10 times higher than it should be.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk is the latest of several tech firms or leaders who have criticized Apple over the 30% fee \u2013 which applies to paid downloads and other purchases for developers earning $1 million or more in annual revenue through the store.<\/p>\n

Apple representatives did not immediately respond to a request for comment.<\/p>\n

Bloomberg reported that PayPal, the payments platform that Musk co-founded and later sold, played a key role in the potential European regulatory crackdown over Apple Pay \u2013 grumbling to the European Commission about the iPhone maker\u2019s business practices.<\/p>\n

Musk was responding to a complaint from European regulators over Apple\u2019s mobile payments system.Patrick McMullan via Getty Images <\/p>\n

U.S. regulators are currently eyeing action against Apple and Google over their app store business practices. One piece of legislation, the Open App Markets Act, would block online marketplace operators from giving preferential treatment to their own app, among other measures meant to promote a level playing field.<\/p>\n

Musk previously slammed Apple\u2019s App Store fees during the company\u2019s high-profile legal battle with \u201cFortnite\u201d maker Epic Games. Apple booted the video game company from its App Store in 2020 after it introduced its own payments system.<\/p>\n

\u201cApple app store fees are a de facto global tax on the Internet. Epic is right,\u201d Musk tweeted during the trial.<\/p>\n

Apple CEO Tim Cook is actively lobbying against antitrust legislation in Congress.Getty Images for Vanity Fair <\/p>\n

During the trial, an Apple executive revealed the company made at least $100 million in fees from \u201cFortnite\u201d commissions during the time the game was available in the App Store.<\/p>\n

A federal judge later delivered a split verdict in the case \u2014 though the ruling was largely in Apple\u2019s favor.<\/p>\n

Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg also jabbed Apple in a June 2021 blog post detailing that Facebook would not charge creators for work posted on the social media platform through 2023.<\/p>\n
