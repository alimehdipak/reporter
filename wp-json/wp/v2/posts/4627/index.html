Ethan Hawke says he\u2019s on the final leg of his acting career. <\/p>\n

The \u201cTraining Day\u201d star, 51, revealed he thinks he\u2019s in the last stage of making movies in a lengthy interview with IndieWire.<\/p>\n

Hawke noted that he feels that he\u2019s \u201cdefinitely made the turn from being an old young person to being a young old person.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI prefer this. I feel like playing John Brown in \u2018The Good Lord Bird\u2019 was that for me: the beginning of my \u2018old man\u2019 career, the beginning of my last act,\u201d the father of four went on.<\/p>\n

Ethan Hawke has solidified himself in Hollywood as an acclaimed actor, writer, director and producer.FilmMagic <\/p>\n

Hawke starred in, created and produced the 2020 Showtime miniseries about Brown, an abolitionist in the 1850s.<\/p>\n

He continued: \u201cBut it\u2019s the beginning of it, you know? I definitely find myself looking over a filmography and thinking about which ones I could\u2019ve cut out, because I only have so much time left. I know I only have so many movies left.\u201d<\/p>\n

Hawke\u2019s next project, a six-part CNN documentary series that premieres on HBO Max Thursday, is called \u201cThe Last Movie Stars\u201d \u2014 a look at the love between and the careers of late actors and spouses Paul Newman and Joanne Woodward.<\/p>\n

He then explained that he now has an \u201cawareness of time\u201d regarding his Hollywood career. <\/p>\n

Ethan Hawke in a scene from the 1995 film \u201cBefore Sunrise.\u201d Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cWhen I was younger, I was like, \u2018I\u2019ll do this, I\u2019ll do that, that\u2019ll be a good learning experience, and then I\u2019ll try this\u2019 \u2014 thinking I had all the time in the world. Now, I\u2019m like, \u2018I didn\u2019t learn anything from that one or that one, and that one would\u2019ve been better spent in three months with my family,’\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

The \u201cGreat Expectations\u201d actor also discussed his daughter Maya Hawke\u2019s success on the Netflix hit \u201cStranger Things.\u201d<\/p>\n

He dished that he\u2019s \u201cso proud of her\u201d and has one episode of the latest season he\u2019s waiting to watch.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhen I was younger, I was like, \u2018I\u2019ll do this, I\u2019ll do that, that\u2019ll be a good learning experience, and then I\u2019ll try this\u2019 \u2014 thinking I had all the time in the world,\u201d Hawke noted.Getty Images <\/p>\n

He noted: \u201cI feel strangely connected to that show, because that whole episode with the first kid getting sucked into the Upside Down happens on what was my 13th birthday in 1983, which was the year I made \u2018Explorers,\u2019 so I\u2019m in the universe. Also, I think Maya does a great job on it and the show\u2019s so fun.\u201d<\/p>\n
