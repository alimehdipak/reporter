<\/p>\n

NASA is closing in on the launch of an ambitious mission to probe an asteroid worth an estimated 10,000 quadrillion dollars.<\/p>\n

The space rock 16-Psyche is twice as wide as Wales and is nestled in the asteroid belt between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter.<\/p>\n

Discovered in 1852, it was once known as the \u201cgolden asteroid\u201d as scientists believed it was mostly composed of the precious metal.<\/p>\n

Subsequent observations, however, hint that Psyche is most likely made up of a mix of rock, iron and nickel.<\/p>\n

This summer, NASA will embark on a mission to study the metal-rich object in an effort to determine its origins.<\/p>\n

It will be NASA\u2019s first visit to a metallic asteroid.<\/p>\n

After lifting off from the Kennedy Space Centre in Florida, the Psyche probe will arrive at its target in early 2026.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s thought that the asteroid is the shattered core of a planetesimal \u2013 a small world the size of a city or small country.<\/p>\n

The blobs of material are the first building blocks of planets and it\u2019s thought they were plentiful in the early universe.<\/p>\n

If Psyche is a planetesimal, it can shed light on the interior of terrestrial planets like Earth.<\/p>\n

The core of our own world is hidden beneath miles and miles of mantle and crust, making it nearly impossible to study.<\/p>\n

NASA\u2019s spacecraft will use an array of instruments to investigate Psyche\u2019s magnetic field, gravitational pull, surface makeup and more.<\/p>\n

NASA says: \u201cDuring 21 months in orbit, the spacecraft will map and study Psyche.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe mission\u2019s goal is, among other things, to determine whether Psyche is indeed the core of a planetesimal.\u201d<\/p>\n

Astronomers on Earth have studied Psyche in great detail and believe it\u2019s shaped somewhat like a potato.<\/p>\n

In total, it\u2019s estimated that Psyche\u2019s various metals are worth a gargantuan $12,000 quadrillion.<\/p>\n

That means if we carried it back to Earth, it would destroy commodity prices and cause the world\u2019s economy \u2013 worth \u00a359.5 trillion \u2013 to collapse.<\/p>\n

We\u2019ve known about Psyche 16 for a while, but its potential to cause havoc on Earth was recently touched upon by a veteran miner.<\/p>\n

Scott Moore, who heads up EuroSun Mining, said the sheer amount of gold in the asteroid threatens to throw the gold industry into chaos.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe \u2018Titans of Gold\u2019 now control hundreds of the best-producing properties around the world,\u201d he told\u00a0Oil Price\u00a0in 2019.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut the 4-5 million ounces of gold they bring to the market every year pales in comparison to the conquests available in space.\u201d<\/p>\n

Fortunately, NASA is taking the trip for scientific purposes and isn\u2019t planning on conducting any mining.<\/p>\n

Psyche was discovered by Italian astronomer Annibale de Gasparis on March 17, 1852.<\/p>\n

He named the asteroid after Psyche, the Greek goddess of the soul who married Eros (Roman Cupid), the god of Love.<\/p>\n
