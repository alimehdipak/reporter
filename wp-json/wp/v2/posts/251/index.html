<\/p>\n

It was already famously known that Dolly Parton will always love you. Well, now the Queen of Country wants to rock you, too.<\/p>\n

And some of the rock world\u2019s most storied producers are now clamoring to produce a head-banging Dolly album \u2014 to make her wishes of conquering the genre come true.<\/p>\n

\u201cThere\u2019s probably nobody in the entire music business who wouldn\u2019t jump at a chance to work with Dolly Parton, so it would just be the easiest thing to put together,\u201d classic rocker Todd Rundgren told The Post.<\/p>\n

When Parton took herself out of the running to be inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame this year \u2014 \u201cI don\u2019t feel that I have earned that right,\u201d she said in an Instagram post\u2014 she revealed that she hopes to one day be \u201cworthy\u201d of another nomination.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis has \u2026 inspired me to put out a hopefully great rock \u2019n\u2019 roll album at some point in the future, which I have always wanted to do!\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

And if Johnny Cash could work with Rick Rubin and Loretta Lynn could collaborate with Jack White, then certainly Parton could get some rock love.<\/p>\n

Dolly Parton hosted the Academy of Country Music Awards on March 7, but now wants to conquer the rock world.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Rundgren \u2014 who in addition to being inducted into the RRHOF last year as an artist, has produced everyone from Badfinger and the New York Dolls to Meat Loaf\u2019s \u201cBat Out of Hell\u201d \u2014 would love to collaborate with Parton behind the boards. \u201cWho wouldn\u2019t?\u201d Rundgren, 73, said.<\/p>\n

\u201cShe\u2019s made so many records, has so much experience, you know there\u2019s not going to be any sort of funny business about doing it. I would just get everybody in the world who ever wanted to play with Dolly Parton \u2026 from Heart to Joan Jett to Pearl Jam to Greta Van Fleet, for God\u2019s sake.\u201d<\/p>\n

And Rundgren knows exactly the kind of record he would want to make with Parton: \u201cThe point is, first of all, do a real rock album,\u201d he said. \u201cIf you\u2019re gonna do a rock album, do a rock album. Don\u2019t diddle around the edges of it. But also, do it like Dolly would do it. Don\u2019t try and make funny things with her voice or anything, trying to tell the world [she\u2019s] tough or anything like that. There isn\u2019t any necessity for that.\u201d<\/p>\n

Nirvana producer Steve Albini has thrown his hat into the ring to work with Dolly Parton. \t \t <\/p>\n

Noted producer Steve Albini \u2014 who worked on Nirvana\u2019s classic \u201cIn Utero\u201d\u00a0 \u2014 has also thrown his hat in the ring to rock with Parton. \u201cDolly Parton do you like analog recording,\u201d he tweeted in response to the legend\u2019s desire for her dream album.<\/p>\n

At the very least, the woman who wrote such classics as \u201cJolene,\u201d \u201c9 to 5\u201d and, of course, \u201cI Will Always Love You\u201d has certainly won some fans in rock circles by respectfully withdrawing herself from RRHOF contention.<\/p>\n

\u201cI think it was a classy move, really,\u201d Judas Priest guitarist Richie Faulkner \u2014 whose band is also nominated for the rock hall this year \u2014 told podcast \u201cRock of Nations With Dave Kinchen.\u201d \u201cI think she recognizes her brand, and it didn\u2019t necessarily fit into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame. And I think it raises questions to what the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame\u2019s brand is, as well.\u201d<\/p>\n

Seventies rock god Todd Rundgren would jump at the chance to produce Dolly Parton.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Rundgren himself didn\u2019t attend last year\u2019s induction ceremony out of his own issues with the institution. \u201cIt never felt right to me either,\u201d he said. \u201cAs an institution, I don\u2019t take it very seriously and never have. So everybody knows that.\u201d<\/p>\n

And he stands with Parton\u2019s decision to bow out of the running: \u201cShe\u2019s actually doing them a favor in a way,\u201d he said. \u201cBecause whatever their original mandate was \u2026 I knew at the time that they were gonna run out of legitimate [rock] nominees eventually.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
