Flip through a typical fashion magazine today, and you\u2019ll find headlines like \u201cWhy I\u2019m Cheating on My Husband\u201d or \u201cI Planned an Orgy with My Best Friend. It was the Most Liberating Experience of My Life\u201d and even \u201cIs Having a Baby in 2021 Pure Environmental Vandalism?\u201d But a huge swathe of young women in this country, many of whom consider themselves conservative, find this woke, hyper-sexualized approach to the world a big turn off.<\/p>\n

Isabelle Redfield, 23, and Jayme Chandler Franklin, 24, decided to do something about it.<\/p>\n

Two years ago, the fashionable San Francisco natives launched The Conservateur, an alternative platform for women who feel left out by mainstream glossy style bibles. Their site, which offers stories under verticals like \u201cStyle,\u201d \u201cBeauty,\u201d \u201cPolitics\u201d and \u201cFaith,\u201d has so far amassed more than 30,000 followers on Instagram. With its glamorous photos and sleek design, The Conservateur is a stark contrast to most conservative websites, which are usually tricked out in camouflage and aimed at men.<\/p>\n

Franklin and Redfield are targeting the vast audience of young American women more interested in reading about politics and faith than orgies or infidelity. The Conservateur, a rare conservative site aimed at young women, is organized into sections like \u201cStyle,\u201d \u201cBeauty,\u201d \u201cPolitics\u201d and \u201cFaith.\u201d theconservateur.com <\/p>\n

\u201cThere are so many conservative women who are just not represented by the likes of Vanity Fair, Vogue, Cosmopolitan, Glamour. People are rejecting it, and they\u2019re coming to us,\u201d Redfield said.<\/p>\n

\u201cOur main components are faith, freedom, family and friends. We\u2019re concentrating on uplifting young women. We want to be builders, not wreckers.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Conservateur presents its stories in the same punchy and provocative style as Cosmopolitan, but with headlines you\u2019ll never see in a fashion mag, such as \u201cAbortion Empowers the \u2018Patriarchy\u2019,\u201d \u201cSex Is No Longer Sacred,\u201d and \u201cShopping on the #BidenBudget.\u201d <\/p>\n

The Conservateur Instagram account has amassed over 30,000 followers thanks to its fashion-forward design. The site\u2019s founders say their goal is to \u201cuplift\u201d women. Instagram @theconservateur Site co-founder Redfield was a White House intern with a passion-for-fashion turned off by magazines like Vogue, whose woke content, she said, began to feel negative and toxic. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe Abolition of Women,\u201d a story that argues against the current notion that gender is a concept and states that \u201cwomen are not men, and men are not women,\u201d would set fire to the hairstyles of the collective Cond\u00e9 Nast workforce.<\/p>\n

The idea for the site first came about in the summer of 2020. The duo had just graduated from college (Franklin from Berkeley; Redfield from Southern Methodist University) and were rooming together and interning in Washington, DC (Franklin at the Senate; Redfield at the White House).<\/p>\n

\u201cWe were both fashionable girls, but we couldn\u2019t read Vogue anymore,\u201d Redfield said. \u201cEverything was just going so left, and we were so turned off by it. We thought their content was really toxic and negative for young women, and we just recognized that conservative women really needed an outlet more than ever. So we ditched our [magazine] subscriptions and got to writing.\u201d<\/p>\n

Franklin was rooming with Redfield and also interning in Washington DC when the duo conjured up The Conservateur. Today she works at Fox News Digital while helping to grow her website. <\/p>\n

The founders said their site primarily attracts college-aged girls \u2014 plus a lot of interested mothers \u2014 mostly from Dallas, New York and DC, who are craving a refuge from the woke fashion world along with some smart political commentary. Redfield said her Instagram inbox is filled with thousands of messages from fans who believe in their mission. <\/p>\n

Since its launch, The Conservateur staff has grown to a core team of four, plus 15 contributors. Redfield and Franklin said they are flooded with hoards of applications from college-aged women who want to contribute, too. \u201cWe can\u2019t even keep up with the applications,\u201d Redfield said. \u201cIt\u2019s unbelievable. They\u2019re all fantastic girls.\u201d<\/p>\n

Lara Trump modeling a Make American Hot Again hat, launched by the founders of The Conservateur. The site now has a core team of four with 15 contributors.Andrea Hanks\/The Conservateur <\/p>\n

As they look towards the future, Redfield and Franklin, both still living\u00a0and working in media in DC,\u00a0and employed\u00a0by Laura Ingraham and\u00a0Fox News Digital respectively,\u00a0would like to make their project a full-time career and branch out into different mediums like podcasting. They\u2019re also continuing a lucrative business selling their hot-pink Make America Hot Again hat, which Lara Trump recently modeled. And they\u2019re planning to post profiles on influential women in the conservative movement, such as South Dakota Governor Kristi Noem, who might run for president in 2024.<\/p>\n

Redfield is bullish about their prospects, declaring: \u201cWe believe we\u2019re the new frontier of the conservative movement.\u201d <\/p>\n
