NEW ORLEANS \u2014 Willie Nelson is cancelling an upcoming performance at the New Orleans Jazz & Heritage festival and postponing other shows after a positive case of the coronavirus in his band.<\/p>\n

The 89-year-old musician posted on\u00a0his band\u2019s website on Friday\u00a0that \u201cdue to a positive Covid case in the Willie Nelson Family Band\u201d two upcoming shows scheduled to happen May 6 and May 7 would be postponed and that Nelson\u2019s Sunday performance at Jazz Fest would be cancelled.<\/p>\n

Nelson was slated to close the Gentilly Stage \u2014 the same stage where his son Lukas Nelson is performing earlier in the day with his band the Promise of the Real. No replacement for the elder Nelson has yet been announced.<\/p>\n

The news comes after Melissa Etheridge announced Thursday that she would not be able to perform Saturday at Jazz Fest due to \u201cCOVID hitting my crew.\u201d Mavis Staples will perform during that time period instead.<\/p>\n

Melissa Etheridge also cancelled on the festival due to her crew being hit with coronavirus. Getty Images for LOVE ROCKS NYC\/ The New Orleans Jazz and Heritage Festival has had multiple acts cancel due to COVID-19. Amy Harris\/Invision\/AP <\/p>\n

\u201cOh my heart hurts not to be there. Mavis will be amazing, I know. I am hoping I can be asked back again. Dave and crew are healing. Thank you for all the well wishes. COVID heartbreak,\u201d tweeted Etheridge after news that Staples would appear on Saturday was announced.<\/p>\n

Also on Friday, legendary New Orleans bass player George Porter Jr. said on Instagram that he had the coronavirus and was out for the rest of the festival.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Times-Picayune \/ The New Orleans Advocate\u00a0reported that Porter played multiple shows during Jazz Fest\u2019s first weekend and had a number of performances scheduled outside the festival during the second weekend.<\/p>\n

The seven-day festival started on Friday, April 29, and ends Sunday, May 8.<\/p>\n

Willie Nelson was booked to close the festival on the same stage that his son Lukas Nelson will play earlier in the day.WireImage for Shock Ink The New Orleans Jazz and Heritage Festival draws tens of thousands of people. AP <\/p>\n

The event draws tens of thousands to the city\u2019s Fair Grounds Race Course, where as many as 80 musical acts perform daily on more than a dozen stages, complemented by art and craft exhibits and an array of booths featuring foods from Louisiana and beyond. <\/p>\n

While it attracts national and international talent such as Nelson and Etheridge, the festival is also known for showcasing the wide gamut of musical talent and genres found in Louisiana such as zydeco, gospel, blues and of course, jazz.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
