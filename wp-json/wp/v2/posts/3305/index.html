How should I sleep with lower back pain?<\/h3>\n

The best sleeping position for lower back pain is on your side with a partial bend in the knees7. Keeping the knees bent helps balance the body and reduces pressure on the lumbar spine. Many people find it helpful to put a small pillow between their knees to make this position more comfortable.<\/p>\n

Should I put a pillow under my lower back?<\/p>\n

If you sleep on your back, place a pillow under your knees to help maintain the normal curve of your lower back. You might try a small, rolled towel under the small of your back for additional support. Support your neck with a pillow. Mayo Clinic does not endorse companies or products.<\/p>\n

Does lumbar pillow help lower back pain?<\/p>\n

Over time, the muscles that support your spine \u2014 also known as the paraspinal muscles \u2014 can begin to fatigue, leading to poor posture. Lumbar pillows are designed to support the natural curvature of your spine, which can help alleviate lower back pain by decreasing strain on these muscles.<\/p>\n

What pillows do chiropractors recommend for back pain?<\/p>\n

Why does my back hurt every morning after sleeping?<\/h3>\n

If you notice back pain every morning, your sleeping posture could be the culprit. Poor sleeping positions can put pressure on your spine, causing its natural curve to flatten. This can also cause back strain and uncomfortable pressure on your joints.<\/p>\n

Where should a lumbar pillow be placed?<\/h3>\n

When seated in a chair, a Lumbar Support Pillow should be placed vertically across the back of the chair so that it is flush against the lower part of the back. It should keep your ears, shoulders and hips in alignment so that the natural curvature of your spine is maintained.<\/p>\n
