Elon Musk reportedly faces a Federal Trade Commission probe into whether he violated federal disclosure rules while amassing an approximately 9% stake in Twitter \u2013 a move that preceded his blockbuster $44 billion takeover of the social media giant.<\/p>\n

The feds are examining whether Musk erred under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act, a 1976 antitrust provision requiring investors to provide a \u201cpre-merger notification\u201d to the FTC and the Justice Department. The law applies for active investors who acquire large stakes in a company.<\/p>\n

Musk, who began buying Twitter shares on Jan. 31, initially disclosed his stake in the company in an April 4 filing that classified him as a passive investor. <\/p>\n

The billionaire refiled the disclosure one day later to reflect that he was an active investor after he publicly engaged with Twitter\u2019s board about potential changes at the company.<\/p>\n

The FTC wants to review communication between Musk and Twitter\u2019s board as it assesses whether he bought shares with plans to play an active role in the company\u2019s activities, The Information reported, citing sources familiar with the matter.<\/p>\n

The FTC is probing whether Elon Musk intended to play an active role in Twitter when he invested.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Musk could face fines if the FTC\u2019s probe determines he violated the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act by failing to properly disclose his investment, according to the report. However, the fine would amount to a maximum of $46,517 per each day Musk failed to adhere to guidelines \u2013 a paltry sum for the world\u2019s richest individual.<\/p>\n

FTC officials did not immediately return a request for comment on the report.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s initial disclosure of his Twitter share purchases immediately drew scrutiny from regulatory experts earlier this month \u2013 some of whom noted that the billionaire missed the SEC\u2019s reporting deadline by several weeks when his stake in the company surpassed 5%.<\/p>\n

The FTC probe could result in some fines for Elon Musk.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Musk exceeded the threshold on March 14, but his stake wasn\u2019t publicly revealed until early April \u2013 meaning the broader market was unaware and share prices did not react to his investment.<\/p>\n

The SEC has yet to publicly say if Musk is under scrutiny for that apparent misstep.<\/p>\n

While Musk\u2019s personal transactions are under review, antitrust regulators aren\u2019t expected to block his $44 billion Twitter deal.<\/p>\n

FTC chairwoman Lina Khan has signaled a crackdown on major tech deals and mergers.Getty Images <\/p>\n

As The Information noted, Twitter has little overlap with Musk\u2019s other business interests, such as Tesla, SpaceX and The Boring Company. However, the FTC is chaired by antitrust crusader Lina Khan, who has signaled a crackdown on major tech deals and mergers.<\/p>\n
