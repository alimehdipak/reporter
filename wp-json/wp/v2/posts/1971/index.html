Elon Musk may be laying the groundwork to combine Tesla, SpaceX and Twitter into a new super company.<\/p>\n

As the world\u2019s richest man continues his crusade to take over Twitter, Musk registered three new companies on Tuesday in the tax-friendly state of Delaware under variations of the name \u201cX Holdings.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The three new companies appear to be financial maneuvering as part of Musk\u2019s Twitter bid.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The first company, X Holdings I Inc., lists Musk as president, treasurer and secretary and would be the parent company of Twitter if the billionaire\u2019s bid succeeds, according to Bloomberg, which first spotted the fillings.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Another entity, X Holdings II Inc., would merge with Twitter as part of the acquisition, while X Holdings III LLC would be used to help fund the transaction, the outlet reported.<\/p>\n

Tesla could potentially be merged into an Elon Musk-led super-company. REUTERS <\/p>\n

But the filings have also revived speculation about whether Musk plans to merge all his ventures \u2014 Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, The Boring Company and potentially Twitter \u2014 into a single super-conglomerate.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In December 2020, an investor and Musk booster named Dave Lee wrote on Twitter that Musk should \u201cform a holding company called X\u201d that would become the \u201cparent company of Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink and Boring Company\u201d with the \u201cmission to ensure human survival and progress.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cGood idea,\u201d Musk responded.\u00a0<\/p>\n

But Musk also appeared to throw cold water on the idea of forming a super-company last week, blaming Wall Street investors for short-term thinking.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cAs a public company, you\u2019re just constantly sued,\u201d Musk said in a TED interview. \u201cIt\u2019s not that easy to sort of combine these things.\u201d<\/p>\n

Elon Musk is currently pursuing a bid to take over Twitter. AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

In an apparent confirmation of Musk\u2019s reservations, investor Gary Black slammed the idea of the mogul combining his companies on Thursday following reports of the new \u201cX Holdings\u201d entities. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis is fine if done so\u00a0@elonmusk\u00a0can combine his equity stakes to finance a personal bid for $TWTR,\u201d wrote Black, who\u2019s typically a Tesla bull and is a managing partner of investment firm the Future Fund. <\/p>\n
