New Mexico\u2019s River Ranch, aka\u00a0<\/em>Estancia del Rio \u2014 which is built on 1,000 acres once owned by fashion designer (and Santa Fe native) Tom Ford \u2014 is on the market for $8.95 million. <\/p>\n

It first hit the market for $12 million in 2019. <\/p>\n

The four-bedroom, 4\u00bd-bath steel-and-concrete home at 411 Mesa Prieta Road in Youngsville is 4,500 square feet. <\/p>\n

It was built, along with additional casitas<\/em>, around 2011 to 2012 \u201cto take advantage of the pink and red hills and valley of the Piedra Lumbre land grant,\u201d made famous by artist Georgia O\u2019Keeffe, notes the listing.\u00a0 <\/p>\n

Ford owned the land before he bought a bigger and \u201cmore glamorous\u201d ranch, known as Cerro Pelon, which sits on 20,662 acres and is anchored by a modern concrete home with glass walls and a reflecting pool, designed by Pritzker Prize-winning architect Tadao Ando. <\/p>\n

Estancia del Rio sits on 1,000 acres.Marshall Elias Photography One of the estate\u2019s four bedrooms.Marshall Elias Photography There\u2019s 4,500 square feet of living space inside.Marshall Elias Photography The ranch\u2019s (much appreciated, given the location) pool.Marshall Elias Photography <\/p>\n

Ford first listed Cerro Pelon, in the Galisteo Basin just outside of Santa Fe, for $75 million in 2016, before selling it last year during the pandemic, when it was last asking $48 million, said broker Clayton Orrigo of Compass, who repped the buyer. That property was also used to film movies like\u00a0\u201cSilverado,\u201d <\/em>\u201cAll the Pretty Horses\u201d and\u00a0\u201cThor.\u201d <\/p>\n

The seller of\u00a0Estancia del Rio is\u00a0Fred Haas, a musician, philanthropist and horse owner, who says he is selling because he needs to be closer to Santa Fe for his horses. <\/p>\n

\u201cI love it here and bought it for the peace and quiet, to just be in nature and as a get away. It\u2019s so beautiful, I hate to sell it,\u201d Haas told Gimme Shelter. <\/p>\n

The nature vistas are sublime.Marshall Elias Photography The scenery-rich ranch was built in 2011.Marshall Elias Photography <\/p>\n

Estancia del Rio is also near Ghost Ranch, a beloved ranch in the southwest, made famous by O\u2019Keeffe, who painted the Cerro Pedernal mountain next to Estancia del Rio. \u201cShe called it her mountain, and you can see it from my doorstep,\u201d Haas said. <\/p>\n

The property is made up of two separately deeded 500-acre tracts and borders Carson National Forest and the Chama River Canyon Wilderness area.\u00a0<\/p>\n
