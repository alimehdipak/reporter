Former Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey tangled with CNN personality Brian Stelter \u2014 criticizing the network and other prominent media outlets for stoking division in a lengthy tweetstorm Monday night.<\/p>\n

The tweets marked a rare outburst for Dorsey, 45, who typically maintains a low profile on the social media platform he co-founded. <\/p>\n

Dorsey has faced rampant criticism over his handling of Twitter\u2019s content moderation policies \u2014 with many critics accusing the tech executive of engaging in censorship.<\/p>\n

Dorsey began his Twitter rant in response to a post from Stelter, who shared a link from the Washington Post that accused Fox News host Tucker Carlson of \u201cselling doubt\u201d to his audience.<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd you all are selling hope?\u201d Dorsey quipped in response to Stelter\u2019s post.<\/p>\n

Dorsey\u2019s initial remark drew a strong response from other media pros, including Newsmax correspondent Alex Salvi \u2014 who shared a screenshot of the exchange and said the ex-Twitter boss was \u201cdefending Tucker Carlson.\u201d Dorsey denied that was the case.<\/p>\n

\u201cNot defending a thing,\u201d Dorsey said. \u201cHolding up a mirror.\u201d<\/p>\n

Dorsey targeted CNN in a separate tweet while responding to another user who alleged that the left-leaning network\u2019s personalities \u201csometimes sell false news.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI know this from being on the streets of Ferguson during the protests and watching them try to create conflict and film it causing the protestors to chant \u2018f\u2014k CNN,\u2019\u201d Dorsey said.<\/p>\n

CNN representatives did not immediately respond to a request for comment<\/p>\n

\u201cI thought his point was that the press does the same thing, sowing doubt to promote white supremacy and get engagement, often amplifying bad takes, but now I\u2019m not sure anymore,\u201d Pao said.<\/p>\n

\u201cYes,\u201d Dorsey said in response.<\/p>\n

Dorsey also fired back at a user who argued it was \u201ckind of weird\u201d for the Twitter founder to comment on the spread of extremism, given concerns that social media platforms contribute to the problem.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m sure. and we certainly have seen what twitter does positive and negative. I think the common denominator is the current ad model for all media, not just social media,\u201d Dorsey said.<\/p>\n

Dorsey has been outspoken this week as Twitter considers a buyout offer from Elon Musk, who offered last week to acquire the company for $43 billion. Twitter\u2019s board of directors has yet to say whether it will accept the bid.<\/p>\n
