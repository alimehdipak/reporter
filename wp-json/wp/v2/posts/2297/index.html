Billionaire Elon Musk slammed far-left progressives on Friday morning \u2014 serving up another glimpse of his political views as he proceeds with a $44 billion takeover of Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe far left hates everyone, themselves included!\u201d Musk tweeted Friday morning.<\/p>\n

Musk, however, followed up 16 minutes later, adding, \u201cBut I\u2019m no fan of the far right either. Let\u2019s have less hate and more love.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk weighed in on politics as his buyout deal sparks fierce resistance from Twitter employees and left-leaning pundits \u2013 some of whom have pledged to leave the platform altogether, citing fears the billionaire\u2019s leadership will result in more abusive or hateful content.<\/p>\n

The Friday morning tweets also came hours after he sparked dustup on Twitter by sharing a cartoon depicting the country\u2019s shifting political landscape over the last decade.<\/p>\n

The cartoon, drawn by online magazine Quillette\u2019s Colin Wright, depicts a stick figure standing slightly left-of-center on the political spectrum as of 2008 \u2013 only to end up as right-leaning conservative in 2021 as a figure labeled \u201cwoke progressive\u201d gradually moved further left.<\/p>\n

Musk later elaborated on his initial post, noting that he was prominent supporter of Democratic former President Barack Obama.<\/p>\n

\u201cI strongly supported Obama for President, but today\u2019s Democratic Party has been hijacked by extremists,\u201d Musk added.<\/p>\n

Musk has stepped up his criticism of far-left politics in recent days. Earlier this month, he blamed Netflix\u2019s recent subscriber losses to a \u201cwoke mind virus\u201d that has made the platform \u201cunwatchable.\u201d<\/p>\n

The billionaire has also frequently exchanged jabs with Democratic moderates, such as President Biden, and far-left politicians such as Sens. Bernie Sanders (I-VT) and Elizabeth Warren (D-Mass.)<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s move to buy Twitter drew sharp criticism from progressives, including MSNBC anchor Joy Reid \u2013 who suggested that the South African-born billionaire bought the company because he \u201cmisses\u201d apartheid.<\/p>\n
