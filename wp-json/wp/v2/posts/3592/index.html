Dental implants are replacements for the root of teeth. Their purpose is to provide a sturdy foundation for permanent or removable replacement teeth. They are designed to fix the fake teeth to the gum, keeping the new teeth in place.<\/span><\/p>\n

What are some advantages of having implants?<\/span><\/h2>\n

Implants are needed when teeth<\/a> need to be replaced. They can be used to replace a single tooth or multiple teeth pending the individual. You\u2019ll likely experience one or more of the following advantages after getting dental implants!<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Improved speech<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Self-esteem increases<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Improved physical appearance<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Oral health enhancements<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Enhanced comfort<\/span><\/li>\n
  • An easier ability to eat<\/span><\/li>\n
  • Request An Appointment<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Why would you need dental implants?<\/span><\/h2>\n

    You\u2019ll first need to be evaluated by your dentist in order to determine if you need dental implants. Most of the time, dental implants are recommended for the following reasons:<\/span><\/p>\n

      \n
    • You\u2019re experiencing excessive tooth decay<\/span><\/li>\n
    • You have damage to the root of your tooth<\/span><\/li>\n
    • You have an oral complication or condition that has caused dry rot<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      These symptoms can be caused by a number of factors, but most of the time they are the result of:<\/span><\/p>\n

        \n
      • Poor oral health<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Underlying health conditions<\/span><\/li>\n
      • Treatment of underlying health conditions such as through chemotherapy<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        Who is eligible for dental implants?<\/span><\/h2>\n

        Most adults are eligible for dental implants should they need them. However, there are risk factors to consider and some aspects that dentists and oral surgeons like to see in their patients.<\/span><\/p>\n

          \n
        • If you are a heavy smoker, you may not be able to receive dental implants unless you quit smoking<\/span><\/li>\n
        • If you have an underlying health condition that affects your immune system, you may be at risk<\/span><\/li>\n
        • Patients who have undergone chemotherapy or radiation treatment are unlikely to be able to receive dental implants<\/span><\/li>\n
        • Your gums<\/a> and bone need to be able to hold the implant, so you need to be in excellent oral health<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          Who performs the implant surgery?<\/span><\/h2>\n

          Most of the time, dental implant surgery is performed by an oral surgeon. These are doctors who go through roughly 10-12 years of schooling to prepare them to be able to conduct surgery on the mouth. They\u2019ll perform your surgery and often times a dentist will conduct follow-ups for you.<\/span><\/p>\n

          How do you care for dental implants?<\/span><\/h2>\n

          Since dental implants help hold your new permanent or removable teeth in place, they require the same kind of care you\u2019d provide to your natural teeth. You\u2019ll need to brush your teeth twice daily with a recommended toothpaste. You\u2019ll also want to floss between the teeth every day and rinse with an antibacterial mouth wash!<\/span><\/p>\n

          Are dental implants covered by insurance?<\/span><\/h2>\n

          At this time, dental implants are not usually covered by dental insurance. However, pending the reason for the implants being needed, the procedure may be covered under medical insurance instead. This is on a case-by-case basis and you\u2019ll need to contact your insurance company to find out!<\/span><\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
