Canadian pop star Grimes says she was behind a 2012 hack and a \u201cblackmail\u2019 of the popular culture blog Hipster Runoff after it ran a photo of her kissing an unnamed woman.<\/p>\n

The singer confessed to the deed in an interview with Vanity Fair. She said she and a friend who was in the video game industry carried out a DDoS \u2014 or \u201cdistributed denial of service\u201d \u2014 attack against the indie site after it ran a photo of her kissing a friend as well as an unflattering article.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was just at a party with my friends,\u201d Grimes told\u00a0Vanity Fair, which ran a feature of the singer in its April editions ahead of the release of her new album, \u201cBook1\u201d. <\/p>\n

\u201cSomeone took this photo, and it got leaked to this website called Hipster Runoff.\u201d<\/p>\n

Grimes continued: \u201cAnd then he like ran this story and this was like I was trying to be like all integrity and you know, like start my career\u2026 And it was like Grimes gone wild or something.\u201d<\/p>\n

She added: \u201cAnd it was just this like super wack, like mean story. And it was like this meme that was going all over the internet. But my friend who worked for \u2014 I will not say which video game had access to\u2026Okay, well I don\u2019t wanna get him in trouble, but anyway\u2026we were actually able to DDoS Hipster Runoff and basically blackmail them.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

\u201cWe were like, like we\u2019re not gonna let you run your put your site back up until you take the story down,\u201d Grimes said. \u201cAnd he did in fact take the story down.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd it was like my coolest hacker moment.\u201d<\/p>\n

Grimes, whose legal name is Claire Boucher, has been in the news in recent months because of her relationship with Tesla CEO Elon Musk.<\/p>\n

Hackers who launch a DDoS attack, which are usually aimed at businesses to extort money, flood their target website with more information and traffic than it is built to take on. As a result, the site cannot access the internet and is forced to shut down.<\/p>\n

Grimes told Vanity Fair she enlisted a friend who works in the video game industry to hack the blog after it published a photo of her kissing an unnamed woman.Courtesy of Vanity Fair <\/p>\n

The most common way to execute a DDoS hack is to use \u201cbotnets,\u201d or a collection of computers or devices that are infected with malware. These botnets are then used to inundate the target site until it crashes the servers.<\/p>\n

Jackie Singh, the author of a cybersecurity blog, said that since Canada does not have a statute of limitation on cybercrime, Grimes\u2019 statements could possibly leave her vulnerable to criminal penalties.<\/p>\n

Grimes shares two children with her on-again, off-again boyfriend Elon Musk.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n
