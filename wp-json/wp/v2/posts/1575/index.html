What is Sleep Apnea?<\/h2>\n

Sleep apnea is a condition that obstructs breathing while you sleep. In other words, this condition causes people to stop breathing during sleep. The duration can vary in frequency and length. Here are two types of sleep apnea:<\/p>\n

Obstructive sleep apnea \u2013<\/strong> this condition occurs when your airways are partially or entirely blocked. It\u2019s the most common type of sleep apnea.
\nCentral sleep apnea \u2013<\/strong> this condition occurs when the brain fails to send proper signals to your respiratory system. This causes a delay or stops breathing.<\/p>\n

Sleep apnea symptoms<\/h2>\n

The symptoms associated with sleep apnea are both physical and behavioral, including:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Insomnia<\/li>\n
  • Short of breath when waking up<\/li>\n
  • Restless sleep<\/li>\n
  • Memory loss<\/li>\n
  • Daytime sleepiness<\/li>\n
  • Loud snoring<\/li>\n
  • Dry mouth when waking up<\/li>\n
  • Mood swings<\/li>\n
  • Lack of focus<\/li>\n
  • Headaches<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    In some cases, patients with sleep apnea couldn\u2019t recognize the signs themselves. But their bed partner often identifies the issue because of their partner\u2019s loud snoring or sudden gasping for air. If you notice a similar problem in your partner, encourage them to visit A+ Star Smile Dental.<\/p>\n

    Treatment Options for Sleep Apnea<\/h2>\n

    Sleep apnea can affect each individual differently. The breathing obstruction can cause difficulty in breathing significantly for some patients and mildly for others. The treatment options differ with these variations. Your medical or dental professional will recommend you treatment options depending on the severity of your condition.<\/p>\n

    Sleep apnea treatment options range from invasive options to simple non-invasive treatments and lifestyle alterations, including:<\/p>\n

    Lifestyle changes<\/h3>\n
      \n
    • Quitting tobacco usage<\/li>\n
    • Working to lose weight<\/li>\n
    • Exercising frequently to improve oxygen and blood flow in the body<\/li>\n
    • Changing positions as you sleep<\/li>\n
    • Taking prescriptions for allergies<\/li>\n
    • Using nasal decongestants<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Oral devices<\/h2>\n

      Your dentist might recommend you use oral devices that help with breathability at night, including a special mouthguard made to improve this condition. It will hold your jaw in a specific position to keep your airway open as you sleep.<\/p>\n

      Surgical procedure<\/h2>\n

      This treatment option is typically used for severe cases.<\/p>\n

        \n
      • Jaw surgery \u2013 it involves shifting the upper and lower jaw to enlarge the space between your tongue and soft palate.<\/li>\n
      • Uvulopalatopharyngoplasty (UPPP) \u2013 it involves the removal of tissue in the back of your mouth that can potentially obstruct the airway.<\/li>\n
      • Implants \u2013 these prosthetics are inserted to hold your soft palate and reduce the collapsing of muscles as you breathe at night.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
