For the first time since its construction, the Silicon Valley house where the social media platform, Facebook (now known as Meta), was created, has hit the market for $5.3 million, The Post can report. <\/p>\n

The unassuming blue house with a painted yellow door was first constructed back in 1998.<\/p>\n

Made up of six bedrooms and 4\u00bd bathrooms, the Los Altos property had only ever been up for rent since 2004. <\/p>\n

And its first tenants? <\/p>\n

Then 19-year-olds Mark Zuckerberg and Dustin Moskovitz, along with 24-year-old Sean Parker, who cofounded what is now a multi-billion-dollar global company.<\/p>\n

Judy Fusco was looking to rent her newly refurbished home that August when Zuckerberg and Moskovitz, now 37, and Parker, now 42, stopped by to look at the house. <\/p>\n

Dustin Moskovitz, Mark Zuckerberg and Sean Parker were roommates in the early days of Facebook.Getty Images (3) <\/p>\n

\u201cMark stood outside and never went into the house, while Sean and Dustin ran up the front stairs to see the house,\u201d Fusco recalled to The Post in an interview. \u201cMark stood there and asked if they could rent the place, without even looking inside.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI read the check and asked him what he did, and he told me about a company called Facebook, and how he planned to connect the world,\u201d Fusco said, adding that Zuckerberg was wearing black jersey shorts, sandals and his trademark hoodie at the time. \u201cI said, \u2018I don\u2019t care if you are going to connect the world, if this check does not pass, you\u2019re not moving in.\u2019 \u201d<\/p>\n

The\u00a0now-embattled tech prodigy\u00a0wrote her a check on the spot.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The first deposit check written by Mark Zuckerberg for the Facebook cofounders\u2019 lease of the house they eventually called Casa de Facebook. Their rent was $5,500 a month. <\/p>\n

A first-time landlord herself, she didn\u2019t think much about the reason for Zuckerberg\u2019s urgency. But what she didn\u2019t know was that the motivated teens were forced to move out of their previous Palo Alto pad following an incident involving a zip line tied around a chimney, leading down to the pool \u2014\u00a0the scene immortalized in the film \u201cThe Social Network.\u201d<\/p>\n

The sunroom at Casa de Facebook. Realtor.com <\/p>\n

Requesting first month\u2019s rent and a deposit totaling $10,000, two weeks later, on Sept. 14, 2004, they moved in, along with some unforeseen guests.<\/p>\n

\u201cThere were only six bedrooms, and the sunroom alone had about 10 interns staying there huddled up in bunk beds,\u201d Fusco said. \u201cThe home just exploded with engineers and Facebook employees,\u201d not unlike the \u201chacker house\u201d featured in HBO\u2019s \u201cSilicon Valley.\u201d<\/p>\n

Features of the home, which Zuckerberg referred to as \u201cCasa de Facebook,\u201d include a living room with bay windows and a balcony, an entertainment room with lots of natural light, the listing notes. The kitchen comes with stainless-steel appliances, a center island and a breakfast nook.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis slice of Silicon Valley history sits at the end of a quiet cul-de-sac with a personalized soundwall in the expansive backyard and gazebo perfect for BBQs and get-togethers,\u201d the listing states. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis home is perfect for multigenerational living with two owners\u2019 suites, and bedrooms on various levels,\u201d the listing suggests. <\/p>\n

Crystal Souza and Hannah Isaka of Live Play Real Estate hold the listing. <\/p>\n

\u201cAfter scheduling a listing presentation with Judy, I was happy to have the opportunity to show this strong, successful woman the business I am building with Live Play Real Estate,\u201d Souza told The Post. <\/p>\n

\u201cWhen she told me she would like to move forward with the listing, I was honored and eager to get started and take on the challenge of finding the perfect buyers for this unique home.\u201d<\/p>\n

The founding employees would lease the home for a year, before they needed a much larger space to hold the growing company. Parker would be the first to move out of the home \u2014 in January\u2014\u00a0after developing an allergy from the jasmine flower that would bloom at night. <\/p>\n

Before renting the house \u2014\u00a0which took three years to complete \u2014 Fusco recalled inviting a monk over to bless the property. The monk had predicted that someone \u201cwho was going to be very rich and famous will come live here. <\/p>\n

\u201cSean [Parker] would come to me repeatedly, asking me to invest, telling me I would be a billionaire one day if I did,\u201d Fusco admitted. \u201cThey proposed giving me stock instead of the rent \u2014 a dollar a stock. I said no. And looking back, I always think about the monk that had come to my house. I had no idea that would be the Facebook brand. I had a daughter to put through school, I was afraid to take the risk at the time. But Sean was right.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI guess I just wasn\u2019t meant to be a billionaire,\u201d Fusco said. \u201cThat was not my fate.\u201d<\/p>\n
