Answer: Cheese can typically sit out at room temperature anywhere from 4 to 8 hours, depending on the type, and remain safe to eat. What happens if you eat cheese that was left out?<\/h3>\n

Does packaged cheese go bad if not refrigerated?<\/p>\n

Soft cheeses such as cream cheese, cottage cheese, shredded cheeses, and goat cheese must be refrigerated for safety. As a general rule, hard cheeses such as cheddar, processed cheeses (American), and both block and grated Parmesan do not require refrigeration for safety, but they will last longer if kept refrigerated.<\/p>\n

Should cheese be served at room temperature?<\/p>\n

Cheese should be served at room temperature, which is ideally between 67 and 70 degrees Fahrenheit. Cool temperatures can compromise the flavors that our taste buds can detect.<\/p>\n

Is there cheese that doesn\u2019t need to be refrigerated?<\/p>\n

Typically, processed cheeses like American cheese don\u2019t need to be refrigerated either, at least until you open them. Some common examples of these are canned cheese, squeezable cheese, and sealed loaves or blocks of American cheese.<\/p>\n

Why does cheese need to be refrigerated?<\/p>\n

Some types of cheese and meat products require refrigeration and others do not. Some cheeses and meats have a low moisture content, higher level of acid, have been smoked, or contain ingredients that help control bacterial growth.<\/p>\n

Does cheese go bad?<\/p>\n

Know when to say when: Shelf lives vary from cheese to cheese. Once opened, hard cheeses like cheddar and Swiss will stay fresh three to four weeks in your fridge, while softer varieties like ricotta, Brie and Bel Paese will hold up for about one to two weeks.<\/p>\n

Can you leave Parmesan cheese out?<\/p>\n
