\u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d paid tribute to future Supreme Court Justice Ketanji Brown Jackson in a cold open that had her visiting the ghosts of historical figures in the Oval Office.<\/p>\n

Jackson, played by Ego Nwodim, was greeted by James Austin Johnson\u2019s President Joe Biden, who she teased about not being able to pronounce her name.<\/p>\n

\u201cBet you can\u2019t say that three times fast,\u201d Nwodim said.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m shocked I was able to say it one time slow,\u201d Johnson admitted, before bragging about fulfilling his campaign promise to place a black woman on the nation\u2019s highest court.<\/p>\n

\u201cOne campaign promise down, 74 more to go,\u201d the fake Biden cracked.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was happy to do my part. Work twice as hard as white man all my life, then spend a week listening to Ted Cruz call me a pedophile,\u201d the new justice said of her contentious confirmation hearings.<\/p>\n

Biden then left Jackson in his office to commune with the spirits of Americans that paved the way for her historic confirmation.<\/p>\n

Kate McKinnon\u2019s Ruth Bader Ginsberg delivered one of her trademark \u201cGins-burns\u201d about Cruz. Kenan Thompson\u2019s Thurgood Marshall and Punkie Johnson\u2019s Harriet Tubman offered their best wishes while Chris Redd\u2019s Jackie Robinson was shocked to hear that even \u201cbad\u201d modern baseball players are millionaires.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

After the opening, host Jake Gyllenhaal, 41, lamented that he hadn\u2019t hosted the NBC show since 2007, when he was promoting \u201cBrokeback Mountain,\u201d and was worried that he forgot how to do it.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat was like 400 Marvel movies ago,\u201d he cracked.<\/p>\n

His nerves quickly gave way to confidence as he launched into a soaring rendition of \u201cIt\u2019s All Coming Back to Me Now\u201d by Celine Dion.<\/p>\n

Jake Gyllenhaal poked fun at his 2007 monologue, in which he dressed in drag and sang a rendition of \u201cAnd I Am Telling You I\u2019m Not Going\u201d from \u201cDreamgirls.\u201dYouTube\/ Saturday Night Live <\/p>\n

Kenan Thompson hosted a game show called \u201cWhy\u2019d You Like It,\u201d where contestants were put on the spot to reveal the true nature of why they liked specific Instagram posts.<\/p>\n

Under interrogation, Gyllenhaal eventually spilled the beans about why he double tapped a thirst trap photo of an attractive woman in a bikini.<\/p>\n

\u201cI guess some part of me thought that if I liked it she would see that I liked it and then she would follow me back and then we\u2019d DM and then maybe at some point, I don\u2019t know, she\u2019d want to have sex with me,\u201d Gyllenhaal admitted.<\/p>\n

\u201cJust to be clear your girlfriend is here tonight,\u201d Thompson said.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou are a stupid man,\u201d Nwodim, playing his girlfriend, angrily chided.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

In a pre-taped sketch, Gyllenhaal and Mikey Day played reality show renovators that were forced to alter their plans for Heidi Gardner\u2019s dream house to please the overbearing religious mother of her \u201cquiet milquetoast husband Pat \u2014 who is deeply uncomfortable on camera,\u201d feebly played by Austin Johnson.<\/p>\n

Mikey Day and Jake Gyllenhaal spoofed HGTV with \u201cDream Home Cousins.\u201dYouTube\/Saturday Night Live <\/p>\n

Gyllenhaal broke into song again in two consecutive skits \u2014 first as part a middle aged cabaret group that sung about their mundane accomplishments in a tune called \u201cThat\u2019s Enough For Me,\u201d and then as part of an ensemble of flowers that didn\u2019t appreciate being pollinated and choked by horny bees and weeds.<\/p>\n

Next, Gyllenhaal played a corporate human resources manager tasked with mediating a violent dispute between catty coworkers Chloe Fineman, Villase\u00f1or, Nwodim and a pint size \u201cChucky,\u201d played by Sarah Sherman.<\/p>\n

Gyllenhaal then retook center stage, portraying a sickly Doc Holiday who was no longer quick on the draw in a spoof of \u201cWyatt Earp,\u201d and a man in couple\u2019s counseling whose therapist had her own relationship issues.<\/p>\n
