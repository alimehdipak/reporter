What size ceiling fan do I need for a 12\u00d712 room?<\/h3>\n

A 42-inch ceiling fan is just right for a 12\u00d712 room. As you can see, the size of ceiling fans is measured in the diameter of the blades.<\/p>\n

How is a 52 inch ceiling fan measured?<\/p>\n

Measuring the Span of Your Ceiling Fan<\/p>\n

If your ceiling fan has an even number of blades, simply measure from one blade tip across to the opposite side\u2019s blade tip. This is your fan blade span or diameter.<\/p>\n

What size room is a 52 inch ceiling fan for?<\/p>\n

Buy the Right Size<\/p>\n

Select a fan with the appropriate span: 36 inches for rooms up to 100 square feet, 42 inches for up to 200 square feet, 52 inches for up to 400 square feet, and either one 60-inch fan or two 52- or 56-inch fans for rooms larger than 400 square feet.<\/p>\n

Is a 54 inch ceiling fan too big for a bedroom?<\/p>\n

The ALA recommends a 29- to 36-inch fan for a room up to 75 square feet, such as a bathroom; a 36- to 42-inch fan for a medium-size room up to 144 square feet; and a 50- to 54-inch fan for a room around 225 square feet; 54- and 60-inch or larger fans are available for larger spaces. 2.<\/p>\n

What size room do I need for a 44 inch ceiling fan?<\/h3>\n

Rooms 144 to 225 square feet = 44 inches.<\/p>\n

What size room do I need for a 42 inch ceiling fan?<\/p>\n

Ceiling Fans Between 42 and 48 Inches<\/p>\n

Most ceiling fans are between 42 and 48 inches in size, and they work well for spaces from 75 to 175 square feet. This includes large bathrooms, large bedrooms, medium-sized living rooms, and kitchens.<\/p>\n

How many blades on a ceiling fan is best?<\/p>\n

Three fan blades are ideal for the optimal efficiency of indoor and outdoor ceiling fans. However, fans with four or five blades will provide adequate air circulation yet have other considerations regarding the installation and overall performance, such as weight, noise, and power draw.<\/p>\n

What size should a bedroom fan be?<\/p>\n

Ceiling Fans for Medium-Sized Rooms<\/p>\n

These dimensions are typical of a standard bedroom or kitchen \u2014 both of which can become quickly uncomfortable if there isn\u2019t enough air circulation. We recommend a ceiling fan with a blade span of 52- to 60-inches to ensure optimal airflow.<\/p>\n

Does the number of blades on a ceiling fan matter?<\/h3>\n

Fans with more blades are known to be quieter than those with fewer blades. Further, fans with more blades tend to be better balanced and less ambient noise. Consider the size of your space and your needs when you choose your fan.<\/p>\n

How many square feet is a 10\u00d710 room?<\/p>\n

How many square feet is a 10\u00d710 room? The square footage of a room 10 feet wide by 10 feet long is 100 square feet. Find the square footage by multiplying the width (10 ft) by the length (10 ft).<\/p>\n

How far should a ceiling fan be from the wall?<\/p>\n

Ceiling fans should be installed, or mounted, in the middle of the room and at least 7 feet above the floor and 18 inches from the walls. If ceiling height allows, install the fan 8 \u2013 9 feet above the floor for optimal airflow.<\/p>\n

What happens if you put a big fan in a small room?<\/p>\n

It\u2019s important to understand how size and height affect the fan\u2019s efficiency and overall look of a room. A ceiling fan that\u2019s too big will overpower the room\u2019s design and visually pull the ceiling downwards; a fan that\u2019s too small won\u2019t properly circulate the air.<\/p>\n

Do ceiling fans add value to house?<\/p>\n

Add Ceiling Fans<\/p>\n

Ceiling fans come in a wide range of prices depending on the size, style, and quality of ceiling fan. Adding ceiling fans to rooms like bedrooms, the living room, or family room will add value to your home immediately.<\/p>\n

Can you buy a ceiling fan too big?<\/h3>\n

Ceiling fans can be too big for a room, resulting in low airflow and less cooling. However, ceiling fans can also be too small, which creates too much airflow. Some tips for sizing your ceiling fan include measuring the longest wall, measuring floor-to-ceiling, and considering light kits.<\/p>\n
