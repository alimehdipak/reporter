The importance of air duct cleaning<\/strong><\/p>\n

Your family breathes the same air flowing through the ducts every day. If left unchecked, your home’s plumbing can contain allergens, such as pet dander and pollen, that can return to your home and cause health problems. These pollutants can cause health problems for people with allergies, asthma and other respiratory conditions. But when you clean the ducts in your home, you’ll help improve air quality and keep your environment free of airborne microbes.<\/p>\n

Dirt can restrict airflow to the HVAC system. This means your AC furnace has to work harder and use more energy to do its job. However, by keeping the air ducts clean and clear, you will ensure maximum efficiency and performance of your equipment.<\/p>\n

Dirty or clogged ventilation systems can lead to damaged coils, contamination, and other problems that often result in expensive repairs. Incorporating duct cleaning into routine maintenance will extend the life of your HVAC system.<\/p>\n

    When to clean your home’s air ducts<\/strong><\/p>\n

    Air ducts are passages that allow cooling and heating air to flow through your home. Similar to any other HVAC system, these ducts also require regular maintenance to ensure maximum efficiency. According to the National Air Duct Cleaners Association (NADCA), you should clean your air ducts every 3 to 5 years. With some methods, cleaning recommendations can be extended to 6-8 years. You might consider cleaning your air ducts right away to reduce the buildup of mold and other contaminants that can cause allergic reactions and other health problems for you and your family.<\/p>\n
