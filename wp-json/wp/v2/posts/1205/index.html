The Sunshine State is the new Silicon Valley.<\/p>\n

Florida solidified its standing as a national tech hub last year \u2014 adding more new companies in the sector than any other state.<\/p>\n

According to a report from the trade group CompTIA, Florida added 2,715 tech businesses last year \u2014 ahead of both Texas and California.<\/p>\n

Florida had the second-highest number of tech jobs added last year at 10,522 \u2014 second to Texas which gained 10,851 positions.<\/p>\n

Much of Florida\u2019s tech growth was concentrated in Miami, which added 2,072 positions in the field last year.<\/p>\n

New York \u2014 which still ranks third in the number of total tech jobs behind California and Texas \u2014 did not make the top 10 in either category, CompTIA found.<\/p>\n

Driven by rising costs and challenging tax structures, established tech companies and startups have been looking outside of traditional business centers in recent years.<\/p>\n

The trade group CompTIA said that Florida added 2,715 tech businesses in 2021. CompTIA Florida is the fourth biggest employer of tech workers with 446,000 jobs. Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Industry analysts have also attributed tech booms in Florida, Texas and other states to the establishment of remote employment during the COVID-19 pandemic.<\/p>\n

Despite this flight, California still reigned comfortably as the biggest tech employer in the nation with 1.4 million jobs last year, according to the study.<\/p>\n

Texas was second with 790,000, New York third at 506,000 and Florida fourth at 446,000, the report showed.<\/p>\n

The ascent of both Florida and Texas in the tech realm has been accompanied by population surges in both states.<\/p>\n

Florida added 211,000 residents in 2021 while Texas increased by 310,000, according to new U.S. Census data.<\/p>\n

California shed 262,000 residents last year while New York\u2019s population shrank by 400,000.<\/p>\n
