A 33-year-old man lost his life savings after getting involved with a cryptocurrency trader who died, taking the password to access funds to the grave.<\/p>\n

Tong Zou is one of thousands of customers left seriously out of pocket following\u00a0Gerald \u2018Gerry\u2019 Cotten\u2018s mysterious death in 2018 at the age of 30.<\/p>\n

Zou trusted Quadriga CX, Canada\u2019s largest cryptocurrency exchange, with C$500,000.<\/p>\n

A big chunk of it was given to him by his parents.<\/p>\n

But when co-founder Cotten died during a honeymoon in\u00a0India, the money was seemingly lost forever.<\/p>\n

The shocking story has been recounted in a new Netflix documentary, Trust No One: The Hunt For The Crypto King.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt just makes me more depressed about it,\u201d Zou told\u00a0Sky News.<\/p>\n

\u201cI could have invested it in real estate. I could have put it in stocks.<\/p>\n

\u201cSo far, nothing\u2019s been found. It sucks.\u201d<\/p>\n

Quadriga\u2019s accounts are estimated to have held $250 million.<\/p>\n

An official investigation concluded that Quadriga was\u00a0\u201can old-fashioned\u00a0fraud\u00a0wrapped in modern technology\u201d.<\/p>\n

Some 76,000 investors like Zou collectively lost out.<\/p>\n

At first, the now-closed company allegedly blamed the delay on a legal issue with a bank.<\/p>\n

\u201cI kept asking them: Where\u2019s my money? \u2013 October, November, December \u2013 during all that time,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey kept saying it was the lawsuit.<\/p>\n

\u201cI couldn\u2019t get any sleep. I just prayed. I really prayed it wasn\u2019t a scam.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy parents were worried about it too.<\/p>\n

\u201cAt that time, there was nothing I could do. There was no way of getting my money back.<\/p>\n

\u201cOnce I deposited it, it was basically gone.\u201d<\/p>\n

Is Gerald \u2018Gerry\u2019 Cotten really dead?<\/h2>\n

Some speculate that Cotten, who suffered with\u00a0Crohn\u2019s disease, might actually be alive.<\/p>\n

He died just nine days into his honeymoon due to an acute stomach ache.<\/p>\n

His wife Jennifer Robertson said he had promised to create a mechanism that turned the\u00a0passwords\u00a0over to her when he died.<\/p>\n

She says she never received the passwords.<\/p>\n
