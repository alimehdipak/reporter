Victoria Beckham didn\u2019t \u201cWannabe\u201d on a scale in front of a live TV audience in the late 1990s.<\/p>\n

But alas, the Spice Girls member, 48, was coerced into weighing herself on the British chat show \u201cTFI Friday\u201d in May 1999 \u2014 just two months after welcoming her son Brooklyn.<\/p>\n

Posh Spice recalled the embarrassing event during an interview with Vogue Australia.<\/p>\n

\u201cI went on a TV show with Chris Evans many years ago and I\u2019d just had Brooklyn and lost a lot of weight after,\u201d the designer said of the humiliating incident. \u201cIt happened to my mum after her pregnancies. It doesn\u2019t mean you have an eating disorder. And he made me stand on the scales to be weighed.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cCan you imagine doing that nowadays?\u201d\u00a0Beckham added.<\/p>\n

In the clip, Evans, 56, asked the English superstar about her new baby and her husband, footballer David Beckham. \u201cA lot of girls want to know, because you look fantastic again, how did you get back to your shape after birth?\u201d\u00a0Evans inquired.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

Beckham revealed at the time that she wasn\u2019t working out as much because she was \u201clazy.\u201d <\/p>\n

The radio host then asked if her weight was \u201cback to normal.\u201d The fashion icon noted that her weight was fine and Evans took out a body scale from under his desk. <\/p>\n

He petitioned her to get on it and implored her: \u201cCan I check, do you mind?\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Victoria Beckham was snapped while pregnant with son Brooklyn in 1998 in London.PA Images via Getty Images Beckham steps onto the scale.Channel 4 <\/p>\n

Beckham, who called the stunt \u201chorrible,\u201d sheepishly stepped on the scale and Evans revealed the number to his audience. \u201cEight stone\u2019s [112 lbs.] not bad at all, is it?\u201d\u00a0he said at the time.<\/p>\n

Beckham also discussed in her interview with the Australian publication the names that people have called her over the course of her career. <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019ve had \u2018Porky Posh,\u2019 I\u2019ve had \u2018Skeletal Posh.\u2019 After I had Brooklyn, there was a picture pointing to every single part of my body where I had to focus on losing the weight from,\u201d she recounted.<\/p>\n

The mother of four also told Grazia\u00a0earlier this year about how the idea of being too thin is \u201cold-fashioned.\u201d<\/p>\n

Beckham \u2014 who loves eating \u201cwhole-grain toast with salt on it\u201d \u2014 explained that women nowadays \u201cwant to look healthy, and curvy. They want to have some boobs \u2014 and a bum.\u201d<\/p>\n

When asked about her own small physique, she responded: \u201cIt\u2019s not about being a certain size. It\u2019s about knowing who you are and being happy with who you are. I have found my own balance between wanting to have fun and being disciplined about eating healthily and working out. When you\u2019re younger you fight against that balance, but through being older I\u2019ve reached a place where I know what that balance looks like. I just know what works for me.\u201d<\/p>\n
