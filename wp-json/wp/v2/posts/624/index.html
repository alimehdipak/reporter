Francis Ford Coppola\u2019s operatic mob drama, \u201cThe Godfather,\u201d premiered on March 24, 1972.<\/p>\n

The landmark film, up there with \u201cCitizen Kane\u201d in terms of cinematic style and influence, altered the career trajectories of its stars \u2014 including James Caan, who played fiery, doomed Santino \u201cSonny\u201d Corleone, the eldest son of Don Vito Corleone (Marlon Brando) and brother to Fredo (John Cazale), Connie (Talia Shire) and Michael (Al Pacino).<\/p>\n

Prior to his breakout, Oscar-nominated role in \u201cThe Godfather,\u201d The Bronx-born Caan was best-known for roles on a slew of \u201960s-era television series \u2014 including \u201cThe Untouchables,\u201d \u201cThe Alfred Hitchcock Hour,\u201d \u201cGet Smart,\u201d \u201cWagon Train,\u201d \u201cNaked City\u201d and \u201cRoute 66\u201d \u2014 and for playing cancer-stricken Chicago Bears running back Brian Piccolo opposite Billy Dee Williams (as Gale Sayers) in \u201cBrian\u2019s Song,\u201d ABC\u2019s tearjerking Movie of the Week which aired in November 1971.<\/p>\n

(He also appeared in several movies, including \u201cThe Rain People\u201d in 1969, directed by Coppola \u2014 Caan\u2019s classmate at Hofstra \u2014 and co-starring Robert Duvall, aka Corleone Family consigliere<\/em> Tom Hagen.)<\/p>\n

Caan, who turns 82 on Saturday (March 26), spoke to The Post in a wide-ranging interview about his memories of filming \u201cThe Godfather,\u201d which airs somewhere on television virtually every day \u2014 in all of its iterations \u2014 and will be re-released Tuesday (March 22) in a comprehensive 4K ultra-high-definition Blu-ray package.<\/p>\n

\u201cOne of the things that made \u2018The Godfather\u2019 successful, besides brilliant directing and writing and wonderful actors \u2026 was that everyone really enjoyed making it, and that comes off on the screen,\u201d Caan said. \u201cAnd I think the audience can tell that we were having a good time doing what we were doing up there.<\/p>\n

Sonny and Don Vito Corleone (James Caan, Marlon Brando) in a scene from \u201cThe Godfather.\u201dGetty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cMarlon was great,\u201d Caan said of Brando. \u201cHe had a great sense of humor, but he would have trouble figuring out the f\u2014ing punch line sometimes. We\u2019d go to lunch \u2014 those stupid Polish jokes were coming out at the time \u2026 and then two hours later, in the middle of a scene, I\u2019d look at him and say something and all the sudden [imitates Brando laughing] I\u2019d say, \u2018What the f\u2013k\u2019s the matter with you?\u2019 and he said [referring to the joke] \u2018That\u2019s funny.\u2019 Two hours later it was playing in his f\u2014ing head. He was like a child like that. I loved him.\u201d<\/p>\n

Caan recalled how Coppola asked him to loosen up Lenny Montana, a hulking former pro wrestler and bodyguard for the Colombo crime family making his screen debut as gruff, feared Corleone enforcer Luca Brasi \u2014 who first appears in the opening scene during the wedding of Don Vito\u2019s daughter, Connie.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe knocks on the door and he says, \u2018Don Corleone, I am honored to be here on the day of your daughter\u2019s wedding, may her first child \u2026\u2019 He did it a couple of times, the door opens, with camera on the other side of the door with Marlon. Francis comes running out and said to me, \u2018Jimmy, do something!\u2019 I said, \u2018What are you talking about Francis?\u2019 He said, \u2018Do something with him. I said, \u2018What do you want me to do with that f\u2014king beast?\u2019 \u2018Do something to goose him up, do some of your s\u2013t.\u2019 He was stiff, and Francis was mad that he couldn\u2019t get more out of him than [said in flat Luca Brasi voice] \u2018Don Corleone.\u2019<\/p>\n

Brothers Sonny and Michael Corleone (James Caan, Al Pacino) in the wedding scene from \u201cThe Godfather.\u201dGetty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cSo I grab Lenny and said, \u2018Lenny, come here. You gotta do something when you open the door.\u2019 I was trying to give him this snow job. \u2018When you say, \u201cDon Corleone,\u201d stick your tongue out\u2019 \u2014 his tongue looked like a shoebox for f\u2013k\u2019s sake \u2014 \u2018and I\u2019ll put a piece of tape on it that says \u201cF\u2013k you.\u201d He was like a baby, \u2018Oh, Jimmy, don\u2019t make me do this,\u2019 and I said, \u2018Lenny, please, it\u2019s humor, everyone\u2019s gonna laugh and you\u2019ll feel better. Please do this for me.\u2019<\/p>\n

\u201cSo I get a piece of tape and put it on his tongue \u2026 boom, they say \u2018Action!\u2019 and that\u2019s when he says, \u201cDon Corleone\u201d \u2014 he sticks his tongue out and everybody falls down laughing. He was smiling. He was happy. Marlon\u2019s line was, \u2018Luca, my most cherished friend,\u2019 and when he did that scene he had \u201cF\u2013k you\u201d written on his tongue, too.\u201d<\/p>\n

Caan also said he used a bit of on-the-spot improvisation in the scene where Sonny beats up his pregnant sister\u2019s husband, Carlo (Gianni Russo) on the street with his fists, his legs and the top of a garbage can.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe stick I threw at [Carlo] when he\u2019s running away, that wasn\u2019t in the script,\u201d he said. \u201cI took one of those industrial brooms and cut the end off and put it under my seat [in the car as Sonny drives up to confront Carlo]. They said, \u2018It\u2019s not in the script\u2019 and I said, \u2018What the f\u2013k\u2019s the difference, just put it down there.\u2019 I swear to God I had no idea what I was going to do with it, but I knew that\u2019s what a lot of guys did in my neighborhood. We called them \u2018attitude adjusters.\u2019 I just grabbed it and fired [the stick] at him and Francis said, \u2018That\u2019s great, Jimmy, you looked like you were going to miss him.\u2019 So he\u2019s lying behind the cars on the other side and luckily, on the upswing, I caught him on the top of the coconut.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sonny, bloody Sonny: gunned down in a hail of bullets at a tollbooth.Getty Images James Caan in 2017. He was born and raised in The Bronx.FilmMagic <\/p>\n

Caan said he also went back to his Bronx roots for the scene in which Sonny confronts the FBI outside the gates of the Corleone compound on the day of Connie\u2019s wedding _ and for Sonny\u2019s iconic black-and-white shoes.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe [FBI] guy took a picture of me with one of those old square box cameras,\u201d he said. \u201cThis was not written in the script [but] I got so f\u2014king hot that I grabbed his camera and smashed it on the ground. I took like $40 out of my pocket and kept walking \u2014 because, in my neighborhood you paid for it. It didn\u2019t matter what you did, but you paid for it.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe didn\u2019t really have a big budget and we added to it constantly,\u201d he said with a chuckle. \u201cThey said, \u2018The shoes are not in the script\u2019 and I said, \u2018A lot of Italian guys in my old neighborhood, they have maybe two suits to their name but they\u2019ve got 10 pairs of shoes.\u2019 So I went to The Bronx one day and bought those black-and-white shoes for $10 and wore them in the movie.\u201d<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
