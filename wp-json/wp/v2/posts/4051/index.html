PASADENA, Calif. \u2014 Mishael Morgan of \u201cThe Young and the Restless\u201d won as lead drama actress, becoming the first Black actor to win in a lead category at the Daytime Emmys on Friday night.<\/p>\n

Morgan was previously nominated in the supporting category. The 35-year-old from Trinidad and Tobago plays Amanda Sinclair on the CBS soap.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am being honored regardless of the color of my skin, regardless of my passport, for being the best at what I do,\u201d Morgan said. \u201cNow there are little girls all around the world and they\u2019re seeing another step forward and they know that no matter their industry, no matter their vocation, no matter what, they can strive to be the best at what they do. Not only can they achieve it, but they will be celebrated.\u201d<\/p>\n

Morgan has been a member of \u201cThe Young and the Restless\u201d cast since 2013. AP Photo\/Chris Pizzello <\/p>\n

John McCook earned lead actor honors for his role as Eric Forrester on CBS\u2019 \u201cThe Bold and the Beautiful.\u201d It was the 78-year-old\u2019s first win in four nominations.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am so glad to get this Emmy, any Emmy actually,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

\u201cGeneral Hospital\u201d won daytime drama honors, the ABC show\u2019s 15th triumph in the category. It was the fifth trophy earned by the show Friday.<\/p>\n

Morgan is in her second stint on \u201cY&R.\u201d She first joined the soap in 2013 and stayed until 2018. She returned in 2019 as a different character.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am so immensely proud of our generation. We are breaking glass ceilings left, right and center, and I am so honored to be a vessel and experience this moment,\u201d she said. \u201cIt\u2019s because of everybody who is out there today who is proving to the world that we can and we will do this thing called equality and unity together.\u201d<\/p>\n

Nicholas Alexander Chavez, winner of the Outstanding Younger Performer in a Drama Series award, gets emotional during the 49th Daytime Emmy Awards at Pasadena Convention Center on June 24, 2022 in Pasadena, California.Kevin Winter\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

Three \u201cGeneral Hospital\u201d stars won honors and the soap\u2019s directing team won for the third consecutive year.<\/p>\n

Kelly Thiebaud was chosen supporting actress for playing Dr. Britt Westbourne and Jeff Kober scored as supporting actor for his role as Cyrus Renault. Nicholas Chavez won as younger performer in the soap.<\/p>\n

\u201cWow!\u201d Chavez said. \u201cJust a little over a year ago I was selling cars in a little beach town in Florida. I always had the love and support of my family, but none of us had ever dreamed I\u2019d be up on this stage.\u201d<\/p>\n

General Hospital\u2019s Kelly Thiebaud takes home the award for Outstanding Performance by a Supporting Actress in a Drama Series award. Getty Images <\/p>\n

A gleeful Thiebaud won on her first nomination.<\/p>\n

\u201cI cannot believe this. I am so shocked,\u201d she said. \u201cMy brother, when I first started acting, he followed my work and he\u2019s like, \u2019You\u2019re not that good.\u2019 So thank you for giving me something to rub in his face.\u201d<\/p>\n

Kober is known for his extensive film and primetime work on such shows as \u201cSons of Anarchy\u201d and \u201cThe Walking Dead.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWow, thank you for making me feel so welcome in daytime. What a trip,\u201d he said. \u201cI love what we get to do for a living, it\u2019s play.\u201d<\/p>\n

NBC\u2019s \u201cDays of Our Lives\u201d won for writing team.<\/p>\n

Kelly Clarkson earned her third consecutive trophy as entertainment talk show host. Her eponymous show also won its second straight trophy for entertainment talk show. The singer is spending the summer with her children and didn\u2019t attend.<\/p>\n

Although Kelly Clarkson wasn\u2019t present, The Kelly Clarkson Show still won big at the 49th Daytime Emmy Awards. Getty Images <\/p>\n

Tamron Hall picked up her second straight trophy as informative talk show host.<\/p>\n

Steve Harvey of \u201cFamily Feud\u201d won as game show host.<\/p>\n

\u201cJeopardy!\u201d won game show honors.<\/p>\n

\u201c\u2019Jeopardy!\u2019 is a show about facts,\u201d executive producer Michael Davies said. \u201cFacts, with everything going on in the world, are more important than ever. \u2018Jeopardy!\u2019 is the last place where everyone agrees about facts.\u201d<\/p>\n

Daytime doyenne Susan Lucci, who starred on \u201cAll My Children\u201d during the show\u2019s entire 41-year network run, received a standing ovation. She introduced the In Memoriam segment, which included a photo of her husband, Helmet Huber, who died in March. Michael Bolton crooned his hit \u201cHow Am I Supposed to Live Without You.\u201d<\/p>\n

Susan Lucci speaks onstage during the 49th Daytime Emmy Awards at Pasadena Convention Center on June 24, 2022 in Pasadena, California. Kevin Winter\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

John Aniston of \u201cDays of Our Lives\u201d was honored with a lifetime achievement award for his 37-year role as Victor Kiriakis. The 88-year-old actor wasn\u2019t present.<\/p>\n

He was introduced via video by his daughter, Jennifer Aniston, who said, \u201cHis career is literally the definition of lifetime achievement.\u201d She noted he began acting in 1962 in primetime and also appeared in such defunct soaps as \u201cLove of Life\u201d and \u201cSearch For Tomorrow\u201d before joining \u201cDays\u201d in 1985.<\/p>\n

After two years of pre-taped presentations because of the coronavirus pandemic, daytime returned to honoring its own at nighttime with an in-person ceremony. In 2020, the show went virtual for the first time and last year the audience was limited.<\/p>\n

Still, the specter of COVID-19 was felt.<\/p>\n

Two nominees from \u201cThe Young and the Restless\u201d were forced to bow out. Lead actor nominee Jason Thompson, who plays Billy Abbott, and supporting actress nominee Melissa Ordway, who plays Abby Newman, both said on social media they had tested positive for COVID-19 and would miss the show.<\/p>\n

Kevin Frazier and Nischelle Turner of \u201cEntertainment Tonight\u201d co-hosted the 49th annual awards held at the Pasadena Convention Center, where many of the nominees sat at socially distanced cocktail tables and others sat in rows next to each other.<\/p>\n

Kevin Frazier, Drew Barrymore and Nischelle Turner speak onstage during the 49th Daytime Emmy Awards at Pasadena Convention Center on June 24, 2022 in Pasadena, California.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Frazier and Turner found themselves accepting a Daytime Emmy when \u201cET\u201d won for entertainment news series.<\/p>\n

Turner opened the show with a reference to the Supreme Court overturning women\u2019s constitutional protections for abortions earlier Friday. She emphatically told Frazier it was \u201cher choice\u201d to wear her blue gown. The crowd cheered and some women stood up.<\/p>\n

Supporting actress nominee Nancy Lee Grahn of \u201cGeneral Hospital\u201d walked the indoor red carpet wearing references to the day\u2019s news.
