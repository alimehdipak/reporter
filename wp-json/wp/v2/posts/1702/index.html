Caitlyn Jenner said she was \u201cshadow-banned\u201d by Twitter after signing on as a contributor for the Fox News Channel last month and that she was hopeful Elon Musk would take over the company.<\/p>\n

\u201cI joined Fox News and guess what happened,\u201d Jenner, who was known as former Olympic champion Bruce Jenner before transitioning to a female in 2015, told the network Thursday.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI got shadow-banned on Twitter.\u00a0My engagements went down\u00a0dramatically, and I was\u00a0absolutely shocked.\u201d <\/p>\n

Shadow banning refers to allegations when Twitter sets its algorithms to no longer serve up a user\u2019s tweets as often or as prominently as before, as opposed to yanking someone off the system altogether. Some have accused Twitter of instituting \u201cshadow bans\u201d of controversial figures. It\u2019s not clear if Jenner was actually shadow-banned; The Post has contacted Twitter seeking comment.<\/p>\n

Still, Twitter has been accused of censoring right-leaning voices and de-platforming prominent conservatives, such as former President Donald Trump and others, by either hiding or deleting tweets or by kicking people off the platform altogether, as in the case of Trump.<\/p>\n

Jenner, the former reality television star who appeared on the long-running E! hit show \u201cKeeping Up With The Kardashians,\u201d told Fox News that she \u201c100%\u201d backs Musk in his efforts to take over Twitter.<\/p>\n

Jenner said she \u201c100%\u201d supports Elon Musk\u2019s bid to buy Twitter and take it private.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cI love Elon Musk, I\u00a0agree with him 100%,\u201d\u00a0she told FNC. \u201cI love Twitter \u2026\u00a0I joined in 2015 and broke the\u00a0world record \u2026 fastest\u00a0ever to 1 million followers. I did\u00a0it in 4 hours and 2\u00a0minutes. I\u2019ve used Twitter for\u00a0years, and enjoy using it.\u201d<\/p>\n

But after Jenner signed with FNC, she says, her Twitter reach decreased.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy reach is much less today,\u201d she said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI hope Elon Musk comes in here\u00a0and changes this.\u00a0Because these woke companies are\u00a0not going to survive \u2014 I don\u2019t\u00a0think \u2014 in the future.\u201d<\/p>\n

Jenner said her experience shows that tech giants like Twitter are \u201ccaptive to their employees, these woke employees in this woke movement we have out there.\u201d<\/p>\n

Jenner joined the Fox News Channel last month as a contributor.WireImage <\/p>\n

\u201cThere is this woke mentality\u00a0with their employees, instead of\u00a0doing what\u2019s right for the\u00a0company, they are doing what\u00a0they think is right for society\u00a0and right for their employees,\u201d she claimed.<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd it\u2019s killing these\u00a0companies.\u201d<\/p>\n

Last year, Jenner mounted an unsuccessful run for California governor \u2014 coming in 13th place among candidates seeking to oust Gavin Newsom in a special recall election.<\/p>\n
