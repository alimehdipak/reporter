It\u2019s a fashionable get.<\/p>\n

A 35-foot-wide Neoclassical limestone property formerly owned by the late Italian fashion designer Gianni Versace has listed for $70 million, according to its listing brokerage and a report in the Wall Street Journal.<\/p>\n

Not only does that price buy a lasting piece of Versace\u2019s legacy, but it also gets a grand East 64th Street spread with 17 rooms, six stories, some 14,175 square feet of interior space and 3,025 square feet on the outside.<\/p>\n

Versace, who launched his eponymous fashion house in 1978, purchased this home for $7.5 million in 1995 \u2014 or about $14.34 million in today\u2019s figures. Two years later, in July 1997, 27-year-old serial killer Andrew Cunanan murdered Versace, 50, on the front steps of his Miami Beach mansion \u2014 the events of which most recently appeared in the 2018 FX series \u201cThe Assassination of Gianni Versace: American Crime Story.\u201d<\/p>\n

(That Miami Beach property, meanwhile \u2014 located on Ocean Drive and tucked away from the tourist-trap locations of South Beach \u2014 now operates as a high-end hotel named Villa Casa Casuarina.)<\/p>\n

The current owners of the New York property are Swedish hedge-fund manager Thomas Sandell, 61, and his 46-year-old wife Ximena Sandell. The Journal notes they purchased the home in 2005 for $30 million from the Versace family.<\/p>\n

To this day, four floors of the property still feature finishes installed by Versace himself \u2014 among them marble floors, painted ceilings and walls, and classical columns. The Sandells have restored a number of these touches.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe pursued extensive restorations and renovations with an incredible team of artists and artisans honoring the magnificent legacy left by Gianni Versace,\u201d the couple told the Journal in a statement.<\/p>\n

Marble touches abound in the property.Travis Mark for Sotheby\u2019s International Realty An office space with oversize windows.Travis Mark for Sotheby\u2019s International Realty A home gym.Travis Mark for Sotheby\u2019s International Realty <\/p>\n

For instance, the great room has a ceiling with restored 19th-century panels that Versace sourced from a Florentine palazzo.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s like going into the \u2018Alice in Wonderland\u2019 of Gianni Versace,\u201d Nikki Field, of Sotheby\u2019s International Realty \u2014 who shares the listing with her daughter, Amanda Field Jordan \u2014 told the Journal. She added that the couple are \u201cVersace devotees\u201d and they \u201chave been custodians of this piece of art.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s going to be purchased by someone who has that same reverence,\u201d Field added.<\/p>\n

The home can fit up to nine bedrooms.Travis Mark for Sotheby\u2019s International Realty A plush lounging area with a fireplace.Travis Mark for Sotheby\u2019s International Realty Versace died in 1997 at age 50.Ron Galella Collection via Getty <\/p>\n

The top two floors, however, come redesigned. The fifth level has a Moroccan-style media room, as well as a game room that includes arcade games.<\/p>\n

Other perks of the home include French-style windows that look out to a trellised garden below. A balcony that runs along the full length of the rear of the property also overlooks that green space. Up top, a roof deck has a gazebo with views of Fifth and Madison avenues, and a new owner can fit up to nine bedrooms inside. The eat-in kitchen comes newly renovated, the listing notes, and Versace-inspired furnishings are also available for purchase.<\/p>\n

The mansion had previously been on the market, but for rent, according to the Journal. It once asked a cool $100,000 per month. One renter, city developer Joe Chetrit, sued Thomas Sandell, and alleged there were maintenance issues with the home. But Field told the Journal the case was \u201camicably resolved.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Sandells have another property now on the market: an approximately 10-acre piece of land on Meadow Lane in Southampton that asks $75 million.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
