An English beauty pageant queen was once an insecure youngster after a scary incident that resulted in years of agonizing skin grafts and surgery.<\/p>\n

\u201cI just wanted to hide away, until my mom got me into pageants,\u201d Harriotte Lane told Chronicle Live. <\/p>\n

When she was 12 years old, the Newcastle native spilled boiling gravy on her thigh \u2014 which was followed by years of recovery procedures. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe skin on my leg started to peel off and it got infected,\u201d the 21-year-old said. <\/p>\n

\u201cFor four days, it was burning through the tissue of my skin,\u201d Lane recalled, as the gravy\u2019s oil content classified the injury as a \u201cfat burn,\u201d which differs from being hit with hot water. <\/p>\n

\u201cI needed skin grafts and they took the skin from my upper thigh to place on my leg. It took five years to heal and there is still a scar there on my upper thigh,\u201d she said. <\/p>\n

Now, on July 9, Lane will compete in her country\u2019s top beauty pageant, Miss Universe Great Britain. Onstage, she will model swimwear and an evening gown, as well as answer a question in front of the audience. She will also interview with the judges privately.<\/p>\n

Lane still has a large scar on her thigh from undergoing skin grafts and surgery. Instagram\/@thecrowncoach \u201cI just wanted to hide away until my mom got me into pageants,\u201d Lane told Chronicle Live. Instagram\/@thecrowncoach <\/p>\n

Her mom, Sarah, encouraged her to enter her first pageant, Miss Teen Great Britain, in order to bid to build her confidence after her accident. <\/p>\n

Now, she has over 65,700 Instagram followers and documents her journey to the crown \u2014 Lane won Miss International UK 2019, among a number of other competitions \u2014 fundraisers, modeling and travel.<\/p>\n

Lane\u2019s also an entrepreneur, too. When she was 18, she founded her own pageant coaching company where she hosts group and individual classes.<\/p>\n

Over the past nine years, Lane has raised $100,000 for charity, including A-Sisterhood, which helps women in India who have suffered acid attacks.<\/p>\n

For Lane, competing in beauty pageants isn\u2019t about \u201cthe look\u201d \u2014 it\u2019s much more.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt is very much, are you personable? Have you done a great interview? Can you communicate with the audience? Because that is the girl who walks away with the crown.\u201d<\/p>\n

She\u2019s also hellbent on educating others about the beauty of pageants.<\/p>\n

\u201cThere are plenty of stereotypes but actually pageantry is about being a spokesmodel,\u201d she said. \u201cIt\u2019s about being someone who is a feminist, someone who has got a real reason to empower other people. They have often overcome adversities.\u201d<\/p>\n

Lane got into pageants to help gain back confidence after her accident. Instagram\/@thecrowncoach <\/p>\n

Her own adversities are also serving to spread positivity, too.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m going into schools across the northeast to talk to them about removing self-limits and sharing my story. I\u2019m telling them to go for the jobs that they want to do [even though they] might be something society might push them away from,\u201d she continued. <\/p>\n
