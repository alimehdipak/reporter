Chain Drive Openers<\/p>\n

The chains used on residential garage door openers typically have higher tensile strength and greater lifting capacity than belts. They also may last longer in certain applications.<\/p>\n

Belt openers also move a lot quicker than chain openers, so it\u2019s less time waiting in the driveway to get into your garage. While belt garage doors offer durability and strength, there are limitations to their abilities. Belt openers aren\u2019t really recommended for heavy doors, or two-door garages.<\/p>\n

Belt drive systems also run much smoother, with much less jerks as compared to chain drives, and produce incomparably less noise too.<\/p>\n

If you take really good care of your garage door opener, doing regular maintenance and making sure the tension is correct, the belt could last upwards of 20 years. Of course, a lot of this depends on the weight of your door is, the quality of material, and how often you\u2019re opening and closing your door.<\/p>\n

Belt-drive garage door openers are generally more expensive than chain-drive systems. But with a belt-drive system, you get a smoother, quieter, and more durable operation. Many homeowners find that the extra expense is worth it, provided that the belt-drive system is able to lift the door.<\/p>\n

If your old chain garage door opener ceases working or you\u2019re tired of the noise and vibrations, you can switch it out with a belt drive. Since both models use a similar mechanism, switching them out doesn\u2019t amount to a herculean effort.<\/p>\n

While LiftMaster door openers tend to be aimed at the professional market or people with large garages, Chamberlin openers are better suited to you if you\u2019re interested in DIY or have a small garage.<\/p>\n

However, they require an internally-geared hub and are less mechanically efficient than a chain drive.<\/p>\n

Belt Drive Garage Door Openers<\/p>\n

The low-vibration belt is made from polyurethane, steel-reinforced rubber, or fiberglass. Belt drives are the quietest openers and cost a little more than chain drives.<\/p>\n

Both chain drives and belt drives are methods to transfer power and objects within a machine. The main difference between the two drives is that a chain drive is powered by a chain loop, while a belt drive is powered by a belt.<\/p>\n
