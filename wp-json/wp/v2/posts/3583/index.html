Brad Johnson, the actor who appeared on \u201cMelrose Place\u201d and in Steven Spielberg\u2019s film \u201cAlways,\u201d has passed away. He was 62.<\/p>\n

Johnson died from COVID-19 complications on Feb. 18 in Fort Worth, Texas.<\/p>\n

His rep, Linda McAlister, confirmed the news of his death to\u00a0the Hollywood Reporter. <\/p>\n

\u201cAlthough he was taken too early, he lived life to the fullest and taught his children to do the same,\u201d his family said in a statement. <\/p>\n

\u201cBrad greatly enjoyed improving and enhancing land, in a way that maintained and respected its natural beauty,\u201d the statement continued. \u201cHe always felt most at home outdoors, and his passion for the land made that evident. As much as he loved cowboying, hunting and land, Brad loved nothing more than his family.\u201d <\/p>\n

His family members also remembered him as \u201ca true renaissance man\u201d in the obituary dedicated to the soap opera star.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe was not only interested in all life had to offer, but was gifted in it as well,\u201d they remembered. \u201cAlthough he was taken too early, he lived life to the fullest.\u00a0He was larger than life. Brad was loved by many and loved people back, a genuine friend, trusted colleague, and a dedicated husband and father, he will never be forgotten, and his legacy will go on.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cHe was not only interested in all life had to offer, but was gifted in it as well,\u201d family members remembered. \u00a9United Artists\/courtesy Everet \t \t <\/p>\n

Johnson is survived by his wife of 35 years, Laurie, his eldest son, Shane, his daughters Bellamy, Rachel, Eliana, Eden, Rebekah, Annabeth, and his youngest son, William.<\/p>\n

He also earned notoriety as a Calvin Klein model before coming to Hollywood and starred in several cigarette ads as a Marlboro Man.<\/p>\n

In 1989, he caught the attention of director Spielberg and starred in his romantic fantasy drama \u201cAlways\u201d alongside Holly Hunter and Richard Dreyfuss.<\/p>\n

On Fox\u2019s hit \u201990s drama \u201cMelrose Place,\u201d Johnson scored an iconic recurring role as Dr. Dominick O\u2019Malley.<\/p>\n

Johnson was born in October 1959 in Tucson, Arizona, to a horse-trainer father and had a passion for the great outdoors.<\/p>\n

Brad Johnson died Feb. 18, 2022, of complications from COVID-19 in Fort Worth, Texas.\u00a9Aaron Spelling Prods\/Courtesy <\/p>\n

Johnson later became involved in a professional rodeo circuit in 1984 and was scouted by a casting director, in search of cowboys for a new beer commercial, while he wrestled steers in Wyoming.<\/p>\n

Other credits that Johnson had to his name include \u201cSoldier of Fortune, Inc.,\u201d \u201cRiverworld,\u201d \u201cCSI,\u201d \u201cDallas,\u201d \u201cThe Birds II: Land\u2019s End\u201d and \u201cRough Riders.\u201d<\/p>\n
