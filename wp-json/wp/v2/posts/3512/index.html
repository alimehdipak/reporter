A man who made a bet with an unlikely outcome has just eaten his words \u2014 and his shoe. <\/p>\n

Matt Patches, the deputy entertainment editor\u00a0of Polygon who said in 2010 that he\u2019d \u201ceat a shoe\u201d if Top Gun sequel ever happened, was forced to swallow his pride and some laces this week.<\/p>\n

On October 26, 2010, at exactly 7:42 pm., Patches declared on Twitter, \u201cIf\u00a0\u2018Top Gun 2\u2019\u00a0happens, I will eat a shoe.\u201d<\/p>\n

As \u201cTop Gun: Maverick,\u201d the highly-anticipated sequel to the classic \u201980s movie, hits theaters Memorial Day weekend, Patches admitted, \u201cI made a grave error.\u201d<\/p>\n

The original tweet has since been deleted, but that hasn\u2019t stopped it from resurfacing throughout the years. Paramount reportedly even joined in on the joke, sending Patches a cake shaped like a shoe. <\/p>\n

On Thursday, Patches uploaded a video to YouTube, titled \u201cI ate a shoe because of Top Gun 2.\u201d <\/p>\n

The 20 minute parody of the action thriller begins as Patches, doing his best Tom Cruise impression, details the saga that led him to this podiatric predicament, and his eventual decision to make his own edible creation of fruit leather, edible glue, and licorice laces. <\/p>\n

But it was no walk in the park \u2014 except when it was. Even if he couldn\u2019t eat a real shoe, he did simulate one, by walking around his street and lawn in the edible footwear he\u2019d cobbled together.<\/p>\n

\u201cI had a very bad tummy ache that I have survived,\u201d Patches later told Vanity Fair. <\/p>\n
