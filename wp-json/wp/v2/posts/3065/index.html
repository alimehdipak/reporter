Ricky Gervais isn\u2019t dumbing down his humor for the woke crowd.<\/p>\n

The comedian is stirring the online cauldron yet again after claiming that \u201csmart people\u201d don\u2019t get offended by jokes about AIDS, Hitler and other taboo topics. The 60-year-old UK comic dropped the anti-PC bombshell while promoting his new Netflix special, \u201cSuperNature,\u201d on \u201cThe Late Show With Stephen Colbert.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cSmart people know you can deal with anything, particularly when dealing with something like irony,\u201d \u201cThe Office\u201d creator told Colbert, 58.<\/p>\n

During the interview, Gervais explained that he\u2019d added new material to the special, which he\u2019d tried out prior to the coronavirus quarantine. Colbert then asked if the comedian had to switch up his material due to his two-year layoff from stand-up during the lockdown.<\/p>\n

Ricky Gervais (left) has joked that his ultimate goal is to get cancelled.CBS <\/p>\n

That\u2019s when Gervais dropped the bombshell. \u201cIt [his comedy] was always evolving,\u201d the funnyman replied. \u201cThere was a thought, \u2018Will this be out of date?\u2019 And then I realized that when you\u2019re dealing with, you know, famine, AIDS, cancer, Hitler, those dudes are evergreen. Yeah, so they\u2019re not going to, they don\u2019t date.\u201d<\/p>\n

Colbert seemed to agree with his sentiment, saying, \u201cThere is no audience that isn\u2019t going to love that.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cBoo! Not Hitler! It was a long time ago,\u201d Gervais replied facetiously, prompting the host to laughingly reply, \u201cLike, you don\u2019t go to a Ricky Gervais concert for just feel good.\u201d<\/p>\n

However, while the aforementioned topics are certainly sensitive to many, the \u201cAfter Life\u201d star insisted that audiences \u201cdo feel good\u201d and that the ultimate goal \u201cis to make them laugh.\u201d<\/p>\n

Gervais was promoting his new special, \u201cSuperNature,\u201d on \u201cThe Late Show With Stephen Colbert.\u201dCBS <\/p>\n

\u201cThey do laugh, but they know that I deal with taboo subjects,\u201d he explained in his impromptu lecture. \u201cBut I deal with taboo subjects because I want to take the audience to a place it hasn\u2019t been before, and there is a tension.\u201d<\/p>\n

He insisted that too often people get offended by conflating a quip with the actual \u201ctarget,\u201d and that intelligent audiences know how to take things in their comedic context.<\/p>\n

But he\u2019s not just making offensive jokes for the fun of it: Elsewhere in the interview, Gervais touted the healing power of dark humor, equating it to a vaccine against the harsh reality of existence.<\/p>\n

Gervais joked that his ultimate goal is to get canceled.CBS <\/p>\n

\u201cHumor gets us over bad stuff,\u201d declared the Emmy winner. \u201cThat\u2019s why I laugh about terrifying bad things. You know, that\u2019s why comedians are obsessed with death because, you know, it gets us through . . . it\u2019s an inoculation to the real things that are going to happen.\u201d<\/p>\n

And Gervais appears to be putting his funny where his mouth is. In the past, the \u201cDerek\u201d mastermind has taken his rhetorical blowtorch to a variety of supposedly taboo topics, from the Holocaust to pedophilia. <\/p>\n

Gervais equated offensive dark humor to a vaccine against the harsh reality of existence. <\/p>\n

Most recently, the five-time Golden Globe Awards host controversially poked fun at Will Smith slapping Chris Rock for cracking wise about Jada Pinkett Smith at the 2022 Oscars. In a viral Twitter thread, Gervais claimed he wouldn\u2019t have mocked Smith\u2019s hair loss as Rock had done \u2014 but rather would\u2019ve targeted her open relationship. <\/p>\n

Gervais joked that his ultimate goal is to get canceled by the public. <\/p>\n

\u201cOne thing I\u2019ve decided to definitely do, and can\u2019t wait to start on, is my new stand-up show,\u201d the Brit told\u00a0Heat\u00a0magazine while promoting his upcoming show \u201cArmageddon.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cBut I deal with taboo subjects because I want to take the audience to a place it hasn\u2019t been before, and there is a tension,\u201d the funnyman explained.CBS <\/p>\n

Gervais added, \u201cI\u2019m treating it like it\u2019s my last one ever. It won\u2019t be, but I want to put everything into it. I want to try and get canceled. No, I just want to go all out there.\u201d<\/p>\n
