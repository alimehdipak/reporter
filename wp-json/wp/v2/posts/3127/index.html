Pinworms, also known as threadworms, are tiny white or light gray worms that cause the common infection called enterobiasis.<\/p>\n

What do you do if you have little white worms?<\/h3>\n

For most people, treatment will involve taking a single dose of a medication called mebendazole to kill the worms. If necessary, another dose can be taken after 2 weeks. During treatment and for a few weeks afterwards, it\u2019s also important to follow strict hygiene measures to avoid spreading the threadworm eggs.<\/p>\n

Where does white little worms come from?<\/p>\n

You should look for maggots themselves or the appearance of adult flies. Maggots will appear in the form of small white worms in the house wiggling about in the food or filth they are found on. Flies will be hovering around filthy areas like garbage and feces and will breed and lay their eggs.<\/p>\n

Why am I seeing white worms?<\/p>\n

These little \u2018worms\u2019 are nothing to worry about, unless they are severely impairing your vision, so what are they? Floaters\u2019 are caused by tiny fragments of cell debris within the vitreous humour of the eye \u2013 that\u2019s the gelatinous substance between the retina and the lens.<\/p>\n

Why am I finding little worms in my house?<\/p>\n

Moisture is a major factor in a worm infestation in a private home. Often, moist conditions will allow bacteria and mold to grow inside walls, as well as cause wood to decay. Millipedes and centipedes feed on decaying plant matter and sometimes even on other insects which are drawn to this moisture.<\/p>\n

Do pinworms go away if untreated?<\/h3>\n

Strict hygiene measures can clear up pinworm infection without medication, and reduce the risk of reinfection. The worm has a lifespan of about six weeks, therefore the strict hygiene needs to last that long.<\/p>\n

How did I get pinworms?<\/p>\n

People become infected, usually unknowingly, by swallowing (ingesting) infective pinworm eggs that are on fingers, under fingernails, or on clothing, bedding, and other contaminated objects and surfaces. Because of their small size, pinworm eggs sometimes can become airborne and ingested while breathing.<\/p>\n

What happens if threadworms are left untreated?<\/p>\n

In rare cases, if the infestation is left untreated, pinworm infections can lead to a urinary tract infection (UTI) in women. Pinworms can also travel from the anus into the vagina, affecting the uterus, fallopian tubes, and other pelvic organs. This may result in other infections, including vaginitis and endometritis.<\/p>\n

Can threadworms go away on their own?<\/p>\n

Threadworms do not go away by themselves, and people do not build up immunity to them, so they must be treated in order to eradicate them totally from the body.<\/p>\n

How do I get rid of tiny worms in my house?<\/h3>\n

Regular household cleansers like bleach and borax do wonders, and vacuuming and steam cleaning will kill some kinds of bugs and worms and get rid of eggs. Don\u2019t forget to clean your drains and drain guards, especially in the kitchen.<\/p>\n

How do white worms get inside the house?<\/p>\n

White worms crawling up the walls of your home may be a sign of rotting food, feces, or a decaying animal. In most cases, these tiny worms are fly larvae. These maggots eventually become adult flies, so it\u2019s crucial to find their source and remove them as soon as possible.<\/p>\n

What are little worms in my bed?<\/p>\n

Bed worms are larvae of beetles, bugs, and moths that end up on your bed either accidentally or your bed has elements that draw them. A dirty bed with food crumbs, grease, and food stains attract bed worms. However, these worms can also end up in your closet, dresser drawers, and wardrobes too.<\/p>\n

Can pinworms harm you?<\/p>\n

It\u2019s a common infection that affects millions of people each year, particularly school-age kids. If your child develops a pinworm infection, try not to worry. Pinworms don\u2019t cause any harm (just itching and restless sleep), and it won\u2019t take long to get rid of them.<\/p>\n

How do I get rid of pinworms?<\/p>\n

Most pinworm infections are mild and easily treated. Your doctor may prescribe a single chewable tablet of a medicine called mebendazole. A second tablet is taken about 3 weeks later if the infection isn\u2019t cured. Or your doctor may recommend another kind of medicine called pyrantel, which is taken as a single dose.<\/p>\n

What can be mistaken for pinworms?<\/h3>\n

Pinworms are also called \u201cthreadworms.\u201d They\u2019re the most common type of intestinal worm infection in the U.S., and one of the most common in the world. They\u2019re thin and white, and about one-quarter to one-half inch long \u2014 about as long as a staple. Tapeworms are flat worms that look a bit like ribbons.<\/p>\n

What are the tiny worms that curl up?<\/p>\n

Common Characteristics of Millipedes<\/p>\n

While their name means \u201cthousand legs\u201d, this arthropod doesn\u2019t actually have that many (80-400 is typical.) When they walk, their legs appear to move in a wave-like motion, and they will curl up into a small coil when resting or disturbed.<\/p>\n

What are these little worms in my carpet?<\/p>\n

If you see little white worm like creatures, they are actually moth larvae! Carpet moths can wreak absolute havoc on your gorgeous fine rugs. Moths have an affinity for the materials used to create Oriental rugs. (Wool, in particular!)<\/p>\n

What is a dust worm?<\/p>\n

Dust Worms are another common name for these creatures. The long oval-shaped little case or \u201cbag\u201d that is most commonly found in homes and garages are the cocoon created by the larval or caterpillar stage of a moth.<\/p>\n
