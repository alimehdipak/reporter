The partner of former Yankees and Mets pitcher David Cone has been accused in a lawsuit of starting a fire at the couple\u2019s Manhattan apartment in a bid to land a role on \u201cThe Real Housewives of New York City.\u201d<\/p>\n

The blaze broke out inside Cone and partner Taja Abitbol\u2019s 14th-floor pad at a luxury high-rise in Lincoln Square on Nov. 6, 2019 \u2014 the day before Abitbol was slated to appear on Tamron Hall\u2019s ABC talk show.<\/p>\n

The flames triggered sprinklers which significantly damaged the apartment of downstairs neighbor Daniel Rice, an Army vet and Purple Heart recipient, he claims in his suit, filed in Manhattan Supreme Court on Friday.<\/p>\n

Rice claims that Abitbol, a real estate broker, used the fire to promote her upcoming TV appearance and \u201cadvance\u201d her candidacy for the Bravo reality show.<\/p>\n

\u201cAbitbol\u2019s desire for fame and notoriety is well-known,\u201d the suit alleges.<\/p>\n

The \u201cTamron Hall Show\u201d Twitter account had posted on the day of the blaze that, \u201cWe\u2019re talking to Taja Abitbol. She\u2019s obsessed with astrology and recently didn\u2019t think what her astrologists said would come true but it did.\u201d<\/p>\n

In her appearance on the show, Abitbol then explained how her astrologers told her she would move out of the apartment she has just moved into \u2014 a prediction that came true when she was forced to relocate to a hotel in the aftermath of the blaze, the suit states.<\/p>\n

\u201cYesterday morning, I basically set the house on fire during Mercury retrograde and I had to move. I came here from my hotel room,\u201d the filing says Abitbol told Hall.<\/p>\n

She also explained on the show that she had her \u201ccandles burning during Mercury retrograde.\u201d<\/p>\n

Abitbol posted a photo of herself at the studio on Instagram with the caption, \u201cWhat sign are you? I\u2019m a double Aries with Leo Rising all fire \u2026 Tune in to @Tamronhallshow on ABC.\u201d<\/p>\n

The suit claims her post, which included ten fire emojis, \u201cwas an explicit reference to the fire in Unit 14B that damaged Unit 12G.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rice accused Abitbol and Cone of \u201cdeliberately, knowingly, intentionally and\/or recklessly,\u201d having \u201ccaused the fire \u2026 in order for Abitbol to have a story to discuss on the Tamron Hall Show and\/or to advance Abitbol\u2019s candidacy as a castmate for the Real Housewives of New York [City] television show, among other things.\u201d<\/p>\n

Abitbol, 50, tried to get onto \u201cRHONY\u201d at least four times, the suit claims, citing Page Six.<\/p>\n

Rice \u2014 who is suing for at least $320,000 in damages \u2014 says he couldn\u2019t move back into the unit at the West End Avenue address until this year.<\/p>\n

The damage from the blaze and water sprinklers forced him to carry out a \u201cgut renovation\u201d of the home, the court papers claim. He said during that time he was forced find \u201calternative temporary living arrangements.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rice claims the couple \u201cnegligently and wantonly left candles burning in Unit 14B which started a fire and caused significant damage to Unit 12G,\u201d according to the filing.<\/p>\n

The fire was ruled\u00a0an accident, according to a report by the fire department obtained by The Post.<\/p>\n

Rice received a Purple Heart when he was injured by an improvised explosive device in 2005 after serving multiple tours in the Middle East. <\/p>\n

He is currently serving as the Special Advisor to Commander-in-Chief of the Ukrainian Armed Forces General Valeriy Zaluzhnyy in the Russian invasion of Ukraine, the court papers say.<\/p>\n

Eric Lerner a lawyer for Cone and Abitbol said, \u201cThey have been wrongfully accused of a crime despite the FDNY ruling this an accident after the investigation was done. And likewise, the insurance company also ruled this to be an accident after an investigation was done.\u201d<\/p>\n

Todd Shapiro, a rep for Abitbol, said any allegations that she caused the fire intentionally to further her career are \u201cmisguided and untrue.\u201d<\/p>\n

Shapiro also noted that Abitbol had been paid by an insurance company for flooding caused by sprinklers.<\/p>\n

\u201cThere was a defective candle that caused smoke and flame,\u201d Abitbol told The Post. \u201cThe sprinklers immediately went off and there was no damage from a fire \u2013 that was clearly an accident.\u201d<\/p>\n

He said the damage was caused by broken sprinklers in the building, which caused a flood.<\/p>\n

Abitbol\u2019s other rep Brian Finnegan said she \u201cfeels horrible\u201d that others in the building also sustained property damage and \u201cin essence reached out to make sure that others were OK.\u201d He denied that any candles were left unattended.<\/p>\n

Cone, 59, retired from the MLB in 2003 and is currently a commentator for the Yankees on the YES Network and WPIX. He and Abitbol have been together for 10 years.<\/p>\n
