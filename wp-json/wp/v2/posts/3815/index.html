An elderly chimpanzee who appeared in the film \u201cBuddy\u201d with actor Alan Cumming was found alive last week after his former owner faked his death to avoid having him confiscated by PETA, according to Rolling Stone.<\/p>\n

Tonka\u2019s owner, Tonia Haddix, had previously claimed that he had passed away from heart failure and that she cremated him in a fire pit, the outlet reported.<\/p>\n

She fabricated the story after PETA sounded the alarm about dire conditions at her Missouri animal sanctuary and demanded its closure, according to the report.<\/p>\n

The facility eventually went defunct, but Haddix claimed Tonka was no more.<\/p>\n

PETA didn\u2019t buy her story and joined Cumming in offering a $20,000 for information leading to Tonka\u2019s shrouded whereabouts.<\/p>\n

Alan Cumming and Tonka in 1997.Ron Galella Collection via Getty Cumming and PETA teamed up to offer $20,000 for information about Tonka.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cDuring the months we filmed together, baby Tonka and I became good friends, playing and grooming each other and just generally larking about,\u201d Cumming said in an April statement.<\/p>\n

The aging primate was eventually located in a small room on a property owned by Haddix, who conceded that she had lied about his death, Rolling Stone said.<\/p>\n

Tonka, 38, has now been placed in a sprawling Florida sanctuary, PETA said this week.<\/p>\n

Haddix said that she does not fear legal consequences for her treatment of Tonka.PETA\/Facebook Tonka now resides at a Florida chimp sanctuary.Save the Chimps\/PETA <\/p>\n

Cumming was elated by his old pal\u2019s new lease on life.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m dancing a jig that PETA has rescued Tonka from the woman who locked him away alone in a basement and lied about it,\u201d he said, according to PETA. \u201cThe thought of Tonka being able to wander free and happy at Save the Chimps\u2019 lush, spacious sanctuary for the rest of his life has me singing a happy song.\u201d<\/p>\n

Haddix told Rolling Stone that she expects legal consequences.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m sure that there\u2019ll be some jail time in this,\u201d she said. \u201cDo I care? No, I don\u2019t care. It\u2019s because it\u2019s about that kid. As long as that kid is safe, I don\u2019t care about nothing out there.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tonka, meanwhile appeared to relish his fresh start.<\/p>\n

\u201cIn the van on the way here, all he wanted to do was look out the window,\u201d Dr. Andrew Halloran of Save the Chimps, according to WPBF. \u201cHe didn\u2019t seem nervous at all.
