<\/p>\n

Beloved Foo Fighters drummer Taylor Hawkins\u2019 first \u2014 and possibly final \u2014 posthumous project just arrived for fans.<\/p>\n

The musician died on March 25 at the age of 50 after being found in a Colombian hotel room.<\/p>\n

As of Friday, Hawkins\u2019 vocals can be heard on a cover of the classic 1970\u00a0Johnny Winter\u00a0song \u201cGuess I\u2019ll Go Away.\u201d<\/p>\n

Hawkins is featured on the propulsive rock track with the late Johnny\u2019s brother\u00a0Edgar\u00a0(of \u201cFree Ride\u201d fame) Winter and veteran guitar player Doug Rappoport.<\/p>\n

\u00a0\u201cGuess I\u2019ll Go Away\u201d is part of Edgar\u2019s new album\u00a0\u201cBrother Johnny.\u201d<\/p>\n

The 75-year-old rocker shared a heartwarming message following Hawkins\u2019 death last month and teased news about the upcoming collaboration.<\/p>\n

\u201cI had the honor and privilege of working with him as one of the guest artists on my forthcoming but yet to be released tribute album \u2018Brother Johnny,\u2019 \u201d Edgar wrote on social media. (Edgar\u2019s sibling died in July 2014 at the age of 70.)<\/p>\n

The keyboardist went on, \u201cI had only just met Taylor, and barely got to know him, but I was so impressed by his sincerity, positive energy, and pure enthusiasm. He had a unique spontaneous style different from anyone I\u2019ve ever worked with before.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

\u201cThe name of the song is \u2018Guess I\u2019ll Go Away.\u2019 Now, both Johnny and Taylor have gone a way no one can know, but which we all one day must travel. Although I know Johnny has departed this world, I still think of and speak to him every day, and will now see Taylor\u2019s face along side his,\u201d Edgar added.<\/p>\n

\u201cWherever that way may lead, I wish them Peace and Love on the journey, knowing I will join them soon \u2014 as we all will someday,\u201d Edgar concluded his touching note.<\/p>\n

The 17-track record showcases many iconic blues and rock singers giving tribute to Winter, Billboard reported, including Joe Bonamassa, Kenny Wayne Shepherd, Derek Trucks and Billy Gibbons, Joe Walsh, Ringo Starr, Michael McDonald, Steve Lukather, Doyle Bramhall II, Phil X, John McFee, Robben Ford, Keb\u2019 Mo\u2019, Bobby Rush, Waddy Wachtel and Gregg Bissonette.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, the Foo Fighters paid tribute to their fallen friend and bandmate at the time of Hawkins\u2019 death. <\/p>\n

They tweeted, \u201cThe Foo Fighters family is devastated by the tragic and untimely loss of our beloved Taylor Hawkins. His musical spirit and infectious laughter will live on with all of us forever.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cOur hearts go out to his wife, children and family, and we ask that their privacy be treated with the utmost respect in this unimaginably difficult time,\u201d they continued.<\/p>\n

At the time of his death, the band was scheduled to perform in Bogot\u00e1, Colombia, for the Festival Est\u00e9reo Picnic on March 25, following their Latin American tour.<\/p>\n

The Foo Fighters\u00a0later scored\u00a0Best Rock Song, Best Rock Performance and Best Rock Album at\u00a0the 2022 Grammy Awards earlier this month. Hawkins was honored at the event, and host Trevor Noah remembered the musician\u2019s life, legacy and work.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
