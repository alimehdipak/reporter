Continental System, in the Napoleonic wars, the blockade designed by Napoleon to paralyze Great Britain through the destruction of British commerce. The decrees of Berlin (November 21, 1806) and Milan (December 17, 1807) proclaimed a blockade: neutrals and French allies were not to trade with the British.<\/p>\n

What was the Continental System and why did it fail?<\/h3>\n

5: The Continental System. The Continental System was Napoleon\u2019s strategy to weaken Britain\u2019s economy by banning trade between Britain and states occupied by or allied with France, which proved largely ineffective and eventually led to Napoleon\u2019s fall.<\/p>\n

What was the Continental System of Napoleon Bonaparte?<\/p>\n

The continental system was the name given to those measures of Napoleon Bonaparte taken between 1806 and 1812 that were designed to disrupt the export trade of Great Britain and ultimately to bring that country financial ruin and social breakdown.<\/p>\n

Why did Napoleon created the Continental System?<\/p>\n

Napoleon wanted to hobble the British economy and give France a chance to build up its own manufacturing and industry. The Continental System began in 1806 with Napoleon\u2019s Berlin Decree, which banned British ships from entering European ports.<\/p>\n

The episode seriously hurt France itself. Shipbuilding, and its trades such as rope-making, declined, as did many other industries that relied on overseas markets, such as the linen industries. With few exports and lost profits, many industries were closed down.<\/p>\n

How did the Continental System backfire on France?<\/p>\n

However bad the Continental System was for Britain, it was disastrous for Napoleon because it backfired on him. French custom\u2019 revenue fell and European nations were starved of British colonial goods: coffee, sugar, tobacco, cocoa, and cotton textiles.<\/p>\n
