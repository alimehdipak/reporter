Elon Musk gave a glimpse of his plans for Twitter while walking the red carpet at the Met Gala in New York City on Monday night \u2013 revealing his ambition for the divisive social-media platform to be \u201cas inclusive as possible.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cRight now, it\u2019s sort of niche. I want a much bigger percentage of the country to be on it, engaging in dialogue,\u201d a tuxedo-clad Musk told reporters at the event, according to Reuters.<\/p>\n

In an interview with Vogue, Musk added that he wanted to \u201cmake Twitter as inclusive as possible and to have as broad a swath of the country and the rest of the world on Twitter and that they find it interesting and entertaining and funny and it makes their life better.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, Musk downplayed concerns about an employee exodus on the red carpet, telling reporting \u201cit\u2019s a free country\u201d and they were free to seek other opportunities as they wished.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk attended the Met Gala with his mother, model Maye Musk.EPA <\/p>\n

\u201cCertainly if anyone doesn\u2019t feel comfortable with that, they will on their own accord go somewhere else. That\u2019s fine,\u201d Musk said.<\/p>\n

Musk attended the swanky event alongside his mother, model Maye Musk, in what was one of his first public appearances since Twitter\u2019s board accepted his offer to buy the company for $44 billion.<\/p>\n

The tech entrepreneur\u2019s deal to buy Twitter for $54.20 per share is expected to close later this year. Both sides have agreed to a $1 billion breakup fee under certain conditions if the deal falls apart.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk\u2019s appearance at the Met Gala was one of his first since buying Twitter.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s remarks at the Met Gala echoed previous statements about his plan to make Twitter \u201cmaximum fun\u201d for its user base. The billionaire is known for making irreverent use of his own account, posting memes and cartoons as often as he does serious information about his business dealings.<\/p>\n

But Musk\u2019s brash commentary on the platform has also upset many Twitter employees and critics who say his willingness to publicly criticize others and plan to dial down the company\u2019s content moderation policy will lead to more abusive content.<\/p>\n

Last week, Musk drew scrutiny after he tweeted criticism of current Twitter executives, including the social media firm\u2019s top legal office and \u201cchief censor.\u201d Employees have also expressed concern about the possibility that Musk will look to slash jobs once his takeover is complete.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk spoke about his plans for Twitter on the Met Gala red carpet.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

A \u201ctired\u201d looking Twitter CEO Parag Agrawal acknowledged likely changes to the company culture during an all-hands meeting last week \u2013 and admitted that he has some regrets about how the process has unfolded.<\/p>\n
