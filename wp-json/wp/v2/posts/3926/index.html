The Art-Deco-Era<\/b><\/h2>\n

The term Art Deco was not used to refer to the movement until several years after it had finished. Many of the styles included had a familiar thread that was not noticeably visible to the creators who were generating the works at that moment.<\/span><\/p>\n

The movement extends from the want that was expressed in Art Nouveau for the very first time to make entirely new, latest designs that took benefit of the development of the innovative advanced school of thought. Art Deco was greatly impacted by the stark geometric forms pioneered in cubism.<\/span><\/p>\n

Most of the ideals of the art deco designs were not different from those that exist in the Edwardian era. The main focus of both periods was on fine materials, craftsmanship, and a sense of exuberance and luxury. Where they are dissimilar in Art Deco astonishing faith in social progress and technology. The exciting and new designs which were being pioneered had an inherent optimism regarding the upward trajectory of human and artistic experience.<\/span><\/p>\n

Even though art deco originated in France, and is considered one of the first amazing international artistic movements. The advanced ideas for the movement mean that these latest technologies were instantly embraced, enabling Art Deco to penetrate across national borders with ease.<\/span><\/p>\n

Yellow gold was not admired much between 1920 and 1930, so there was high demand for platinum jewelry. At that time platinum was short in supply so jewelers were in search of other materials to offer similar kinds of platinum jewelry. Therefore, the answer appeared in terms of white gold.<\/span><\/p>\n

Art-Deco-Necklaces & Style<\/b><\/h2>\n

The commercial availability of white gold was observed in 1912. It was a great help at that time when more affluent individuals used to opt for platinum jewellery. Platinum was a rare and heavy material that was hard-wearing and tarnish-resistant. Therefore Art Deco necklaces came into style as less expensive and more readily available to wear. Most of the art deco jewellery used to be built around huge angular central stones. <\/span>Vintage emerald cut diamond<\/span> style, relatively simple but straight lines formed famous centerpieces.<\/span><\/p>\n

Art-Deco-Jewellery<\/b><\/h2>\n

With the following of all the above-mentioned forms, art deco jewellers also welcomed new materials, the mixture of existing gemstone with materials made by men like plastics and glass. The use of platinum and stronger, new gold alloys enabled the amount of metalwork in different pieces to be decreased without impairing their durability. These aspects led to all the designs in a minimalist way.<\/span><\/p>\n

Although jewellers from past decades had invested their time and efforts in the intricate and complicated metalwork which were the fashion of those days, jewellers from the period of art deco transformed their style into smaller and trimmed forms of jewellery that could achieve more without compromising on the form of beauty.<\/span><\/p>\n
