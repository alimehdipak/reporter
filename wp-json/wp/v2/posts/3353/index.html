On Wednesday, Kate Moss will testify in court how her ex-love Johnny Depp once swept her up and carried her after she slipped down stairs, according to sources.<\/p>\n

The British supermodel, 48, will\u00a0give evidence via video link from London in the defamation trial between Depp and his ex-wife Amber Heard. Moss will be the first of the actor\u2019s exes to give evidence in the case that\u2019s gripping the globe. <\/p>\n

A source familiar with the case told The Post that Moss will recount an incident when she \u201cwas walking down some stairs in Jamaica. She was wearing flip-flops and she slipped on the last two stairs. Johnny caught her and tended to her.\u201d<\/p>\n

Moss was drawn into the legal drama when Heard, 36, told the jury about a March 2015 fight during which she punched Depp, 58, in the face out of fear he was going to shove her sister, Whitney Henriquez, down a staircase.<\/p>\n

The \u201cAquaman\u201d actress said Henriquez was near a staircase \u201cin the line of fire \u2026 trying to get Johnny to stop,\u201d which reminded her of a rumored incident between Moss and Depp.<\/p>\n

\u201c[Whitney\u2019s] back was to the staircase, and Johnny swings at her,\u201d Heard testified. \u201cI don\u2019t hesitate, I don\u2019t wait \u2014 I just, in my head, instantly think of Kate Moss and stairs.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd I swung at him,\u201d Heard continued of the alleged incident.<\/p>\n

Amber Heard (right) mentioned Moss during her testimony against ex-husband Depp, paving the way for the model to be called to the stand.AP <\/p>\n

This prompted Depp\u2019s attorney Benjamin Chew to triumphantly pump his fist in the air in the Fairfax, Va., courtroom \u2014 a \u201ccelebratory acknowledgment\u201d that Moss could be brought as an impeachment witness to the trial, legal sources told\u00a0The Post.<\/p>\n

By mentioning the alleged misconduct, Heard inadvertently allowed Depp\u2019s lawyers to call the model as a witness.<\/p>\n

But the familiar source said Moss\u2019 testimony about her own staircase incident likely won\u2019t help Heard.<\/p>\n

Moss will reportedly testify via video from London.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cJohnny picked up Kate and carried her \u2014 this was hardly Amber\u2019s scary interpretation of events,\u201d said the source. <\/p>\n

Depp\u2019s legal team has been in talks with\u00a0Moss over the past week, according to insiders.<\/p>\n

\u201cKate has nothing to gain in giving testimony, but she feels strongly about defending her friend,\u201d the familiar source said.<\/p>\n

The style icon, who dated Depp from 1994 to 1997, is also expected to testify that the actor was never abusive to her and that they remain close friends.<\/p>\n
