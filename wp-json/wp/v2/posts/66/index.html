<\/p>\n

Crypto\u00a0firms in the United Arab Emirates are being deluged with requests to liquidate billions of dollars of virtual currency as\u00a0Russians seek a safe haven for their fortunes, company executives and financial sources said.<\/p>\n

Some clients are using\u00a0cryptocurrency to invest in real estate in the UAE, while others want to use firms there to turn their virtual money into hard currency and stash it elsewhere, the sources said<\/p>\n

One\u00a0crypto\u00a0firm has received lots of queries in the past 10 days from Swiss brokers asking to liquidate billions of dollars of bitcoin because their clients are afraid Switzerland will freeze their assets, one executive said, adding that none of the requests had been for less than $2 billion.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019ve had like five or six in the past two weeks. None of them have come off yet \u2013 they\u2019ve sort of fallen over at the last minute, which is not rare \u2013 but we\u2019ve never had this much interest,\u201d the executive said, adding that his firm normally receives an inquiry for a large transaction once a month.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe have one guy \u2013 I don\u2019t know who he is, but he came through a broker \u2013 and they\u2019re like, \u2018we want to sell 125,000 bitcoin\u2019. And I\u2019m like, \u2018what? That\u2019s $6 billion guys\u2019. And they\u2019re like, \u2018yeah, we\u2019re going to send it to a company in Australia\u2019,\u201d the executive said.<\/p>\n

Switzerland\u2019s financial market supervisor declined to comment on\u00a0cryptocurrency transaction volumes.<\/p>\n

Some Russians holding crypto are using it to invest in real estate in the UAE.Getty Images <\/p>\n

The country\u2019s economic affairs secretariat (SECO) said in an emailed statement that\u00a0crypto\u00a0assets were subject to the same sanctions and measures Switzerland has imposed on \u201cnormal\u201d\u00a0Russian assets and individuals, so if a person is sanctioned their\u00a0crypto\u00a0assets must also be frozen in Switzerland.<\/p>\n

Dubai, the Gulf\u2019s financial and business center and a growing\u00a0crypto\u00a0hub, has long been a magnet for the world\u2019s ultra-rich and the UAE\u2019s refusal to take sides between Western allies and Moscow has signaled to\u00a0Russians that their money is safe there.<\/p>\n

One real estate broker, whose company has partnered with a\u00a0cryptocurrency service to help people buy property, said: \u201cWe\u2019ve been seeing a lot of\u00a0Russians and even Belarusians coming to Dubai and bringing whatever they can bring, even in\u00a0crypto.\u201d<\/p>\n

Are Russians finding a way to evade sanctions?<\/h2>\n

A financial source in the UAE confirmed that\u00a0Russians were buying property in Dubai, using\u00a0crypto\u00a0as a way of getting their money out of other jurisdictions and into the Gulf state.<\/p>\n

Russians are looking to liquidate billions of dollars of cryptocurrency.Chesnot\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

Cryptocurrency exchanges have said, while giving few details, that they are blocking the accounts of\u00a0Russians sanctioned by the West over Moscow\u2019s invasion of Ukraine, which\u00a0Russia\u00a0calls a \u201cspecial operation.\u201d<\/p>\n

Major exchanges such as Coinbase\u00a0and Binance say they are taking steps to ensure that\u00a0crypto\u00a0is not used as a vehicle to evade sanctions, and that they collaborate with law enforcement on the issue.<\/p>\n

Still, as\u00a0crypto\u00a0offers users a high degree of anonymity, European countries such as Germany and Estonia have this week called for tighter oversight to snuff out any loopholes that could allow sanctions busting.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Major cryptocurrency exchanges such as Coinbase say they are taking steps to ensure that crypto is not used as a vehicle to evade sanctions.Chesnot\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

Three Western diplomats said they were increasingly alarmed by the number of\u00a0Russians who in recent weeks were seeking a refuge in the UAE for their fortunes, including in property, and were wary that some could be acting on behalf of those under sanctions.<\/p>\n

Two of the diplomats said they were skeptical that the UAE would crack down on\u00a0Russian wealth in the Gulf state, which they said was predominantly held in Dubai, citing the country\u2019s neutral stance in the conflict.<\/p>\n

A third said they hoped the UAE, which is also a gold trading hub, understood the implications for its reputation and would take action.<\/p>\n

Sanctions imposed since Russia\u2019s invasion of Ukraine have many Russians rushing to cash out their crypto investments.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The UAE was put on a \u201cgrey list\u201d this month for increased monitoring by financial crime and money laundering watchdog the Financial Action Task Force (FATF).<\/p>\n

The FATF cited risks in certain industries, including real estate agents and precious metals dealers. Dubai adopted a virtual assets law this week and established a regulator. The UAE\u2019s regulator said it was close to issuing regulations and has consulted on money laundering risks in the sector.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Dubai government\u2019s media office and the UAE central bank did not immediately respond to requests for comment.<\/p>\n

The US Treasury, headed by Janet Yellen, is calling for vigilance from companies in the cryptocurrency industry against moves that would get around sanctions imposed on Russia.Pool\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

The UAE\u2019s Foreign Ministry said it had no further comment beyond previous statements that the government has a \u201cstrong commitment\u201d to working with FATF on areas for improvement in its anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing regime.<\/p>\n

Why UAE real estate is attractive to Russians<\/h2>\n

Some experts say the relative transparency of\u00a0cryptocurrency transactions, which are recorded on the blockchain ledger that underpins bitcoin and other tokens, makes large-scale sanctions evasion difficult.<\/p>\n

The U.S. Treasury said on Monday that sanctions-busting using\u00a0crypto\u00a0was \u201cnot necessarily practicable,\u201d and called for vigilance from companies in the industry.<\/p>\n

Dubai, an attractive tourism destination, has long been popular with Russians.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Two sources familiar with the matter said UAE companies had reputational concerns about doing business with\u00a0Russians, but felt the state\u2019s abstention at the U.N. Security Council, when\u00a0Russia\u00a0vetoed a resolution condemning its invasion of Ukraine, was a signal they should not impose restrictions on\u00a0Russians.<\/p>\n

The UAE, which has deepened ties with\u00a0Russia\u00a0over the years, has not matched sanctions imposed by Western nations and its central bank has not issued any guidance regarding the measures.<\/p>\n

Dubai, an attractive tourism destination, has long been popular with\u00a0Russians, who were among the top visitors and purchasers of real estate even before the war and ensuing sanctions threw the\u00a0Russian economy into turmoil.<\/p>\n

Apurv Trivedi of Healy Consultants, which advises on setting up businesses, including\u00a0crypto\u00a0companies, said they had definitely been getting more interest from\u00a0Russian clients.<\/p>\n

Since Russia\u2019s invasion of Ukraine, Swiss brokers have received requests by Russians asking to liquidate billions of dollars of bitcoin because they fear Switzerland will freeze their assets.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cThey\u2019re basically trying to protect themselves against the inflationary pressures that are happening against the\u00a0Russian currency. So\u00a0crypto\u00a0has been a very good exit for them to manage the risks that they\u2019re facing,\u201d Trivedi said. \u201cIt\u2019s a good liquidity provider for them.\u201d<\/p>\n

Healy\u2019s Sami Fadlallah said a lot of the money coming from\u00a0Russia\u00a0has been moving into Dubai\u2019s real estate, citing both industry talk and their company\u2019s experience.<\/p>\n

\u201cPeople parking their money in dozens of apartments in the Marina, Downtown,\u201d Fadlallah said.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019ve seen a lot of\u00a0Russians hedging their bets against the devaluation of the rouble by moving a lot of assets into\u00a0crypto. And the UAE is relatively loose in terms of its regulation and authorities over transferring\u00a0crypto\u00a0here.\u201d<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
