Elon Musk\u2019s deal to buy Twitter on Monday means big changes could be in store for the social media site \u2014 including potentially bringing back Donald Trump, verifying every human user of the site and converting the company\u2019s headquarters into a homeless shelter.\u00a0<\/p>\n

While Musk hasn\u2019t explicitly promised to allow Trump back onto Twitter, he has said that the site\u2019s current moderation policies are too restrictive, prompting speculation about a potential Trump return.\u00a0<\/p>\n

But the former president, who was banned last year for his role in the Jan. 6 Capitol riots, claimed in a Fox News interview on Monday that he doesn\u2019t want to return to Twitter despite its new owner \u2014 and instead plans to use his own\u00a0struggling Twitter alternative\u00a0TRUTH Social, even though he hasn\u2019t posted on the site since February.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk is considering big changes for Twitter. Yichuan Cao\/Sipa USA <\/p>\n

In another indication of how Musk might handle content moderation issues on Twitter, he wrote last week that \u201ca social media platform\u2019s policies are good if the most extreme 10% on left and right are equally unhappy.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The billionaire has also said he wants to verify \u201call real humans\u201d who use the site in an effort to crack down on spam.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf our Twitter bid succeeds, we will defeat the spam bots or die trying!\u201d he tweeted earlier in April.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In addition, Musk has said he\u2019s considering auditing an \u201cedit button\u201d to Twitter, adding in an interview last week\u00a0that a way to reduce abuse of the feature could be to \u201czero out\u201d retweets and favorites when a tweet is edited.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Musk has said he\u2019s looking to make Twitter\u2019s algorithm publicly available online in order to address concerns of some users who claim certain tweets are unfairly promoted or demoted.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI also want to make Twitter better than ever by enhancing the product with new features, making the algorithms open source to increase trust, defeating the spam bots, and authenticating all humans,\u201d Musk said in a statement on Monday.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIf our Twitter bid succeeds, we will defeat the spam bots or die trying!\u201d Musk said. ZUMAPRESS.com <\/p>\n

Musk has also mused about more outlandish changes including converting Twitter\u2019s San Francisco headquarters into a homeless shelter \u2014 an idea endorsed by Jeff Bezos \u2014 and dropping the word \u201cw\u201d from Twitter\u2019s name.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m serious about this one,\u201d the billionaire said earlier in April about changing Twitter\u2019s name to an apparent reference to breasts. <\/p>\n

Ahead of the announcement that Twitter\u2019s board had accepted Musk\u2019s proposal, employees were divided over the potential takeover. One Twitter employee called Musk \u201ca racist demagogue with a god complex,\u201d\u00a0while another said, \u201cLet\u2019s go Daddy Elon!\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

One Twitter employee who\u2019s likely unhappy about Musk\u2019s acquisition is CEO Parag Agrawal, who critics say has enforced unfair bans on the users. <\/p>\n

Prior to Musk\u2019s takeover bid, he posted a meme comparing Agrawal to Joseph Stalin.<\/p>\n
