Not only does \u201cFriends\u201d co-creator Marta Kauffman\u00a0regret the cast\u2019s lack of diversity, she also feels bad about the show\u2019s portrayal of a transgender character. <\/p>\n

In a clip from Kauffman\u2019s upcoming interview on\u00a0the Conversation on the BBC World Service, she recalled the choice to refer to the transgender mother of Chandler Bing (Matthew Perry) as his \u201cdad.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cPronouns were not yet something that I understood,\u201d Kauffman admitted. \u201cSo we didn\u2019t refer to that character as \u2018she.\u2019 That was a mistake.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

Played by Kathleen Turner, Chandler\u2019s trans parent, Amanda Bing, appeared in several of \u201cFriends\u201d episodes, including \u201cThe One With Monica And Chandler\u2019s Wedding, Part 1.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cFriends\u201d co-creator Marta Kauffman also created Netflix\u2019s \u201cGrace and Frankie.\u201dWireImage Kathleen Turner played Amanda Bing in \u201cFriends.\u201d \u00a9Warner Bros\/Courtesy Everett C <\/p>\n

In 2019, Turner said on \u201cWatch What Happens Live With Andy Cohen\u201d that she would not accept the role nowadays \u201cbecause there would be real people able to do [the role].\u201d<\/p>\n

Kauffman, who also created Netflix\u2019s \u201cGrace and Frankie,\u201d told the BBC how she\u2019s changed her sets to be more inclusive. <\/p>\n

\u201cI like very much to create an environment where we have a happy set and a happy crew. It\u2019s very important to me that where we are is a safe place, a tolerant place, where there\u2019s no yelling. I fired a guy on the spot for making a joke about a trans cameraperson. That just can\u2019t happen,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

Marta Kauffman has apologized for the show\u2019s lack of diversity. Above, Turner as Amanda Bing and Perry as Chandler Bing.\u00a9Warner Bros\/Courtesy Everett C <\/p>\n

\u201cFriends\u201d has long been\u00a0criticized for its lack of diversity. The popular show, which ran from 1994 to 2004, features a group of six white, heterosexual best friends living in Greenwich Village, a famously gay neighborhood in New York \u2014 a historically diverse city.<\/p>\n

Recently, Kauffman finally admitted to the show\u2019s faults and pledged $4 million to her alma mater, Brandeis University, to fund an endowed chair in the school\u2019s African and African American studies department, one of the oldest in the country.<\/p>\n
