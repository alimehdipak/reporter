Shaun King logged back on to Twitter to clap back at Donald Trump Jr. \u2014 a day after the controversial Black Lives Matter activist had apparently left the site following Elon Musk\u2019s $44 billion acquisition of the social media giant.<\/p>\n

King on Monday appeared to delete his account for good after Twitter\u2019s board announced that it had accepted Musk\u2019s offer to buy the firm and take it private, with some users tweeting out screenshots of King\u2019s blank home page.<\/p>\n

When King\u2019s Twitter status became a trending topic, Don Jr. tweeted that the possibility of his deleting his account would \u201calone\u201d be worth $44 billion. <\/p>\n

Shaun King, the controversial Black Lives Matter activist, denied that he deleted his Twitter account.Getty Images Visitors to King\u2019s account were met with a blank page, leading many to believe that he deactivated his Twitter handle.via FOX Donald Trump Jr. celebrated rumors that King deleted his account in protest of Elon Musk\u2019s purchase of Twitter.REUTERS <\/p>\n

In response, King logged back on to Twitter to deny that he deleted his account.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou wish, motherf\u2013ker,\u201d King tweeted at Don Jr. \u201cI didn\u2019t delete my account. I was getting death threats and hack attempts from your buddies who stormed the Capitol and had to make it more secure.\u201d <\/p>\n

King added: \u201cGood luck not perjuring yourself when you testify before the January 6th committee.\u201d<\/p>\n

Before visitors to King\u2019s Twitter timeline were met with a blank page on Monday night, he tweeted that Musk\u2019s takeover of Twitter was about \u201cwhite power.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cAt its root, Elon Musk wanting to purchase Twitter is not about left vs right,\u201d King tweeted.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe man was raised in Apartheid by a white nationalist. He\u2019s upset that Twitter won\u2019t allow white nationalists to target\/harass people. That\u2019s his definition of free speech.\u201d<\/p>\n

King responded to Don Jr.: \u201cYou wish, motherf\u2013ker.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cSo for me, this isn\u2019t about left vs right. Not at all. It\u2019s about how the richest man in the world, a son of Apartheid, raised by a white nationalist, wants to be sure his speech, and that of other white men, isn\u2019t censored.\u201d<\/p>\n

King and other liberals and progressives have bemoaned the possibility of Musk taking over the site since he has pledged to take a \u201cfree speech absolutist\u201d approach to content moderation.<\/p>\n

Republicans and conservatives have celebrated Musk\u2019s bid to acquire the site, saying that it will allow their views to be heard on more mainstream platforms.<\/p>\n
