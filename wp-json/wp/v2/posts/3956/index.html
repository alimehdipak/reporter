\u201cObi-Wan Kenobi\u201d boss Joby Harold revealed Wednesday that he had to swear his kids to secrecy when they found out a fan favorite was going to play a pivotal role in the new Disney+ hit. <\/p>\n

Spoiler alert: Harold, who doubles as writer and executive producer of the series, joked that he had to warn his offspring he would be forced to disown them if they told anyone how important Princess Leia was to the journey that Obi-Wan was about to go on, reports Newsweek. <\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t tell a lot of people. I kept it a big secret \u2014\u00a0but within my home someone\u2019s got to, you know?\u201d Harold told the outlet. \u201c[To] carry the weight with me, I can\u2019t do it all by myself.\u201d<\/p>\n

He continued: \u201cThey had known about Leia and that was the one thing I [said] \u2018I will disown you if ever you [tell someone],\u2019 because they knew the codename [for Leia] so they figured it out, and I was just like \u2018you can never tell a soul.’\u201d<\/p>\n

In the series, Leia \u2014 played by 10-year-old Vivien Lyra Blair \u2014 is kidnapped by an Inquisitor named Reva (Moses Ingram) to lure exiled Jedi Master Obi-Wan Kenobi (Ewan McGregor) out of hiding and into the clutches of Darth Vader (Hayden Christensen). <\/p>\n

\u201cI think I was probably supposed to do that, I knew that,\u201d Harold said when asked about whether or not he was supposed to keep the secret from his family. \u201cI couldn\u2019t not tell them, you know?\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cBecause to a great degree we know where we\u2019re going [with the show] \u2026 so, getting to do that with Leia, and also getting to bring to the conversation the notion that she\u2019s important, and it\u2019s not all about Luke and they are both of equal importance in the Skywalker saga.\u201d <\/p>\n
