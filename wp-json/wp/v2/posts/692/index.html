Ten years ago, LL Cool J stepped up to the challenge of hosting the Grammys for the first time the day after Whitney Houston died.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was just an unbelievable time,\u201d\u00a0the New York hip-hop icon told The Post. \u201cKen [Ehrlich, longtime Grammys producer,] was like, \u2018What do we do?\u2019 And I just said, \u2018Let\u2019s say a prayer. Let\u2019s address the elephant in the room \u2026 and embrace the crowd so that they feel the love and they can kind of take that weight off their shoulders. And then after that, let\u2019s celebrate them.\u2019 \u201d<\/p>\n

A decade after that moving opening of the Grammys, LL will be presiding over \u2014 and performing at \u2014 the iHeartRadio Music Awards 2022 on Tuesday at the Shrine Auditorium in Los Angeles. The ceremony, which will be broadcast live on Fox at 8 p.m., takes place just five days before the big daddy of all awards shows \u2014 the Oscars \u2014 on Sunday.<\/p>\n

LL Cool J will open the 2022 iHeartRadio Music Awards with a performance before taking over hosting duties.Getty Images for iHeartRadio <\/p>\n

Despite doin\u2019 it and doin\u2019 it well in previous hosting gigs, the rapper-actor isn\u2019t interested in rocking the Academy Awards. \u201cNah, I don\u2019t think so,\u201d he said. \u201cNo disrespect \u2026 but I just really enjoy hosting music shows and just being closer to the music [world]. I guess you can never say never, but that\u2019s my preference.\u201d<\/p>\n

While the artist born James Todd Smith has been juggling rapping and acting for years \u2014 he\u2019s the longtime star of the CBS series \u201cNCIS: Los Angeles\u201d \u2014 music remains his first love. And he\u2019ll soon be returning to it with his first album since 2013\u2019s \u201cAuthentic.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cQ-Tip executive-produced it, and it\u2019s coming together; it\u2019s almost done,\u201d he said. \u201cAnd it\u2019s gonna be a lot of fun to get back in the game on that level and give the people a new body of work.\u201d<\/p>\n

LL Cool J performed at We Love NYC: The Homecoming Concert last August.Getty Images for Live Nation <\/p>\n

LL and his \u201cNCIS\u201d co-star Chris O\u2019Donnell are also executive-producing a new dance competition show, \u201cCome Dance With Me,\u201d which will premiere on CBS on April 15. Clearly, at 54, he has no plans to rest on his legendary laurels \u2014 even though he was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in October.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was one of the most amazing nights of my life, man \u2026 Just being recognized for your body of work and your contribution to the culture,\u201d said LL. \u201cYou\u2019re included in the pantheon of great acts and great artists that have done so much for music. To be able to hang out with Chuck Berry and James Brown and Michael Jackson, I mean, that\u2019s an amazing feeling.\u201d<\/p>\n

And LL felt the love from a certain two-time Hall of Famer who was in the crowd: \u201cPaul McCartney was standing up with his phone, taping while we was rocking, and that was beautiful,\u201d he said. \u201cI was, like, absolutely blown away by that.\u201d<\/p>\n

LL Cool J was joined onstage by longtime friend and collaborator Jennifer Lopez during his Rock & Roll Hall of Fame induction performance in October.Getty Images for The Rock and Ro <\/p>\n

The star was joined onstage during his induction performance by his \u201cAll I Have\u201d collaborator Jennifer Lopez, who will receive the Icon Award at the iHeartRadio ceremony.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe met 1,000 years ago \u2014 I don\u2019t know where,\u201d he said of his fellow New York native. \u201cWhen she needs something, I\u2019m there for her; when I need something, she\u2019s there for me. We just got a good friendship, a good vibe.\u201d<\/p>\n

Like the ageless J.Lo, LL has somehow managed to defy time, maintaining the youthful look that still makes Ladies Love Cool James. His secret? \u201cA good makeup artist,\u201d he said with a laugh. Plus, he added, \u201ca little melanin never hurt nobody, baby!\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

One of hip-hop\u2019s first sex symbols, LL famously posed bare-chested on the cover of his 1990 classic \u201cMama Said Knock You Out.\u201d These days, though, he would only take his shirt off \u201cwhen I get home.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

LL Cool J has been immortalized with a new statue that resides in Flushing Meadows Corona Park.Stephen Yang <\/p>\n
