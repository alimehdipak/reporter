Veteran actor Denzel Washington takes punches like a pro, Ryan Reynolds revealed.<\/p>\n

The Canadian actor, 45, accidentally gifted Washington, 67, two nasty black eyes during the filming of their 2012 action flick, \u201cSafe House.\u201d<\/p>\n

Reynolds opened up about what went down a decade ago in a sit-down chat with David Letterman for the Netflix series, \u201cMy Next Guest Needs No Introduction With David Letterman.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cProposal\u201d star and the Oscar winner were shooting a car fight sequence in Cape Town, South Africa, when the incident occurred.<\/p>\n

He explained to Letterman, 75, that it was just the second day of shooting when the actors were fighting \u201cin an out of control car\u201d where he was supposed to \u201csmash\u201d Washington in \u201cthe face.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cThere\u2019s a scene in the movie where he crawls through the trunk in the back and he grabs me and chokes me. We were really driving a car at top speed,\u201d Reynolds said, recalling that stunt drivers were not being used to perform the scenes.<\/p>\n

The \u201cFences\u201d director was gracious towards Reynolds following the accident. WireImage <\/p>\n

He added, \u201cHe and I are in this out-of-control\u00a0car\u00a0and my corner of my head \u2014 I felt it hit his eye so hard I was sure that it split wide open.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cDeadpool\u201d actor feared for his life and jokingly assumed he would be fired and even killed for causing pain to the \u201cTragedy of Macbeth\u201d actor.<\/p>\n

Reynolds noted, \u201cI\u2019m thinking I\u2019m going to be sent home via crematorium, like, this is it, not only my career but my actual pulse will cease.\u201d<\/p>\n

But Washington was a class act about it and politely told Reynolds he was OK. \u201cHe was fine, he was like, \u2018Hey, accidents happen. Let\u2019s do it again and we\u2019ll shoot it from the other side so we can keep shooting so you don\u2019t see this,’\u201d the Aviation Gin stakeholder said. <\/p>\n

Reynolds and Washington were filming a scene in Cape Town, South Africa involving a car and physical violence.Getty Images for The Met Museum\/ <\/p>\n

Everything on set seemed fine and dandy following the mishap, but the second take proved to be just as hard \u2014 and Reynolds socked Washington in the other eyeball. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe did it again, and I got the other eye, Dave,\u201d Reynolds told the late-night host. \u201cFor a minute, he looked like a\u00a0Christmas\u00a0ornament.\u201d<\/p>\n
