Memorial Day is here, and whether you plan to go on vacation or spend time relaxing on your couch, there\u2019s a slew of TV specials to watch and movies to stream \u2014 some are themed to the military, and some are just new and fun.<\/p>\n

Here\u2019s what to keep an eye out for.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cDunkirk\u201d<\/h2>\n

If you want to watch a war movie, it doesn\u2019t get much better than this gritty Christopher Nolan modern classic. Depicting the Dunkirk evacuation of World War II from the sky, sea, and land, its star studded cast includes Cillian Murphy, Tom Hardy, and Harry Styles.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to watch: HBO Max\u00a0<\/p>\n

Cillian Murphy in \u201cDunkirk.\u201d \u00a9Warner Bros\/Courtesy Everett C <\/p>\n

\u201cTheodore Roosevelt\u201d\u00a0<\/h2>\n

This is a new two-part docuseries about the life and presidency of Roosevelt, executive- produced by Leonardo DiCaprio and based on the\u00a0 Doris Kearns Goodwin bestseller, \u201cLeadership: In Turbulent Times.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to watch: History Channel (8 p.m. on May 30)\u00a0<\/p>\n

A new History Channel doc explores the life of Teddy Roosevelt. Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cNorm Macdonald: Nothing Special\u201d<\/h2>\n

The late comedian has a posthumous final stand-up special coming out. MacDonald, who died of leukemia in 2021 at age 61, reportedly self-taped this special all in one take alone in his living room during the summer of 2020.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to watch: Netflix\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThe Lost City\u201d<\/h2>\n

If you want some lighter and goofier viewing, check out this new movie starring Sandra Bullock as a romance novelist, Channing Tatum as her cover model and Daniel Radcliffe as a nefarious billionaire that they must escape \u2013 all while hunting for a lost ancient city from her books. Brad Pitt also has a small role.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to watch: Paramount+\u00a0<\/p>\n

Channing Tatum and Sandra Bullock in their goofy 2022 comedy \u201cThe Lost City.\u201d \u00a9Paramount\/Courtesy Everett Col <\/p>\n

\u201cSaving Private Ryan\u201d\u00a0<\/h2>\n

The 1998 Steven Spielberg movie is another modern classic, set during World War II and follows Captain John H. Miller (Tom Hanks) and his squad as their search for paratrooper private Ryan (Matt Damon), who\u2019s missing in action and is the last surviving brother in his family.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to Watch: AMC (3 p.m. on May 30)\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cAmbulance\u201d\u00a0<\/h2>\n

The latest bombastic Michael Bay action flick is newly available. Jake Gyllenhaal and Yahya Abdul-Mateen II star as two adoptive brothers who team up on a heist (to get money to cover medical bills) and end up on an adventure where they hijack an ambulance.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to watch: Peacock\u00a0<\/p>\n

Jake Gyllenhaal in action thriller \u201cAmbulance.\u201d \u00a9Universal\/Courtesy Everett Col <\/p>\n

\u201cTogether as One: Celebrating Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander Heritage\u201d<\/h2>\n

Part of ABC\u2019s \u201cSoul of a Nation\u201d series, this special is hosted by George Takei and celebrates the Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander communities, and it will include segments featuring celebrities such as Margaret Cho, \u201cEverything Everywhere All At Once\u201d director Daniel Kwan, \u201cCrazy Rich Asians\u201d director Jon M. Chu, actors such as James Hong, and more.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Where to watch: ABC (Friday at 8 p.m.), Hulu thereafter\u00a0<\/p>\n

George Takei hosting \u201cTogether as One.\u201d ABC <\/p>\n

\u201cTop Gun\u201d<\/h2>\n

Since the long awaited sequel \u201cTop Gun: Maverick\u201d is in theaters now, with Tom Cruise and Val Kilmer reprising their roles, why not go back to where it all began? This 1986 movie follows naval aviator Pete \u201cMaverick\u201d Mitchell (Tom Cruise), who is given the chance to train at the US Air Forces\u2019 Fighter Weapons School.<\/p>\n
