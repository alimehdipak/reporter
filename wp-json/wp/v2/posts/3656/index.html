Brushing your teeth twice a day and flossing daily helps you fight against tooth decay, bad breath, and gum disease. However, cleaning your teeth every day at home is only a part of a good oral hygiene routine. Having a “full dental exam<\/em>” helps your dentist look for more significant problems, such as oral cancer, gum disease, or other oral issues that we only see when it’s in severe condition. On the other hand, getting your teeth checked at the dental office prevents oral diseases and treats them at an early stage if your dentist finds any.<\/p>\n

So, how often should you visit your dentist for a dental exam? Let’s find out.<\/p>\n

When to visit a dentist for a dental exam<\/strong><\/h2>\n

Dental treatments are no one-size-fits-all models. Dentists and hygienists know each patient’s dental needs and plan the treatment accordingly. A schedule of a regular dental check-ups twice a year works well for most people who have average teeth<\/a>. Some of you can get away with fewer visits, whereas others need to go more often.<\/p>\n

For a comprehensive dental exam, it’s recommended to visit once after every three years. Your dentist<\/a> can check for problems that you may not see or feel yet, like oral cancer and gum disease.<\/p>\n

Who needs more frequent visits?<\/strong><\/h2>\n

Some health and lifestyle-related conditions require more frequent visits. Your dentist might recommend your regular dental check-ups every three or four months if any of these conditions apply to you:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Following a diet rich in sugar<\/li>\n
  • Ongoing gingivitis or other periodontal issues<\/li>\n
  • Medications causing dry mouth<\/li>\n
  • Smoking or tobacco<\/li>\n
  • Systemic disease like diabetes<\/li>\n
  • Prone to cavities or tartar buildup<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    \nWho needs less frequent visits?<\/strong><\/h2>\n

    Even if you’ve got an excellent oral hygiene routine, you still need to regularly see your dentist. A few individuals can get away with one a year dental check-ups. These include:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Young and healthy without risk of gum disease<\/li>\n
    • Excellent oral hygiene and cleaning<\/li>\n
    • Eat a healthy diet without sugary foods and beverages<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Make sure you get your teeth checked at a dental office and find out about the schedule that’s best for you and your health.<\/p>\n

      Benefits of a Dental Exam<\/strong><\/h2>\n

      The dental exam’s benefit is to provide a full assessment and diagnose any issues in an early stage. Between each dental check-up, your dentist will perform a recall exam. This exam will be done every 6-12 months, depending on your dental needs.<\/p>\n

      While regular dental check-up is beneficial, it lacks several diagnostic tools and assessments used in a complete dental exam.
