Two days before she is set to defend herself against Johnny Depp\u2019s $50 million defamation suit, Amber Heard took to Instagram to say she has \u201calways maintained a love for Johnny\u201d despite publicly feuding for years.<\/p>\n

The trial in a Fairfax, Va. court begins Monday, where a jury will ultimately decide if Heard defamed Depp in a\u00a02018 Washington Post op-ed implying he was a domestic abuser \u2014which the actor says hurt his reputation and cost him work.<\/p>\n

The headline of Heard\u2019s piece read: \u201cI spoke up against sexual violence \u2014 and faced our culture\u2019s wrath. That has to change.\u201d<\/p>\n

In her Instagram post Saturday, Heard addressed the op-ed, writing:\u00a0\u201cI never named him, rather I wrote about the price women pay for speaking against men in power. I continue to pay that price, but hopefully when this case concludes, I can move on and so can Johnny.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI have always maintained a love for Johnny,\u201d the actress wrote, \u201cand it brings me great pain to have to live out the details of our past life together in front of the world.<\/p>\n

\u201cAt this time, I recognize the ongoing support I\u2019ve been fortunate to receive throughout these years, and in these coming weeks I will be leaning on it more than ever,\u201d she continued.<\/p>\n

The trial is sure to be explosive, with a star-studded witness lists including Heard\u2019s ex-lover Elon Musk, actor James Franco, \u201cWandaVision\u201d star Paul Bettany and Ellen Barkin.<\/p>\n

Both exes are also expected to testify, and their lawyers will likely focus on whether Heard\u2019s account of Depp\u2019s alleged abuse were truthful.<\/p>\n

Heard said in her post that she is \u201cgoing offline\u201d while the trial is underway.<\/p>\n

In 2020, the former couple duked it out in UK court over a 2018 story in The Sun, which called the \u201cWhat\u2019s Eating Gilbert Grape\u201d star a \u201cwife beater.\u201d <\/p>\n
