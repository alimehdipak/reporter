Break out your fur trim and lam\u00e9 miniskirts because \u201cRomy and Michele\u2019s\u201d high school may have a reunion in the works.<\/p>\n

During the red carpet premiere of \u201cBetter Nate Than Ever\u201d in Hollywood on Tuesday, star Lisa Kudrow stopped to reminisce with ET correspondent Matt Cohen about \u201cRomy and Michele\u2019s High School Reunion.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cTell Disney you want a sequel!\u201d Kudrow told Cohen, who pressed the 58-year-old actress about the possibility of revisiting the iconic characters. \u201cRobin Schiff will figure it out. She\u2019s got ideas brewing.\u201d<\/p>\n

The film inspired a made-for-TV prequel \u201cRomy and Michele: In the Beginning\u201d for ABC Family in 2005, starring Katherine Heigl and Alexandra Breckenridge. But Kudrow may have her sights set on a sequel for herself and co-star Mira Sorvino, 54. <\/p>\n

Lisa Kudrow starred as Michele Weinberger in \u201cRomy and Michele\u2019s High School Reunion\u201d in 1997.Getty Images Mira Sorvino co-starred as Romy White as the best friends made a road trip from Los Angeles to Tucson, Arizona, for their 10-year high school reunion.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cYeah, I would. I think it would be really fun,\u201d the \u201cFriends\u201d actress told Cohen. \u201cAnd then a little like, \u2018What are they like at our age?’\u201d<\/p>\n

The duo was seen together recently last month as they presented the Screen Actors Guild award for outstanding performance by an ensemble in a comedy series \u2014 dressed in bright pink and blue suits in an homage to the \u201990s cult classic.<\/p>\n

And there\u2019s never been a better time for it, during an age in which women are expected to \u201cget [their] f\u2013king ass up and work\u201d and look fierce while doing it. Besties Romy and Michele were the late-1990s epitomai of self-made women who bucked settling down to launch their own fashion line and boutique in Los Angeles. <\/p>\n

Fashion was a crucial element of the film about two boutique workers in the late-1990s as they revisit their 1987 graduating class. \u00a9Buena Vista Pictures\/Courtesy <\/p>\n

Kudrow\u2019s comments coincide with the 1997 flick\u2019s 25th anniversary next month. The original, produced by Schiff, follows as the LA best friends return to their high school in Tuscon, Arizona, for the 10-year reunion of their graduating class. It also stars Janeane Garofalo, Alan Cumming, Elaine Hendrix and Camryn Manheim.<\/p>\n
