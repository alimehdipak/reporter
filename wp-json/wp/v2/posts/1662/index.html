Fifty years after \u201cCabaret\u201d made her a superstar, Liza Minnelli lives a quiet life in an apartment in West Hollywood. <\/p>\n

At 76, and with enough headlines behind her for three lifetimes, the singer and actress is rarely seen in public.<\/p>\n

But in what would prove to be the second most surprising moment at March\u2019s Oscars,\u00a0the icon joined Lady Gaga to hand out the Best Picture award \u2014\u00a0and her frail appearance and confused demeanor was a shock to many.<\/p>\n

Sources said Lady Gaga personally asked that Liza Minnelli present with her at the Oscars last month, but the legend\u2019s apparent frailty and confusion left many fans, and longtime friends, concerned.NY Post photo composite <\/p>\n

Now in a wheelchair after battling years of ill health, an emotional Minnelli was given a standing ovation by the A-list audience.<\/p>\n

\u201cOh! It\u2019s so exciting,\u201d Minnelli exclaimed as Gaga \u2014 who had stepped away to allow her a moment in the sun \u00a0\u2014 told her: \u201cYou see that? The public, they love you.\u201d<\/p>\n

It was Minnelli\u2019s former publicist Scott Gorenstein who helped make the moment happen, after he was approached by Academy officers who said that Gaga personally asked that the actress present with her in honor of the anniversary of \u201cCabaret.\u201d<\/p>\n

Now in a wheelchair after battling years of ill health, an emotional Minnelli \u2014\u00a0presenting with Lady Gaga \u2014 was given a standing ovation at the Oscars.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cLiza is living her best life, not having to be in front of the cameras,\u201d Gorenstein told The Post. \u201cShe\u2019s been under tremendous pressure her entire life to perform for audiences. The past couple of years have allowed her to relax and enjoy another phase of her life.<\/p>\n

\u201cShe has had some health problems. However, when an opportunity comes in front of me that says \u2018Would Liza Minnelli like to present the Best Picture Oscar with Lady Gaga?\u2019 the quiet life goes out of the window! Liza is a legend and she deserves to be at the Academy Awards, so I put it in front of her people and recommended that she do it,\u201d he added \u201cI said it would be an historic occasion.<\/p>\n

\u201cI wanted to remind people who she is,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

Minnelli was to be the big surprise of the evening \u2014\u00a0until Will Smith slapped host Chris Rock onstage.<\/p>\n

Before the Oscars, one of the most recent times Minnelli was seen in public was with George Hamilton (right) in 2019.GC Images <\/p>\n

\u201cUnfortunately, Will Smith stole a little bit of that thunder,\u201d Gorenstein said. \u201cBut Liza earned every bit of that ovation. They were standing for her, for her mother and for her father. There was a lot of history in that moment.\u201d<\/p>\n

Minnelli\u2019s life is the stuff of Tinseltown legend. Born to \u201cThe Wizard of Oz\u201d actress Judy Garland and director Vincente Minnelli \u2014 and named after the Ira Gershwin song \u201cLiza (All the Clouds\u2019ll Roll Away),\u201d she made her silver screen debut as a toddler alongside her mom in 1949\u2019s \u201cIn the Good Old Summertime.\u201d<\/p>\n

At 18, she performed with her mother at a London Palladium concert that was released as an album. A year later, Minnelli won a Tony for \u201cFlora the Red Menace.\u201d<\/p>\n

She released a slew of records \u2014 usually standards and songs from musicals \u2014 and acted in such films as \u201cNew York, New York\u201d (the title song would become her signature number) and \u201cTell Me That You Love Me, Junie Moon.\u201d That one was notable because a nude scene filmed in a Massachusetts graveyard led to what was known as the \u201cLiza Minnelli Bill\u201d and prohibited filming in that state\u2019s cemeteries without permission.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Minnelli is the daughter of actress Judy Garland and director Vincente Minnelli.Bettmann Archive <\/p>\n

Her role as the tragic Sally Bowles in \u201cCabaret\u201d would sear her gamine image into pop-culture history, and her TV special \u201cLiza With A \u2018Z’\u201d meant no one would mispronounce her name again.<\/p>\n

She is just one of a handful of people to win an EGOT \u2014 an Emmy, Grammy, Oscar and Tony \u2014 although Gorenstein pointed out that the Kennedy Center has yet to bestow an honor on her, which has created online grumbling from her fans for years.<\/p>\n

But Minnelli\u2019s life has been marked by pain. She has been married and divorced four times, suffered three miscarriages (she has no children), and talked openly about her struggles with substance abuse.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy whole life, this disease has been rampant,\u201d she told The Guardian of her alcoholism in 2008. \u201cI inherited it, and it\u2019s been horrendous, but I have always asked for help.\u201d<\/p>\n

She performed with her mother as a teen.Bettmann Archive <\/p>\n

Her mother suffered tremendously from alcohol and drug problems. It was said that Garland began using barbiturates and amphetamines as a teenager, to help her stay peppy, sleep and stay slim as she made such movies \u201cThe Wizard of Oz,\u201d \u201cBabes in Arms\u201d and the Andy Hardy franchise with Mickey Rooney.<\/p>\n

Garland was a full-on alcoholic and drug addict by age 30, when Liza was just 6 years old. <\/p>\n

When her mother died in 1969, at just 45, Minnelli was prescribed Valium to help deal with her grief. It led to her own abuse of prescription drugs and alcohol. In his diaries, Andy Warhol noted that she arrived at fashion designer Halston\u2019s home in 1978 and said, \u201cGive me every drug you\u2019ve got.\u201d<\/p>\n

Minnelli had the first of multiple trips to rehab in 1985.<\/p>\n

Oscars officials said that Lady Gaga personally asked Minnelli to present with her in honor of the 50th anniversary of \u201cCabaret.\u201dABC Photo Archives\/Disney General Entertainment Content via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cSo what do you do?\u201d she asked on the \u201cToday\u201d show in 2005 after a relapse. \u201cYou get up and you go on, and you try not to do it again.\u201d<\/p>\n

In 2000, Minnelli was admitted to the ICU at a Florida hospital with partial paralysis, slurred speech and drooping facial muscles. She was diagnosed with viral encephalitis and, at the time, a doctor said that \u201cshe was quite sick and was in very serious condition\u201d when she came in.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI couldn\u2019t walk and I couldn\u2019t talk, and they told me I wouldn\u2019t \u2026 ever again,\u201d she told NBC. \u201cAfter I was told that, everybody left the room and I turned my face to the wall and started to go \u2018A \u2013 B \u2013 C.\u2019 You know, that\u2019s what it felt like because I had Carnegie Hall to rehearse. I want to live. I have always wanted to live.\u201d<\/p>\n

In 2015, Minnelli sold her Upper East Side co-op for $8.37 million and moved to the West Coast, leaving behind Gorenstein and longtime assistant Nicole Guest, who were, he said, both deeply devoted to her and devastated by her departure.<\/p>\n

Minnelli appeared in a string of hit films in the 1970s and early \u201980s, including \u201cArthur\u201d with Dudley Moore.Orion Pictures Corp <\/p>\n

But they no longer have direct contact with Minnelli. Multiple sources added that many from Liza\u2019s old life in New York City have lost touch with her. <\/p>\n

\u201cThere are definitely people [in New York City] who love Liza, who miss her,\u201d Gorenstein said.. \u201cWe want her to be healthy and we worry that there is no one around [in LA] to look after her like we would in New York.\u201d<\/p>\n

She is believed to have caretakers who help her. One of her closest friends is pianist and performer Michael Feinstein, whom she discovered playing in a piano bar in NYC in the 1980s.<\/p>\n

\u201cLiza is someone who would admit to being needy. She\u2019ll say, \u2018Do this for me, honey,’\u201d one showbiz insider told The Post. \u201cWhen she moved out to LA, she reached out to Michael and he\u2019s helped take control of her life.\u201d<\/p>\n

She had a career renaissance in the early 2000s on \u201cArrested Development.\u201d20th Century Fox <\/p>\n

In January, Feinstein and Minnelli appeared together on CBS \u201cSunday Morning,\u201d chatting with Jane Pauley, who asked Minnelli: \u00a0\u201cDo you recognize that you have achieved the status of legend?\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cNo, I have to be told a lot,\u201d Minnelli replied. \u201cLike, I keep saying to Michael, \u2018Is that all right?\u2019 I had great people around me. The biggest thing I got was to recognize somebody else\u2019s talent.\u201d<\/p>\n

Of her friendship with Feinstein, Minnelli said: \u201cI mean, we met each other and we were joined at the hip.\u201d<\/p>\n

A rep for Feinstein told The Post, \u201cLiza relies on Michael as anyone would a best friend of several decades and vice versa. They give each other advice and they are each other\u2019s sounding board both in their personal and professional lives. They are, simply, family.\u201d<\/p>\n

The last of her four marriages was to David Gest.FilmMagic, Inc <\/p>\n

Minnelli is currently working with Feinstein as executive producer of an upcoming album called \u201cGershwin Country,\u201d and producing his new tour, celebrating Judy Garland\u2019s 100th birthday in June, one of many projects around the world celebrating her centennial.<\/p>\n

Asked about Minnelli\u2019s relationship with her younger sister Lorna Luft, which has reportedly been rocky over the years, the showbiz insider said: \u201cThat whole \u2018sisters don\u2019t talk\u2019 thing is bulls\u2013t. Like any family relationship, they have their good moments, their bad moments. I happen to know that Lorna knew the Oscars moment was happening, Liza wanted Lorna to know, and [Lorna] would have been proud of it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Requests for comment were not returned by Luft\u2019s reps.<\/p>\n

At the Oscars, Minnelli appeared befuddled as she held up her cue cards and said \u201cNow what am I? I don\u2019t quite understand.\u201d which prompted Gaga to hold her hand and say, \u201cI got it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Minnelli (left, with Garland) has talked openly about how substance abuse has torn up her family.Getty Images <\/p>\n

When Minnelli stumbled as she began to introduce the nominees, Gaga jumped in to finish her sentences as the films played in the montage. Gaga was heard whispering, \u201cI gotcha,\u201d as an appreciative Minnelli laughed and they held hands.<\/p>\n

\u201cI know,\u201d Minnelli replied, before announcing \u201cCODA\u201d as the winner.<\/p>\n

\u201cI have to give a lot of props to Gaga,\u201d said Gorenstein, referencing how the singer recorded and performed with Tony Bennett, who now has Alzheimer\u2019s. The two recorded the TV special \u201cOne Last Time: An Evening With Tony Bennett and Lady Gaga,\u201d said to be his last performance, in 2021. \u201cWe all know how dear she has been to Tony Bennett and she really respects talent and I knew that Liza would be in great hands.\u201d<\/p>\n

Gaga has made it no secret that she considers Minnelli to be a hero. The pair first met when Gaga invited Minnelli to a concert at Madison Square Garden, filmed for HBO, back in 2011.<\/p>\n

Minnelli with her mother and stepfather Sid Luft.Bettmann Archive <\/p>\n

Gaga told the crowd how her teachers at NYU\u2019s Tisch School of the Arts, had told her she didn\u2019t have what it took to \u201cbe a superstar.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cYou\u2019ll never be the main character\u2026 the blonde \u2026 your hair\u2019s too dark \u2026 you look too ethnic,\u201d she recalled being told \u2014\u00a0to which she would reply, \u201cWhat about Liza?\u201d She then asked the audience to give a thrilled Minnelli a standing ovation.<\/p>\n

Despite seeming frail, Minnelli is still able to put her famous talent to use.<\/p>\n

Giving viewers a treat of a performance of \u201cI Love A Violin\u201d on CBS last month, Minnelli said wistfully: \u201cWhen I\u2019m singing to an audience, I\u2019m not singing to an audience, I\u2019m singing to you<\/em> \u2026What I wanna say to the audience is, \u2018Have you ever felt like this? \u2018Cause it\u2019s what I\u2019m going through now.\u2019 I just want people to know I\u2019ve been through what they\u2019ve been through.\u201d<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
