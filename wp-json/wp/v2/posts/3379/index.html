Bradley Cooper is unrecognizable in the first look of his upcoming film \u201cMaestro.\u201d<\/p>\n

The biographical drama stars Cooper as the legendary Broadway composer Leonard Bernstein, alongside Carey Mulligan as Bernstein\u2019s wife Felicia Montealegre. <\/p>\n

The celebrated composer, credited with reinventing the American musical,\u00a0died in November 2021\u00a0at age 91.<\/p>\n

Netflix released photos from the set on Monday, showing Cooper transformed into Bernstein at various stages in the musical icon\u2019s life. <\/p>\n

The stunning hair and make up work morphed the Oscar-nominated actor and director, 47, into Bernstein, from a handsome young Broadway upstart to an aged veteran composer.<\/p>\n

Bradley Cooper nails his transformation as the late Leonard Bernstein.NETFLIX Bradley Cooper directs and stars in \u201cMaestro,\u201d a film that chronicles the life of Broadway icon Leonard Bernstein.NETFLIX <\/p>\n

Before production began, Scott Stuber, the head of global films at Netflix, told\u00a0Variety\u00a0about the time and effort put into Cooper\u2019s transformation. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019ve done a lot of work on the makeup. We\u2019ve done a lot of work on the voice. I\u2019m excited to see someone so deeply focused on creating a story that means so much to him. And Carey Mulligan is an incredible actress,\u201d he said. <\/p>\n

Although the first look was released on Monday, the film is not scheduled for release until 2023. <\/p>\n

Cooper recently shared that he contemplated quitting acting before being called for this role, admitting that he always wanted to be a composer himself. <\/p>\n

Carey Mulligan play\u2019s Leonard Bernstein\u2019s wife, Felicia Montealegre, alongside Bradley Cooper in \u201cMaestro.\u201dNETFLIX The Netflix biopic about legendary Broadway composer Leonard Bernstein debuts in 2023.NETFLIX <\/p>\n

\u201cI was obsessed with it, asked Santa Claus for a baton when I was 8. Listening to music, falling in love with it and being able to really know every single moment of a piece, like Tchaikovsky\u2019s Opus 35 in D major, this violin concerto. I could do it as if I know everything about it without really being able to speak the language, obviously,\u201d he told Variety. <\/p>\n

Steven Spielberg, who just finished directing the remake of Bernstein\u2019s \u201cWest Side Story,\u201d was originally attached and recruited Cooper to star in the film despite the actor\u2019s hesitance. <\/p>\n

\u201cI always knew that Steven Spielberg knew that I had this obsession with conducting,\u201d Cooper said. \u201cHe had this biopic idea and was talking to me about potentially acting in it.
