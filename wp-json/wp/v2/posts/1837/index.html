Uber and Lyft dropped\u00a0their\u00a0mask\u00a0requirements\u00a0for US passengers and drivers on Tuesday after a\u00a0federal judge ruled against\u00a0the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention\u2019s mandate.<\/p>\n

\u201cRiders and drivers are not required to wear masks when using Uber,\u201d\u00a0Uber\u00a0told users in a message early Tuesday. \u201cHowever, the CDC still recommends wearing a mask if you have certain personal risk factors and\/or high transmission levels in your\u00a0area.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Uber also said that it would allow riders to sit in the front seats of vehicles \u2014 a practice the company banned two years ago during the outbreak of the pandemic.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Hours after Uber\u2019s announcement, Lyft also nixed its mask requirement for riders and drivers<\/p>\n

\u201cWearing a mask is now optional\u00a0for everyone in the car,\u201d Lyft said. \u201cWhile riders and drivers can always cancel any ride they don\u2019t wish to take, health safety reasons \u2013 like not wearing a mask \u2013 will no longer appear as cancellation options in the app.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

On a Reddit forum for Uber drivers, some users celebrated the decision.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Uber notified drivers and passengers about the rule change on Tuesday. Uber <\/p>\n

\u201cYay! About time,\u201d one driver wrote.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cHallelujah!\u201d said another.\u00a0<\/p>\n

However, others said they would quit over the decision. \u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m done,\u201d one driver wrote. \u201cCase counts are rising in my area and I am tired of mask arguments. Done. Finished. If Uber policy won\u2019t protect me & my riders, it\u2019s over.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m out,\u201d another said, attributing the decision to Uber allowing passengers to ride in the front seat.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m done,\u201d one driver wrote. \u201cIf Uber policy won\u2019t protect me & my riders, it\u2019s over.\u201d\u00a0Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cLyft hasn\u2019t changed their policy yet,\u201d another driver added. \u201cThis might cause some issues if they don\u2019t change it today.\u201d<\/p>\n

Under Monday\u2019s court ruling from a Trump-appointed federal judge in Florida, individual companies would still have the power to enforce mask mandates if they choose to do so.<\/p>\n

However, all major US airlines quickly moved to mask-optional policies late Monday, with Uber following suit hours later.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Uber said that riders who are unhappy with the mandate can cancel their trips.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cRemember: many people still feel safer wearing a mask because of personal or family health situations, so please be respectful of their preferences,\u201d the company said. \u201cAnd if you ever feel uncomfortable, you can always cancel the\u00a0trip.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

New York City subways and buses, as well as the Metro North and Long Island railroads, are continuing to require masks for now, the Metropolitan Transit Authority said Monday.\u00a0<\/p>\n
