Elon Musk mocked Warren Buffett after the legendary investor once again hit out at cryptocurrencies over the weekend, saying he wouldn\u2019t agree to buy \u201call of the bitcoin in the world\u201d for $25.<\/p>\n

Posting a video of Buffett speaking at Berkshire Hathaway\u2019s annual shareholder meeting on Saturday, venture capitalist Marc Andreessen wrote it was \u201cwild\u201d that Buffett criticized bitcoin \u201cwhile nakedly shilling diabetes\u201d \u2014 a reference to the boxes of See\u2019s Candie\u2019s that surrounded the legendary investor at a table.<\/p>\n

\u201cHaha, he says \u2018bitcoin\u2019 so many times,\u201d Musk tweeted in response.<\/p>\n

Buffett and his top deputy, Berkshire Hathaway Vice Chairman Charlie Munger, had few good things to say about cryptocurrencies at the annual meeting.<\/p>\n

Buffett, who is currently the sixth richest person in the world with a net worth valued by Forbes at around $116.8 billion, said he is opposed to bitcoin because it\u2019s not a tangible asset like real estate or other businesses.<\/p>\n

Musk is a supporter of cryptocurrency, frequently promoting dogecoin.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cIf you told me you own all of the bitcoin in the world and you offered it to me for $25 I wouldn\u2019t take it because what would I do with it?\u201d he said. \u201cI\u2019d have to sell it back to you one way or another. It isn\u2019t going to do anything.\u201d<\/p>\n

Last month, crypto supporter Peter Thiel, the billionaire investor, hit out at Buffett, calling him a \u201csociopathic grandpa from Omaha\u201d and an \u201cenemy of bitcoin.\u201d<\/p>\n

Buffett\u2019s No. 2 at Berkshire Hathaway, Charlie Munger, has also criticized bitcoin.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Thiel said investors should embrace cryptocurrency as a hedge against inflation.<\/p>\n

Munger said he would never invest in bitcoin because \u201cin my life, I try to avoid things that are stupid and evil and make me look bad\u2026and bitcoin does all three.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk, meanwhile, has been an enthusiastic supporter of dogecoin, the meme-inspired cryptocurrency which was started as a joke in 2013 as a way to make fun of the hype surrounding bitcoin.<\/p>\n

The digital currency features the face of the Shiba Inu dog from the viral \u201cDoge\u201d meme as its logo and namesake.<\/p>\n

Buffett has been opposed to bitcoin because he says it\u2019s not a tangible asset like real estate or other businesses.Photothek via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Last month, Musk, whose $44 billion bid to take over Twitter was accepted by the company\u2019s board of directors last week, floated the idea of Twitter users paying for a monthly subscription using dogecoin. <\/p>\n

Musk has repeatedly\u00a0touted Dogecoin in recent months, once referring to himself as \u201cThe Dogefather\u201d and infamously referencing the meme token\u00a0during his appearance on \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d last year.<\/p>\n

Bitcoin is the most valuable cryptocurrency on the market. As of Monday morning, it was trading at $38,907.51 per bitcoin. The digital currency also has a market capitalization of $740.2 billion.<\/p>\n
