<\/p>\n

Alex Trebek\u2019s name and legacy live on through a new homeless shelter, the Trebek Center, serving the Los Angeles area.<\/p>\n

The facility opened in the area of the former Northridge roller rink Skateland, on May 12. His widow, Jean Trebek, was there for the ribbon-cutting ceremony, according to the Los Angeles Daily News.<\/p>\n

The beloved \u201cJeopardy!\u201d host died in November 2020 after a battle with pancreatic cancer.<\/p>\n

The shelter contains 107 beds and will provide help for people to find permanent housing.<\/p>\n

The Trebek Center also includes the Trebek library, Los Angeles Daily News reported.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe all know that homelessness is complicated,\u201d Jean said. \u201cThere is no single pathway to the streets. There\u2019s no single intervention that ends homelessness. But there is a single remedy and it\u2019s called love. Supporting another is loving another and the transformational power of support will surely be known at this site.\u201d<\/p>\n

Services that the site will provide for residents include hot meals, showers, case management, mental health services, substance abuse treatment, employment counseling and permanent housing placement services. <\/p>\n

The homeless shelter site was previously the home of the Skateland roller rink. The area was closed down due to the coronavirus pandemic. MediaNews Group via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Inhabitants will be allowed to reside at the center until they have received permanent housing accommodations.<\/p>\n

Ken Craft, founder of\u00a0Hope of the Valley\u00a0Rescue Mission \u2014 which runs the new shelter \u2014 revealed that the first person to move into the complex will arrive next week.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis center will be a life-transformation center, a chance for people to get a second chance in life to get renewed dreams, their vision ignited. And to start over,\u201d Craft said.<\/p>\n

Much of the mission\u2019s funding came from a $500,000 donation that the Trebek family made in 2021, TMZ reported. They received permission from the Trebeks before the game show host\u2019s passing to use the cash for the Trebek Center.<\/p>\n

The 23,000-square-foot facility houses 107 beds and provides a medley of services.CBS News <\/p>\n

Los Angeles City Council member John Lee noted that by opening the facility, the town is \u201ctaking the necessary footsteps to combat what is happening on our city streets.\u201d<\/p>\n

Lee said that while it was hard to lose the famed roller rink, which was forced to shut down in March 2020 amid the COVID-19 pandemic, he\u2019s grateful for the shelter.<\/p>\n

\u201cLosing something that was so incredibly valuable to us, with so many incredible memories, was not an easy pill for people to swallow,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Trebek died at the age of 80 in 2020 from pancreatic cancer. ABC via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Almost a year after Trebek\u2019s death, Jean opened up about her late husband\u2019s life and career.<\/p>\n

\u201cAlex was my dear husband and my most beloved friend. Like most long-term relationships, we had our ups and downs, but the truth of it was that we were very close and experienced a lot of life together,\u201d Jean told\u00a0the Eden Magazine last October.<\/p>\n

She continued, \u201cIn looking back, the fact that I did not identify Alex as an \u2018icon\u2019 was a gift. We were equal partners in a marriage, and we had our own personal growth issues to deal with just like any ordinary couple.\u201d<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
