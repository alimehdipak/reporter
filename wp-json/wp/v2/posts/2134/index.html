New York City nightclubs are raising their game this spring as they look to revive the post-pandemic scene with over-the-top entertainment that includes sword swallowers, belly dancers, magicians and \u201cnext-level pole dancing.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Wonderland, a nightclub and \u201cadult\u201d venue that opens this Wednesday in the Flatiron District, is beckoning guests with a human blockhead to go with their charcuterie plates and Wagyu cheeseburger sliders. Jewel Thief, a speakeasy in Manhattan\u2019s NoMad District, is offering up belly dancers to electric violinists. And Keys & Heels, an Upper East Side speakeasy, has tapped a trio of magicians to work the tables.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Olivur Yu, a show producer who is providing the talent for opening night at Wonderland, has booked fire eaters, jugglers of axes and swords and avant-garde variety shows with drag and pole-dancing artists who use truck chains instead of silks.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThere are some shocking moments. The acts are very tough,\u201d Yu said. \u201cPeople are risking their lives and their physical well being to create their art. This is full-body dedication.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

One of the sword swallowers is 36-year-old La Reine the Thrill, 36, of Brooklyn, who (it hurts to even write this) also will be inserting unusually long objects into nasal cavity.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Sam Urdang juggling axes at Wonderland.Courtesy of Hideki Aono \u201cThere are some shocking moments,\u201d show producer Olivur Yu says. \u201cThe acts are very tough.\u201dCourtesy of Hideki Aono <\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t intend to do this work. It found me,\u201d La Reine said. \u201cIt\u2019s not for the faint of heart. I never give up on my dream to just keep creating.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Jewel Thief, which opened last November at 30 West 30th St. down a staircase under the Italian eatery Spritz, features live performers, DJs and shared plates \u2014 from Neapolitan-style thin-crust pizzas and homemade pastas to wood-fired skirt steaks. There\u2019s also a $10,000 option to rent out the back of the vault, which includes a private bar and \u201ccurated entertainment.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Keys & Heels, which opened last month at 1488 Second Ave., now offers \u201cMagic Unlocked\u201d twice a month. The space is a shoe repair and locksmith store that has been transformed into a hidden lounge. As patrons dine on light bites and cocktails, a trio of magicians move from table to table.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Sam Urdang, Olivur Yu and Sword Swallower La Reine The Thrill outside Wonderland. Courtesy of Hideki Aono Wonderland opens Wednesday in the Flatiron District.Courtesy of Hideki Aono <\/p>\n

And at Wonderland \u2013 a 10,000-square-foot space at 20 W. 20th St. that seats 150 people over two floors \u2013 the entertainment also will include celebrity hosts, live DJs and entertainers. The acts will become increasingly \u201cadult\u201d as the night goes on, says Wonderland\u2019s creative director Richie Romero, also of Nebula nightclub and other eateries.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019re expecting an audience of adult men and women of all ages looking for a voyeuristic night out,\u201d Romero said.<\/p>\n

Bottle service ranges in price from $500 for Tito\u2019s Vodka to a $9,000 splurge on a Jeroboam of Dom Perignon Brut.<\/p>\n

\u201cAfter living through a time filled with restrictions and lockdowns across the globe, there\u2019s now a massive demand by both locals and tourists alike to explore our city and all that it has to offer both during the day and after dark,\u201d Romero said. \u201cPeople want to see something they can\u2019t find on TV or TikTok.\u201d<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
