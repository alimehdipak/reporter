<\/p>\n

Wreckage from a rocket ship came crashing down to Earth this week, giving observers on the ground a shock.<\/p>\n

Parts of the rocket were recovered in western India.<\/p>\n

A video posted to Reddit shows the debris cutting across the sky.<\/p>\n

The Redditor claims that his video is first hand and that they are in the city where the explosion was visible.<\/p>\n

Another Reddit user referred the original poster to an expert who posted some answers about the space debris on Twitter.<\/p>\n

Jonathan McDowell, an astronomer at the Center for Astrophysics tweeted \u201cI believe this is the reentry of a Chinese rocket stage\u2026it was expected to reenter in the next hour or so and the track is a good match.\u201d<\/p>\n

One piece of debris weighed over 90 pounds \u2013 fortunately, there were no casualties.<\/p>\n

Space travel becoming more popular means there will be more space trash.<\/p>\n

Countries are accusing each other of space pollution and creating a situation where a collision is likely \u2013 and India\u2019s space program ran a sloppy missile test that would be the cosmic equivalent of littering.<\/p>\n

Astronomer Jonathan McDowell argued the falling debris came from a \u201cChinese rocket.\u201d@VdCyberPunk2077 \/ Reddit <\/p>\n

But it\u2019s Elon Musk\u2019s Starlink satellites that cause most close calls in space.<\/p>\n

NASA and the United States Space Force have a network of sensors scanning the sky and keeping tabs on all 27,000 pieces of space debris in Earth\u2019s orbit.<\/p>\n

Objects in Earth\u2019s orbit travel at over 17,000 miles per hour \u2013 fast enough that even if a small piece collided with a satellite or space ship it could do serious damage.<\/p>\n

Scientists fear more debris will keep crashing into Earth\u2019s atmosphere while space trash gathers in orbit.@VdCyberPunk2077 \/ Reddit <\/p>\n

Scientists have considered the threat that space trash poses to life on Earth and to our dreams of traveling space.<\/p>\n

The Kessler Syndrome is a theory that Earth\u2019s orbit will become so crowded that debris will just keep colliding, creating an increasingly hard-to-manage field of space trash around our planet.<\/p>\n

The incident in India also brings up an interesting ethical question about space trash accountability.<\/p>\n

The US Space Force estimates there are approximately 27,000 active pieces of space trash.@VdCyberPunk2077 \/ Reddit <\/p>\n

Is outer space subject to the laws written on Earth? Time will tell as space becomes more frequently visited.<\/p>\n
