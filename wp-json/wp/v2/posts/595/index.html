<\/p>\n

As the\u00a0Russia-Ukraine war\u00a0continues, many industries have sanctioned\u00a0Russia, including Hollywood.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Although Russia isn\u2019t a leading market for Hollywood, it was one of the top six box office markets worldwide in revenue in 2021, according to\u00a0Statista.\u00a0<\/p>\n

And over the years, celebs have met with Russian President Vladimir Putin while visiting the country for events, like professional fights and concerts.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Here\u2019s a look at some celebs who either once hobnobbed or briefly met with Putin.<\/p>\n

Mickey Rourke<\/p>\n

In an interview with Fox News Digital, Mickey Rourke recalled meeting Putin when Rourke had a \u201cprofessional fight four years ago\u201d in Russia.<\/p>\n

\u201cI had the time of my life,\u201d Rourke told Fox News Digital about visiting the country. \u201cThe people couldn\u2019t have been nicer, more polite, more respectful. I actually went with Putin. He took us to a very famous museum in St. Petersburg.\u201d<\/p>\n

Many entertainers, including Gerard Depardieu, have met with Vladimir Putin since his presidency of Russia.RIA-NOVOSTI\/AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Rourke said he also visited a children\u2019s cancer hospital.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd we went in there and visited the really, really sick, little, tiny, tiny kids,\u201d Rourke recalled. \u201cAnd, you know, I looked over at him and I saw him and \u2014 nobody\u2019s going to want to hear this \u2014 but I saw a man with empathy and who was really moved by what these children are going through.\u201d<\/p>\n

Now Rourke, who once wore a shirt with Putin\u2019s face on it and reportedly spoke kindly about him at the time, feels differently, calling what\u2019s happening in Ukraine a \u201cnightmare kind of scenario.\u201d<\/p>\n

Mickey Rourke wears a T-shirt with an image depicting Russia\u2019s President Vladimir Putin, as he visits a souvenir shop in Moscow.REUTERS\/Maxim Zmeyev <\/p>\n

\u201cThen now there\u2019s this whole other thing that\u2019s happening,\u201d the former boxer said, noting that he prays to God that Putin will end the war.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhen I pray at night, I pray, \u2018Please God, \u2026 let him find something inside his soul to just say, \u2018Okay, that\u2019s it,\u201d\u201d Rourke said.<\/p>\n

Leonardo DiCaprio<\/p>\n

Leonardo DiCaprio was once photographed alongside Putin after the two attended the International Tiger Conservation Forum at the Mikhailovsky Theatre in Saint Petersburg in 2010.<\/p>\n

In the wake of Putin\u2019s invasion of Ukraine, DiCaprio donated to humanitarian groups CARE, International Rescue Committee, the United Nation\u2019s High Commissioner for Refugees and Save the Children in support of Ukraine, a person close to DiCaprio said. However, no dollar amounts were provided.<\/p>\n

The actor further shared resources Friday on his personal Instagram account.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe victims of warfare in Ukraine have lost everything from their loved ones to basic materials for survival,\u201d\u00a0he wrote, alongside photos from Ukraine. \u201cHumanitarian organizations like @SavetheChildren @careorg @rescueorg and @refugees are on the ground, dedicating their personnel and resources to support families and innocent people.\u201d<\/p>\n

Oliver Stone<\/p>\n

Film director Oliver Stone, who previously worked with the Russian president for the documentary \u201cThe Putin Interviews,\u201d told KCRW\u2019s Robert Scheer in early February that the United States media had \u201cno proof\u201d that Russia intended to invade Ukraine.<\/p>\n

Stone was proven wrong when Putin launched his attack on Feb. 24, and Stone subsequently made a statement condemning Russia\u2019s \u201caggression.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cAlthough the United States has many wars of aggression on its conscience, it doesn\u2019t justify Mr. Putin\u2019s aggression in Ukraine. A dozen wrongs don\u2019t make a right. Russia was wrong to invade,\u201d Stone said.<\/p>\n

Oliver Stone poses with Vladimir Putin after shooting his documentary \u2018The Putin Interviews\u2019 for Showtime.Komandir\/Courtesy of SHOWTIME <\/p>\n

Gerard Depardieu<\/p>\n

Gerard Depardieu, a reported friend of Putin, has taken a stance against the\u00a0Russia-Ukraine war. The French actor became a Russian citizen in 2013 to avoid taxes, according to The New York Times. In a letter to\u00a0Russian state television\u00a0he wrote, \u201cI love your president, Vladimir Putin, very much, and it\u2019s mutual.\u201d<\/p>\n

Before Putin launched a full-scale attack on Ukraine, Depardieu went on French television to say, \u201cLeave Vladimir alone.\u201d<\/p>\n

Once the war began, the French actor denounced it but didn\u2019t call out Putin specifically.<\/p>\n

\u201cRussia and Ukraine have always been brother countries,\u201d Depardieu said during an\u00a0interview with Agence France-Presse. \u201cI am against this fratricidal war,\u201d he added. \u201cI say, \u2018Stop the weapons and negotiate.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

Steven Seagal<\/p>\n

Steven Seagal was granted Russian citizenship in 2016 and, prior to the war, had publicly praised Putin.<\/p>\n

The 69-year-old American actor once called Putin \u201cone of the world\u2019s greatest living leaders\u201d but has since tempered his support for the Russian president.<\/p>\n

Steven Seagal has long had a close relationship with Vladimir Putin and was granted Russian citizenship in 2016.AFP\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cI look at Russia and Ukraine as one family and really believe it is an outside entity spending huge sums of money on propaganda to provoke the two countries to be at odds with each other,\u201d he\u00a0told Fox News Digital.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy prayers are that both countries will come to a positive, peaceful resolution where we can live and thrive together in peace,\u201d Seagal added.<\/p>\n

Paul McCartney<\/p>\n

Paul McCartney met with Putin at the Kremlin May 24, 2003, in Moscow as he prepared for a concert on Red Square.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Russian invasion of Ukraine prompted the Beatles star to share a photo of himself performing in Kyiv in 2008.<\/p>\n

\u201cRemembering playing for our friends in Ukraine in Independence Square in 2008 and thinking of them in these difficult times,\u201d the musician shared on\u00a0his official website. \u201cWe send our love and support.\u201d<\/p>\n

McCartney also shared links to resources providing\u00a0support to Ukraine.<\/p>\n

Paul McCartney meets with Vladimir Putin for a talk at the Kremlin in 2003.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Jane Fonda<\/p>\n

Although there are no photos of Putin and Jane Fonda together, the actress previously claimed that the now-Russian president was once her tour guide.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis guy,\u201d\u00a0Fonda said\u00a0during a 2020 interview with Andy Cohen as she pointed to Putin on a screen, \u201cwas our travel guide. When I was married to Ted Turner, and he was going to do his Goodwill Games in Leningrad.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe landed at the airport, and the person who picked us up, and drove us around, was Vladimir Putin. He was the travel guide. He was like our interpreter at the time.\u201d<\/p>\n

The actress recalled a moment during the trip when Putin refused to go meet his wife at the hospital.<\/p>\n

\u201cHis wife had gotten sick, and she was heading to the hospital,\u201d Fonda said. \u201cHe told us that in the car and Ted said, \u2018You have to go to the hospital. You have to go to the hospital to check on your wife,\u2019 and he wouldn\u2019t. Vladimir Putin wouldn\u2019t do it. He stayed with us.\u201d<\/p>\n

She has not publicly commented on the Russia-Ukraine war.<\/p>\n
