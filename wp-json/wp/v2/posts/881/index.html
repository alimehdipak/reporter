<\/p>\n

The service would allow fans to pay a monthly fee to get the latest Apple gadgets as and when they\u2019re released.<\/p>\n

It could launch in late 2022 or early 2023, sources told\u00a0Bloomberg\u00a0in a report published Thursday.<\/p>\n

Apple already runs a monthly payment plan for gadgets \u2013 including iPhones and iPads \u2013 purchased from its website.<\/p>\n

Customers on the program effectively spread the price of the device over a period of 12 or 24 months.<\/p>\n

According to Bloomberg, the new service would be different in that it wouldn\u2019t simply cover the cost of the device divided by 12 or 24 months.<\/p>\n

Instead, members would pay a still-undecided monthly cost that may come with an option to upgrade to new hardware as its released.<\/p>\n

Currently, Apple\u2019s monthly plans do not automatically roll over to new devices as they\u2019re launched.<\/p>\n

Bloomberg said the change would \u201cmake device ownership similar to paying a monthly app fee\u201d.<\/p>\n

Like Apple\u2019s other subscriptions, it would be tied to a user\u2019s existing Apple ID account.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s possibile customers will have the option to bundle in AppleCare or Apple One services, too.<\/p>\n

It could end up saving you money in the long-run with small discounts offered to those who combine services into one subscription.<\/p>\n

The iPhone plan is part of drive by the company to shift towards subscription services, which it sees as a largely untapped source of potential revenue.Shutterstock <\/p>\n

Apple has previously focussed on selling iPhones at full cost outright. New devices can cost well above $1,000.<\/p>\n

The iPhone remains the company\u2019s biggest source of revenue, generating $192billion last year.<\/p>\n

The iPhone plan is part of drive by the company to shift towards subscription services, which it sees as a largely untapped source of potential revenue.<\/p>\n

Customers on the program effectively spread the price of the device over a period of 12 or 24 months.Gabby Jones\/Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

In 2020, the firm launched Apple One, which bundles a number of its digital services together into a single subscription tier.<\/p>\n

Users get access to Apple Music, Apple TV Plus, Apple Arcade, Apple News Plus and more all for a monthly fee.<\/p>\n

You can select from one of three tiers depending on how much you want to spend and who will be using it.<\/p>\n
