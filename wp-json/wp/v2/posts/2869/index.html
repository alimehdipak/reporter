The musician and model spoke with PopSugar about some of the lessons her famous father instilled in her before he passed away in 2009.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was raised by a free-love, hippie-type dude, so we were taught that beauty comes from the inside \u2014 and to this day, I still believe that,\u201d Paris said in an interview released Thursday. <\/p>\n

Her favorite way to express herself is with her many piercings and tattoos \u2014 most of which have no particular meaning, she admitted \u2014 but her recent years in the fashion and entertainment industry have led her to appreciate the artistry of makeup. <\/p>\n

\u201cAll of the experiences that I\u2019ve had in the last few years have gotten me to that point,\u201d she says. \u201cI appreciate the artistry behind it.\u201d <\/p>\n

She explained that growing up in the public eye as part of one of the most famous families in entertainment led her to have a complicated relationship with beauty. <\/p>\n

\u201cRecently, I\u2019ve learned how to cope with it all by practicing self-love and affirmations and diving deeper into my spiritual life,\u201d Jackson shared. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt wasn\u2019t until the last couple of years that I\u2019ve started feeling really good about myself and my body, and feeling comfortable and everything. Those moments of self-love aren\u2019t 24\/7, but the bad moments are fewer and further between.\u201d <\/p>\n
