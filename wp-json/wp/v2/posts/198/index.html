A hacker group backed by the Chinese government has breached the networks of at least six US state governments in the past year.<\/p>\n

Experts are warning of a group of cybercriminals that has been targeting state government computer networks in the United States, according to a\u00a0new report\u00a0from American cybersecurity firm Mandiant.<\/p>\n

The hacker collective, known as APT41, has been deliberately cyber-attacking state-level government networks from May 2021 to February 2022.<\/p>\n

The group has successfully comprised at least six states\u2019 government networks through the \u201cexploitation of vulnerable Internet-facing web applications, often written in ASP.NET,\u201d the report noted.<\/p>\n

In most of the successfully breached web applications, APT41 conducted deserialization attacks, which is when untrusted data is used to inflict a service attack (DoS attack), execute code, or bypass authentication.<\/p>\n

Other times, the bad actors exploited SQL injection and directory traversal vulnerabilities, which is a\u00a0common attack that utilizes malicious SQL code to access backend databases.<\/p>\n

In those instances, Mandiant Managed Defense was able to detect and contain the attack; however, APT41 re-compromised the network using \u201cexploiting a previously unknown zero-day vulnerability in a commercial-off-the-shelf (CoTS) application, USAHerds,\u201d the report said.<\/p>\n

The hacker collective has been deliberately cyber-attacking state-level government networks from May 2021 to February 2022.Silas Stein\/dpa\/Sipa USA <\/p>\n

Experts at Mandiant said that the extent of the attacks and breaches could include more than six US states\u2019 networks.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe say \u2018at least six states\u2019 because there are likely more states affected, based on our research, analysis, and communications with law enforcement,\u201d Rufus Brown, a senior threat analyst at Mandiant, told\u00a0The Verge.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe know that there are 18 states using USAHerds, so we assess that this is likely a broader campaign than the six states where we have confirmation,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

As recently as February, APT41 re-compromised two US state governments it has previously breached in what appears to be a continuation of their campaign from 2021.<\/p>\n

Mandiant said that while APT41\u2019s goals remain unknown, their investigations into each of these breaches have illuminated \u201ca variety of new techniques, malware variants, evasion methods, and capabilities.\u201d<\/p>\n
