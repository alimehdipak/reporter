<\/p>\n

Meta has hired a top GOP consulting firm to gin up rage against TikTok and deflect political scrutiny away from Facebook and Instagram, according to a report on Wednesday.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The consulting firm, Targeted Victory, pushed to \u201cget the message out that while Meta is the current punching bag, TikTok is the real threat especially as a foreign owned app that is #1 in sharing data that young teens are using,\u201d according to an internal email from February reported by the Washington Post. \u00a0<\/p>\n

Targeted Victory reportedly advanced Meta\u2019s agenda by pushing news stories blaming dangerous online trends such as the \u201cslap a teacher challenge\u201d on TikTok \u2014 even though the trend actually originated on Facebook.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The group also helped place op-eds and letters to the editor in local papers like the Denver Post and Des Moines Register, raising concerns about China \u201cdeliberately collecting behavioral data on our kids,\u201d according to the report.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg has blamed TikTok for Facebook\u2019s slowing user growth, which has contributed to its stock tanking 32.5% so far this year.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Targeted Victory reportedly pushed the message that \u201cTikTok is the real threat especially as a foreign owned app that is #1 in sharing data that young teens are using.\u201d Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cPeople have a lot of choices for how they want to spend their time, and apps like TikTok are growing very quickly,\u201d Zuckerberg said during an earnings call in February.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Targeted Victory\u2019s mandate from Meta appeared to be twofold: stop TikTok from drawing users from Instagram and Facebook while also sapping support for antitrust lawsuits and bills targeting Meta.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Targeted Victory CEO Zac Moffatt claims some of the Washington Post\u2019s reporting is \u201cinaccurate or just totally wrong.\u201d targetedvictory.com <\/p>\n

\u201cBonus point if we can fit this into a broader message that the current bills\/proposals aren\u2019t where [state attorneys general] or members of Congress should be focused,\u201d one Targeted Victory staffer wrote in an email obtained by the newspaper.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Targeted Victory\u2019s CEO Zac Moffatt, who previously worked digital director of Mitt Romney\u2019s 2012 presidential campaign, told The New York Post he is \u201cworking on a list of points where the [Washington Post\u2019s] reporting was inaccurate or just totally wrong.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cTargeted Victory\u2019s corporate practice manages bipartisan teams on behalf of our clients,\u201d Moffatt added. \u201cIt is public knowledge we have worked with Meta for several years and we are proud of the work we have done.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

He also pointed to several Washington Post headlines from 2021 that had linked viral trends \u2014 including the \u201cslap a teacher challenge\u201d \u2014 to TikTok.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThese viral stories about TikTok they claims\u00a0[sic] are \u2018rumors\u2019 were actually reported by their own newspaper, some from over 6 months ago,\u201d Moffatt added in a Twitter thread.\u00a0<\/p>\n

A TikTok spokesperson said that the company is \u201cdeeply concerned that the stoking of local media reports on alleged trends that have not been found on the platform could cause real world harm.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Washington Post said in a statement that it stood by its reporting.<\/p>\n
