WhatsApp is a prime target for crooks \u2013 so spotting the warnings signs of a dangerous text can help you stay safe.<\/p>\n

Even messages that seem to come from friends or family can be a sinister trap.<\/p>\n

So you need to watch out for anyone\u00a0asking for personal information.<\/p>\n

We spoke to cyber-experts who revealed the tell-tale signs of a\u00a0WhatsApp\u00a0scam.<\/p>\n

And texts from unknown numbers can be one of the biggest clues.<\/p>\n

\u201cScam messages often come from unknown numbers. If you keep a tidy contact list they will be easier to spot,\u201d said Brian Higgins, security specialist at Comparitech, speaking to The Sun.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey also disguise themselves as official messages, offering tech support to solve problems you probably didn\u2019t know you had for example, or telling you\u00a0you\u2019ve won a prize.<\/p>\n

\u201cAlways make sure you\u2019re running the most up to date version of any platform and you can confidently delete the first one.<\/p>\n

\u201cThen think about how many times in your life you\u2019ve won a prize for absolutely no reason at all and you can get rid of the second one too.<\/p>\n

\u201cTry to think before you click and don\u2019t let FOMO scare you away from the \u2018delete\u2019 button. It\u2019s your best way to stay safe.\u201d<\/p>\n

If you\u2019re a regular WhatsApp user, you need to be on high alert.<\/p>\n

Here\u2019s what Tom, a senior director at security firm Lookout, told The Sun\u2026<\/p>\n

Tom\u2019s three WhatsApp warnings<\/h2>\n

Here\u2019s what you need to look out for:<\/p>\n

Clue #1<\/h3>\n

Messages coming from unknown contacts that try to get you to interact, especially if the spelling or grammar seems off.<\/p>\n

The same goes for messages from known contacts that sound different to usual.<\/p>\n

Clue #2<\/h3>\n

Messages with unsolicited offers like free giveaways, prize drawers or discounts, even if they appear to come from a reputable brand and especially if they ask you to submit personal details.<\/p>\n

Clue #3<\/h3>\n

Any messages that include clickable links that you were not expecting to receive.<\/p>\n

To be safe you can manually type the link into another browser.<\/p>\n

Attackers may use special characters in the message to disguise a malicious link that looks at first glance like a familiar or reputable site.<\/p>\n

You need to watch out for anyone asking for personal information.Rafael Henrique\/SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Staying safe on WhatsApp<\/h2>\n

There are some other key security tips you can take advantage of.<\/p>\n

The first is to make sure you keep your phone and\u00a0apps\u00a0updated with the\u00a0latest software patches.<\/p>\n

These updates often include important security fixes that squash bugs used by hackers to attack your device.<\/p>\n

And you should also be generally wary of anyone asking for personal information (like credit card details or login codes), even if the text appears to be coming from a friend.<\/p>\n

If a hacker gains access to your WhatsApp account, you can kick them out by logging back in again.<\/p>\n
