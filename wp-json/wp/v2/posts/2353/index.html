Mark Zuckerberg downplayed recent employee turnover at Facebook\u2019s parent company Meta, even as the tech giant reported strong earnings that sent its shares soaring on Thursday.<\/p>\n

During the company\u2019s first-quarter earnings call, an analyst asked Meta executives to address the state of its \u201cemployee retention\u201d and whether the company\u2019s sinking share price in recent months led them to shift toward cash compensation rather than stock grants.<\/p>\n

As The Post reported in March, Meta\u2019s plunging stock price earlier this year after a downturn in users led some employees to gripe about wanting to change jobs \u2013 citing concerns that the company\u2019s performance was killing their stock options.<\/p>\n

Meta\u2019s CFO Dave Wehner acknowledged turnover has recently \u201cstepped up since what we experienced during the pandemic,\u201d though he noted it was \u201cstill broadly consistent with levels that we had seen pre-pandemic.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s definitely something that we\u2019re tracking and obviously, the stock price is something that we watch, but the trends are relatively in line with expectations,\u201d Wehner said. \u201cSo from that sense, we feel like we\u2019re doing OK.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meta said it added about 5,800 net new hires last quarter.AP <\/p>\n

Zuckerberg eventually addressed worker retention while discussing the company\u2019s current spending priorities as it shifts toward the metaverse \u2013 noting the company was \u201crecruiting a lot.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t think that this sort of volatility that companies face is always that unhealthy for making sure that you have the right people at companies,\u201d Zuckerberg said.<\/p>\n

Zuckerberg\u2019s remarks on \u201cvolatility\u201d among Meta\u2019s workforce came as his obsession with transforming the Facebook parent into a metaverse company reportedly results in some internal friction \u2013 with some workers left confused about their responsibilities.<\/p>\n

Mark Zuckerberg\u2019s focus on the metaverse has reportedly rankled some Facebook employees.VIA REUTERS <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m just trying to lead the company in a way where we\u2019re positioning ourselves as the premier company for building the future of social interaction and the Metaverse,\u201d Zuckerberg said. \u201cIf you care about those things, I think we\u2019re getting the best people to come work here.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meta said it had about 5,800 net new hires for the quarter. Wehner argued the number showed Meta was still having \u201csuccess in recruiting.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meta\u2019s most recent results provided a boost to employee stock options, with shares trading 11% higher as of Thursday morning. The surge occurred after the Facebook parent beat Wall Street\u2019s expectations on profit and reported it had returned to user growth after the previous quarter\u2019s downturn.<\/p>\n
