Naomi Judd\u2019s daughters \u2014 Wynonna and Ashley \u2014 fought through the tears to honor their late mom at Sunday\u2019s Country Music Hall of Fame induction. <\/p>\n

The honor was delivered just 24 hours after the music matriarch\u2019s passing by suicide after a battle with a longtime illness. She was 76.<\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t prepare anything tonight because I knew mom would probably talk the most,\u201d Wynonna, 57,\u00a0began. \u201cI\u2019m gonna make this fast, because my heart\u2019s broken, and I feel so blessed. It\u2019s a very strange dynamic, to be this broken and this blessed.\u201d<\/p>\n

The sisters also quoted Psalm 23, a common hymn for funeral services.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy mama loved you so much \u2014\u00a0and I\u2019m sorry that she couldn\u2019t hang on until today,\u201d\u00a0a sobbing Ashley, 54, told to the crowd gathered to honor her mother and sister. <\/p>\n

\u201cYour esteem for her and your regard for her really penetrated her heart, and it was your affection for her that did keep her going in the last years, and please come see Pop,\u201d the \u201cDivergent\u201d actress said. \u201cPop\u201d was her stepfather and Naomi\u2019s husband, Larry Strickland.<\/p>\n

Ashley and Wynonna Judd accept the induction on behalf of their late mother, Naomi Judd, at the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum in Nashville, Tennessee, on May 1.Getty Images for Country Music Hall <\/p>\n

\u201cWhile this is so much about the Judds as a duo, I want to take a moment to recognize my sister, a GOAT,\u201d she went on. Wynonna chimed in, \u201cThough my heart is broken, I will continue to sing.\u201d <\/p>\n

Throughout the day before the ceremony, fans had gathered outside the museum where a photo of Naomi was surrounded by flowers at the entrance to pay homage to her musical legacy.<\/p>\n

Naomi\u2019s death cast a dark shadow over the otherwise celebratory event, where other country stars came out to perform in honor of the other inductees. Alongside the Judds, Ray Charles, Eddie Bayers and Pete Drake were also memorialized at the event.<\/p>\n

The sisters held each other close during the Medallion Ceremony at the Country Music Hall Of Fame induction ceremony.Invision \t \t <\/p>\n

Garth Brooks, Trisha Yearwood, Vince Gill and many more took the stage to perform their iconic songs during the event.<\/p>\n

Wynonna and Ashley\u00a0announced their mother\u2019s death on Saturday, sharing the tragic news across social media. <\/p>\n

\u201cToday we sisters experienced a tragedy. We lost our beautiful mother to the disease of mental illness,\u201d the two said in a statement. \u201cWe are shattered. We are navigating profound grief and know that as we loved her, she was loved by her public. We are in unknown territory.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m gonna make this fast, because my heart\u2019s broken, and I feel so blessed. It\u2019s a very strange dynamic, to be this broken and this blessed,\u201d Wynonna sobbed during the induction. Invision <\/p>\n
