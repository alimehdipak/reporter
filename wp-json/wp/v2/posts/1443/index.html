A member of the Motion Picture Academy called Will Smith\u2019s 10-year Oscar ban a \u201ctoothless penalty\u201d and said the performer should \u201cvoluntarily\u201d return his award for best actor.<\/p>\n

Veteran screen and stage actor Harry Lennix compared Smith\u2019s penalty for smacking Chris Rock over a joke \u201can extended time out for a playground bully\u201d that \u201clays bare the shallowness of Hollywood morals\u201d in a scathing Variety op-ed Saturday.<\/p>\n

Lennix, a prolific character actor best known for his role in \u201cThe Five Heartbeats,\u201d said it was \u201cgalling\u201d for Smith to have been allowed to accept his \u201cKing Richard\u201d Oscar and refer to the \u201cwill of God\u201d in his speech.<\/p>\n

\u201cSmith\u2019s brutality stripped the entire evening of its prestige,\u201d Lennix, 57, wrote. \u201cThat was proven when stunned Oscar attendees gave a standing ovation to someone who\u2019d just committed an assault in front of their eyes.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rock had cracked a bald joke about Smith\u2019s wife, Jada Pinkett Smith, who suffers from alopecia. Lennix had played a love interest of Pinkett Smith\u2019s character Niobe in \u201cThe Matrix\u201d series.<\/p>\n

Harry Lennix said it was irritating for Will Smith to be able to accept his Oscar and refer to the \u201cwill of God\u201d in his speech.Patrick McMullan via Getty Image Will Smith accepts the Actor in a Leading Role award during the 94th Annual Academy Awards on March 27, 2022 in Hollywood.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cWith one deft blow, Will Smith created an existential crisis for the Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences,\u201d Lennix said. \u201cThe incident was such a jolt to societal norms that it will gnaw away at our national conscience until somehow properly atoned for.\u201d<\/p>\n

Smith resigned from the Academy before it levied its decade-long ban on the actor, but Lennix said the punishment did not fit the crime.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe stain on the Motion Picture Academy cannot be easily remediated. The only hope for a justifiable grace must involve Smith voluntarily returning his award for best actor.\u201d<\/p>\n

Will Smith slaps Chris Rock during the 94th Oscars at the Dolby Theatre in Hollywood, California on March 27, 2022. AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Smith apologized to Rock online a day after the incident. Rock had yet to speak in detail about the attack and declined to press charges.<\/p>\n

The Academy was said to be split on forcing Smith to return his trophy. Some dissenters noted that honorees convicted of serious crimes like Harvey Weinstein\u00a0and\u00a0Roman Polanski were allowed to hold onto theirs, while others argued the extremely public nature of Smith\u2019s attack demanded the unprecedented rescindment.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
