This year\u2019s will be a history-making Platinum Jubilee.<\/p>\n

For the first time in 70 years, Queen Elizabeth II won\u2019t receive the royal salute during the Trooping the Colour parade on June 2.<\/p>\n

The event is part of the 96-year-old monarch\u2019s upcoming Platinum Jubilee, a slew of celebrations to honor her seven-decade reign, as well as the Windsors who came before her, held from June 2 to June 5.<\/p>\n

Trooping the Colour refers to a military parade tied to the anniversary of the British monarchy, which enters its 260th year with the upcoming festivities. Traditionally, the queen would attend the parade in Whitehall, either on horseback or, in later years, by carriage, to be greeted by a royal salute as she carries out a symbolic inspection of the troops.<\/p>\n

Instead, she\u2019ll receive her salute by proxy \u2014 via her son\u00a0and heir Prince Charles, 73, daughter Princess Anne, 71, and grandson Prince William, 39. <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, the queen will appear on the Buckingham Palace balcony after Trooping the Colour and a personal wave to the public, the Sunday Telegraph reported per the Mirror. She\u2019ll then be joined by much of her close kin, including her parade proxies and their spouses, Charles\u2019 wife, Camilla, 74; Anne\u2019s husband, Vice Admiral Timothy Laurence, 67; and Duchess Kate Middleton, 40, with her and William\u2019s three children, George, 8, Charlotte, 7, and Louis, 4.<\/p>\n

Trooping the Colour refers to the military parade tied to the anniversary of the British monarchy.AFP via Getty Images The image shows Elizabeth II\u2019s first Trooping the Colour parade as queen in 1952.Getty Images <\/p>\n

US-based Prince Harry, 37, and his wife Meghan Markle, 40, along with children Archie, 3, and Lilibet, 11 months, are also expected to attend the parade as spectators, and join the royal family during a service of thanksgiving at St Paul\u2019s Cathedral on June 3.<\/p>\n

Her majesty\u2019s embattled son Prince Andrew, 62, is not expected to officially re-appear until Garter Day at Windsor Castle on June 13. <\/p>\n

Royal insiders have suggested it\u2019s a sign of the queen\u2019s advanced age. The announcement comes amid reports that the she is experiencing \u201cepisodic mobility problems.\u201d Worries about her health have surfaced recently, as she tested positive for COVID-19 in February. She has since recovered from the virus. <\/p>\n

The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge and their children, Princess Charlotte and Prince George, watch the flypast from the balcony of Buckingham Palace during the 2016 Trooping the Colour.Getty Images <\/p>\n

She was also absent at the state opening of Parliament on May 10, with Charles and William taking her place.<\/p>\n

However, Queen Elizabeth didn\u2019t miss the Royal Windsor Horse Show on May 13, and on May 17 surprising royal fans at London\u2019s Paddington Station who came to see the unveiling of a brand new train terminal named in her honor.<\/p>\n
