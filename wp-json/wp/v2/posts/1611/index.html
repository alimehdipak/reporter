<\/p>\n

The average Bitcoin investor is a calculating psychopath with an inflated ego, according to scientists.<\/p>\n

A team of experts recently surveyed more than 500 people to uncover the personality traits that are most common among crypto nuts.<\/p>\n

They identified that many investors exhibit signs of the \u201cdark tetrad\u201d, a group of four unsavory traits made up of narcissism, Machiavellianism, psychopathy and sadism.<\/p>\n

In plain English, that means dark tetrads have an inflated sense of self-importance and derive pleasure from the pain of others.<\/p>\n

They also find it difficult to empathize with others and are sly and manipulative.<\/p>\n

Scientists at Queensland University of Technology described their findings in research published in the journal\u00a0Personality and Individual Differences\u00a0earlier this month.<\/p>\n

They asked 566 people to complete personality surveys as well as answers questions about their attitudes to crypto.<\/p>\n

Of the participants, one in four reported that they owned crypto and two-thirds showed an interest in crypto investing.<\/p>\n

All four dark tetrad traits correlated with an affinity for investing, each for their own reasons.<\/p>\n

According to the researchers, dark tetrads are partly drawn to crypto because they are prepared to take risks.<\/p>\n

Digital assets such as Bitcoin are infamously volatile and the feast-or-famine nature of investing is particularly enticing to some.<\/p>\n

Study lead author Dr. Di Wang wrote in\u00a0The Conversation: \u201cDark tetrad traits are \u2018dark\u2019 because of their \u2018evil\u2019 qualities: Extreme selfishness and taking advantage of others without empathy.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe dark tetrad are also often related to risk-taking behaviors.\u201d<\/p>\n

The link between the traits and an affinity for Bitcoin is also driven by the fact that the currency does not have a central authority, meaning it is separate from banks and governments.<\/p>\n

Machiavellians \u2013 who are good at deception and take a calculated approach to achieving goals \u2013 like crypto \u201cprimarily because they distrust politicians and government agencies\u201d, Dr. Wang said.<\/p>\n

He added: \u201cMany crypto supporters believe governments are corrupt, and crypto avoids government corruption.\u201d<\/p>\n

Narcissists, on the other hand, are drawn to the technology because they tend to focus on the positive side of life.<\/p>\n

The researchers found that narcissists like crypto because of their great faith in the future and confidence that their own lives will improve.<\/p>\n

Lastly, impulsive psychopaths and sadists like crypto because they are thrill-seekers who fear missing out on the rewards of investing.<\/p>\n

\u201cTo them, perhaps both the pleasure from seeing another\u2019s pain and the fear of missing out are related to selfishness,\u201d Dr. Wang wrote.<\/p>\n

If you\u2019re a crypto fan yourself, don\u2019t fear \u2013 investing doesn\u2019t suddenly make you evil.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe studied only a subset of people interested in crypto who do have these traits,\u201d Dr. Wang said.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf you happen to be a Bitcoin or other crypto holder, you may or may not exhibit them.\u201d<\/p>\n
