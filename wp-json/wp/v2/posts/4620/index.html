Actress and comedian Mo\u2019Nique and Netflix have made amends \u2014 and now she will get her own comedy special.<\/p>\n

The Oscar-winning entertainer, 54, announced the collaboration in a video message posted Tuesday on Netflix\u2019s Twitter account.<\/p>\n

\u201cCan y\u2019all believe this s\u2013t?\u201d the \u201cPrecious\u201d star joked \u2014 twice \u2014 before delivering the good news. <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m excited to say that I\u2019ll be shooting my first Netflix special, in addition to reuniting with my friend, director Lee Daniels on the Netflix film \u2018The Deliverance,\u2019\u201d she said with a smile. <\/p>\n

\u201cYou won\u2019t want to miss either of them, so stay tuned,\u201d the Grammy-nominee continued. \u201cThank you all and I love y\u2019all to life,\u201d Mo\u2019Nique added. <\/p>\n

Her comedy special is set to be filmed in Atlanta in August. <\/p>\n

Mo\u2019nique filed a lawsuit against Netflix in 2019. <\/p>\n

Her new project with Daniels, meanwhile, comes just three months after the filmmaker and the actress \u2014 who won an Oscar working with Daniels on the 2009 movie \u201cPrecious\u201d \u2014 made up following a years-long feud. \u201cI am so sorry for hurting you in any way that I did,\u201d Daniels said to the comedian during an event in New York City in early April. <\/p>\n

Mo\u2019Nique had previously criticized Daniels because she felt she had been \u201cblackballed\u201d in Hollywood after she decided not to \u201ccampaign\u201d for honors before the 2010 awards season. <\/p>\n

Comparatively, Amy Schumer was offered $11 million and Dave Chappelle and Chris Rock were both offered $20 million for their specials. <\/p>\n
