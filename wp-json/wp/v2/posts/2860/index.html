Copper gutters may be the perfect accent to make your dwelling stand out. They are shiny and gorgeous when installed, and if left untreated, they turn an elegant blue-green called \u201cpatina\u201d as they age due to oxidation.<\/p>\n

What is the benefit of copper gutters?<\/h3>\n

Copper has a beautiful finish that adds a little dramatic flair and beauty of your house. Also, copper gutters do not rust as easily as aluminum gutters, nor does it crack or wear out as fast as aluminum. So, if maintained properly, copper gutters can last up to 50 years without needing to be replaced or repaired.<\/p>\n

Are copper gutters trendy?<\/p>\n

Copper: One of the most expensive gutter materials, copper is increasingly popular because of its long lifespan.<\/p>\n

Do copper gutters increase home value?<\/p>\n

All that being said, gutters themselves typically won\u2019t increase the value of your home. They\u2019re standard, especially in areas with a lot of rain, and having them isn\u2019t anything special.<\/p>\n

What happens to copper gutters over time?<\/p>\n

When copper gutters are first installed, they look like a shiny, new penny. Over time, the copper metal interacts with the air and ambient moisture. This interaction causes oxidation, and as the copper gutters age, they will slowly form a green patina, similar to the green color of the Statue of Liberty.<\/p>\n

How long does copper gutters last?<\/h3>\n

Copper gutters have a life expectancy of 50 years or more while copper downspouts can last an impressive 100 years before needing to be replaced.<\/p>\n

Are copper gutters more expensive than aluminum?<\/p>\n

Very. Copper gutters and downspouts cost between $25 and $30 a foot, installed. Compare that to steel ones that are $8 to $10 per foot, and aluminum ones that are $5 to $8 per foot, and copper\u2019s more than twice the cost of other materials.<\/p>\n

Which is better aluminum or copper gutters?<\/p>\n

The pros and cons of copper gutters<\/p>\n

Copper gutters can add 30 more years of durability compared to aluminum, providing a potential half-century of service. Copper gutters are also much lower maintenance than aluminum and even tougher when it comes to rust and heavy weather, making them less prone to dings and dents.<\/p>\n

Are copper gutters a good investment?<\/p>\n

Think about installing copper gutters if you\u2019re planning a copper roof replacement. Copper gutters may cost more to install, but these benefits, and the fact that you never have to replace them again (they can last up to 100%20 years) make them worth the investment.<\/p>\n

Is it OK to paint copper gutters?<\/h3>\n

While any material can be painted, you should never paint copper gutters. People do choose copper gutters for their strength, but they\u2019re mainly chosen for their natural beauty and patina.<\/p>\n

How do you maintain copper gutters?<\/p>\n

Clean the Exterior Portion<\/p>\n

The warm sheen of your copper gutters will eventually tarnish over time. Fortunately, you can preserve their natural good looks by cleaning the exterior portion regularly. We recommend using a soft brush, along with a standard dish-cleaning solution and lightly scrubing the gutters.<\/p>\n

Can you seal copper gutters?<\/p>\n

Many elastomeric polyurethane, silicone, butyl, polysulfide or other inorganic or rubber based sealants have shown acceptable performance. Acrylic, neoprene, and nitrile based sealants have been observed to actively corrode copper. The use of such sealants is, therefore, not recommended.<\/p>\n
