Elon Musk lashed out at NBC, detailing the network\u2019s slew of embarrassing scandals, after one of its hosts alleged that the billionaire\u2019s purchase of Twitter will give \u201cthe far right\u201d control over the social media giant.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s late Monday tweets \u2014 in which he blasted the Peacock network over its dismissal of The Post\u2019s reporting on Hunter Biden\u2019s laptop, as well as its fumbling of the Harvey Weinstein story and the Matt Lauer sexual misconduct scandal \u2014 came in response to a Sunday monologue by MSNBC\u2019s Mehdi Hasan, who hosts \u201cThe Mehdi Hasan Show\u201d airing on Peacock and MSNBC.<\/p>\n

In the segment, Hasan warned that Musk\u2019s $44 billion Twitter takeover is happening in an \u201cunspeakably dangerous moment,\u201d as what he called the \u201cpro-QAnon, pro-Neo Nazi faction\u201d of the Republican Party was poised to gain support during the 2022 midterm elections.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf that happens, we may look back on this week as a pivotal moment, when a petulant and not-so-bright billionaire casually bought one of the most influential messaging machines and just handed it to the far right,\u201d Hasan quipped.<\/p>\n

Hasan\u2019s remarks drew a harsh response from Musk, who tweeted that his monologue was \u201cNBC basically saying Republicans are Nazis\u2026\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk went on to add that NBC was the \u201csame org that covered up Hunter Biden laptop story, had Harvey Weinstein story early & killed it & built Matt Lauer his rape office.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cLovely people,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

Mehdi Hasan ripped Elon Musk\u2019s decision to buy Twitter in a scathing monologue. Getty Images for National Geogra <\/p>\n

NBC News did not immediately respond to a request for comment on Musk\u2019s tweets.<\/p>\n

Musk referenced the New York Post\u2019s exclusive report on Hunter Biden\u2019s personal laptop ahead of the 2020 presidential election. Last month, the billionaire tweeted that Twitter\u2019s decision to ban The Post\u2019s article on Biden\u2019s overseas business dealings was \u201cobviously incredibly inappropriate.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk was also referring to journalist Ronan Farrow\u2019s assertion that NBC News declined to publish his reporting on Weinstein, the Hollywood producer who has faced allegations of sexual abuse from dozens of women and was later convicted of sex crimes. Farrow eventually won the Pulitzer Prize for his reporting.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk mentioned several of NBC\u2019s past scandals.Gilbert Carrasquillo <\/p>\n

The billionaire also jabbed NBC over its relationship with disgraced former \u201cToday\u201d show anchor Lauer, who was fired in 2017 following allegations of sexual misconduct.<\/p>\n

Musk has faced widespread pushback from progressives since he revealed his intention to buy Twitter and alter the company\u2019s policies to emphasize free speech. Many left-leaning individuals have threatened to leave the platform entirely, while concerns among Twitter employees has sparked internal worries about a potential worker exodus.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, Musk said at the Met Gala on Monday that his goal is to make Twitter more \u201cinclusive\u201d and encourage more Americans to participate on the platform.<\/p>\n

Matt Lauer was fired in 2017 after allegations of sexual misconduct.NBCU Photo Bank\/NBCUniversal via <\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s criticism of NBC came on the same day the company admitted that it had found instances of plagiarism in at least 11 articles written by politics reporter Teaganne Finn.<\/p>\n

As The Post exclusively reported, an NBC News executive noted the reporter\u2019s use of unattributed passages from other sources was a \u201cbright line that we can\u2019t cross.\u201d<\/p>\n

Hasan on Monday fired back at Musk \u2014 challenging the billionaire to appear on his show to discuss his view of free speech.<\/p>\n
