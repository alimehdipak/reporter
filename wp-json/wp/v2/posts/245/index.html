<\/p>\n

It\u2019s an Irish hello. <\/p>\n

This Thursday, March 17, St. Patrick\u2019s Day will mark the first holiday in two years that Gothamites will be able to celebrate without COVID restrictions. NYC dropped mask and vaccine mandates on March 7, with Mayor Adams declaring that the city was \u201cback.\u201d <\/p>\n

Now, revelers are primed to raise an unapologetic pint of green beer and toast to the city\u2019s return. <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m excited to pick up the St. Patricks day plans I left behind March 13, 2020,\u201d Maggie McNeil, 24, a Murray Hill resident and associate at Price Waterhouse Coopers, told The Post. <\/p>\n

Aine McGirr, 26, and Sean Magee, 27, flew from Ireland to celebrate their homeland\u2019s beloved holiday. McGirr told The Post they are expecting \u201cto be a bit nuts.\u201d <\/p>\n

Danny Andrade, who is based in Queens, is equally enthused: \u201cGreen vomit all the way!!!!\u201d he hyped on Facebook. <\/p>\n

Bar and restaurant owners are eager to serve. <\/p>\n

Sean Magee and Aine McGirr toast to the return of St. Paddy\u2019s partying at Kevin\u2019s Pub on 7 West 45th Street.Brian Zak\/NY Post Bartender Trevor Kelly poured a perfect glass of Guinness at Megan\u2019s Bar + Kitchen as people began getting in the Irish mood on Wednesday. Brian Zak\/NY Post <\/p>\n

\n

\u201cWe\u2019re prepared for massive crowds. People are itching to back to do something. It seems long overdue. People [will] be a bit more rowdy this year.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sean Reilly of Midtown\u2019s Sean\u2019s Bear + Kitchen<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

\u201cI think the city is ready for a bit of a party now,\u201d said Danny McDonald \u2014 owner of Swift Hibernian Lounge, Ulysses, The Dead Rabbit, Underdog, Harry\u2019s and The Bar at Moynihan Food Hall \u2014 told The Post. <\/p>\n

\u201cEverybody is chomping at the bit to feel a bit like old New York,\u201d the restaurateur from Ireland said. <\/p>\n

He remembers the heartbreak when he was forced to close house in 2020 just days before St. Patrick\u2019s Day, as New York City enforced a coronavirus lockdown and required bars to shutter their doors.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe had all the Guinness and Irish whiskey ready,\u201d McDonald lamented.<\/p>\n

Some bars held small celebrations in March 2021, but COVID-19 was still top-of-mind \u2014 restricting capacity, limiting bar hours, requiring patrons to order food and stay seated. <\/p>\n

Sean, Bernie and Kevin Reilly are excited to welcome massive and rowdy crowds to their nine Irish pubs including Sean\u2019s Bar + Kitchen and Reilly\u2019s Plates & Pours. Brian Zak\/NY Post Ciarra, Mary-Kate and Marquerite Hamill hoist pre-St. Patrick\u2019s pints at at Connolly\u2019s Pub, 121 West 45th Street.Brian Zak\/NY Post <\/p>\n

This year, St. Patrick\u2019s Day falls on a Thursday, so many of the celebrations will blast through the weekend. <\/p>\n

Ireland native Bernie Reilly and his sons, Sean and Kevin, are ready for their busiest day of the year and have already changed out several of their beer lines for the 700 extra kegs of Guinness they ordered for their Midtown bars, which include Sean\u2019s Bar + Kitchen, Kevin\u2019s Pub, Megan\u2019s Bar + Kitchen, Reilly\u2019s Plates & Pours, Connolly\u2019s, The Perfect Pint and Merrion Row Hotel & Public House. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019re prepared for massive crowds. People are itching to back to do something. It seems long overdue,\u201d said Sean, who expects \u201cpeople to be a bit more rowdy this year.\u201d<\/p>\n

Flashback to March 16, 2020: A final round of ales sit on a table at McSorley\u2019s Old Ale House, established in 1854 and referred to as NYC\u2019s oldest Irish pub, was ordered to close at 8 p.m. as part of a city-wide order to close bars and restaurants in an effort to slow the spread of coronavirus the day before Saint Patrick\u2019s Day.REUTERS New York City is back in full swing just in time for St. Patrick\u2019s Day \u2014 one of the booziest holidays of the year. Sipa USA via AP <\/p>\n

Some Irish hot spots were too excited to contain their excitement and have already begun their holiday celebrations. <\/p>\n

The Wicked Monk in Brooklyn has been honoring the Irish holiday with traditional Irish music sessions since football season ended in February. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt feels like we\u2019re going back to normal. Feels like we\u2019re back home,\u201d Megan Wynne, manager of the venue, told The Post. <\/p>\n

\u201cYou can smell the corned beef down the street,\u201d Wynne added. <\/p>\n

But some New Yorkers aren\u2019t quite ready to celebrate. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s cool to sit back and know St. Patrick\u2019s Day in New York City is going to go the same way as Santa Con. Tons of drunk people, new Covid variant, lifted restrictions, etc. Like watching a car crash in slow motion,\u201d Ben Natan, an actor in New York City, tweeted.<\/p>\n

Aaron Doyle, Mark Lynch, Jamie Andrews, John Darcy, Aidan Morris, Daniel Kavanagh and Eoghan Doyle gearing up for the party Wednesday at Connolly\u2019s Pub, 121 West 45th Street.Brian Zak\/NY Post New York City was placed in lockdown due to COVID days before St. Patrick\u2019s Day in 2020. \u201cIt feels like we\u2019re going back to normal. Feels like we\u2019re back home,\u201d said Megan Wynne, manager of the Wicked Monk, pictured here.Courtesy of @wickedmonk <\/p>\n

But many are ready to party. Up in the Bronx, An B\u00e9al Bocht Cafe manager Bronagh Harmon told The Post that celebrations this year will be the first time they will have live music since the pandemic started. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis is a big, big coming-back\u00a0for the city,\u201d said Harmon.<\/p>\n

St. Patrick\u2019s Day falls on a Thursday this year, and celebrations are expected to last all weekend. Getty Images \t \t <\/p>\n

The bar is also ready to welcome back what they\u2019ve billed as\u00a0the \u201cWorld\u2019s Shortest St. Patrick\u2019s Day Parade,\u201d\u00a0which stretches a wee 47 steps from one door of the bar to another. The annual parade usually raises money to feed the homeless, but, this year, funds will be reallocated to World Central Kitchen to support Ukrainian refugees. <\/p>\n

New York City\u2019s big St. Patrick\u2019s Day Parade is also back on this year after a two-year hiatus. It\u2019s the oldest and largest parade of its kind in the world, according to organizers. <\/p>\n

Harmon hopes that a return to a normal St. Patrick\u2019s Day celebration will provide locals with a much needed opportunity to cut loose. <\/p>\n

\u201cForget about your problems for the next three or four days,\u201d she said. \u201cYou can deal with them on Monday.\u201d<\/p>\n

Paddy Maguire, who owns Paddy Maguire\u2019s Ale House in Gramercy, had similar advice.<\/p>\n

\u201cGet out there. Go to the parade. Have fun and drop into every bar and make sure mine is one of them,\u201d said the 62 year old, who opened his namesake bar in 1995. \u201cBut don\u2019t get too drunk \u2014 I don\u2019t want you here at the last stop.
