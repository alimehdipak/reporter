Zachary Levi is getting candid about his lifelong battles with anxiety, mental health and depression.<\/p>\n

The 41-year-old actor details his struggles in his new book \u201cRadical Love: Learning To Accept Yourself and Others,\u201d as well as trying to find a place where he achieved self-love.<\/p>\n

Levi opened up about his battles with suicidal thoughts and his mental breakdown at age 37 with journalist\u00a0Elizabeth Vargas\u00a0on her\u00a0\u201cHeart of the Matter\u201d podcast ahead of his book\u2019s release, according to the Hollywood Reporter.<\/p>\n

The \u201cShazam!\u201d star noted, \u201cI didn\u2019t realize that I was struggling with these things until I was 37, about five years ago . . . and I had a complete mental breakdown.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cShazam!\u201d star revealed that the incident occurred once he moved to Austin, Texas, about five years ago. FilmMagic <\/p>\n

He said the breakdown happened once he moved to Austin, Texas, and he realized what was happening when he was just driving around town and could not decide on a restaurant to eat at.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m sitting in my truck, and vividly, I remember I was holding onto the wheel and I was just shaking back and forth, that like almost trying to shake myself out of what it was going on, and I\u2019m just weeping. I\u2019m just crying,\u201d he recounted. \u201cI\u2019m like, \u2018God, help me.\u2019 \u201c<\/p>\n

\u201cI had been in dark places in my life before, but I guess in those moments I had people around me,\u201d Levi added. WireImage <\/p>\n

He explained he even had \u201cvery active thoughts of ending my life.\u201d Despite them not being the first time, this instance was different as he \u201cdidn\u2019t have anybody\u201d after moving to Texas.<\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t have a support structure. So, in this particular moment, I\u2019m out here in this wonderful city, but basically by myself, and the darkness surrounds me again,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

He went on, \u201cThe lies are whispering into my ear and the failure that I felt that I was enough to be like, \u2018Zach, it doesn\u2019t feel like you\u2019re going to make it out of this.\u2019 \u201c<\/p>\n

A friend of his then suggested he go through \u201cintensive life-changing, life-saving therapy\u201d at a psychiatric hospital for about a month.<\/p>\n

Levi\u2019s book \u201cRadical Love: Learning To Accept Yourself and Others\u201d will be released on June 28.Getty Images <\/p>\n

The \u201cChuck\u201d alum also explained that he spent his childhood growing up with a stepfather who had high expectations of him and a mother \u201cwho was a borderline personality.\u201d<\/p>\n

He later turned to \u201csex or drugs or booze\u201d to sedate himself \u201cfrom the pain that I was running away from most of my life.\u201d <\/p>\n

Levi said: \u201cThe irony is that booze can give you this temporary relief, but then the next day amplifies that anxiety tenfold. So, then you\u2019re running back to get more and it just becomes this vicious cycle.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cTangled\u201d star\u2019s next project will be the \u201cSpy Kids\u201d franchise reboot and he will be appearing alongside \u201cJane the Virgin\u201d alum Gina Rodriguez.<\/p>\n

The plot line of the as-yet-untitled revival reads: \u201cWhen the children of the world\u2019s greatest secret agents unwittingly help a powerful game developer unleash a computer virus that gives him control of all technology, they must become spies themselves to save their parents and the world.\u201d<\/p>\n
