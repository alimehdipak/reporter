Joe Rogan took aim at woke \u201cactivist\u201d employees of large Silicon Valley tech firms, calling them \u201cmentally ill\u201d and \u201clunatics who are running the asylum to a certain extent.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rogan made the comments on his Spotify podcast during an interview Wednesday with Antonio Garc\u00eda Mart\u00ednez, an ex-engineer at Apple and Facebook who parted ways with the iPhone maker after employees complained of \u201cmisogynistic\u201d writings in his book.<\/p>\n

During the segment, Rogan asked Mart\u00ednez what it was like to work for a large tech firm.<\/p>\n

\u201cFor someone from the outside, we look at it and say: \u2018How are those f\u2013king places run?’\u201d Rogan said.<\/p>\n

The stand-up comedian then said he had a conversation with a \u201cgood friend\u201d who was in a management position at Google before leaving the firm for another \u201clarge tech company.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe way she described it to me was, \u2018It is utter madness,’\u201d Rogan said.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe lunatics are running the asylum to a certain extent because there\u2019s a lot of people working inside the company now that legitimately are mentally ill and they consider themselves activists,\u201d according to Rogan.<\/p>\n

He added that tech bosses at these companies \u201chave to placate\u201d the workers \u201cbecause they\u2019re a certain percentage of the population that works for the company, and they\u2019re the loudest, and they oftentimes don\u2019t get work done.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rogan made the comment while interviewing Antonio Garcia Martinez, a former Apple engineer who left after employees petitioned management over allegedly misogynistic writings in his book.PowerfulJRE\/YouTube <\/p>\n

Rogan said that when these workers are \u201cconfronted\u201d over not getting work done, \u201cthey talk about their activism.\u201d<\/p>\n

He said his friend was forced to reprimand tech workers by telling them: \u201cYou are here for X amount of hours per day. This is your f\u2014\u2013g job. You\u2019re not an activist.\u201d<\/p>\n

Mart\u00ednez told Rogan that the companies are partly to blame because workers are encouraged by human resources departments to \u201cbring the real self to work.\u201d<\/p>\n

He described big tech firms like Facebook as \u201ckind of like a cult\u201d since employees are brought to a \u201ccampus lifestyle\u201d where \u201cthey do your laundry for you\u201d and \u201cfeed you.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cFacebook was a cult, and I joined it, and I was a happy member of it,\u201d Mart\u00ednez said. \u201cIt was very powerful. Everyone sacrificed themselves for the sake of the empire and its emperor.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rogan has been a frequent critic of tech firms for censoring and de-platforming those with viewpoints deemed outside the mainstream.<\/p>\n

Spotify, which signed Rogan to a nine-figure licensing deal, was pressured to dump the controversial podcaster after rocker Neil Young and other artists removed their songs from the streaming platform.<\/p>\n
