Ali Wentworth and her news anchor husband George Stephanopoulos have kept a house in the Hamptons for 20 years: first in East Hampton, then Southampton, and now back to East \u2014 on the very same street where they used to reside.<\/p>\n

\u201cEast Hampton is the place we go to for relaxation, family time and decompression,\u201d Wentworth, 57, tells Alexa. \u201cI have been pregnant there, raised children, celebrated birthdays, holidays and made great friends. Most importantly, I learned how to rake for clams there.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ali Wentworth and her husband George Stephanopoulos have called the Hamptons home for two decades.Getty Images <\/p>\n

It\u2019s also the place the actor and writer (who\u2019s starred in everything from \u201cIn
Living Color\u201d to the Pop series \u201cNightcap,\u201d which she also created) spent much of the early days of the pandemic, recovering from COVID-19 and penning her new book, \u201cAli\u2019s Well That Ends Well: Tales of Desperation and a Little Inspiration.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI wrote it as a journal of what it was like being in quarantine with my family and what our coping mechanisms were,\u201d she said. \u201cI hope people who read it remember the lessons they learned during the extremely unprecedented time of a global pandemic. And maybe laugh a little \u2014 particularly with the chapter about our obsession with the show \u2018Love Island Australia.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

Here are a few of the comedian\u2019s favorite homes away from home in the Hamptons.<\/p>\n
