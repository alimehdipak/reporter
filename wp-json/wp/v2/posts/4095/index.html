The Prince of Darkness is back releasing music barely two weeks after his \u201clife-altering surgery.\u201d<\/p>\n

On Friday, fans were treated to his new song, \u201cPatient Number 9,\u201d and a music video directed by \u201cThe Amazing Spider Man\u201d artist Todd McFarlane.<\/p>\n

The video features Osbourne portraying a patient at a mental institution along with an illustrated version of him drawn by McFarlane. He fights demons in the two distinct styles.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe song is about a mental institution,\u201d Osbourne said of the title track in a statement to Loudwire. \u201cHaving someone like Jeff Beck play on my album is just incredible, a total honor. There\u2019s no other guitar player that plays like him and his solo on \u2018Patient Number 9\u2019 is just jaw-dropping.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

The music video for \u201cPatient Number 9\u201d was released Friday morning. Ozzy Osbourne in February 2020.Getty Images for iHeartMedia <\/p>\n

The album is set to have an all-star lineup including Pearl Jam\u2019s Mike McCready, Red Hot Chili Peppers drummer Chad Smith, Zakk Wylde, Metallica\u2019s Robert Trujillo, Duff McKagan, Eric Clapton and the late Taylor Hawkins. Even Osbourne\u2019s former Black Sabbath bandmate, Tony Iommi, is making his first debut on one of his solo albums.<\/p>\n

\u201cPatient Number 9\u201d comes out shortly after the singer underwent surgery to realign pins in his neck and back. This is in addition to suffering from Parkinson\u2019s Disease.<\/p>\n

The former Black Sabbath star had been suffering from neck injuries dating back to 2003 which stemmed from a quad biking accident. A fall in 2019 made the condition worse and 15 screws were inserted into his back.\u00a0<\/p>\n

A still from Osbourne\u2019s new video.Ozzy Osbourne <\/p>\n

Osbourne was discharged one day after the surgery, June 14, and is still on the road to recovery.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am definitely feeling the love and support from all my fans and send everyone a big thank you for their thoughts, prayers and well wishes during my recovery,\u201d he said in an Instagram post.\u00a0<\/p>\n
