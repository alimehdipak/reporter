    BANGKOK, Thailand — Manchester United manager Erik ten Hag insisted Cristiano Ronaldo is not available for transfer and that he is planning on having the striker in his squad next season.<\/p>\n

    Ten Hag revealed he had a conversation with Ronaldo before news broke that the 37-year-old wants to leave Old Trafford this summer but said the pair have not spoken since.<\/p>\n

    Ronaldo has been granted time off to deal with a family issue and has not travelled to Bangkok ahead of the first game of the preseason against Liverpool at the Rajamangala National Stadium on Tuesday.<\/p>\n

    “He’s not with us and it’s due to personal issues,” ten Hag said at a news conference Monday.<\/p>\n

    “We are planning for Cristiano Ronaldo for the season and that’s it. I’m looking forward to working with him. I have read [that he wants to leave], but what I say is Cristiano is not for sale, he is in our plans and we want success together.<\/p>\n

    “I spoke with him before this issue came up, I had a conversation with him and I had a good talk. That is between Cristiano and me, what I can confirm is we had a really good conversation together.”<\/p>\n

    Ten Hag has arrived in Thailand with Tyrell Malacia as the only new signing in the squad.<\/p>\n

    Erik ten Hag said that Cristiano Ronaldo is in his plans for next season, despite the striker saying he wants to leave.\u00a0Ash Donelon\/Manchester United<\/p>\n

    Sources have told ESPN that United are chasing Frenkie de Jong and Lisandro Martinez, and ten Hag admitted he is keen to strengthen his midfield.<\/p>\n

    Christian Eriksen is expected to sign a three-year deal following a medical and could join up with the squad in Australia.<\/p>\n

    “We know what we want,” ten Hag said. “We not only want new players, we want the right new players. “We have a good squad, there’s huge potential, only when we find [the right players to make] an improvement in our squad we will do [it].<\/p>\n

    “Definitely we are searching for players in midfield. We have a need and we are still looking for players.<\/p>\n

    “It’s going to be a long season but a season that is quite unusual due to the World Cup, so you need to be prepared for this and need more players than in a normal season.”<\/p>\n

    Ten Hag also confirmed that Harry Maguire will remain club captain.<\/p>\n

    “I have to get to know all of the players, and that takes time, but he has been captain for years,” he said. “He has achieved a lot of success, so I don’t doubt this issue.”<\/p>\n
