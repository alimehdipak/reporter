Did you know that tooth decay is one of the most common chronic diseases in children and adults? It’s also the most preventable disease. In this post, we’ll discuss some of the top signs of tooth decay so that you can catch it early and seek treatment if necessary. Keep reading to learn more!<\/span><\/p>\n

Causes: What causes tooth decay?<\/span><\/h2>\n

Tooth decay is a problem that can affect any person, regardless of their age or race. It’s caused by a combination of factors, including genetics, diet, and lifestyle choices. Here are some of the most common causes of tooth decay:<\/span><\/p>\n

Poor oral hygiene \u2013 Not brushing your teeth enough and not using mouthwash on a regular basis can lead to tooth decay.<\/span><\/p>\n

Smoking \u2013 Cigarette smoke contains nicotine, which is an ingredient in many kinds of toothpaste and gels that can damage teeth.<\/span><\/p>\n

Eating foods and drinks that contain harmful acids (like citrus fruits) or sugar (like sugary drinks) also contributes to tooth decay.<\/span><\/p>\n

Symptoms: What are the top signs of tooth decay?<\/span><\/h2>\n

Tooth decay is a common problem that can lead to tooth loss. Here are the top signs of tooth decay:\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  1. You may notice changes in your teeth’s color, shape, or appearance.<\/span><\/li>\n
  2. You may experience pain when you chew or eat.<\/span><\/li>\n
  3. You may have trouble swallowing or speaking because of the pain.<\/span><\/li>\n
  4. Tooth decay can cause permanent damage to your teeth and gums.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    If you have tooth decay, you should see a dentist as soon as possible to get it treated properly. There are several ways to treat tooth decay, but all require professional help from a dentist.<\/span><\/p>\n

    If you have any of the top signs of tooth decay, don’t wait to see a dentist<\/a> – get treatment right away!<\/span><\/p>\n

    Diagnosis and Treatment: How is tooth decay diagnosed and treated?<\/span><\/h2>\n

    Tooth decay<\/a> is a problem that can be diagnosed through a dentist’s examination and can be treated with various methods. The dentist will look at the teeth to see if there are any signs of decay, such as browning, softness, or changes in shape. If there is evidence of tooth decay, the dentist may recommend routine dental cleanings and checkups to monitor the progress of the disease. Treatment options may include filling cavities with a material like an amalgam or porcelain and removing infected teeth.<\/span><\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
