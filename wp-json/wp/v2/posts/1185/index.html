<\/p>\n

A man has snapped one of the clearest photos of the International Space Station ever taken from Earth.<\/p>\n

German photographer Sebastian Voltmer snapped a remarkable image in which you can make out two astronauts performing a spacewalk.<\/p>\n

Sebastian captured the \u201conce-in-a-lifetime\u201d photo earlier last week and later shared his creation\u00a0on Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cI feel like I just made a once in a lifetime image,\u201d he wrote of the photograph.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s probably the first ground-based picture showing two spacewalkers on the\u00a0ISS\u00a0at the same time.\u201d<\/p>\n

The photo was snapped on March 23 as the space station \u2013 which orbits 250 miles above Earth \u2013 passed over Germany.<\/p>\n

At the time, astronauts Raja Chari and Matthias Maurer were clambering around the extraterrestrial lab\u2019s exterior during a planned spacewalk.<\/p>\n

They performed a number of minor part installations and fixes during the seven-hour, zero-gravity maintenance job.<\/p>\n

Sebastian took the photo through his C11 EdgeHD telescope from St. Wendel in west Germany, the hometown of ESA astronaut Maurer.<\/p>\n

He\u2019s visible at work near the ISS\u2019s Columbus European lab segment, while Raja appears on the robotic arm \u201cCanadarm2\u201d.<\/p>\n

Sebastian\u2019s original goal was to nab a picture of the ISS while his countryman was at work on the exterior.<\/p>\n

It was only two days later, following a tip from fellow astrophotographer Philip Smith, that he realised he had also captured Raja.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwo days after the spacewalk happened, Smith contacted me,\u201d Sebastian wrote on\u00a0spaceweather.com.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe is a world well-known photographer of all things ISS and has been published in many forms of media.<\/p>\n

\u201cSmith with his ISS obsession noticed on my picture that NASA astronaut Raja Chari is also detectable.\u201d<\/p>\n

The pair remain on the orbiting outpost and make up half of the current occupants.<\/p>\n

Yesterday, a Nasa astronaut finally returned to Earth after Russia had earlier threatened to abandon him in space.<\/p>\n

Mark Vande Hei, 55, arrived from the International Space Station on March 30 onboard a Russian Soyuz spacecraft.<\/p>\n

Vande Hei and two Russian cosmonauts, Anton Shkaplerov and Pyotr Dubrov, landed shortly after 07.28 a.m. ET (12.28 p.m. UK time).<\/p>\n

In response to sanctions imposed on Russia over its\u00a0invasion of Ukraine, the country\u2019s space agency had hinted that they would leave him behind.<\/p>\n

Head of Roscosmos Dmitry Rogozin posted a sinister video on March 13 that depicted the mission\u2019s two cosmonauts flying home without Hei.<\/p>\n

Rogozin is known for making inflammatory comments on social media in order to spark panic in the West.<\/p>\n

Roscosmos later clarified that it had no intention of leaving Hei behind.<\/p>\n
