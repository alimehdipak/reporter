After a video of a Sesame Place Philadelphia character ignoring two black children went viral amid accusations of racism, the organization is promising to make changes.<\/p>\n

The footage shared by a mother \u2014 who has now retained a lawyer, according to TMZ \u2014 on Instagram shows her young daughters being ignored by the Rosita character from \u201cSesame Street.\u201d The Muppet appears to high-five the park visitors around them, but skipped over the two little girls of color.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m going to keep posting this, because this had me hot,\u201d one of the girl\u2019s mothers, posting under the Instagram handle\u00a0@__jodiii__, wrote Saturday.<\/p>\n

The original video has racked up more than 550,000 views as of Tuesday morning. A repost of the video on Twitter has gained over 8.5 million additional views.<\/p>\n

The incident caught the attention of many celebrities, including actress Yvette Nicole Brown, who tweeted, \u201cEvery Black woman was once a little Black girl who made this face when the way things are for us in America first broke her heart.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sesame Workshop, the nonprofit behind \u201cSesame Street,\u201d responded to the situation in a statement on social media.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe have been in contact with Sesame Place, our licensed park partner, and they have assured us that they will conduct bias training and a thorough review of the ways in which they engage with families and guests,\u201d they wrote in the statement.<\/p>\n

The video shared by a mother on Instagram shows her young daughters being ignored by the Rosita character from \u201cSesame Street.\u201dInstagram\/@__jodiii__ <\/p>\n

\u201cAs a global nonprofit educational organization with a mission to help children grow smarter, stronger and kinder, Sesame Workshop has always stood for respect, inclusion and belonging and is committed to providing the highest quality engaging experiences for all children and families,\u201d the statement went on.<\/p>\n

Moments after the nonprofit shared its statement, Sesame Place Philadelphia shared a new statement that read, \u201cWe sincerely apologize to the family for their experience in our park on Saturday; we know that it\u2019s not OK.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWe will conduct training for our employees so they better understand, recognize and deliver an inclusive, equitable and entertaining experience to our guests,\u201d it continued.<\/p>\n

This comes after Sesame Place Philadelphia\u2019s initial statement in defense, claiming the incident was not racially motivated.<\/p>\n
