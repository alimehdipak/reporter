The future of \u201cWicked\u201d has been changed \u2014 but is it \u201cFor Good\u201d?<\/p>\n

Director Jon M. Chu announced that his upcoming film adaptation of the musical will be split into two parts.<\/p>\n

The movies, based on the hit Broadway show, will be released on Christmas Days 2024 and 2025.<\/p>\n

Chu declared in his announcement via Twitter that \u201cit would be impossible to wrestle the story of \u2018Wicked\u2019 into a single film without doing some real damage to it.<\/p>\n

\u201cAs we tried to cut songs or trim characters, those decisions began to feel like fatal compromises to the source material that has entertained us all for so many years,\u201d the 42-year-old filmmaker continued.<\/p>\n

Chu explained that giving themselves a \u201cbigger canvas\u201d with two movies will allow for the story of \u201cWicked\u201d to be told \u201cas it was meant to be told while bringing even more depth and surprise to the journeys of these beloved characters.\u201d<\/p>\n

The movie will star Ariana Grande, 28, as Glinda and Cynthia Erivo, 35, as Elphaba. In its original Broadway run, the roles were played by Kristin Chenoweth and Idina Menzel, respectively.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Grande also shared the news to Instagram with the caption: \u201csome thrillifying news.\u201d<\/p>\n

While some fans did seem thrilled with getting \u201cWicked x2,\u201d others called it \u201cjust a money grab\u201d and a \u201cbad decision,\u201d while another skeptic asked: \u201cHow is it that a ~2.5 hour stage musical (with a 15-20 minute intermission) doesn\u2019t fit into a ~2.5 hour movie? I mean, that already gives you ~20 *more* minutes of time in the movie for the same material?\u201d<\/p>\n

A prequel to \u201cThe Wizard of Oz,\u201d \u201cWicked\u201d tells the story leading up to Dorothy\u2019s arrival in Oz. The musical is based on the 1995 novel \u201cWicked: The Life and Times of the Wicked Witch of the West\u201d by Gregory Maguire.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Director Jon M. Chu announced that the \u201cWicked\u201d movie will be split into two parts.FREDERIC J. BROWN\/AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cWicked\u201d opened on Broadway in 2003 and is the 11th longest-running Broadway show ever. It\u2019s earned more than $1 billion and is in second place as the highest-grossing Broadway musical of all time, behind \u201cThe Lion King,\u201d according to Variety.<\/p>\n

The adaptation isn\u2019t Chu\u2019s first. His musical adaptation of \u201cIn the Heights\u201d premiered in theaters and on HBO Max last summer.<\/p>\n
