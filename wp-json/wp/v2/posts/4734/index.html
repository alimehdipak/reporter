It\u2019s no secret celebrities have more cash to splash on a dazzling diamond ring than the average person \u2014 but how much do you think the jewels are really worth?<\/p>\n

One expert has revealed the actual cost of the bling \u2014 and, better yet, he\u2019s also found a way to re-create some of the pricier pieces for a fraction of the cost.<\/p>\n

Michael Fallah, 36, from Sydney, runs his own jewelry business where he creates designs for clients who typically spend $4,900 to $13,900 per piece.<\/p>\n

Fallah launched a TikTok account to share his knowledge with fans, including videos in which he estimates how much celebrities\u2019 engagement rings are really worth.<\/p>\n

Starting with Kourtney Kardashian\u2019s engagement ring \u2014 which she received from now-husband Travis Barker \u2014 Fallah estimates the ring is worth around $1 million.<\/p>\n

\u201cKourtney\u2019s ring features a 12-carat oval diamond, set in platinum, and features a thin diamond band,\u201d he says. \u201cI would estimate [it\u2019s worth] around $1 million.\u201d<\/p>\n

To replicate the ring, he recommends fans start by looking for an oval diamond.<\/p>\n

\u201cA 2- to 3-carat will range from $13,900 to $38,000,\u201d he told JamPrime.com. \u201cOpt for a thin diamond band, select platinum and then you\u2019re done.\u201d<\/p>\n

Fallah launched a TikTok account to share his passion.Jam Press Vid\/@michaelarthurdiam <\/p>\n

As for model and actress Nicola Peltz\u2019s ring \u2014 she recently wed Brooklyn Beckham \u2014 the emerald cut ring is estimated to be worth around $346,200.<\/p>\n

\u201cBrooklyn Beckham proposed with an emerald cut valued at $242,340,\u201d Fallah explains.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut after getting married, Nicola upgraded to an oval-cut diamond with 8 to 10 carats, worth roughly $969,360.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIt is complemented by her wedding band, which is roughly $470,832.\u201d<\/p>\n

Nicola Peltz upgraded to an oval-cut diamond when she got married.Jam Press Vid\/@michaelarthurdiam <\/p>\n

If you don\u2019t have a million dollars or so lying around, Fallah says lab-grown diamonds are a great way to bring the cost of wedding rings down.<\/p>\n

\u201cLab-grown diamonds are chemically the same as natural diamonds,\u201d he says. \u201cWe have been able to copy the natural process of a diamond being formed in the earth with machines.<\/p>\n

\u201cI would suggest all the diamonds including the side diamonds to be lab-grown to bring the costs down and make it more achievable for the average individual,\u201d he continues, before saying you could re-create Nicola\u2019s engagement ring for $13,848.<\/p>\n

Fallah also has a look at Paris Hilton\u2019s huge jewel \u2014 a 24-carat emerald with trapezoid accent diamonds \u2014 which he estimates is worth a huge $3.4 million.<\/p>\n

Paris Hilton\u2019s ring is estimated to be worth millions, but Fallah says you can re-create it for less using lab-grown diamonds.Jam Press Vid\/@michaelarthurdiam <\/p>\n

Revealing how to get a ring like the hotel heir, Fallah says to \u201cselect an emerald cut for the center stone. A 1- to 2-carat would be worth roughly $4,900 to $13,900.<\/p>\n

\u201cAdd a small trapezoid or baguette diamond on either side,\u201d he explains. \u201cSelect platinum for the middle and voil\u00e0 \u2014 you\u2019re done.\u201d<\/p>\n
