With cybercrime on the rise, it\u2019s important to know if your information has been breached. Here\u2019s how:<\/p>\n

Today, cybercriminals target\u00a0our data in a wide variety of ways: fake texts, malware-laden links, and even fraudulent mail that impersonates government officials.<\/p>\n

Sometimes, cyberattacks can be so discreet that you have no idea your device is even affected.<\/p>\n

Hackers can target portals of information like your Gmail, Facebook, and iCloud accounts.<\/p>\n

Through these accounts, they can then access sensitive information like bank details.<\/p>\n

However, there are certain signs you can keep your eyes open for \u2014 and the sooner you learn these red flags, the more quickly you can tackle them.<\/p>\n

Signs that your accounts\/device has been hacked<\/h2>\n

If your phone, laptop or desktop starts acting glitchy or sluggish, that could mean you have been hacked.<\/p>\n

Similarly, if your device is getting inundated with fake warnings or suspicious-looking browser pop-ups, you may be a victim of a malware attack.<\/p>\n

One of the many ways to notice if your device has been hacked is if your device is getting flooded with fake warnings or pop-ups.Shutterstock <\/p>\n

Another tell-tale sign is if your browser is constantly redirected to websites without you initiating it.<\/p>\n

If your browser also begins displaying X-rated pop-ups, your device may be the victim of malicious software.<\/p>\n

Are your friends receiving random messages from your Facebook account? That\u2019s a good indication that your social media account has been breached.<\/p>\n

Certain viruses need to be taken to a professional cybersecurity expert to resolve.Shutterstock <\/p>\n

Furthermore, if your browser or device notifies you that something has been compromised, then it likely has been.<\/p>\n

What to do if your device has been hacked<\/h2>\n

One of the easiest ways to relieve your device of a virus or malware is to undergo a factory reset.<\/p>\n

This is a simple procedure that completely cleans your device\u2019s internal storage.<\/p>\n

You can also run an anti-virus program to clear out the malware, though most of these services will cost you money.<\/p>\n

If the virus attacking your device is especially sophisticated, you may need to take it to a professional cybersecurity expert.<\/p>\n
