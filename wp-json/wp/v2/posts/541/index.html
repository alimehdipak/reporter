<\/p>\n

Walt Disney World has reportedly denounced a routine by a high school drill team that was accused of \u201cracism\u201d and borrowing from Native American stereotypes during a recent performance there. <\/p>\n

Video shot March 15 of the Port Neches-Groves High School Indianettes from Texas shows the all-women squad dressed in purple with white fringe as they march and dance \u2014 seemingly borrowing from Native culture \u2014 while also chanting, \u201cScalp \u2019em, Indians, scalp \u2019em.\u201d<\/p>\n

The footage of the group, who were invited to perform in Orlando, sparked a backlash by Native American officials who called their act \u201cdehumanizing.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe live performance in our park did not reflect our core values, and we regret it took place,\u201d Disney spokesperson Jacquee Wahler told Deadline in a statement. \u201cIt was not consistent with the audition tape the school provided and we have immediately put measures in place so this is not repeated.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Post also has reached out to Disney representatives for comment.<\/p>\n

Ojibwe tribal attorney Tara Houska was among those offering takedowns of the performance via social media, writing on Twitter: \u201cCuz a bunch of kids in fringe chanting \u2018scalp \u2018em Indians, scalp \u2018em\u2019 is honor, right? And any Natives who attend @pngisd should prolly just accept their classmates dehumanizing them cuz \u2018tradition\u2019, right? Shame on @DisneyParks hosting this.\u201d<\/p>\n

Her complaint on Thursday attracted more than 11,000 supporters who called out the \u201cracist\u201d performance, including other tribal community leaders \u2014 such as Kansas State Rep. Christina Haswood, who identifies as Din\u00e9 and implored Disney to \u201cdo better,\u201d adding, \u201cIt\u2019s ignorance at this point.\u201d <\/p>\n

Kelly Lynne D\u2019Angelo, a writer on TNT\u2019s \u201cMiracle Workers\u201d and a Tongva, slammed the lack of urgency to recognize racism against Native people.<\/p>\n

\u201c99% percent of the people sharing their outrage about this are Native people. Can\u2019t you see that\u2019s the problem too?\u201d she wrote. \u201cWhy must WE be the ones to speak up of all the blatant racism against us? Of our constant mistreatment? Why must we fight, tooth and nail, for you to understand we are human and alive and thriving too?\u201d<\/p>\n

D\u2019Angelo went on to condemn the \u201csavage\u201d stereotype encouraged by the \u201cscalp \u2019em\u201d chant. \u201cThe thing is: our ways were right and always have been,\u201d she said. \u201cWe know how to make bounty on this earth. How to live EASILY. Our relational practices with each other and the earth are a FUNDAMENTAL CORE to a healthy and harmonious human experience.\u201d<\/p>\n

Houska, who was an advisor to Sen. Bernie Sanders during his 2016 campaign, continued her dig in a tweet thread, suggesting that Disney may have green-lit the routine on just one condition. <\/p>\n

\u201c@pnghsndns cheerleaders weren\u2019t allowed to wear the fake headdresses as they usually do, but the scalp \u2018em chant was approved,\u201d she shared with an article published Wednesday on Disney Parks fan site WDW News Today.<\/p>\n

In recent years Disney Parks has pledged to operate and develop new attractions with one guiding principle in mind: \u201cInclusion.\u201d To that end, they\u2019ve made several changes to demonstrate their dedication to the cause, such as scrapping the phrase \u201cladies and gentleman, boys and girls\u201d during the \u201cHappily Ever After\u201d fireworks show at Magic Kingdom. <\/p>\n

Last year, they also updated their \u201cjaw-dropping\u201d Jungle Cruise ride, first opened in 1955, by removing problematic depictions of religious symbols and references to indigenous societies as headhunting \u201csavages.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

\u201cThe exciting changes we\u2019re making to one of @Disney\u2019s most popular classic attractions, Jungle Cruise, reflect our commitment to creating unparalleled experiences that reflect, not only the best in storytelling, but also the values and rich diversity of our world,\u201d Disney President Bob Iger tweeted at the time.<\/p>\n

Disney\u2019s good-faith inclusion campaign was most recently compromised by their CEO Bob Chapek, who found himself embroiled in Florida\u2019s \u201cDon\u2019t Say Gay\u201d bill debate after fans and employees \u2014 many of whom are part of the LGBTQ+ community \u2014 decried the company\u2019s public silence on the issue.<\/p>\n
