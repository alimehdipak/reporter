He was given an offer that he refused.<\/p>\n

Actor Gianni Russo, who played Carlo Rizzi in \u201cThe Godfather,\u201d said that once filming on the cinema classic wrapped, he had no contact with James Caan\u00a0\u2014\u00a0until the late actor wanted a part in one of his movies.<\/p>\n

When the Little Italy native helped produce\u00a0the 1999 football drama \u201cAny Given Sunday,\u201d he said, he got a call from Caan\u2019s agent while they were casting.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe said, \u2018You know, Jimmy would be great in this movie. He\u2019s a great sports buff,\u2019\u201d Russo, 78, told The Post of the Al Pacino-led flick, in which he had a supporting role.<\/p>\n

\u201cI said, \u2018Do you think I would hire James Caan? I wouldn\u2019t give Jimmy Caan a part in anything.\u2019 He was so rude to me,\u201d the Upper East Sider explained.<\/p>\n

Russo claims that Caan, who played Sonny Corleone, the brother-in-law of Russo\u2019s character, was not friendly to him from the first \u201cGodfather\u201d rehearsal at Patsy\u2019s restaurant on West 119th Street.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe biggest thorn in his side was, \u2018Who\u2019s Gianni Russo?\u2019 This guy, he studied with Stella Adler, you name it. He was never nice to me from day one. But I\u2019m thinking he was in character cause he\u2019s a thespian,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Russo said he quickly discovered that he was wrong, because Caan, who grew up in Sunnyside, Queens, really did have beef with his castmate.<\/p>\n

And it didn\u2019t help that Russo, who was\u00a027 when he was cast, had ties with real-life mobsters such as Frank Costello, Joe Colombo and Carlo Gambino.<\/p>\n

\u201c\u2019Cause he\u2019s hanging around with mob guys, like Carmine Persico, and mob guys all know me already. I came from Mulberry Street, how do you live on Mulberry Street and not know mob guys?\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

He said that Caan \u201cfell in love with mobsters\u201d through his experience on the 1972 set. \u201c\u2019Til the day he died, he thought he was a mobster. He thought he was Sonny Corleone,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Russo also claims that Caan went too far in an infamous \u201cGodfather\u201d fight scene where Sonny pummels Carlo for hitting his sister.<\/p>\n

Although Russo said that a stunt double was used, particularly when Carlo was thrown over a railing, he also was in the scene and was injured by Caan.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat\u2019s me. He\u2019s biting my hand; he\u2019s banging my elbow with that steel garbage pail.
