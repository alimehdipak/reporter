Elon Musk said Thursday that he\u2019s \u201cliterally on the toilet\u201d when he posts on Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m tweeting more or less stream of consciousness,\u201d the Tesla CEO said in an interview amid his push to buy the social media site for $41 billion. \u201cI\u2019m literally on the toilet or something, like, \u2018Oh this is funny\u2019 and then tweet that out.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat\u2019s like most of them, you know, oversharing,\u201d Musk added during the interview with TED Talks chief Chris Anderson. \u201cI try to tweet out things that are interesting or funny and then people seem to like it.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The mogul \u2014 who uses Twitter to post memes and musings, often several times per day \u2014 made the comments just hours after his bid to buy Twitter was made public.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The bid, which Twitter\u2019s board said it is considering, capped a two-week stretch of taunts Musk has launched on the embattled social media giant.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Shortly after disclosing his stake, Musk immediately began teasing possible moves including\u00a0converting Twitter\u2019s San Francisco headquarters into a homeless shelter.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk wants to buy Twitter for $41 billion. SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Gett <\/p>\n

Musk also has suggested adding an edit button for tweets.<\/p>\n

During Thursday\u2019s interview, Musk mused that a way to reduce abuse of the edit button would be to \u201czero out\u201d retweets and favorites when a tweet is edited.\u00a0<\/p>\n

He also said that \u201celiminating the spam and scam bots and the bot armies\u201d would be a top priority if he takes over Twitter.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m literally on the toilet or something and I think, \u2018Haha this is funny\u2019 and I tweet it out,\u201d Musk said. TED <\/p>\n

In the Thursday securities filing announcing his takeover offer, Musk used blunt language, telling the Twitter board: \u201cI am not playing the back-and-forth game.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI have moved straight to the end,\u201d the entrepreneur said. \u201cIt\u2019s a high price and your shareholders will love it.\u201d<\/p>\n

The billionaire Tesla CEO has asked to terminate a 2018 consent decree with the Securities and Exchange Commission that has required his tweets about the electric car maker and its business be vetted by a company lawyer. It isn\u2019t clear whether any of his toilet tweets have been company-related.<\/p>\n

But he also admitted in the TED interview that he has a \u201cPlan B\u201d if Twitter doesn\u2019t accept the offer. He declined to give details on the plan.\u00a0<\/p>\n
