Twitter executives met with employees on Wednesday to address worker apathy as a $44 billion takeover by Elon Musk puts the social-media giant in line for a major overhaul.<\/p>\n

Jay Sullivan, Twitter\u2019s product vice president, convened the meeting under the title \u201cWhy Bother?\u201d as he urged employees to remember the vital service that the platform provides, according to a Bloomberg News report.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhy show up and build stuff for an app that\u2019s about to be in the hands of a new owner\u2026who has said he plans to make serious changes?\u201d Bloomberg reported, summing up staffer\u2019s gripes. \u201cWhy keep earning stock options at a company that\u2019s about to go private?\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk has vowed to revamp the company\u2019s content moderation policies that have resulted in bans of controversial figures, many of them on the political right. Twitter is fearful that employees could resign en masse ahead of Musk\u2019s takeover, according to a regulatory filing that was submitted this week. <\/p>\n

\u201cSullivan told employees that they have a responsibility to each other, and to a product used by hundreds of millions of people, which hosts the world\u2019s most urgent and important public conversations,\u201d Bloomberg reported. \u201cThey\u2019re all in this together, he added, according to the people, who asked not to be named sharing internal discussions.\u201d<\/p>\n

Twitter executives are reportedly concerned about a mass exodus of employees before Musk\u2019s potential takeover.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Musk is likely to clean house and rid the company of its current management structure once he takes over, according to the report. In the meantime, Twitter won\u2019t make any major hires, nor does it plan to make changes to its product ahead of Musk\u2019s takeover. <\/p>\n

Musk, who has secured $7.14 billion in funding from a group of investors which includes Oracle co-founder Larry Ellison and Saudi Prince Alwaleed, has told lenders that he plans to make changes after his acquisition of Twitter is complete later this year. <\/p>\n

The Tesla boss wants to cut jobs and reduce costs while seeking new ways to generate revenue, including potentially charging a subscription fee and monetizing tweets.<\/p>\n

Last week, Twitter CEO Parag Agrawal and other executives fended off questions from upset staffers about Musk\u2019s takeover of the company. <\/p>\n

Leaked details from the meeting revealed that Agrawal appeared \u201ctired\u201d while struggling to placate frustrated employees who face an uncertain future at the San Francisco-baseed social media site.<\/p>\n

An employee in attendance reportedly described Agrawal \u2014 who will reportedly\u00a0get a $42 million payout upon completion of the deal\u00a0\u2014 as appearing \u201ctired and at times annoyed\u201d during the meeting, even as he attempted to assuage workers\u2019 fears, according to Business Insider, which\u00a0obtained leaked audio\u00a0from the all-hands meeting.<\/p>\n

Twitter employees are reportedly upset over Musk\u2019s plans to overhaul the company\u2019s content moderation policies.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Agrawal also expressed a degree of remorse during the call, according to the news site \u2014 telling employees that he wished \u201cwe had done better with lots of things\u201d during talks with Musk.<\/p>\n

\u201cThere are things I disagree with fundamentally,\u201d Agrawal said, without providing specifics.<\/p>\n
