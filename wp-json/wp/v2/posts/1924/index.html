Most Twitter employees don\u2019t think Elon Musk has the company\u2019s best interests in mind, with some calling him an \u201ca\u2014hole\u201d and questioning whether he is \u201cpumping and dumping\u201d the company\u2019s stock, according to a new survey. <\/p>\n

A whopping 68% of Twitter employees disapprove of Musk\u2019s Musk\u2019s tweets about company strategy, which have included musings about potentially eliminating ads and making Twitter a paid service, the poll shows. <\/p>\n

An even greater 78% say he doesn\u2019t have \u201cthe best interests of Twitter and its shareholders\u201d in mind when he makes such posts, according to a survey of verified Twitter employees conducted by corporate message board Blind and shared with The Post on Monday.<\/p>\n

\u201cDon\u2019t want him as CEO,\u201d one employee wrote. \u201cHe has no compassion.\u201d \u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m not convinced he\u2019s in the best place mentally,\u201d another employee said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

A third labeled him a \u201cdangerously insane oligarch.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m not convinced he\u2019s in the best place mentally,\u201d one Twitter employee said.\u00a0SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Gett <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, a slim majority \u2014 52% \u2014 say it\u2019s better that Musk isn\u2019t joining the company\u2019s board, the poll shows. More than two-thirds of the 232 Twitter employees surveyed also said they disapprove of Musk\u2019s tweets about company strategy, which have included musings about potentially eliminating ads and making Twitter a paid service.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cElon\u2019s vision and Twitter don\u2019t align,\u201d one employee wrote.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Others questioned whether Musk\u2019s purchase of a 9.2% stake in Twitter, which contributed to its stock surging about 25% earlier this month, was all part of a \u201cpump and dump scheme\u201d that could see the mogul make billions by selling his shares at a premium.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cHe\u2019s either just pumping and dumping or [has] no plan,\u201d another Twitter employee said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Most Twitter employees surveyed said they disapproved of Musk\u2019s tweets about company strategy. Anadolu Agency via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Despite the fury toward Musk, Twitter employees also appear to be unhappy with the company\u2019s board and Parag Agrawal, who succeeded founder Jack Dorsey as CEO in November.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Agrawal initially said Musk would join Twitter\u2019s board, saying he would bring \u201cgreat value\u201d to the boardroom. Days later, Agrawal said Musk was not joining Twitter\u2019s board after all. Then Musk mounted a bid for Twitter, prompting the company\u2019s board to fight back with a \u201cpoison pill\u201d defensive maneuver on Friday.\u00a0<\/p>\n

A whopping 69% of surveyed employees said the CEO and board have done a \u201cpoor\u201d job handling the confrontations with Musk.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cElon\u2019s vision and Twitter don\u2019t align,\u201d one Twitter employee wrote.\u00a0AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

However, employees were divided over Musk\u2019s path forward. Asked if they have concerns about Musk buying more shares of Twitter, respondents were split 50\/50.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The survey was conducted from April 12 to 17. Blind verifies users\u2019 identities through their corporate email addresses.\u00a0<\/p>\n
