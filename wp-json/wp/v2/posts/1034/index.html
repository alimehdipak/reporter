<\/p>\n

Apple is making it harder for thieves to flog stolen iPhones.<\/p>\n

According to\u00a0MacRumours, the California firm has instructed staff at its stores not to repair iPhones reported as missing or stolen.<\/p>\n

The policy change, distributed through an internal memo, is intended to stop criminals from cashing in on genuine repairs.<\/p>\n

The global GSMA registry allows gadget owners to register devices and list them as lost or stolen.<\/p>\n

Apple\u2019s memo orders staff to check the register using their regular diagnostic tools before accepting an iPhone for repair.<\/p>\n

If the registry notifies them that the device in question has gone missing, they will have to turn down the service.<\/p>\n

The instruction was issued to workers at Apple\u2019s brick-and-mortar stores as well as authorized third-party service providers.<\/p>\n

There\u2019s no suggestion that staff will keep hold of phones or report potential thefts to the police.<\/p>\n

Apple has already placed limits on repairing iPhones in Lost Mode, which you can turn on using iCloud.<\/p>\n

Lost Mode also locks your device so that thieves can\u2019t access your personal information.<\/p>\n

In addition, Apple technicians won\u2019t perform certain services unless you provide proof of purchase.<\/p>\n

The policies are designed to reduce the number of stolen iPhones brought to Apple for repair.<\/p>\n

It follows the news that Apple will let customers fix their own iPhones for the first time starting this year.<\/p>\n

iPhone owners can report their phones as lost or stolen on a global GSMA registry.REUTERS\/Mike Segar <\/p>\n

In a statement in November, the Cupertino tech titan said that it will enable self-repairs by allowing access to Apple genuine parts and tools.<\/p>\n

The new Self Service Repair scheme will begin in the US, with more countries to follow in 2022. Parts will first be available for the iPhone 12 and 13 ranges.<\/p>\n

Apple said the new service would allow those who are comfortable with completing their own repairs the opportunity to do so.<\/p>\n

The first stage of the program will offer repair parts for the iPhone display, battery and camera.<\/p>\n

As part of the scheme, Apple will launch a new online store from which customers will be able to order replacement parts and any tools required.<\/p>\n

Those who return used parts for recycling will be given credit towards their purchase.<\/p>\n

The company said the new store would offer more than 200 individual parts and tools.<\/p>\n
