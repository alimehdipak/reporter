Oasis guitarist Noel Gallagher found himself in hot water over his comments about wheelchair users at this year\u2019s Glastonbury Festival. <\/p>\n

The musician, 55, performed at the popular music extravaganza on June 25, and stayed behind to watch others perform after his set, including British rapper Jamie T. <\/p>\n

Speaking on\u00a0\u201cThe Matt Morgan Podcast,\u201d\u00a0Gallagher said he was initially unable to get up close to the stage where the VIPs usually watch performances. <\/p>\n

Instead, he and his friends watched from the tent\u2019s disabled platform, admitting he didn\u2019t initially realize the purpose of it.<\/p>\n

After flashing his all-access pass, he told security, \u201cThis lot are with me, and we walked onto this platform \u2013 unbeknownst to us it was the disabled platform.\u201d<\/p>\n

The controversial performer went on, \u201cI said, \u2018See that pass mate? It gets me any-f\u2013ing-where.\u201d<\/p>\n

Gallagher\u2019s comments prompted fury from UK charity Scope.Redferns <\/p>\n

Laughing, Gallagher said he would tip a disabled fan out of their wheelchair in order to get his son a better view.<\/p>\n

\u201cI gotta say, those disabled cats have a great view of everything,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

His comments prompted mass fury from UK\u2019s disability charity Scope, which slammed the musician on Twitter.<\/p>\n
