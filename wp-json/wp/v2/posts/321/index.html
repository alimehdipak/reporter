The It girls are better off red.<\/p>\n

Tress trendsetters like catwalk queen Kendall Jenner, 26, and screen gems Zendaya, 25, and Sophie Turner, 26, are turning heads with crimson-colored locks. <\/p>\n

\u201cRed hair is bold, different, and a redhead is typically the only redhead in the room,\u201d Manhattan-based hair colorist Jenna Perry, 35, told The Post of the sizzling swing toward scarlet. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe red hair movement is pushing people to be adventurous,\u201d added the mane mastermind, who\u2019s responsible for red haute highlights rocked by Bella Hadid, 25; Dua Lipa, 26, and Jennifer Lawrence, 31. Perry is also a proud redhead herself. <\/p>\n

\u201cEveryone has \u2018gone blond,’\u201d she continued. \u201cBut it takes someone bold and confident to \u2018spice it up\u2019 and try something different.\u201d<\/p>\n

Natural brunette Kendall Jenner recently debuted red hair. Marc Piasecki <\/p>\n

Perry and LA colorist Matt Rez collaborated to create Jenner\u2019s new shade, which the supermodel debuted on the runway at Prada\u2019s Milan fashion week show Thursday.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The 5-foot-10, natural brunette of \u201cKeeping Up With the Kardashians\u201d fame continued flaunting her fiery locks in Paris Tuesday, while palling around with sister Kourtney Kardashian\u2019s exes Luka Sabbat, 24, and Younes Bendjima, 28, as well as \u201cEuphoria\u201d star Jacob Elordi, 24.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Elordi\u2019s \u201cEuphoria\u201d co-star, Zendaya, has also been living in the red zone. The Emmy Award winner elevated her auburn waves \u2014 a warm tint the actress adopted in December after deciding it was \u201ctime for a change\u201d \u2014 with a glossy dab of honey to illuminate her look this week. <\/p>\n

\u201cGame of Thrones\u201d star Turner got similar online praise when she ditched her icy-blonde strands late last year and went back to the cherry hue she rocked on the hit TV series. <\/p>\n

\u201cSophie Turner with her dark red hair is just something so personal to me,\u201d praised a fan on Twitter.\u00a0<\/p>\n
