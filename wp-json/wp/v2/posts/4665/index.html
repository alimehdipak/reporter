This bag is getting lots of trash talk.<\/p>\n

Balenciaga is raising eyebrows \u2014 and getting trashed \u2014 over its \u201cTrash Pouch,\u201d a baffling design \u201cinspired by a garbage bag\u201d with an even more outrageous price tag: a hefty $1,790.<\/p>\n

The luxury fashion house\u2019s shiny drawstring bag \u2014 which is made out of calfskin leather, emblazoned with a subtle logo and comes in the basic Hefty bag black along with the solid Glad-esque white as well as yellow and blue \u2014 is making its way to stores after debuting in the Fall 2022 ready-to-wear collection in Paris last March.<\/p>\n

\u201cI couldn\u2019t miss an opportunity to make the most expensive trash bag in the world, because who doesn\u2019t love a fashion scandal?\u201d Balenciaga\u2019s creative director Demna Gvasalia told Women\u2019s Wear Daily at the time.<\/p>\n

Now being sold in stores, the \u201cTrash Pouch\u201d has gone viral thanks to an Instagram post that has garnered lots of trash talk, with reactions ranging from puzzled to outraged to fits of laughter.<\/p>\n

\u201cHmm this is rude,\u201d wrote one commenter, while another called out the brand for being full of garbage: \u201cBalenciaga is a meme designer.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trashy but classy? The white \u201cTrash Pouch\u201d has red drawstring ties like the kitchen essential.Balenciaga There\u2019s also a blue colorway for those who prefer to carry a recycling blue-esque design.Balenciaga <\/p>\n

One person questioned whether it was a \u201csocial experiment\u201d and another mused, \u201cThis getting out of hand.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThey really trying to make a joke out of the consumers at this point,\u201d chimed in another.<\/p>\n

Someone else not amused by the trashy design even warned, \u201cPlease dont let theze brandz play you.\u201d<\/p>\n

Waste not, want not: An extravagant yellow option.Balenciaga <\/p>\n

Still, some found the humor in the wacky design. \u201cI got 40 of those under my sink,\u201d one person commented.<\/p>\n

One jokester went for the jugular with garbage bag puns, writing, \u201cIt\u2019s a CINCH some will be GLAD to own this bag, but they will have to pay a HEFTY price.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWhoever buys this needs to be thrown out in it,\u201d said another.<\/p>\n

Several commenters compared it to the 2001 comedy \u201cZoolander\u201d in which Ben Stiller\u2019s character models a collection inspired by the homeless. \u201cThis is \u2018Zoolander\u2019 in real life with the clothing line Derelict. Where is Mugatu at this rate?\u201d<\/p>\n

But one man\u2019s trash is another man\u2019s treasure. At least one person plans to buy the bag, simply commenting, \u201cneed.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

But Demna, who goes by his first name, said the collection is no joke, writing in his show notes from Fashion Week that despair over the war in Ukraine informed the mood for his runway show, which was attended by Kim Kardashian wearing caution tape.<\/p>\n

As WWD pointed out, guests at the Paris show arrived at the venue, which was covered in manufactured snow made out of bits of paper swirling in the air and covering the seats, where Ukrainian flags were prominently placed atop the front row. Models struggled to walk through the makeshift blizzard as the designer\u2019s voice read a Ukrainian poem about love.<\/p>\n
