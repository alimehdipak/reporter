This could be the hottest, most wildly viral sensation since OnlyFans went mainstream \u2014 or the New York Times launched, well, Wordle. <\/p>\n

As the Times named Joseph Kahn its new executive editor Tuesday, succeeding former honcho Dean Baquet, the Old Gray Lady sanctioned a splashy portrait and profile of their nascent leader for New York Magazine \u2014 which captured the promotion in a disarmingly seductive way.<\/p>\n

The resulting, perhaps unintentional, erotica \u2014 featuring enough arched-back, pointed toe and come hither gaze to make pinup pioneers Rita Hayworth and Marilyn Monroe proud \u2014 might just drive foot fetishists everywhere into a frenzy, as well. <\/p>\n

The glowing feature ran alongside a photo spread of Kahn, 57, including a now widely-mocked image \u2014 on social media, of course \u2014 showing the well-dressed but shoeless<\/em> Gray Lady boss in repose, sinuously stretched across a pricey Asian rug on the floor of his regal, mahogany-trimmed Fifth Avenue home office, beside his Chinese-lettered coffee mug and a morning read \u2014 the Times, of course.<\/p>\n

Needless to say, the stocking-feet portrait hyping the \u201cnew, old school\u201d exec editor \u201crelaxing with the paper,\u201d as it\u2019s captioned, was a baffling choice. Readers noted as much online \u2014 while drawing a number of comparisons to \u201cZoolander,\u201d as well as the iconic image of \u201cSeinfeld\u201d character George Costanza posed alluringly in only socks and undies on a fainting couch.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhat was the goal here?\u201d journalist Mairead Elordi asked about the photo in question, snapped by Chris Buck. Newtown shooting survivor and anti-violence advocate JT Lewis added to the sentiment, \u201cI just don\u2019t understand how this probably had to be approved by a slew of people and no one objected.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cThere has not been enough cringe produced in all of human history to properly react to this,\u201d author Jim Hanson responded to the photo.<\/p>\n

\u201cI too \u2018relax with the paper\u2019 while sitting uncomfortably on the floor right next to my desk and chaise,\u201d Noah Blum\u00a0joked.<\/p>\n

\u201cDraw me like one of your French girls,\u201d RealClearInvestigations senior writer Mark Hemingway\u00a0parodied, along with another who quipped in a now-deleted reply, \u201cI bet he has many leather bound books, and his office smells of rich mahogany.\u201d<\/p>\n

Writer Shawn McCreesh aptly conveyed Kahn \u2014 heir to \u201cjournalism\u2019s Iron Throne,\u201d the reporter mused, as well as his father\u2019s co-founding Staples fortune \u2014 as privileged and \u201cfabulously wealthy,\u201d thanks to an elite Boston upbringing that led to a prestigious boarding school education before graduating from Harvard.<\/p>\n

During one passage, McCreesh described the top editor\u2019s enviable lifestyle with his wife Shannon, who formerly worked at the World Bank, and two elite school-educated sons, while insisting they\u2019re not \u201cflashy.\u201d<\/p>\n

Unconvincingly, McCreesh wrote, \u201cBut he does have a taste for the finer things. He is a\u00a0familiar\u00a0face at opening night of the Metropolitan Opera. He is an oenophile with a particular fondness for Burgundy and is part of a wine consortium that includes the billionaire\u00a0Joseph Bae.\u201d<\/p>\n
