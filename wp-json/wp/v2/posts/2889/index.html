How do you pronounce G in Old English?<\/p>\n

When g is used before other vowels it is pronounced the same as Modern English \u201cg\u201d in \u201cgolden\u201d: goda (\u201cgo-da\u201d) (this pronunciation is called velar g). Some editors indicate this voiced pronunciation of \u201cg\u201d by putting a dot above the consonant. h is never silent.<\/p>\n

Did ye used to be pronounced the?<\/p>\n

Ye in \u201cYe Olde Coffee Shoppe\u201d is just an older spelling of the definite article the. The y in this ye was never pronounced (y) but was rather the result of improvisation by early printers.
