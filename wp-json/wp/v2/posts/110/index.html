As Paris Fashion Week wraps up Tuesday, one prominent Russian designer will not be showing off his duds \u2013 and not because he couldn\u2019t travel to the City of Lights.<\/p>\n

Valentin Yudashkin, who designed the Russian army\u2019s uniform in 2008, was kicked out of the show on Sunday, because he has not spoken out against the war in Ukraine.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cOur team wanted to verify the position of Valentin Yudashkin,\u201d Ralph Toledano, president of the group that organizes the show told Women\u2019s Wear Daily. \u201cIf he had doubt about the war in Ukraine, it would have been hard to remove him from the program. That was not the case.\u201d<\/p>\n

Toledano, who heads up the Federation de la Haute Couture et de la Mode, added \u201cIt became clear that he is an affiliate of the regime.\u201d<\/p>\n

The designer, whose work has appeared in Vogue, Elle, Tatler and other major publications and who has dressed celebrities worldwide, did not immediately respond for comment.<\/p>\n

Yudashkin at a Paris Fashion Week runway show in 2014.Richard Bord Yudashkin has not spoken out against the Ukraine war.Sasha Mordovets Yudashkin is Russia\u2019s most famous fashion designer.Richard Bord <\/p>\n

A spokeswoman for Paris Fashion Week told The Post that Yadushkin is the only Russian designer who was banned from participating in the show. <\/p>\n

Born in Moscow where his company is based, Yudashkin\u2019s Facebook page says his brand is sold in 25 countries. His designs have been featured in the Metropolitan Museum of New York.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In 2008, the Russian government tapped him to redesign the military\u2019s uniform.<\/p>\n

It had been one of his most demanding projects, including 85 uniforms across the air force, navy and army, he told Reuters at the time.<\/p>\n

Valentin Yudashkin, to the left of Vladimir Putin with designs in his hands, did the uniforms for the Russian military.VLADIMIR RODIONOV The uniforms designed by Valentin Yudashkin for the Russian military were personally presented to Vladimir Putin at the time.AFP Valentin Yudashkin sketched out the military uniforms that were to be redesigned for Russian forces.AFP <\/p>\n

Yadushkin had to personally present the final designs to Russian president Vladimir Putin at the time. His association with the Russian army contributed to Toledano\u2019s decision, the executive told WWD.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf they live abroad then they have no excuse not to speak out,\u201d Gary Wassner, chief executive of Hilldun Corp. a lender to the fashion industry. \u201cFor those living in Russia currently, it\u2019s not so easy. Though it\u2019s hard for them to speak out, they must in order to prevent this genocide.\u201d<\/p>\n
