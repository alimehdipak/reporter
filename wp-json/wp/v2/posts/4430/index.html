Johnny Depp has donated nearly $800,000 to four children\u2019s hospitals after selling NFTs of his artwork.<\/p>\n

The 59-year-old \u201cPirates of the Caribbean\u201d star sold non-fungible tokens \u2013 unique digital assets traded through blockchain technology \u2013 of his art collection \u201cNever Fear Truth\u201d and donated the proceedings to the Children\u2019s Hospital of Los Angeles, Perth Children\u2019s Hospital Foundation, the Footprint Coalition and the Great Ormond Street Hospital Children\u2019s Charity, according to a Tuesday tweet from Depp\u2019s Never Fear Truth account.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are grateful and very thankful to the Never Fear Truth community for this tremendous accomplishment \u2013 making this project one of the most philanthropic NFT sales to date \u2013 and we are excited to build on this success and making an even greater impact going forward,\u201d another tweet reads.<\/p>\n

The generous donations come after Depp endured oftentimes embarrassing testimony in his defamation case against ex-wife Amber Heard \u2014 with jurors seeing photos of the star passed out during drinking and drug binges and hearing about his and Heard\u2019s explosive fights, including when he allegedly lost his fingertip on a vodka bottle.<\/p>\n
