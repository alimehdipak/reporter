<\/p>\n

Uber is adding planes, trains, hotels, buses and rental cars to its app in the UK as part of its quest to become a \u201cone-stop shop\u201d for travel, the company said on Wednesday. <\/p>\n

The additions to Uber\u2019s UK app will serve as a pilot program before they\u2019re potentially rolled out around the world. <\/p>\n

The company said that train and bus services are will launch over the summer, while flights will be added later this year and hotels at an unspecified date. <\/p>\n

Uber, which is pushing to sustain profitability, will sell the tickets through partnerships with other vendors.<\/p>\n

Uber\u2019s regional manger for the UK and northern and eastern Europe Jamie Heywood said in a statement that the company wants to become \u201ca one-stop shop for all your travel needs.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cWe plan to incorporate flights, and in the future hotels, by integrating leading partners into the Uber app to create a seamless door-to-door travel experience,\u201d Heywood said. <\/p>\n

Uber wants to give \u201cseamless door-to-door experience\u201d to travel customers.\u00a0Bloomberg via Getty Images UberChopper helicopter is seen in Gdynia, Poland in 2017.NurPhoto via Getty Images <\/p>\n

In another sign that Uber is looking beyond its core ride-share business model, the company said in March that it plans to start listing all New York City taxis through its app.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The move came after years of acrimony between Uber and Big Apple cabbies, who have accused the Uber and Lyft of decimating the yellow cab industry.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Lyft, meanwhile, told The Post that it\u2019s not looking to add cabs to its app. But in its own apparent effort to become a \u201cone-stop shop,\u201d Lyft has added Citibikes and rental cars to its app in New York City.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Uber\u2019s Dara Khosrowshahi first said in 2018 that the company is looking to become a \u201csuper app,\u201d but the pandemic snarled its plans.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s fair to say that Covid made it a little bit hard for us to progress as quickly as we would like,\u201d Heywood told the Financial Times, which first reported news of the UK expansion. \u201cWith Covid behind us, with this big push into new modes of transport, we want to signal that this is a very important growth lever for us over the coming years.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n
