May the Force be with you! Or more specifically, with your wallet \u2014 because the much-anticipated \u201cLego Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga\u201d video game was just released on Tuesday.<\/p>\n

At first, we thought, \u201cWell, why can\u2019t you play Legos?\u201d <\/em>and \u201cWell, why can\u2019t you just watch the movie?<\/em>\u201d But, with this new, stunning debut, you can feel like you have it all in one bundle.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s already out of stock at a few retailers \u2014 thanks to fans flooding Twitter with rave reviews \u2014 but the title is available at Amazon, as well as Best Buy, GameStop, Target and Xbox.<\/p>\n

The golden question is: What\u2019s the hype? Notably, the ability to \u201cfly, fly away\u201d across galactic graphics at many of the saga\u2019s legendary locations is reportedly unmatched.<\/p>\n

However, although fans are instantly loving the new release, the \u201cStar Wars\u201d franchise\u2019s original Luke Skywalker (Mark Hamill) might not be so thrilled to discover it includes a years-old meme he has previously denounced. <\/p>\n

The infamous meme moment documents Obi-Wan Kenobi handing Luke his father\u2019s lightsaber for the first time. Luke proceeds to aim it at his face \u2014 as though he is staring down the barrel of a gun. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis makes me cringe every time I see it,\u201d Hamill, 70, tweeted last March. \u201cI don\u2019t remember doing this in the movie. I think it\u2019s just an on-set production still, otherwise Obi-Wan wouldn\u2019t look so remarkably unconcerned.\u201d<\/p>\n
