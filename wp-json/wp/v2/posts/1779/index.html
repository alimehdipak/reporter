Elon Musk\u2019s potential takeover of Twitter has Donald Trump supporters once again salivating over the potential return of the former president \u2014 but Trump insists he has no interest in resuming tweeting.<\/p>\n

Trump told Americano Media on Wednesday: \u201cI probably wouldn\u2019t have any interest [in returning to Twitter if Musk bought the company].\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter\u2019s become very boring. They\u2019ve gotten rid of a lot of good voices on Twitter, a lot of their conservative voices.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trump added: \u201cIt used to be a war on Twitter, but it was a very interesting war\u2026Mentally, we had some pretty good fights. We would go after the progressives, who I call the un-progressives.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019d be fighting back and forth and it was great stuff,\u201d Trump said, adding that \u201cfriends of mine\u201d have recently griped that \u201cit\u2019s not the same.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trump was banned from Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, and all other large social media platforms following the Jan. 6, 2021, rioting at the US Capitol. <\/p>\n

Trump has since launched a social media platform of his own, Truth Social, but it has been beset by technical glitches and departures of key executives.<\/p>\n

Last week, a Twitter spokesperson told DailyMail.com that there were no plans to reinstate Trump\u2019s account.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter is committed to impartiality in the development and enforcement of its policies and rules,\u201d the company said. \u201cOur policy decisions are not determined by the Board or shareholders, and we have no plans to reverse any policy decisions.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u2018As always our Board plays an important advisory and feedback role across the entirety of our service,\u201d the company added. \u201cOur day to day operations and decisions are made by Twitter management and employees.\u201d<\/p>\n
