<\/p>\n

Virtual police may need to patrol the metaverse to keep users safe from abuse, according to a human trafficking expert.<\/p>\n

International human trafficking expert Matthew Friedman raised his concerns about criminals exploiting virtual reality in a recent article for the\u00a0South China Morning Post.<\/p>\n

Friedman wrote: \u201cSafeguards must be put in place to prevent vulnerable individuals being harassed, or groomed into exploitative situations.<\/p>\n

\u201cUsers need to be educated about the potential for human trafficking.<\/p>\n

\u201cFor these worlds to be safe, online police might be required to patrol the environments and detect human-rights violations, before confronting the abusers in real life.\u201d<\/p>\n

Friedman is currently a chief executive officer of The Mekong Club, which fights human trafficking in Asia.<\/p>\n

The Hong Kong based-based organization is now looking into how it could help to fight human traffickers in the virtual world of the metaverse.<\/p>\n

Friedman claims its essential that organizations like his own are able to aid companies like Meta and Microsoft as they establish virtual worlds.<\/p>\n

The Mekong Club has already used virtual reality to allow people to have an eye-opening view into the crimes of sex trafficking and forced labor without being put in any physical danger.<\/p>\n

Although you enter\u00a0the metaverse\u00a0as a virtual avatar, experts are concerned that avatars could be groomed.<\/p>\n

This grooming could lead to devastating real-life consequences for someone outside of the virtual world.<\/p>\n

Concerns about sexual assault inside the metaverse have already been made.<\/p>\n

Earlier this year, a woman recounted a\u00a0harrowing experience\u00a0in VR that left her feeling violated and unsafe.<\/p>\n

In a\u00a0Medium blog post, Nina Patel, 43, describes how she was \u201cverbally and sexually harassed\u201d in Facebook\u2019s Metaverse.<\/p>\n

Upon entering the Horizon Venues Metaverse experience, Patel says she was accosted \u201cwithin 60 seconds\u201d of joining the lobby area.<\/p>\n

Her avatar \u2014which was made to mirror her real-life appearance \u2014 was quickly surrounded by three to four male avatars, with male voices.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was verbally and sexually harassed,\u201d she recalls, adding that their inappropriate behavior took a dark turn.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201c[They] virtually gang-raped my avatar and took photos\u00a0\u2014 as I tried to get away they yelled \u2014 \u2018don\u2019t pretend you didn\u2019t love it\u2019 and \u2018go rub yourself off to the photo\u2019.\u201d<\/p>\n

She describes the ordeal as \u201csurreal\u201d and a \u201cnightmare\u201d.<\/p>\n

Nina Patel isn\u2019t the only one who\u2019s experienced sexual harassment and inappropriate behavior in the game.<\/p>\n

The\u00a0reviews for Horizon Venues\u00a0paint a similarly sordid picture, and it currently has 2.5\/5 stars as a result.<\/p>\n
