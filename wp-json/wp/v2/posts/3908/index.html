Gullible TikTok users beware: You may be at risk of getting #Krissed.<\/p>\n

A new trend is spreading across the app in which users post videos, usually accompanied with false information about celebrities, that end with a video of Kris Jenner dancing to a sped-up version of \u201cLady Marmalade\u201d from \u201cMoulin Rouge.\u201d<\/p>\n

The now-viral clip of Jenner in a sparkly green dress and black bow tie originates from an amateur-made music video on the set of the Kardashian-Jenners\u2019 2011 family Christmas card photo shoot. Uploaded to Kendall and Kylie Jenner\u2019s former YouTube channel, the vid now has more than 4.6 million views.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, the hashtag #Krissed currently has over 229 million views on TikTok. At the end of the videos, users often encourage their followers to prank their own friends and family.<\/p>\n

One example, which has racked up over 7.7 million views, includes a Photoshopped photo seemingly from Kendall\u2019s Instagram grid, claiming the supermodel announced she is pregnant. Before fans can even gather their thoughts, the 818 Tequila owner\u2019s momager pops up. <\/p>\n

\u201cI got krissed again how do I keep falling for it,\u201d one bamboozled TikTok user commented. <\/p>\n

\u201cMy therapist will be hearing about this,\u201d another quipped.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

JoJo Siwa fans got \u201cKrissed\u201d on Wednesday when the pop star falsely announced she would be temporarily stepping away from public life. @itsjojosiwa <\/p>\n

JoJo Siwa used the bait-and-switch tactic with her 41.7 million TikTok followers. On Wednesday, she falsely announced that she would be taking a break from public life before the dancing Jenner took over the screen. <\/p>\n

\u201cNo way did I just get Krissed by Jojo Siwa,\u201d one user commented.<\/p>\n

\u201cI WAS TYPING OUT A PARAGRAPH ON HOW YOU DESERVE A NICE BREAK AND AS SOON AS I SAW KRIS I WAS LIKE \ud83e\udd2c,\u201d another admitted. <\/p>\n

The trend is a bait-and-switch technique that hooks users in with outlandish false information, then tells them they got trolled with a video of Kris Jenner singing and dancing. @shesjustsadig <\/p>\n

Some are deeming the new trend this generation\u2019s version of \u201cRickrolling.\u201d If unfamiliar, the phrase refers to people who are tricked into clicking on a hyperlink that leads to the music video for Rick Astley\u2019s 1987 hit\u00a0\u201cNever Gonna Give You Up.\u201d The clip has since reached more than\u00a01 billion views on YouTube.<\/p>\n
