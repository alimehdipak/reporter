ANAHEIM, Calif. — The Toronto Blue Jays scratched slugger Vladimir Guerrero Jr. from their starting lineup for Sunday’s 11-10 win over the Los Angeles Angels.<\/p>\n

Guerrero, who has a sore left wrist, was in the Blue Jays’ initial lineup as the designated hitter before being removed about 2\u00bd hours before first pitch. He made an eighth-inning appearance as a pinch-hitter and was intentionally walked.<\/p>\n

Thanks to that appearance, the slugger has missed only one game this season and only two of Toronto’s past 269 contests dating to 2020.<\/p>\n

Guerrero went 2-for-12 in the first three games of the series in Anaheim, where he spent several summers growing up while his father played for the Angels. Guerrero homered off the left-field pole at Angel Stadium against Shohei Ohtani on Thursday night.<\/p>\n

Guerrero’s homer off Ohtani was his second in two games after a power drought that lasted through most of May for the slugger, who finished last season tied for the most homers in the majors.<\/p>\n

Guerrero is batting .256 this season with nine homers and 24 RBIs.<\/p>\n
