Brace for it.<\/p>\n

Welded-on bracelets and other forms of permanent jewelry are being touted as a growing, glamorous alternative to tattoos and piercings \u2014 not to mention a really cute way to commemorate friendship.<\/p>\n

Style influencer Jaclyn Forbes recently showed viewers how she and her bestie had matching bracelets created by Canadian jewelry designer Leah Alexander. <\/p>\n

\u201cI got a bracelet permanently welded on my wrist, again,\u201d Forbes narrates over a TikTok shared Monday. \u201cNow there\u2019s two. Let me show you the process because I know it sounds kind of crazy.\u201d<\/p>\n

The trend is apparently growing among well-followed fashionistas on TikTok \u2014 among them Forbes, Victoria Jameson and Vienna Skye \u2014 where the hashtags #permanentjewelry and #permanentbracelet boast a combined total of 160 million views.<\/p>\n

Style influencer Jaclyn Forbes gets fitted for a bracelet.Courtesy of @jaclynforbes <\/p>\n

The lineup of chains available at Sparks Studio\u2019s Toronto location \u2014 along with several other independent designers offering a similar service \u2014 are usually dainty yet casual enough to wear daily and great for stacking, as Forbes demonstrates on her second trip to get bracelets welded on her wrist.<\/p>\n

The video shows Forbes and a friend choosing chains and then having them fitted for the perfect length. <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m pretty particular about the fit,\u201d added Forbes, whose videos on her permanent pieces have drawn millions of views. A jewelry welder then peers through \u201cmagnifying goggles\u201d as they use a handled \u201cmicrolaser which zaps [each end] together.\u201d<\/p>\n

The spools of gold chains at Mettle Gals in Dallas, Texas.Courtesy of @victoria <\/p>\n

\u201cIt is not painful,\u201d she reassures skeptical viewers. \u201cYou don\u2019t feel it. It doesn\u2019t zap your actual skin.\u201d <\/p>\n

However, her caption does suggest that the trend has been polarizing among followers: \u201cYou\u2019ll either love it or hate it: A PERMANENT BRACELET!!?\u201d <\/p>\n

Folks in the comments certainly came with the tough questions, with one asking, \u201cHow do you take them off?\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cYou don\u2019t,\u201d concluded one viewer, while another suggested \u201cwire cutters.\u201d<\/p>\n

Others pointed out the many scenarios in which one might find themselves required to break their bijou.<\/p>\n

The chain is fastened with a handheld tool that \u201czaps\u201d the ends of the bracelet together.Courtesy of @jaclynforbes <\/p>\n

\u201cWait what do u do if u do sports,\u201d asked one curious commenter.<\/p>\n

Others had medical concerns, asking, \u201cWhat happens if you get surgery though\u201d and \u201cWhat happens if you ever have to have an MRI or an x-ray where you can\u2019t have any metal on you.\u201d <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, someone reportedly preparing for a career in health care noted: \u201cI\u2019m in medical school and you can\u2019t have any bracelets on in the OR :(\u201c<\/p>\n
