Here comes the bride, all dressed in \u2014 black?<\/p>\n

Today\u2019s brides are no longer interested in wearing white for their big day.<\/p>\n

While the conventional wedding attire features elegant long, white dresses with veils and trains, that tradition is no longer in vogue.<\/p>\n

A new trend that has blossomed for the modern bride includes donning darker hues for dresses, such as black.<\/p>\n

Google Trends had high searches for \u201cblack wedding dress\u201d over the past 12 months, CNN reported. In fall 2021 specifically, \u201cblack wedding dress\u201d was searched more times than \u201cwhite wedding dress\u201d on Google.<\/p>\n

Wedding gown and formalwear mega-chain David\u2019s Bridal is currently featuring a selection of custom-order versions of their popular dress styles in black.<\/p>\n

Laura McKeever, senior manager of brand PR at David\u2019s Bridal, told CNN that brides are leaning toward going dark on their most joyous day as a way to buck tradition.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is by far our favorite trend and we are loving it. Black gowns are chic and dramatic,\u201d\u00a0she told CNN. \u201cThe concept of what\u2019s considered traditional has evolved.\u201d<\/p>\n

David\u2019s Bridal also saw an increase in demand for black wedding dresses.David\u2019s Bridal <\/p>\n

McKeever noted that the pandemic has shifted lifestyles and customs, explaining that \u201cCouples wanting to throw traditional wedding rules out the window.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cBrides who\u2019ve had to postpone their weddings because of the pandemic now want their special day to be unique,\u201d she added.<\/p>\n

David\u2019s Bridal increased its selection of differently colored dresses by 30% this year, up from 10% in 2021.Enaura Bridal <\/p>\n

\u201cThey want to wear what they want on their big day. They are picking unique venues, they\u2019re wearing sneakers and having food trucks at their wedding,\u201d McKeever stated. She also revealed that David\u2019s Bridal increased its selection of differently colored dresses by 30% this year, up from 10% in 2021.<\/p>\n

Other colors that are added to the paint palette include blue, champagne, purple and red.<\/p>\n

The tradition of rocking white frocks to walk to the altar stretches back to the era of Queen Victoria. She famously wore the shade resembling purity when she married Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg, who was also her first cousin, in 1840. She popularized the white color style during the time and many other brides wanted to follow suit in an effort to mimic their monarch\u2019s tastes.<\/p>\n

Queen Victoria and her husband Prince Albert. Getty Images <\/p>\n

Victoria was only 20 years old when she tied the knot and opted to choose a dress that would reflect her own innocence and chastity and show she was fit to rule the United Kingdom.<\/p>\n

Celebrities are also reviving the black-dress style, setting trends for the rest of the public to chase for the \u2018gram.<\/p>\n
