You won\u2019t get celebrated post-slap \u00e0 la <\/em>the Oscars. <\/p>\n

Amid the fallout from last month\u2019s Oscars slap, producers for the upcoming Tony Awards are warning potential copycats to refrain from following suit \u2014 or they\u2019ll face severe consequences. Event organizers included the anti-slap advisory in a memo sent out to potential ticket-holders, Deadline reported.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe Tony Awards has a strict no violence policy,\u201d read the PSA. \u201cIn the event of an incident, the perpetrator will be removed from the event immediately.\u201d<\/p>\n

Along with discouraging slap-happy Broadway buffs, the letter also reminded potential ticket-buyers of other restrictions, including wearing black tie and bringing proof of COVID vaccination. (And don\u2019t even think of scalping your tickets, as they are non-transferable.)<\/p>\n

Another view of Will Smith\u2019s infamous slap at the 2022 Oscars.REUTERS Since the incident, Smith has apologized to Rock and resigned from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences. AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The warning comes nearly a month after the infamous Smackademy Awards incident, in which Will Smith slapped Oscars presenter Chris Rock after the 57-year-old comic made a crack about his bald-headed wife, Jada Pinkett Smith, who has alopecia.<\/p>\n

Despite the attack, the 54-year-old \u201cHancock\u201d actor was permitted to remain at the event, and went on to nab the Best Actor Academy Award for his role in \u201cKing Richard.\u201d <\/p>\n

Since the incident, Smith has\u00a0apologized to Rock\u00a0and\u00a0resigned from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences. The Academy later announced that the \u201cKing Richard\u201d star is\u00a0banned from all of the organization\u2019s events\u00a0for 10 years.<\/p>\n

The 2022 Tony Awards will be held Sunday, June 12, at 8 p.m. at New York\u2019s Radio City Music Hall. <\/p>\n
