    If you run an online or retail store, you need a small business credit card processor that will let you accept credit card payments as well as debit cards and other payment types. Small businesses sometimes have trouble choosing between two of the biggest names in the industry: Stripe and Square.<\/p>\n

    Stripe and Square both offer a single merchant account and allow you to accept payments through online point-of-sale (POS) stations or portals. Both services come with unique benefits and have different advantages and downsides.<\/p>\n

    With that in mind, let’s take a closer look at Stripe and Square. This comparison page should help you determine which payment processor best fits your business needs.\u00a0<\/p>\n

    Stripe vs. Square side-by-side comparison<\/h2>\n

    Stripe is a relatively new payment processing company compared to most of its competitors. It offers reasonably low rates and a variety of mobile-focused and online-focused tools, including the following:\u00a0<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Small POS devices<\/li>\n
    • Easy integration with third-party apps\u00a0<\/li>\n
    • Developer-friendly API kits<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      In contrast, Square focuses on providing hardware POS systems for brick-and-mortar businesses or larger online enterprises. Many business owners find Square a little easier to jump into and get working immediately, but it lacks Stripe’s payment versatility.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n

      Similarities between Stripe and Square<\/h3>\n
        \n
      • Processing costs: Stripe and Square have the same online payment processing cost \u2013 2.9% plus 30 cents per transaction.<\/li>\n
      • Hardware: Both offer POS hardware like card readers or POS terminals and online payment processing.<\/li>\n
      • Integration: Both can integrate with third-party tools.<\/li>\n
      • Customer support: Both offer multiple customer support channels.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        Differences between Stripe and Square<\/h3>\n
          \n
        • User focus: Stripe is more developer-friendly, while Square is better for brick-and-mortar business owners.<\/li>\n
        • Ease of use: Square is easier to use and jump into, whereas Stripe requires a bit more setup time if you want to take full advantage of its capabilities.<\/li>\n
        • Suitability: Square is better for POS hardware needs and site building, whereas Stripe is more suitable if you have an online business or primarily accept payments online.<\/li>\n
        • Software: Stripe is much better in terms of third-party integration but doesn’t offer its own POS software as Square does.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          Did you know? Square is one of the best POS systems for retail stores and restaurants. Its retail POS plans include an online store so that you can also sell your products online.<\/p>\n

          Stripe: Who do we recommend it for?<\/h2>\n

          Stripe is a versatile and easy-to-use online payment service that gives your customers a speedy and convenient checkout experience. It offers a dedicated POS terminal and a handheld terminal for multiple credit card readers, with integrated readers available as an add-on.<\/p>\n

          But Stripe’s real focus is the online space. It’s a perfect solution for businesses that conduct international e-commerce or that are mostly or entirely online. Its online payment tools make it great for accepting various currencies from all around the world.<\/p>\n

          Stripe may also be a better choice if you have an in-house developer who can make the payment processor work for you. The company provides extensive documentation and tutorials to speed things along.<\/p>\n

          These are a few of Stripe’s primary benefits:\u00a0<\/p>\n

            \n
          • Integration:\u00a0Stripe offers excellent integrations with hundreds of third-party apps.<\/li>\n
          • Design:\u00a0It’s designed to be an online payment system.<\/li>\n
          • Payment methods:\u00a0Stripe accepts all kinds of payment methods as well as multiple currencies.<\/li>\n
          • Convenience for developers:\u00a0Stripe is great for businesses with in-house web developers.\u00a0<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

            Tip: Read our full review of Stripe to learn more about the payment processor and its service offerings.<\/p>\n

            Square: Who do we recommend it for?<\/h2>\n

            Square is another big name in POS and payment solutions for businesses, and for a good reason: It’s been a dominant market force since 2009. It’s one of the best choices for small businesses thanks to its excellent POS tools and hardware solutions.\u00a0<\/p>\n

            Square is also easy for employees to use, featuring quality out-of-the-box business management and employee management tools. No developer experience is necessary to use Square, and it can help you manage your company and your customer base relatively quickly.<\/p>\n

            However, Square isn’t quite as versatile in terms of accepted currencies. On the plus side, Square is a little cheaper if you need a POS hardware terminal, since you get your first one for free when you sign up.<\/p>\n

            • Ease of use: Square is easy to get up and running.<\/li>\n
            • Versatility:\u00a0Square is designed for brick-and-mortar payment processing, but it offers online payment options as well.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                    Pricing<\/h3>\n
                      \n
                    • Stripe: Stripe has a straightforward fee and pricing scheme. If you want to use Stripe’s payment platform for your current website, it’ll cost you a fee of 2.9% plus 30 cents for each credit card charge.\u00a0<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                      In-person credit card processing costs 2.7% plus 5 cents per credit card charge. International cards are more expensive, with another 1% processing fee for either type of credit card payment above.\u00a0<\/p>\n

                      Stripe doesn’t charge any monthly fees, and there aren’t extra charges if you accept mobile payment options like Google Pay or Apple Pay. But it does charge a $15 disputed payment fee if you ever have an issue with chargebacks.<\/p>\n

                      The first Stripe card reader you buy is a $59 flat fee. Stripe does offer a slightly cheaper POS terminal than Square, at $249.<\/p>\n

                        \n
                      • Square: If you only use Square to accept online payments, the company’s pricing is straightforward. It costs 2.9% plus 30 cents per transaction, which is the same fee that Stripe charges.\u00a0<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                        Square is a little cheaper if you use it for in-person credit card payments. That fee is just 2.6% plus 10 cents per transaction. However, if you want to build an e-commerce store using Square, you need a paid plan and will likely incur a monthly fee.<\/p>\n

                        Square gives each customer their first card reader for free. An additional card reader costs $10. POS terminals start at $299, which is a little more expensive than what Stripe charges.<\/p>\n

                          \n
                        • Winner: Square, thanks to its low in-person fees.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
