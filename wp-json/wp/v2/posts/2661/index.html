Facebook parent Meta is instituting a hiring freeze for some higher-level positions \u2014 a move that comes as Mark Zuckerberg\u2019s embattled social media giant warns of slowing revenue growth.<\/p>\n

Meta will slow down or entirely pause hiring for most of its mid- or senior-level positions, according to the company. The latest change occurred weeks after the company paused hiring for lower-level engineer positions.<\/p>\n

The social media giant \u2014 which is attempting to reinvent itself as a metaverse company after a series of data privacy and user safety scandals \u2014 said it has no current plans to conduct layoffs.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe regularly re-evaluate our talent pipeline according to our business needs and in light of the expense guidance given for this earnings period, we are slowing its growth accordingly,\u201d a Meta spokesperson told The Post. \u201cHowever, we will continue to grow our workforce to ensure we focus on long term impact.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meta representatives confirmed the hiring pause weeks after executives gave a revenue forecast for its current quarter that fell short of Wall Street\u2019s expectations. If the forecast holds, it would put Meta in danger of posting its first quarterly revenue decline on record when it next reports results.<\/p>\n

Meta has said the Ukraine-Russia war is partially to blame for its slowing sales.Getty Images for SXSW <\/p>\n

In the first quarter, Meta\u2019s revenue grew just 7% to $27.9 billion \u2014 its slowest rate of expansion since the company went public.<\/p>\n

The freeze was enacted after a heavy recruiting push at Meta, the company said. The Facebook parent hired more new workers in its first quarter than it did in the entirety of 2021.<\/p>\n

Meta\u2019s employee headcount grew 28% to 77,800 employees in the first quarter, according to the company\u2019s earnings release.<\/p>\n

Zuckerberg and Meta CFO Dave Wehner discussed the company\u2019s revenue challenges during their call with analysts in late April \u2014 revealing \u201cthe loss of revenue in Russia\u201d during the Ukraine war was cutting into its sales.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019ve been blocked in Russia and we decided to stop accepting ads from Russian advertisers globally,\u201d Zuckerberg said. \u201cAnd we\u2019ve also seen effects on business globally following the start of the war.\u201d<\/p>\n

Meta\u2019s hiring freeze applies to higher-level positions.NurPhoto via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The company also highlighted an obstacle in the form of changes to Apple\u2019s privacy policy \u2014 which limited ad-tracking capabilities \u2014 that are expected to cost Meta about $10 billion this year.<\/p>\n

Meta has also faced some internal pushback from employees over its increased focus on the metaverse, with some workers reportedly confused about their responsibilities. And others have grumbled online that the company\u2019s poor performance is hurting their stock options.<\/p>\n

Zuckerberg noted \u201cvolatility\u201d in Meta\u2019s workforce retainment efforts \u2014 but downplayed concerns about an employee exodus.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t think that this sort of volatility that companies face is always that unhealthy for making sure that you have the right people at companies,\u201d Zuckerberg said.<\/p>\n
