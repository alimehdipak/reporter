<\/p>\n

A practicing attorney from Kentucky, who attended the Johnny Depp v. Amber Heard defamation trial, is auctioning off the wristbands that gave him a front-row seat to the action.<\/p>\n

Larry Forman, who was not involved professionally in the case, told Insider that he attended the trial in person between May 16 and May 22 because he was \u201cenamored with the case.\u201d <\/p>\n

A wristband currently posted on eBay listed a starting bid of $5,000 as of Friday afternoon.<\/p>\n

A large portion of his fascination was focused on the jury\u2019s reactions to the messy couple\u2019s claims. Forman took 60 pages of notes on jurors\u2019 facial expressions \u2014 a \u201cpiece of history\u201d that he sold on eBay on Wednesday for $14,969. <\/p>\n

\u201cYou have to see how they react to defense witnesses. Then you have to see how they react to the plaintiff\u2019s witnesses,\u201d he said. \u201cThen you have to see how they react during breaks. Who do they look at? Who do they smile at?\u201d he added. <\/p>\n

\u201cWatching the jury, I was absolutely convinced that some of them, not all of them \u2014 No. 2 and No. 6 and No. 5 were the hardest ones to read \u2014 were going to be on Johnny\u2019s side,\u201d Forman said. \u201cAfter seeing their reactions, I was firmly convinced that Johnny was going to win.\u201d<\/p>\n

Larry Forman\u2019s 60 pages of jury notes sold on eBay for nearly $15,000.eBay Bidding for Larry Forman\u2019s courtroom wristband is still active. eBay <\/p>\n

The superfan said he plans to donate all the proceeds from both the journal and the wristbands to the Children\u2019s Hospital Los Angeles. <\/p>\n

The Post reached out to CHLA for comment. <\/p>\n

The hospital was mentioned several times throughout the trial, as Heard previously said she would donate $3.5 million of her divorce settlement from Depp to the establishment, However, during the trial, a hospital representative said that she has only donated $250,000 of the promised sum. <\/p>\n

Amber Heard has not yet paid her full promised donation of $3.5 million to Children\u2019s Hospital Los Angeles.AP <\/p>\n

The remaining amount may not be expected anytime soon, as Heard is reportedly \u201cbroke\u201d from the hefty legal fees associated with the defamation trial.<\/p>\n

In an apparent swipe at her, the eBay posting for the wristband includes the message: \u201cWhile others have failed, lied and misled the world about their philanthropy. \u2026\u00a0Now is the time for the public to step up and donate where others have faltered in their pledges. Now is the time to become role models in altruism.\u201d<\/p>\n

Wristbands like Forman\u2019s were a hot commodity for fans waiting outside the Fairfax County Court during the trial in Virginia. Every morning, courthouse officials gave them to only the first 100 people in line. Neama Rahmani, president of West Coast Trial Lawyers,\u00a0told Insider that eager bystanders started lining up at midnight every night to get one. <\/p>\n
