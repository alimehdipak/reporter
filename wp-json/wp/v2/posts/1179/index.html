Apple\u2019s return-to-office policy is far stricter than other big tech companies \u2014 and some employees say they plan to quit in protest.\u00a0<\/p>\n

While Meta, Google and Amazon are letting at least some employees work remotely forever, Apple CEO Tim Cook is ordering all corporate employees back into the office at least one day per week beginning on April 11. The mandate ratchets up to two days per week on May 2 and three days per week on May 23.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t give a single f\u2014k about ever coming back to work here,\u201d one Apple employee ranted on corporate message board Blind this week, saying they planned to resign the day they come back to the office. <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m going to go in to say hello and meet everyone since I haven\u2019t since I started and then sending in my resignation when I get home,\u201d the employee wrote. \u201cI already know I won\u2019t be able to deal with the commute and sitting around for 8 hours.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Another Apple employee responded with a laughing emoji and wrote: \u201cI\u2019m gonna do the same.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cHell YEAH my man let\u2019s do this!\u201d a third employee chimed in. \u201cF\u2013k RTO.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t give a single f\u2013k about ever coming back to work here,\u201d one Apple employee raged about Tim Cook\u2019s return-to-office push. Evan Agostini\/Invision\/AP Apple employees will be required to work three days per week at the company\u2019s offices, including its California headquarters (above). REUTERS <\/p>\n

Apple employees will be required to go in on Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays, while Wednesdays and Fridays will be \u201cflexible.\u201d Employees will also be allowed to work fully remotely for up to four weeks per year.\u00a0<\/p>\n

By comparison, Google is also requiring many employees to come in three days per week starting this coming Monday \u2014 but unlike Apple, Google is giving some employees the option of switching offices or working fully remotely forever. <\/p>\n

The offer includes the caveat that employees could take pay cuts if they leave the San Francisco Bay Area or New York City for less expensive parts of the country. <\/p>\n

Meta is also letting employees apply to work from anywhere, although \u201cMetamates\u201d who move may also face pay cuts. And Amazon originally planned to push employees back into the office three days per week but later reversed that policy, leaving the decision up to individual teams.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Several Apple employees said they are jealous of other tech firms\u2019 remote work policies and are looking to quit.Apple <\/p>\n

Several Apple employees said they\u2019re jealous of other tech firms\u2019 remote work policies and are looking to quit. <\/p>\n

\u201cTotally bummed and looking into full remote jobs now,\u201d one Apple employee wrote. <\/p>\n

Another said they were in the final stages of interviewing at several rival tech firms due to Apple\u2019s return-to-office policy.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cTotally bummed and looking into full remote jobs now,\u201d one Apple employee wrote.REUTERS <\/p>\n

In a March memo announcing Apple\u2019s return-to-office policy, Cook acknowledged that some employees might not want to come back. \u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cFor many of you, I know that returning to the office represents a long-awaited milestone and a positive sign that we can engage more fully with the colleagues who play such an important role in our lives,\u201d Cook said. \u201cFor others, it may also be an unsettling change.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Or as another Apple employee put it on Blind: \u201cF\u2013k apple RTO.\u201d<\/p>\n
