The heroes they deserve.<\/p>\n

Marvel warmed comic book fans\u2019 hearts after publicly denouncing \u201cALL\u201d anti-LGBTQ laws following parent company Disney\u2019s bungled response to Florida\u2019s \u201cDon\u2019t Say Gay\u201d bill. The movie studio issued their vote of support in a Twitter statement Wednesday.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe strongly denounce any and ALL legislation that infringes on the basic human rights of the LGBTQIA+ community,\u201d they wrote. \u201cMarvel Studios stands for hope, inclusivity and strength; and we proudly stand with the community.\u201d<\/p>\n

Marvel continued, \u201cToday, we pledge to continue our strong commitment as allies who promote the values of equality, acceptance and respect.\u201d<\/p>\n

Their statement came after the Florida Senate passed the \u201cDon\u2019t Say Gay\u201d bill, which would ban teachers from discussing\u00a0LGBTQ\u00a0topics like sexual orientation or gender identity with students until after third grade. Ron DeSantis say he plans to sign the bill into a law. <\/p>\n

Marvel Studios released the Tweet following Disney\u2019s silence on the \u201cDon\u2019t Say Gay\u201d bill.@MarvelStudios\/ Twitter <\/p>\n

Last week, Disney CEO Bob Chapek came under fire after a memo leaked explaining a decision by the Mouse House to remain silent on the controversial legislation.<\/p>\n

While the Disney brass stated that they \u201cunequivocally stand in support of our LGBTQ+ employees,\u201d they wrote that \u201ccorporate statements do very little to change outcomes or minds.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cInstead, they are often weaponized by one side or the other to further divide and inflame,\u201d they added. \u201cSimply put, they can be counterproductive and undermine more effective ways to achieve change.\u201d<\/p>\n

The company also noted that they donated to state institutions that supported the bill.<\/p>\n

Bob Chapek, CEO of the Walt Disney Company during the opening ceremony for Avengers Campus inside Disney California Adventure in Anaheim, CA, on Wednesday, June 2, 2021.MediaNews Group via Getty Images <\/p>\n

However, following an internal backlash from employees, Chapek apologized Monday for not taking a stand, writing: \u201cYou needed me to be a stronger ally in the fight for equal rights and I let you down. I am sorry.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cOur employees see the power of this great company as an opportunity to do good,\u201d said the Disney boss, who has since pledged to pause political donations in the state of Florida. \u201cI missed the mark in this case but am an ally you can count on\u2014and I will be an outspoken champion for the protections, visibility and opportunity you deserve.\u201d<\/p>\n

However, this mea culpa proved too little too late for Disney employees, who staged a walkout to protest Chapek\u2019s perceived inaction.<\/p>\n

\u201cBy supporting the politicians who brought this legislation and not taking a public stand against it, Chapek and [The Walt Disney Company] leadership have made it clear they are more than willing to sacrifice their employees\u2019 health and wellness in service of the bottom line. We will not stand for this anymore,\u201d the demonstration\u2019s organizers wrote in a statement.<\/p>\n

Governor Ron DeSantis plans to pass the \u201cDon\u2019t Say Gay\u201d bill into law.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cCompanies that do business in Florida, Texas, and the dozens of other states considering anti-LGBTQ legislation not only have a responsibility to speak out for their LGBTQ customers and employees, they also have a real opportunity to educate the public about these unpopular and harmful bills and stop them from becoming law,\u201d Ellis said.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
