Michael Kors\u2019 chief executive is leaving the company just seven months after taking the reins of the luxury brand \u2014 and despite the fact he was on track for a big promotion.<\/p>\n

Joshua Schulman is stepping down despite having been poised to take the helm of Capri Holdings, the owner of Michael Kors, Jimmy Choo, and Versace, from the holding company\u2019s current CEO, John Idol, in September, the company announced last year.<\/p>\n

The sudden and unexplained reversal sent Capri\u2019s shares down by more than 12% at $47.93 on Monday.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe remain well-positioned to achieve meaningful long-term revenue and earnings growth as well as deliver increased value for our shareholders,\u201d Idol said in a statement, adding,\u00a0\u201cI am fully committed to continuing to lead Capri Holdings and driving our future success.\u201d<\/p>\n

Idol, 62, had been slated to give up the CEO title and become executive chairman in September.<\/p>\n

John Idol is chairman and CEO of Capri Holdings and was supposed to step down as CEO in September 2022.Jamie McCarthy <\/p>\n

Schulman, 50, was named CEO of Michael Kors in August after stints with Tapestry, where he is credited with turning around the Coach brand. He has also held senior positions at Gucci, Jimmy Choo and Bergdorf Goodman, where he was the president from 2014 to 2017.<\/p>\n

There is no indication that Schulman was forced out of Capri and industry insiders were surprised by the announcement on Monday.<\/p>\n

\u201cSometimes those CEO-in-waiting roles are difficult to execute,\u201d said one source who did not want to be identified. \u201cFrom the outside looking in, business was good and Josh seemed happy.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sales at Michael Kors have been strong recently. <\/p>\n

According to a regulatory filing, Schulman will continue to receive his $1.3 million base salary for two years as well as $5.2 million and a reduction in his non-compete obligations from two years to September 2022.<\/p>\n

Given the generous severance package, it\u2019s less likely that Schulman\u2019s departure involves \u201cconduct-related disciplinary action,\u201d wrote JP Morgan analyst Matthew Boss in a research note.<\/p>\n

His unexplained departure from Capri follows on the heels of a top Ralph Lauren executive \u2014 Howard Smith \u2014 who was ousted from the company last week for alleged violations of the company\u2019s ethics code.<\/p>\n

Smith was the brand\u2019s executive vice president and chief commercial officer. On Feb. 28, Estee Lauder ousted a veteran top executive, executive group president John Demsey, for an inappropriate Instagram post in which he used a meme containing the N-word and joked about COVID-19.<\/p>\n

Michael Kors will be led by John Idol now.josephine schiele <\/p>\n

\u201cMichael Kors has a talented management team in place that will continue to execute on its strategic initiatives,\u201d Idol said in a statement Monday. \u201cThe Board and I remain extremely optimistic about the future growth of Michael Kors and Capri Holdings. We are grateful to Josh for his contributions to our organization.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Last year when Schulman joined the company, Idol said in a statement, \u201cJosh\u2019s appointment is part of a thoughtfully planned leadership succession.\u00a0Over the course of the next year, Josh will immerse himself in the Michael Kors brand. The board and I are confident in Josh\u2019s unique abilities to lead Capri Holdings.\u201d<\/p>\n
