Twitter\u2019s board of directors took its \u201chigh\u201d stakes battle with Elon Musk to another level on Monday \u2013 appearing to slip a \u201c420\u201d cannabis reference into an SEC filing detailing its \u201cpoison pill\u201d measure against the billionaire\u2019s takeover bid.<\/p>\n

Twitter\u2019s filing included a detailed breakdown of its \u201cshareholder rights plan.\u201d The defensive tactic aims to limit Musk\u2019s ability to acquire a larger stake in the company by making discounted Twitter shares available for purchase if Musk exceeds 15% ownership \u2013 effectively diluting his position.<\/p>\n

According to the filing, If Musk or another shareholder were to exceed a 15% stake, other shareholders can exercise a right to pay $210 to acquire more stock in the company. <\/p>\n

The filing notes that stock purchased at the $210 \u201cexercise price\u201d will have \u201ca then-current market value of twice the Exercise Price\u201d \u2013 or $420.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s unclear whether Twitter\u2019s board intended to make the reference, which was first spotted by Bloomberg columnist Matt Levine. The number \u201c420\u201d has a specific significance to pot smokers \u2013 and to Musk, who has slipped the figure into his buyout proposals on more than one occasion.<\/p>\n

Twitter did not immediately return The Post\u2019s request for comment on the filing.<\/p>\n

Last week, Musk revealed that he had offered to buy Twitter outright for roughly $43 billion, or $54.20 per share. Social media users quickly seized on the fact that the billionaire made another weed joke.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am offering to buy 100% of Twitter for $54.20 per share in cash, a 54% premium over the day before I began investing in Twitter and a 38% premium over the day before my investment was publicly announced,\u201d\u00a0Musk said in a letter\u00a0to Twitter board chairman Bret Taylor detailing the offer.<\/p>\n

And in the days before Musk\u2019s deal to take a seat on Twitter\u2019s board fell apart, the Tesla CEO joked about smoking pot during company meetings \u2013 posting a meme from an infamous 2018 appearance on \u201cThe Joe Rogan Experience\u201d in which he and the podcast host shared a joint.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter\u2019s next board meeting is gonna be lit,\u201d the meme said.<\/p>\n

Twitter enacted a \u201cpoison pill\u201d measure to defend against Elon Musk\u2019s takeover bid.Associated Press <\/p>\n

An earlier \u201c420\u201d reference led to legal trouble for Musk. In August 2018, the billionaire tweeted that he was \u201cconsidering taking Tesla private at $420\u201d with\u00a0\u201cfunding secured.\u201d At the time, Tesla was experiencing financial troubles that threatened to lead to bankruptcy.<\/p>\n

The tweet prompted the SEC to sue Musk for allegedly making false or misleading statements. In a subsequent court filing, the agency said Musk\u2019s use of \u201c$420\u201d was a weed reference meant to impress his on-and-off girlfriend, the electronic pop star Grimes.<\/p>\n

In a court filing last Friday, Tesla shareholders suing Musk over the tweet asserted that a judge had agreed with their claims that he had knowingly made false claims regarding the funding.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, Twitter\u2019s board has yet to decide whether to accept Musk\u2019s offer. The prospect of working for the billionaire has rankled many of the social media platform\u2019s current employees.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe Board adopted the Rights Agreement to protect stockholders from coercive or otherwise unfair takeover tactics,\u201d Twitter said in its filing. \u201cIn general terms, it works by imposing a significant penalty upon any person or group that acquires 15 percent or more of the shares of Common Stock without the approval of the Board.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Musk has called on the board to open the decision up to shareholders \u2013 arguing it would be \u201cutterly indefensible\u201d if company executives ignored public will in making their choice.<\/p>\n
