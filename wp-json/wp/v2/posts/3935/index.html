\u201cLightyear\u201d star Chris Evans has slammed critics of a same-sex kiss in his new movie, calling them \u201cidiots.\u201d <\/p>\n

The remarks from Evans \u2014 whose brother, actor Scott Evans, is gay \u2014 come after the flick was banned in 14 countries due to a kiss shared between two female characters, according to the Independent. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe real truth is, those people are idiots.\u201d stated Evans, 41, during an interview with Reuters that was released Tuesday.<\/p>\n

\u201cEvery time there\u2019s been social advancement as we wake up, the American story, the human story is one of constant social awakening and growth and that\u2019s what makes us good,\u201d he added. <\/p>\n

The controversy stems from a character named Hawthorne (played by Uzo Aduba), who kisses her wife in a brief scene that depicts milestones in the character\u2019s life. <\/p>\n

\u201cThere\u2019s always going to be people who are afraid and unaware and trying to hold on to what was before. But those people die off like dinosaurs,\u201d continued Evans. <\/p>\n

\u201cI think the goal is to pay them no mind, march forward and embrace the growth that makes us human.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cLightyear\u201d producer Galyn Susman also chimed in, saying that the kiss adds to the story. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe have a relationship here which lasts an entire lifetime. It\u2019s loving, it\u2019s supportive and it shows Buzz exactly what he doesn\u2019t have and that\u2019s the whole point,\u201d said Susman.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe should all be so lucky to have that kind of relationship in our life,\u201d Susman concluded.<\/p>\n
