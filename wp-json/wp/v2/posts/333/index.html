ORLANDO, Fla. — Saddiq Bey went off for a career-high 51 points in the Detroit Pistons’ 134-120 win over the Magic on Thursday night, continuing what has already been a torrid month of individual scoring in the NBA.<\/p>\n

Bey’s performance marked the league’s eighth 50-point game in March, the most in one calendar month over the past 50 seasons, according to ESPN Stats & Information research. The last month with more was December 1962, when there were nine 50-point games.<\/p>\n

Overall, there have been 17 50-point games this season, and Bey is the 13th player to pull off the feat, matching the most in a season in NBA history. At 22, he is the youngest player with a 50-point game in Pistons history, according to ESPN Stats & Information.<\/p>\n

    Two nights after being torched for 16 points in the first quarter by Brooklyn’s Kyrie Irving on his way to a 60-point game, the Magic gave up 21 points in the first quarter to Bey.<\/p>\n

    “I saw those highlights and it was just very efficient. It was within the flow of the game,” Bey said of Irving. “That was a great performance to watch, and I think just throughout the NBA there’s been a lot of great performances in the past two weeks. I’m just grateful to be in this league to have the opportunity.”<\/p>\n

    Marvin Bagley III added 20 points and 11 rebounds for Detroit in the matchup of the bottom two teams in the Eastern Conference. Franz Wagner led Orlando with 26 points.<\/p>\n

    “Rebuilding is always ugly,” Detroit coach Dwane Casey said, “but there’s a lot of beautiful moments like tonight.”<\/p>\n

    Bey, whose previous career-high was 34, finished the first half in spectacular fashion, scoring five points in less than five seconds. He hit a 20-footer while falling out of bounds with 8.8 seconds left, came up with a backcourt steal and made a 3-pointer with 3.9 seconds left, giving the Pistons a 73-62 lead.<\/p>\n

    “He had 30 points at half so we were definitely expecting 50,” said Pistons center Isaiah Stewart, who had 16 points and 10 rebounds.<\/p>\n

    The Magic scored the first nine points of the game, but that lead was quickly erased by Bey. The Pistons moved ahead for keeps on a dunk by Stewart that broke a 50-50 tie midway through the second quarter.<\/p>\n

    “I think it was just the flow of the game,” Bey said. “I didn’t come into the game saying this is what I’m going to do, but you catch a rhythm and you get in a zone. My teammates did a great job of finding me and just encouraging me to stay aggressive.”<\/p>\n

    The Pistons shot 50% and their 134 points was also a season high.<\/p>\n

    “[Bey] got going early and he hit some really tough shots, but we’ve got to do a better job — I’ve got to do a better job — of not letting him do what he wants to do,” Wagner said. “He shot 14 3s, and we knew before the game we didn’t want him shooting 3s.”<\/p>\n
