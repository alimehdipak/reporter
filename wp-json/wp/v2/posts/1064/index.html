METAIRIE, La. — Quarterback Jameis Winston\u2019s NFL future was in limbo while the New Orleans Saints pursued Deshaun Watson two weeks ago. But Winston said that ultimately turned into a positive because of the \u201ctough conversations\u201d he had with the Saints before agreeing to a two-year deal with a base value of $28 million.<\/p>\n

\u201cI know if their pursuit of Deshaun didn\u2019t happen, I would not have been able to have that growth and those conversations with people in important roles,\u201d Winston, 28, said Monday. \u201cIt was great to get an understanding of where I stood in this organization, and how the business side and the coaching side view me as a player, and where did they believe that I could take this team?\u201d<\/p>\n

The New Orleans Saints brought back quarterback Jameis Winston after he went 5-2 last season as a starter before suffering a season-ending knee injury.\u00a0Ken Murray\/Icon Sportswire<\/p>\n

Winston said the end result was a \u201ccompromise.\u201d He didn’t get the blockbuster long-term contract he was hoping to earn before he tore an ACL in Week 8, but his new deal cements him as New Orleans\u2019 starter under new coach Dennis Allen instead of the situation he faced last summer, in an open competition with Taysom Hill throughout training camp.<\/p>\n

\u201cNow, it\u2019s just the action,\u201d Winston said. \u201cIt\u2019s just going out there, it\u2019s me getting healthy, and it\u2019s us going out there and winning games. And making everybody happy. Because that\u2019s what it\u2019s about. That\u2019s how they keep their jobs on the business side. That\u2019s how DA stays the head coach. And that\u2019s how I stay the quarterback.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou’ve been around me, I’m a very optimistic dude. Everything happens for a reason in my book. It’s all a part of God’s plan for me, and I embrace that.\u201d<\/p>\n

Allen echoed Winston’s comments on Tuesday morning.<\/p>\n

\u201cJameis understands how our business operates,\u201d Allen said. \u201cHe and I had a couple of good — call it tough — conversations. And I thought it was a good process.\u201d<\/p>\n

Allen declined to go into extensive detail about the Saints\u2019 pursuit of Watson, but he did state that there was an “interest on our part.”<\/p>\n

“We did our due diligence, we did our homework and he ended up going somewhere else,\u201d Allen said. \u201cSo we moved forward from there and went full speed ahead with Jameis, and we\u2019re excited about that.\u201d<\/p>\n

Winston, who was speaking to the New Orleans media for the first time since last season, said his injury rehab is ahead of schedule and he is \u201cgoing to be back well before training camp.\u201d<\/p>\n

He also admitted he was surprised when former Saints coach Sean Payton decided to step away in January, which also weighed on his decision.<\/p>\n

\u201cI mean, we’re tied together. So I got faith in DA.\u201d<\/p>\n

Winston also pointed out that offensive coordinator Pete Carmichael Jr. and quarterbacks coach Ronald Curry remain in their roles. That \u201ccontinuity\u201d is a big reason why he is excited about returning to the Saints for a third consecutive season.<\/p>\n

And Winston said he is especially looking forward to working with former All-Pro receiver Michael Thomas, who missed all of last season with an ankle injury. The Saints\u2019 passing offense finished last in the NFL last season in large part because of his and Thomas’ injuries and others across the offensive line. But Winston said Thomas’ return should help change things \u201cdrastically.\u201d<\/p>\n

Winston has shown a great deal of humility since his original team, the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, decided to part ways with him in 2020 and he signed a minimal contract to learn under Payton and Brees.<\/p>\n
