Dressing in designer threads doesn\u2019t hold a candle to owning a luxury brand\u2019s former global headquarters.<\/p>\n

A historic townhouse that once served as fashion house Gucci\u2019s main office has hit the market. It asks $66 million and is also available to rent for a jaw-dropping $48,000 a week.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The palatial 12,621-square-foot home is located in the tony Mayfair neighborhood in London\u2019s West End and boasts a double garage, a cinema, a sunroom, an indoor swimming pool, a marble-lined plunge pool, a steam room, a sauna and even \u201cfour secure vaults.\u201d It is the last remaining private townhouse on Grafton Street, according to the listing, which is held by Beauchamp Estates.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Neoclassical-style building was completed in 1772 and, before the luxury brand moved in, was home to one Lord Chancellor Brougham, who entertained the likes of Queen Victoria and the first Duke of Wellington there. Gucci moved there in 1998 when, following the fatal shooting of Maurizio Gucci by a hitman hired by his ex-wife, Patrizia Reggiani Gucci, in the lobby of the company\u2019s Milan headquarters, head honchos decided the business needed a new main office. So they moved to the Grafton Street space, which is near Gucci\u2019s Bond Street store.\u00a0<\/p>\n
