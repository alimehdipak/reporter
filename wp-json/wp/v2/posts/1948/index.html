Google has officially unveiled its new campus for New York City employees, a sprawling facility atop a pier that juts into the Hudson River with amenities that include a two-acre rooftop park with views of Lower Manhattan and Little Island.<\/p>\n

The 630,000-square-foot Hudson River campus on Pier 57 includes three buildings of office space for some 450 employees just above the West Side Highway near 15th Street in Chelsea.<\/p>\n

The two-acre park space is open to the general public. There\u2019s also a food hall and an area for outdoor screening of films during the Tribeca Film Festival.<\/p>\n

The new campus officially opened this week at a ceremony attended by Google chief financial officer Ruth Porat, New York Gov. Kathy Hochul, and Mayor Eric Adams.<\/p>\n

Google is the key tenant at the pier, which cost more than $500 million to restore, according to New York Business Journal.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m excited about this venture and others we are going to do,\u201d said Adams.<\/p>\n

The unveiling comes after a restoration of the pier that cost more than $500 million.Courtesy of Google The facilities are expected to hold some 450 office workers.Courtesy of Google <\/p>\n

Adams and other public officials have urged companies to bring their employees back into the office as a way to jumpstart the local economy, which has struggled since the onset of the coronavirus pandemic.<\/p>\n

\u201cSo get some new work clothes, because you might have put on a couple of COVID pounds \u2014 I\u2019m guilty too \u2014 and get back to work. Come back here,\u201d said Hochul.<\/p>\n

Google, which employs 12,000 workers in the city, is looking to expand its footprint in the Big Apple.<\/p>\n

In September, the company snapped up a 1.3 million-square-foot waterfront building in Manhattan\u2019s Hudson Square for $2.1 billion \u2014 which at the time was the most ever paid for an office building.<\/p>\n

Google, which employs 12,000 workers in New York City, is looking to expand its footprint in the Big Apple.Courtesy of Google Google hopes the new offices will make it more attractive for workers to return to the office.Courtesy of Google <\/p>\n

Google also owns two smaller buildings in Chelsea: 111 Eighth Ave. and the Milk Building at 450 W. 15th St.<\/p>\n

Earlier this month, Google asked its employees to return to the office for at least three days a week as part of its new hybrid model.<\/p>\n
