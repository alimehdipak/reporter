Travis Scott stopped his Coney Island concert on Monday to ensure fans\u2019 safety in the wake of his deadly Astroworld show in November, which was labeled a \u201cmass casualty event.\u201d<\/p>\n

The 31-year-old \u201cSicko Mode\u201d rapper was performing at The Day Party at The Coney Art Walls when fans scaled a lighting truss for a better view of the show. <\/p>\n

Scott spotted the fans \u2014 including one that was dressed as Spider-Man \u2014 dangling from the contraption and ordered them to come down. They obliged, and the show went on, TMZ reported. <\/p>\n

\u201cTravis is committed to doing his part to ensure events are as safe as possible so that fans can have fun, and he encouraged fans to listen to requests from security and climb down from the lighting structures so that everyone would be able to safely enjoy last night\u2019s performance,\u201d a spokesperson for Scott told The Post on Tuesday. <\/p>\n

Footage from the performance shows similar conditions to those of last year\u2019s Astroworld Festival in Houston, where 10 attendees were crushed to death in a massive crowd surge. <\/p>\n

Travis Scott told fans to get back on the ground. TMZ.com The fans obeyed his request and came down.TMZ.com <\/p>\n

Scott alleged that he did not see fainting fans and ambulances in the sea of 50,000 as he was performing. Still, he\u2019s been hit with dozens of lawsuits. <\/p>\n

Scott has slowly returned to live performances. With the help of Diddy, he performed at the Billboard Music Awards on May 15. He was set to perform at Nevada\u2019s Day N Vegas festival, but the event was scrapped due to \u201ca combination of logistics, timing, and production issues.\u201d<\/p>\n

Instead, he spent his Fourth of July weekend performing at Miami club E11even on Saturday.<\/p>\n
