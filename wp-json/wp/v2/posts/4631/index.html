\u201cLate Night Talking\u201d will turn into late-night studying for some Texas college students.<\/p>\n

Texas State University in San Marcos is offering a class about Harry Styles starting in the spring of 2023.<\/p>\n

The course, titled \u201cHarry Styles and the Cult of Celebrity: Identity, the Internet and European Pop Culture,\u201d will be taught by Associate Professor of Digital History Dr. Louie Dean Valencia, who posted about it on social media over the weekend.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s official, official. I\u2019m teaching the world\u2019s first ever university course on the work of Harry Styles,\u201d he wrote.<\/p>\n

The honors college course will focus on Styles\u2019 music, in addition to \u201cthe cultural and political development of the modern celebrity as related to questions of gender and sexuality, race, class, nation and globalism, media, fashion, fan culture, internet culture, and consumerism,\u201d according to the class flyer.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The course is titled \u201cHarry Styles and the Cult of Celebrity: Identity, the Internet and European Pop Culture.\u201dLouie Dean Valencia\/Texas State <\/p>\n

Valencia told KXAN that he\u2019s a \u201chuge fan\u201d of Styles and started listening to him during his One Direction days. He even went to see him in concert as part of Styles\u2019 Love on Tour.<\/p>\n

\u201cAs a historian, I want the class to get to really see how the world has changed in the last 12 years or so, but also how to put that into historical context, through the lens of Harry Styles,\u201d he told KXAN.<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out for comment on the curriculum from the \u201cWatermelon Sugar\u201d singer\u2019s representatives.<\/p>\n

The idea for the course came from research Valencia started during the coronavirus lockdown in the summer of 2020.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Assignments will include studying his music \u2014 his solo albums and One Direction albums \u2014 and films.Louie Dean Valencia\/Texas State <\/p>\n

\u201cWhen I couldn\u2019t travel to do my regular research, I started researching Harry \u2014 focusing on his art, the ways masculinity has changed in the last decade, celebrity culture and the internet,\u201d the professor told NBC Dallas-Fort Worth.<\/p>\n

\u201cHarry Styles and the Cult of Celebrity\u201d will function like a history class. Assignments will include studying his music \u2014 his solo albums and One Direction albums \u2014 and films, with readings from Susan Sontag, Alain de Botton, Haruki Murakami and more. Students will also be required to create their own podcast.<\/p>\n

Registration for the class begins this fall.\u00a0<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
