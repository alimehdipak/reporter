She didn\u2019t ace this one. <\/p>\n

Four-time Grand Slam tennis champ Naomi Osaka is being hit with backlash over the unintentionally \u2014 or cluelessly, depending on whom you ask \u2014 offensive name of her new media company, Hana Kuma. <\/p>\n

The sporting superstar, who was born in Japan, told the New York Times Tuesday that hana kuma<\/em> means \u201cflower bear\u201d in Japanese \u2014 but Twitter users quickly noted it has a far more explicit meaning in Swahili, where it translates to \u201cwoman without a vagina.\u201d<\/p>\n

The lost-in-translation controversy began trending on Twitter in multiple African countries, with dozens of users urging Osaka, 24, to change the name of the company, which she founded in partnership with NBA legend LeBron James, 37. <\/p>\n

\u201cNaomi Osaka has partnered with LeBron James to start a media company called Hana Kuma. Well, this \u2018Hana Kuma\u2019 name means something different in Swahili. I won\u2019t say it,\u201d Ugandan journalist Daniel Kibet tweeted. <\/p>\n

\u201cDid they do a global search on what the name means?\u201d another asked. \u201cShe should have been well advised [that] it has a very vulgar meaning in Swahili which is spoken by many people.\u201d <\/p>\n

The tennis ace excitedly announced her new business venture earlier this week. The company will produce TV shows, documentaries and branded content.AFP via Getty Images \t \t <\/p>\n

A third blasted: \u201cAmazing the lack of research. Lots of money but that brand won\u2019t fly in Africa. Let it stay in America and Japan. It\u2019s offensive. Do you even have a clue what it means? Get rid of your ego and change the name. God forgive your ignorance.\u201d<\/p>\n

Another also urged Osaka to consider a name change, stating: \u201cIf you keep the name #hanakuma, the name will override the news and the stories you wish to share. The name of the brand is already news! I suggest a change and also, just being a bit respectful to global culture. It\u2019s not overthinking here, it\u2019s protecting a brand!\u201d<\/p>\n

Others invariably described the name as \u201cunethical\u201d and \u201cexplicit.\u201d <\/p>\n

Swahili is estimated to be spoken by approximately 200\u00a0million around the world, with a majority of speakers concentrated in East Africa. Osaka hasn\u2019t yet responded to the controversy, but she shared a cryptic tweet Wednesday night, writing: \u201cPrayers for all my overthinkers. We be going through it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Osaka told the Times that she hopes Hana Kuma will become a top Hollywood media company that produces\u00a0TV shows, documentaries and branded content. The company has been founded in partnership with\u00a0SpringHill \u2014 a marketing firm co-founded by James. <\/p>\n

Osaka and James are pictured together in 2019. The pair \u2014 who have teamed up for the new venture \u2014 are two of the most influential athletes in the world. NBAE via Getty Images \t \t <\/p>\n

Osaka \u2014 who was born to a Japanese mother and a Haitian father \u2014 said the company will place an emphasis on diverse storytelling. <\/p>\n

\u201cWhat excites me is being able to inspire people and tell new stories, particularly ones that I would have wanted to see when I was a kid,\u201d she stated. \u201cI always wanted to kind of see someone like me.\u201d<\/p>\n

The tennis ace is currently the world\u2019s highest-paid female athlete, raking in a whopping $57 million last year. <\/p>\n

Much of Osaka\u2019s earnings come from endorsement deals, with the savvy star inking lucrative partnerships with brands including Nike and Louis Vuitton. <\/p>\n

Earlier this week, the former World No. 1 announced she was withdrawing from the upcoming Wimbledon tournament, due to an Achilles injury. <\/p>\n
