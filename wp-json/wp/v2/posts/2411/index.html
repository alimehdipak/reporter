Naomi Judd, one half of the iconic country music duo The Judds, reportedly died by suicide after years of struggling with mental illness.<\/p>\n

Naomi\u2019s daughters, Wynonna and Ashley, said they lost their mom to \u201cthe disease of mental illness\u201d in a statement released Saturday.<\/p>\n

On Monday, multiple sources told People that the 76-year-old country music icon had killed herself. No further details were provided.<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out for comment from representatives of the singers. <\/p>\n

Larry Strickland, Naomi\u2019s husband of 32 years, had also previously made a statement. \u201cNaomi Judd\u2019s family request privacy during this heartbreaking time. No additional information will be released at this time,\u201d he said. <\/p>\n

Naomi and Wynonna, 57, were inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame on Sunday.<\/p>\n

\u201cI didn\u2019t prepare anything tonight because I knew Mom would probably talk the most,\u201d a tearful Wynonna\u00a0told the audience at the Nashville ceremony. \u201cI\u2019m gonna make this fast, because my heart\u2019s broken, and I feel so blessed. It\u2019s a very strange dynamic, to be this broken and this blessed.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

Naomi Judd died Saturday at age 76.Getty \t \t <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m sorry that she couldn\u2019t hang on until today,\u201d a crying Ashley said, adding, \u201cWhile this is so much about the Judds as a duo, I want to take a moment to recognize my sister, a GOAT.\u201d <\/p>\n

Earlier last month, Naomi and Wynonna had announced they would reunite for a celebratory final tour that was set to launch in September.<\/p>\n

\u201cThough my heart is broken, I will continue to sing,\u201d Wynonna said Sunday.<\/p>\n

Naomi had been candid about her mental health struggles in the past. In 2016, she revealed her battles with \u201cextreme\u201d and \u201csevere depression\u201d that left her housebound on \u201cGood Morning America.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201c[Fans] see me in rhinestones, you know, with glitter in my hair, that really is who I am,\u201d she told anchor Robin Roberts at the time. \u201cBut then I would come home and not leave the house for three weeks, and not get out of my pajamas, and not practice normal hygiene. It was really bad.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWhen I came off the tour, I went into this deep, dark, absolutely terrifying hole and I couldn\u2019t get out,\u201d she continued. \u201cI spent two years on my couch.\u201d The country icon further shared that she had considered taking her own life at a bridge near her farm.<\/p>\n

In 2016, Naomi wrote a book, titled \u201cRiver of Time: My Descent Into Depression and How I Emerged With Hope,\u201d to go public with her diagnosis and to let people know that mental illness is \u201cnot a character flaw, it\u2019s a stinking disease.\u201d<\/p>\n
