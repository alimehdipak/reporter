Bette Midler \u2014 who caused outrage on Monday after tweeting about the trans community and the language used to describe them \u2014 is now firing back at critics of the tweet. <\/p>\n

The tweet \u2014 which said: \u201cWOMEN OF THE WORLD! We are being stripped of our rights over our bodies, our lives and even of our name!\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cThey don\u2019t call us \u2018women\u2019 anymore; they call us \u2018birthing people\u2019 or \u2018menstruators,\u2019 and even \u2018people with vaginas!\u2019 Don\u2019t let them erase you! Every human on earth owes you,\u201d the tweet continued. <\/p>\n

Bette Midler is firing back after causing an uproar on Twitter on July 4, 2022.Amanda Andrade-Rhoades\/For The Washington Post via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The now-viral tweet has gained more than 102,000 likes and nearly 20,000 comments, according to the Hollywood Reporter. <\/p>\n

\u201cAs a woman without a uterus, I support inclusive language to normalize that trans men need reproductive healthcare too, 100% It\u2019s not trans people who are taking away my right to bodily [autonomy]. We\u2019re all fighting the same fight,\u201d said one user. <\/p>\n

\u201cDon\u2019t do this, Bette. \u2018Birthing people\u2019 is language used to make sure we don\u2019t exclude trans comrades. It doesn\u2019t erase women at all,\u201d\u00a0pleaded another.<\/p>\n

Several of the more extreme comments have compared the 76-year-old \u201cHocus Pocus\u201d star to being a TERF \u2014 trans-exclusionary radical feminist, a charge that has been leveled at \u201cHarry Potter\u201d author J.K. Rowling. <\/p>\n

Midler\u2019s tweet seemingly came in response to the Supreme Court\u2019s decision to overturn the landmark case Roe v. Wade. <\/p>\n

The outrage against the \u201cFirst Wives Club\u201d star comes as several states have begun restricting health-care options for transgendered people.<\/p>\n

Midler once again took to Twitter on Tuesday in an attempt to clarify her tweet. <\/p>\n

The now-viral tweet has gained over 102,000 likes and nearly 21,000 comments. <\/p>\n
