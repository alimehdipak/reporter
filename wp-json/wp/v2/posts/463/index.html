Elon Musk, the world\u2019s richest man, challenged Russian President Vladimir Putin to \u201csingle combat\u201d amid the war in Ukraine \u2014 prompting a Kremlin official to fire back at the Tesla boss, calling him a \u201cweakling\u201d and \u201clittle devil.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI hereby challenge Vladimir Putin to single combat,\u201d Musk tweeted early Monday, a term that refers to a person-to-person fight on a battlefield. \u201cStakes are Ukraine.\u201d<\/p>\n

The words \u201cVladimir Putin\u201d and \u201cUkraine\u201d were written in Cyrillic letters used in both Russian and Ukrainian.<\/p>\n

In another tweet, he tagged Putin\u2019s official Twitter account and wrote: \u201cDo you agree to this fight?\u201d<\/p>\n

Dmitry Rogozin, head of the Russian space agency Roscosmos, tweeted in response to Musk, writing: \u201cYou, little devil, are still young.\u201d <\/p>\n

Rogozin added: \u201cCompete with me weakling; it would only be a waste of time. Overtake my brother first.\u201d<\/p>\n

Elon Musk challenged Russian President Vladimir Putin to a fight on Twitter on Monday.Maja Hitij <\/p>\n

Ukraine\u2019s vice prime minister, Mykhailo Fedorov, came to Musk\u2019s defense, tweeting: \u201cI am sure that\u00a0@elonmusk can send Putin to Jupiter.\u201d<\/p>\n

Fedorov then posted a meme showing Putin inside a spaceship with the words \u201cSend Putin to Jupiter.\u201d The meme links to a website where donors can contribute money toward Ukraine\u2019s defense.<\/p>\n

After Russia launched an invasion of Ukraine nearly three weeks ago, Musk agreed to the Ukrainian government\u2019s request to provide Starlink internet terminals in case the country was cut off from landline connections.<\/p>\n

Starlink is Musk\u2019s start-up internet provider where users can connect to the web through a dish that links up to satellites in space.<\/p>\n

Last week, Musk trolled Rogozin after he declared that he would not allow the sale of Russian rockets to the US in retaliation for its sanctions regime.<\/p>\n

Rogozin drew widespread mockery from Musk and others after he declared on state television that he would deny access to Russia\u2019s \u201cworld\u2019s best rocket engines\u201d \u2014 adding the US would have to \u201cfly on something else like their broomsticks.\u201d<\/p>\n
