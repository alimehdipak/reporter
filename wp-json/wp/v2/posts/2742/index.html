<\/p>\n

With anticipation sky-high for \u201cTop Gun: Maverick,\u201d which hits theaters on May 27, Lady Gaga takes flight on \u201cHold My Hand,\u201d the new single from the movie\u2019s soundtrack.<\/p>\n

But when Berlin recorded \u201cTake My Breath Away\u201d \u2014 the Oscar-winning smash that sent the hearts of Tom Cruise and Kelly McGillis soaring in the original 1986 film \u2014 the band was hardly in Gaga\u2019s superstar stratosphere.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe got really lucky because at the time we were nothing,\u201d Terri Nunn, lead singer of Berlin, told The Post. \u201cWe had not had a hit at all. We were an underground band.\u201d<\/p>\n

But while working with Giorgio Moroder \u2014 the legendary producer of \u201cTake My Breath Away\u201d \u2014 on some other material, Nunn and Berlin got their big break. \u201cHe approached a bunch of other female artists who were way bigger than me and Berlin, and a few of them did demos, but the [\u2018Top Gun\u2019] producers didn\u2019t like them,\u201d said Nunn. \u201cSo finally, I got a shot at it. And they loved it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Berlin in its electro-pop heyday circa the 1980s.Michael Ochs Archives <\/p>\n

Thirty-six years after Berlin\u2019s synth-pop swooner was released, Nunn can hear echoes of the tune in the retro-\u201980s power balladry of Gaga\u2019s \u201cHold My Hand,\u201d which dropped earlier this week.<\/p>\n

\u201cWhat I love about it is that nod to Giorgio Moroder\u2019s production, because the drums are so big and reverbed,\u201d she said, noting that Gaga was the perfect singer to pilot a \u201cTop Gun\u201d tune. \u201cShe\u2019s a great writer, she\u2019s a great artist. If they can get her to do it, yeah, it\u2019s a good choice.\u201d<\/p>\n

After Nunn breathed a cool sensuality into Berlin\u2019s 1986 chart-topper, the tune \u2014 co-written by Moroder and Tom Whitlock \u2014\u00a0 followed Kenny Loggins\u2019 \u201cDanger Zone\u201d as the second hit single from the blockbuster \u201cTop Gun\u201d soundtrack. But after \u201cTake My Breath Away\u201d was nominated for Best Original Song at the 1987 Oscars, Nunn declined an offer to perform the truncated tune as part of a medley at the Academy Awards.<\/p>\n

Berlin\u2019s \u201cTake My Breath Away\u201d hit home as the romantic theme song for Kelly McGillis and Tom Cruise in the original \u201cTop Gun.\u201dGetty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cI said, \u2018Well, no, because if I can\u2019t do the whole song, I\u2019m not doing it,\u2019 \u201d said Nunn, who was on tour with Berlin when Lou Rawls and Melba Moore performed her song instead. \u201cThat actual night I watched the Oscars on television in Taiwan. And we won.\u201d<\/p>\n

To this day, Nunn regrets that decision. \u201cI was just young and dumb,\u201d she said. \u201cThat\u2019s just stupid.\u201d<\/p>\n

After Berlin \u2014 which, despite its name, is actually a Los Angeles-based band \u2014 broke up at the end of the \u201980s, the group reunited in 1998, and they\u2019ve been recording and touring ever since. In fact, after a pandemic pause, the group is now back on the road in support of its latest album, 2019\u2019s \u201cTranscendance.\u201d<\/p>\n

Terri Nunn\u2019s band Berlin released its latest album, \u201cTranscendance,\u201d in 2019.Louis Rodiger <\/p>\n

And while after all this time, Nunn, 62, has never gotten the chance to meet \u201cTop Gun\u201d star Cruise, she did have an emotional encounter with McGillis about 10 years ago.<\/p>\n

\u201cI met Kelly McGillis in Italy doing [an event], honoring great songs from movies,\u201d she said. \u201cAnd she cried when we met. She said, \u2018I just can\u2019t tell you what that song means to me.\u2019 She was very emotional about it. So it was great to meet her, finally.\u201d<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
