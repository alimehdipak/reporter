Legendary singer Tony Bennett made a rare appearance in Central Park on Thursday, where he was seen for the first time using a wheelchair beside his wife, Susan Benedetto.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The 95-year-old \u201cI Left My Heart in San Francisco\u201d crooner, who has publicly battled Alzheimer\u2019s disease since 2016, appeared to be relaxed and in good spirits, sporting a blue tracksuit and escorted by an aide near some of the park\u2019s many horses and carriages.\u00a0<\/p>\n

No stranger in paradise, the Queens-born entertainer, who has lived in a sun-drenched, penthouse apartment overlooking Central Park for 25 years, was seen walking without a wheelchair in April with Benedetto, 55, on Easter Sunday, taking selfies with fans \u2014 who recognized the star even with a face mask on.\u00a0<\/p>\n

On Wednesday, the \u201cStranger in Paradise\u201d singer proudly expressed his support for Alzheimer\u2019s and Brain Awareness Month, smiling in a purple plaid shirt alongside Benedetto and holding up a sign that read #EndAlz in a post shared with his 170,000 fans on Twitter.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cSusan and I are going purple with the @alzassociation to raise awareness for a disease that is impacting our family and many of yours. We hope you join us and #GoPurple,\u201d the musician shared.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Bennett retired from live performances in 2021, canceling rescheduled shows delayed in 2020 as a result of the coronavirus pandemic. He went out on a high note, closing the curtain on a seven-decade career with a series of performances alongside pop icon Lady Gaga. The duo performed their emotional last show together \u2014 billed as \u201cOne Last Time: An Evening With Tony Bennett and Lady Gaga\u201d \u2014 last August at Radio City Music Hall, featuring mega-hits like \u201cI Get a Kick Out of You.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThis was a hard decision for us to make, as he is a capable performer. One, however, that was made by doctors\u2019 orders,\u201d his son Danny Bennett told Variety last August of his father\u2019s final live show.<\/p>\n

Bennett was first diagnosed with Alzheimer\u2019s, the disease resulting in progressive memory loss and decreased mental function, in 2016. However, it wasn\u2019t until 2018 that his family realized his battle with the mind-stealing disease was causing his health to decline, while he was recording the album \u201cLove for Sale\u201d \u2014 which includes iconic ballads like \u201cI\u2019ve Got You Under My Skin\u201d and \u201cYou\u2019re the Top\u201d \u2014 with Lady Gaga over a two-year span, AARP reported.<\/p>\n
