Pals Ben Affleck and Matt Damon are coming together once again for a new project about Nike sneakers executive Sonny Vaccaro.<\/p>\n

Affleck, 49, is set to direct, write, co-star and produce in the sports biopic, while Damon, 51, will\u00a0star, co-write and produce, according to the Hollywood Reporter.<\/p>\n

Amazon Studios and Skydance Sports are reportedly in the midst of finalizing the deal for the flick alongside the two Oscar winners.<\/p>\n

The \u201cFord v Ferrari\u201d star will play Vaccaro and Affleck will portray Nike co-founder Phil Knight.<\/p>\n

The story will revolve around the sports sneaker company and Vaccaro attempting to sign basketball player Michael Jordan in the mid-\u201980s for an endorsement deal. The partnership seemed impossible at the time; however, the deal became one of the most significant in sports history. The quest took the shoe executive to the b-baller\u2019s mom, as well as to former coaches, advisers and friends. <\/p>\n

LeBron James (right) stands alongside Roundball Chairman Sonny Vaccaro (center) during the EA Sports Roundball Classic game in 2003 in Chicago, Illinois.NBAE via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The ex-Chicago Bulls player won\u2019t be seen in the film and will just be a shadowy figure watching the scenes unfold from afar.<\/p>\n

The project holds a special place in the \u201cArgo\u201d star and Damon\u2019s hearts. It marks the first time that Affleck will direct a story starring his Hollywood bestie.<\/p>\n

The duo scored an Oscar for penning the script for\u00a0their 1997 drama, \u201cGood Will Hunting,\u201d and they also co-wrote and co-starred in last year\u2019s bomb of a historical drama, \u201cThe Last Duel.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cGood Will Hunting\u201d executive producer Kevin Smith\u00a0revealed last October that disgraced Miramax founder Harvey Weinstein\u00a0wanted to pull the indie film from theaters early to mess with star Robin Williams\u2019 career.<\/p>\n

Damon (left) and Affleck previously collaborated on projects such as \u201cGood Will Hunting\u201d and \u201cThe Last Duel.\u201dGetty Images <\/p>\n

The Gus Van Sant-directed drama earned more than $225 million at the box office at the time.<\/p>\n

In the \u201cMallrats\u201d director\u2019s book \u201cKevin Smith\u2019s Secret Stash,\u201d the 51-year-old said that Williams had a back-end deal that said if the film grossed over $100 million, Williams would cash in a larger portion of the profits and split that money with Miramax. <\/p>\n

In a past interview with\u00a0the Daily Beast, Smith noted he\u2019s \u201cnot sure if it was a 50\/50 split.\u201d<\/p>\n

He added, \u201cI remember when \u2018Good Will Hunting\u2019 was leaving theaters, and it felt weird because it was like, \u2018Wait? There\u2019s all this Oscar buzz, so why would you pull it if it was just making money?\u2019 And they did it because keeping it in theaters meant that more of the money would go to Robin, whereas the moment it went to video, the split wasn\u2019t Robin-heavy.
