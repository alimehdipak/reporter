Dyson, the company best known for its vacuum cleaners and hair care products, has unveiled a futuristic take on headphones \u2013 complete with an odd-looking, air-purifying visor that\u2019s already drawing comparisons to a certain comic book villain\u2019s costume.<\/p>\n

The \u201cDyson Zone\u201d is a pair of noise-canceling headphones that also functions as the company\u2019s first \u201cwearable purifier,\u201d capable of filtering out air pollution and allergens.<\/p>\n

Dyson said it spent six years researching the system and developed more than 500 prototypes before landing on the final version.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe result of over a decade of air quality research and development, the Dyson Zone air-purifying headphones simultaneously tackle the urban issues of air quality and noise pollution,\u201d Dyson said in a release.<\/p>\n

The headset\u2019s strange look drew immediate attention among social media users \u2013 many of whom pointed out that it resembled the mask worn by Bane, the Batman villain portrayed by actor Tom Hardy in the 2012 film \u201cThe Dark Knight Rises.\u201d<\/p>\n

Dyson said it made more than 500 prototypes before landing on a final design.Dyson <\/p>\n

\u201cThe Dyson Bane, coming soon to an affluent\/gullible High Street near you,\u201d one Twitter user joked.<\/p>\n

\u201cI mean, we could just clean up air pollution in cities, but no, let\u2019s all just cosplay Blade Runner instead,\u201d another quipped.<\/p>\n

Others joked that the device seemed like an elaborate April Fool\u2019s Day joke \u2014 which Dyson quickly denied.<\/p>\n

Dyson said the distinctive visor is \u201cnon-contact\u201d and \u201ckept near the nose and mouth\u201d rather than fully masking them. The device works through compressors contained in each ear cup, which work to \u201cproject two streams of purified air to the wearer\u2019s nose and mouth\u201d through the visor<\/p>\n

The \u201cZone\u201d is capable of filtering up to 99% of particle pollution, Dyson officials told The Verge. The visor can also be removed if the wearer prefers to just use the headphones. The device is meant to protect against air pollution, not to serve as a replacement for masks that protect against COVID-19, according to Mashable.<\/p>\n
