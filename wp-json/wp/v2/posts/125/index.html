Bob Odenkirk opened up about the near-death \u201cheart incident\u201d he experienced back in July while on the set of his AMC show \u201cBetter Call Saul.\u201d<\/p>\n

The 59-year-old actor sat down with NBC \u201cToday\u201d show host Willie Geist to discuss his \u201cshocking\u201d July 2021 health scare in a candid interview set to air Sunday. <\/p>\n

He explained that doctors clarified he had a \u201cheart incident\u201d and not a heart attack, explaining his \u201cwidow-maker artery was completely blocked.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat\u2019s why it\u2019s called the widow-maker, \u2019cause you die when that happens. But I went down. And I was very lucky that my co-stars, Rhea Seehorn and Patrick Fabian, were right nearby.\u201d<\/p>\n

He recounted how his colleagues rushed over and sounded the alarm as health officers began CPR and eventually used a defibrillator three times to revive him. <\/p>\n

Actor Bob Odenkirk talked about his \u201cheart incident\u201d eight months after he collapsed on set. Courtesy of Today <\/p>\n

\u201cI was not present for any of it. But I\u2019m told it was a pretty shocking day on set. And traumatizing for all my costars and crew members and people I love very much who love me. And stood by my side and then went to the hospital with me,\u201d Odenkirk said. <\/p>\n

He had previously shared that he shockingly didn\u2019t have a pulse and had no memory when he initially collapsed between filming.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe were shooting a scene, we\u2019d been shooting all day, and luckily I didn\u2019t go back to my trailer,\u201d he recently told the\u00a0New York Times.<\/p>\n

The \u201cBetter Call Saul\u201d star was rushed to Presbyterian Hospital in Albuquerque, New Mexico, where doctors surgically \u201cblew up the little balloons and knocked out that plaque and left stents in two places.\u201d<\/p>\n

Odenkirk admitted that he discovered a plaque buildup in his heart in 2018 but chose to forgo treatment at the time. The father of two said he was fine until last year when \u201cone of those pieces of plaque broke up.\u201d<\/p>\n
