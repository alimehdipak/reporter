<\/p>\n

Elvis Presley experts and historians have long known about a collection of jewelry the singer gave to his manager Colonel Tom Parker.<\/p>\n

Both collectors and fans have spent decades searching for the items, which were thought to be lost. Thanks to Brigitte Kruse, founder and CEO of GWS Auctions, who found and assembled the pieces, they can now be seen together for the first time.<\/p>\n

Two hundred items, including gold rings encrusted with jewels, cufflinks, watches and chains, will go up for auction on Aug. 27. Also included is the guitar played by Presley during his famous \u201ccomeback\u201d TV special of 1968.<\/p>\n

Presley\u2019s former wife Priscilla worked with Kruse on the collection, although Priscilla does not own any of the pieces.<\/p>\n

\u201cWell, it brings back memories for sure,\u201d Priscilla Presley told Reuters.<\/p>\n

Priscilla Presley looks at a collection of personal jewelry of Elvis Presley.REUTERS The collection was thought to be given to Colonel Tom Parker and was lost for decades.REUTERS Colonel Parker was a Dutch-born musical entrepreneur, best known for being Elvis Presley\u2019s manager.Redferns The items were part of a lost collection and do not belong to Priscilla Presley. REUTERS There are 200 items, including gold rings encrusted with jewels, cufflinks, watches and chains.REUTERS <\/p>\n

She added that it was a running joke with her former husband that he constantly bought or commissioned jewelry for Parker because the manager already had everything he needed and the Presleys did not know what else to buy him.<\/p>\n

Priscilla Presley said she felt protective of the items because she designed some of them, including artifacts with the logo for TCB Band, the musicians who formed the core rhythm section of Presley\u2019s backing band in his later years. \u201cTCB\u201d stood for \u201ctaking care of business,\u201d a favorite expression of Presley\u2019s.<\/p>\n

Priscilla Presley supported the auction in part because she was weary of seeing so many fake Elvis artifacts for sale.<\/p>\n

One item is engraved with his initials.REUTERS Everything will go up for auction on Aug. 27.REUTERS \u201cWell, it brings back memories for sure,\u201d Priscilla Presley said.REUTERS Priscilla Presley said there are many fakes out in the world.REUTERS Priscilla Presley said she hopes the collection will \u201cgo to someone who is going to care for it.\u201dREUTERS Elvis, the \u201cKing of Rock \u2018n\u2019 Roll,\u201d died August 16, 1977.AP <\/p>\n

\u201cThere is so much product out there that is not authentic at all and that worries me,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n
