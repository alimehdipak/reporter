Jamie Dornan and Robert Pattinson\u2019s decades-long friendship still stands today.<\/p>\n

The \u201cFifty Shades\u201d star, 39, responded to claims that Pattinson, 35, made earlier this month about receiving \u201cpity invites\u201d from Dornan and his flatmates \u2014 the now super-famous crew of Andrew Garfield, Charlie Cox and Eddie Redmayne.<\/p>\n

Dornan quipped to Entertainment Tonight at the\u00a02022 Critics Choice Awards\u00a0on Sunday that the \u201cTwilight\u201d actor was in a different league than him and his friends because of his fame from the vampire romantic drama film.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe pity invite? No. I think with Rob it\u2019s always been like, he sort of had success earlier, so we were a bit like, \u2018Does he really fit in with us?\u2019 Because we were not working, and he\u2019s working all the time,\u201d Dornan said. \u201cHe did \u2018Twilight\u2019 and was suddenly in a different stratosphere than us.\u201d<\/p>\n

Jamie Dornan and Robert Pattinson shared a flat with fellow British actors Eddie Redmayne, Charlie Cox and Andrew Garfield early on in their careers. <\/p>\n

He continued: \u201cWe\u2019ve sort of, not caught up, but we all started working more consistently, but yeah, Jesus, we\u2019ve known each other a long time.\u201d<\/p>\n

At the premiere for \u201cThe Batman\u201d on March 1, Pattinson noted to ET that early on in his career, he shared a pad with the foursome. He stated that his roommates would leave him out and go to events without him.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey were all roommates, and I was kind of the last one invited,\u201d Pattinson said. \u201cI was invited as an afterthought. There\u2019d be like one slice of pizza left and I\u2019d be like, \u2018Is there any for me?’\u201d<\/p>\n
