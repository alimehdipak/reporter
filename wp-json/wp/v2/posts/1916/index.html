Twitter\u2019s founder and former top boss Jack Dorsey slammed the company\u2019s board of directors on Sunday \u2013 even as the embattled executives are locked in a dispute over whether to accept billionaire Elon Musk\u2019s $43 billion offer to buy the social media platform.<\/p>\n

Dorsey criticized the board in response to a post in which a user quipped that the company\u2019s \u201cearly beginning\u201d was \u201cmired in plots and coups\u201d among its founding executives.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s consistently been the dysfunction of the company,\u201d Dorsey said.<\/p>\n

Later, when asked by another user if he was \u201callowed to say this\u201d about Twitter\u2019s board, Dorsey replied \u201cno.\u201d<\/p>\n

In another tweet, Dorsey responded to a user who quoted venture capitalist Fred Destin as praising a \u201cSilicon Valley proverb\u201d that holds \u201cGood boards don\u2019t create good companies, but a bad board will kill a company every time.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cBig facts,\u201d Dorsey responded.<\/p>\n

Jack Dorsey stepped down as Twitter\u2019s CEO last November.Bloomberg via Getty Images Jack Dorsey owns just over 2% of Twitter shares.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\n

it\u2019s consistently been the dysfunction of the company<\/p>\n

\u2014 jack\ufe0f (@jack) April 17, 2022<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

The tweets were some of the most direct criticisms to date from Dorsey, who resigned from his second stint as Twitter\u2019s CEO last November. Dorsey is also leaving Twitter\u2019s board of directors when his current term expires at the company\u2019s annual shareholder meeting in late May.<\/p>\n

Dorsey has yet to comment directly on Musk\u2019s offer to buy Twitter.<\/p>\n

Musk offered to buy Twitter at $54.20 per share last week after backing out of an agreement to take a seat on the company\u2019s board following disagreements over the company\u2019s future. Before that agreement fell apart, Dorsey tweeted that he was \u201creally happy\u201d Musk would be joining the board.<\/p>\n

\n

I\u2019m really happy Elon is joining the Twitter board! He cares deeply about our world and Twitter\u2019s role in it.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk has offered to buy Twitter for $43 billion.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, Musk stepped up his own criticism of Twitter\u2019s board after it enacted a \u201cpoison pill\u201d provision to limit his ability to acquire more shares. The Tesla CEO has demanded that Twitter\u2019s board allow shareholders to vote on his offer.<\/p>\n

The billionaire responded to a user who shared a chart showing that Twitter chairman Bret Taylor and other board members only owned a tiny portion of the company\u2019s stock.<\/p>\n

\u201cWow, with Jack departing, the Twitter board collectively owns almost no shares! Objectively, their economic interests are simply not aligned with shareholders,\u201d Musk said.<\/p>\n

\n

Board salary will be $0 if my bid succeeds, so that\u2019s ~$3M\/year saved right there<\/p>\n

\u2014 Elon Musk (@elonmusk) April 18, 2022<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

In another tweet, Musk indicated that he would cut all board member salaries to zero if his offer is accepted. Investor Gary Black had suggested that Twitter board members could be opposing Musk\u2019s bid because they stood to lose a payday.<\/p>\n

Musk owns approximately 9% of Twitter shares, while Dorsey owns just over 2.2%, according to FactSet data cited by CNBC.<\/p>\n
