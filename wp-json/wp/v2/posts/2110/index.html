Perfecting the right look takes time \u2014 and no one knows that better than fashion icon Sarah Jessica Parker.<\/p>\n

The 57-year-old actress is known for her wild and intricate Met Gala looks over the years. And she\u2019s not afraid to spend most of her year \u2014 seven to 10 months, to be exact \u2014 getting her lewks<\/em> ready in time for fashion\u2019s biggest night.<\/p>\n

Vogue recently asked the \u201cSex and the City\u201d star to take a look back at her Met Gala outfits spanning from 1995 to 2018 in a video shared on Monday.<\/p>\n

\u201cAll I ever think about is the theme. And influence,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

\u201cWherever I go to the Met, I don\u2019t understand how everyone else didn\u2019t spend seven to 10 months working on it,\u201d Parker continued.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m like, \u2018How do you not arrive exhausted by the details of getting it right?\u2019\u201d she added. \u201cAll these people came together and worked really really hard to put together an extraordinary exhibit.\u201d<\/p>\n

She emphasized the importance of focusing on the event\u2019s theme.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

\u201cIt would be so easy to find a beautiful dress to wear that night, like, that would be a great relief, like going on vacation!\u201d she quipped. \u201cBut that is not the assignment. The assignment is the theme.\u201d<\/p>\n

She also noted that dressing up for the Anna Wintour-fronted bash is no easy task. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt is an assignment and you should interpret it, and it should be labor-intensive, and it should be challenging,\u201d Parker said.<\/p>\n

Sarah Jessica Parker wears an Alexander McQueen dress at the Metropolitan Museum of Art Costume Institute Benefit Gala: Anglomania on May 1, 2006.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cSometimes the theme eludes you \u2014 like, you could spend days \u2014 and I have had conversations like, \u2018What does this theme mean?\u2019\u201d the \u201cFailure to Launch\u201d actress went on. \u201cWe\u2019ve had some very abstract themes, where you\u2019re like: \u2018How do you interpret that?\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

In the same interview, Parker reminisced about her time working with the late British fashion designer Alexander McQueen, who died by suicide in 2010. He dressed her in 2006 when the two wore matching plaid looks.<\/p>\n

Sarah Jessica Parker attends the 2013 gala at the Metropolitan Museum of Art.Getty Images <\/p>\n

I summoned the courage or whatever was needed and I reached out and asked, \u2018Could I be with him that night?\u2019 and he said, \u2018Yes,\u2019\u201d Parker said of approaching him to collaborate for the event.<\/p>\n
