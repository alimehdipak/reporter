If music-lovers had \u201cOne Wish\u201d\u00a0\u2014 it\u2019d be that Omarion and Mario\u2019s Verzuz battle wasn\u2019t such a \u201cs\u2013t show.\u201d<\/p>\n

Frustrated fans of early-2000s R&B took up their virtual torches and pitchforks on Twitter Thursday, bashing the hours-long livestream face-off between the two vocal giants. <\/p>\n

The \u201cchaotic\u201d event, held at the Novo Theater in downtown Los Angeles, started with an ill-fated performance by a seemingly inebriated Ray J, 41, and ended with several sour musical notes.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThat was 4.5 hours of pure CHAOS.\u00a0And we sat here and watched all 4.5 hours,\u201d tweeted a disgruntled viewer.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Social media hailed Mario the clear winner of his Verzuz battle against Omarion. MediaPunch \/ BACKGRID <\/p>\n

The highly anticipated toe-to-toe between \u201cLet Me Love You\u201d crooner Mario, 35, and B2K member-turned-soloist, Omarion, 37, opened with an unhinged showcase featuring Ray J and fellows soulful singers Bobby Valentino, 42, Pleasure P, 37, and Sammie, 35.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The collection of hit-makers each belted out\u00a0their most iconic chart-toppers, including \u201cMrs. Officer,\u201d \u201cGrind With Me\u201d and \u201cI Like It.\u201d<\/p>\n

However, Ray J\u2019s rendition of his 2005 hit \u201cOne Wish\u201d left online audience members wishing he never picked up the microphone.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cRay J literally had ONE job last night at that Verzuz, One Wish if you will, and that wish was not fulfilled,\u201d one keyboard commentator criticized.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cRay J need his one wish to be some singing lessons cause what happened lol,\u201d joked another virtual tomato-thrower.\u00a0<\/p>\n
