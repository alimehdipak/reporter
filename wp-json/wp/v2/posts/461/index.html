As companies across the country launch their Return to Work efforts to transition from remote work back to the office, many people are feeling overwhelmed. Never mind tackling the daily commute \u2014 after two years of sweatpants, how are they supposed to get dressed? Is business casual still the way to go, or are there new rules?\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cYou can wear comfortable stylish clothes and feel good about yourself,\u201d says Brenda Cooper, author of the new book \u201cThe Silhouette Solution: Using What You Have to Get the Look That You Want\u201d (Clarkson Potter.) \u201cStyle isn\u2019t about Neiman Marcus. It\u2019s about knowing what the right pieces are, coordinating them into simple silhouettes that flatter your body, and then add an overpiece. So you can find your style ID. Dressing is not a complicated task. You can look and feel good when you go out the door.\u201d<\/p>\n

Author Brenda Cooper says you don\u2019t need to buy a whole new wardrobe when you return to the office \u2014\u00a0just focus on a few pieces that fit well. Cooper is a costume designer and stylist who has worked with everyone from Fran Drescher (pictured) to William Shatner. Getty Images <\/p>\n

Cooper is a costume designer and stylist who has worked with everyone from Fran Drescher to William Shatner. She created the solution years ago on the set of \u201cThe Nanny\u201d as a way of dealing with a small budget \u2014 and has sworn by it ever since.\u00a0<\/p>\n

For the return to office, she insists it\u2019s not necessary to go out and buy a whole new wardrobe \u2014\u00a0it\u2019s just about finding your Style ID, and adding to it. She recommends that everyone own a pair of straight-legged pants and a pair of jeans \u2014 \u201cstraight because it\u2019s universally flattering and comfortable,\u201d she says, in addition to a pencil skirt and a maxi-skirt. For tops, everyone should own a tank top, a T-shirt shape, \u201cand what I call an ultimate neckline \u2014\u00a0a square neckline. It looks good on anyone. You\u2019re building your silhouettes this way,\u201d says Cooper, \u201cand you can do it in black, brown, or navy.\u201d Top it off with an over layer \u2014 a blazer, or a light jacket, and you\u2019re good to go.\u00a0<\/p>\n

It works across the board. You can find it on any budget from Target to Neiman Marcus.
