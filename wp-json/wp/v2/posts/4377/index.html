Alexander Volkanovski has won 22 straight fights, the last 12 of them in the UFC. Last Saturday at UFC 276, the featherweight champion defeated former champ Max Holloway for the third time. It was a shockingly dominant performance by the Australian fighter, one worthy of a boost in the ESPN pound-for-pound MMA rankings.<\/p>\n

Volkanovski even put a dent in the supremacy of UFC welterweight champ Kamaru Usman, who for several months has garnered every first-place vote in the men’s top 10. One voter, Brett Okamoto, now gives Volkanovski the nod for the top spot.<\/p>\n

“If I had to bet my life on one fighter in the world getting me a win right now, it’s easily Alex,” Okamoto said.<\/p>\n

Another voter, Jeff Wagenheim, was so impressed by Volkanovski’s thrashing of Holloway that he moved him from a tie for No. 4 up to No. 2. “What Volk did over the weekend,” Wagenheim said, “no one does to Max.”<\/p>\n

The voting panel also welcomes a new member, former UFC featherweight and ex-Invicta FC champ Megan Anderson, now a fight analyst for ESPN.<\/p>\n
