<\/p>\n

It\u2019s a Barbie world; we\u2019re just living in it.<\/p>\n

Thanks to buzz generated by Greta Gerwig\u2019s in-progress film, \u201cBarbie,\u201d being bleach blond and rocking hot pink ensembles is no longer limited to the iconic Mattel doll.<\/p>\n

Plenty of Barbie girls, inspired by the movie\u2019s star, Margot Robbie, and her parade of pretty pink outfits, have taken over TikTok, bringing the Barbiecore trend to life.<\/p>\n

The hashtag #Barbiecore has more than 7 million views on TikTok and, according to Google Trends data, interest in Barbie has spiked to new heights as fans wait for the live-action movie, set to be released next year.<\/p>\n

But it\u2019s Robbie\u2019s uncanny resemblance to the original blond Barbie that has hordes of people \u2014 and celebrities \u2014 thinking pink.<\/p>\n

Photos of the 31-year-old Australian babe riding in a pink corvette and rollerblading at Venice Beach \u2014 with Robbie fashioned in all pink, of course \u2014 had fans reeling on TikTok, where the hashtag #MargotRobbieBarbie racked up 4.2 million views in a matter of days.<\/p>\n

Robbie, who stars alongside Ryan Gosling in \u201cBarbie,\u201d has inspired TikTok\u2019s #MargotRobbieBarbie hashtag, which has racked up more than 4 million views.APEX \/ MEGA <\/p>\n

\u201cI just wanna say that seeing these paparazzi photos of the filming of \u2018Barbie\u2019 makesme wanna see this movie so freakin\u2019 bad,\u201d TikToker Kirbie Johnson gushed in a clip with over 325,000 views.<\/p>\n

And it\u2019s not just women clamoring for Barbie, everyone\u2019s favorite childhood toy who celebrated her 60th anniversary in 2019. Marvel\u2019s \u201cShang-Chi\u201d star, Simu Liu, signed onto the film after his agent called the \u201cBarbie\u201d script one of the best he\u2019d ever read and convinced him to audition for a role.<\/p>\n

Kim Kardashian had to get in on the fun, rocking an all-pink get-up just a few weeks ago. <\/p>\n

\u201cHe literally said this verbatim,\u201d Liu\u00a0told GQ UK. \u201cHe was like, \u2018If I could stake my career on any one script, it\u2019s the \u201cBarbie\u201d script. I really think you should do it.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

Director Gerwig, known for her Oscar-nominated \u201cLady Bird,\u201d is putting a new spin on the plastic classic. While people might think they know what\u2019s in store for a flick about Barbie, a doll created in 1959, Gerwig\u2019s may not be the piece of cinema viewers are expecting.<\/p>\n

Megan Fox turned into a real-life Barbie with her Ken, also known as Machine Gun Kelly.GC Images <\/p>\n

\u201cI wish I could just show you what we do day to day because it\u2019s crazy,\u201d Liu said of filming, describing the set as \u201cwild\u201d and \u201cincredibly unique.\u201d<\/p>\n

While Gerwig is thinking of Barbie outside the doll box, Robbie\u2019s costumes, designed by Gerwig\u2019s \u201cLittle Women\u201d\u00a0collaborator Jacqueline Durran, for which she won an Oscar, are hitting the streets too. The toy\u2019s aesthetic has entered the fashion zeitgeist, inspiring and A-listers and everyday dolls alike.<\/p>\n

Lizzo stunned in Valentino\u2019s color of the season earlier this month.Getty Images for Prime Video <\/p>\n

On Monday, Megan Fox rocked a rendition of the fuchsia fashion trend \u2013 making an IRL Barbie-and-Ken moment with fianc\u00e9 Machine Gun Kelly.<\/p>\n

No strangers to being on top of trends, Khlo\u00e9 Kardashian was a Barbie girl for her birthday on Tuesday, while sister Kim, now a bleach blond, shared a snap on Instagram earlier in June of her wrapped in a skintight pink pants-and-heals combo, a matching cropped hoodie and, of course, a Balenciaga bag.<\/p>\n

\u201cI would say it\u2019s definitely, like, future alien Barbie vibes,\u201d the Skkn founder said of her style muse last week.<\/p>\n

Hailey Bieber had her own bombshell Barbie moment, posing for a sexy Instagram snap on Wednesday in a bubblegum pink corseted minidress from Versace\u2019s upcoming fall collection.<\/p>\n

Even brunettes have been tickled pink. Lizzo donned a magenta peplum dress with matching Valentino gloves and tights, along with a sleek, doll-like high pony at an event for her reality show, \u201cWatch Out for the Big Grrrls,\u201d in early June. <\/p>\n

Fellas are getting in on the trend too \u2014 and not just Robbie\u2019s co-star Ryan Gosling, who has embodied Barbie\u2019s beau Ken in photos from the set. Along with Machine Gun Kelly, Sebastian Stan rocked a Valentino number in a playful pink shade at the Met Gala.<\/p>\n

When Valentino\u2019s fall collection debuted in March, the line of catwalkers were in a showstopping magenta.Vianney Le Caer\/Invision\/AP <\/p>\n

Celebs and influencers aren\u2019t the only ones passionate about pink, either.<\/p>\n

\u201cBarbieCore is the summer\u2019s\u00a0latest fashion trend\u00a0influencing everything\u00a0from clothing to home decor, and we are here for it,\u201d Kim Culmone, senior vice president and global head of design Barbie & fashion dolls\u00a0at Mattel Inc., told The Post. \u201cFashion is a form of self-expression and BarbieCore celebrates living\u00a0boldly and\u00a0authentically. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s been delightful seeing celebrities decked out in their best pink\u00a0looks\u00a0\u2013 Barbie would approve,\u201d Culmone declared.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Even high fashion is heralding the hue, declaring pink the new black. Valentino designer Pierpaolo Piccioli debuted a perfectly pink collection in partnership with Pantone for his fall collection this year. At the catwalk, even the backdrop, floors, seats and runway were the same shade,\u00a0letting the palette dazzle.<\/p>\n

\u201cPinks are no doubt \u2018having a moment.\u2019\u00a0In fact, pink is having more than a moment,\u201d Laurie Pressman, vice president of the Pantone Color Institute, told The Post. \u201cIt is a color family we have seen growing in popularity across the spectrum since 2013, one which sparked the intro of Millennial Pink and with the rise of the \u2018gender blur\u2019 became even more prominent.\u00a0A time where we began doing away with all color rules and breaking down the boundaries.\u201d<\/p>\n

Pressman suggested that today\u2019s pink explosion is about self-expression and, especially, release.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe bright pinks and fuchsias we are seeing today are exultant and empowering. They are stand-out statements being worn with confidence,\u201d Pressman continued. \u201cVibrant and high-energy. they help us to feel uninhibited and free.\u201d<\/p>\n

Or, as the Italian house Valentino told Forbes, it is \u201ca liberation from the need for realism.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sebastian Stan donned a hot pink statement outfit to the Met Gala in May.Patrick McMullan via Getty Image <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, fashion stylist\u00a0Shea Daspin, who styled Zoe Margaret Colletti in head-to-toe pink recently, told InStyle that Barbie \u201crepresents a lot more\u201d than the color pink.<\/p>\n

\u201cShe represents ambition and female agency,\u201d Daspin said, \u201cand that can manifest in how someone dresses, or who they decide they want to become.\u201d<\/p>\n

And the answer to the latter may be fairly simple.<\/p>\n
