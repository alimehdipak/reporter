Character actor Mike Hagerty \u2014 who memorably played roles on \u201cFriends\u201d and the series \u201cSomebody, Somewhere\u201d \u2014 has died. He was 67.<\/p>\n

His cause of death has not been revealed, though a statement from his \u201cSomebody, Somewhere\u201d co-star Bridget Everett was shared on social media.<\/p>\n

\u201cWith great sadness, the family of Michael G. Hagerty announced his death yesterday in Los Angeles,\u201d Everett posted on\u00a0Instagram\u00a0Friday. <\/p>\n

\u201cWe are very saddened to hear about the sudden passing of Mike Hagerty,\u201d HBO said in a statement. \u201cA member of the HBO family for many years, his most recent role as Bridget Everett\u2019s father in \u2018Somebody Somewhere\u201d showed his special talent for bringing heart to a performance. Mike was a joy to work with and brought warmth and kindness to all who knew him. We send our deepest condolences to his family and friends.\u201d<\/p>\n

In her tribute, Everett called Hagerty \u201ca beloved character actor, his love of his hometown of Chicago and his family were the cornerstones of his life.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cMike, a devoted husband, is survived by his wife Mary Kathryn, his sister Mary Ann Hagerty [and] her wife Kathleen O\u2019Rourke, and their daughter Meg. He will be sorely missed,\u201d she wrote.<\/p>\n

Character actor Mike Hagerty has died.\u00a920thCentFox\/Courtesy Everett David Steinberg and Mike Hagerty perform onstage during the Second City 25th Anniversary performance at the Vic Theater, Chicago, Illinois, December 16, 1984.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Hagerty was best known for playing building superintendent Mr. Treeger on the \u201990s NBC sitcom \u201cFriends.\u201d<\/p>\n

In the HBO comedy \u201cSomebody, Somewhere,\u201d Hagerty portrayed farmer Ed Miller, the father of Everett\u2019s character.<\/p>\n

He appeared on a plethora of TV series over the course of his career, including guest spots on \u201cBoston Legal,\u201d \u201cBrooklyn Nine-Nine,\u201d \u201cCheers,\u201d \u201cCommunity,\u201d \u201cCSI,\u201d \u201cCurb Your Enthusiasm,\u201d \u201cEntourage,\u201d \u201cGlee,\u201d \u201cGrey\u2019s Anatomy,\u201d \u201cHappy Endings,\u201d \u201cShameless,\u201d \u201cSeinfeld\u201d and many more.<\/p>\n

He stayed true to the sitcom genre with regular roles on Fox\u2019s\u00a0\u201cThe George Carlin Show\u201d in 1994 and HBO\u2019s\u00a0\u201cLucky Louie,\u201d which premiered in 2006. <\/p>\n

Mike had the classic Chicago mustache and accent, and a natural onscreen presence. He always came to play.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cNo. No. No. no. Oh terrible loss and k will miss him on your show. Heartbreaking. Admired him for years. X,\u201d Sarah Jessica Parker wrote on Instagram.<\/p>\n
