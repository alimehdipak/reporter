<\/p>\n

Sen. Bernie Sanders lashed out at Elon Musk and Jeff Bezos over their space ambitions on Friday \u2013 claiming in a scathing op-ed that the two billionaires are taking advantage of federal support to pursue even greater wealth among the stars.<\/p>\n

Sanders (I-Vt.) warned that the burgeoning space race between Musk\u2019s SpaceX and Bezos\u2019 Blue Origin threatens to become a \u201cvast boondoggle\u201d that will \u201copen up outer space to corporate greed and exploitation\u201d rather than benefit the country.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am concerned that NASA has become little more than an ATM machine to fuel a space race not between the US and other countries, but between the two wealthiest men in America \u2013 Elon Musk and\u00a0Jeff Bezos, who are worth more than $450 billion combined,\u201d Sanders wrote in an op-ed for The Guardian.<\/p>\n

Musk, who has said he paid the steepest tax bill in history in 2021, has sparred with Sanders on several occasions. Musk infamously slammed the self-avowed socialist last November after the senator called for the implementation of a new tax on billionaires.<\/p>\n

\u201cI keep forgetting that you\u2019re still alive,\u201d Musk\u00a0said at the time.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk\u2019s SpaceX is a leading private space firm.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Bezos took a more tactful approach last July just before he personally participated in Blue Origin\u2019s first civilian flight \u2014 acknowledging that he felt critics who said he invest more in addressing Earth\u2019s problems were \u201clargely right.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cWe have to do both,\u201d Bezos said.<\/p>\n

NASA has engaged with SpaceX and other privately held firms in recent years as it looks to achieve a goal of completing another mission to the Moon by 2025. The federal space agency awarded a lunar lander contract to SpaceX and recently indicated it would open bidding on a second contract to other firms \u2013 with Blue Origin expected to compete for the gig.<\/p>\n

Jeff Bezos\u2019 Blue Origin has conducted several civilian flights on its shuttles.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Sanders has vocally opposed a provision in Congress that would $10 billion in additional federal funding toward the development of lunar landers, Politico reported last month.<\/p>\n

\u201cAt a time when over half of the people in this country live paycheck to paycheck, when more than 70 million are uninsured or underinsured and when some 600,000 Americans are homeless, should we really be providing a multibillion-dollar taxpayer bailout for Bezos to fuel his space hobby? I don\u2019t think so,\u201d Sanders said.<\/p>\n

SpaceX and Blue Origin did not immediately return requests for comment.<\/p>\n

Blue Origin is set to bid for a second NASA lunar lander contractAFP via Getty Images SpaceX was awarded a NASA contract to build a lunar lander.APJohn Raoux <\/p>\n

Bezos and Musk are heated rivals in an increasingly competitive private space exploration sector. Both SpaceX and Blue Origin have conducted flights with civilian passengers as part of their space tourism efforts.<\/p>\n

Earlier this month, Blue Origin unveiled a plan to conduct as many as 83 satellite launches over a five-year period as part of its secretive \u201cProject Kuiper\u201d broadband internet project \u2013 a service that will directly complete with SpaceX\u2019s Starlink.<\/p>\n

And before NASA opened up bidding for a second lunar lander contract, Blue Origin filed and lost a federal lawsuit accusing the agency of improperly awarding the first deal to SpaceX.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe time is now to have a serious debate in Congress and throughout our country as to how to develop a rational space policy that does not simply socialize all of the risks and privatize all of the profits,\u201d Sanders added.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
