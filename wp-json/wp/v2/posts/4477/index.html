During their 15 years of marriage, Ivana Trump played a pivotal role in creating Donald Trump\u2019s empire and made up one-half of the quintessential power couple of the 1980s.<\/p>\n

Ivana,\u00a0born Ivana Marie Zeln\u00ed\u010dkov\u00e1, married the former president in 1977 and they divorced in 1992. They had three children together: Donald Jr., Ivanka and Eric.<\/p>\n

But during their time together the pair were a fixture in the tabloids and New York\u2019s social scene until their fiery divorce in 1992. <\/p>\n

And she was often credited with helping build both their public image and the family business.<\/p>\n

Ivana \u2014 who died Thursday at age 73 \u2014 was a competitive skier and model and also held\u00a0many roles in Trump family businesses after she married the now-former president, becoming successful business partners as well as husband and wife.<\/p>\n

She held many positions in the Trump Organization, including managing the iconic Plaza Hotel in Manhattan.<\/p>\n

While Trump built his Manhattan empire, his wife worked alongside, him helming the interior designs in some of Trump\u2019s most iconic buildings. <\/p>\n

Ivana and Donald Trump were married for 15 years and share three children.Corbis via Getty Images <\/p>\n

She worked alongside her husband in developing his signature building \u2014 Trump Tower \u2014 on Fifth Avenue in Manhattan, as well as other high-profile projects including the Trump Taj Mahal Casino Resort in Atlantic City, New Jersey, according to The Cut.<\/p>\n

In 1985, she became the CEO of\u00a0Trump\u2019s Castle\u00a0casino resort in Atlantic City and managed the Plaza hotel after he bought it in 1988.<\/p>\n

In a 1988 interview with Vanity Fair, she was described as being highly competitive and determined to prove her role in her husband\u2019s business.<\/p>\n

\u201cDonald calls me his twin as a woman,\u201d she told the publication at the time. <\/p>\n

Despite their public and messy divorce, the two rekindled their friendship.Getty Images <\/p>\n

According to Donald Trump biographer Wayne Barrett, an example of her dedication to her role in the Trump business was when she went to an Italian quarry to pick out the rosy-beige Breccia Perniche marble that adorns the floors and walls of Trump Tower.<\/p>\n

Trump later made a cameo appearance in the 1996 hit film \u201cThe First Wives Club\u201d with a tongue-in-cheek nod to her divorce, uttering the now-famous line: \u201cLadies, you have to be strong and independent, and remember: don\u2019t get mad \u2014 get everything!\u201d<\/p>\n

The two divorced in 1992, but she kept her ex-husband\u2019s surname. <\/p>\n

Ivana was discovered at the bottom of a staircase in her home in Manhattan at 12:40 p.m. on Thursday. Police sources told The Post that they believe she suffered cardiac arrest, but the official cause of death has yet to be determined<\/p>\n

In a\u00a0statement, the Trump family called Ivana \u201ca force in business, a world-class athlete, a radiant beauty, and caring mother and friend.<\/p>\n

The Trumps became partners in love and business.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cIvana Trump was a survivor. She fled from communism and embraced this country. She taught her children about grit and toughness, compassion and determination,\u201d it continued. <\/p>\n
