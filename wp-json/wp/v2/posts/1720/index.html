      USC women’s basketball commit Aaliyah Gayles was shot multiple times at a house party in North Las Vegas over the weekend, the school confirmed.<\/p>\n

      Gayles, 18, was one of four people shot after a fight occurred inside the residence, the Las Vegas Review-Journal reported, citing police. The four people were hospitalized with survivable gunshot wounds, per the Review-Journal.<\/p>\n

      Gayles had two emergency surgeries Sunday morning, sources told the newspaper.<\/p>\n

      Aaliyah Gayles is a point guard for Las Vegas’ Spring Valley High School and recently played in the Jordan Brand Classic.\u00a0Chris Kohley\/Icon Sportswire<\/p>\n

      Gayles, a point guard who attends Las Vegas’ Spring Valley High School, is the No. 8 recruit in the class of 2022, according to ESPN HoopGurlz rankings, and recently played in the McDonald’s All American Game and Jordan Brand Classic.<\/p>\n

      “I know there is some that will say it’s my fault and I take full responsibility for it but please please keep my baby girl in your prayers Las Vegas,” Gayles’ father, Dwight, tweeted Sunday night. “I will give everyone that support Aaliyah an update but she’s ok she go make it like always thank you all, true warrior.”<\/p>\n

      Gayles had decommitted from USC following the retirement of former coach Mark Trakh before recommitting to the Trojans under coach Lindsay Gottlieb in August.<\/p>\n

      “Aaliyah is one of the strongest, most resilient young people I have ever known,” Gottlieb said in a statement to ESPN on Sunday night. “I have no doubt she will continue to face this unfathomable situation with courage and resolve.
