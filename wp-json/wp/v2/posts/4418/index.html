Actor Tony Sirico \u2014 best known for his role as mobster Paulie Walnuts on \u201cThe Sopranos\u201d \u2014 is dead, his brother, Robert, announced on Facebook on Friday. He was 79. <\/p>\n

\u200b\u201dIt is with great sadness, but with incredible pride, love and a whole lot of fond memories, that the family of Gennaro Anthony \u2018Tony\u2019 Sirico wishes to inform you of his death on the morning of July 8, 2022,\u201d wrote Robert, who is a priest.<\/p>\n

\u201cTony is survived by his two beloved children, Joanne Sirico Bello and Richard Sirico, grandchildren, siblings, nieces, nephews and many other relatives.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe family is deeply grateful for the many expressions of love, prayer and condolences and requests that the public respect its privacy in this time of bereavement.\u201d<\/p>\n

His \u201cSopranos\u201d co-star Michael Imperioli \u2014 who played Christopher Moltisanti, prot\u00e9g\u00e9 to Tony Soprano, portrayed by the late James Gandolfini \u2014 also posted a remembrance on Instagram, saying in part: \u201cTony was like no one else: he was as tough, as loyal and as big hearted as anyone i\u2019ve ever known.\u201d<\/p>\n

Another co-star, Steven Van Zandt, who played tough guy Silvio Dante, tweeted his condolences to the actor\u2019s family.<\/p>\n

\u201cA larger-than-life character on and off-screen. Gonna miss you a lot my friend,\u201d he wrote. <\/p>\n

Their \u201cheartbroken\u201d co-star Jamie Lynn Sigler also posted to social media about the special bond she felt for her colleague and friend.<\/p>\n

\u201cI loved you so much. I have never been able to walk into a room that you were in without you giving me an enormous hug, drenching me in your cologne, and making sure I knew any man that came near me would have to answer to you first,\u201d she wrote, sharing a photo of them together.<\/p>\n

Sources told TMZ that he had been living in an assisted living home in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, for a few years due his declining health.<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out to Sirico\u2019s representatives for confirmation. <\/p>\n

When he was 55, Sirico was sleeping on a cot in his mother\u2019s living room in Brooklyn when he was cast as Walnuts. He originally auditioned for the role of Uncle Junior \u2014 who would be played by Dominic Chianese. <\/p>\n

Sirico and Walnuts\u2019 lives had many parallels. Born in Brooklyn, New York, on July 29, 1942, the late actor was just 7 years old the first time he got arrested for stealing nickels from a newspaper stand. He would be arrested 28 more times and would and two trips to the slammer.<\/p>\n

Tony Sirico attends SopranosCon 2019 at Meadowlands Exposition Center on Nov. 23, 2019.Getty Images <\/p>\n

In 1971, he\u00a0even spent 20 months\u00a0at the notorious Sing Sing prison for felony weapons possession.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe first time I went away to prison, they searched me to see if I had a gun \u2014 and I had three of \u2019em on me,\u201d he told the LA Times in 1990.<\/p>\n

\u201cIn our neighborhood, if you weren\u2019t carrying a gun, it was like you were the rabbit during rabbit-hunting season.\u201d<\/p>\n

James Gandolfini and Tony Sirico as Tony Soprano and Paulie Walnuts. <\/p>\n

The hit HBO crime drama ran from 1999 to 2007.\u00a0 He won two Screen Actors Guild Awards for his ensemble role: one in 2000 and another in 2008.<\/p>\n

In a 2020 interview, Sirico\u00a0praised his former \u201cSopranos\u201d co-star Gandolfini, who died in 2013 at age 51, calling the actor his \u201cright f\u2013king arm\u201d and saying he was \u201cso f\u2013king happy to be as close\u201d as the two were.<\/p>\n

In his decades-long career, Sirico appeared in numerous guest roles on other shows including \u201cKojak,\u201d \u201cMiami Vice,\u201d \u201cChuck,\u201d \u201cMedium,\u201d \u201cLilyhammer\u201d and \u201cAmerican Dad.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tony Sirico at \u201cThe Sopranos: The Complete Fifth Season\u201d DVD launch party on June 6, 2005.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Sirico played gangsters in over a dozen movies, including \u201cGoodfellas,\u201d \u201cMob Queen,\u201d \u201cMighty Aphrodite\u201d and \u201cGotti.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI make a pretty good living because I die well. I get hired to get killed,\u201d he once told the LA Times, estimating that he died in 13 of his then-27 films. <\/p>\n

Additionally, he temporarily lent his voice to \u201cFamily Guy\u201d character Vinny Griffin \u2014 who was the family\u2019s dog for three episodes in 2013.<\/p>\n

Sirico and former \u201cSopranos\u201d actors Federico Castelluccio\u00a0and\u00a0Vincent Pastore\u00a0reunited in the 2018 film\u00a0\u201cSarah Q.\u201d<\/p>\n
