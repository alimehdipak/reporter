Paul Wesley is trading in his fangs for a spaceship.<\/p>\n

The \u201cVampire Diaries\u201d star, 39, will be taking on the part of Captain James T.\u00a0Kirk in Paramount+\u2018s \u201cStar Trek: Strange New Worlds.\u201d<\/p>\n

Wesley was in for quite a surprise on Tuesday when he found himself sitting next to Captain Kirk himself, William Shatner, on a plane.<\/p>\n

The \u201cTell Me A Story\u201d actor tweeted out a snap of the two men smiling from behind their masks on the aircraft.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am deeply humbled and still a little startled to have been given the honor of playing the inimitable James T Kirk,\u201d Wesley wrote. <\/p>\n

\u201cEver since I was a kid, I have been awed by the imaginative world Gene Roddenberry created,\u201d he noted. \u201cRecently, I boarded a flight to LA to discover that the man in the empty seat beside me was the one and only William Shatner, having himself just returned from space.\u201d<\/p>\n

Shatner, 90, and Wesley, 39, bumped into each other on a plane.Paul Wesley\/ Twitter <\/p>\n

Shatner, 90, flew to the edge of space in October 2021 and became the oldest person\u00a0to have ever traveled to the great beyond.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In a follow-up tweet, Wesley added, \u201cI could barely put two words together but ultimately I managed to say hello and we chatted. I\u2019m not one who usually believes in fate but this was more than a coincidence.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd of course, I had to get a picture. So thanks Mr. Shatner for the good company. And for seating us together, my thanks to the great bird in the sky. Can\u2019t wait for all of you to see our Captain Kirk on your screens,\u201d he continued.<\/p>\n

While the Polish American actor is set to appear in Season 2 of \u201cStar Trek: Strange New Worlds,\u201d the show itself has yet to premiere its first season. The series is set to drop this May.<\/p>\n

Wesley dressed as Captain James T. Kirk in Season 2 of \u201cStar Trek: Strange New Worlds.\u201dParamount+ <\/p>\n

The series will\u00a0chronicle Capt. Christopher Pike (Anson Mount), Science Officer Spock (Ethan Peck) and Number One (Rebecca Romijn\/Una Chin-Riley) as they investigate new places in the galaxy. <\/p>\n

Other members of the cast include Jess Bush as Nurse Christine Chapel, Christina Chong as La\u2019an Noonien-Singh, Celia Rose Gooding as Cadet Nyota Uhura, Melissa Navia as Lt. Erica Ortegas and Babs Olusanmokun as Dr. M\u2019Benga.<\/p>\n
