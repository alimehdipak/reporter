Wynonna Judd opened up about \u201cthe pain\u201d of losing her mother and longtime singing partner Naomi Judd to suicide. <\/p>\n

\u201cThere is so much happening in the world right now. So before I sat down to write this, I thought, \u2018No\u2026I just don\u2019t know what to say.\u2019 Then, I heard the words from my life coach asking me, \u2018What do you know?\u2019 And I began to cry,\u201d Wynonna, 58, wrote in an emotional Instagram post. <\/p>\n

The country music icon, 76, died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound on April 30 after struggling with her mental health for several years.<\/p>\n

Wynonna Judd (left) said \u201cthe pain\u201d of losing her mother Naomi Judd \u201cto suicide is so great\u201d in an emotional Instagram post. Getty Images for CMT <\/p>\n

\u201cWHAT DO I KNOW?? I DO know, that the pain of losing Mom on 4\/30 to suicide is so great, that I often feel like I\u2019m not ever going to be able to fully accept and surrender to the truth that she left the way she did. This cannot be how The Judds story ends,\u201d she wrote.<\/p>\n

Wynonna wrote, \u201cI often feel like I\u2019m not ever going to be able to fully accept and surrender to the truth that she left the way she did.\u201dAP <\/p>\n

\u201cI DO know, that in order to be a healthier grandparent to my firstborn grandchild Kaliyah, {born 4\/13, 2 weeks & 2 days before Mom left}, to break the cycle of addiction & family dysfunction, that I must continue to show up for myself {first} and do the personal healing work,\u201d Wynonna said. <\/p>\n

She acknowledged that there is \u201ca simple steps program\u201d but noted that \u201cthose steps are not easy to take at times.\u201d Yet, the grieving daughter has \u201cmade a commitment to keep doing the \u201cnext right thing,\u201d and \u201cschedule weekly appointments\u201d to continue to work through the pain. <\/p>\n

The singer continued, \u201cI DO know, that I feel so helpless \u2014 right now especially.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI DO know, that as corny as it sounds, \u2018Love Can Build A Bridge.\u2019 I find myself humming the song that Mom wrote for the fans, to myself here on the farm at night,\u201d she added. \u201cI really DO know, that I\u2019m not able to do this grieving thing all by myself, and that it\u2019s okay to reach out for help. I will continue to fight for my faith, for my SELF, for my family, and I WILL continue to show up & sing.\u201d<\/p>\n

The country music star announced that she will continue the national tour she and her mother had previously scheduled. Getty Images <\/p>\n

Wynonna ended the post by thanking her fans and friends for their love and support. <\/p>\n

She previously announced that she will continue the 11-date national tour she had scheduled with her mom before her untimely death.<\/p>\n

\u201cAfter a lot of thought, I\u2019m going to have to honor her and do this tour. I\u2019m just going to have to,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

Naomi\u2019s daughter Ashley Judd, 54, revealed that she was the one to find her mother\u2019s body. \u201cI have both grief and trauma from discovering her,\u201d she previously shared on \u201cGood Morning America.\u201d<\/p>\n
