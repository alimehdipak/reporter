Dolly Parton is the country gift that keeps on givin\u2019.<\/p>\n

The \u201c9 to 5\u201d legend has donated $1 million to pediatric infectious disease research at Vanderbilt University Medical Center in Nashville, the organization announced Wednesday. <\/p>\n

According to the center, the gift will help doctors with their research in understanding \u201chow viruses and bacteria cause disease, understanding and preventing resistance to antibiotics, preventing and treating infections, diagnosing and treating infections in children with cancer and research to define the impact of childhood infections throughout the world.\u201d<\/p>\n

The country legend alluded to doing what she could to end the suffering of kids in a statement.<\/p>\n

\u201cI love all children.\u00a0No child should ever have to suffer,\u00a0and I\u2019m willing to do my part to try and keep as many of them as I can as healthy and safe\u00a0as\u00a0possible,\u201d Parton said.<\/p>\n

This isn\u2019t the first time Parton, 76, has graciously helped Vanderbilt. Fans joked that she \u201ccured the coronavirus\u201d when she gave $1 million to scientists involved in\u00a0Moderna\u2019s COVID-19 vaccine trials in April 2020.<\/p>\n

\u201cI knew something bad was on the rise and I just wanted to kind of help with that,\u201d Parton then told the Hollywood Reporter at the time.<\/p>\n

\u201cMine was a small part, of course. I probably get a lot more credit than I deserve,\u201d she added of her involvement with the vaccine. \u201cI was happy to be a part of that and to be able to try to stop something in its tracks that\u2019s really become just such a monster for all of us.\u201d<\/p>\n

Over the years, Parton has used her estimated $375 million net worth to give back to causes involving healthcare, education and equality.<\/p>\n

In 1995, she founded Imagination Library, which has donated almost 170 million books to children under age 5.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m kind of addicted to the feeling of giving,\u201d she\u00a0told People\u00a0in December 2021. \u201cKnowing that I\u2019m doing something good for someone else.\u201d<\/p>\n

The modest and cautious mogul reportedly was forced to turn down the Presidential Medal of Freedom two times due to scheduling conflicts during the coronavirus pandemic.<\/p>\n

\u201cI couldn\u2019t accept it because my husband was ill and then they asked me again about it and I wouldn\u2019t travel because of the COVID,\u201d Parton said in February 2021. <\/p>\n
