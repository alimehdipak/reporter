Blue Origin has revealed that one of its key employees will replace Pete Davidson on an upcoming flight to space, days after the \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d comedian dropped out of the mission under unclear circumstances.<\/p>\n

Gary Lai, the chief architect of Blue Origin\u2019s \u201cNew Shepard\u201d shuttle program, will be the sixth crew member on the March 29 mission \u2013 the 20th in the firm\u2019s history. Lai joined Blue Origin in 2004 and was one of the company\u2019s first employees.<\/p>\n

\u201cGary is best known as the architect of the New Shepard system, including leading the team responsible for the design and development of many of the key safety systems on the Crew Capsule,\u201d Blue Origin said in a release announcing the change.<\/p>\n

Lai will join five other crew members who were previously announced \u2014 investor Marty Allen; SpaceKids Global founder Sharon Hagle; her husband and Tricor International CEO Mark Hagle; entrepreneur Jim Kitchen and Commercial Space Technologies President George Nield.<\/p>\n

Blue Origin added that Lai is an integral part of its operations, with involvement \u201cin product development, strategic planning, and business development for all Blue Origin product lines, including the New Glenn orbital launch vehicle, rocket engine programs, and Blue Moon.\u201d<\/p>\n

Gary Lai was one of Blue Origin\u2019s first employees.Michael Baltierra <\/p>\n

Blue Origin and Davidson have yet to publicly explain why he is no longer participating in the mission. Billionaire Jeff Bezos\u2019 firm announced the launch was delayed by six days at the same time it revealed the comedian would no longer participate.<\/p>\n

While multiples sources told Page Six that Davidson had trained for the space flight, one insider questioned a possible explanation \u2013 that the comedian left the mission due to a conflict that arose with his filming schedule after the launch date changed.<\/p>\n

\u201cAs with previous flights, there are always issues with weather conditions. Before\u00a0Michael Strahan\u2019s flight, they shifted launch by about a week due to the wind. So there is always this [possibility], and Pete would have known that,\u201d the insider told Page Six. \u201cI know the party line is that it\u2019s a filming clash, but I\u2019m not too sure.\u201d<\/p>\n

Comedian Pete Davidson dropped out of the Blue Origin mission last week.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n
