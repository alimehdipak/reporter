Twitter employees who were reportedly apoplectic about the prospect of Elon Musk taking over the company got targeted with scathing memes as users poked fun at their plight.<\/p>\n

Memes depicting panic, dread, doom, and gloom were posted online as \u201cTwitter HQ\u201d was trending all morning following the SEC filing indicating Musk\u2019s $41 billion all-cash offer for the company.<\/p>\n

The famous \u201cThis is fine\u201d meme featuring a comic book dog character sipping coffee while the room is engulfed in flames went viral \u2014 the room in this case being Twitter\u2019s headquarters.<\/p>\n

Another Twitter user posted a GIF showing Ilana Glazer from the Comedy Central show \u201cBroad City\u201d screaming, indicating that this was the sentiment among Twitter employees.<\/p>\n

Others posted GIFs of Vince McMahon confidently sauntering into the wrestling arena, likening him to Musk\u2019s entrance into headquarters after he presumably buys the company.<\/p>\n

Another Twitter user said that employees there were as upset as Obama administration officials after Donald Trump defeated Hillary Clinton in the 2016 elections.<\/p>\n

One Twitter user posted a video of a scene from the Netflix drama Peaky Blinders in which actor Cillian Murphy walks out of a home that was destroyed by a blast.<\/p>\n

\u201cElon Musk walking out of Twitter HQ this morning,\u201d read the caption.<\/p>\n

Another Twitter user posted a GIF of the well-known scene from the hit NBC comedy \u201cThe Office\u201d in which Michael Scott panics as the other employees flee during a fire.<\/p>\n

Earlier this week, it was reported that Musk\u2019s looming battle with Twitter\u2019s board was a source of major anxiety for company employees \u2013 many of whom are said to be worried that billionaire\u2019s campaign to force changes is just beginning.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s surprise rejection of a Twitter board seat last weekend emerged ahead of a scheduled \u201cday of rest\u201d for company employees, according to a report.<\/p>\n

Twitter CEO Parag Agrawal broke the news on his account the previous night, describing Musk\u2019s decision as \u201cfor the best\u201d even as he warned workers they would face \u201cdistractions ahead,\u201d Bloomberg reported late Monday.<\/p>\n

The uncertainty left Twitter workers feeling \u201csuper stressed\u201d about the future, with employees reportedly \u201cworking together to help each other get through the week.\u201d<\/p>\n

The report cited interviews with Twitter employees who asked not to be identified while discussing the company\u2019s inner workings. Several employees told the outlet that Twitter\u2019s internal environment was a \u201cs\u2013t-show\u201d after Musk\u2019s deal with the board fell through.<\/p>\n

One Twitter employee griped that Musk was likely \u2018just getting started\u201d with pushing for change at the company \u2013 a development the worker described as \u201cunfortunate,\u201d according to Bloomberg.<\/p>\n

Musk appears to be enjoying himself as the drama unfolds. His $54.20 per share offer just happens to include a \u201c420\u201d reference to pot smoking.<\/p>\n
