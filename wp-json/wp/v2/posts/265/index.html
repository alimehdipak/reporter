\u201cThe Goldbergs\u201d actress Wendi McLendon-Covey has slammed her former co-star Jeff Garlin, who abruptly departed the ABC sitcom last year while still filming its ninth season.<\/p>\n

McLendon-Covey, 52, derided the 59-year-old actor as not wanting \u201cto be there\u201d on set to film scenes and suggested he would want to leave \u201cmid-scene.\u201d She shared her opinions in response to a tweet by freelance journalist Noel Murray, who suggested the series should either kill off Garlin\u2019s character, family patriarch Murray Goldberg, or should get canceled entirely, rather than repurpose already-shot footage in an attempt to make scenes gel.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey should either cancel The Goldbergs or kill off Jeff Garlin\u2019s character because the workarounds they\u2019ve been using this season ain\u2019t working,\u201d Murray wrote, adding a clip of a wedding scene that appeared heavily edited.<\/p>\n

Wendi McLendon-Covey and Jeff Garlin appear in an episode of the ABC series \u201cThe Goldbergs.\u201dABC via Getty Images <\/p>\n

His tweet brought a sharp response from McLendon-Covey.<\/p>\n

\u201cThanks for the great suggestion Noel!\u201d McLendon-Covey shaded right back before alluding to Garlin\u2019s alleged disinterest appearing in the sitcom. \u201cThis season threw us for a loop because a.) it\u2019s hard to incorporate someone who doesn\u2019t want to be there and wants to leave mid-scene, and b.) we werent about to re-write the 2nd half of the season.\u201d<\/p>\n

She concluded her retort: \u201cWe\u2019re doing our best.\u201d<\/p>\n

McLendon-Covey and ABC both declined to comment through representatives when contacted by The Post, which has also reached out for a statement from Garlin.<\/p>\n

Variety previously reported that the series would repurpose off-camera dialogue, unused takes and other footage of Garlin, as well as include the actor\u2019s stand-in \u2014 shown from the back \u2014 in group scenes.<\/p>\n

Garlin exited the series last year following allegations of misconduct and \u201cabusive\u201d behavior on set. <\/p>\n

\u201cHe is extremely verbally and emotionally abusive,\u201d an anonymous \u201cGoldbergs\u201d employee told Deadline in December.<\/p>\n

Jeff Garlin appears in a cast photo for \u201cThe Goldbergs\u201d along with Hayley Orrantia, George Segal, Wendi McLendon-Covey, Sean Giambrone and Troy Gentile.ABC via Getty Images <\/p>\n

He previously admitted in a Vanity Fair interview that he was the subject of a human resources probe into allegations over a three-year period, but he also claimed that he \u201cwas not fired\u201d from the show.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s always the same thing. It\u2019s about me and my silliness on set. They don\u2019t think it\u2019s appropriate. I do. That\u2019s where we\u2019re at. I\u2019ve not been fired because of it. We just think differently,\u201d he said during the Vanity Fair interview.<\/p>\n

Cast and crew were reportedly ruffled by Garlin\u2019s allegedly demeaning language, which Garlin himself didn\u2019t seem to understand.<\/p>\n

\u201cI gotta be honest with you. I don\u2019t even know how to respond to that, because as a comedian, if somebody is offended by what I say I, all I can say is, I\u2019m sorry. Okay? I have never physically come at anyone, for any reason, so that I find it terribly confusing and untrue,\u201d he told the publication, adding that he believed, \u201cwhoever it is that feels this way has it out for me.\u201d<\/p>\n

Garlin also said he\u2019s \u201ca person who hugs for sure\u201d in response to allegations that show staffers also were even upset with his penchant for doing that.<\/p>\n

\u201cAnd it\u2019s quick hugs, but I didn\u2019t realize that anyone was feeling \u2014 so if you want to write that story, that\u2019s a true story. It puts the onus on them to say something, and I would gladly respect anyone who said, \u2018Please don\u2019t hug me. I don\u2019t feel comfortable.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n
