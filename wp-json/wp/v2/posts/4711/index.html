Move over, Kylie Jenner. Applebee\u2019s has entered the lip gloss game. <\/p>\n

The national restaurant chain and makeup company Winky Lux have flocked together to create Saucy Gloss \u2014 four wearable shades that allegedly taste like the restaurant\u2019s wing sauces. <\/p>\n

Brave buyers can choose between the creamy coral \u201cGet Me Hot Buffalo,\u201d the gold-flecked deep red \u201cSweet Chile Kiss,\u201d the golden \u201cBe My Honey Pepper\u201d and the smoky burgundy \u201cHoney BBQ-T\u201d \u2014 all retailing for $18 each.<\/p>\n

Can\u2019t decide on a flavor? Winky Lux sells a four-piece combo kit for $65.<\/p>\n

And as if the idea couldn\u2019t seem more outrageous, the collaboration also spawned a high-gloss dance music video directed by Hannah Lux Davis, who usually works with A-listers like Doja Cat, Demi Lovato, Ariana Grande, Katy Perry and Halsey. <\/p>\n

Applebee\u2019s has entered the makeup game with wing sauce-flavored lip glosses.Winky Lux <\/p>\n

Winky Lux founder Natalie Mackey told Fast Company that she received a LinkedIn message from a rep for Applebee\u2019s a year ago, asking something along the lines of, \u201cAre you able to make lip glosses that taste like BBQ sauce?\u201d<\/p>\n

The company quickly started experimenting with food-grade ingredients \u2014 like garlic oil, chamomile oil and honey \u2014 that could be used in lip gloss. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe entire lab smelled like BBQ sauce,\u201d Mackey said.<\/p>\n

The collection includes the creamy coral \u201cGet Me Hot Buffalo,\u201d the gold-flecked deep red \u201cSweet Chile Kiss,\u201d the golden \u201cBe My Honey Pepper\u201d and the smoky burgundy \u201cHoney BBQ-T.\u201dWinky Lux <\/p>\n

Each gloss is made to resemble a real-life sauce served on chicken wings at Applebee\u2019s, so \u201cBe My Honey Pepper,\u201d inspired by the chain\u2019s Honey Pepper flavor, has a sparkly finish, while \u201cGet Me Hot Buffalo,\u201d like the eatery\u2019s Classic Buffalo offering, gives off a \u201c\u2018pepper aroma,’\u201d according to Mackey.<\/p>\n

Although real sugar is commonly used across chicken-wing sauce varieties, as a lip-gloss ingredient it made the texture too sticky\u2014 so, Mackey explained, the products taste \u201cmostly savory.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI think [people] are going to think it\u2019s either genius or super weird,\u201d Mackey said. Social media reactions have pretty much reflected that divide, with some saying it\u2019s \u201cso amazing\u201d and others simply uttering, \u201cNo, no, no.\u201d <\/p>\n

In the new product\u2019s splashy promotional video, four single people head to a fancy Applebee\u2019s for a late-night date night as they sing that their dates want to \u201cTaste My Face.\u201d The catchy tune was created by ad agency Grey Global. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe music video features an all-single ensemble and their Instagram handles, so those looking for love can \u2018@ the cast\u2019 to ask them out,\u201d Applebee\u2019s wrote on TikTok. <\/p>\n

The Saucy Glosses are available now on Winky Lux\u2019s website. <\/p>\n
