Starlink satellites sent into space by Elon Musk\u2019s SpaceX are helping Ukrainian military drones destroy Russian tanks and army trucks, according to a report.<\/p>\n

A Ukrainian air reconnaissance specialist says his military has been operating drones specially fitted with thermal cameras that can detect Russian vehicles at night, according to the Times of London.<\/p>\n

The drones are equipped with anti-tank grenades that are launched at their target.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf we use a drone with thermal vision at night, the drone must connect through Starlink to the artillery guy and create target acquisition,\u201d the Ukrainian specialist told the Times of London.<\/p>\n

Russia\u2019s invasion of Ukraine has resulted in widespread power outages \u2014 thus requiring anyone who wants to connect to the internet to use Starlink.<\/p>\n

Last month, Musk sent Starlink terminals into Ukraine at the request of Ukraine Vice Prime Minister Mykhailo Fedorov, who has said the broadband internet service \u201ckeeps our cities connected and emergency services saving lives\u201d in the event of outages. <\/p>\n

SpaceX sent a shipment of Starlink terminals to\u00a0provide Ukraine with internet access.<\/p>\n

SpaceX delivered Starlink terminals to Ukraine after a request from top government officials.Anadolu Agency via Getty Images <\/p>\n

But as Russia stepped up its bombardment of major cities, Musk and others have cautioned that the satellite signals could turn Ukrainians into targets.<\/p>\n

\u201cImportant warning: Starlink is the only non-Russian communications system still working in some parts of Ukraine, so probability of being targeted is high,\u201d\u00a0Musk tweeted earlier this month. <\/p>\n
