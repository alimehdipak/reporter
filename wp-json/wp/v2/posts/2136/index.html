Twitter is reportedly taking drastic steps to maintain smooth operations this week amid fears that disgruntled employees could try to disrupt the service following Elon Musk\u2019s takeover of the company.<\/p>\n

The social media platform has blocked routine changes such as product updates through Friday of this week \u2013 with alterations only possible if they\u2019re identified as \u201cbusiness critical\u201d and approved by an executive with a vice president title or higher.<\/p>\n

Twitter implemented the ban out of concern that workers who are upset with the company\u2019s board for accepting Musk\u2019s $44 billion offer could \u201cgo rogue,\u201d a source familiar with the matter told Bloomberg.<\/p>\n

A ban on product changes is not unprecedented at Twitter, which reportedly takes similar action ahead of major events such as the Super Bowl to ensure there are no hiccups on its platform.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk\u2019s takeover has prompted a mixed reaction from Twitter employees.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Twitter representatives did not immediately return a request for comment on the report or whether a specific concern prompted the policy.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s takeover bid has divided Twitter users and employees alike since he first disclosed his plans \u2013 with conservatives lauding his involvement as a victory for free speech and progressive-minded individuals fearful of the billionaire\u2019s plan to get rid of strict content moderation practices.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s initial disclosure that he had taken a 9% stake in Twitter reportedly prompted major anxiety among employees earlier this month \u2013 with the situation inside the company described as a \u201cs\u2014show.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cMost of us believe deeply that Twitter is much more than a tech platform; we have a deep responsibility to society. I hope our new owner gets that,\u201d Perez said.<\/p>\n

Twitter has encountered trouble involving rogue employees making unwanted changes in the recent past. In 2017, a customer service employee temporarily deactivated then-President Donald Trump\u2019s Twitter account on their last day working at the company.<\/p>\n

Musk has a major supporter in former Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey, who endorsed him and said the company had taken \u201cthe right path\u201d by accepting his offer.<\/p>\n
