<\/p>\n

Scientific research is normally about chemistry but this time its history.<\/p>\n

Researchers are using epic computing powers to develop a simulation of the ancient universe.<\/p>\n

Some 13 billion years of galactic evolution are being recreated by Thesan, a computer program built by the Center for Astrophysics.<\/p>\n

The name Thesan is a nod to the mythological goddess of dawn.<\/p>\n

The simulation\u2019s code is built on our present understanding of how time, space, and matter interact.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are able to take basic physics equations and governing theoretical models to simulate what happened in the early universe,\u201d astrophysicist Rahul Kannan told Universe Today.<\/p>\n

The period under observation by Thesan is called the Cosmic Dark Ages \u2013 which started about 400,000 years after the Big Bang and ended one billion years later.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe scales of space and time are too large, so the only way we can do experiments is on computers,\u201d Kannan said.<\/p>\n

In the simulation, the universe lit up gradually as the cosmos formed.<\/p>\n

Researchers are using a computer program called Thesan to develop a simulation showing the universe approximately 400,000 years after the Big Bang.Getty Images\/Science Photo Library <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, the James Webb Space Telescope is collecting data that might validate the simulation\u2019s projection.<\/p>\n

Aimed into deep space, the James Webb Space Telescope can look back in time.<\/p>\n

The light from distant space can take eons to reach our sector of the galaxy and the telescope\u2019s lens, giving us glimpses of the past.<\/p>\n

A simulation from the Thesan program depicts the universe 251 million years after the Big Bang. Thesan Collaboration The Thesan program generated a simulation showing how gas in the universe goes through the reionization process.Thesan Collaboration <\/p>\n

The full library of Thesan simulations will be made available to the public by mid-2022.<\/p>\n

Some of the simulations yielded by Thesan have been uploaded to the project\u2019s website.<\/p>\n
