The creator of the political cartoon Elon Musk used to illustrate his current political views is looking to cash in after his sketch sparked a viral social media debate.<\/p>\n

Colin Wright, a contributing editor for the online culture commentary site Quillette, is selling the cartoon as an NFT and has opened bidding on the OpenSea digital marketplace.<\/p>\n

The auction page for the drawing \u2013 dubbed \u201cmy political journal\u201d \u2013 showed at least two active bids. The top bid for 1.25 ether \u2013 a prominent cryptocurrency token \u2013 is worth nearly $3,600.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe auction for the #NFT of my political cartoon that @elonmusk shared (230K retweets, 1.3M likes) is currently at 1.25 $ETH! There are 13 days left to bid, so don\u2019t miss your chance to own a bit of internet history!\u201d Wright wrote in an update on Friday afternoon.<\/p>\n

Musk caused a stir on Thursday when he tweeted out Wright\u2019s cartoon. It depicts a stick figure standing slightly left-of-center on the political spectrum as of 2008 \u2013 only to end up as right-leaning conservative in 2021 as a figure labeled \u201cwoke progressive\u201d gradually moved further left during the intervening years.<\/p>\n

Musk, who is in the process of buying Twitter for $44 billion, later elaborated on his political stance, noting he was a staunch supporter of Democratic former President Barack Obama. However, he asserted the current Democratic party has been \u201chijacked by extremists.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe far left hates everyone, themselves included!\u201d Musk\u00a0tweeted Friday morning.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk\u2019s Twitter buy has angered left-leaning pundits and activists.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Musk followed minutes later, adding, \u201cBut I\u2019m no fan of the far right either. Let\u2019s have less hate and more love.\u201d<\/p>\n

The NFT auction isn\u2019t Wright\u2019s only effort to monetize the cartoon. In a post thanking Musk for his post, he also posted an Etsy listing for a mug depicting the political doodle.<\/p>\n

In another tweet, Wright said he \u201creally [loved] the discussion it has kindled.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThough admittedly simplistic, I think it speaks to a truth many feel. Of course, politics are more complex than can be captured in a political cartoon with stick figures,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n
