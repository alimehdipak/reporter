Lyft suspended\u00a0its\u00a0surge pricing citywide Tuesday\u00a0after the\u00a0horrific subway attack that injured at least 29 people\u00a0\u2014\u00a0and\u00a0even as its larger competitor Uber stubbornly limited its own suspension of demand-based rates to Brooklyn where the shooting occurred.<\/p>\n

The shooting\u00a0around 8:30 a.m, snarled train service across the city for hours and left\u00a0fearful\u00a0New Yorkers scrambling for rides.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m pretty scared to take the subway,\u201d said\u00a0a\u00a0Midtown office worker who identified herself as Tricia. \u201cLuckily,\u00a0my boss is letting my team expense Ubers\u00a0[and]\u00a0Lyfts home today.\u201d<\/p>\n

Lyft spokeswoman Katie Kim said the company\u2019s \u201cPrime Time\u201d pricing was suspended during the afternoon but did not say\u00a0exactly\u00a0at what time.\u00a0<\/p>\n

An Uber spokeswoman said\u00a0her\u00a0company had instituted a \u201ccap\u201d on\u00a0all\u00a0prices but did not specify an amount.\u00a0Meanwhile, Uber only suspended its surge pricing in the\u00a0borough of Brooklyn, which includes the Sunset Park neighborhood where the shooting occurred.<\/p>\n

But as of 4:40 p.m., an Uber trip from Sunset Park to Manhattan\u00a0still\u00a0cost more than double the price of a similar trip using Lyft.<\/p>\n

Both Uber and Lyft\u00a0initially\u00a0appeared to implement surge pricing \u2014 which involves upping prices when demand is high \u2014 for at least some customers in the\u00a0area\u00a0after\u00a0the\u00a0attack.<\/p>\n

\u201cFare surge after a mass shooting in brooklyn when subways are shut down,\u201d one Twitter user wrote, accompanied by a screenshot of Uber offering an $85.05 ride to Manhattan that the user said was taken at 10:40 a.m. \u201cShame on you @uber.\u201d<\/p>\n

Uber and Lyft were accused of jacking up rates following a horrific subway attack in Brooklyn on Tuesday. Christopher Sadowski <\/p>\n

The user, who posted under the Twitter name Captain Harvel, is Harleigh Tensen. She told The Post she needed to get a ride to a doctor\u2019s appointment in Manhattan.<\/p>\n

Another New Yorker shared a screenshot of a $68.49 ride from Sunset Park to Long Island City\u00a0in Queens\u00a0around the same time.<\/p>\n

\u201c@Uber\u00a0turn\u00a0off surges in sunset park,\u201d the user wrote. \u201cPeople are scared, let them get out safely.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Shortly after The Post asked Uber for comment, spokeswoman Freddi Goldstein said the company had suspended surge pricing in the \u201cvicinity,\u201d which an Uber spokesman later said encompassed all of Brooklyn.<\/p>\n

\u201cFollowing the incident, Uber disabled surge pricing in the vicinity and capped pricing citywide,\u201d Goldstein told The Post. \u201cIf anyone on our platform experienced unintended charges during this emergency, we will work to get them refunded.<\/p>\n

\u201cOur hearts go out to the victims of this morning\u2019s terrible shooting in Sunset Park,\u201d she added. \u201cAs always, Uber strives to be a resource for New Yorkers aiming to get around New York City.\u201d<\/p>\n

Goldstein did not specify what time Uber suspended surge pricing.<\/p>\n

Uber users expressed frustration to be saddled with sky-high for-hire vehicle rates.<\/p>\n

\u201c@Uber @lyft the subway shooter is still at large and people are scared to take the subway,\u201d\u00a0Twitter user @cassievan posted. \u201cYou should NOT be increasing fares due to high demand because people are afraid to take the train.\u201d<\/p>\n

A Brooklyn Lyft rider, meanwhile, shared a screenshot\u00a0earlier Tuesday\u00a0of a $69.85 fare with a \u201climited availability\u201d warning.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cDidnt see anything about \u2018Shooting Surge Pricing\u2019 in @lyft terms of use,\u201d the Twitter user said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Passengers run from a subway train after the shooting in Brooklyn.Will B. Wylde via AP <\/p>\n

\u201cSomeone tell Lyft\u2019s AI bot that surge pricing should not apply as a result of a mass shooting,\u201d another Twitter user wrote.<\/p>\n

On Tuesday afternoon, Lyft\u00a0initially\u00a0told\u00a0The Post it\u00a0would give partial refunds to riders who had been overcharged.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are actively monitoring the situation in Sunset Park and have currently suspended Prime Time pricing for riders who are in the area,\u201d spokeswoman Katie Kim told The Post. \u201cWe are working to adjust fares for certain riders who paid Prime Time prices when the situation first unfolded.\u201d<\/p>\n

This isn\u2019t the first time Uber and Lyft have been accused of upping prices after a tragedy.<\/p>\n

Following a 2017 blast near the Port Authority in Midtown Manhattan,\u00a0both apps coughed up refunds to riders\u00a0who said they had been charged exorbitant fares as they tried to flee the scene.<\/p>\n
