<\/p>\n

Michael Jordan is not the only athlete with Nike cachet \u2014 this week, a sneaker auction at Sotheby\u2019s Hong Kong bets that skateboarding shoes are probably worth a lot more than you think: even up to $44,000. <\/p>\n

\u201cInitially, Nike did not see value in making sneakers for skateboarders, so they were produced in limited quantities,\u201d Bernie Gross, creative director of Extra Butter, a purveyor of collectible kicks, told The Post. \u201cThe company made editions of under 1,000 because they did not want to see the sneakers going to clearance racks.\u201d<\/p>\n

Team Nike skaters such as Danny Supa, Paul Rodriguez and Reese Forbes lent their names to snazzy shoes for the Nike SB line. Now, those early offerings are coveted by deep-pocketed sneakerheads.<\/p>\n

\u201cInitially costing $75 or $80 a pair,\u201d Gross added, \u201cthese were accessible by any means \u2014 but you had to have the right perspective. Later on, in the secondary market, people paid for not being in the know.\u201d<\/p>\n

Here are six coveted kicks on the Sotheby\u2019s auction block through April 25:<\/p>\n

Sotherby’s <\/p>\n

Nike SB Dunk Low Pro Supreme \u2018Black Cement\u2019<\/h2>\n

Auction estimate: up to $12,000 <\/h3>\n

Designed to pay homage to the Air Jordan 3\u2014 using a similar color scheme and even some of the same material as that shoe \u2014 \u201cBlack Cement\u201d (2002) was produced in conjunction with Supreme, just as the streetwear pioneer was achieving worldwide recognition. Gross recalled a scene he witness in 2008, while waiting on line for a different sneaker drop in the East Village: \u201cA kid was wearing a pair of Cements and someone offered $2,000 for him to take them off his feet right then and there. But the kid wouldn\u2019t sell.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sotherby’s <\/p>\n

Nike SB Blazer Premium \u2018Milk Carton\u2019<\/h2>\n

Auction estimate: up to $2,550 <\/h3>\n

These 2007 sneaks were made in collaboration with Jeff Han, who owns the successful Fly Skate Shop in Shanghai. As a kid, he would ditch school and \u201cdisappear\u201d to skate. Created to resemble milk cartons with images of missing children on them, Han put a line drawing of his face on the ankle of this cheeky sneak.<\/p>\n

Sotherby’s <\/p>\n

Nike SB Dunk Low Pro \u2018Freddy Krueger\u2019 <\/h2>\n

Estimate up to $38,000 <\/h3>\n

Nike paid \u201cblood\u201d-splattered tribute to the \u201cA Nightmare on Elm Street\u201d psycho in 2007, right down to his signature stripes \u2014\u00a0but the film\u2019s copyright holders weren\u2019t flattered. \u201cThere were issues between Nike and the cinema company, so Nike scrapped the release,\u201d Brahm Wachter, head of streetwear and modern collectibles for Sotheby\u2019s, told The Post. \u201cThis pair, somehow, did not get destroyed. I\u2019d say there are fewer than 150 out there. But nobody knows for sure.\u201d<\/p>\n

Sotherby’s <\/p>\n

Nike SB Dunk Low Premium \u2018Yellow Lobster\u2019 Promo Sample <\/h2>\n

Auction estimate: up to $44,000 <\/h3>\n

In 2009, Concepts, a streetwear shop in Boston, worked with Nike on a couple of sneaker models that honored Bean Town\u2019s most luxurious seafood: lobsters. \u201cThen they made 36 of these [shoes], in yellow, for friends and family,\u201d said Wachter. \u201cAccording to Nike, the odds of pulling up a lobster trap with a yellow lobster in it is 1 in 40 million.\u201d Complete with rubber bands clenching the toes \u2014 just like the elastics used to keep the crustaceans\u2019 claws closed \u2014 and insides that look like a lobster bib, these sneakers are designed to cleverly connote in-your-face rarity.<\/p>\n

Sotherby’s <\/p>\n

Nike SB Dunk Low Pro Reese Forbes \u2018Denim\u2019 <\/h2>\n

Auction estimate: up to $12,000 <\/h3>\n

\u201c[Nike] kept the edges unfinished, so the sneakers unravel as you skated\u201d \u2014\u00a0just like a pair of beloved jeans \u2014 said Gross. \u201cIn the skate world, beating up a pair of these [2002] sneakers is a bit of a flex: \u2018I know they\u2019re coveted, but I will bear the s\u2013t out of them.\u2019\u201d<\/p>\n

Sotherby’s Sotherby’s <\/p>\n

Nike SB Dunk Low Pro \u201cPigeon\u201d with Jeff Staple signed box <\/h2>\n

Auction estimate: up to $40,800 <\/h3>\n
