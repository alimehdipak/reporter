Roofing scams are a growing problem in the United States. The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has received reports of people being taken advantage of by unscrupulous roofers who promise to repair or replace their roof but then ask for more money than the job is actually worth. The scammers often use high-pressure tactics, saying that they’ll leave the job incomplete or worse if you don’t pay them right away.<\/span><\/p>\n

Here are some common roofing scams to watch out for.<\/span><\/p>\n

Types of roofing scams: flashing, shingles, solar panels, and more<\/b><\/h2>\n

The roofing industry is one that can be rife with scams, including flashing, shingles, solar panels, and more. Here are eight types of roof scams to watch out for:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Flashing: This scam involves a contractor installing unauthorized flashing on your roof without obtaining the necessary permits. This can lead to water damage and structural failure.<\/span><\/li>\n
  2. Shingles: Fake shingles are often sold online or at flea markets. These shingles may not meet safety standards and could result in structural damage or even death if installed incorrectly.<\/span><\/li>\n
  3. Solar Panels: Many unscrupulous contractors<\/a> will offer you a solar panel installation as an add-on to your roof job without informing you that this is a costly and time-consuming process that requires a permit from your local government.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    How to avoid roofing scams: be cautious when dealing with contractors and ask plenty of questions<\/b><\/h2>\n

    When looking for a contractor to do work on your home, be cautious. There are many scams out there, and you don\u2019t want to fall, victim. Here are some tips to help you stay safe:<\/span><\/p>\n

      \n
    1. Ask plenty of questions. Make sure you understand what the contractor is proposing and ask for references. Don\u2019t let them pressure you into making a decision right away.<\/span><\/li>\n
    2. Verify the credentials of the contractor. Look online or contact other professional organizations to see if they have had any complaints filed against them. Also, check with the Better Business Bureau to see if they have a good record of customer service.<\/span><\/li>\n
    3. Get written estimates and contract documents in advance. This will help ensure that both sides are clear about what needs to be done and who is responsible for costs.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

      Conclusion<\/b><\/h2>\n

      Roofing scams can be costly, dangerous, and frustrating. Be sure to do your research before hiring a contractor, and be aware of common roofing scams. If you think you may have been scammed, don’t hesitate to report it to the authorities.<\/span><\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
