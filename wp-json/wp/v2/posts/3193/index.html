Amber Heard has insisted that her role in the upcoming \u201cAquaman\u201d sequel was drastically cut back after her ex-husband Johnny Depp launched a smear campaign against her by saying she lied about abuse allegations.<\/p>\n

But in a new blow for the actress, the high-powered head of DC Films testified Tuesday that the star\u2019s role in the big-budget flick was in no way impacted by innuendo surrounding her tumultuous marriage to Depp. <\/p>\n

Walter Hamada made the claim via video link at the former couple\u2019s blockbuster defamation trial in Virginia. <\/p>\n

Hamada \u2014 who has been president of DC Films since 2018 \u2014 alleged it was always intended for Heard to have a smaller role in the film franchise.<\/p>\n

However, he added that producers were so \u201cconcerned\u201d about a lack of chemistry between Heard and co-star Jason Momoa in the first \u201cAquaman\u201d film that they even considered recasting her in the sequel. <\/p>\n

\u201cI think editorially they were able to make that relationship work in the first movie,\u201d the movie boss stated. \u201cBut there was a concern that it took a lot of effort to get there and that would we be better off finding someone with more natural chemistry with Jason Momoa and move forward that way.\u201d<\/p>\n

DC Films President Walter Hamada (inset) testified Tuesday that Heard\u2019s salary and screen time in the upcoming \u201cAquaman\u201d sequel was unrelated to gossip about her turbulent marriage to Johnny Depp. Hamada said producers were \u201cconcerned\u201d about a lack of chemistry between Jason Momoa (left) and Heard in the first \u201cAquaman\u201d movie. AP <\/p>\n

Although Heard managed to keep her spot in the sequel, she alleged that her role was significantly reduced. <\/p>\n

\u201cI was given a script and then given new versions of the script that had taken away scenes\u2026they basically took a bunch out of my role. They just removed a bunch out,\u201d Heard testified last week.<\/p>\n

However, Hamada directly disputed that claim, saying she was always supposed to be a minor character in the sequel. <\/p>\n

High-powered Hamada \u2014 who has been president of DC Films since 2018 \u2014 testified via video link on Tuesday. POOL\/AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cThe size of the role in the film that she had was determined in the early development of the script, which would be in 2018,\u201d he alleged. \u201cThe character\u2019s involvement in the story was sort of what it was from the beginning.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cFrom the early stages of the development of the script, the movie was built around the character of Arthur [Momoa] and the character of Orm [Patrick Wilson],\u201d Hamada further stated. <\/p>\n

On Monday, Heard\u2019s legal team brought entertainment industry consultant Kathryn Arnold as a witness, who said Heard was paid $2 million for the sequel \u2014 a sum negotiated around the time that the first \u201cAquaman\u201d film came out in 2018.<\/p>\n

However, the consultant said the actress would have been able to renegotiate for up to $6 million, had it not been for Depp and his lawyer Adam Waldman defaming her by calling her accusations of abuse a \u201choax.\u201d<\/p>\n

Arnold claimed Heard\u2019s earning capacity and negotiating powers were significantly diminished by those claims, which began circulating in 2020. <\/p>\n

But Hamada further insisted on Tuesday that the actress\u2019s compensation was in no way impacted by the claims made by Depp and Waldman. <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, since the trial started, more than 4 million Depp fans\u00a0have flocked to sign a petition\u00a0urging DC Films to drop Heard from \u201cAquaman 2\u201d altogether.<\/p>\n

The highly-anticipated film is set for release next March. The first \u201cAquaman\u201d film \u2014 released in 2018 \u2014 was a box office smash, grossing more than $1 billion across the globe. <\/p>\n
