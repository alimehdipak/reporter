They\u2019re moving on to \u2014 hopefully \u2014 less violent pastures.<\/p>\n

Hillsong megachurch pastors Josh and Leona Kimes have stepped down from their position as leaders of the Australian institution\u2019s Hillsong branch, the couple wrote on social media this week.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The announcement comes days after a leaked internal investigation alleged that Leona was once repeatedly punched in the face \u201cwith a closed fist\u201d by scandalized ex-pastor Carl Lentz\u2019s wife, Laura. The incident reportedly occurred one night in 2016, when Laura caught Carl and Leona canoodling on a couch in the home of former NBA player Tyson Chandler.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was never in our plans to ever leave Hillsong Church,\u201d Josh Kimes captioned a Monday Instagram post. \u201cIt\u2019s been home for 22yrs of my life. It\u2019s where I\u2019ve pastored for the last 16yrs.\u201d<\/p>\n

The post goes on to detail the many life landmarks the couple celebrated in their time at the once celebrity-beloved, now disgraced Pentecostal congregation, the founder and former leader of which recently stepped down amid misconduct accusations: The Kimes met and got married at Hillsong, moved across the world for the church, helped start Hillsong NYC and Hillsong Boston, to name a few of their listed experiences there.\u00a0<\/p>\n

As for why, after all this time, they\u2019ve suddenly decided to join the deluge of pastors stepping away from the church, they only offer the explanation that \u201cwe\u2019ve made the difficult decision to resign as we know God is directing our steps to walk confidently & humbly into the unknown & trust Him to open the right door & plant us somewhere new.\u201d<\/p>\n

Josh and Leona Kimes with their children. josh.kimes\/Facebook Carl Lentz preaching at NYC\u2019s Irving Plaza in 2013.AP <\/p>\n

The pair have spent the last year or so doing \u201ca lot of work,\u201d they vaguely offer, and therapy to \u201cprocess the past, learn & unlearn, forgive & seek forgiveness.\u201d As a result, they now say they have \u201chealthy boundaries,\u201d a better marriage and are more equipped to help build the church branch they are now resigning from but which \u201cwe\u2019re still very passionate about.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cHillsong will always hold a special place in our hearts,\u201d Josh noted.<\/p>\n

Before heading Hillsong\u2019s Boston church with her husband, Leona spent seven years nannying for the Lentzs, during which time she was \u201csubjected to manipulation, control, bullying, abuse of power and sexual abuse\u201d according to a Medium essay she wrote last May.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Lentzs have vehemently denied the allegations.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The Kimes\u2019 own former nannies have also come forward with allegations. One New Jersey sitter previously told The Post that Leona regularly paid her less than her rate and generally \u201cexpected people to work for nothing.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cPersonally, I think it\u2019s one of the better exits \/ announcements from a Hillsong leader,\u201d one former Hillsong NYC congregant told The Post. \u201cMost go quietly into the night with maybe a small statement.\u201d<\/p>\n
