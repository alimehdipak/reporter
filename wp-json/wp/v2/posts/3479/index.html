It\u2019s almost a given that you will, inevitably, say something that sounds just like one (or both) of your parents.<\/p>\n

Dr. Rick is here to help.<\/p>\n

He\u2019s the world-renowned \u201cParenta-life Coach\u201d with the mustache and sweater-vest who stars in those clever Progressive Insurance ads, dispensing tough-love wisdom to his clients who can\u2019t stop themselves from imitating their mothers and fathers \u2014 whether it\u2019s clapping at the end of a film (\u201cNo one who made the movie is here\u201d), telling anyone who will listen about their morning (\u201cYou woke up early \u2014 no one cares\u201d), stopping themselves from commenting on someone\u2019s blue hair (\u201cWe all see it. We all see it.\u201d) or correcting their placement of pillows on the couch (\u201cIf you have nowhere to sit, you have too many\u201d).<\/p>\n

\u201cI call it a sort of generational triple-dip,\u201d said Bill Glass, 51, the amiable Chicago-area native who\u2019s played Dr. Rick since the commercials began airing two years ago. \u201cIt\u2019s kids looking at their parents and thinking, \u2018Oh boy,\u2019 and a parent looking at their parents and going \u2018Oh boy.\u2019<\/p>\n

\u201cThere are some people you see or hear who say, \u2018You\u2019re making fun\u2019 but there\u2019s no malice intended here,\u201d he said. \u201cThis is something we\u2019re all going to do matter what \u2014 and whether or not you want to admit it, everybody is going to take a phrase or a habit from their parents, whether they like it or not.\u201d<\/p>\n

There\u2019s even a book, \u201cDr. Rick Will See You Now: A Guide to Un-Becoming Your Parents,\u201d that features Glass (as the good doctor) on its cover.<\/p>\n

Actor Bill Glass as \u201cDr. Rick\u201d in the Progressive Insurance ads.Progressive Casualty Insurance <\/p>\n

Glass has been acting in commercials for 25 years and has a background in improvisation (Chicago\u2019s Second City and ImprovOlympic troupes), as do the actors who appear with him in the Progressive spots. \u201cI\u2019ve done some other stuff but nothing has gotten quite the response that this has, so it\u2019s kind of neat and lots of fun,\u201d he said. \u201cIt\u2019s fun and challenging to make people laugh in a 30-second window.\u201d<\/p>\n

The ensemble cast is directed by Martin Granger and is based in LA, where they shoot the commercials on location (including a movie theater in Simi Valley and a residential home in Pasadena). <\/p>\n

\u201cTwo 30-second commercials will be two 12-to-14-hour days,\u201d Glass said. \u201cThe writers come up with a lot of vignettes and they want to have a lot of options, so we\u2019re shooting the setup and moving, shooting the setup and moving \u2026 it\u2019s a fun way to work but it\u2019s a long workday. As much fun as it is, we\u2019re working \u2014 don\u2019t kid yourself.\u201d<\/p>\n

The actors playing Dr. Rick\u2019s clients have remained virtually the same since the campaign kicked off, with some new faces mixed in every now and then. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe writers at the ad agency [Arnold Worldwide] and the director are really great,\u201d he said. \u201cThey hire a lot of improv actors; I wish you could see the whole cast here because it\u2019s a great comedy ensemble and the success of the campaign is due to all the actors who bring it to life.\u201d<\/p>\n

Bill Glass, who hails from the Chicago area, has a deep background in improvisation. <\/p>\n

Glass said the actors do improvise, just a bit, and are allowed some free rein from Granger while they\u2019re shooting the commercials. \u201cWe\u2019ll cover the script, but Martin \u2026 hired all these people with improv backgrounds to he lets us play a bit \u2026 we mix in a little improv and then they edit it and spit out what you see on TV.<\/p>\n

\u201cI think [the ads] are like the appetizer tray of comedy \u2014 and right now we\u2019ve got the shrimp wrapped in bacon.\u201d<\/p>\n

Glass has mixed his commercial work with acting in movies and on TV shows, including a small role as a lawyer on \u201cRutherford Falls\u201d (Season 2 premieres June 16 on Peacock with stars Ed Helms and Janna Schmieding ). \u201cThe [\u2018Rutherford Falls\u2019] casting director Allison Jones, enjoys the [Progressive] campaign so she called me in,\u201d he said. \u201cIt\u2019s such a pleasure for me to have a small part in the show.\u201d<\/p>\n

The fame that comes along with starring in a successful ad campaign does have its limits, particularly for jobbing actors including Glass.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m a lunch pail actor, and to have a nice bit in the midst of the grind \u2026 hopefully I will keep this gig for a while and maybe I\u2019ll be a 30-year \u2018overnight success,’\u201d he said with a chuckle. \u201cI\u2019ve come to a place in my career where it\u2019s going to be what it\u2019s going to be and it [the ads] show that I can do comedy when the camera is pointed at me. Hopefully, other sitcom casting directors will say, \u2018Hey, this guy\u2019s pretty good, we can use him for something.\u2019<\/p>\n
