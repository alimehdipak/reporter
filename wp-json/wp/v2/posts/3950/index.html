Emma Thompson is truly unrecognizable in the upcoming \u201cMatilda: The Musical,\u201d playing the treacherous Miss Trunchbull.<\/p>\n

The Oscar winner, 63, sported quite the transformation for the character in a new trailer for the Netflix film based on the 1988 Roald Dahl book.<\/p>\n

The rock musical is in turn based on the Tony-winning project directed by Matthew Warchus and written by Dennis Kelly. Warchus also returns to direct the silver screen adaption of the show.<\/p>\n

Trunchbull is the mean, old Crunchem Hall headmaster who is abusive toward a bright, young girl, Matilda (played by Alisha Weir). <\/p>\n

\u201cThis school is full of rebels,\u201d Thompson shrills in the teaser. <\/p>\n

The \u201cLove Actually\u201d star dons a tight gray and white bun, a green trench dress, a medley of gadgets and a scowl. <\/p>\n

Other quick snapshots show swarms of kids wearing school uniforms, singing and dancing through the halls as Matilda joins in. <\/p>\n

There was even a snippet of the Bruce Bogtrotter character stuffing his face with chocolate cake, a scene made infamous by young actor Jimmy Karz in a 1996 adaption directed by Danny DeVito. It starred DeVito alongside Mara Wilson, Rhea Perlman, Pam Ferris and Embeth Davidtz.<\/p>\n

Lashana Lynch is also starring in the new kids\u2019 film as the kind-hearted Miss Honey, a woman who motivates and inspires Matilda to dream big. Stephen Graham and Andrea Riseborough are playing Matilda\u2019s inattentive parents who neglect her daily.<\/p>\n
