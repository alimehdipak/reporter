How much attention is paid to what a groom will wear on his wedding day? Odds are, not much, since historically a groom\u2019s options have been pretty limited, with nowhere near the many varied options brides enjoy.<\/p>\n

But grooms \u2014 fret no longer. Thanks to designers and celebrities taking more style risks on the runway and red carpet, guys have more choice and inspiration in the design, color and accessories than ever before.<\/p>\n

\u201cToday, guys are more aware of trends and they want to bring their individual style in new ways,\u201d said Jeff Jones, vice-president of merchandising for Joseph Abboud.<\/p>\n

When shopping for a suit or tuxedo, think about what your partner will be wearing. \u201cThe couple\u2019s attire sets the tone for the formality of the event. The groom should always be a level more formal than the expectations of their guests,\u201d said Carrie Goldberg, founder and creative director of CLG Creative.<\/p>\n

Formality can be dictated by where the wedding will be held, as well as the time of day. A tuxedo, for example, is more appropriate after 6 p.m., while a suit can be worn at any time.<\/p>\n

Getting married during the day calls for a more relaxed look. J.Crew\u2019s Ludlow slim-fit unstructured suit jacket in Irish cotton-linen has a two-button closure and a notched lapel. A tan suit by the Black Tux is available in both classic and slim fits. $149 to rent; $475 to purchase, by appointment only, 243 Centre StHandout <\/p>\n

\u201cTypically, formal events, often referred to as black-tie events, call for a tuxedo,\u201d said Tina Sroat, chief merchandising and planning officer at the Black Tux. \u201cSemiformal events imply a suit. If an event specifies, it\u2019s highly recommended you respect this and dress accordingly.\u201d<\/p>\n

White tuxedo jackets are having a bit of a moment. Associated with destination weddings, summertime affairs and tropical settings, Jones said that it\u2019s the James Bond effect. \u201cIt innately brings a sense of cool to the groom.\u201d<\/p>\n

Personalization, as with all the wedding\u2019s details, is a great way for a couple or individual to convey their personality and style.<\/p>\n

For a semi-formal affair, Musika\u2019s wool silk two-piece suit with peak lapels in navy can be dressed up or down. $2,900, by appointment only.Handout Dress down, yet still dapper, in Brooks Brothers\u2019 Milano Slim-fit stretch seersucker stripe sport coat and pants. Sold separately: $118 for the pants, $498 for the sport coat. 1270 Sixth Ave., Stes. A & E.Handout <\/p>\n

\u201cColor is a great way for him to express his personal style through either a colored tuxedo or suit and\/or novelty neckwear and accessories,\u201d said Sroat. \u201cWith the latter, the options are endless \u2014 vibrant neckwear, patterned socks, fun shoes, lapel pins, heirloom jewelry. It\u2019s a great way to wear a more traditional tuxedo or suit but add his own touch.\u201d<\/p>\n

And like a bride, it\u2019s not uncommon for a groom to change into a second look for the reception, \u201ceither by changing the style of suit from formal tuxedo to disco tux or simply changing jackets,\u201d said Goldberg. \u201cWe do recommend that grooms purchase a second shirt, given that they are likely to sweat through the first one.\u201d<\/p>\n
