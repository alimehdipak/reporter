Betsey Johnson may have gotten her start in fashion 50 years ago, but her \u201cpretty n\u2019 punk\u201d designs feel fresher than ever today \u2014 thanks to the return of Y2K style trends, pop-punk tunes and one teen superstar: Olivia Rodrigo.<\/p>\n

The \u201cDrivers License\u201d singer, 19, has sported multiple looks by 79-year-old Johnson on her \u201cSour\u201d tour in recent months, from slinky slip dresses to tutus.<\/p>\n

And on the Met Gala 2022 red carpet, she adorned her hair with sparkling butterfly clips, a collaboration between Johnson and hair accessory aficionado Jennifer Behr.<\/p>\n

By putting her own spin on Johnson\u2019s vintage creations, Rodrigo\u2019s introduced the vibrant octogenarian\u2019s designs to a whole new generation.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m in awe every day to see there is still such a love for the work I\u2019ve done,\u201d Johnson tells Page Six Style exclusively. \u201cI never thought in a million years that my designs from the \u201990s would be so popular 20 years later. And worn by some of the most stylish girls on the planet!\u201d<\/p>\n

Added the designer, \u201cMy \u2018pretty n\u2019 punk\u2019 motto was never a trend \u2014 it\u2019s a mindset, and it\u2019s incredible that it still resonates.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cGirls have always loved wearing my dresses for prom. For Olivia to wear this to her \u2018Sour Prom\u2019 party felt like the beginning of a new era, because she introduced my brand to a new generation,\u201d Johnson tells us.oliviarodrigo\/Instagram <\/p>\n

Rodrigo first started stepping out in Johnson\u2019s dresses after the star\u2019s stylists, Chloe and Chenelle\u00a0Delgadillo, reached out to borrow some archival styles. As the relationship grew \u2014\u00a0\u201cChenelle even shopped in my own closet!\u201d Johnson says \u2014 Rodrigo attended her \u201cSour Prom\u201d premiere party in a pink printed slip dress.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m always so honored when she chooses one of my vintage pieces to wear because the way she wears them is exactly how they were designed \u2014\u00a0to make them your own,\u201d Johnson says.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou can see this is her true style, the way she mixes a feminine slip dress with punk boots. I hope that young girls always find the freedom to dress for themselves and not for anyone else.\u201d<\/p>\n

Betsey Johnson teamed up with Jennifer Behr to create these butterfly hair clips for Rodrigo\u2019s 2022 Met Gala look.Getty Images <\/p>\n

At the Met, Rodrigo accessorized her lilac metal mesh Versace dress with the aforementioned winged clips \u2014 now available to shop for between $75 and $175 \u2014\u00a0which were inspired by ones seen in Johnson\u2019s spring 1997 runway show.<\/p>\n

\u201cButterflies have always been a major inspiration to me when it comes to design for my prints and accessories. I handmade those specifically for the show, but they were so delicate they didn\u2019t survive in my archives,\u201d Johnson explains.<\/p>\n

Johnson and Behr\u2019s teams \u201cworked around the clock\u201d to create the accessories, which marked the fashion designer\u2019s first-ever presence at the Met Gala \u2014\u00a0and we suspect it won\u2019t be her last.<\/p>\n
