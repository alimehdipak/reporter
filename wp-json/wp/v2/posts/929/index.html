Elon Musk said he is \u201cgiving serious thought\u201d to creating a social media platform that would compete with Twitter, saying that the latter has been stifling free speech.<\/p>\n

\u201cFree speech is essential to a functioning democracy. Do you believe Twitter rigorously adheres to this principle?\u201d Musk tweeted in a Twitter poll that he posted on Thursday.<\/p>\n

The next day, Musk took it a step further, writing: \u201cGiven that Twitter serves as the de facto public town square, failing to adhere to free speech principles fundamentally undermines democracy. What should be done?\u201d<\/p>\n

In the same thread, a Twitter user asked the Tesla CEO about possibly \u201cbuilding a new social media platform\u201d that would boast \u201can open-source algorithm.\u201d<\/p>\n

The user proposed that the new platform would be one \u201cwhere free speech and adhering to free speech is given top priority\u201d and where \u201cpropaganda is very minimal.\u201d<\/p>\n

To which Musk replied: \u201cAm giving serious thought to this.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tech giants like Twitter, Facebook, Google, and Amazon have been accused by some conservatives and free speech advocates of banning users with unpopular views.<\/p>\n

Then-Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey said last year that his company erred when it censored a New York Post article about Hunter Biden\u2019s laptop in the weeks leading up to the presidential election.<\/p>\n

Former President Donald Trump, who was kicked off the large social media platforms immediately following the Jan. 6 Capitol riots, launched his own network, Truth Social, which bills itself as a conservative-friendly alternative to Twitter.<\/p>\n

It rocketed to the number-one spot on Apple\u2019s free apps chart when it was made available for download in February \u2014 days after Trump made his only post on the site, telling fans that \u201cyour favorite president will see you soon.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

But a combination of technical glitches, a lengthy waiting list to join, a collapse of the share price, and Trump\u2019s apparent disinterest in posting messages has marred the app\u2019s rollout.<\/p>\n

Musk, the world\u2019s richest person, has also gotten in trouble for his itchy Twitter fingers.<\/p>\n
