\u201cHow I Met Your Mother\u201d star Neil Patrick Harris and David Burtka have found a buyer for their historic Harlem brownstone, Gimme can report exclusively. <\/p>\n

The five-story home listed last August for $7.3 million. But it was pulled off the market following a storm that caused some water damage. <\/p>\n

While the furniture was being redone, brokers and buyers kept circling \u2060\u2014 with multiple offers. <\/p>\n

The buyer \u2060\u2014\u00a0a local New Yorker in love with the house and the \u2018hood, we hear \u2060\u2014 is in contract for more than $7.1 million, sources say. <\/p>\n

It\u2019s a record for the neighborhood and yet another example of the boom Harlem is having. <\/p>\n

Harris bought the five-bedroom, 20-foot-wide home in 2013 for close to $3.6 million, as Gimme reported exclusively at the time. <\/p>\n

The massive five-story home at 2036 Fifth Ave. is 8,000 square feet. Built in 1908,\u00a0its original details \u2060\u2014 like moldings, millwork, dark wood, a restored wood staircase and high ceilings \u2060\u2014 remain intact even after updates by New York architect Jeffery Povero and LA designer Trace Lehnhoff. <\/p>\n

Quirky new details include a top-floor office hidden behind a trick door that opens when you press the eye of a magician on a vintage poster. And like Jimmy Fallon\u2019s Gramercy Park penthouse, this home comes with a vintage bar. In this case, it\u2019s sourced from an old hotel and restored with new appliances, plumbing and electrical, including two built-in taps. <\/p>\n

There\u2019s also a formal dining room, library, music room, wine cellar, three fireplaces and a playroom. <\/p>\n

An old hotel bar now adorns the home. Courtesy of Compass Of course, there is a home theater.Courtesy of Compass Sun bathe in the buff on this private roof terrace.Courtesy of Compass <\/p>\n

The outdoor space includes a furnished, irrigated and landscaped roof deck with an outdoor grill, and a dining table for eight under a wood pergola, as well as a Jacuzzi. Altogether, the outdoor space totals more than 2,000 square feet of terraces, gardens and decks. <\/p>\n

The home \u2060\u2014 especially the chef\u2019s kitchen \u2060\u2014 is also recognizable to readers of Burtka\u2019s cookbook, \u201cLife is a Party,\u201d says broker Vickey Barron, who is the co-listing broker with Pacey Barron, both of Compass. \u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThey have so many great memories here and there are great images of the house in the book. It represents what the house meant for both of them \u2060\u2014 raising their family and entertaining here,\u201d Vickey Barron said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The couple and their twins, Gideon and Harper -\u2060\u2014 along with their dogs, Gidget, Spike and Ella \u2060\u2014 are spending more of their time in their Hamptons home these days. That four-bedroom home in East Hampton sits on 13.5 acres and is adjacent to another 10-acre reserve. They bought it for $5.5 million in 2017. <\/p>\n

\u201cThey simply don\u2019t need two large homes,\u201d Vickey Barron said. <\/p>\n
