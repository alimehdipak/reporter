Tooth Decay<\/b><\/h2>\n

The best way to prevent tooth decay is to brush and floss at least twice a day. You should also visit your dentist every 6 months for a dental checkup. He or she will scrape the plaque off your teeth, which is a thin film that forms on the surface of the teeth. The dentist will then use a special tool to remove the film. This will help to prevent tooth decay.<\/span><\/p>\n

Gum Disease<\/b><\/h2>\n

If you have gingivitis, you will notice that your gums become swollen and tender. They may bleed easily when you brush and floss your teeth.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

You should also avoid chewing gum and smoking. These habits can cause your gums to become irritated.<\/span><\/p>\n

Periodontitis<\/b><\/h2>\n

If you have periodontitis, you may notice that your gums are red, swollen, and tender. You may also notice bleeding and pus from your gums.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

If you do not treat your periodontitis, you may get tooth loss. You should see your dentist right away if you notice any of these symptoms.<\/span><\/p>\n

Bad Breath<\/b><\/h2>\n

The main cause of bad breath is the bacteria in the mouth. Bacteria in the mouth can cause bad breath. This is because the bacteria can produce a foul odor.<\/span><\/p>\n
