Rhea Seehorn veers from the darkness of \u201cBetter Call Saul\u201d to \u201cCooper\u2019s Bar,\u201d a short-form meta comedy Web series in which she plays a horrendous Tinseltown executive dubbed \u201cthe biggest d\u2013k in Hollywood.\u201d<\/p>\n

The series, available on AMC.com and AMC\u2019s YouTube channel, was created by stars Lou Mustillo and David Conolly along with Hannah Davis-Law, Nick Morton and Seehorn \u2014 who also directs two of the six episodes and is an executive producer.<\/p>\n

It revolves around longtime character actor Cooper Marino (Mustillo), who\u2019s tired of the soul-crushing Hollywood treadmill and longs to return to his native Buffalo with his wife, Mary (Kila Kitu), a therapist. He runs a cozy, colorful neighborhood bar from the backyard of his LA home \u2014 which, through a series of events, attracts the attention of Kris Latimer (Seehorn), who wants to shoot a \u201cCheers\u201d-type comedy pilot about the bar and its denizens \u2026 starring Amy Schumer. <\/p>\n

Cooper balks at the idea \u2014 it\u2019s his <\/em>life, after all \u2014 and the series plays out in a back-and-forth that touches on, among other themes, Hollywood backstabbing and its (often deflating) show-business dealmaking.<\/p>\n

\u201cOriginally we were going to do a short film with Nick Morton, Hannah and David Conolly, the three creators, and we all know Lou,\u201d Seehorn told The Post. \u201cWe used to work out together in this boot camp in Glendale and Lou always had these very funny stories. He\u2019s such a great actor and a great personality \u2026 and we started thinking about how we could film these stories.<\/p>\n

Kris Latimer (Rhea Seehorn) and Cooper Marino (Lou Mustillo) face off in a wings-eating contest as Cyrus Long (Phil LaMarr) looks on.Mike Taing\/IFC\/AMC <\/p>\n

\u201cHe has this backyard that is exactly what you see [on \u2018Cooper\u2019s Bar\u2019],\u201d she said. \u201cIt\u2019s a fully-realized Tiki Bar \u2014 our set designer boosted it up a little \u2014 but it was just there and is funny. We did a short [film] and it did well at SXSW [the film festival] and we ended up connecting with AMC and the Bacardi [Rum] people and they wanted to make it into a short series.\u201d<\/p>\n

Casey Washington and Conolly play Cooper\u2019s buddies Brandon and David who are, respectively, a wannabe screenwriter\/mall cop and a sketchy director who drives an Uber (and introduces Kris to Cooper). There\u2019s also a millennial Web influencer named Billy Canasta (Lechristopher Williams) who lives next door and Antonia (Daniella Garcia), Kris\u2019 ambitious underling who\u2019s got her own agenda.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt has a lot of silliness and broad comedy but it has these friendships at the heart of it and this band of people finding their own family,\u201d Seehorn said. \u201cWe worked really hard on this; there are a lot of meta jokes and \u2018inside baseball\u2019 stuff but it\u2019s got those friendship at its heart.<\/p>\n

Seehorn and Bob Odenkirk as Kim Wexler and Jimmy McGill\/Saul Goodman in \u201cBetter Call Saul,\u201d which returns for its final episodes July 11 on AMC.Greg Lewis\/AMC\/Sony Pictures Tel <\/p>\n

\u201cKris, my super-villain executive, became this sort of cesspool reception area for every note or issue [with Cooper\u2019s deal] that all three of our writers have had to go through,\u201d she said. \u201cWe tried to keep it accessible; even those situations in this business where if someone gets ahead or moves out of their hometown you see that conflict, or when does one give up their dream?<\/p>\n

\u201cWe tried to include things in the story that felt universal so the whole [series] didn\u2019t feel like one big inside joke.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cCooper\u2019s Bar\u201d was shot on-location in Mustillo\u2019s backyard. \u201cWe did it for love and we are all friends and we just wanted to make something fun together,\u201d Seehorn said. \u201cI wanted to try directing again after I did Episode 604 of \u2018Saul\u2019 [\u2018Hit and Run\u2019] and this was very different \u2026 We had to shoot super-fast because we were in a real-life location and they were mostly all night shoots \u2014 we did a few daytime scenes, but not too many, and started to get these incredible co-stars who wanted to come play with us, like Phil LaMarr\u201d [who plays Kris\u2019 boss, Cyrus Long].<\/p>\n

\u201cBetter Call Saul\u201d returns to AMC for its final six episodes starting July 11 with the futures of Jimmy McGill\/Saul Goodman (Bob Odenkirk) and Kim Wexler (Seehorn) on the line with the reappearance of cold-blooded drug kingpin Lalo Salamanca (Tony Dalton), who nonchalantly blew away Howard Hamlin (Patrick Fabian) in the midseason finale.<\/p>\n

\u201cThere\u2019s no easy way to explain [what happens] and I don\u2019t mean just because I can\u2019t spoil anything,\u201d Seehorn said. \u201cIt\u2019s a bit like playing the game \u2018Taboo.\u2019 It\u2019s like if there were 30 words on the card and you\u2019re trying to make a sentence. I\u2019ve never seen something so complex, dense and very thoughtful but also very exciting. [Showrunner] Peter Gould has been very carefully sorting out complex milestones that have to be hit and things that need to be answered \u2026 it\u2019s not just connecting the dots. It\u2019s much more thoughtful.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was mesmerized by how they insisted to be completely true to the characters and advance [the story] forward,\u201d she said. \u201cIt\u2019s deeply moving and fraught and suspenseful and terrifying and devastating and very psychological.<\/p>\n
