The posh home in northern California where disgraced ex-Theranos executives Ramesh \u201cSunny\u201d Balwani and Elizabeth Holmes maintained their clandestine love affair for a decade has been sold. <\/p>\n

The house in the upscale Silicon Valley enclave of Atherton, Calif. was sold by Balwani this past January for $15.8 million \u2014 double what he paid to buy out Holmes\u2019 part-ownership of the property four years prior, according to The Information.<\/p>\n

The 6,800-square-foot home at 227 Park Lane is a one-acre property that boasts five bedrooms, seven bathrooms, a stone courtyard, swimming pool, French provincial architecture, a gated entrance, crystal chandeliers, manicured gardens and a one-bedroom guest house.<\/p>\n

During her trial last fall, Holmes, who faces up to 80 years in prison after she was convicted of defrauding investors, testified that she and Balwani created a limited liability company to buy the home in July 2013. The couple paid $9 million for the home.<\/p>\n

Ramesh \u201cSunny\u201d Balwani, who was COO of the now-defunct company Theranos, sold the house for more than $15 million.Justin Sullivan <\/p>\n

In 2018, Balwani bought out Holmes, who owned a 50% stake in the LLC, for $7.9 million. The transaction came under sharp scrutiny from prosecutors at Holmes\u2019 trial, in which Holmes testified that she had received no proceeds from the sale, according to the report. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe timing of the transfer of the property, the imminent charges [that Holmes was facing] and the fact that she received no consideration for the interest in the property\u2026it sounds like a liability protection strategy,\u201d tax advisor Robert Willens, told the publication, calling the sale \u201ca red flag\u201d for investigators. \u201cBefore an adverse judgment is about to be rendered and you are held liable for monetary damages, you want to clear out assets as quickly as possible.\u201d<\/p>\n

At the time they bought the house, Holmes was CEO of Theranos, the now-defunct healthcare start-up that claimed it had developed a technology to allow people to perform blood tests on their own using a finger prick. Balwani was the company\u2019s chief operating officer.<\/p>\n

During her trial last year, Holmes alleged that Balwani was abusive toward her during their decade-long relationship.VICKI BEHRINGER <\/p>\n

Balwani is set to stand trial for his role in fraud and deception related to blood testing machines that never did what they were claimed to do. <\/p>\n

Like his former partner \u2014 who was\u00a0found guilty on four counts of fraud\u00a0in January and is out on $500,000 bail while awaiting sentencing \u00a0\u2014 he faces multiple counts of wire fraud. Jury selection is currently underway in San Jose, Calif.<\/p>\n

During her trial, Holmes claimed her ex was physically, emotionally and sexually abusive.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe told me that I didn\u2019t know what I was doing in business, that my convictions were wrong, that he was astonished at my mediocrity and if I followed my instincts I was going to fail,\u201d she\u00a0testified. <\/p>\n

\u201cHe would force me to have sex with him when I didn\u2019t want to because he wanted me to know he still loved me.\u201d<\/p>\n

Jury selection is underway in the federal trial of Balwani, who like Holmes has been charged with defrauding investors.Stephen Lam <\/p>\n

She also alleged under oath that he exerted high degrees of control over her. <\/p>\n

Balwani is said to have told Holmes what to eat, estranged her from her family, and put her on a schedule that had her working seven days per week. <\/p>\n

According to Slate, he snooped on her text messages and hurled \u201csharp, hard objects\u201d at her.<\/p>\n

Balwani has denied Holmes\u2019 allegations. He has also pleaded not guilty to fraud charges. <\/p>\n
