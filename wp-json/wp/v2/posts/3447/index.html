Years before Ray Liotta landed meaty roles in \u201cField of Dreams\u201d and \u201cGoodfellas,\u201d the charismatic actor was rolling in dough at a pizzeria in Cranford, NJ.<\/p>\n

Liotta was boyhood pals with Vincent Preziosi, Jr., whose mom, Ronnie, now 93, and dad, Vincent Sr., started\u00a0Pizza House\/Pizza Chef\u00a0in Newark in the 1950s before relocating the business to Cranford in 1970.<\/p>\n

Liotta worked at the pizzeria during his high summer vacations from 1971 to 1973. \u201cWe were making $3.50 an hour back then,\u201d Preziosi Jr. recalled.<\/p>\n

\u201cRay was a great worker. \u2026 He used to show before me. He was a pizzamaker. He was excellent at tossing the pie, making the circle bigger.\u201d<\/p>\n

With his ice blue eyes and broad smile, Liotta was a box office draw even back then. <\/p>\n

Liotta worked at Pizza House\/Pizza Chef during his summer vacations from 1971 to 1973. Ray Liotta\u2019s yearbook photo. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe pizzeria opened at 10 a.m., but there were always five or six girls lined up outside the store about 40 minutes earlier,\u201d Preziosi Jr. said. \u201d I was like, \u2018Who eats pizza that early?\u2019 It wasn\u2019t hard to figure out. The girls were coming to see Ray.\u201d<\/p>\n

Preziosi Jr. considered the actor,\u00a0who died Thursday\u00a0at the age of 67, a dear friend who never forgot where he came from. <\/p>\n

\u201cRay was an extremely loyal, generous, funny, responsible guy. I was super shocked when I heard he passed,\u201d Preziosi Jr. told The Post from his home in Orlando, Fla.<\/p>\n

Vincent Preziosi, Jr. (left) in white trunks and Liotta (right). Preziosi, Jr. \u2014 on the left in bowtie \u2014 and Ray Liotta \u2014 wearing orange \u2014 in their second-grade class photo. <\/p>\n

About five years ago, Liotta sent the Preziosi family \u2014 currently co-owned by Ronnie and daughter, Gina, 59 \u2014 an autographed photo that reads, \u201cI miss the best pizza on the East Coast!\u201d The framed photo proudly hangs on a wall of Pizza House\/Pizza Chef, which like Liotta\u2019s work, draws rave reviews for its thin-crust and margherita pies.<\/p>\n

The two Jersey Boys followed the same path through college, attending the University of Miami, where they were roommates for a year. They made the drive to Miami in Liotta\u2019s circa-1969 orange Mercury.<\/p>\n

It was at college that Liotta majored in drama \u2014 \u201che hated math and science\u201d \u2014 before moving to the Big Apple, said Preziosi Jr., now a chiropractor.<\/p>\n

Ray Liotta poses with his old New Jersey pals, Gene Laguna and Jules Geltzeiler. <\/p>\n

In addition to Preziosi Jr., that inner circle included Gene Laguna, an insurance company owner; Jules Geltzeiler, a urologist; Freddy Silverman, a former security industry expert, and Gary Hecker, who became a Beverly Hills attorney. <\/p>\n

Liotta would say, \u201cWe\u2019re all Jersey Guys,\u201d and to that, the group, when together, \u201cdropped their share of \u2018F-bombs,’\u201d Preziosi Jr. said.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
