\u201cTop Gun 3\u201d could soon be ready for takeoff. <\/p>\n

Miles Teller has revealed he\u2019s talked with Tom Cruise about filming a third \u201cTop Gun\u201d movie, just days after their smash-hit sequel surpassed a staggering $1 billion at the box office. <\/p>\n

Teller, 35, revealed the news to \u201cEntertainment Tonight\u201d on Thursday, saying: \u201cI\u2019ve been having some conversations with him [Cruise] about it. We\u2019ll see.\u201d<\/p>\n

The prospect of another installment is sure to delight millions of \u201cTop Gun: Maverick\u201d fans who\u2019ve flocked to cinemas to see the summer blockbuster, which has also won over critics. <\/p>\n

But Teller \u2014 who plays mustachioed pilot Bradley \u201cRooster\u201d Bradshaw in the fast-paced flick \u2014 says it\u2019s ultimately up to Cruise as to whether \u201cTop Gun\u201d will become a trilogy. <\/p>\n

\u201cThat would be great, but that\u2019s all up to T.C.,\u201d Teller clarified. \u201cIt\u2019s all up to Tom.\u201d<\/p>\n

Teller and Cruise are pictured together in France back in May.FilmMagic <\/p>\n

Last month, \u201cTop Gun: Maverick\u201d director Joseph Kosinski revealed that Cruise \u201creally didn\u2019t want to\u201d make a sequel to the original 1986 blockbuster, in which he famously played Pete \u201cMaverick\u201d Mitchell. <\/p>\n

In an interview with Polygon, Kosinski recalled reading the script for the sequel before flying to Paris in a bid to persuade Cruise to sign on. <\/p>\n

\u201cBasically had 30 minutes to pitch this film \u2026 But when I got there, I found that Tom really didn\u2019t want to make another \u2018Top Gun,\u2019\u201d the director stated. <\/p>\n

Cruise is pictured reprising his role as Pete \u201cMaverick\u201d Mitchell in the smash-hit sequel.\u00a9Paramount\/Courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

However, Kosinski convinced Cruise the flick would be a hit, and the \u201cMission: Impossible\u201d superstar subsequently used his incredible influence to help get the movie made. <\/p>\n

Kosinski recalled Cruise picking up his phone and calling the head of Paramount Pictures, defiantly telling the exec: \u201cWe\u2019re making another \u2018Top Gun.’\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was pretty impressive to see the power of a real movie star in that moment,\u201d the director declared. <\/p>\n

Cruise is seen in the original \u201cTop Gun\u201d movie, released back in 1986.Corbis via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Indeed, it was the right move for Cruise to reprise his role as the fighter pilot, with \u201cTop Gun: Maverick\u201d becoming his best-performing film ever. <\/p>\n

Last week, it surpassed $1 billion in international box-office receipts, becoming the highest-grossing film around the world for 2022 so far. <\/p>\n

The action movie has surpassed Disney\u2019s Marvel adventure \u201cDoctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness,\u201d which has made $943 million at theaters across the globe. <\/p>\n
