<\/p>\n

Former President Donald Trump said on Monday that he won\u2019t return to his former favorite social media site if new owner Elon Musk lifts Twitter\u2019s controversial permanent ban against him.<\/p>\n

Trump told Fox News that he planned to instead use his own fledgling Truth Social app, which launched in February, because his platform is \u201cmuch better\u201d than Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am not going on Twitter, I am going to stay on Truth,\u201d Trump said.<\/p>\n

\u201cI hope Elon buys Twitter because he\u2019ll make improvements to it and he is a good man, but I am going to be staying on Truth.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trump said Truth Social was \u201cmuch better than being on Twitter,\u201d adding: \u201cTwitter has bots and fake accounts.\u201d<\/p>\n

He also said that Twitter \u201cbecame very boring because conservatives were thrown off or got off the platform when I left.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trump told Fox News that he planned to begin posting regularly on Truth Social within a week.<\/p>\n

He hasn\u2019t been heard from on the site since an introductory February message that said, \u201cGet Ready! Your favorite President will see you soon!\u201d<\/p>\n

Former President Donald Trump prefers using the Truth app over Twitter.AP\/Joe Maiorana <\/p>\n

Twitter agreed Monday to accept a $44 billion takeover offer from Musk, the world\u2019s richest man and a self-described \u201cfree-speech absolutist.\u201d<\/p>\n

Trump was banned from Twitter and other social-media platforms after his supporters stormed the US Capitol on Jan. 6 and disrupted congressional certification of his electoral loss to President Joe Biden.<\/p>\n

Twitter cited \u201cthe risk of further incitement of violence\u201d by Trump, who was a prolific tweeter with nearly 90 million followers.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk is now the new owner of Twitter.John Lamparski\/Sipa USA Trump has hope Musk will improve Twitter immensely. AP\/Gregory Bull <\/p>\n

Truth Social has been plagued by problems since its free iPhone app was unveiled, with would-be users put on a long waitlist and Android owners shut out entirely.<\/p>\n

Two key execs resigned earlier this month and the share price of the company that plans to take it public has plunged nearly 40 percent since Musk disclosed he\u2019d bought a stake in Twitter.<\/p>\n

Truth Social, whose CEO is Trump ally and former US Rep. Devin Nunes (R-Calif.), switched over to a new, cloud-based hosting service four days ago, Fox News said.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
