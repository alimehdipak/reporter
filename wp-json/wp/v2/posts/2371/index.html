Elon Musk said he was \u201cbuying Coca-Cola to put the cocaine back in\u201d as he joked about what he plans to do next following his $44 billion acquisition of Twitter. <\/p>\n

In a late Wednesday gab session with his 87 million Twitter followers, Musk then re-posted a meme suggesting that he should buy McDonald\u2019s in order to fix the restaurant chain\u2019s chronically broken ice cream machines.<\/p>\n

\u201cListen, I can\u2019t do miracles ok,\u201d he tweeted.<\/p>\n

Musk vowed to make Twitter \u201cmaximum fun\u201d as he continued to drop hints how he intends to run the company after its board accepted his $44 billion buyout bid earlier this week as the world\u2019s richest man.<\/p>\n

Musk posted several tweets late Wednesday night and into Thursday morning in which he offered some suggestions to upgrade the site.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter DMs should have end to end encryption like Signal, so no one can spy on or hack your messages,\u201d Musk tweeted.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s $44 billion takeover bid was accepted by Twitter\u2019s board of directors earlier this week.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Musk has vowed to buy Twitter and alter its content moderation policies so as to honor its original mission as a platform that promotes free speech.<\/p>\n

He reiterated the pledge on Wednesday night, tweeting: \u201cFor Twitter to deserve public trust, it must be politically neutral, which effectively means upsetting the far right and the far left equally.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk also appeared to be enjoying the memes that have been posted mocking Twitter employees who are reportedly upset over the Tesla\u2019s boss\u2019 plans to acquire the San Francisco-based company.<\/p>\n

When Twitter user Kyle Mann wrote that his post of a Babylon Bee video depicting a Twitter employee who \u201cundergoes therapy due to imminent @elonmusk takeover\u201d was flagged by the service for \u201csensitive content,\u201d Musk replied with two laughing hysterically emojis.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, some experts are predicting that Musk won\u2019t be buying Twitter after all. They view his tweets critical of the company \u2014 which is reportedly in violation of his pledge not to criticize the platform \u2014 as a sign of a lack of seriousness.<\/p>\n

Wall Street analysts point to the impact that Musk\u2019s imminent takeover of Twitter has had on his electric car company, Tesla.<\/p>\n

Tesla has lost more than $100 billion in value as investors speculated that Musk may have to sell shares to fund his $21 billion equity contribution to his $44 billion buyout of Twitter.<\/p>\n

Tesla is not involved in the Twitter deal, yet its shares have been targeted by speculators after Musk declined to disclose publicly where his cash for the acquisition is coming from. <\/p>\n

The 12.2% drop in Tesla\u2019s shares on Tuesday equated to a $21 billion drop in the value of his Tesla stake, the same as the $21 billion in cash he committed to the Twitter deal.<\/p>\n

Wedbush Securities analyst Daniel Ives said that worries about upcoming stock sales by Musk and the possibility that he is becoming distracted by Twitter weighed on Tesla shares. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis (is) causing a bear festival on the name,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s pledge to alter content moderation could run afoul of European Union regulations that require social media platforms to crack down on hate speech.<\/p>\n

There is also the question of how Musk\u2019s acquisition of Twitter, which has angered authorities in China for allowing protesters in Hong Kong to post freely about their pro-democracy struggle, will affect the car company\u2019s operations in the country.<\/p>\n

If Musk were to acquire Twitter, China could threaten to limit Tesla\u2019s footprint in the country if he did not play ball and heed the Chinese Communist Party\u2019s dictates as it relates to the social media platform \u2014 undermining his \u201cfree speech absolutist\u201d credentials.<\/p>\n

Amazon founder Jeff Bezos trolled\u00a0Musk\u2019s takeover of Twitter, questioning whether it could wind up empowering China \u2014 only to later conclude his theory was \u201cprobably\u201d far-fetched.<\/p>\n

\u201cMusk\u2019s tweets criticizing some company actions \u2014 potentially flouting the merger agreement \u2014 already suggest he might be starting to lose interest,\u201d according to Reuters BreakingViews.<\/p>\n

\u201cMost likely, Musk\u2019s attention will wander elsewhere. It wouldn\u2019t be the first time.\u201d<\/p>\n

While Musk continues to tweet freely about the company he is set to acquire, a federal judge ruled against him to do the same about Tesla.<\/p>\n

US District Court Judge Lewis Liman on Wednesday rejected Musk\u2019s bid to end a 2018 consent decree limiting his ability to tweet about Tesla, dealing a blow to the billionaire in his ongoing feud with the SEC.<\/p>\n
