An extreme take on the micro bikini trend has taken over Miami Swim Week.<\/p>\n

The \u201cloincloth\u201d bikini, a two-piece swimwear set with bottoms that resemble the ancient style of clothing, has been steadily gathering momentum the past few years.<\/p>\n

But the loincloth has officially exploded now, with numerous brands featuring the unique swimmers during the six-day runway event.<\/p>\n

However they\u2019ve had an extreme makeover, using a much smaller section of fabric and becoming much more revealing.<\/p>\n

Australian swimwear brand VDM The Label featured multiple \u201cloincloth\u201d bikinis, in an array of colors including pink, blue and orange.<\/p>\n

One model strutted her stuff in a skimpy floral print loincloth and matching itsy bitsy bra, while another rocked the design with an \u201cupside down\u201d bikini top in a bright turquoise blue.<\/p>\n

A new skimpier take on the \u2018loincloth\u2019 bikini trend has exploded at Miami Swim Week 2022.Getty Images for VDM The Label <\/p>\n

\u201cThe collection was inspired by the beautiful Amalfi Coast\u201d, Maddy Marchesani, the Australian brand\u2019s designer, said.<\/p>\n

VDM The Label states on its website it is \u201cbreaking boundaries further than ever before\u201d, by \u201ctaking new leaps and bounds\u201d on its swimwear designs.<\/p>\n

Another brand that showcased a number of \u201cloincloth\u201d bikinis were LA-brand Normal Culture whose social media post featuring a model in a pink and camo print design caused a stir.<\/p>\n

The swimsuit style leaves little to the imagination. Getty Images for VDM The Label <\/p>\n

\u201cYou\u2019re kidding me,\u201d one user wrote, as another said: \u201cThis is so intense.\u201d<\/p>\n

Beau Swim also had a similar design that featured two straps across the hips, creating a sexy pattern on the skin.<\/p>\n

The \u201cethically made luxury swimwear\u201d brand also had a one-piece which had straps crisscross across the body.<\/p>\n

A model walks the runway for VDM The Label Fashion Show during Paraiso Miami Beach at The Paraiso Tent on July 17, 2022 in Miami Beach, Florida. Getty Images for VDM The Label <\/p>\n

Earlier in the event, Miami Swim Week 2022 spectators saw the\u00a0return of duct tape bikinis.<\/p>\n

However this year, the controversial swimwear trend \u2013 created by Black Tape Project \u2013\u00a0appeared to be skimpier than ever.<\/p>\n

A parade of models wearing the sticky tape designs strutted their stuff in front of a packed audience \u2013 with one revealing on Instagram it took 12 hours to get into her \u201cbikini\u201d.<\/p>\n

Chanel\u2019s swimmers were in an intricate design that just about covered her modesty, but put her entire body on full display.<\/p>\n

The black tape snaked up her left leg and around her wrists, covering her bust and crotch areas and little else.<\/p>\n

Another model\u2019s \u201cbikini\u201d was equally as jaw-dropping, in a minuscule Borat-style one-piece that left little to the imagination.<\/p>\n
