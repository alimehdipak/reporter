Elon Musk said Twitter\u2019s move to ban The New York Post\u2019s exclusive story about Hunter Biden\u2019s overseas business dealings ahead of the 2020 presidential election was \u201cobviously incredibly inappropriate.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk, who is set to buy the social media platform, wrote his opinion Tuesday evening in response to reporter Saagar Enjeti, who tweeted a Politico article about how the social media company\u2019s top lawyer was brought to tears when discussing its new ownership.<\/p>\n

\u201cVijaya Gadde, the top censorship advocate at Twitter who famously gaslit the world on Joe Rogan\u2019s podcast and censored the Hunter Biden laptop story, is very upset about the @elonmusk takeover,\u201d Enjeti wrote.<\/p>\n

\u201cSuspending the Twitter account of a major news organization for publishing a truthful story was obviously incredibly inappropriate,\u201d Musk replied.<\/p>\n

The Post\u2019s expos\u00e9 revealed the existence of tens of thousands of emails that detailed how the then-presidential candidate\u2019s son leveraged his political access in a series of eyebrow-raising overseas business deals after the newspaper obtained a copy of the hard drive from the laptop.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe images contained in the articles include personal and private information \u2014 like email addresses and phone numbers \u2014 which violate our rules,\u201d the company tweeted about the article at the time.<\/p>\n

Many mainstream outlets also discredited the article, before later reporting on its veracity long after President Joe Biden was elected.<\/p>\n
