After \u201cdying to go\u201d to the Met Gala \u201cfor years,\u201d New York City Mayor Eric Adams finally got his wish.<\/p>\n

Adams, 61, arrived wearing a statement suit, making a political splash on the red carpet alongside girlfriend Tracey Collins, who wore a white Oscar de la Renta gown with iridescent flourishes. <\/p>\n

Adorned with custom art from Laolu, a Nigerian artist who is based in New York City, the mayor\u2019s outfit made a plea to \u201cEnd Gun Violence.\u201d<\/p>\n

The suit was already hanging in his closet and Laolu turned it into his canvas, drawing the phrase with a pistol in a red circle with a prohibitive slash across it.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe mayor has a great sense of style. And he came up with the idea\u201d of working with Laolu, Adam\u2019s press secretary Fabien Levy told The Post. \u201cHe wants to send a message specifically. One of the things that is causing problems in our society is gun violence.\u201d<\/p>\n

New York City Mayor Eric Adams arriving at the red carpet for the 2022 Met Gala. <\/p>\n

Levy added that the faces on the jacket represent young children who lost their lives to senseless gun violence.<\/p>\n

Laolu, who practiced law in his native Nigeria, infuses his art with his Yoruba heritage and aesthetic, and he has worked with other celebrities such as Taraji P. Henson, Lupita Nyongo, Alicia Keys, Swizz Beats and Beyonce.<\/p>\n

But the artist and the mayor also kept with the gala\u2019s theme of \u201cGilded Glamour.\u201d And so the suit features depictions of other enduring Big Apple innovations that came about during the Gilded Age, including the Brooklyn Bridge and the railways.<\/p>\n

Then there are nods to the subways, such as the R and the N train, the same line where accused subway shooter Frank James opened fire inside a crowded train last month, shooting ten and leaving another 19 injured.<\/p>\n

His jacket read \u201cEND GUN VIOLENCE\u201d on the back.Getty Images for The Met Museum\/ <\/p>\n

Adams\u2019 move to make a political statement on the red carpet follows Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, who wore a gown last year that read \u201cTAX THE RICH.\u201d<\/p>\n

His look was also complete with a fresh pedicure, which he debuted on Twitter. His Monday appointment confirmed his appearance on one of celebrity fashion\u2019s biggest nights after some speculation.<\/p>\n

Known for his keen sense of fashion, Adams has kept up his style while serving as mayor, most notably his custom suits from the high-end Mohan Custom Tailors, which ring in at $2,000 apiece.<\/p>\n

And he\u2019s no stranger to the fashion-forward life, having attended New York Fashion Week in February \u2014\u00a0seated next to Anna Wintour, no less. He\u2019s also chummy with Naomi Campbell and Jon Batiste, and has been pictured with Cara Delevingne and A$AP Rocky.<\/p>\n

His suit had a graphic lapel and forearms, which matched the back of the coat.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Despite the life of seeming glitz, glamor and luxury, lawyer and lobbyist Brad Gerstman, who listed Adams on his Best Dressed Politicians roundup, said the mayor isn\u2019t allowed to accept clothes for free or discounted \u2014\u00a0including for the Met Gala.<\/p>\n

Known now as the mayor of nightlife in the Big Apple, the Met Gala won\u2019t be his first rodeo \u2014 and probably not his last \u2014 after appearing at other star-studded events, such as the opening night of \u201cThe Music Man\u201d on Broadway earlier this year.<\/p>\n
