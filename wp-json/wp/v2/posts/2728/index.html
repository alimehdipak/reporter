Donald Trump quietly \u201cencouraged\u201d\u00a0Elon Musk\u2019s takeover of social media giant Twitter\u00a0ahead of the $44 billion deal, according to the boss of his own social-media app Truth Social \u2014 though Musk quickly disputed the claim.<\/p>\n

\u201cPresident Trump, basically before Elon Musk bought it, actually said to go and buy it because the goal of our company is really to build a community where people are in a family-friendly, safe environment,\u201d Devin Nunes said in an appearance on\u00a0Fox Business Network.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat\u2019s why we encouraged Elon Musk to buy it, because someone has to take on these tech tyrants,\u201d Nunes said. \u201cDonald Trump wanted to make sure that the American people got their voice back and that the internet was open and that\u2019s what we are doing.\u201d<\/p>\n

Nunes, a former House GOP lawmaker who is CEO of Trump Media & Technology Group, added that Truth Social was \u201call for it.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk\u00a0denied Nunes\u2019 account\u00a0in a tweet following publication of this article, writing, \u201cThis is false. I\u2019ve had no communication, directly or indirectly, with Trump, who has publicly stated that he will be exclusively on Truth Social.\u201d<\/p>\n

Truth Social launched in the App Store earlire this year.dpa\/picture alliance via Getty I Donald Trump has said he won\u2019t return to Twitter even if Elon Musk buys the company and unblocks his account.David Butow <\/p>\n

Trump recently insisted that he has no interest in rejoining Twitter. However, news of the Twitter buyout tanked shares of Digital World Acquisition Corp, the company that owns Truth Social.<\/p>\n

Truth Social executives portray the platform as a social media alternative for conservatives who feel they are being censored on mainstream Big Tech platforms. Musk\u2019s pledge to re-center Twitter with a focus on free speech is a likely setback for Truth Social and other right-leaning upstarts.<\/p>\n

Shares of Digital World Acquisition Corp., the SPAC planning to take Truth Social public if it can gain regulatory approval, are down more than 16% since Musk first disclosed his Twitter stake.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk is buying Twitter for $44 billion.WireImage <\/p>\n

Nunes claimed that Truth Social users \u201cget much more engagement on our platform\u201d than Twitter, even though the app has \u201cbarely been open.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s buy has also sparked speculation that Trump\u2019s Twitter account could be reinstated after it was permanently suspended in the wake of the Jan. 6 Capitol riot. Trump has said he will not return to Twitter even if the deal is completed.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am not going on Twitter, I am going to stay on Truth,\u201d Trump told Fox News. \u201cI hope Elon buys Twitter because he\u2019ll make improvements to it and he is a good man, but I am going to be staying on Truth.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk personally addressed Truth Social last month, noting the firm was leading the Apple App Store\u2019s most-downloaded list. He also poked fun at the app\u2019s name.<\/p>\n

Donald Trump launched Truth Social on February 21, 2022.REUTERS <\/p>\n

\u201cTruth Social (terrible name) exists because Twitter censored free speech,\u201d Musk tweeted. \u201cShould be called Trumpet instead!\u201d<\/p>\n

Truth Social has struggled since its debut in the App Store in February, with some users unable to access the app or stuck on a long wait list.<\/p>\n

As The Post reported, Musk spent the last several days\u00a0amassing the necessary funding\u00a0to buy Twitter after the company\u2019s board accepted his $44 billion offer.<\/p>\n
