Oddsmakers say there\u2019s a 90% chance that former President Donald Trump rejoins Twitter if Tesla CEO Elon Musk completes his takeover of the company.<\/p>\n

Musk has vowed to restore Twitter\u2019s original mission as a platform that promotes free speech \u2014 a prospect that has energized Republicans and conservatives while upsetting Democrats and liberals.<\/p>\n

Sports bookies now say that a Musk-owned Twitter means there\u2019s a high probability that Trump will be back on the site, according to OLBG.<\/p>\n

Oddsmakers also say there\u2019s a 99% chance that Musk will institute an edit button for tweets.<\/p>\n

They also said there\u2019s a 50% chance that Jack Dorsey, the company co-founder who stepped down as CEO late last year, will return to the social media platform in some capacity.<\/p>\n

Musk, whose $44 billion takeover bid was accepted by Twitter\u2019s board of directors earlier this week, has vowed to restore the platform\u2019s commitment to free speech.via REUTERS <\/p>\n

Jeff Bezos, who speculated that Musk\u2019s imminent acquisition of Twitter will benefit China, could start his own rival social media platform, though the likelihood is slim, according to oddsmakers. There\u2019s just a 10% chance that will happen, bookies claim.<\/p>\n

Twitter\u2019s board of directors accepted Musk\u2019s $44 billion buyout offer earlier this week \u2014 sparking anticipation that he will reverse some of the company\u2019s most high-profile decisions, including de-platforming the 45th president.<\/p>\n

Trump was removed from Twitter for his role in the Jan. 6, 2021 riots at the US Capitol.<\/p>\n

Trump was removed from Twitter over his role in the Jan. 6, 2021 US Capitol riots.EPA <\/p>\n

The former president launched his own rival social media app, Truth Social, but technical glitches and an exodus of key executives have hampered its growth.<\/p>\n

Trump has stated on numerous occasions that he has no interest in returning to Twitter \u2014 even if Musk assumes control.<\/p>\n

Trump told\u00a0Fox News\u00a0that he planned to instead use his own fledgling Truth Social app, which launched in February, because his platform is \u201cmuch better\u201d than Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am not going on Twitter, I am going to stay on Truth,\u201d Trump said.<\/p>\n
