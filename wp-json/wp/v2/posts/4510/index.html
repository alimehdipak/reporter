A family of innovators and adventurers<\/h2>\n

Given Elon Musk\u2019s interest in exploration and adventure, it\u2019s no surprise he descends from intrepid stock, and it\u2019s Errol\u2019s side of the family that is seemingly responsible for his innovative, envelope-pushing genes. <\/p>\n

Errol\u2019s grandmother was Canada\u2019s first chiropractor, while Errol\u2019s own mom and dad were the first two people to fly from South Africa to Australia in a single-engine airplane, according to Forbes magazine.<\/p>\n

Errol himself has an adventurous streak and is an avid sailor and pilot. He began his career as an electromechanical engineer. <\/p>\n

In 1970, Errol married model\u00a0Maye Haldeman Musk before the pair welcomed three children: Elon, Kimbal and Tosca.<\/p>\n

When Errol and Maye divorced in 1979, a then-8-year-old Elon chose to live with his dad. <\/p>\n

According to Ashlee Vance\u2019s 2017 biography of the Tesla founder, \u201cThe Musk family owned one of the biggest houses in Pretoria thanks to the success of Errol\u2019s engineering business,\u201d which included \u201clarge projects such as office buildings, retail complexes, residential subdivisions, and an air force base.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Daily Mail has reported that Errol was a multimillionaire before the age of 30. <\/p>\n

A violent act<\/h2>\n

On an undisclosed date, Errol reportedly shot and killed three of up to a half-dozen armed intruders who broke into his Johannesburg home, according to Rolling Stone. <\/p>\n

The patriarch confirmed the shootings to the publication in 2017, saying manslaughter charges were later dropped because he acted in self-defense. <\/p>\n

It\u2019s unclear how old Elon was at the time \u2014 and whether he was even present is in dispute.<\/p>\n

However, in the recent Channel 4 documentary \u201cElon Musk: Superhero or Supervillain,\u201d co-founder of SpaceX, Jim Cattrell, claimed Elon was home at the time. In an interview with Express, Cattrell recalled: \u201cOne night, they were sitting in the living room and his dad said \u2018Everybody down! Get down!’\u201d<\/p>\n

He continued: \u201cHe grabbed some weapons, as you could see on the cameras there were some intruders that were coming over the walls, and they were also armed. There was a shooting in the living room, and I guess his father shot them dead.<\/p>\n

\u201cElon was a witness to this, bullets flying around,\u201d Cattrell further claimed. \u201cSo it was very formative for him. You could tell by the way that he told the story. He had this sense that it was definitely not a safe place to be, and he had to get the hell out.\u201d<\/p>\n
