Here are some reasons to use your dental plan before time runs out.<\/p>\n

1. Annual maximum doesn\u2019t carry over<\/b><\/h2>\n

The Annual maximum or annual benefit maximum is the total amount of money that a dental insurance plan will pay for your dental care<\/a> within 12-months. 2. Premium by-monthly plan<\/b><\/h2>\n

If you\u2019re paying premium dental insurance through your employer, you\u2019ll see deductions in each paycheck. This means if you don\u2019t use the benefit, your money is wasted. 3. Fee typically increases at the start of the year<\/b><\/h2>\n

Another reason you should use your benefits now is the possibility of a fee increase at the start of the next year. Yes, many dentists increase their prices due to the increased cost of equipment, living, and materials. This will also increase the cost of your dental work. So, why shouldn\u2019t we use a dental plan when the prices are low?<\/p>\n

4. Oral health issues can worsen if left untreated<\/b><\/h2>\n

Delaying dental treatment is not a good idea in any circumstances. By delaying the treatment, you\u2019re risking more expensive and severe treatment in the future. You might end up turning a simple tooth decay procedure into a root canal problem simply by delaying the treatment.<\/p>\n
