<\/p>\n

A SpaceX launch left a wild phenomenon in the sky as the Falcon 9 rocket traveled to space at break-neck speeds.<\/p>\n

Clear weather conditions and early morning sun rays lit up the rocket\u2019s exhaust plume creating a \u201cspace jellyfish\u201d.<\/p>\n

On average, Elon Musk and SpaceX will launch a rocket once a week this year.<\/p>\n

This morning, a Falcon 9 rocket carrying 53 Starlink satellites made a successful launch and left behind a fascinating glow in the sky.<\/p>\n

\u201cBasically, what\u2019s happening is, it\u2019s still dark outside, but you have the sun illuminating the plume as it\u2019s in space,\u201d a SpaceX executive said.<\/p>\n

The rocket took off at 5.43 am this morning from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida.<\/p>\n

When the Falcon 9 rocket lifts off with almost two million pounds of thrust, the fuel is exploded in a combustion chamber.<\/p>\n

In a paper published by the Glenn Research Center, NASA wrote \u201cThe combustion produces hot exhaust which is passed through a nozzle to accelerate the flow and produce thrust.\u201d<\/p>\n

The hot exhaust was illuminated as the Falcon 9 reached heights where sun rays were beginning to fill the early morning sky.<\/p>\n

Musk tweeted \u201cAnother Starlink mission completed\u201d after the satellites were deposited in orbit.<\/p>\n

SpaceX\u2019s Falcon 9 rocket launch created a jaw-dropping \u201cspace jellyfish,\u201d out of its exhaust fumes thanks to a clear sky.SpaceX \/ YouTube SpaceX is aiming to launch more batches of its Starlink internet satellites.Malcolm Denemark\/Florida Today via AP <\/p>\n

Starlink is a subdivision of SpaceX that beams high-speed internet to customers all over the globe via satellite.<\/p>\n

Musk earned heroic treatment from the press when he fast-tracked Starlink terminals to Ukraine at the start of the Russian invasion.<\/p>\n

This most recent payload adds to the 132 metric tonnes of equipment SpaceX has deposited in lower Earth orbit.<\/p>\n

The clear weather conditions were perfect for the \u201cspace jellyfish,\u201d to form from exhaust fumes.SpaceX \/ YouTube <\/p>\n

The next launch is scheduled for May 10th \u2013 another round of Starlink satellites will blast off on a Falcon 9 rocket from the Vandenberg Space Force Base in California.<\/p>\n

While SpaceX and Starlink are ahead of the pack on satellites, Jeff Bezos\u2019 space company is gaining ground.<\/p>\n

Project Kuiper is Amazon\u2019s internet-from-space initiative and they have been given clearance from the FCC to put over 3,000 satellites in lower Earth orbit.<\/p>\n
