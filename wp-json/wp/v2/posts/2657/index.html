Mickey Gilley, country music star and owner of a famed eponymous Texas honky-tonk that inspired the movie \u201cUrban Cowboy,\u201d died Saturday at the age of 86.<\/p>\n

Gilley \u201cpassed peacefully with his family and close friends by his side\u201d in Branson, Missouri, a statement from Mickey Gilley Associates said.<\/p>\n

The \u201cWindow Up Above\u201d singer and piano player, who was a cousin of rock legend Jerry Lee Lewis, had performed as recently as last month but had been in declining health in the past week.<\/p>\n

He opened Gilley\u2019s, \u201cthe world\u2019s largest honky tonk,\u201d in the early 1970s in Pasadena, Texas. Several years later he hit the charts with \u201cRoom Full of Roses\u201d and enjoyed follow-up success with a string of hits like \u201cDon\u2019t the Girls All Get Prettier at Closing Time\u201d and \u201cShe\u2019s Pulling Me Back Again.\u201d<\/p>\n

Gilley had 39 Top 10 country hits over the course of his career, including 17 No. 1 records. In addition, he was known for his acting roles in shows like \u201cMurder, She Wrote\u201d and \u201cThe Dukes of Hazzard.\u201d<\/p>\n

Mickey Gilley was known for his song \u201cWindow Up Above.\u201dMike Stone\/REUTERS Mickey Gilley shows off his diamond rings during the 34th Annual Academy of Country Music Awards.Kevork Djansezian\/AP <\/p>\n

An Esquire article about the nightspot Gilley\u2019s inspired the 1980 John Travolta film \u201cUrban Cowboy,\u201d which was filmed at the bar and gave rise to a nationwide trend of pearl snap shirts, longneck beers and mechanical bulls.<\/p>\n

The club was shut down in the late \u201980s and was later destroyed in a fire. A high-end version of the honky-tonk opened in Dallas in 2003.<\/p>\n

The Natchez, Mississippi, native grew up poor and learned boogie woogie piano by sneaking into Louisiana rhythm and blues clubs with Lewis and cousin Jimmy Swaggart, a future Pentecostal televangelist.<\/p>\n

Mickey Gilley\u2019s honkey-tonk venue inspired the movie \u201cUrban Cowboy.\u201dMediaPunch \/ BACKGRID <\/p>\n

\u201cIf I had one wish in life, I would wish for more time,\u201d Gilley told The Associated Press in March 2001 as he celebrated his 65th birthday. Not that he\u2019d do anything differently, the singer said.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am doing exactly what I want to do. I play golf, fly my airplane and perform at my theater in Branson, Missouri,\u201d he said. \u201cI love doing my show for the people.\u201d<\/p>\n
