Say what you will about Amber Heard, but her face is almost perfect \u2014 according to the so-called Golden Ratio.<\/p>\n

As the dust settles over ex-husband Johnny Depp\u2019s defamation case against her, a trial that enthralled the nation for weeks, facts about the embattled actress are resurfacing in the media. <\/p>\n

Fortunately, it\u2019s not all bad for Heard: Despite having to pay $15 million in damages after a Virginia jury ruled that her case didn\u2019t hold up in court, at least she\u2019s still got a near-flawless face, according to scientific standards.<\/p>\n

First described by the ancient Greeks, the Golden Ratio (numerically: 1.618) refers to a formula of proportions that have been purported to identify the most mathematically perfect visage. <\/p>\n

British cosmetic surgeon Dr. Julian De Silva shared his rationale for declaring Heard\u2019s face exactly 91.85% close to perfect \u2014 about as good as it gets \u2014 by using digital facial-mapping technology.<\/p>\n

British cosmetic surgeon Dr. Julian De Silva used facial-mapping technology to find that Amber Heard\u2019s face is 91.85% close to perfect, according to the Golden Ratio.Gamma-Rapho via Getty Images Signified by the Greek letter phi (\u03a6), the Golden Ratio is calculated as 1.618 \u2014 which is demonstrated in this mathematically perfect spiral shape.Getty Images\/iStockphoto \t \t <\/p>\n

The Golden Ratio, alternatively signified by the Greek letter phi (\u03a6), can be observed in all manner of life and design. And the closer the proportions of any given object are to phi, the more perfectly balanced they appear.<\/p>\n

De Silva analyzed 12 points on Heard\u2019s face \u2014 using a red carpet image from 2016 \u2014 to measure between her eyes, nose, lips, chin and overall head to get the nearly 92% score.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe Greeks discovered that the ratio occurs everywhere in nature, and for thousands of years it has been thought to hold the secret formula of the world\u2019s most beautiful faces,\u201d the doctor told US Weekly in 2016.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe phi ratio of 1.618 has long been thought to hold the secret for beauty, but now with the computer mapping, we can calculate how it applies to real women,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

De Silva, who runs the Centre for Advanced Facial Cosmetic & Plastic Surgery in London, has applied the same formula to countless well-known beauties. According to his research, Kim Kardashian has the most perfect eyebrows in the industry; Emily Ratajkowski has the most enviable lips; Kate Moss has the right forehead; and Scarlett Johansson has the most fetching eyes.<\/p>\n

But it\u2019s Bella Hadid who scored highest of them all overall, with 94.35%, followed by Beyonc\u00e9 (92.44%), Heard and, in fourth place, Ariana Grande (91.81%).<\/p>\n
