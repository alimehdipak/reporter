A petition to see Amber Heard ousted from the \u201cAquaman\u201d sequel is going swimmingly.<\/p>\n

The 36-year-old actress \u2014 who is currently embattled in a hefty trial with ex-husband Johnny Depp \u2014 has very little screen time in next year\u2019s follow-up to the 2018 DC film, according to film journalist Grace Randolph, who has a rep for breaking this type of comic book flick scoop, TMZ first reported.<\/p>\n

Heard, who plays Mera in the \u201cAquaman and the Lost Kingdom\u201d alongside Jason Momoa\u2019s Aquaman, will see \u201cless than 10 minutes of screen time\u201d in the sequel.<\/p>\n

The news comes as a petition continues to circulate online that calls for Heard to be axed from the film altogether, which has just reached 3 million signatures. <\/p>\n

\u201cDo the right thing. Remove Amber Heard from Aquaman 2,\u201d Jeanne Larson wrote in the Change.org description of the petition, which she created back in 2020.<\/p>\n

As one petition to see Amber Heard axed from the \u201cAquaman\u201d sequel reaches 3 million signatures, an insider has revealed new details about her role in the 2023 film.AP Amber Heard during her ongoing defamation trial with ex-husband Johnny Depp.EPA <\/p>\n

The superhero film\u2019s producer, Peter Safran,\u00a0shut down speculation in July 2021 about Heard\u2019s role.<\/p>\n

Safran told\u00a0Deadline at the time, \u201cI don\u2019t think that we\u2019re ever going to react to, honestly, pure fan pressure. You gotta do what you feel is best for the movie. We felt that if it\u2019s [director] James Wan and Jason Momoa, it should be Amber Heard. That\u2019s really what it was.\u201d<\/p>\n

Heard starred in 2018\u2019s \u201cAquaman\u201d as Mera.\u00a9Warner Bros\/courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

\u201cListen, one is not unaware of what is going on in the Twitterverse, but that doesn\u2019t mean you have to react to it or take it as gospel or accede to their wishes,\u201d Safran added. \u201cYou have to do what you feel is right for the film, and that\u2019s really where we landed on it.\u201d<\/p>\n

The sequel was originally scheduled for a theatrical release on Dec. 16, 2022, then bumped to March 2023.<\/p>\n

Johnny Depp arrives to the courtroom at in Fairfax, Virginia, on Monday, May 2.AP <\/p>\n

Depp, 58, was been dumped by Disney from his role as\u00a0Captain Jack Sparrow in the fifth \u201cPirates of the Caribbean\u201d movie after Heard alleged she suffered abuse during their nearly two-year marriage in a 2018 op-ed. Citing damage to his professional reputation as a result of the essay, Depp has sued Heard for defamation, asking for more than $50 million in return.<\/p>\n

She reportedly switched from crisis p.r. firm Precision Strategies to Shane Communications following widespread negative press amid her court battle.<\/p>\n
