<\/p>\n

An architecture firm has plans to design a metaverse centering around self-governance.<\/p>\n

Zaha Hadid Architects (ZHA) \u2013 one of the world\u2019s most prominent architecture firms \u2013 has shared plans to develop a utopian\u00a0digital world, according to a new\u00a0report\u00a0by CNN.<\/p>\n

Several illustrations of the futuristic and urban\u00a0metaverse, designated Liberland, were also shared by ZHA.<\/p>\n

Curved buildings, a city hall, collaborative working spaces, shops, and business incubators can be found throughout the virtual platform.<\/p>\n

Perhaps most notably, the architecture firm\u2019s digital community will focus on self-governing ideals based on Liberland \u2013 a micronation founded in 2015 by Czech politician Vit Jedlicka.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf you start off with a clean environment, and\u2026you don\u2019t throw garbage around, that can be maintained without much policing,\u201d ZHA principal architect Patrik Schumacher told CNN.<\/p>\n

Liberland Metaverse is based on the Republic of Liberland.Zaha Hadid Architects <\/p>\n

The real Liberland is situated between Serbia and Croatia and has 7,000 approved residents.\u00a0<\/p>\n

While the micronation is not recognized by any other country, it boasts its own flag, anthem, and cryptocurrency: the Liberland merit.<\/p>\n

And now, the micronation \u201cis hoping to stake its claim in the virtual world,\u201d CNN writes.<\/p>\n

A metaverse, which functions on a decentralized platform and utilizes blockchain technology, is an ideal space for libertarianism ideals (such as those adopted by Liberland) to thrive, Schumacher said.<\/p>\n

The architect added that he has been fascinated by Liberland for a number of years now.<\/p>\n

Some of the designs are based on real-life infrastructures.Zaha Hadid Architects <\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s a very lively scene of contributors\u2026a lot of IT and crypto and tech entrepreneurs who find the world too restrictive,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Libertarian philosophy isn\u2019t the only inspiration developers are taking from the real world to create the Liberland metaverse.<\/p>\n

The virtual platform\u2019s aesthetics and digital infrastructure are also meant to be based on real-life designs; it even mimics the same border shape as the Free Republic of Liberland.<\/p>\n

In particular, the buildings were created with a parametric design \u2013 an algorithm that generates complex forms.<\/p>\n

\u201cI believe the metaverse is not a fantasy world. It\u2019s not a video game,\u201d Schumacher said.<\/p>\n

The real Liberland is situated between Serbia and Croatia and has 7,000 approved residents. Zaha Hadid Architects <\/p>\n

\u201c[Liberland metaverse\u2019s architecture] is unusual and unique, but at the same time is realistic with respect to the kinds of architecture we have built already and which will be coming,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n

ZHA also plans to merge its virtual world with the physical world, saying that they believe \u201cthese hybrid mixed-reality spaces will be very important in every place of work.\u201d<\/p>\n

Currently, part of the metaverse is being tested through two virtual floors in one of the buildings, CNN said.<\/p>\n

According to Jedlicka, Liberland citizens and residents will have first access to the metaverse once it\u2019s up and running.<\/p>\n
