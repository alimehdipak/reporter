Former Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey poked fun at fellow tech billionaire Mark Zuckerberg after the Facebook founder said that employees at his company, Meta, \u201clovingly\u201d referred to him as \u201cthe Eye of Sauron.\u201d<\/p>\n

Zuckerberg made the revelation during an interview last week with podcaster Tim Ferriss. <\/p>\n

Ferriss asked Zuckerberg how he divides his energy in the workplace while being inundated with information.<\/p>\n

\u201cMaybe I\u2019m not strong-willed enough or calm enough to do just straight-up meditation,\u201d the Meta CEO said. <\/p>\n

\u201cI actually need to put myself in a situation where it\u2019s difficult to not focus on that thing.\u201d<\/p>\n

He continued: \u201cSome of the folks I work with at the company \u2014 they say this lovingly \u2014 but I think that they sometimes refer to my attention as the Eye of Sauron.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cYou have this unending amount of energy to go work on something, and if you point that at any given team, you will just burn them.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cEye of Sauron\u201d is an evil Dark Lord from J.R.R. Tolkien\u2019s trilogy \u201cThe Lord of the Rings\u201d who seeks to exert total power over Middle-earth.<\/p>\n

The news site Mediaite cited a passage written by Tolkien in \u201cThe Silmarillion,\u201d which is a prequel to \u201cThe Hobbit\u201d and \u201cThe Lord of the Rings.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cSauron was become now a sorcerer of dreadful power, master of shadows and of phantoms, foul in wisdom, cruel in strength, misshaping what he touched, twisting what he ruled, lord of werewolves; his dominion was torment,\u201d Tolkien wrote.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Dorsey replied to a post on Twitter linking to an article about Zuckerberg\u2019s nickname, writing: \u201cMakes sense.\u201d<\/p>\n

During the same interview with Ferriss, Zuckerberg revealed that he likes to surf every morning before starting work as he struggles to cope with getting \u201cpunched\u201d by bad news.<\/p>\n
