Is Dorinda Medley saying goodbye to the Big Apple?<\/p>\n

The \u201cReal Housewives of New York City\u201d alum just sold her apartment at 120 E. 87th St. for just under $2.5 million \u2014\u00a0slightly above her $2.495 million asking price,\u00a0according to property records.<\/p>\n

But the \u201cMake it nice!\u201d maven won\u2019t go far \u2014 she promised.<\/p>\n

\u201cNot leaving,\u201d Medley, 57, told The Post when she listed the property in February. \u201cJust a change after 21 years.\u201d<\/p>\n

The current star of \u201cThe Real Housewives Ultimate Girls Trip\u201d \u2014\u00a0ex-wives edition \u2014 bought the two-bedroom, two-bath unit for $1.2 million in 2006. It comes with 14-foot ceilings, oversized windows, hardwood floors, a chef\u2019s kitchen and lots of storage space.<\/p>\n

The chic residence is at the Park Avenue Court, a 17-story building that once served as Gimbels Department Store before it was converted to condominiums in 1989. Legendary architectural firm Skidmore, Owings & Merrill realized its residential renovation.<\/p>\n

The Upper East Side residence is located at the Park Avenue Court building. Douglas Elliman Real Estate <\/p>\n

Building amenities include a landscaped roof deck, a courtyard, a gym, a pool, a children\u2019s playroom, a yoga studio, storage and an adjacent garage.<\/p>\n

The living room. Douglas Elliman Real Estate \t \t <\/p>\n

While she has been spending most of her days in her glamorous\u00a0and custom-designed Berkshires home \u2014 known to loyal Bravo viewers as her beloved Blue Stone Manor \u2014 until now Medley had also held on to her Upper East Side digs.<\/p>\n

Speaking of Blue Stone Manor, that\u2019s the setting for Season 2 of Peacock\u2019s \u201cThe Real Housewives Ultimate Girls Trip,\u201d on which the often outspoken reality TV star now appears. Things got a little heated on the show after Medley called co-star Jill Zarin a lapdog. The women have known each other for two decades \u2014 although they\u2019d never been on \u201cRHONY\u201d at the same time.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Occasionally, she would list her Manhattan home \u2014 hyped as \u201cdecorator done meticulous and extensive renovation just completed\u201d in the original listing \u2014\u00a0for rent at $9,950 a month\u00a0while quarantining in Massachusetts during the pandemic, as The Post previously reported.<\/p>\n

\u201cContrary to what some may think, I\u2019m not leaving New York City,\u201d Medley told People magazine.<\/p>\n

\u201cBut 2022 for me is about change,\u201d she added. \u201cI see my life differently now, and\u00a0I want an apartment that\u2019s more suited for my lifestyle; an apartment that\u2019s all mine. It\u2019s time to start a new chapter in a different location.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n
