Selena Quintanilla P\u00e9rez\u2019s legacy lives on as one of the greatest Tejano singers of all time. <\/p>\n

Her family is planning to release a posthumous album in April 2022, almost 27 years after her murder. <\/p>\n

However, some fans aren\u2019t happy with the project and feel that her family is exploiting the Texas-born starlet \u2014 who was shot in 1995 by crazed fan Yolanda Sald\u00edvar \u2014 for profit. <\/p>\n

Selena\u2019s brother, A.B. Quintanilla, recently shared details about the record in an interview with Tino Cochino Radio.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s a crazy concept album,\u201d Quintanilla said on the radio program. \u201cI remixed all her vinyls and just, with this album, with an EDM world, with arpeggiators and with keyboards, I made her flow to cumbia. Normal songs that were not normally recorded in cumbia.\u201d<\/p>\n

Cumbia is a musical rhythm and traditional folk dance that originated in Colombia in the 1940s.<\/p>\n

Quintanilla went into more detail about the production side of the album, revealing that the team was able to digitally alter her voice to make her sound aged. Several of the tracks were recorded when she was a teen.<\/p>\n

Selena was murdered in March 1995 by Yolanda Sald\u00edvar. Barbara Laing\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cWe were also able to de-tune her voice to make her sound older than what she was. So, she was 14 or 15, we were able to make her sound like she just stepped out of the booth at 23 years old,\u201d he stated. <\/p>\n

However, \u201cby de-tuning her voice a little bit, it actually made her sound deeper, like she sounded before she passed.\u201d<\/p>\n

Fans tweeted out their concern about the album, with several questioning its authenticity. \u201cThere\u2019s literally no other reason to do this except $$$,\u201d one person wrote.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is sickening. Abraham Quintanilla should let his daughter Rest In Peace,\u201d another asked on Twitter. \u201cHow hard up for cash is Abraham?\u201d<\/p>\n

One user penned, \u201cIt\u2019s not authentic.\u201d <\/p>\n

A.B. Quintanilla poses next to a wax figure of Selena at Madame Tussauds Hollywood in 2017.Joshua Blanchard\/Getty Images <\/p>\n

Abraham Quintanilla, the father and manager of Selena, told\u00a0Latin Groove News that the album will have more than 12 songs produced by her brother.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf you listen to it, you know, she sounds on these recordings like she did right before she passed away,\u201d Abraham noted in his interview.<\/p>\n

\u201cI said right after she passed away that I was going to try to keep her memory alive through her music, and we have done that,\u201d Abraham added. \u201cTwenty-six years later, Selena is very present in today\u2019s music world.\u201d<\/p>\n
