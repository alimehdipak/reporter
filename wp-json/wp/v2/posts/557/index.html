\u201cWhen [I\u2019m] so manic and upset, sometimes that\u2019s the only thing that will work for me,\u201d he declared.<\/p>\n

Odes to Ariana Grande<\/h2>\n

Davidson\u2019s whirlwind romance with Grande began in May 2018, with the pair getting engaged the following month.<\/p>\n

Almost immediately, the funnyman headed to the tattoo parlor to have his affection for the pop queen emblazoned on his body.<\/p>\n

Grande famously wore distinctive bunny ears on her \u201cDangerous Woman\u201d album cover, prompting Davidson to have the design tattooed below his left ear.<\/p>\n

Davidson had Grande\u2019s distinctive bunny ear mask tattooed below his left ear. After their painful and public break-up, he had the tattoo turned into a love heart. FilmMagic <\/p>\n

Grande herself had the exact same tattoo in the exact same location, with Davidson believing his matching design was a cute way to show his love for the \u201cGod Is A Woman\u201d crooner.<\/p>\n

But tattoo artists warned Davidson against getting the impulsive ink.<\/p>\n

A tribute to his dad <\/h2>\n

Davidson has a drawing of a firefighter crouched on his knees with his head in his hand \u2014 a tribute to his father Scott Davidson, who died on 9\/11. <\/p>\n

While romantic relationships come and go, Davidson\u2019s admiration for his late fireman father has been unending \u2014 so it seems unlikely he\u2019ll laser off a tattoo he had done in his dad\u2019s memory.<\/p>\n

On his left bicep, Davidson has a drawing of a firefighter crouched on his knees with his head in his hand \u2014 a tribute to his father Scott, who died on 9\/11.<\/p>\n

Davidson was just seven years old at the time of his dad\u2019s untimely death, and the openly-emotional actor has admitted that his grief spiraled into a depression that affected him for close to two decades. <\/p>\n

In addition to the drawing of a firefighter, Davidson also has his father\u2019s badge number \u201c8418\u201d tattooed on his left forearm. <\/p>\n
