Famed photographer David Bailey is shedding some light on the late Princess Diana\u2019s iconic golden locks.<\/p>\n

The 84-year-old spoke to\u00a0the Telegraph about his lengthy career and divulged a few secrets about photographing Diana, as well as Queen Elizabeth II, back in the day.<\/p>\n

Bailey claimed in his interview that the Princess of Wales \u2014 who died in 1997 after a car crash \u2014 had \u201cterrible hair\u201d while the \u201cgirlish\u201d queen had \u201cbeautiful\u201d locks.<\/p>\n

Photographer David Bailey said that Princess Diana had \u201cplastic dummy\u201d hair.Getty Images <\/p>\n

He noted a moment in 1988 when he was shooting the young royal and his assistant dropped a light on her head.<\/p>\n

\u201cI thought, \u2018Oh f\u2013k!,\u2019 \u201d he recalled, adding that Diana replied: \u201cDon\u2019t think about it; it was a terrible accident.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI told her she had been very magnanimous. That\u2019s right, because she asked me what \u2018magnanimous\u2019 meant,\u201d he continued.<\/p>\n

Photographer David Bailey recounted a time in 1988 during a photo shoot when he shot Princess Diana and his assistant dropped a light on her head.
Joel Ryan\/Invision\/AP <\/p>\n

The artist then claimed that Diana\u2019s tresses were \u201csolid as a plastic dummy\u201d due to the copious amounts of hairspray used.<\/p>\n

Bailey earned notoriety for his eclectic black-and-white portraits, with one of his most famous works being the one of Diana taken in 1989 when she was just 27.<\/p>\n

Prince William poses next to a David Bailey portrait of Queen Elizabeth in 2015.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Photographer Norman Parkinson was the first nominated to take snapshots of the mother of two, though she later chose Bailey. He had previously shot Princess Margaret\u2019s husband, Antony Armstrong-Jones, the First Earl of Snowdon.<\/p>\n

Diana\u2019s legacy and untimely passing will be featured in the fifth season of Netflix\u2019s \u201cThe Crown,\u201d which is due out on the streaming service later this year.<\/p>\n

Emma Corrin first portrayed the royal in her younger years for Season 4 and Elizabeth Debicki is taking over for Season 5, portraying Diana as a settled mother and the People\u2019s Princess.<\/p>\n

Hairstylist Sam McKnight revealed back in 2020 that he experienced some d\u00e9j\u00e0 vu while recreating Diana\u2019s coiffure for Corrin. He was Diana\u2019s personal hairstylist for seven years until 1997.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt felt weird,\u201d McKnight told British Vogue at the time. Even though the hairdo gig was one of his \u201cfirst jobs after lockdown,\u201d he admitted he questioned taking on the role to replicate Diana\u2019s blond mane.<\/p>\n

\u201cTo be honest,\u00a0Emma is playing Diana\u00a0before I even met her, so it felt fine,\u201d the stylist added. \u201cWe took a nod to the photos of Diana [in that period] and made a little pastiche of it.\u201d<\/p>\n
