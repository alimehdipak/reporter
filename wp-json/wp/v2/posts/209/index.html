I proceeded with caution. <\/p>\n

Staggering down Eighth Avenue, I feigned a sultry strut while wrapped from boobs to boots in 500 feet of black-and-yellow tape.<\/p>\n

\u201cOh my god, I have that same outfit,\u201d a man yelled out at me.<\/p>\n

A few blocks later an elderly woman pushing a shopping cart cheered: \u201cYou better work it, girl.\u201d A construction worker almost dropped his phone to exclaim \u201cThat\u2019s f\u2013king hot.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

As I teetered toward the subway, I finally encountered a man who understood my inspiration. <\/p>\n

\u201cYes Kim!\u201d he rooted.<\/p>\n

Last Sunday at Paris fashion week, Kim Kardashian wowed in a not-yet-on-the-market (and not-yet-priced) Balenciaga bodysuit made out of crime scene tape. <\/p>\n

New York Post reporter Asia Grace wore an outfit inspired by Kim Kardashian\u2019s Balenciaga bodysuit, which was made out of crime scene tape.FRAT\/Tamara Beckwith It took two hours to re-create the look.Tamara Beckwith New Yorkers commented, of course: from \u201cYou better work it, girl\u201d to \u201cThat\u2019s f\u2013king hot.\u201d\u00a0Tamara Beckwith <\/p>\n

I re-created the designer look by heading to Home Depot and purchasing three rolls of caution tape for a mere $25.97.\u00a0<\/p>\n

But unlike the \u201cKeeping Up With the Kardashians\u201d voluptuary \u2014 who blasted viral snaps of herself being draped in tape by no less than five assistants \u2014 I DIY\u2019ed my eye-catching look all myself.\u00a0It was no cinch.<\/p>\n

First, I put on a pair of black biker shorts and a sports bra, then I began winding caution tape, which unfortunately has no self-adhesive properties, around my left ankle. I sealed the wrap with a slice of Gorilla Tape and reworked that pattern all the way up to my hip. I repeated the task on my right leg, then strapped down my torso, chest and arms.\u00a0<\/p>\n

After purchasing three rolls of caution tape for a mere $25.97 at Home Depot, Grace wound the tape around herself. Kardashian had five assistants help in the taping.Tamara Beckwith <\/p>\n

Two hours later, I finally was ready to hit the streets at last \u2014 sort of.<\/p>\n

While my look didn\u2019t have the designer pedigree that Kim\u2019s did, it presented similar mobility issues. Kardashian was snapped waddling around and struggling to sit due to the constricting limitations of being locked in a cocoon of plastic. Similarly, I found it almost impossible to walk in my getup. Oh, and caution tape isn\u2019t exactly a breathable fabric.<\/p>\n

It felt like wearing a sauna, though my body heat and sweat did help the tape stay in place. I could barely move; my gait was a wobble. Each short, clumsy step was accompanied by a crinkling racket of plastic rubbing against more plastic. <\/p>\n

\u201cI could barely move; my gait was a wobble,\u201d Grace writes.Tamara Beckwith The catsuit felt like a sauna.Tamara Beckwith <\/p>\n

I hopped on the A train, which was mostly empty, but I was too constricted to take a seat. I felt miserable, save for the parade of flattery my handmade togs elicited. <\/p>\n

\u201cCan you make me [a caution tape outfit] so we can match on our date?\u201d asked a cheeky drifter near Madison Square Garden. \u201cCan I be your Kanye, Ms. Kim?\u201d joked another.\u00a0<\/p>\n

But I couldn\u2019t take it any longer. I got off the train, found the nearest bathroom and eagerly cut myself free with a large pair of scissors. I changed into a pair of beloved sweats and UGGs and relished in being able to move freely again.
