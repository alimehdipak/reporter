After nine years, exes Tarek El Moussa and Christina Haack vaguely announced they were ending their popular HGTV series \u201cFlip or Flop\u201d without giving viewers a real reason why. \u00a0<\/p>\n

Now there seems to be one.<\/p>\n

Haack, 38, initially said she was \u201cfilled with gratitude\u201d to have filmed 10 seasons of the show, but didn\u2019t mention her ex by name in a social media post.<\/p>\n

\u201cBittersweet news to announce, it\u2019s the end of an era. Next week\u2019s episode of Flip or Flop will be the SERIES finale,\u201d Haack\u00a0captioned an Instagram post\u00a0on Thursday.<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, El Moussa, 40 \u2014\u00a0who shares 11-year-old Taylor and 6-year-old Braydon with Haack \u2014 announced his appreciation for the viewers who supported him over the years and teased a new project. <\/p>\n

\u201cYou watched me beat cancer, you\u2019ve watched the babies grow up into the most amazing little humans, and everything in between,\u201d he wrote in an\u00a0Instagram post on Thursday.\u00a0\u201cBut I\u2019m not going anywhere and I can promise that you are going to love what comes next.\u201d<\/p>\n

A source told The Post that following El Moussa\u2019s marriage to \u201cSelling Sunset\u201d star Heather El Moussa, staying on the show \u201ccomplicated things.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s not ideal to have two exes who share children working together so closely while their significant others are just standing by,\u201d the insider explained. <\/p>\n

El Moussa\u00a0proposed to Heather in August 2020. The two\u00a0tied the knot\u00a0in October. They have been\u00a0documenting their fertility journey\u00a0on social media.<\/p>\n

Tarek El Moussa and Heather El Moussa got married in late 2021. Getty Images for MTV\/ViacomCBS <\/p>\n

Meanwhile, a separate source told People magazine that the environment became \u201ctoo intimate\u201d between the two. <\/p>\n

\u201cTarek and Christina are generally cordial,\u201d the insider claimed. \u201cThey\u2019re co-parenting and nothing gets in the way of that. The show was just too intimate of a setting at this point and it was time to close that chapter.\u201d<\/p>\n

Reps for Haack and El Moussa did not immediately respond to The Post\u2019s request for comment.<\/p>\n

The news comes less than one year after the former couple got into a blowout fight on the set of their house-flipping renovation show.<\/p>\n

The former couple, who wed in 2009, finalized their divorce in January 2018. It came one year after officially calling it quits in December 2016 after police were called to their home over an incident involving a gun and a 911 call.<\/p>\n
