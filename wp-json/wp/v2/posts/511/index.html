It\u2019s a good day to be a part of the itty bitty committee!<\/p>\n

Lady Gaga, Mandy Moore, Kaitlyn Dever, Elizabeth Olsen and Thuso Mbedu all wowed at Sunday\u2019s Critics Choice Awards in plunging necklines that showcased lots of clavicle and breasts that\u00a0conformed to the laws of gravity regardless of size. <\/p>\n

There was nary a fantasy pushup bra in sight.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe plunging necklines are a classic eveningwear glamour statement,\u201d Kathlin Argiro, a designer and Fashion Institute of Technology professor, told The Post. <\/p>\n

This time, though, the stars embracing the look let their natural shapes take center stage \u2014 and didn\u2019t allow smaller cup sizes stop them from daring to bare.<\/p>\n

Argiro said this change reflects a \u201cseismic shift in fashion,\u201d with the classic cut being modeled by a range of women \u2014 from 25-year-old Kathryn Newton to 48-year-old Kristen Wiig. <\/p>\n

\u201cI can\u2019t imagine it doesn\u2019t have to do with confidence,\u201d Argiro added. <\/p>\n

Kaitlyn Dever, 25, looked red hot in a bright evening dress with a plunging neckline. Kristen Wiig, 48, teased fans with her sheer lace black dress with a deep V.Kevin Mazur <\/p>\n

They\u2019re not the only ladies diving into the trend. Rather, they\u2019re supported by the growing movement of women ditching bras all together or opting for softer, wire-free numbers, which now outsell their uncomfortably rigid counterparts, according to a recent report from market research group NPD. <\/p>\n

The trend ramped up during the pandemic and appears to be here to stay. Per NPD, women are increasingly trading in their waist cinchers and bodysuits for more easygoing tank tops and thongs.<\/p>\n

And when they do dress up, women of all sizes are more likely to accentuate their natural shapes, donning pieces with plunging necklines and drastic cutouts or made of yards of sheer fabric. <\/p>\n

Elizabeth Olsen, 33, was a vision of classic Hollywood glamour in her low-cut maroon dress. Alberto E. Rodriguez <\/p>\n

\u201cBreasts are accessories,\u201d Kimmay Caldwell, an undergarment expert, told The Post. <\/p>\n

In her 17 years of bra fitting, Caldwell has seen many changes in how women want to shape and display their breasts. <\/p>\n

She\u2019s noticed a recent swing toward body acceptance \u2014 influenced in part by living braless at home for two years. <\/p>\n

Mandy Moore, 37, shined in an Elie Saab dress.Alberto E. Rodriguez <\/p>\n

Caldwell said that many women simply don\u2019t want to shape their bodies in uncomfortable and unnatural ways. <\/p>\n

\u201cWomen are always told we\u2019re either too much or not enough,\u201d Caldwell said. <\/p>\n

And smaller-busted fashionistas amping up the sex appeal in deep-cut dresses is just one aspect of women freeing themselves from the constraints of outmoded fashion decrees. <\/p>\n

Susanna Moyer, a fashion consultant in New York City, agreed that fashion\u2019s \u201cbiggest trend is body positivity and really showing off what you feel comfortable with.\u201d<\/p>\n
