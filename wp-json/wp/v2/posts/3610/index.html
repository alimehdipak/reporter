People are still clamoring to get a piece of the action from the Johnny Depp v. Amber Heard trial.<\/p>\n

Wristbands from the Fairfax County, Virginia, court \u2014 which were being hawked by scalpers at the height of the defamation trial \u2014 are now being auctioned online for big bucks.<\/p>\n

Sellers on eBay are letting the public bid and buy their access bands from the case for three and four-figure price tags. <\/p>\n

An orange wristband is going for $500, while a blue one is being sold for almost $5,000.<\/p>\n

The description for the orange bracelet reads, \u201cThis is a wristband that was used to enter one of the last days of the Johnny Depp Trial on Thursday, May 26, 2022. It is number 77 for number 77 in line to enter the courtroom. This wristband is priceless in that the trial is a part of pop culture history. I am willing to negotiate.\u201d<\/p>\n

The blue band is from the May 23 and was from the first day of the final week of the trial and from \u201cone of the busiest days,\u201d according to the product synopsis. \u201cI was there and you are obtaining this from a verified first person source,\u201d it continued.<\/p>\n

It gave the wearer access to the courtroom and \u201cis a piece of celebrity culture history. The wristbands are not fancy or specially made, they are your typical paper wristbands you see at events.\u201d<\/p>\n

Heard, 36, and Depp, 58, were embroiled in a contentious defamation trial with the \u201cPirates of the Caribbean\u201d actor suing the \u201cStepfather\u201d actress for $50 million over allegations of abuse in a 2018 Washington Post op-ed she penned.\u00a0Heard countersued her ex for $100 million.<\/p>\n

A verdict was reached on June 1, with the\u00a0jury awarding Depp $10 million\u00a0in compensatory damages and $5 million in punitive damages. Virginia law states that punitive damages must be capped at $350,000, meaning Depp will actually receive a maximum total of $10.35 million. Heard was awarded $2 million in compensatory damages and no punitive damages.<\/p>\n

In the days following the multimillion-dollar verdict, it has been reported that Heard is \u201cbroke\u201d and can\u2019t pay the hefty judgement due to mounting legal costs and alleged lavish spending.<\/p>\n
