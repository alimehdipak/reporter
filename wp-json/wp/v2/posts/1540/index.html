<\/p>\n

NASA is developing space suit technologies that will make future astronauts on the moon safe from the Sun\u2019s unfiltered rays.<\/p>\n

The moon has no atmosphere, which means the Sun\u2019s rays hit the lunar surface with full force, reaching 250 degrees Fahrenheit during the day.<\/p>\n

NASA\u2018s Artemis program has a loaded schedule of manned and unmanned missions set to travel the cosmos.<\/p>\n

In 2025, Artemis 3 will bring humans to the moon with the first woman and person of color to reach the lunar surface on board.<\/p>\n

The crew will touch down near the moon\u2019s south pole, which is understood to have\u00a0large amounts of ice.<\/p>\n

The astronauts will need protection from the ruthlessness temperatures of space as they traverse the moon for the first time since the 1970s.<\/p>\n

In a new\u00a0video\u00a0published by NASA, engineers are shown developing new spacesuits that make a walk on the moon a walk in the park.<\/p>\n

On eight-hour spacewalks, heat generated by astronauts\u2019 bodies is a factor that must be accounted for.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

A spandex layer loaded with tubes pumping water around the astronauts\u2019 bodies will be worn underneath the bulky white spacesuits.<\/p>\n

NASA calls this layer a \u201cliquid cooling ventilation garment\u201d and it absorbs heat created by astronauts\u2019 movements.<\/p>\n

The latest developments in liquid-cooling technologies are an expansion on similar techniques used by the Apollo astronauts decades ago and present-day space travelers.<\/p>\n

Right now, astronauts aboard the\u00a0International Space Station\u00a0(ISS) conduct spacewalks using water recycled from a unit onboard the station.<\/p>\n

The crew will touch down near the moon\u2019s south pole, which is understood to have large amounts of ice.Shutterstock <\/p>\n

Engineers are working to condense the necessary resources to fit into a self-contained suit so that astronauts can explore the moon without an attachment to the lunar module.<\/p>\n

To date, two of these compact units have been built \u2013 one is here on Earth, while the other is aboard the ISS, waiting to be tested in zero gravity.<\/p>\n

NASA is open to engaging in partnerships with private companies to streamline the design and construction of the spacesuit technologies<\/p>\n

The next-generation space suits will open the door to a new era of safe and efficient spacewalks.<\/p>\n
