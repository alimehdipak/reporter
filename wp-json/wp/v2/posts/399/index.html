Uber and Lyft drivers are getting hammered by sky-high gas prices \u2014 and some are saying that they\u2019re going to quit altogether.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The news comes as Russia\u2019s invasion of Ukraine, as well as increased commuting and travel, send gas prices soaring to record levels. As of Monday morning, a gallon of regular-grade fuel cost $4.33 \u2014 up from $3.49 a month ago and just $2.86 at the same time last year, according to AAA data.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Since rideshare drivers are responsible for their own maintenance and fuel costs, rising gas prices can effectively wipe out large parts of their paychecks.\u00a0<\/p>\n

On a Reddit forum for Lyft drivers, one driver wrote, \u201cLyft must be crazy if they think imma drive.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI was out last night and it went okay,\u201d the driver said. \u201cTill I had to pump gas and noticed I barely broke even. Lyft is a joke.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cNope, I\u2019m done,\u201d declared another user on a forum for Uber drivers.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cLyft must be crazy if they think imma drive,\u201d one driver wrote. Smith Collection\/Gado <\/p>\n

Food delivery drivers for Uber Eats and Postmates also showed frustration with surging gas prices, which have reached an average of $5.74 in California and $4.45 in New York.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019ve been driving for Postmates and now Uber Eats for years but at the moment it isn\u2019t even worth it anymore,\u201d wrote one driver. \u201cWe don\u2019t even make enough to get the money in our tanks back. So sad. I was doing deliveries to have some extra cash and help with bills when I didn\u2019t have enough. Now I\u2019m just home cuz I\u2019m not seeing the benefit.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

One driver in Northern California said driving for Uber was simply \u201cnot feasible\u201d anymore due to the price of gas.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The average price for a gallon of gas has soared from $2.86 to $4.33 over the past year, according to AAA data. DON EMMERT <\/p>\n

Other drivers wrote that they planned to only work during \u201csurge\u201d hours \u2014 when high demand leads to increased payouts \u2014 because driving during normal hours is not longer worth the gas.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Starting Wednesday, Uber plans to add a surcharge of\u00a045 cents or 55 cents on each rideshare trip and 35 cents or 45 cents on each Uber Eats in an effort to help make up for surging gas prices.<\/p>\n

Lyft said in a statement to The Post that it had been \u201cclosely monitoring\u201d rising bas prices and the effect they\u2019re having on drivers. \u201cDriver earnings overall remain elevated compared to last year, but given the rapid rise in gas prices we\u2019ll be asking riders to pay a temporary fuel surcharge, all of which will go to drivers,\u201d the statement said.<\/p>\n

New York City recently bumped up minimum pay for rideshare drivers by 5.3%. Kian Hong Ng \/ EyeEm <\/p>\n

And in New York City, Uber and Lyft recently upped their minimum pay rate for drivers by 5.3% in March following an order from the city\u2019s Taxi and Limousine Commission. Uber spokesperson Freddi Goldstein said that brings the the minimum hourly wage for Uber drivers in the city to $29.78 per hour.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cOur platform only works if it works for drivers, so we\u2019ll continue to monitor gas prices and listen to drivers over the coming weeks,\u201d Goldstein said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Uber also recently launched a service for drivers with a site called GetUpSide that helps drivers get cash back on gas purchases, Goldstein added.\u00a0<\/p>\n
