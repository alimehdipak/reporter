Chris Rock is finally ready to crack public jokes about the 2022 Oscars smackdown that blew up pop culture back in March.<\/p>\n

Will Smith infamously slapped the stand-up comic, 57, onstage during the 94th Academy Awards, after Rock cracked a bald joke about the actor\u2019s wife, Jada-Pinkett Smith, who\u2019s openly discussed her struggles with alopecia.<\/p>\n

Rock has mostly refused to discuss the incident since then \u2014\u00a0 but on Thursday, during his new live comedy show, \u201cEgo Death,\u201d he quipped that words don\u2019t hurt as much as being hit in the face.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m OK, if anybody was wondering,\u201d Rock said during the show at the UK\u2019s Royal Albert Hall, according to the Times of London. \u201cI got most of my hearing back.\u201d<\/p>\n

He added that he still wouldn\u2019t be talking about the altercation between him and Smith, 53.<\/p>\n

\u201cPeople expect me to talk about the bulls \u2013 \u2013 t. I\u2019m not going to talk about it right now. I\u2019ll get to it eventually \u2014 on Netflix,\u201d he said with a laugh. <\/p>\n

Rock continued, \u201cAnyone who says \u2018words hurt\u2019 \u2014 has never been punched in the face.\u201d<\/p>\n

The comedian previously stated that he wouldn\u2019t be talking about the slap until he sees a check with his name on it: \u201cI\u2019m OK. I have a whole show, and I\u2019m not talking about that until I get paid,\u201d he told his audience during a comedy set at Fantasy Springs Resort Casino in Indio, California, last month.<\/p>\n

However, just a week before his casino appearance, the \u201cEverybody Hates Chris\u201d creator said to fans during a surprise visit to the Comedy Cellar in New York City,\u00a0\u201cLower your expectations. I\u2019m not going to address that s \u2013 \u2013 t.\u201d<\/p>\n
