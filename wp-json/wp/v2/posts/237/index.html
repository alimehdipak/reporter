Sports Illustrated has unveiled its finalists for the 2022 Swim Search \u2014 and readers are remarking on the diversity of the chosen beauties.<\/p>\n

The 14 finalists are competing for a single spot in the magazine\u2019s next Swimsuit Issue, where they\u2019ll appear alongside some of the world\u2019s leading models.<\/p>\n

Among the candidates are a 42-year-old breast cancer survivor, an astrophysicist and an ICU nurse.<\/p>\n

Sports Illustrated says all the finalists \u201cinspire action throughout their communities,\u201d with fans taking to Twitter to reveal how impressed they were with the line-up. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe diversity here is AMAZING,\u201d one reader cooed.<\/p>\n

However, one critic moaned that the magazine is attempting to be \u201cwoke,\u201d writing: \u201cThe Swimsuit Issue is 100% woke again this year. Who is the readership of Sports Illustrated? Are regular guys like \u2018bring on the \u2019empowering changemakers!’\u201d<\/p>\n

But there\u2019s no denying that the magazine has picked 14 finalists whose beauty is only matched by their interesting and varying backgrounds.<\/p>\n

Here are the competitors:<\/p>\n

Michelle F. Steffan<\/h2>\n

Michelle F. Steffan.mich.steffen\/Instagram <\/p>\n

Steffan, who hasn\u2019t revealed her age, hails from Argentina. The curvy beauty trained as a lawyer before leaving her lucrative legal career to pursue modeling full time. The stunner told People that Ashley Graham\u2019s 2016 Swimsuit Issue cover was eye-opening for her.<\/p>\n

\u201cI couldn\u2019t believe my eyes because I wasn\u2019t used to seeing curvy models in my home country or pretty much anywhere,\u201d she marveled.<\/p>\n

Gigi Robinson<\/h2>\n

Gigi Robinson.Credit:itsgigirobinson\/Instagram <\/p>\n

Robinson, 23, is an American content creator who boasts a huge following on TikTok. <\/p>\n

The USC grad is also a health advocate who suffers from\u00a0Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (EDS) \u2014 a rare illness that affects the connective tissue in the body. <\/p>\n

Robinson shared videos EDS in a bid to educate her TikTok followers.<\/p>\n

Hillory Fields<\/h2>\n

Hillory Fields. <\/p>\n

Fields \u2014 who was named after Hilary Clinton \u2014 is more than just a model. <\/p>\n

The brunette beauty currently works as an ICU nurse in Los Angeles, and recently developed an app for fatigued medical staff to review and rate hospitals where they\u2019ve worked.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Hannah Godwin <\/h2>\n

Hannah Godwin.Credit:hannahg11\/Instagram <\/p>\n

Alabama-born Godwin, 27, is the most famous of the 14 finalists, having previously appeared on Colton Underwood\u2019s season of \u201cThe Bachelor.\u201d<\/p>\n

The blonde now lives in Los Angeles and has amassed more than 1.5 million Instagram fans.<\/p>\n

Sadly for her many male admirers, Godwin is off the market, and is dating entrepreneur Dylan Barbour. <\/p>\n

Ashley N. Byrd<\/h2>\n

Ashley N. Byrd. <\/p>\n

Louisiana-born Byrd is blessed with both brains and beauty. The finalist holds a Bachelor\u2019s degree in Criminal Justice and a Master\u2019s degree in Public Administration. <\/p>\n

Byrd is now based in Los Angeles and is pursuing modeling on a full-time basis. <\/p>\n

She frequently flaunts her incredible physique on California\u2019s sunny beaches, making her a competitive candidate for the Swim Search. <\/p>\n

Kelly Crump<\/h2>\n

Kelly Crump. <\/p>\n

Crump, 42, is the oldest of the 14 finalists and is a proud breast cancer survivor. <\/p>\n

The inspiring beauty has undergone six surgeries and 60 rounds of chemotherapy. I<\/p>\n

In 2019, cancer spread to other areas of her body including her neck, lung, armpit, ribs and spine, but the London-native turned her turmoil into inspiration, sharing her story on Instagram and amassing a giant following. <\/p>\n

Mady Dewey<\/h2>\n

Mady Dewey. <\/p>\n

Dewey hails from California and is a prominent podcaster and TikTokker. <\/p>\n

According to Sports Illustrated, the stunner co-founded the company Herd Social \u2014 \u201ca pressure-free photo-sharing platform for Gen Z women and non-binary people.\u201d<\/p>\n

The brunette beauty shared the above photo to her Instagram, excitedly announcing she was one of Sports Illustrated\u2019s finalists.<\/p>\n

Drew Dorsey<\/h2>\n

Drew Dorsey <\/p>\n

Dorsey, 28, is a Los Angeles-based influencer, with a whopping 98,000 Instagram followers.<\/p>\n

She has a Bachelor\u2019s degree in communications and is an aspiring entertainment journalist.<\/p>\n

The stunner is thrilled to be in the final 14, sharing her excitement in an Instagram post that read: \u201cY\u2019all listen, I don\u2019t mean to preach, but please please don\u2019t ever give up on your dreams. This was my second year auditioning and look what happened because I didn\u2019t give up.\u201d<\/p>\n

Victoria Vesce <\/h2>\n

Victoria Vescevictoriavesce\/Instagram <\/p>\n

Vesce is also a cancer survivor, having been diagnosed with a brain tumor while she was in college. <\/p>\n

The Florida-based beauty lost half of her hearing but managed to beat her bout of cancer after undergoing life-threatening surgery. <\/p>\n

Vesce failed to be defined by her health struggles and is now a practicing lawyer in Boca Raton. <\/p>\n

Manju Bangalore<\/h2>\n

Manju Bangalore <\/p>\n

Bangalore is a physicist who has worked at two NASA centers and dreams of being an astronaut. <\/p>\n

According to Sports Illustrated, she also has a philanthropic side. The California-based brunette founded a \u201cmenstrual health nonprofit\u201d in 2015,\u201d which has provided more than 200,000 menstrual products to those in need.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Nicole Petrie<\/h2>\n

Nicole Petrie. <\/p>\n

Petrie is a full-time caregiver for her mother, who suffers from FTD (Frontal Temporal Degeneration).<\/p>\n

The blonde is determined to raise awareness of the rare disorder and frequently posts about it on her Instagram page.<\/p>\n

Petrie describes herself in her Instagram bio as a \u201cmodel, activist, caregiver and vegan.\u201d<\/p>\n

Ashley Callingbull<\/h2>\n

Ashley Callingbull <\/p>\n

Callingbull, who hails from Canada, is the first indigenous woman to be included in Sports Illustrated\u2019s Swim Search.<\/p>\n

The 32-year-old is a Cree First Nations woman from the Enoch Cree Nation in the province of Alberta. <\/p>\n

Callingbull is thrilled by her inclusion in the final 14, after finding herself shut out of previous competitions due to her heritage. <\/p>\n

\u201cFor a lot of my life, a lot of the things that I\u2019ve had to chase, they weren\u2019t open to me because I\u2019m an Indigenous woman, whether that be acting or modeling,\u201d she told\u00a0E! News\u00a0last year.<\/p>\n

Sarafina Nance <\/h2>\n

Sarafina Nance. <\/p>\n

Nance, who lives and works in California, is an analog astronaut who recently completed a Mars astronaut simulation.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The brave beauty also underwent a double mastectomy at 26 years old, after being diagnosed with the cancer-causing BRCA2 genetic mutation. <\/p>\n

Lotta Hintsa <\/h2>\n

Lotta Hintsa.lottahintsa\/Instagram <\/p>\n

Hinsta was born in Finland and raised in Ethiopia. <\/p>\n
