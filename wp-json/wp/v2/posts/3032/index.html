Different stages of tooth decay<\/span><\/h2>\n

Tooth decay develops in different stages. First, it starts with removing the minerals from your teeth. Later on, it attacks the enamel and dentin layer and goes to the pulp. Once it reaches the pulp, decay has progressed into an abscess that is dangerous to your oral and general health.<\/span><\/p>\n

Plaque is the main contributor to tooth decay. Food debris, saliva, and bacteria combine to form a biofilm covering your tooth surface. It progresses into tartar over time if you don\u2019t remove it. When it turns into tartar, it\u2019s difficult to remove it, so you should visit your dentist immediately.<\/span><\/p>\n

That being said, here are five stages of tooth decay.<\/span><\/p>\n

Stage 1: Demineralization<\/b><\/h3>\n

When you don\u2019t floss and brush your teeth and observe poor dental hygiene, bacteria and food debris become plaque. The enamel is your body\u2019s hardest tissue, consisting of minerals. The plaque robs the minerals by attacking the enamel with its acids. This robbing of minerals is called demineralization. If that\u2019s the case for you, white spots on your tooth will appear.<\/span><\/p>\n

Stage 2: Enamel damage<\/b><\/h3>\n

Your tooth enamel is in great danger if demineralization isn\u2019t stopped. The white spots that you noticed earlier will soon turn into darker spots, and you may start to see cavities and small holes in your teeth. You can fill the holes with dental fillings before the condition worsens.<\/span><\/p>\n

Stage 3: Dentin decay<\/b><\/h3>\n

After damaging the enamel, the bad guys will reach your tooth\u2019s inner layer called the dentist. The bacteria and plaque had already damaged enamel – the most challenging part to infiltrate. Rest is easy for them to infect and damage. This part of your tooth is softer and more prone to infection. This is where you\u2019ll start experiencing severe tooth sensitivity and faster decay advancement.<\/span><\/p>\n

Stage 4: Nerve damage<\/b><\/h3>\n

Your tooth contains pulp underneath the dentist with blood vessels and nerves responsible for keeping your teeth healthy. When this part of your tooth decays, the patient will experience severe pain, irritation, and swelling. The only option left for treatment is to undergo a root canal procedure.<\/span><\/p>\n

Stage 5: Abscess<\/b><\/h3>\n

When the bacteria enter the pulp and cause infection, it leads to dental abscess or puss. At this point, your general health and wellness are at risk. That\u2019s because the infection can reach your face, jaw, lungs, or brain. If that\u2019s the case for you, immediately go to your oral surgeon. Otherwise, the abscess will penetrate other body parts and even cause death.<\/span><\/p>\n
