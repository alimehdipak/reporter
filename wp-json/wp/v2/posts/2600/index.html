<\/p>\n

Elon Musk sharply denied a claim this week by Truth Social\u2019s boss Devin Nunes that former President Donald Trump had \u201cencouraged\u201d him to acquire Twitter before he reached a buyout deal with the company\u2019s board of directors.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is false. I\u2019ve had no communication, directly or indirectly, with Trump, who has publicly stated that he will be exclusively on Truth Social,\u201d Musk tweeted on Friday in response to a story by The Post that detailed Nunes\u2019 claims.<\/p>\n

Nunes, the CEO of Trump Media & Technology Group, made the claims earlier this week \u2013 asserting that Trump nudged Musk to pursue a takeover even though Truth Social and Twitter are competitors.<\/p>\n

\u201cPresident Trump, basically before Elon Musk bought it, actually said to go and buy it because the goal of our company is really to build a community where people are in a family-friendly, safe environment,\u201d Nunes said during an appearance on\u00a0Fox Business Network.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat\u2019s why we encouraged Elon Musk to buy it, because someone has to take on these tech tyrants,\u201d Nunes added later in the interview. \u201cDonald Trump wanted to make sure that the American people got their voice back and that the internet was open and that\u2019s what we are doing.\u201d<\/p>\n

Donald Trump says he has no intention of returning to Twitter.REUTERS <\/p>\n

A spokesman for Trump Media & Technology Group declined to comment on Musk\u2019s rebuttal. On Friday afternoon, Nunes \u2014 who hadn\u2019t mentioned Trump\u2019s recent public comments during this week\u2019s Fox Business interview \u2014 scrambled to clear up the confusion.<\/p>\n

\u201cAnyone who watched or read every interview @realDonaldTrump or I have given the last few weeks know our position clearly,\u201d Nunes tweeted. \u201cThere\u2019s been no secret or anything QUIET we\u2019ve been very vocal and public.\u201d <\/p>\n

Trump said last month that he has no intention of returning to Twitter \u2013 where his account has been permanently banned since the Jan. 6 Capitol riot \u2013 and that he would remain on Truth Social.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am not going on Twitter, I am going to stay on Truth,\u201d Trump told Fox News at the time. \u201cI hope Elon buys Twitter because he\u2019ll make improvements to it and he is a good man, but I am going to be staying on Truth.\u201d<\/p>\n

Elon Musk previously said that Truth Social was a \u201cterrible name.\u201dSOPA Images\/LightRocket via Gett <\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s acquisition of Twitter marked a setback for Truth Social, which has positioned itself as an alternative to mainstream social media platforms and sought to lure conservatives wary of Big Tech censorship.<\/p>\n

The Tesla CEO has pledged to implement a renewed focus on free speech on Twitter when his takeover is complete.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s ownership of Twitter has roiled some left-leaning employees and outside pundits who have expressed fears that his free speech emphasis will empower the far-right. <\/p>\n

Devin Nunes said President Trump told Elon Musk to \u201cgo and buy\u201d Twitter.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Musk briefly tangled with Truth Social last month \u2013 posting a screenshot showing that Trump\u2019s app was leading the Apple App Store\u2019s most-downloaded list. He also jabbed at the app\u2019s name.<\/p>\n

\u201cTruth Social (terrible name) exists because Twitter censored free speech,\u201d Musk tweeted. \u201cShould be called Trumpet instead!\u201d<\/p>\n

Truth Social has struggled since its soft launch in the App Store in February. Some users were unable to access the app or have been stuck on a long wait list.<\/p>\n

Shares of Digital World Acquisition Corp., the SPAC poised to take Truth Social public, are down about 18% since Musk first disclosed his Twitter stake.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
