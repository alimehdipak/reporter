As William Shakespeare once wrote, \u201cWhat\u2019s in a name?\u201d<\/p>\n

Legendary actor Martin Sheen changed his birth name of \u201cRamon Est\u00e9vez\u201d to his now-famous stage name for his Hollywood career.<\/p>\n

However, the 81-year-old now wishes he never fixed his moniker for his acting profession.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat\u2019s one of my regrets,\u201d the \u201cWest Wing\u201d star recently told Closer Weekly. \u201cI never changed my name officially. It\u2019s still Ramon Est\u00e9vez on my birth certificate. It\u2019s on my marriage license, my passport, driver\u2019s license.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cSometimes you get persuaded when you don\u2019t have enough insight or even enough courage to stand up for what you believe in, and you pay for it later. But, of course, I\u2019m only speaking for myself,\u201d Sheen added.<\/p>\n

Charlie Sheen and Martin Sheen attend the premiere of \u201cInto The Night\u201d on February 12, 1991, in Los Angeles, California.Ron Galella Collection via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The Ohio native also revealed that his father tried to dissuade him from going into show business. \u201cYou couldn\u2019t blame him. Both my parents were immigrants. My father was from Spain, and my mother was from Ireland,\u201d Sheen said.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy father was very practical. He was a factory worker for most of his adult life, and he wanted me to go to college and improve my chances of making a better living than he did. We had some very, very painful confrontations about it,\u201d the \u201cApocalypse Now\u201d icon went on.<\/p>\n

Sheen\u2019s sons, \u201cBreakfast Club\u201d actor Emilio Estevez and Ramon Estevez, are both actors and chose not to Anglicize their names for the biz. His daughter Ren\u00e9e Pilar Estevez also opted not to alter her identity for Tinseltown.<\/p>\n

Charlie Sheen opted to change his birth name of \u201cCarlos Estevez\u201d like his father for Hollywood.Sygma via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The \u201cBadlands\u201d star\u2019s other son, \u201cTwo and a Half Men\u201d alum Charlie Sheen, was born Carlos Irwin Est\u00e9vez and went on to modify his moniker.<\/p>\n

As for if he objected to any of his four children making their way to Hollywood, Sheen explained: \u201cNo, but I honestly have to say I wasn\u2019t aware of it. I was so self-involved and trying to be a provider that I wasn\u2019t aware of their inclination to being actors.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI was doing a show one time, and\u00a0Emilio\u00a0showed up. I thought he was there to visit me, but he\u2019d gotten a part in the same show,\u201d Sheen noted. \u201cThe only influence I had on Emilio\u00a0was to keep his name.
