A fashion fan went on the hunt to find the best swimsuits for bigger busts \u2013 and she\u2019s shared her helpful tips on TikTok.<\/p>\n

London-based designer Liza Belmonte tried on 60 swimsuits in all and discovered what works well for larger-cup ladies and what doesn\u2019t.<\/p>\n

\u201cDo you want to know how to\u00a0look very sophisticated on the beach even if you have very big boobs?\u201d she asked.<\/p>\n

By trying on five dozen swimsuits \u2013 including bikinis \u2013 she found the best styles and features for women who wear a D-cup and above.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf you have a D-cup or above, you will probably be very familiar with the struggle of finding a shape that flatters our figure,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

\u201cPerhaps what you want this summer is to look more sophisticated, perhaps a bit more modest,\u201d she went on.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou do not want that part of your body to be what people notice first about you, and you want to be able to take a dip in the sea without worrying about where things are.<\/p>\n

\u201cAn issue we\u2019re dealing with here is that most brands grade their swimwear in a really, really bizarre way,\u2019 she said.<\/p>\n

Designer Liza Belmonte tried on 60 different swimsuits and discovered what works well for larger-cup ladies and what doesn\u2019t.@Lizabelmonte <\/p>\n

Instead of sizing swimwear like underwear, they size it like other clothing.<\/p>\n

She noted that many brands assume that if a woman has a large cup size, she is also larger all over with a bigger circumference.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is problematic for people who have a large chest with a small back or vice-versa.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cIf you have a large cup and a small back and they try to sell you a size XL, they\u2019re doing it wrong,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

Once you find brands that size swimwear by cup size, there are a few tips that will help you find the most flattering fit.<\/p>\n

First, Liza said, avoid triangle-shaped tops because they don\u2019t have enough support and can create a gaping issue around the armpit.<\/p>\n

Second, avoid ties around your neck because they can lead to neck pain and red marks.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t need to tell you that this stuff is heavy,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

She also said to avoid flimsy straps, straps that come down the middle of the chest, and one-shoulder suits.<\/p>\n

\u201cIf you have a small frame and a large bust, you need adjustable straps,\u201d she said.<\/p>\n

\u201cLook for nicely sewn-in cups that can give you support,\u201d she added, warning against flimsy ones that move around inside the lining after the suit has been washed.<\/p>\n

Wide straps are always good, and she said she has never gone wrong with a square neckline, calling it the \u201cmagic shape.\u201d<\/p>\n
