Free agent guard Malik Monk is signing a two-year, $19 million deal to join the Sacramento Kings, his brother and agent, Marcus Monk, told ESPN’s Dave McMenamin.<\/p>\n

Monk, 24, was one of the rare bright spots for a disappointing Los Angeles Lakers team last season, averaging career highs in minutes (28.1), points (13.8), assists (2.9) and true shooting percentage (59.7%).<\/p>\n

He gives Sacramento more depth in a backcourt that lost Tyrese Haliburton and Buddy Hield after the Kings moved on from them at the trade deadline last season.<\/p>\n

Selected 11th overall in the 2017 draft, Monk spent the first four seasons of his career in Charlotte but struggled to get consistent touches amid a crowded backcourt that included Terry Rozier, LaMelo Ball and Devonte’ Graham.<\/p>\n

On the Lakers, Monk was given more responsibility as the roster thinned due to injuries. He played the second-most total minutes of anyone on L.A.’s roster and was one of the few players who had a positive point differential when he was on the court.<\/p>\n
