Two celebrity stylists were loaned more than $35,000 worth of clothes for Cardi B\u2019s younger sister Hennessey Carolina \u2014 but have refused to return or pay for the bulk of the designer duds, a new lawsuit alleges.<\/p>\n

Stylists Charles Reynolds and Xavier Banks got 52 items of clothing worth $35,306 from luxury fashion retailer Dover Street Market on April 14, saying the garments were \u201cbeing taken on consignment for the purchase by their client Hennessey Carolina,\u201d according to Manhattan Supreme Court suit filed last week.<\/p>\n

Banks and Reynolds were loaned 52 items of clothing worth $35,306 from Dover Street Market in April that were on consignment for Hennessey Carolina. instagram\/@hennessycarolina <\/p>\n

The stylist duo agreed to either buy or return the garments by April 21 \u2014 but instead gave some of the duds to rapper Melody \u201cMellowrackz\u201d Faith to rock at Coachella, and \u201cpersonally wore\u201d pieces to the music festival themselves, the filing claims.<\/p>\n

Faith \u201chas now refused to return the merchandise,\u201d the suit alleges. <\/p>\n

On April 27 \u2013 nearly a week after the clothes were due to be returned \u2013 only six of the items were brought back to the high-end Kips Bay store and four of the pieces were damaged, Dover Street Market claims. <\/p>\n

To date, the fashion duo still hasn\u2019t returned or paid for the damaged and unreturned 49 pieces totaling $34,331, the suit alleges.<\/p>\n

\u201cDover Street Market works with many industry leading stylists on this consignment basis and, regrettably, these stylists abused the privilege,\u201d a spokesperson for the company said in a statement.<\/p>\n

Faith is not named as a defendant in the lawsuit. The suit also does not name Carolina as a defendant, nor bring any accusations against her.<\/p>\n

Reynolds said the case does not involve his client Carolina, the 26-year-old sister of \u201cWAP\u201d rapper Cardi B, who has made appearances on VH1\u2019s \u201cLove & Hip Hop: New York.\u201d <\/p>\n

Carolina is the younger sister of rapper Cardi B.Photo by Katja Ogrin\/Redferns <\/p>\n

The stylist claimed he pulled the clothes for Faith \u2014 who allegedly took them and never paid for them.<\/p>\n

\u201cMellow Rackz is the person that stole the clothes,\u201d Reynolds told The Post, adding that he has retained a lawyer and filed a police report against her.<\/p>\n

Similarly, Banks claimed, \u201cMellow Rackz is a thief and she is the reason that this is all happening.\u201d<\/p>\n

Banks accused rapper Melody \u201cMellowrackz\u201d Faith of stealing the clothes from him and Reynolds.instagram\/@xavierbanks__ <\/p>\n

\u201cMellowracks is truly scamming her way to the top and using whoever she can,\u201d the stylists\u2019 rep said in a statement. \u201cCelebrity stylists, Charles Reynolds and Xavier Banks are her latest victims.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe celebrity style team has been sued by Dover Street Group for not returning clothes they pulled for Mellowrackz. The rapper who pretended she was going to a room to sort through the clothes and decide what she wanted to wear to Coachella left Banks and Reynolds at the fitting never to return with the clothes,\u201d the statement claimed.<\/p>\n

The duo\u2019s lawyer Ezra Salami said in May that legal notices sent to Faith and her manager went unanswered and the pair are now planning to sue.<\/p>\n
