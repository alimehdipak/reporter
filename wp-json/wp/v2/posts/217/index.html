<\/p>\n

Emilio Delgado, who captured the hearts of children and adults alike for more than 40 years as Luis on \u201cSesame Street,\u201d has died.<\/p>\n

His death at 81 years old was confirmed to The Post by a Sesame Workshop Rep. <\/p>\n

\u201cA beloved member of the Sesame family for over 50 years, his warmth and humor invited children to share a friendship that has echoed through generations,\u201d a statement from the rep read. <\/p>\n

\u201cAt the forefront of representation, Emilio proudly laid claim to the record for the longest-running role for a Mexican-American in a TV series.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are so grateful he shared his talents with us and with the world.\u201d\u00a0\u00a0 \u00a0<\/p>\n

Emilio Delgado was a cherished icon on \u201cSesame Street\u201d for more than 40 years.\u00a9PBS\/Courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

The actor, singer and voice artist\u2019s wife, Carole Delgado, told TMZ that her husband had been battling blood cancer for the past few years and died surrounded by his family in NYC. The Post has reached out to his rep for confirmation.<\/p>\n

Delgado \u2014 also a stage actor who appeared in several productions of \u201cSesame Street\u201d \u2014 was reportedly working up until his death in a theater run of \u201cQuixote Nuevo.\u201d <\/p>\n

The Mexican American actor first became a household name playing Luis \u2014 the singing owner of the neighborhood \u201cFix-It Shop\u201d \u2014 on the long-running children\u2019s television show in 1971.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

He remained on the series until 2016 but continued making appearances at conventions for the show. Over the years he also appeared in numerous other television shows, including \u201cQuincy M.E.,\u201d \u201cFalcon Crest,\u201d \u201cLou Grant,\u201d \u201cLaw & Order,\u201d \u201cPerson of Interest\u201d and \u201cHouse of Cards.\u201d<\/p>\n

Delgado was born on the California side of the US-Mexico border and was raised in Mexico by his grandparents, he told the Houston Chronicle in 2020. He worked in a real-life fix-it-type shop, repairing bicycles. When his family moved to Los Angeles, he picked up the trombone and drums and later pursued singing and acting, landing his first major gig on the Mexican American soap opera \u201cCancion de la Raza,\u201d all while pursuing voiceover work. <\/p>\n

When he was hired to play Luis, he embraced the opportunity as a way to improve the representation of Latinos on TV.<\/p>\n

Emilio Delgado said \u201cSesame Street\u201d gave him a chance to show Latinos in a positive light.Sesame Workshop \/ Courtesy: Everett Collection During Sesame Street\u2019s 19th season in 1988, Delgado\u2019s character Luis became engaged to and married Maria, played by Sonia Manzano.\u00a9PBS\/Courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019d been trying all my professional life to be somewhere I can change that, whether I was talking about it or trying to get into a project that showed Latinos in a good light,\u201d he told the Chronicle. \u201cThat\u2019s why \u2018Sesame Street\u2019 was such a good thing. For the first time on television, they showed Latinos as real human beings.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe weren\u2019t dope addicts. We weren\u2019t maids or prostitutes, which were the way we were being shown in television in film,\u201d he added. <\/p>\n

\u201cHere, on \u2018Sesame Street,\u2019 there were different people who spoke different languages and ate interesting foods, and they were all Americans.\u201d<\/p>\n

Generations of fans took to social media to share their appreciation for the bilingual pioneer, calling him a \u201cvery talented and kind soul\u201d who \u201cpaved the way\u201d for Latino representation. <\/p>\n

I didn\u2019t expect to hear this kind of news but I am heartbroken. <\/p>\n

A regular on the show from 1971 to 2015, there\u2019s no doubt that he\u2019s a very talented and kind soul. <\/p>\n
