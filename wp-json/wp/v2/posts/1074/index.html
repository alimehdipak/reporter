<\/p>\n

Elon Musk reiterated his view that Tesla employees are better off without union representation after a former United Auto Workers official pleaded guilty to siphoning more than $2 million from the leading union.<\/p>\n

Timothy Edmunds, the former financial secretary-treasurer of one of the UAW\u2019s Detroit-area branches, copped to federal charges that he pilfered the money to cover personal expenses \u2013 including gambling, guns and child support payments.<\/p>\n

Musk couldn\u2019t resist taking a shot at his frequent foe, tweeting that the \u201cUAW slogan\u201d was \u201cfighting for the right to embezzle money from auto workers!\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cThe UAW stole millions from workers, whereas Tesla has made many workers millionaires (via stock grants). Subtle, but important difference,\u201d Musk added.<\/p>\n

UAW did not immediately return a request for comment.<\/p>\n

Musk\u2019s remarks were the latest blow in a lengthy feud with UAW, whose officials have frequently targeted Tesla because its factories aren\u2019t unionized. Meanwhile, the Biden administration has cultivated close ties to UAW, inviting unionized auto companies such as Ford and General Motors to the White House while shunning Tesla.<\/p>\n

Earlier this month, Musk challenged UAW to hold a union vote at Tesla\u2019s California manufacturing plant \u2013 claiming compensation for the company\u2019s factory workers is \u201cthe highest in the auto industry.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019d like hereby to invite UAW to hold a union vote at their convenience. Tesla will do nothing to stop them,\u201d Musk said on March 3.<\/p>\n

Elon Musk argues Tesla employees are better treated and compensated without union representation.Hearst Newspapers via Getty Imag <\/p>\n

Speaking during an online forum on Tuesday, UAW President\u00a0Ray Curry said the union has not had any discussions with Musk or Tesla since he issued the challenge. Curry added that Tesla should first address lingering complaints at the National Labor Relations Board \u2013 including dropping an appeal of a ruling that found it violated federal labor laws.<\/p>\n

\u201cThat would be a good faith effort if they were interested in having that type of exchange,\u201d Curry said.<\/p>\n

UAW isn\u2019t the only entity pressuring Tesla over its labor prices. In 2019, a California judge\u00a0ruled Tesla violated labor laws\u00a0when Musk suggested workers would lose their stock options if they unionized.<\/p>\n

Tesla employees work at Tesla\u2019s factory\u2019s training center in Fremont, California.The Washington Post via Getty Images <\/p>\n
