Your Echo device can tip you off to deals on Amazon long before they\u2019re available.<\/p>\n

The retail behemoth\u2019s virtual assistant, Alexa, will now list upcoming price drops 24 hours in advance.<\/p>\n

The skill was introduced on Thursday, one of a number of additions that Amazon says make shopping with its digital helper easier.<\/p>\n

According to\u00a0the company, Alexa will notify Prime members when eligible products in their wish list or shopping cart are on sale.<\/p>\n

The artificial intelligence software will also flag price cuts on items they\u2019ve marked as \u201csaved for later\u201d.<\/p>\n

That\u2019s the section of your Amazon account where you earmark items that you want to buy in future.<\/p>\n

When the feature is enabled, you\u2019ll see the Yellow Ring light or a pop-up notification on your Echo device.<\/p>\n

To learn more, just ask, \u201cAlexa, what are my notifications?\u201d<\/p>\n

You can then ask Alexa to order the items when the deal is live or send a reminder.<\/p>\n

Just say, \u201cAlexa, buy it for me,\u201d and when the deal is available, Alexa will use the default payment and delivery address in your Amazon account to make the purchase.<\/p>\n

The alerts only apply to \u201celigible\u201d products, and it\u2019s unclear when this label applies, such as whether or not they\u2019re items sold by Amazon.<\/p>\n

They\u2019re available now on Echo devices in the U.S. and will likely roll out to those in Europe in future.<\/p>\n

You can activate deal notifications through the Amazon Alexa app.<\/p>\n

Just say, \u201cAlexa, buy it for me,\u201d and when the deal is available, Alexa will use the default payment and delivery address in your Amazon account to make the purchase. Shutterstock <\/p>\n

Before you begin, make sure you have the most up-to-date version of the software by heading to Google Play or Apple\u2019s App Store.<\/p>\n

Once that\u2019s out the way, open the Alexa iOS or Android app and tap\u00a0More > Settings > Notifications.<\/p>\n

Tap on\u00a0Amazon Shopping\u00a0and under\u00a0Shopping Recommendations\u00a0enable\u00a0Deal Recommendations.<\/p>\n

Amazon said: \u201cWe\u2019re always looking for opportunities to build new Alexa experiences that make our customers\u2019 lives easier.\u201d<\/p>\n

Alexa, a voice-activated assistant powered by artificial intelligence, was introduced\u00a0in 2014 with the release of the first Amazon Echo\u00a0speaker.<\/p>\n

It\u2019s up against Google Assistant and Apple\u2019s Siri, which each have male and female voice options as well as a range of accents to choose from.<\/p>\n
