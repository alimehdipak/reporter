Jenna Dewan said that she could relate to both the parents and the kids on CBS\u2019s new family dance competition series, \u201cCome Dance with Me.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI was initially taken with the concept of the show. I loved the idea of taking young trained dancers, and asking them to ask one of their untrained parents to dance with them. I was similar to a lot of these kids, I was dancing nonstop, and it was my life,\u201d Dewan, 41, told The Post.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI know what it\u2019s like to be a diehard dancer as a kid. But I also know what it\u2019s like to be the parent who would do anything for her kids,\u201d she said. \u201cI could see it in their eyes [in the show], how much the parents wanted to impress us and be there for their kids. I get it. It was super-fun, but hard for me to judge, because my heart was so invested in their journey.\u201d<\/p>\n

Jenna Dewan as a judge on the new parent-child dance competition series \u201cCome Dance with Me.\u201d CBS Mother and son duo Lucas and Carolina take the stage in \u201cCome Dance with Me.\u201d CBS <\/p>\n

In \u201cCome Dance With Me\u201d (Fridays at 8 p.m.) is hosted by Grammy-winning songwriter Philip Lawrence \u2014 best known for his collaborations with Bruno Mars \u2014 with judges Dewan, Dexter Mayfield and Tricia Miranda.\u00a0<\/p>\n

In each episode, a young trained dancer (between the ages of 8 and 15) taps one of their dance-novice parents to join them on stage, performing a choreographed number for the panel of judges. They\u2019re evaluated based on technique, team chemistry and showmanship. Each week, parent\/child duos are eliminated until the last pair standing wins a $100,000 grand prize.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cI was drawn to the courage that it takes for these parents to say yes,\u201d said Lawrence, 41, who has won seven Grammys and garnered 18 nominations.<\/p>\n

Grammy-winner Philip Lawrence hosts \u201cCome Dance With Me.\u201d CBS Emelyn, 13, dances with her novice mom, Nicole, on \u201cCome Dance with Me.\u201d CBS Ava, 10, dances with her novice dad, John, on \u201cCome Dance with Me.\u201d CBS <\/p>\n

\u201cThey\u2019ve been watching from the sidelines, supporting their kids\u2019 dreams, and they\u2019re now thrust into that arena when they\u2019ve had none of that training. It takes a lot of courage to get out of your comfort zone like that. It drew me to give them encouragement. You get to see the kids telling the parents what to do \u2026 and the parents are trying not to be told!\u201d<\/p>\n

In addition to acting stints on \u201cThe Rookie,\u201d and \u201cSuperman & Lois,\u201d Dewan is best known for the 2006 dance movie \u201cStep Up,\u201d and she\u2019s also been a backup dancer for the likes of Janet Jackson and Pink. <\/p>\n

But, similar to the parents on the show, she\u2019s also a mom to an 8-year old daughter Everly (with ex-husband Channing Tatum) and a 2-year-old son (with fiance Steve Kazee, \u201cShameless\u201d).\u00a0<\/p>\n

Jenna Dewan with fellow judge, Dexter Mayfield, on \u201cCome Dance with Me.\u201d CBS <\/p>\n
