Johnny Depp is planning an official showbiz return after his victorious courtroom showdown with ex-wife Amber Heard \u2014 a new album with guitar god Jeff Beck.<\/p>\n

The \u201cPirates of the Caribbean\u201d star has already joined the \u201cHi Ho Silver Lining\u201d rocker on a series of high-profile UK gigs \u2014 even missing the court verdict that he was defamed by his ex.<\/p>\n

Hard-living Depp, 58, didn\u2019t mention his $10 million win during his latest appearance, in Gateshead \u2014 but buddy Beck referred to it, according to the BBC, which quoted him as saying, \u201cWhat a result.\u201d<\/p>\n

The guitar maestro then revealed what his Hollywood star pal was going to do next \u2014 teasing the crowd about the upcoming album while gushing about their friendship.<\/p>\n

\u201cI met this guy five years ago and we\u2019ve never stopped laughing since,\u201d Beck told the crowd, according to the BBC.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe actually made an album. I don\u2019t know how it happened. It will be out in July,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Johnny Depp joined Jeff Beck to perform at the Royal Albert Hall Thursday night.UnBoxPHD \/ SplashNews.com A new album is due out in July. P. Donovan via REUTERS <\/p>\n

The pair already released their first single as a duo, a cover of John Lennon\u2019s \u201cIsolation,\u201d in April, calling themselves \u201cmusical soulmates.\u201d<\/p>\n

At the time, Beck explained that they had \u201cbeen working on music together for a while now.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cYou\u2019ll be hearing more from Johnny and me in a little while,\u201d the one-time Yardbird teased fans at the time.<\/p>\n

Depp has increasingly turned his back on Hollywood to focus on music \u2014 calling it his first love \u2014 most famously in the Hollywood Vampires with Alice Cooper and Aerosmith\u2019s Joe Perry.<\/p>\n

Beck (right) has called himself and Depp \u201cmusical soulmates.\u201dSTR\/AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

He was clearly \u201chaving fun\u201d at Thursday\u2019s show, Beck fan James Pallister, 32, told the BBC.<\/p>\n

\u201cHe looked really happy. He seemed to be in a great place,\u201d he said. \u201cThe crowd was behind him and I think he gave people what they wanted to see.\u201d<\/p>\n

Miya Wright, 28, told the broadcaster that she got tickets to the gig only after seeing that the actor was in town and likely to appear.<\/p>\n
