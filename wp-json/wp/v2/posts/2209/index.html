Billionaire Elon Musk\u2019s deal to buy Twitter has sparked a selloff of shares for one of the platform\u2019s would-be rivals \u2013 former President Donald Trump\u2019s fledging Truth Social network.<\/p>\n

Shares of Digital World Acquisition Corp, the SPAC planning to take Truth Social public if it can gain regulatory approval, were down 13% by late Monday afternoon, when Twitter\u2019s board announced it would accept Musk\u2019s offer to buy the company for $54.20 per share.<\/p>\n

The stock is down nearly 50% over the last month and nearly 40% since April 4, the day Musk first disclosed he had acquired a stake in Twitter.<\/p>\n

Musk has pledged to take Twitter private and overhaul the company\u2019s policies with an emphasis on protecting free speech. That could spell trouble for Truth Social and other rival platforms that launched as an alternative to Twitter after the company\u2019s past leadership faced accusations of censoring conservative voices.<\/p>\n

\u201cFree speech is the bedrock of a functioning democracy, and Twitter is the digital town square where matters vital to the future of humanity are debated,\u201d Musk said in a press release after the deal was announced.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Twitter\u2019s board agreed to accept Musk\u2019s offer to buy the company for $54.20 per share.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Trump had one of Twitter\u2019s most popular accounts \u2013 amassing nearly 90 million followers \u2013 until he was banned from the platform and other prominent social media venues following the Jan. 6 Capitol riot.<\/p>\n

Some Trump supporters have urged Musk to reinstate the former president\u2019s account. But Trump told Fox News that he does not intend to return to Twitter even after Musk completes his takeover of the company.<\/p>\n

\u201cI am not going on Twitter, I am going to stay on Truth,\u201d said. \u201cI hope Elon buys Twitter because he\u2019ll make improvements to it and he is a good man, but I am going to be staying on Truth.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk has vowed to overhaul Twitter with an emphasis on protecting free speech.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Trump\u2019s remarks echoed the stance he outlined than an interview earlier this month with Siriux XM\u2019s Americano Media, when he said that he \u201cprobably wouldn\u2019t have any interest\u201d in returning to Twitter.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter\u2019s become very boring. They\u2019ve gotten rid of a lot of good voices on Twitter, a lot of their conservative voices,\u201d Trump said at the time.<\/p>\n

But Truth Social faced issues even before Musk bought Twitter. The company\u2019s heads of technology and product development quit earlier this month under unclear circumstances.<\/p>\n

The social media app has also battled various technical issues \u2013 with many users stuck on a waitlist or unable to access the platform long after its launch in Apple\u2019s App Store in February. Trump himself has barely used the app.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
