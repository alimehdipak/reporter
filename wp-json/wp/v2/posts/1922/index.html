When actress Priyanka Chopra Jonas moved to NYC to film the 2015 ABC thriller \u201cQuantico,\u201d her first stop was the iconic Plaza Hotel.<\/p>\n

She \u201cspent several months\u201d living the Eloise dream at the Plaza Hotel before finding an apartment, she told The Post. It started a love affair with iconic hotels like the Le Royal Monceau, Raffles Paris; the Savoy in London; and the Fairmont Century Plaza in Los Angeles. <\/p>\n

Now, Chopra Jonas \u2014 who has appeared in last year\u2019s \u201cThe White Tiger\u201d and \u201cThe Matrix Resurrections\u201d \u2014 is prepping to do a lot more luxury hotel habitation thanks to a new dream role as the face of hospitality giant Accor\u2019s new summer campaign, \u201cIt\u2019s ALL Possible.\u201d<\/p>\n

Accor has a portfolio of more than 5,300 properties across 40-plus brands including Fairmont, Raffles, the Plaza and the Orient Express. The campaign hopes to inject travel with a dose of Hollywood glamour to get people excited about globetrotting post-pandemic.<\/p>\n

The Los Angeles-based Chopra Jonas spoke to The Post about her latest role, her favorite places in New York and the destinations she can\u2019t wait to visit \u2014 some for the first time and others, once again.<\/p>\n

The new ad campaign is all about Hollywood glamour. ALL \u2013 Accor Live Limitless <\/p>\n

What is the sign of a great hotel?<\/h2>\n

The service, the food, the atmosphere \u2014 all of these matter, of course, but I would give complete priority to the service. I\u2019m from Asia, and we have a strong culture of hospitality there.<\/p>\n

You lived in New York. What was your favorite neighborhood?<\/h2>\n

I lived in both Tribeca and the Upper East Side, and I have to say that I loved the neighborhood and residential feel of being uptown the most.<\/p>\n

Do you have a favorite hotel in the city?<\/h2>\n

The Plaza is obviously a special place for me given my time there. Then there\u2019s Baccarat, the Mark, the Greenwich and Four Seasons Downtown. I like to experience different hotels.<\/p>\n

Accor operates thousands of top hotels around the world.ALL \u2013 Accor Live Limitless <\/p>\n

You are a partner at the Indian eatery Sona in the Flatiron District, but what are some of your other favorite restaurants?<\/h2>\n

I love Cipriani for the great pastas and cocktails, and Temple Bar and the ABC restaurants. I\u2019m an explorer, though, and love trying different places.<\/p>\n

Describe your perfect Sunday in the city.<\/h2>\n

If I\u2019m not working, which I usually am, I wake up late \u2014 like 10 or 11 a.m. \u2014 and then wander around. I end up someplace for brunch, often Sona because I love dosas, and the restaurant does great ones. I go to the park with my dogs, get lost in the Strand and check out boutiques in Soho. I take in the diversity of the city.<\/p>\n

What about your ideal Saturday night out?<\/h2>\n

It\u2019s about our group of friends hanging out and ordering awesome food. Or going out for a great meal. Maybe Polo Bar. I love the vibe there. Or maybe it\u2019s seeing a Broadway show and discussing it afterwards over dinner.<\/p>\n

Chopra Jonas says that she loves to explore the dining scenes in the cities she visits. ALL \u2013 Accor Live Limitless <\/p>\n

What are your other favorite city\u2019s to explore?<\/h2>\n

Goa, Rome and Los Angeles where I live are my favorites. I find peace in the community in LA and hiking with my dogs.<\/p>\n

What destinations are on top of your must-visit list?<\/h2>\n

I really want to start traveling for leisure again. I want to go to Venice and back to Turks and Caicos to feel the sand in my feet \u2014 then the South of France, Hawaii and back to Asia. I love Thailand and India, which is home. I haven\u2019t been back in two years.<\/p>\n

Tell us what kind of traveler you are: Go, go, go? Chilled out? <\/h2>\n

If I\u2019m going for work, I\u2019m focused on the job and won\u2019t see the city I\u2019m in. For vacations, I make no plans or schedule because my life is a schedule with a call sheet and set times. Vacations need to be the opposite. I don\u2019t pre-book tours and don\u2019t want to be told when to go to dinner. I\u2019ll see where the day takes me.<\/p>\n

Do you use a travel advisor to book your trips, or do you like to be in the driver\u2019s seat yourself?<\/h2>\n
