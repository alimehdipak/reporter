Elon Musk\u2019s bid to mount a hostile takeover of Twitter is helping his Q rating \u2014 or popularity \u2014 as the world\u2019s richest man is generating more online interest than pop stars Rihanna and Britney Spears, according to a study.<\/p>\n

Musk is the tech mogul who has drawn more internet buzz than any of his mutli-billionaire peers in Silicon Valley.<\/p>\n

According to the online tracking company Taboola, Musk has had more mentions online than Amazon founder Jeff Bezos, Meta Platforms CEO Mark Zuckerberg and Apple CEO Tim Cook combined over the past 90 days.<\/p>\n

During the course of the last three months, Musk has generated 23 million page views alone, the firm says.<\/p>\n

In the past month-and-a-half, he has generated a 32% increase in readership \u2014 beating out Spears and Rihanna.<\/p>\n

The two pop stars have recently made news by announcing their pregnancies, but that hasn\u2019t stopped Musk from surpassing them in online clicks.<\/p>\n

Rihanna also joined Musk on the Forbes list of billionaires for 2022 \u2014 thanks to her line of fashion and designer products.<\/p>\n

\u201cDespite his controversies\u2026one thing Elon continues to show us about leadership is how to build personal brand awareness, especially as he continues to successfully dominate the news cycle,\u201d according to the study by Taboola.<\/p>\n

Musk has been embracing his celebrity status in recent years. Last year, he hosted Saturday Night Live \u2014 though the reviews were less than stellar.<\/p>\n
