These men are saying \u201cyes\u201d to the vest.<\/p>\n

Spring has sprung, the city is reopening, workers are returning to offices, and, Midtown men are out in vests in full force.<\/p>\n

\u201cYou don\u2019t want to wear a sports jacket to the office anymore. The vest just comes in perfect for every day,\u201d said Julio Maya, 33, an accountant for a property-management company who was spotted in a navy-blue fleece vest on 48th Street and Sixth Avenue on Tuesday. \u201cOne day I saw some guy wearing a vest and I said, \u2018Hey, I think I need to get one.\u2019 \u201d <\/p>\n

Queens native Allen Fok, 30, a customer-success manager for a tech company with offices near West 46th Street, is a big fan of his Uniqlo puffer vest that he snagged for $50.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Julio Maya, 33, in his company-branded fleece vest. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s the most versatile article of clothing in my closet,\u201d Fok told The Post. \u201cIt\u2019s warm, lightweight and perfectly complements dress shirts while still appearing professional. It can also be easily dressed down in a casual setting.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Indeed, the vest has become the unofficial \u201cMidtown Uniform,\u201d with an Instagram account of the same name and 175,000 followers parodying the finance-and-tech-bro types that tend to sport it (and who the account\u2019s creator refers to as \u201cBrads and Chads\u201d). Fleece or quilted toppers from outdoorsy brands such as Patagonia spawned the nickname \u201cpower vests\u201d for their popularity among Silicon Valley honchos, including Jeff Bezos, Uber chief executive officer Dara Khosrowshahi and Dropbox founder Drew Houston.<\/p>\n

Allen Fok, 30, who works for a tech company, loves his Uniqlo puffer down vest he bought for $50. Courtesy of Allen Fok Amazon founder Jeff Bezos was an early wearer of the wardrobe staple worn among tech bros in Silicon Valley and beyond. Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cWhen you don\u2019t want to wear a big, bulky sweater and a blazer is too formal, a vest is that easy answer,\u201d said image consultant and fashion stylist Amanda Sanders, who recently styled a few male executive clients in Peter Millar\u2019s $200 reversible quilted takes on the trends. Sanders noted that some clients especially love the vest because it conceals the infamous \u201cdad bod\u201d or any pounds packed on during the pandemic. <\/p>\n

\u201cA vest hides a multitude of sins \u2014 if it\u2019s something darker it\u2019s going to make you look slimmer and hide that belly,\u201d she said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Vests are currently ubiquitous at retailers all over New York City. Wall Street types are more prone to purchasing pricey iterations such as a $595 Corneliani vest, Sanders told The Post.<\/p>\n

Image consultant and stylist Amanda Sanders has been supplying clients with a slew of high-end vests. The Corneliani display at Bloomingdale\u2019s on 59th Street features vests front and center. <\/p>\n

At Brunello Cucinelli\u00a0boutiques across the city, men\u2019s vests are top sellers in spring and fall, particularly in nylon, woven and tailored fabrics, suede and leathers, a spokesperson for the company told The Post in an email.\u00a0And at mom-and-pop retailer Peter Elliot Blue on the Upper East Side, which sells its own private label and high-end brands like Canada Goose, vests have been flying off the racks.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cWe sell them like hotcakes,\u201d owner Eliot Rabin said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

His longtime customer, Charles Fuller, 64, from the Upper East Side, said he\u2019s been wearing vests to work for years and couldn\u2019t care less whether they\u2019re hot with billionaire types.<\/p>\n
