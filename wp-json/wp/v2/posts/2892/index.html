Why do car windows cost so much?<\/p>\n

Demand. As with all products, demand weighs heavy on price determination. The less a windshield is needed, the more expensive it will be. The same goes for the other way around, the more one is needed, the less it will cost.<\/p>\n

Why the small window on a car is more expensive?<\/p>\n

Windshields break often, so manufacturers make lots of them. These large production runs make the per-window-cost very low. Quarter windows don\u2019t break very often, so they produce fewer pieces and the per-window-cost can be significantly higher.<\/p>\n
