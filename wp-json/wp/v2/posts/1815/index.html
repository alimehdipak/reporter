Midtown is swapping out its blades for wheels this weekend as the Rockefeller Center ice skating rink temporarily transforms into a roller disco for the first time in 80 years. <\/p>\n

The last time you could rollerskate at the iconic Lower Plaza in front of 30 Rock was back in 1940. But this time the vibes are solidly disco. <\/p>\n

Beginning today and continuing through Oct., skaters of all skill levels are invited to roll on down to The Rink at Rockefeller Plaza seven days a week, and til midnight on Thursday, Friday and Saturday nights. Free day lockers are available (bags are not permitted on the rink) and tickets for one-hour skate sessions cost $20 for adults who bring their own skates and $30 who need to rent. (Tickets for children under $15 with skates cost $12 or $22 for those without.)\u00a0<\/p>\n

The seasonal skate spot, Flipper\u2019s Roller Boogie Palace, will offer not just a space to glide but also has a host of live music performances and DJs lined up to perform at the open-air venue, which is styled in the vein of its beloved if short-lived namesake.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The original Flipper\u2019s had a gold dome, operated from 1979 to 1981 in Los Angeles and \u201cwas Studio 54 on wheels\u201d according to \u201cCharlie\u2019s Angels\u201d actress Jaclyn Smith.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cOur original rink was the stuff of Los Angeles legend. For three glittering years an entire generation of eccentrics, outsiders, punk rockers and disco queens came together to experience a unique kind of freedom on roller skates,\u201d Flippers\u2019 website says. \u201cCut to 2022 \u2014 The legendary roller rink is back, opening our doors at Rockefeller Center.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Liberty Ross, the model daughter of Flipper\u2019s founder Ian \u201cFlipper\u201d Ross, leaned heavily on \u201870s nostalgia in designing her new homage to her father\u2019s venue.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cNothing is like getting on the rink at such an iconic and special place like Rockefeller Center, letting the wind blow through your hair, and feeling truly free. It\u2019s a relief to put down your phone these days and be present, which is how we came up with our mantra, \u2018less scroll, more roll,’\u201d Liberty told The Post.<\/p>\n

\u201cA year ago this place was a ghost town,\u201d she\u00a0added to Vogue, speaking of the landmark plaza during the pandemic. \u201cIt feels amazing to bring this joy to the city.\u201d<\/p>\n
