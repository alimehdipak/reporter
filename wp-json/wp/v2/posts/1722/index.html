Elon Musk suggested on Thursday that buying Twitter and taking it private may not be in the cards as the social media platform\u2019s board of directors appears ready to throw up obstacles to thwart a hostile takeover bid from the world\u2019s richest man.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m not actually sure I will be able to acquire\u201d Twitter, Musk told a large audience at a TED conference in Vancouver, Canada, on Thursday.<\/p>\n

Perhaps Musk was aware of the Wall Street Journal report indicating that a \u201cperson familiar with the matter\u201d said the company is planning a \u201cpoison pill\u201d that would dissuade the Tesla boss from completing his takeover bid.<\/p>\n

\u201cPoison pill\u201d is a strategy used by a company to prevent a hostile takeover. It usually entails making the company less attractive to the party that seeks to acquire it.<\/p>\n

Twitter could thwart Musk by offering to let its shareholders purchase additional shares at a steep discount, diluting the value of the stock and driving up the cost of a potential takeover.<\/p>\n

In 2018, the board of Papa John\u2019s pizza restaurants deployed the same strategy to prevent company founder John Schnatter from gaining full control of the firm.<\/p>\n

Musk made his first public comments on Thursday about his hostile takeover bid on Twitter.TED <\/p>\n

Saudi Prince Alwaleed bin Talal Al Saud, a billionaire who is also one of Twitter\u2019s largest shareholders, tweeted that he will vote to reject Musk\u2019s bid for the company.<\/p>\n

In his first public comments since an SEC filing Thursday indicating that he submitted a $41 billion all-cash bid for the company, Musk said making money isn\u2019t the driving force behind the takeover bid.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is not a way to make money, my strong intuitive sense is that having a public platform that is maximally trusted and broadly inclusive is extremely important to the future of civilization, I\u00a0don\u2019t\u00a0care about\u00a0the economics\u00a0at all,\u201d Musk said.<\/p>\n

Musk told a live audience that money wasn\u2019t the primary driving force behind the takeover bid.TED <\/p>\n

The multibillionaire said Twitter needs to adhere to the same freedom of speech principles that govern countries.<\/p>\n

\u201cTwitter has become kind of the de facto town square,\u201d Musk said. <\/p>\n

\u201cSo it\u2019s just really important that people have both the reality and the perception that they\u2019re able to speak freely within the bounds of the law.\u201d<\/p>\n

When asked why he wanted to buy Twitter, Musk joked: \u201cI don\u2019t know. A little bird tweeted in my ear or something.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk called on Twitter to \u201copen-source the algorithm\u201d \u2014 which would give software engineers a glimpse into how the service prioritizes its content. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe code should be on Github so people can look through it,\u201d Musk said.<\/p>\n

When asked what content moderation policies he would enact as the head of the company, Musk said: \u201cIf in doubt, let the speech, let it exist.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cBut I do think we want to be very reluctant to delete things \u2026 just be very cautious with permanent bans.\u201d<\/p>\n

Musk added: \u201cI wouldn\u2019t personally be in there editing tweets.\u201d<\/p>\n
