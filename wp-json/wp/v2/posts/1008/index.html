Jeff Bezos\u2019 space firm Blue Origin safely completed its fourth-ever crewed trip to low orbit on Thursday morning without \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d comedian Pete Davidson, who dropped out of the mission days earlier.<\/p>\n

The six passengers took off in a \u201cNew Shepard\u201d shuttle at roughly 9 a.m. local time from their launch site in Texas and returned to the ground roughly 10 minutes later. The spacecraft reached a maximum speed of 2,236 mph, according to Blue Origin.<\/p>\n

Davidson\u2019s replacement, longtime Blue Origin employee Gary Lai, was the first person to exit the capsule upon its return to Earth. <\/p>\n

Lai is the chief architect of Blue Origin\u2019s \u201cNew Shepard\u201d shuttle program and was one of the first people to join the firm.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t know that I could even, words could do that justice. You just have to feel it,\u201d Lai said after the mission landed. He added that the sensation of flying to space was \u201cdifferent\u201d from what he expected.<\/p>\n

Dubbed \u201cNS-20,\u201d the latest launch was the 20th in Blue Origin\u2019s history. <\/p>\n

Pete Davidson appears on SNL in a spacesuit \u2014 but backed out of his Blue Origin flight.Saturday Night Live The Blue Origin mission lasted roughly 10 minutes from start to finish.Reuters <\/p>\n

Aside from Lai, the mission\u2019s crew members included investor Marty Allen; SpaceKids Global founder Sharon Hagle; her husband and Tricor International CEO Mark Hagle; entrepreneur Jim Kitchen and Commercial Space Technologies President George Nield.<\/p>\n

Blue Origin identified Kitchen as a \u201cworld explorer who has visited all 193 U.N.-recognized countries.\u201d The trip to space added another accomplishment to his resume of adventure.<\/p>\n

Kitchen displayed a flag with the number 194 when he exited the capsule.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis was pushing that final boundary, going to space. Let me tell you, that was an out-of-body experience,\u201d Kitchen said. \u201cOn the way up, you\u2019re going 2,300 miles per hour and you feel every bit of that.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Blue Origin crew included longtime employee Gary Lai.Reuters Passenger Jim Kitchen displayed a flag with the number 194 when he exited the capsule.REUTERS <\/p>\n

Davidson\u2019s plan to fly to space with Blue Origin generated widespread buzz this month \u2013 until the firm revealed on March 18 that the mission was delayed and the comedian would no longer participate. <\/p>\n

An exact reason for Davidson\u2019s exit hasn\u2019t surfaced, though conflicts with his filming schedule may have been a factor.<\/p>\n
