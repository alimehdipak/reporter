Rise and Shine<\/h2>\n

Heels, $1,200 at Bottega VenetaPhoto by Troy Studio; Prop Stylist: Miako Katoh <\/p>\n

With a return to festive dressing on the rise, it\u2019s inevitable that our heel heights would also soar. After two years of slippers, sneakers and flats, it\u2019s time to level up in spring\u2019s silhouette du jour. Taller, zanier and, yes, brighter than ever before, Bottega Veneta\u2019s eye-catching platforms are a nod to the retro OG styles from the \u201870s. But they\u2019re full of refreshingly modern details, too, like a striking square toe, futuristic clasp and glossy finish. And much like those of us who\u2019ve been dreaming of a night out on the town, these towering beauties are primed to party.<\/p>\n

High society<\/h2>\n

Wedgwood x Sheila Bridgesteacup and saucer set, $124 at BloomingdalesGetty Images\/iStockphoto <\/p>\n

\u201cBridgerton\u201d fans, rejoice! Just in time for Friday\u2019s hotly anticipated Season 2 debut, The Carousel @ Bloomingdale\u2019s: Bridgerton pop-up is stocking fabulous Regency London-inspired fashion and home items, many created by women and designers of color. Among the finds: Wedgwood x Sheila Bridges china (featuring the interior designer\u2019s Harlem Toile de Jouy patterns), a Hope for Flowers by Tracy Reese capsule collection, Malone Souliers\u2019 bow-bedecked shoes, and Beekman 1802 goat-milk soaps wrapped in Lady Whistledown Society Papers. You\u2019ll also find many of the costumes worn on the hit Netflix series. Swoon!<\/p>\n

Diesel power<\/h2>\n

Julia Fox shows off a custom vinyl look \u2014 price upon request at Diesel.Getty Images for Diesel <\/p>\n

With a starry new creative director, Glenn Martens, and a just-launched Soho flagship, legendary denim brand Diesel is getting a serious upgrade. Martens (who\u2019s also a guest designer for Jean Paul Gaultier Couture and leads the Y\/Project label) is infusing Diesel\u2019s offerings with his own irreverent, expert tailoring. He toasted the new two-story boutique at a splashy opening party last month, with a DJ spinning for guests like La La Anthony and Alexandra Richards. The industrial-chic, exposed-brick space also showcases Diesel Library, the label\u2019s new sustainable denim collection.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Hats off<\/h2>\n

Ami x Borsalino felt hat, $595 at AmiCourtesy of the designer <\/p>\n

Parisian label Ami is celebrating its 10th anniversary in style. In December, the brand opened its first US outpost: a chic, 2,000-square-foot mirrored emporium in Soho, filled with men\u2019s and women\u2019s fashions and accessories. Imagined by Ami\u2019s creative director Alexandre Mattiussi, the flagship also features limited-edition collabs, including a sophisticated new range of floppy felt hats (in luxe hues of burgundy, green and black) with storied Italian milliner Borsalino.\u00a0<\/p>\n
