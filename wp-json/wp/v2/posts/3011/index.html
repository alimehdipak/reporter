Americans are grappling with soaring inflation, the ongoing pandemic and the likely overturn of Roe v. Wade \u2014 but they are interested in one issue above all else.<\/p>\n

The blockbuster defamation trial between Johnny Depp and Amber Heard is currently generating more social media engagement than any other topic, according to data from media monitoring platform NewsWhip.<\/p>\n

The data firm claims that the captivating courtroom drama has garnered an average of 508 social media interactions \u2014 i.e. likes, comments and shares \u2014 for each published article over the past month. <\/p>\n

That is almost five times as much engagement as articles pertaining to abortion (141 social media interactions) and more than 10 times as much engagement as articles about COVID-19 (44 social media interactions).\u00a0<\/p>\n

Meanwhile, people across the country claim to be concerned about the rising costs of goods and services, but articles about inflation only garner an average of 70 likes, comments and shares. <\/p>\n

The war in Ukraine doesn\u2019t enthrall Americans as much, with articles on the conflict only managing to attract 91 social media interactions on average. <\/p>\n

Even President Joe Biden doesn\u2019t come close to attracting the same level of interest that Depp and Heard do. Published stories about the commander in chief receive an average of 170 interactions on social media. <\/p>\n

Data shows that, in the past month, there have been four times as many Google searches for Amber Heard\u2019s name than for abortion or the Supreme Court.AP <\/p>\n

And while hundreds of thousands of people took part in pro-choice rallies across the country after the leak of a Supreme Court draft that could strike down Roe v. Wade, data shows millions more Americans care more about Amber Heard \u2014 at least online. <\/p>\n

In the past month, there have been four times as many Google searches for Heard\u2019s name than for abortion or the Supreme Court, Axios reported.<\/p>\n

The only other public figure who has come close to attracting the same level of social media interest as Heard in the past month is her former flame, Elon Musk. <\/p>\n

Google searches for the billionaire are up in recent weeks after his Twitter buyout, but Heard\u2019s name is still searched twice as much as Musk\u2019s, NewsWhip reports. <\/p>\n
