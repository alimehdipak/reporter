A tooth extraction can cause long-term tooth pain. The surgical process of removing a tooth may result in pain, swelling and infection. In some cases, the pain can last for weeks or even months after the surgery. If you experience prolonged pain after a tooth extraction, speak to your doctor about potential treatment options. Some possible treatments include medication or surgery.<\/p>\n

Tooth extractions don’t just happen once in a blue moon. Dental professionals<\/a> remove teeth several times a day, and modern dental technology makes extractions easier and faster than ever before. After extraction, most patients get back to normal in just a few days; nevertheless, some people experience extended pain after their procedure.<\/p>\n

Oral Surgery Recovery- What To Expect<\/h2>\n

Pain medication and instructions for aftercare will be provided to you after your oral surgery. You should rest and take good care of yourself after having your tooth removed. After the procedure, some patients feel fine, while others need a few days to recover. It depends on the number of teeth you had removed as well as the complexity of your oral surgery on how long it will take. It is normal to experience pain, swelling, and bruising. However, these should subside within a few days.<\/p>\n

Extraction of a tooth causing prolonged pain<\/h2>\n

A dentist should be contacted if you experience prolonged pain after tooth extraction. Everyone has a different level of pain tolerance, so after an extraction, some people may have a tender mouth for a while. Extensive pain can also indicate infection. You can also tell your doctor if you notice any other symptoms.<\/p>\n

\nTalking to a Dentist<\/h2>\n

In conclusion, if you have any questions about your oral surgery recovery, you should contact your dentist. Being safe is better than being sorry. You can also get practical tips from your dentist about how to cope with pain.<\/p>\n
