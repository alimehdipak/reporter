Johnny Depp is bouncing back following his defamation trial win against ex-wife Amber Heard. <\/p>\n

His longtime friend of two decades and past co-star Greg Ellis has shed some light on the 59-year-old\u2019s current state of mind.<\/p>\n

Ellis, 54, and Depp starred in the \u201cPirates of the Caribbean\u201d franchise together and first crossed paths at a table read at Los Angeles\u2019 Viper Room\u00a020 years prior.<\/p>\n

Ellis noted to Fox News recently that he spoke to the \u201cEdward Scissorhands\u201d star since the trial ended last month.<\/p>\n

The \u201cDays of Our Lives\u201d actor shared that Depp is \u201cin good spirits\u201d and claimed that \u201cthe weight of the world has been lifted off his shoulders.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

Ellis explained that he thinks that Depp is trying to focus on his music and other film projects, as the latter has recently been seen in the United Kingdom playing concerts alongside Jeff Beck. <\/p>\n

The British filmmaker also pointed out that the people around him will possibly be \u201cvery selective\u201d about who the Kentucky native works with.<\/p>\n

Greg Ellis discussed his old pal Johnny Depp\u2019s trial victory, as well as how the actor is doing since the win last month.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cSo, I think he\u2019s just happy to get his life back and hopefully for this to be, you know, talked about less,\u201d Ellis said, referring to the trial that ultimately led to a $10.3 million victory for Depp. <\/p>\n

Ellis even gave his own take on the court case, which stemmed from a 2018 Washington Post op-ed that Heard, 36, wrote in which she spoke about domestic abuse.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt is exceedingly rare for someone who is tagged as the respondent who has been put in the crosshairs with the false allegation of domestic violence \u2014 the silver bullet \u2014 to come out and get any kind of semblance of a victory, let alone have a voice,\u201d Ellis explained about the \u201cDark Shadows\u201d star\u2019s victory. \u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cSo, the fact that he was able to speak for the first time, and I think we saw from the trial as well, his humanity and the spirit and gentle nature of the man,\u201d Ellis continued. \u201cHe was consistent and only took responsibility for his faults and the mistakes that he made. He\u2019s never said he\u2019s perfect.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The \u201cPirates of the Caribbean\u201d franchise is broken up into five films and premiered from 2003 through 2017.<\/p>\n
