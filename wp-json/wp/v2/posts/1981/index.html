OnlyFans says it has \u201ctemporarily paused\u201d the accounts of Russian creators due to sanctions put in place against the country following President Vladimir Putin\u2019s invasion of Ukraine.<\/p>\n

The social media platform that allows strippers to monetize their content told Motherboard that it can \u201cno longer property serve our Russian creator community\u201d because of uncertainty over payment options complicated by Western sanctions.<\/p>\n

\u201cOnlyFans is a creator first business,\u201d the company said in a statement. <\/p>\n

\u201cOver the past few months we have explored several options to continue providing our services to creators impacted by the Russia \/ Ukraine war.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n

The company added: \u201cHowever due to a further tightening of payment restrictions to and from Russia, OnlyFans can no longer properly serve our Russian creator community.\u201d <\/p>\n

In February, OnlyFans suspended payments to Russian content creators \u2014 resulting in losses of thousands of dollars in income for sex workers who rely on the money to get by.<\/p>\n

Western tech companies have cut ties with Russia after the country\u2019s military invaded Ukraine in late February.AFP via Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cPlease be informed that your country is not supported for payouts, therefore we are unable to assist you regarding that matter at this time,\u201d OnlyFans told creators in February.<\/p>\n

But the payments resumed after the company said it resolved the issue.<\/p>\n

Russia-based creators said the decision will deprive them of a key source of income.Twitter\/@Mizuki__tyan <\/p>\n

Now OnlyFans says it needs to re-impose the restrictions.<\/p>\n

Content creators said they were being punished for their government\u2019s actions even though they are opposed to the invasion of Ukraine.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe can\u2019t work for OnlyFans now,\u201d one of them told Motherboard.<\/p>\n

This is the second time in recent months that OnlyFans has paused payments to Russian content creators.SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Gett <\/p>\n

\u201cWe have been working on this platform for a year, and now we have to start all over again. It is a pity that all Russians are now perceived as enemies. But not everyone supports the war. And many continue to express their position, despite the fact that it is dangerous in Russia.\u201d<\/p>\n

Since the start of the invasion in late February, Western tech companies have fled en masse from Russia, halting their business ties to the country.<\/p>\n

Facebook, Google, Sony, Nintendo, Microsoft, PayPal, Apple, Twitter, TikTok, Netflix, Spotify, and others are among the more than 400 companies that have pulled up stakes.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
