The 94th Academy Awards is set to have some riveting performers take the stage at the Dolby Theater on March 27.<\/p>\n

On Friday, the telecast\u2019s producers, Will Packer and Shayla Cowan, announced an all-star lineup featuring the show\u2019s music director\u00a0Adam Blackstone, Blink-182 drummer\u00a0Travis Barker and singer\u00a0Sheila E. as among those who will perform at the ceremony.<\/p>\n

Pianist\u00a0Robert Glasper and the Samples, a vocal group led by Jason White, are also set to perform.<\/p>\n

DJ\u00a0D-Nice, who over the last two years united millions of people around the world with his virtual Club Quarantine parties, will rock the house during the show as well as at the Governors Ball, the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences\u2019 official post-Oscars celebration.<\/p>\n

D-Nice will also host the official Oscars Club Quarantine pre-party featuring special guests via Instagram Live on Friday at 9:30 p.m. EST.<\/p>\n

More musical guests will be announced in the days leading up to the show. The Oscars will air live on ABC Sunday, March 27, at 8 p.m. EST.<\/p>\n

DJ D-Nice will also be performing at the Governors Ball, a post-Oscars bash.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Best original song nominees at this year\u2019s Oscars are the frontrunners to perform. The nominees include tracks by Beyonc\u00e9 and DIXON (\u201cBe Alive\u201d from \u201cKing Richard\u201d), Billie Eilish and Finneas (\u201cNo Time to Die\u201d from \u201cNo Time to Die\u201d), Lin-Manuel Miranda (\u201cDos Oruguitas\u201d from \u201cEncanto\u201d), Van Morrison (\u201cDown to Joy\u201d from \u201cBelfast\u201d) and Diane Warren (\u201cSomehow You Do\u201d from \u201cFour Good Days,\u201d by Reba McEntire).<\/p>\n

Amy Schumer, Regina Hall and Wanda Sykes are\u00a0hosting\u00a0the show this year.<\/p>\n
