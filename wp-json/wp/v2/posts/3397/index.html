What will the jet-set be buzzing about in the Hamptons this summer? We\u2019ve rounded up the latest luxury trends on the East End \u2014 from adorable mini cars for jaunts to the beach to professional vacation packers and a new artist studio you won\u2019t want to miss. <\/p>\n

Here\u2019s our Well-To-Do list for the season. <\/p>\n

Ma\u2019s House<\/h2>\n

A 2020 print (from \u201cSacredness of Hills\u201d) by Jeremy Dennis, $3,000 at Ma\u2019s House. Ma\u2019s House <\/p>\n

Skip the sprawling artists\u2019 retreat rumored to be planned by supergallerists David and Monica Zwirner (said to be circling a site on Lake Montauk) and instead make a visit to Ma\u2019s House this summer.<\/p>\n

Artist and Shinnecock Indian Nation member Jeremy Dennis spent the pandemic transforming his late grandmother\u2019s home on the Shinnecock Reservation into a BIPOC artist residency program. Dennis has hosted several creatives so far; visual artist Erica Morales arrives for a stint in May. Locals can make appointments for studio visits and purchase any work on show.<\/p>\n

\u201cEven in Southampton, our neighboring town, people often are surprised that we exist and are right next to them,\u201d Dennis tells Alexa. Snag a few for your collection while you can.<\/p>\n

Hampton Jollys<\/h2>\n

Order your very own tiny beach car (price upon request) at Hampton Jollys.Hampton Jollys <\/p>\n

Quogue-based Andrew Fopeano\u2019s interest in classic cars was first piqued as a young man while working at Carriage House Motor Cars in Greenwich, Conn.<\/p>\n

This summer, he\u2019s launched his own take on the preppy classic-car biz: Hampton Jollys.<\/p>\n

These mini, two-cylinder gadabouts were produced in Italy for just eight years (beginning in 1958) \u2014 think of them as the dolce vita-era answer to a golf cart, designed for the Euro jet set to scoot between their yachts and beach houses.<\/p>\n

Now Fopeano and co-founder Tori Luyckx have commissioned a new round of Jollys, custom-built in Italy.<\/p>\n

Pricing on request, they come with wicker seats, picnic baskets and a choice of seven candy colors. (The hands-down sportiest shade is \u201cWatermelon.\u201d<\/p>\n

Packing queen<\/h2>\n

Lisa Frohlich will help pack your luggage for $225 per hour.Lisa Frohlich <\/p>\n

When East Enders need a weekend away, there\u2019s only one woman they trust to pack their (Rimowa and Montblanc) bags: Lisa Frohlich. The chic fashion whiz, who runs @hamptonsnystyle Insta account and newsletter, is the 1 percent\u2019s secret weapon to keeping travel stress-free \u2014 all for $225 per hour.<\/p>\n

Think of Frohlich as part therapist, part stylist, part butler. \u201cWe start off by creating the \u2018holy grail\u2019 of spreadsheets, charting each day of your trip,\u201d Frohlich says of her process, which details every planned activity with an earmarked outfit.<\/p>\n

Her East End clients try on everything before it\u2019s packed, taking selfies which are then compiled into a lookbook-style album for reference on the road. So you\u2019ll show up looking fabulous, wherever you roam.<\/p>\n

Flying gardens<\/h2>\n

Landscape designer Edwina von Gal is working to save birdlife in the Hamptons. Getty Images\/iStockphoto <\/p>\n

Anyone who\u2019s anyone Out East hires Edwina von Gal to mastermind their garden \u2014 she\u2019s the go-to green thumb for the likes of Frank Gehry, Ina Garten and Calvin Klein.<\/p>\n

Last spring, she launched a campaign to transform luxe greenspaces across the area and beyond: Two-Thirds for the Birds. As she explains, it\u2019s a snappy name for a simple idea: \u201cTwo out of every three plants in your landscape should be native, and don\u2019t spray [pesticides].\u201d<\/p>\n

This dual effort creates an ideal ecosystem for local birdlife, thanks to the insects that flourish as a result \u2014 and are birds\u2019 favorite snacks. <\/p>\n

To help stem the loss of our feathered friends (three billion since the 1970s), Von Gal has launched the site 234Birds.org, offering free hints for any Hamptonite keen on adopting the template.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
