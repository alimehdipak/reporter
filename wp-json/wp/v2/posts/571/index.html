Potterheads shouldn\u2019t expect to see their favorite wizard in any future film adaptions of the play \u201cHarry Potter and the Cursed Child.\u201d<\/p>\n

Harry Potter himself, Daniel Radcliffe, isn\u2019t interested at the moment in reprising his career-making role.<\/p>\n

The British actor, 32, told\u00a0the New York Times\u00a0recently that a \u201cCursed Child\u201d flick is \u201cnot something I\u2019m really interested in doing right now.\u201d<\/p>\n

The \u201cLost City\u201d star continued, \u201cThis isn\u2019t the answer that anybody\u2019s going to want, but I think I was so able to go back and enjoy it because it\u2019s not a part of my day-to-day life anymore.\u201d <\/p>\n

Radcliffe and his co-stars Rupert Grint and Emma Watson reunited earlier this year for the \u201cHarry Potter 20th Anniversary: Return to Hogwarts\u201d special that aired on HBO Max.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019m getting to a point where I feel like I made it out of \u2018Potter\u2019 okay and I\u2019m really happy with where I am now, and to go back would be such a massive change to my life,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Radcliffe added, \u201cI\u2019m never going to say never, but the \u2018Star Wars\u2019 guys had like 30, 40 years before they went back. For me, it\u2019s only been 10. It\u2019s not something I\u2019m really interested in doing right now.\u201d<\/p>\n

Radcliffe and his co-stars Rupert Grint and Emma Watson reunited earlier this year.\u00a9Warner Bros\/Courtesy Everett <\/p>\n

\u201cCursed Child\u201d is a sequel to the \u201cHarry Potter\u201d movies and follows the exploits of grown-up Harry and Ginny as well as their youngest son, Albus. The 2016 play was written by Jack Thorne based on an original story by original HP author J.K. Rowling, John Tiffany and Thorne.\u00a0<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

\u201cHarry Potter and the Sorcerer\u2019s Stone\u201d director Chris Columbus\u00a0reflected on the franchise for the film\u2019s 20th anniversary in a Hollywood Reporter interview published last year. He revealed he would be ecstatic to direct a \u201cCursed Child\u201d film.<\/p>\n

\u201cA version of\u00a0\u2018Cursed Child\u2019\u00a0with Dan, Rupert and Emma at the right age, it\u2019s cinematic bliss,\u201d the 63-year-old said at the time. <\/p>\n
