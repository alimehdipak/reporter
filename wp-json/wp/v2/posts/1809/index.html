A group of 15 Meta shareholders wants the Facebook parent to oust two prominent members from the social media giant\u2019s board of directors \u2014 arguing their close ties to Mark Zuckerberg are hampering their ability to do the job.<\/p>\n

Shareholder Association for Research and Education (SHARE), a group that represents institutional investors, urged Meta shareholders not to re-elect venture capitalist Marc Andreessen and PayPal executive Peggy Alford to the board.<\/p>\n

The group of concerned shareholders \u201cattempted to engage with Meta Platforms\u2019 directors in a letter\u201d by urging them to replace Andreessen and Alford with \u201cnew, highly qualified independent directors\u201d but were \u201cignored,\u201d SHARE said in a filing.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe believe that the lack of independence of board members Peggy Alford and Marc L. Andreessen is undermining the board\u2019s ability to adequately oversee the management team and represent shareholders\u2019 best interests,\u201d the shareholders said in a filing.<\/p>\n

\u201cConsidering the risks and challenges the Company is facing, it is crucial for shareholders to hold Meta Platforms\u2019 board of directors accountable for failing to establish a truly independent board and implement governance reforms to reinforce shareholders\u2019 rights and protect long-term value,\u201d the shareholders added.<\/p>\n

Peggy Alford served as CFO of Mark Zuckerberg\u2019s personal charity.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Aside from SHARE, the group includes the Illinois state treasurer, Arjuna Capital and Storebrand Asset Management \u2014 among other entities pushing for new members on Meta\u2019s board.<\/p>\n

The filing outlines the shareholders\u2019 concerns about both directors.<\/p>\n

Alford joined Meta\u2019s board in 2019 after she was previously hired as the CFO and head of operations for Zuckerberg\u2019s charity, the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, the filing notes. The group argues that Alford\u2019s involvement with the charity disqualifies her from designation as an \u201cindependent director.\u201d<\/p>\n

Marc Andreessen has served on Meta\u2019s board for 13 years.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

The group asserts that Andreessen\u2019s lengthy 13-year tenure on Meta\u2019s board \u201calready raises concerns around a potential lack of independence.\u201d<\/p>\n

The filing also questions Andreessen\u2019s influence over Zuckerberg \u2014 noting lawsuits which accused Andreessen of coaching the company founder on an effort to secure board support for a \u201cnew stock structure giving Mr. Zuckerberg even more control.\u201d<\/p>\n

A Meta representative declined to comment. The company\u2019s annual shareholder meeting is slated for May 25.<\/p>\n

In its 2022 proxy filing, Meta noted that \u201cthe independent members of [its] board of directors provide effective oversight and represent the interests of all shareholders.\u201d<\/p>\n
