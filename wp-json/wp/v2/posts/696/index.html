Megan Thee Stallion has called former MLB All-Star Carl Crawford a \u201cf\u2013king powder head\u201d in a record contract dispute.<\/p>\n

To recap, the 27-year-old rapper signed with Crawford\u2019s 1501 Certified Entertainment record company in 2018, and the two sides have been embroiled in litigation since 2020. And on Monday, the label countersued, according to TMZ.<\/p>\n

Megan Thee Stallion \u2014 who is repped for management by Jay-Z\u2019s Roc Nation \u2014 took to Twitter after the news broke, and appeared to take aim at Crawford\u2019s label.<\/p>\n

Megan Thee Stallion levied several insults at former MLB All-Star Carl Crawford amid a record contract dispute.Getty Images <\/p>\n

\u201cFirst the man over my label said I don\u2019t make him any money \u2026 now he counter suing trying to keep me on his label because he wants to make more money lol if I ain\u2019t making you no money why not just drop me?\u201d she penned Monday, later writing in a separate tweet, \u201cMy lawyers asked him for an expense report (money 1501 supposedly has spent on ME)\u2026 why this grown ass man put his jewelry and chains on there\u2026 lord free me from this joke ass label.\u201d<\/p>\n

Later in the evening Monday, Megan Thee Stallion then proceeded to call out Crawford directly, even accusing him of drug use.<\/p>\n

\u201cCarl I don\u2019t wanna be signed to yo pill popping ass!\u201d she tweeted. \u201cYou talking abt I ain\u2019t paid for a show and you sound slow. I\u2019m the artist I don\u2019t pay you directly maybe fight with THE MAN YOU SIGNED TO AND YOU MIGHT SEE SOME MONEY YOU F\u2013KING POWDER HEAD! You hiding behind [Rap-A-Lot records CEO] JPRINCE.\u201d<\/p>\n

Carl Crawford owns the record label that Megan Thee Stallion is suing.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Crawford addressed Megan The Stallion in an Instagram post on Monday, writing: \u201cStop playing the victim\u00a0@theestallion\u00a0u haven\u2019t paid for 1 show since 2019 \ud83e\udd2c hiding behind\u00a0#Rocnation\u00a0.. U can keep that bulls\u2013t ass mix tape and send over that straight drop whenever u read.\u201d<\/p>\n

The latest specifics in the contract dispute, according to a petition filed by Megan The Stallion\u2019s lawyers in February, include whether a compilation album entitled \u201cSomething for Thee Hottie\u201d released last year counts towards a record in fulfilling her deal with 1501. <\/p>\n

The rapper\u2019s attorney, Brad Hancock, released a statement to TMZ in response to Monday\u2019s news of 1501\u2019s countersuit, stating, \u201cThis is yet another absurd attempt by 1501 to disregard Megan\u2019s album and squeeze more money and more free work out of her for as long as possible. We will ask the court to protect Megan from this type of abuse.\u201d<\/p>\n
