Causes:<\/b><\/h3>\n

Tooth decay, injury, and dental surgery are all causes of tooth loss. Decay is the gradual loss of teeth due to a combination of bacteria, food, and saliva. Injury can occur from banging your teeth together or from a blow to the head. Dental surgery can include extractions (removal of teeth), fillings (putty used to fill in empty spaces in teeth), and crowns (a type of piece of dental work that covers the entire top of a tooth).<\/span><\/p>\n

Signs and Symptoms:<\/b><\/h3>\n

If you are missing teeth, you may experience difficulty chewing, problems with speech, or pain when eating. There are a few key signs and symptoms that should emergence if you are missing teeth: chewing problems, difficulty speaking, and pain when eating. Chewing problems can be the most noticeable symptom as they can lead to missed meals and weight gain. Difficulty speaking can also occur if there is a lack of teeth to support the jawbone. Pain when eating can be due to less contact between the teeth and food which can cause bad breath or tooth decay.<\/span><\/p>\n
