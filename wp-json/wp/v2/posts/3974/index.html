The Toula Portokalos saga continues.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy Big Fat Greek Wedding\u201d writer and star Nia Vardalos revealed on Tuesday that shooting for the third film has finally commenced in Athens.<\/p>\n

The 59-year-old dropped the news on Instagram with a video, while also thanking her producers at Playtone, Gold Circle, HBO and Focus Features.<\/p>\n

\u201cMuch love and gratitude to you all for your support as we waited to film. Greece baby. Greece,\u201d she captioned her clip.<\/p>\n

She also dropped a bombshell in the video, officially announcing that she\u2019ll be directing the feature.<\/p>\n

The Canada native recorded the video from her crisp white hotel room in the Mediterranean country.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe are in Greece filming \u2018My Big Fat Greek Wedding 3\u2019 and thank you for all the lovely messages of just waiting,\u201d Vardalos said.<\/p>\n

She noted that she was in her hotel getting ready to shoot and even gave fans a quick snippet of the set.<\/p>\n

The screenwriter divulged back in September that she finally completed the script for the third installment of her hilarious family comedy series.<\/p>\n

The \u201cMy Life in Ruins\u201d star posted a photo of herself alongside her \u201cMy Big Fat Greek Wedding\u201d co-stars Lainie Kazan, Louis Mandylor and Gia Carides. <\/p>\n

The foursome gathered to honor their old friend and castmate Michael Constantine, who died in August.<\/p>\n

Michael Constantine (second from left), Lainie Kazan, Louis Mandylor and Nia Vardalos starred in 2002\u2019s \u201cMy Big Fat Greek Wedding.\u201d\u00a9IFC Films\/Courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

Vardalos said that the upcoming threequel will honor and be dedicated to Constantine. <\/p>\n

\u201c[Michael] had told me he wouldn\u2019t be able to join us for the third film and his wish was that we go on. I wrote the screenplay to reflect Michael\u2019s decision and will always treasure his last messages to me, hoping we were filming soon,\u201d the \u201cLarry Crowne\u201d actress wrote. \u201cWe miss you Michael, thank you for bringing my words to life with such passion and accuracy, you will always be with us.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cMy Big Fat Greek Wedding\u201d premiered in 2002 and followed Toula (Vardalos), a 30-year-old Greek woman living in Chicago who marries a non-Greek man (John Corbett) amid her family\u2019s objections. A sequel was released in 2016.<\/p>\n

The comedy was a sleeper hit and earned an Oscar nomination for Best Original Screenplay in 2003. <\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
