MIAMI — Heat star Jimmy Butler was ruled out of Game 5 of Miami’s first-round series against the Atlanta Hawks because of right knee inflammation, the team announced Tuesday afternoon about three hours before tipoff.<\/p>\n

“The training staff just made the determination that he will not be available tonight,” Heat coach Erik Spoelstra said during his pregame news conference. “There’s not [medical imaging] tests planned, or anything.”<\/p>\n

Butler woke up Tuesday feeling soreness in the knee and is considered day-to-day moving forward, a team source told ESPN. Spoelstra said the team was unaware if there was a specific cause of the injury in Game 4, or if it was the result of general wear and tear.<\/p>\n

Butler had been dominant in the series thus far, averaging 30.5 points — second most in the postseason behind only Nikola Jokic of the Denver Nuggets — on 54.3% shooting (43.8% from 3), 7.8 rebounds, 5.3 assists and 2.8 steals as Miami built a 3-1 lead over the Hawks.<\/p>\n

2 Related<\/p>\n

Miami was also without Kyle Lowry, who has yet to return from a left hamstring strain suffered in Game 3.<\/p>\n

The Heat handled the Hawks 110-86 in Game 4 with backup point guard Gabe Vincent posting 11 points and four assists in Lowry’s place.<\/p>\n

Miami started former All-Star Victor Oladipo — who made his series debut with 6 points and 8 rebounds in Game 4 — in Butler’s place Tuesday night, alongside Vincent, Max Strus, P.J. Tucker and Bam Adebayo.<\/p>\n

Miami went 15-10 in the regular season without Butler and have gone 13-7, including Game 4 in Atlanta, without Lowry.<\/p>\n

The Heat played only three games without the both of them, going 1-2.<\/p>\n

“We’re not wrapping our mind around trying to win for weeks [without them],” Spoelstra said. “We’re wrapping our mind around just winning one game and we’ve had a lot of different variations of our rotation that’s given our team confidence to be able to handle the things that sometimes are unpredictable. And we’ve gained a lot of confidence to be able to figure out ways to win, regardless of who is playing.”<\/p>\n

Hawks coach Nate McMillan said that Atlanta could change its rotation to match how the Heat make up for Butler’s presence.<\/p>\n

“We have to make some adjustments with [Butler] out of the lineup,” McMillan said during his pregame news conference. “He does a lot for them.”<\/p>\n
