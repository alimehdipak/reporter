<\/p>\n

Melissa McCarthy was \u201cnervous\u201d to do the late Chris Farley proud during the \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d 40th anniversary special in 2015.<\/p>\n

The \u201cBridesmaids\u201d star, 51, had to don the comedian\u2019s iconic plaid jacket and green tie from his recurring character of motivational speaker Matt Foley, who is divorced and \u201clives in a van down by the river\u201d and first appeared in a sketch in 1993.<\/p>\n

Farley passed away due to an overdose in December 1997.<\/p>\n

McCarthy recently dished to\u00a0Andy Cohen\u00a0on his SiriusXM radio show about how she felt wearing Farley\u2019s costume for a skit.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt was really his jacket and they just kind of, like, clipped it back,\u201d McCarthy said. \u201cAnd they\u2019re like, \u2018We\u2019re gonna take it in, but we don\u2019t wanna damage it. And we\u2019re gonna do all this.’\u201d <\/p>\n

Farley embodies motivational speaker Matt Foley in a \u201cSaturday Night Live\u201d sketch with David Spade and Christina Applegate from 1993.Courtesy Everett Collection <\/p>\n

She also noted that it was \u201creally wild\u201d for everyone to see her as the late funnyman in his clothes \u2014 though the experience \u201crattled\u201d her. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt was the one time right before I came through the door, I was so nervous,\u201d said the \u201cIdentity Thief\u201d actress said. <\/p>\n

\u201cThe sweet stage manager [then] came up and he goes, \u2018Honey, are you OK?\u2019 My legs were bouncing to the point where I couldn\u2019t \u2014 I was holding onto the door. And I was like, \u2018I don\u2019t know if I can get the handle open.\u2019 Because I couldn\u2019t. I was not in control of my body,\u201d McCarthy said as she looked back. \u201cAnd I\u2019ve never done that before. I was just rattled.\u201d<\/p>\n

Comedians Phil Hartman and Farley during their \u201cSNL\u201d monologue on May 14, 1994.NBCU Photo Bank\/NBCUniversal via <\/p>\n

Due to her nerves, she phoned her husband, Ben Falcone, before she went onstage to help ease her tensions.<\/p>\n

\u201cChris isn\u2019t gonna do it halfway; you can\u2019t either,\u201d the \u201cTammy\u201d director, 48, advised his wife. \u201cCome through that door like a train. Do it to make him proud.\u201d<\/p>\n

That apparently was enough for McCarthy. <\/p>\n

\u201cI literally was like, \u2018I\u2019m either gonna black out or I\u2019m gonna come through like a train.\u2019 So, thank God, I think I picked the train,\u201d she recalled.<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

Farley died in 1997.Saturday Night Live <\/p>\n

Farley\u2019s Matt Foley character first appeared to tell a teenage brother and sister \u2014 played by David Spade and Christina Applegate \u2014 to avoid drugs or they\u2019ll also be living in a \u201cvan down by the river.\u201d<\/p>\n
