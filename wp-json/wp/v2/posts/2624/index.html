Advanced health trackers are becoming increasingly popular as people strive to live healthier lives. There are a variety of different advanced health trackers on the market, each with its own unique features and benefits. Here is a list of the top 10 most advanced health trackers, along with their key features, pros and cons.<\/p>\n

1. Fitbit Charge 3 \u2013 Best Overall<\/h3>\n

The Fitbit Charge 3 is one of the most popular and well-rounded advanced health trackers on the market. It tracks a variety of health metrics including steps taken, heart rate, calories burned, and sleep quality. It also has some nice additional features such as water resistance and notifications for calls and texts. The battery life is also excellent, lasting up to 7 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks a variety of health metrics, water resistant, long battery life<\/p>\n

Cons: Doesn\u2019t track GPS data, no built-in music storage<\/p>\n

2. Garmin Vivoactive 3 \u2013 Best for Sports and Fitness Enthusiasts<\/h3>\n

The Garmin Vivoactive 3 is an excellent choice for sports and fitness enthusiasts. It tracks all the key health metrics that the Fitbit Charge 3 does, but it also features GPS tracking for running, biking, and other activities. It also has built-in support for popular apps like Strava and Nike+. The battery life is good, lasting up to 7 days in smartwatch mode or 13 hours with GPS tracking enabled.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks key health metrics, GPS tracking, built-in support for popular apps<\/p>\n

Cons: No built-in music storage, shorter battery life with GPS tracking enabled<\/p>\n

3. Samsung Gear S3 Frontier \u2013 Best for Smartwatch Features<\/h3>\n

The Samsung Gear S3 Frontier is one of the most feature-packed advanced health trackers on the market. In addition to tracking all the usual health metrics, it also has a built-in GPS, LTE connectivity, and payments capability. It also has a rotating bezel that makes it easy to navigate the interface. The battery life is good, lasting up to 4 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Feature-packed, built-in GPS, LTE connectivity, rotating bezel<\/p>\n

Cons: Short battery life, expensive<\/p>\n

4. Apple Watch Series 3 \u2013 Best for iPhone Users<\/h3>\n

The Apple Watch Series 3 is the best choice for iPhone users. It has all the same features as the Samsung Gear S3 Frontier, but it also has built-in support for Apple\u2019s ecosystem of apps and services. It also doesn\u2019t have LTE connectivity, so it relies on your iPhone for a data connection. The battery life is average, lasting up to 18 hours on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Built-in support for Apple ecosystem, no LTE connectivity<\/p>\n

Cons: Short battery life, requires an iPhone for a data connection<\/p>\n

5. Fitbit Ionic \u2013 Best for Serious Athletes<\/h3>\n

The Fitbit Ionic is the best choice for serious athletes. It has all the same features as the Fitbit Charge 3, but it also has built-in GPS and support for popular apps like Strava and Nike+. It also has a higher price tag than the Charge 3. The battery life is good, lasting up to 5 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Built-in GPS, support for popular apps, long battery life<\/p>\n

Cons: More expensive than the Charge 3<\/p>\n

6. Xiaomi Mi Band 2 \u2013 Best Budget Option<\/h3>\n

The Xiaomi Mi Band 2 is the best budget option. It tracks all the key health metrics that the other advanced health trackers do, but it doesn\u2019t have any extra features like GPS or LTE connectivity. It also has a very basic design and the battery life is only up to 20 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks key health metrics, long battery life<\/p>\n

Cons: No extra features, basic design<\/p>\n

7. Huawei Honor Band 3 \u2013 Best for Swimmers<\/h3>\n

The Huawei Honor Band 3 is the best choice for swimmers. It tracks all the key health metrics that the other advanced health trackers do, but it also has built-in support for swimming apps like Swim.com. It\u2019s also water resistant up to 50 meters. The battery life is good, lasting up to 30 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks key health metrics, built-in support for swimming apps, water resistant<\/p>\n

Cons: No extra features<\/p>\n

8. Misfit Ray \u2013 Best for Simplicity<\/h3>\n

The Misfit Ray is the best choice for simplicity. It tracks all the key health metrics that the other advanced health trackers do, but it doesn\u2019t have any extra features like GPS or LTE connectivity. It also has a very basic design and the battery life is only up to 6 months on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks key health metrics, long battery life<\/p>\n

Cons: No extra features, basic design<\/p>\n

9. Withings Steel HR Sport \u2013 Best Hybrid Smartwatch<\/h3>\n

The Withings Steel HR Sport is the best hybrid smartwatch. It has all the features of a traditional watch, but it also tracks all the key health metrics that the other advanced health trackers do. It also has built-in GPS and support for popular apps like Strava and Nike+. The battery life is good, lasting up to 25 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks key health metrics, built-in GPS, support for popular apps<\/p>\n

Cons: No extra features<\/p>\n

10. Garmin Vivoactive 3 \u2013 Best for Everyday Use<\/h3>\n

The Garmin Vivoactive 3 is the best choice for everyday use. It tracks all the key health metrics that the other advanced health trackers do, but it also has built-in GPS and support for popular apps like Strava and Nike+. It also has a sleek design and the battery life is good, lasting up to 7 days on a single charge.<\/p>\n

Pros: Tracks key health metrics, built-in GPS, support for popular apps, sleek design<\/p>\n

Cons: No extra features<\/p>\n
