Laser-equipped spaceships were believed to be possible by 2030, and a secret government agency wanted to make planes invisible.<\/p>\n

The now-defunct Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP), which ran from 2007 to 2012, examined the viability of these proposals, among other wild plans like\u00a0nuking the moon.<\/p>\n

Most of the sci fi-like ideas \u2014 which were theoretical or speculative and never came to fruition \u2014 were part of a\u00a01,600-page report\u00a0from the secretive Department of Defense (DoD) agency.<\/p>\n

\u201cSpace offers the ideal environment for laser beam propagation\u201d that can crack or melt targets like missiles, the 2009-2010 report said.<\/p>\n

\u201cThese devices do offer the potential for a whole new class of weapons and capabilities which may complement (but not replace) existing kinetic energy (KE) weapons and electronic warfare.\u201d<\/p>\n

Kinetic energy weapons \u2014 bullets, shells, missiles and bombs \u2014 can hit stationary targets but cause collateral damage and have unattended consequences.<\/p>\n

\u201cHigh Energy Laser (HEL) weapons, by contrast, begin delivering the laser beam\u2019s energy to the target at the speed of light,\u201d according to the report.<\/p>\n

\u201cHowever, they require a finite dwell time to accumulate enough thermal energy (heat) on the aim point to achieve the desired effect (similar to a blowtorch).<\/p>\n

\u201cThe strength of an HEL weapon is its ability to precisely deliver a limited quantity of energy to a small spot with little collateral damage to nearby objects of people.<\/p>\n

\u201cSpeed-of-light energy transfer, coupled with precision tracking, allows HEL weapons to efficiently engage softer targets which are highly maneuverable, only visible for short periods of time or at extremely long range.\u201d<\/p>\n

Space has the best environment for laser beam propagation that can break down missiles, according to the report. Getty Images\/iStockphoto <\/p>\n

It was believed that spacecrafts can be equipped with lasers \u201cin 20 years,\u201d but \u201cpolicy (not legal) concerns\u201d would prevent the DoD from deploying it, according to the report.<\/p>\n

It alluded to a \u201cmicrowave device for crowd control, called Active Denial, which has been shown to produce temporary pain without any injury\u201d as an example of technology that \u201cpolicy\u201d shelved.<\/p>\n

\u201cOther counties may not exhibit similar restraint, as evidenced by the open marketing of laser-blinding weapons despite a 1980 Geneva Convention prohibiting their development or use,\u201d the report said.<\/p>\n

\u2018ULTIMATE OPTICAL ILLUSION: INVISIBILITY\u2019<\/h2>\n

The agency explored cloaking technology that would make the fictitious Marvel\u00a0comic book character Invisible Woman a reality.<\/p>\n

The theory behind \u201cinvisible\u201d planes is making \u201clight-refracting materials \u2013 like glass or water \u2014 appear as curved spaces by themselves,\u201d according to the report.<\/p>\n

Bending the light around an object would create the \u201cultimate optical illusion: invisibility.\u201d<\/p>\n

HOW THE REPORT BECAME PUBLIC<\/h2>\n

Officials also feared that it if had gone ahead with the program it could\u2019ve resulted in the militarization of space, fueling fears of a galactic conflict.<\/p>\n

The report was released after The Sun requested a copy of all \u201cfiles, reports or video files\u201d related to the Pentagon\u2019s secretive UFO program \u2014 the\u00a0Advanced Aviation Threat Identification Program (AATIP)\u00a0on December 18, 2017 \u2014 under the Freedom of Information Act.<\/p>\n

The bombshell FOIA haul includes reports on the DIA\u2019s research into the\u00a0biological effects of UFO sightings on humans \u2014 including burns, heart problems, sleep disturbances \u2014 and even bizarre occurrences such as\u00a0\u201capparent abduction\u201d and \u201cunaccounted for pregnancy\u201d.<\/p>\n

The information on the potential weapons came from a 1,600-page report\u00a0from the Department of Defense.Getty Images\/iStockphoto <\/p>\n

One document sets out how to categorize \u201canomolous behaviour\u201d \u2013 with encounters with \u201cghosts, yetis, spirits, elves and other mythical\/ legendary entities\u201d classed as \u201cAN3\u201d and seeing a UFO with aliens on board would be \u201cCE3\u201d.<\/p>\n

Other documents obtained include studies into\u00a0communicating with alien civilizations and plans for deep space exploration\u00a0and colonization.<\/p>\n

It was outed by whistleblower Luis Elizondo, who headed up the programme, back in 2017.<\/p>\n

Two bombshell videos of\u00a0unexplained UFO sightings by US military personnel\u00a0\u2013 investigated by AATIP \u2013 were also published at the time.<\/p>\n

Around $22million of funding was provided over the five-year period.<\/p>\n
