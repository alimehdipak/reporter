A Gilded Age mansion that was the childhood home of the late heiress\/fashion designer\u00a0Gloria\u00a0Vanderbilt, mother of CNN\u2019s Anderson Cooper,\u00a0has sold for $32.2 million, The Post can report exclusively.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Back in 2014, a group of investors in Russia bought the property at 39\u00a0E.\u00a072nd\u00a0St. for $19 million with plans of transforming the palatial, 27-foot wide sandstone-clad mansion into three ultra luxury condos that, they thought, would fetch around $70 million.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cIndividuals in Russia held the primary equity position. They bought it as an investment, not as an oligarch\u2019s hideaway,\u201d a source said. <\/p>\n

The buyers hired upscale architects CetraRuddy to do a gut renovation that included a central elevator and leather-paneled walls in the lobby, along with internal elevators, limestone floors, chef\u2019s kitchens and gas fireplaces in each unit.\u00a0<\/p>\n

But the Russians\u2019 big plans were dashed in part by the pandemic, which tanked real estate prices in New York, and forced the investors to fight off foreclosure by a lender instead.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cThey wanted a home run. What they got was a good, not great, conclusion,\u201d an inside source said.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The neo-Grec residence features\u00a0unique details, like a copper cornice.\u00a0Built in 1891 by Robert B. Lynd, it\u2019s where\u00a0Vanderbilt, the great-granddaughter of railroad baron Cornelius\u00a0Vanderbilt, lived during her \u201cPoor Little Rich Girl\u201d days \u2014 from the time she was born until shortly before she moved to Paris with her mother in 1924.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Vanderbilt\u00a0died in 2019, at age 95.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The buyer, hidden behind an LLC, is domestic, sources say.\u00a0<\/p>\n

By 2019, the townhouse was on the market for $50 million but nearly went into foreclosure the next year, the Real Deal reported, after the owners\u00a0stopped making a $17 million construction loan payment. \u00a0<\/p>\n

The mansion is currently divided into three separate condos, but could return to single-family mansion status.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Altogether, the 18,408-square-foot home comes with 11 bedrooms, 11 bathrooms, four powder rooms and 1,500 square feet of outdoor space.\u00a0<\/p>\n

The listing broker was Carrie Chiang of Corcoran.\u00a0<\/p>\n
