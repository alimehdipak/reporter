Are they in too Depp?<\/p>\n

Johnny Depp\u2019s notoriously diehard fans are dragging one of Amber Heard\u2019s witnesses online following Monday\u2019s testimonies. <\/p>\n

After practicing psychiatrist Dr. David R. Spiegel testified on behalf of Heard\u2019s team against Depp, people began flooding his WebMD page with scathing one-star reviews.<\/p>\n

Spiegel testified that Depp \u201chas behaviors that are consistent with someone that both has substance-use disorder as well as behaviors of someone who is a perpetrator of intimate-partner violence.\u201d<\/p>\n

When Depp\u2019s lawyers questioned just how Spiegel could be sure of his conclusions without having had contact with the 58-year-old movie star, the psychiatrist said he was denied.<\/p>\n

After he testified, devout #JusticeforJohnnyDepp posters spammed Spiegel\u2019s medical page with negative comments and reviews, calling him \u201cunprofessional\u201d and an \u201cidiot.\u201d<\/p>\n<\/p>\n

Dr. David Spiegel testified against Depp, which prompted Depp fans to flood the reviews on WebMD.AP \t \t <\/p>\n

\u201cBased on his crazy testimony and strange behavior during the Depp-Heard trial, I fear for any patient in his \u2018care,’\u201d wrote one hater. \u201cA narcissist, a fraud and an arrogant idiot.\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cSeems like you just wanted to be on TV. Rambled nonsense the entire time, dodged every question, extremely aggressive, ignorant, diagnosing people without even meeting them,\u201d commented someone else. \u201cNot to be mention calling Mr. Depp an \u2018idiot.’\u201d<\/p>\n

Some reviews were screenshotted and posted to Twitter by NBC reporter Kat Tenbarge, which amassed nearly 25,000 likes on the platform, going viral.<\/p>\n

But some Heard supporters were quick to bite back at the subpar reviews, saying Spiegel did a \u201cfantastic job\u201d testifying.<\/p>\n

But Spiegel isn\u2019t the first Heard witness to face backlash for taking the stand. Earlier this month, clinical psychologist Dawn Hughes received negative reviews on a WebMD page that featured her name and practice, according to NBC News.<\/p>\n
