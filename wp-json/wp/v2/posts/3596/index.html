Did you hear the one about the opossum who walks into a bar \u2014 and gets owned by an Alaskan woman?<\/p>\n

A courageous gal from up north came to the rescue of New York revelers last week after heroically \u201cbouncing\u201d an interloping opossum from a Brooklyn bar. A video of the impromptu animal controller\u2019s good deed is now going viral on Instagram, and city slickers praise her varmint-handling prowess.<\/p>\n

\u201cI was like, \u2018Alright I got this.\u2019 I told everyone, \u2018Listen, I\u2019m from Alaska, let me handle it,\u2019 \u201d Sara Fulton \u2014 who goes by @Sarakalee on Instagram \u2014 told The Post of handling the unwanted customer. \u201cNo one\u2019s going to question someone from Alaska. So that\u2019s when I went up to the opossum and scruffed him and took him outside.\u201d<\/p>\n

This now-viral incident went down at Temkin\u2019s Bar in Greenpoint last week, sending patrons into a panic. <\/p>\n

Flatbush resident Fulton, 33, painted the scene: \u201cI was outside hanging out with my friend, and we see this critter run into the bar and we\u2019re like, \u2018What is that? Is that like a rat?\u2019 And then I was like, \u2018Wait, it\u2019s too big to be a rat.\u2019 [So] we go in and we see everyone on the other side of the bar freaking out.\u201d<\/p>\n

That\u2019s when the Anchorage native\u2019s pal \u201ccornered him.\u201d Meanwhile, since the Temkin\u2019s clientele were \u201closing their minds,\u201d Fulton figured, \u201cI\u2019m the calmest one here in this situation,\u201d and rolled down the sleeves of her denim jacket to take care of business.<\/p>\n

\u201cHold my phone \u2014 I\u2019m from Alaska,\u201d Sara Fulton declared in the viral clip after the opossum wandered into the Greenpoint, Brooklyn, bar. Her inadvertent catchphrase has since spawned a T-shirt emblazoned with the now-viral words. Instagram \/ @sarakalee <\/p>\n

In the awe-inspiring footage posted on Fulton\u2019s Instagram \u2014 captioned \u201cI did that\u201d \u2014 bar patrons can be heard screaming as the opossum enters the joint and darts under a table. That\u2019s when Fulton comes to the rescue. <\/p>\n

\u201cHold my phone \u2014 I\u2019m from Alaska,\u201d she declares before calmly reaching down and grabbing the furry interloper with her bare hand. The confident gal then nonchalantly saunters towards the door while holding the critter by the scruff of its neck like a naughty cat as bar-goers look on in disbelief. <\/p>\n

\u201cI just like went up to him, and I was like, \u2018Hey, I know you\u2019re afraid,\u2019 \u201d Fulton recalled. \u201cAnd I was like, \u2018Alright, I think I\u2019m just gonna scruff you and take you out because that would be the least painful for you.\u2019 \u201cInstagram \/ @sarakalee <\/p>\n

The saga ends with the lone hero \u2014 who manages the restaurant Stowaway, located next door to Temkin\u2019s \u2014 depositing the opossum outside, next to a garbage bag a la Patrick Swayze\u2019s character in \u201cRoad House.\u201d <\/p>\n

\u201cI just like went up to him and I was like, \u2018Hey, I know you\u2019re afraid,\u2019 \u201d the opossum whisperer recalled to NBC New York. \u201cAnd I was like, \u2018Alright, I think I\u2019m just gonna scruff you and take you out because that would be the least painful for you.\u2019 \u201c<\/p>\n

While several times bigger than your average New York City rat, the marsupial didn\u2019t faze Fulton, who told The Post: \u201cI grew up with, like, moose in my backyard and I\u2019d go camping with black bears. So an opossum is literally not a concern.\u201d<\/p>\n

Fulton received several rounds of free drinks at Temkin\u2019s Bar in Greenpoint as a reward for saving the day.Instagram \/ @sarakalee Fulton deposited the bar-crasher outside, next to the trash.Instagram \/ @sarakalee <\/p>\n

Needless to say, her heroism struck a chord with the bar goers, who reportedly bought her oodles of free drinks as a reward for saving the day.<\/p>\n

\u201cEveryone bought her so many rounds, it turned into a party afterward,\u201d bartender Rachel Bessemer told her local NBC news outlet.<\/p>\n
