Bruce Willis was exploited by movie producers who kept casting him in films even though he wasn\u2019t able to remember his lines or perform because of aphasia, a new report claims.<\/p>\n

A Los Angeles Times expose on movie producer Randall Emmett accused the 51-year-old of defrauding investors, rampant drug use, tackling his ex-fiancee \u2014 \u201cVanderpump Rules\u201d star Lala Kent, 31 \u2014 and offering acting roles for sexual favors.<\/p>\n

Sources also said Emmett was happy to keep attach Willis\u2019 name to a film, even as the action hero stumbled.<\/p>\n

In September 2020, Emmett was directing Willis, now 67, in \u201cMidnight In the Switchgrass\u201d and couldn\u2019t get the actor to kick in a door \u2014 even with the help of a stunt coordinator, according to several crew members. In frustration, he left the set and a confused Willis asked, \u201cDid I do something wrong?\u201d<\/p>\n

Randall Emmett and Bruce Willis on set.Instagram \/ Randall Emmett <\/p>\n

\u201cYou would have to be blind to not see him struggling,\u201d Alicia Haverland, a property manager on the film, said. <\/p>\n

That evening, Emmett allegedly called his then-fianc\u00e9e Kent in tears. \u201cI can\u2019t do this anymore,\u201d Kent said he told her. \u201cIt\u2019s just so sad. Bruce can\u2019t remember any of his lines. He doesn\u2019t know where he is.\u201d (Emmett has denied the numerous allegations in the LA Times story. He said this conversation did not take place and that he was unaware of Willis\u2019 declining health.)<\/p>\n

The poster for \u201cMidnight in the Switchgrass.\u201d While filming the movie, Emmett reportedly noticed that Willis was ailing. \u00a9Lions Gate\/Courtesy Everett Co <\/p>\n

But he didn\u2019t stop working with the \u201cDie Hard\u201d star. After that phone call, he went on to make five more action movies with Willis and allegedly used the A-lister as a cash cow for Emmett\/Furla Oasis, his former production company with partner George Furla.<\/p>\n

When the pair would need an infusion of money to pay off debts, they\u2019d discuss making \u201canother bulls\u2013t Bruce Willis movie,\u201d according to former assistant Anna Szymanska.<\/p>\n

Emmett carved out a niche in Tinseltown producing bad movies with aging action stars such as Sylvester Stallone, Al Pacino and Willis, the latter of whom reportedly made $2 million for two days of work. While they were domestic straight-to-DVD flops, the flicks would be financial successes in international markets.<\/p>\n

Randall Emmett and Lala Kent attend the screening of \u201cMidnight in the Switchgrass\u201d in July 2021. WireImage <\/p>\n

The Miami native rose through the showbiz ranks with sheer hustle, first becoming part of Mark Wahlberg\u2019s real life entourage as an assistant, and then going on to make the hit \u201cLone Survivor\u201d with his old boss. As his producing credits grew, so did his lavish lifestyle, which included flying private jets and driving a Rolls-Royce \u2014 both of which he flaunted on \u201cVanderpump Rules.\u201d <\/p>\n

Then there was a 2019 beef with 50 Cent, who loaned Emmett, a producer on his show \u201cPower,\u201d $1 million dollars. The rapper said he was late to repay the debt and roasted Emmett and Kent on social media. The pair soon settled, but the \u201cCandy Shop\u201d artist mocked his old adversary on Instagram after the bombshell Bruce Willis story was published.<\/p>\n

Willis poses with his family including ex-wife Demi Moore. <\/p>\n

Beginning in 2006, Willis appeared in two dozen of EFO films, even as his health was declining. On \u201cWrong Place,\u201d one of the \u201cSixth Sense\u201d star\u2019s last movies, he spent two days on set in Alabama where he was fed lines through an earpiece.<\/p>\n

Szymanska recalled Willis with an aide always by his side. However, \u201cwhen nobody from Bruce\u2019s team was around, the crew would say how sad it was to see him in this state.\u201d <\/p>\n

Willis\u2019 attorney, Marty Singer, declined an interview on the star\u2019s behalf.<\/p>\n

Randall Emmett and Bruce Willis in Park City, Utah in 2012.Michael Stewart <\/p>\n

But the lawyer defended Willis\u2019 work with Emmett. \u201cMy client continued working after his medical diagnosis because he wanted to work and was able to do so, just like many others diagnosed with aphasia who are capable of continuing to work,\u201d Singer said, adding that the actor brought jobs to people during the pandemic. <\/p>\n

In a statement to the LA Times, Emmett blamed Kent \u2014 with whom he is locked in a custody battle over their 1-year-old daughter Ocean \u2014 for the salacious allegations.<\/p>\n
