<\/p>\n

Crypto entrepreneur Sina Estavi made headlines in March 2021 when he paid $2.9 million for an NFT of Twitter boss Jack Dorsey\u2019s first tweet. But his efforts to re-sell it have run aground, with a top bid of about $10,000 as of Thursday.<\/p>\n

The initial purchase was at the time among the most expensive sales of a non-fungible token, or NFT, and came amid a flurry of interest in the niche crypto assets which have since generated billions of dollars in sales.<\/p>\n

Estavi put the tweet up for resale on the popular NFT marketplace OpenSea last week, initially asking for $48 million.<\/p>\n

That price tag was removed after offers in the first week were in the low hundreds of dollars. As of Thursday, the highest bid was 3.3 of the cryptocurrency ether \u2014 equivalent to around $10,000.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy offer to sell was high and not everyone could afford it,\u201d Estavi, who was recently freed from jail in Iran, told Reuters via Twitter direct message, adding that he was no longer sure if he would sell the NFT.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s important to me who wants to buy it, I will not sell this NFT to anyone because I do not think everyone deserves this NFT,\u201d Estavi said.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis NFT is not just a tweet, this is the Mona Lisa of the digital world,\u201d Sina Estavi said.via REUTERS <\/p>\n

NFTs are a form of crypto asset which can record the ownership of a digital file such as an image, video or text. There is no guarantee of an NFT\u2019s value and the market is rife with scams, fraud, counterfeits and market manipulation.<\/p>\n

But Estavi was confident in the value of his purchase.<\/p>\n

\u2018Victim of crypto\u2019<\/h2>\n

\u201cThis NFT is not just a tweet, this is the Mona Lisa of the digital world,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Estavi, who lives in Malaysia, said he had been arrested last May during a trip to Iran and held in solitary confinement until he was freed in February. Iranian state media reported in May 2021 that he was accused of \u201cdisrupting the country\u2019s economic system\u201d.<\/p>\n

Estavi said he had been arrested because of the growth of his crypto exchange, Bridge Oracle, and described himself as a \u201cvictim of crypto.\u201d<\/p>\n

Reuters was unable to independently verify these details.<\/p>\n

\u201cI need the support of the cryptocurrency community,\u201d Estavi told Reuters.<\/p>\n

While announcing the NFT sale in a tweet on April 6, he pledged to give 50% of the proceeds \u2014 which he expected to be at least $25 million \u2014 to charity.<\/p>\n
