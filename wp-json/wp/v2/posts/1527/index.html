California Gov. Gavin Newsom interfered in his state\u2019s sexual harassment and discrimination lawsuit against video gaming giant Activision Blizzard, prompting two of the lawyers working the case to quit, Bloomberg News reported.<\/p>\n

Melanie Proctor, an attorney with the state Department of Fair Employment and Housing, told staffers in an email on Tuesday that she was resigning to protest Newsom\u2019s firing of her boss, Janette Wipper, the agency\u2019s chief counsel.<\/p>\n

Both Proctor and Wipper quit the Activision lawsuit earlier this month, though no explanation was given as to why they departed, according to Bloomberg News.<\/p>\n

Proctor told staffers that Newsom\u2019s office \u201cbegan to interfere\u201d with the Activision lawsuit.<\/p>\n

\u201cThe Office of the Governor repeatedly demanded advance notice of litigation strategy and of next steps in the litigation,\u201d Proctor claimed in the email, which Bloomberg obtained.<\/p>\n

\u201cAs we continued to win in state court, this interference increased, mimicking the interests of Activision\u2019s counsel,\u201d Proctor claimed in the email about Newsom\u2019s alleged involvement.<\/p>\n

The publisher of popular hits like \u201cCall of Duty\u201d and \u201cWorld of Warcraft\u201d was sued by California for allegedly fostering a \u201cfrat bro\u201d culture.SOPA Images\/LightRocket via Gett <\/p>\n

When Wipper \u201cattempted to protect\u201d the agency from outside interference, she was \u201cabruptly terminated,\u201d according to Proctor.<\/p>\n

\u201cI hereby resign, effective April 13, 2022, in protest of the interference and Janette\u2019s termination,\u201d Proctor wrote.<\/p>\n

Wipper is reportedly \u201cevaluating all avenues of legal recourse including a claim under the California Whistleblower Protection Act,\u201d according to her spokesperson.<\/p>\n

Newsom spokesman Erin Mellon said the claims of interference are \u201ccategorically false,\u201d according to the Bloomberg report. The governor\u2019s office \u201cwill continue to support DFEH in their efforts to fight all forms of discrimination and protect Californians,\u201d Mellon said.<\/p>\n

Last summer, California filed suit against Activision, alleging that the Santa Monica-based publisher of popular video games like \u201cCall of Duty\u201d and \u201cWorld of Warcraft\u201d fostered a \u201cfrat bro\u201d culture full of rape jokes, crude comments, and groping.<\/p>\n

Activision CEO Bobby Kotick angered employees who were upset over his handling of the allegations.Getty Images <\/p>\n

The atmosphere was so toxic that one female employee who was subjected to abusive behavior was driven to suicide, the lawsuit alleged.<\/p>\n

Women at the firm, whose workforce was 80% male, were \u201chaving to continually fend off unwanted sexual comments and advances by their male co-workers and supervisors and being groped at the \u2018cube crawls\u2019 and other company events,\u201d the suit claims.<\/p>\n

The lawsuit triggered widespread anger among some staffers who walked out in protest of CEO Bobby Kotick\u2019s handling of the scandal.<\/p>\n
