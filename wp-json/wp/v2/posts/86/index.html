Period piece \u201cSanditon\u201d has gone through an unusual journey \u2013 in Dec. 2019, the show was canceled, but two years later, May 2021, Seasons 2 and 3 were announced, and Season 2 will finally premiere on Sunday, March 20 (9 p.m. on Masterpiece PBS).\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cITV in the U.K. was the primary commissioner of broadcast on Season 1, and it aired in August 2019. It didn\u2019t go out in the U.S. until around Feb 2020, so there was quite a gap,\u201d executive producer Belinda Campbell told The Post.\u00a0<\/p>\n

\u201cAlready in the U.K., there was a big fan base going, \u2018There has to be a Season 2!\u2019 So, when ITV announced that there wasn\u2019t going to be one, [Masterpiece PBS] was determined to help us\u2026.As soon as it became clear that it had done well in the U.S., that galvanized us. It took a fair amount of time after that to get the money together. Throughout this process, I was talking to the cast. There were moments where we thought it was going to happen, and then the money fell through, or Theo said he wasn\u2019t coming back. It was a bit of a roller coaster.\u201d<\/p>\n

Rose Williams as Charlotte Heywood goes to a quaint seaside resort to expand her horizons in \u201cSanditon.\u201d Red Planet Pictures \/ ITV <\/p>\n

Based on an unfinished Jane Austen novel of the same name, \u201cSanditon,\u201d is set in the early 1800s England. Season 1 follows Charlotte Heywood (Rose Williams), who travels to the seaside resort of Sanditon. There, she meets a slew of colorful characters including her surly love interest Sidney Parker (Theo James), his entrepreneur brother Tom (Kris Marshall), West Indian heiress and Jane Austen\u2019s first black heroine, Georgiana Lambe (Crystal Clarke) and sassy Lady Denham (Anne Reid). She also learns about threats to the resort\u2019s funding.\u00a0<\/p>\n

Season 1 ended on a cliffhanger separating Charlotte and Sidney\u00a0 \u2013 and after it was announced that the show\u2019s cancellation was reversed, Theo James announced in May 2021 that he wouldn\u2019t return.<\/p>\n

Theo James as Sidney Parker isn\u2019t returning for Season 2 of \u201cSanditon.\u201d \u00a9PBS\/Courtesy Everett Collectio <\/p>\n

\u201cHe never signed an option, so he was never obliged to come back, but we had talked about it. But I think he genuinely believed that it was an interesting ending \u2013 he\u2019s a creative. So that\u2019s the fundamental reason why [he didn\u2019t return] and he was busy with other work,\u201d said Campbell, who\u2019s also the head of Drama at Red Planet (the production company behind the show).<\/p>\n

\u201cAll of Austen\u2019s stories have a love interest, but they are primarily about the female protagonist. This is Charlotte Heywood\u2019s story. If Rose [Williams] hadn\u2019t come back, that would have been really problematic. So, I\u2019m not diminishing the loss of Theo, but it\u2019s her story. She\u2019s the girl who comes to this glamorous resort with plans to live a life less ordinary, and has this huge love affair with this person that makes a difference in her life. But that ends in heartbreak, as many first loves do.<\/p>\n

\u201cSeries 2 and 3 are about that continuation of her journey as a young woman. The producers all thought Theo was brilliant, so that was a setback. But when obstacles arise, opportunities arise, because you find new ways around them.\u201d<\/p>\n

Charlotte Heywood (Rose Williams) dances with a mystery man in \u201cSanditon\u201d Season 2. Red Planet (Sanditon 2) Ltd\/Joss <\/p>\n

Due to the long wait between seasons, the show also lost Leo Suter, who played Young Stringer, to a starring role on the \u201cVikings\u201d spinoff. But as Campbell said, they\u2019re soldiering on. <\/p>\n

After the initial cancellation announcement, there were also several fan campaigns to save the show, including a #SaveSanditon hashtag on social media.\u00a0Campbell said that made a difference.<\/p>\n

Alison Heywood (Rosie Graham), Charlotte Heywood (Rose Williams) and Georgiana Lambe (Crystal Clarke) are determined to find love in \u201cSanditon\u201d Season 2. Red Planet (Sanditon 2) Ltd\/Joss <\/p>\n

\u201cWhat the fans did \u2013 it wasn\u2019t just this social media campaign,\u201d said Campbell. \u201cThey sent gifts, they sent letters to me, to ITV, to Masterpiece. They did stunts, they drew Sidney and Charlotte in the sand on a beach in England. The amount of effort and ingenuity that went into it was incredibly heartening, and it spurred us on. It made Masterpiece think, \u2018There really is an audience here, a dedicated fan base.\u2019 It gave us something to hold onto while on that journey.\u201d\u00a0<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
