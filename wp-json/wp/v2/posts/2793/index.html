<\/p>\n

Elon Musk said he and the European Union are \u201cvery much on the same page\u201d when it comes to regulation of social media as the Tesla boss appears set to complete his $44 billion takeover of Twitter.<\/p>\n

Thierry Breton, the EU\u2019s internal market commissioner, posted a video on his Twitter account showing him shaking hands with Musk at the Tesla gigafactory in Austin, Texas on Monday.<\/p>\n

In the video, the EU official tells Musk that he explained the Digital Services Act to Musk. <\/p>\n

\u201cIt fits pretty well with what you think we should do,\u201d Breton tells Musk in a tweet that included the hashtag #DSA.<\/p>\n

\u201cI think it\u2019s exactly aligned with my thinking,\u201d Musk responds.<\/p>\n

The two did not go into detail on the new law, which levies hefty fines on companies if they do not control illegal content. <\/p>\n

The rules ban advertising aimed at children or based on religion, gender, race and political opinions, for example.<\/p>\n

Musk, who is on the verge of acquiring Twitter for $44 billion, and Breton signaled agreement on regulation of social media platforms.Twitter\/@ThierryBreton <\/p>\n

Musk secured the deal to buy Twitter last month with a pledge to revitalize the company and expand the number of users by cracking down on spam bots and reducing the amount of moderation to facilitate more \u201cfree speech.\u201d<\/p>\n

The billionaire\u2019s comments prompted a rejoinder from Breton that Twitter must comply with new EU rules known as the Digital Services Act tackling illegal online content.<\/p>\n

\u201cElon, there are rules,\u201d Breton\u00a0told the Financial Times last month. <\/p>\n

\u201cYou are welcome but these are our rules. It\u2019s not your rules which will apply here.\u201d<\/p>\n

Breton\u2019s comments came days after the EU\u00a0passed a sweeping bill called the Digital Services Act, which will require sites to more strictly monitor material about pandemics, wars, natural disasters and other emergencies that governments deem to be \u201cdisinformation.\u201d <\/p>\n

It will also require them to take a harder line against what governments deem to be hate speech and harassment.\u00a0<\/p>\n

If Twitter or any other social media site fails to comply with the rules, the EU has the power to fine them up to 6% of their revenue \u2014 or even ban them from operating in Europe altogether.\u00a0<\/p>\n
