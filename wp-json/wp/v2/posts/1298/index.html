The space race between Elon Musk and Jeff Bezos is heating up, with Amazon unveiling plans to launch internet satellites that would directly compete with SpaceX\u2019s \u201cStarlink\u201d service.<\/p>\n

Amazon said it will partner with three companies to conduct as many as 83 launches over a five-year period to facilitate \u201cProject Kuiper.\u201d The service will purportedly offer fast, low-cost internet access to underserved communities around the world \u2013 the same goal SpaceX has set for Starlink.<\/p>\n

Of the 83 total launches, United Launch Alliance, a joint venture between Lockheed Martin and Boeing, will conduct 38; European firm Arianespace will conduct 18; and Bezos\u2019 private space firm Blue Origin will conduct at least 12, with an option for up to 15 more as needed.<\/p>\n

\u201cThese launch agreements reflect our incredible commitment and belief in Project Kuiper, and we\u2019re proud to be working with such an impressive lineup of partners to deliver on our mission,\u201d said Dave Limp, Amazon\u2019s senior vice president of devices and services.<\/p>\n

Amazon said the partnerships will allow \u201cProject Kuiper\u201d to \u201cdeploy the majority of its 3,236-satellite constellation\u201d into orbit and described the plan as the \u201clargest commercial procurement of launch vehicles in history.\u201d Unlike SpaceX, Project Kuiper has yet to bring any satellites online.<\/p>\n

Jeff Bezos\u2019 space race with Elon Musk has been intensified.Getty Images Amazon plans to conduct at least 83 launches for Project Kuiper.Business Wire <\/p>\n

Amazon received approval from the Federal Communications Commission in 2020 to proceed with its satellite launch plan. The company said it envisions the service will aid an array of customers, ranging from individuals to schools, hospitals and government agencies.<\/p>\n

Bezos and Musk have each made major investments out of their own deep pockets to bankroll their respective space firms.<\/p>\n

Both SpaceX and Blue Origin have conducted flights to low orbit with civilian passengers \u2013 with the latter completing its fourth crewed mission just last month. Bezos participated in Blue Origin\u2019s first passenger mission last July \u2013 infamously donning a cowboy hat after he landed.<\/p>\n

Jeff Bezos\u2019 Blue Origin is one of three firms that will be conducting the launches.Business Wire Elon Musk\u2019s SpaceX has conducted flights to low orbit with civilian passengers.REUTERS <\/p>\n

But SpaceX has established a significant lead in the race of affordable broadband internet. Starlink has more than 250,000 global subscribers and launched more than 1,900 satellites to facilitate the service, according to statements by company executives and public filings.<\/p>\n

Starlink gained international headlines last month after Musk deployed the service to assist the people of Ukraine during the Russian invasion. Musk\u2019s initial drew praise from Ukrainian government officials.<\/p>\n

Bezos and Musk have traded barbs in the press and on social media platforms for years about their various overlapping business interests. Last year, Blue Origin lost a federal lawsuit in which it accused NASA of improperly awarding a lunar lander contract to SpaceX.<\/p>\n

In March, NASA said it planned to reopen bidding on the lunar lander contract to a second company \u2013 with Blue Origin set to re-enter the competition, according to CNBC.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
