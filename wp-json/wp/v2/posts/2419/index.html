Kim Kardashian wasn\u2019t the only one copying a classic look at the Met Gala.<\/p>\n

Social media users were pointing fingers at Ralph Lauren, angrily alleging that the designer ripped off the idea of Alicia Keys\u2019 New York skyscraper cape from fellow high-fashion designer Zang Toi.<\/p>\n

Toi, the go-to designer favored by the fashionable society set, showed a white couture silk gazar floor-length cape with black hand-beaded New York skyscrapers in February 2020 during his Fall\/Winter 2020 runway show at New York Fashion Week \u2014 much like Lauren\u2019s design for the 2022 Met Gala.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy cape was inspired by my love for my adopted home, New York City,\u201d Toi told The Post, noting his inbox has been flooded with fashion lovers pointing out the similarities to his original design. \u201cI came here 41 years ago with $300 to study at Parsons School of Design and I wanted to show my love and appreciation for the city that embraced me \u2014 and allowed me to live the American Dream.\u201d<\/p>\n

In fact, Toi debuted his black-and-white skyscraper cape many years ago during his Spring 2009 collection \u2014 later worn by Ling Tan at the 2010 CFDA Fashion Awards.<\/p>\n

\u201cI decided to bring the iconic hand-beaded New York skyline cape out of the archive \u2014 timeless beauty never goes out of style \u2014 to pay tribute to my favorite city at my 2020 \u2018New York, New York\u2019 runway show,\u201d he told The Post, which was attended by former mayor Rudy Giuliani.<\/p>\n

Social media is calling out Ralph Lauren for seemingly copying Zang Toi\u2019s design (right) at the Met Gala (left.)FilmMagic Alicia Keys wore a cape by Ralph Lauren at the Met Gala 2022.Getty Images Zang Toi featured a design two years before the Met Gala, in 2020 during New York Fashion Week.Lev Radin\/Pacific Press\/Shutters <\/p>\n

Eagle-eyed fashionistas couldn\u2019t help but point out that it seems Lauren may have lifted the idea from the Malaysia-born designer\u2019s \u201cNew York, New York\u201d collection, which included a cape with similar NYC embellishments.<\/p>\n

\u201cThey copied Zang because they know Zang is the most creative unparalleled fashion designer,\u201d one fan said. \u201cI thought it was you and then they said Ralph and I was like WTF,\u201d another commented on the designer\u2019s page.<\/p>\n

\u201cI thought that was yours the entire time,\u201d wrote another. \u201cThe NERVE,\u201d one Facebook user simply wrote. <\/p>\n

\u201cThis is a stunning replica of\u00a0Zang Toi\u00a0He\u2019s the OG,\u201d an Instagram user said. \u201cThe second I saw that cape, I screamed, \u2018That\u2019s a knockoff!’\u201d another person said.<\/p>\n

Worse of all, some social media users pointed out this wasn\u2019t the first time Lauren seemingly copied fellow New York courtier Toi, who typically dresses A-listers like Sharon Stone, Patti LaBelle and Kimberly Guilfoyle.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy immediate reaction was \u2018Well, there goes Ralph copying Zang again!!!!’\u201d one user wrote, while another chimed in, \u201cIt\u2019s not a surprise\u2026 he does it all the time.\u201d<\/p>\n

The designer, who started his eponymous line in 1989 after wowing Anna Wintour, and who has appeared in the pages of Vogue alongside Lauren, also noted he\u2019s seen his designs imitated before.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis isn\u2019t the first time,\u201d he simply said.<\/p>\n

Toi featured a similar cape during Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week in fall 2015.Getty Images <\/p>\n

On the red carpet, Keys told Vogue that she and Lauren were inspired by the Big Apple, since both the \u201cEmpire State of Mind\u201d singer and the designer hail from New York.<\/p>\n

\u201cMy inspiration is our city. New York City forever. We just wanted to bring New York to the gala,\u201d Keys told Vogue, noting she loved the cape\u2019s \u201cartistic vibe.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out to Lauren for comment.<\/p>\n
