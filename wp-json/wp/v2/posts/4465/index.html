Comedian Artie Lange, who has been open about his struggles with addiction, announced Thursday that he completed an intensive New Jersey drug court program. <\/p>\n

Howard Stern\u2019s former sidekick spent more than three years in the program after a judge sentenced him to complete it or face jail time. <\/p>\n

\u201cHappy to announce that I\u2019ve completed the NJ drug court program,\u201d the 54-year-old wrote on Twitter. \u201cThx to my family and friends and fans for all your support.\u201d<\/p>\n

Lange was first arrested for cocaine and heroin possession near his Hoboken home in March 2017. He pleaded guilty and enrolled in a drug treatment program, but was cuffed again about two years later for failing to comply with the terms of the program. <\/p>\n

Lange is the host of the podcast \u201cArtie Lange\u2019s Halfway House.\u201d Getty Images <\/p>\n

The star then spent nearly two months in prison starting in May 2019 before heading into another drug court program.<\/p>\n

Lange has been candid about his ongoing battle against drug addiction over the years \u2014 even sharing pics of his misshapen nose caused by drug abuse. <\/p>\n

\n

Happy to announce that I\u2019ve completed the NJ drug court program. Thx to my family and friends and fans for all your support<\/p>\n

\u2014 Artie Lange (@artiequitter) July 14, 2022<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

In a 2019 interview with Joe Rogan, Lange credited his mother \u2014 who he was living with in Hoboken \u2014 for helping him to stay sober. <\/p>\n

\u201cI started to think clearer and think about the consequences, and think about my mom,\u201d\u00a0he said in the interview. <\/p>\n

The comedian took time off from social media and remained out of the spotlight to focus on his sobriety.Getty Images <\/p>\n

He said he began to realize his mother was constantly worried about him fatally overdosing.<\/p>\n

\u201cI thought about her pain and said, \u2018I can\u2019t do this anymore.’\u201d<\/p>\n

About a year ago, Lange seemed to be doing better than ever and avoided making headlines as he hit two-and-a-half years sober, Lange\u2019s lawyer Michael Robbins told the Post. <\/p>\n

The comedian took time off from social media and remained out of the spotlight to focus on his sobriety before briefly bringing back his podcast \u201cArtie Lange\u2019s Halfway House.\u201d<\/p>\n
