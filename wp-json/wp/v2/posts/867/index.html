Tesla is planning to hold a massive, 15,000-person bash to celebrate the grand opening of its gigafactory in Austin \u2014 and the shindig has local community and environmental groups up in arms.<\/p>\n

Locals raised objections to a permit that was granted to Elon Musk\u2019s electric car maker for an April 7 party at the company\u2019s new 8 million-square-foot headquarters.<\/p>\n

Travis County officials signed off on the invite-only event, which will include two stages with live music, food, and a fireworks display, Bloomberg reported.<\/p>\n

Paul DiFiore, who represents the Colorado River Conservancy and the Austin-based group PODER, said that the event will generate noise, force road closures, kick up dust from construction, and increase water pollution.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis grand opening is not a substitute for real community engagement,\u201d DiFiore said. <\/p>\n

\u201cI urge you to consider rejecting this permit and forcing Tesla to delay their celebration until they start treating the community in Eastern Travis County as more than a workforce for Elon Musk but rather as neighbors and partners.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Post has reached out to Tesla seeking comment.<\/p>\n

The April 7 event is scheduled to draw 15,000 people to the gigafactory.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

Tesla last year officially\u00a0moved its headquarters\u00a0from Palo Alto, California,\u00a0to Austin, Texas. At the time, Musk cited the rising cost of living in the Golden State as a key factor in his decision to relocate company headquarters.<\/p>\n

Musk first\u00a0announced the plans to move the company to Texas\u00a0in October, adding the caveat that Tesla will continue \u201cto expand our activities in California, so this is not a matter of, sort of, Tesla leaving California.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Austin gigafactory is about five minutes from the nearest airport and 15 minutes from the city\u2019s downtown.<\/p>\n

Local activists are reportedly unhappy over the noise pollution and the road closures that will be imposed on the day of the party.Bloomberg via Getty Images <\/p>\n

In its most recent earnings report, Tesla said that it had already started producing the Model Y sedan at the gigafactory.<\/p>\n

Musk, also the leader of SpaceX,\u00a0moved from California to Texas himself\u00a0last year that was the start of a very public breakup with Silicon Valley.<\/p>\n

The HQ move came a little over a year after he first\u00a0threatened to move Tesla\u00a0to Texas or Nevada amid a fight with California state health officials over COVID-19 restrictions.<\/p>\n

The company was told it couldn\u2019t reopen its factory last year when the coronavirus shutdown measures were still in effect.<\/p>\n

Musk has reason to celebrate after he opened a Tesla factory in Berlin on Tuesday.Getty Images <\/p>\n

Musk has been in a partying mood as of late \u2014 with good reason. <\/p>\n

The world\u2019s richest man \u00a0danced onstage Tuesday as Tesla officially opened its $5.5 billion manufacturing plant in Germany \u2014\u00a0an event that was delayed for months\u00a0due to local resistance from some government officials and environmental activists.<\/p>\n

Musk presented the first Tesla Model Y cars to 30 customers in attendance at the event lauding the start of production of what his company has dubbed Gigafactory Berlin.<\/p>\n

German Chancellor Olaf Scholz was also in attendance.<\/p>\n
