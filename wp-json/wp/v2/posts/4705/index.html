No, you haven\u2019t gotten punked.<\/p>\n

Tiffany & Co. will now turn CryptoPunk non-fungible tokens (NFTs) into custom pendants \u2014 to the tune of $50,000.<\/p>\n

The jewelry company announced its latest NFT endeavor \u2014 dubbed NFTiff \u2014 on Sunday when they also revealed customers could transform their CryptoPunk NFTs into their very own \u201cbespoke pendant handcrafted by Tiffany & Co artisans.\u201d<\/p>\n

Launching in three days, NFTiff is a \u201ccollection of 250 digital passes,\u201d according to the company\u2019s new website specifically designed for the project. Since the maximum number of pendants is 250, customers are capped at purchasing three.<\/p>\n

Each pendant will be interpreted by a Tiffany & Co. designer and produced using gemstones and enamel.Tiffany & Co. <\/p>\n

The pieces are exclusive to holders of CryptoPunk NFTs, of which there are 10,000 available on the Ethereum blockchain. Each 24\u00d724 \u201cpixel art image\u201d is generated algorithmically, according to the founders at Larva Labs.<\/p>\n

A gem-encrusted pendant from Tiffany & Co. will cost 30 ETH, or nearly $50,000, which includes the creation of the piece as well as the shipping and handling.<\/p>\n

The luxury jewelry brand will only be supplying 250 pendants for nearly $50,000 a pop.Tiffany & Co. <\/p>\n

Each pendant will consist of gemstone and enamel that will closely mimic the 87 attributes and 159 colors that appear in CryptoPunk NFTs. Depending on who purchases the pendant and which NFT they own, Tiffany & Co. designers will use some 30 gemstones and\/or diamonds to craft the custom pendant, producing a render for the customer by October.<\/p>\n

But clients may have to wait until 2023 to receive the physical pendant, the site says, but the digital copy will be available as soon as it\u2019s finished.<\/p>\n

As for the announcement on Twitter, some users seemed thrilled at the prospect of the major luxury jewelry brand cashing in on the NFT fad, while others weren\u2019t too keen on the trendy tokens.<\/p>\n

The company is the latest brand to cash in on the crypto craze and craft NFTs.Tiffany & Co. <\/p>\n

This is one of the top luxury\/jewelry brands in the world\u2026it\u2019s a big deal and brings in new interest on both sides of the fence,\u201d tweeted one crypto enthusiast.<\/p>\n

\u201cThis is the coolest thing ever,\u201d another user gushed.<\/p>\n

The announcement comes after the crypto and NFT market plummeted this summer.Tiffany & Co. <\/p>\n

\u201cDear God, now these people are gonna signal to this as a sign of how \u2018real\u2019 and \u2018important\u2019 NFTs are, as if this isn\u2019t just a sucker money grab,\u201d chided one critic.<\/p>\n

\u201cPSA guys\u2026 If you\u2019re getting your girl something nice in a blue box for the first time & this is what\u2019s inside, it\u2019s probably not going to go over as well as you hope,\u201d quipped someone else.<\/p>\n

The enamel, according to the website, will be made out of 18k rose or yellow gold, depending on the NFT.Tiffany & Co. \t \t <\/p>\n

While Tiffany & Co. might be the latest major brand in the NFT sphere, they aren\u2019t alone. Coca-Cola, Adidas and Mac Cosmetics are amongst the major labels cashing in on the crypto craze \u2014 yet really only appealing to those who can afford it.<\/p>\n

Justin Bieber purchased a Bored Ape NFT earlier this year for $1.29 million, while other A-listers crafted their own tokens for some extra dough. Madonna\u2019s full-frontal NFTs \u2014 which sold for more than $100,000 each \u2014 and a token of EDM musician Diplo are the latest celebrity NFT collectors have fought over.<\/p>\n

Along with the crypto popularity \u2014 which boomed as people grew bored with their stimulus check cash \u2014 came the rise of NFTs into the mainstream, but the niche, somewhat lucrative passion project for many turned sour this year, as the volatile crypto market faltered.<\/p>\n
