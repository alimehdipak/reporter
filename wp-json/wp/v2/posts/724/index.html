Twitter locked the account of a right-leaning parody site, The Babylon Bee, after it awarded Rachel Levine, the transgender Biden administration official, the title of \u201cman of the year.\u201d<\/p>\n

The Babylon Bee story was a reaction to USA Today\u2019s naming of Levine, who is US assistant secretary for health for the US Department of Health and Human Services, as one of its \u201cwomen of the year\u201d last week.<\/p>\n

Twitter says it will restore the account, which has more than 1.3 million followers, if the Bee deletes the tweet, but CEO Seth Dillon says he has no intention of doing so.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019re not deleting anything,\u201d Dillon tweeted from his personal account. \u201cTruth is not hate speech. If the cost of telling the truth is the loss of our Twitter account, then so be it.\u201d<\/p>\n

The banned article notes that Levine \u201cserves proudly as the first man in that position to dress like a western cultural stereotype of a woman. He is also an admiral in the\u00a0US Public Health Service Commissioned Corps. What a boss!\u201d<\/p>\n

\u201cHe often wears a dress, which some people think is weird \u2014 but he doesn\u2019t care one bit,\u201d the article continues. \u201cCome on! Men in India wear dress-type garments, don\u2019t they?\u201d<\/p>\n

Twitter has suspended the account of right-leaning satirical news site The Babylon Bee after it named Rachel Levine, a Biden administration official, as its \u201cman of the year.\u201dAP Twitter suspended the account after it said the Bee violated its \u201chateful conduct\u201d policy.Twitter\/@SethDillon <\/p>\n

Dillon tweeted news of the suspension from his own account, attaching a screenshot of the notice from Twitter.<\/p>\n

Twitter cited its policy on \u201chateful conduct,\u201d which states: \u201cYou may not promote violence against, threaten, or harass other people on the basis of race, ethnicity, national origin, sexual orientation, gender, gender identity, religious affiliation, age, disability, or serious disease.\u201d<\/p>\n

Bee CEO Seth Dillon has refused Twitter\u2019s demand to delete the original post about Levine.Twitter\/@SethDillon <\/p>\n

\u201cThe account you referenced as been temporarily blocked for violating our\u00a0hateful conduct policy,\u201d a Twitter spokesperson said.<\/p>\n
